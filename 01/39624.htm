srimadhwa b say:

Hi,

 

Apologize for the delay in reply.

 

I would like to let you know that many different factors affect the high speed connection you can achieve with a Wi-Fi connection. Some of these factors you can control are the type of wireless router you are using or the positioning of the router and your computer. However, other factors, such as wireless interference from nearby wireless networks and cable Internet congestion, are hard or impossible to control.

 

Here are certain points to remember while using a Wi-Fi internet connection:

 

Distance from Access Point.

Your Wi-Fi connection will be stronger if you are closer to your wireless access point. When you connect using a wireless device, you should see an indicator that shows in bars or waves how strong your connection is. A weak connection will be much slower than a strong one. If possible, move closer to your router.

 

Users on the Network.

Your network only offers so much speed, and the more users on your network, the less there is to go around. As a corollary to being farther from the router, users closer to the access point will have better speeds and interfere with the signal reaching devices farther from the access point – sometimes entirely blocking farther devices from connecting.

 

Bandwidth Use.

The more bandwidth-heavy activities taking place over the network, the slower the overall connection becomes. If someone on a wired computer is downloading large files or playing a bandwidth-heavy game, wireless devices may see a noticeable slowdown in their Internet speeds. Streaming video, VoIP phones and online gaming all use bandwidth.

 

Device Interference.

Other wireless or radio-based devices in your household can interfere with your Wi-Fi connection. Placing your router too close to a wireless phone receiver or a baby monitor can muddle your connection. Standing too close to a device like the microwave can also cause speed-hampering interference over your network. Avoid other wireless devices that use the 2.4 GHz frequency, and consider changing the wireless channel to cut down on interference.

 

Environmental Barriers.

The space between your router and your wireless device is one aspect of signal strength; the barriers present between the access point and device is another often-overlooked one. The more walls, large furniture or appliances between you and the router, the weaker your signal strength. Some elements, such as wood or glass, make little differences; other elements, like metal or water -- including the water present in your body -- have a higher impact in hampering your Wi-Fi signal. It's best to place your router toward the middle of your home or office; using a signal booster can help ensure a stronger connection in larger areas.

On Tue, 22 Jun 2010 13:32:15 +0000, TerriMcCool wrote:
 
> I get many, many advertising emails that are not addressed to me. Where the FROM and TO and SUBJECT line is, theTO is NOT MY email address. Why is this and how can I stop it?
 
 
E-mail programs have a TO: line, a CC: (carbon copy) line, and a BCC:
(blind carbon copy) line.
 
The BCC: line is used by many of us, not for advertising, but when we
want to send a message to a bunch of people, without everyone seeing
the addresses of the others it was sent to. So typically, when we do
that, we put our own address in the TO: line and the addresses of the
real recipients in the BCC: line.
 
That's likely what was done in the advertising mail you get.

a. user say:

Hi Gemini,

Please provide us with the model number of your Computer for a better clarity on the issue.

This issue could have occurred due to outdated Bluetooth driver on your computer. I suggest you to update the Bluetooth driver and check if you can recover the Bluetooth tool. Follow the below steps to update the Bluetooth driver.

 

  1. Select the Start button, type Device Manager, then select Device Manager from the list of results.
  2. Expand the Bluetooth, then right-click on the devices listed, and select Update Driver Software.
  3. Select Search automatically for updated driver software.
  4. If Windows doesn’t find a new driver, you can try looking for one on the device manufacturer’s website and follow their instructions.

 

If the issue still persist, I suggest you to try the hardware troubleshooter and check if the Bluetooth tool is recovered. Follow the below steps to run a hardware troubleshooter.

  1. Typetroubleshooting in the Search tab and select the Troubleshooting from the search results.
  2. Click on view all in the left pan.
  3. Click on Hardware and devices.
  4. Click on Next and follow the onscreen instructions to troubleshoot the issue using the troubleshooter.

 

Let us know if you need further assistance, we will be glad to help you.

★ღ ℓ ღ★彡 say:
Hi evad2066,

The error message could result due to the following reason:

1.     The service associated with Credential Manager does not start.

2.     Some files associated with the application have gone corrupt.

 

Please follow the steps mentioned below to resolve the issue:

 

Method 1:

1.     Click on the “Start”

2.     In the text box that reads “Search Program and Files” type “Services”

3.     Right click on “Services” and select “Run as Administrator”

4.     In the Services Window, look for Credential Manager Service and “Stop” it.

5.     Restart the computer and “Start” the Credential Manager Service and set it to “Automatic”.

6.     Restart the computer and it should work fine.

 

Method 2:

1.     Run System File Checker. Refer to the link mentioned below for additional information:

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/929833

balaji kundalam say:

HKEY is a path present inside Registry. Registry is the place where Windows stores key settings, including the booting settings and other intricate settings, that makes Windows working.

To open registry editor, goto start -> type regedit.exe -> click Enter. If UAC prompts, click Yes.

On the left pane, you can see the HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE. Click the + to expand the tree. Under this expanded tree, look for Software. Expand it. Similarly, look for Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion\FontSubstitutes\MS Shell Dlg. You can notice your current path at the status bar.

Once you click on FontSubstitues, on the right pane, you can see the a list of settings. Goto MS Shell Dlg 2. Double click on it. Type Tahoma, click Ok. Then close Registry Editor window. Now, restart the PC and the check.

Before proceeding, refer the following link for backup of registry, which is recommended:

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/322756
