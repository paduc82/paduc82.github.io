The drive appears to have some issues with 29 of its sectors and this is what the software is reporting. These are usually signs that the drive has started to have its health degrading and it's a good time to back your data up and replace the drive as more of these will eventually appear. 

CHKDSK /R does attempt to isolate those sectors and can prolong the lifespan of the drive but this should be taken as a temporary solution as more of these will appear, regardless of any actions taken as the read/write head flies over them anyway.

captain_wd say:

Cloning the drive may have the same effect on it as chkdsk /r in case you have a problematic sector that cannot be read at all. If you do clone this drive all the problematic sectors will be transferred to the new drive as errors and you will lose that data anyway. CHKDSK /R has a chance to reallocate data from such problematic sectors if the data from them can be read and isolate them. My recommendation would be to run chkdsk /r, run defragmentation and then clone your drive. 

If you have valuable data on the drive I'd suggest to manually copy it first before proceeding with anything.
