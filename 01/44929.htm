Why am I loosing picture quality when burning my movie to a DVD? - Microsoft Community

already lost my mind say:

Why am I loosing picture quality when burning my movie to a DVD?

I am loosing the picture quality when burning my movie to a DVD to be viewed on a tv using a DVD player. The quality is good when viewing the movie in the Movie Maker program on my PC but once transferred onto the DVD the photos are all blurry. Has anyone had this problem before?

considerate_guy say:

Most likely this is caused by a reduction in resolution.  Most video and photos are at a higher resolution compared to the DVD standard.  For example, you might be taking a 12 megapixel (MP) picture with your digital camera.  The preview in Movie Maker tries to maintain as much of that resolution as possible so it looks good there.


But when you save to DVD you are turning that static, 12MP picture into a video that follows the DVD video standard.  Many people tend to forget that DVDs come from the era of analog TVs.  In the U.S., the maximum DVD resolution is 720 x 480 (NTSC) resolution which is about 1/3 megapixel.  That's a large reduction.

 

Some things that can help are to save the movie to your computer in an HD resolution (e.g. 1080p) rather than an analog DVD standard.  At least try that to confirm that the resolution loss is caused by the transfer to DVD.
