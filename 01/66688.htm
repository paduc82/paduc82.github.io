cmdrkeene say:

There's a lot of choices.  You could "roll your own" and just set a scheduled task to run the xcopy command and copy your changed files over, overwriting old backups. That's messy though.  File History gives change-by-change version history of files instead.

You can also use a tool like SyncToy to accomplish the same as xcopy, but still has the same limitations (no versioning).

I personally just store all my files in OneDrive.  Then they are local on my C: drive, plus in the cloud, plus on my other computer, plus also in my automatic backups to my external hard drives. So I'm quite redundant in my backups, even though OneDrive by itself is not meant as a backup service (but it does have change/version history of files).

Given the prevalence of backup services like Carbonite and Norton backup (and the best practice that those are "off site" and in the cloud, so they are safer than local backups), I prefer to recommend those first.  But I'm sure some other forum users have some ideas of good backup software too.
