jrck say:

Ok this is how I managed to solve the problem on my Samsung Series 7 Chronos. PLEASE NOTE: you will need a plug in mouse to control the cursor. Also I had to use the on screen keyboard during this as my keyboard temporarily stopped functioning.

What you need to do is go to Control Panel > Programs > Programs and Features and uninstall the ELAN touchpad program (also labelled ETDWare) then restart your computer.

This will disable your touchpad (and also the keypad in my case). Once rebooted, go to Start Menu > Settings > Update & security. Under Windows Update do a check for updates and this should find the ELAN touchpad driver needed.

For me there was an error message when attempting to install but when you click retry, it says "your device is up to date". Restart the computer again and the touchpad/keyboard starts working with all the multi-touch functions back.

I had to go into the mouse options to activate the multiple finger tap options but scrolling worked immediately. My only gripe is that 2-finger tap is now right click as opposed to open new tab/middle button click as it used to be.
