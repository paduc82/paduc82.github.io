Hi gman7862

On your system you will find 2 types of Recovery partition . . .

One is approx 450MB - 800MB - that is the Windows Recovery Partition (WinRE Tools), which is required to fix your WIndows, reset Windows . . . etc.

The other is a Manufacturers Recovery Partition, that is generally between 10GB and 20GB

The partition you are dealing with is the manufacturers Recovery Partition

If you no longer need to ever perform a Manufacturers Factory Reset (mostly you wont, because it will install a really old version of Windows) you can delete that partition altogether, that will not affect your system at all

Open Disk Management (accessible by right clicking your Start Button)

Right click that partition and choose 'Delete Volume'

Then you need to merge that into your existing OS partition, Windows will not be able to do that, Windows is very limited in its partitioning capability, and because the WinRE Tools partition is between those partitions, it will refuse to merge those partitions
