b) Disable nVidia Power Mizer feature.  Driver versions newer than 200 seem to not be able to turn off Power Mizer with registry modifications anymore.  If I can find 64 bit nVidia drivers for Win 7 prior to 200 I might be able to eliminate the black screens.

This is from a clue that the Dell power supply or BIOS might be incorrectly telling the video card that it's about to overheat and the black screen is an attempt to prevent video card damage (that isn't about to come.  I've gotten black screens at GPU temperatures as low as 43).

d) This fix reduces the number of black screens and might eliminate them.  

Installed SpeedFan and set the GPU settings to between 40 and 50 degrees Celsius.  This only helps if you turn off Dell engineering fan control by holding down 'Shift' + 'Fn' and typing '15324' then holding down 'Fn" + 'r' to enter the white screen and disabling the BIOS thermal controls.  

BE VERY CAREFUL:  Speedfan must be set to Automatic fan speed and be reliably starting on windows boot to prevent thermal shut down events.  Also Speedfan must have the GPU and ACPI settings clicked and a fan Pwm1/Pwm2 selected for ACPI and for the GPU (video card has heat sink grills extending to both fan outlets).

Hi MM Newbie,

You might have saved your project as a movie, and imported it again to Movie Maker.

There are two ways of saving a movie. The first one is saving a project (Alt + F, Save Project). Saving with this option allows you to edit the changes you have made on the movie as many times as you want.

After you are done with the editing, you can save it as a movie (Alt + F, Save Movie). This will save your movie as a final product, which you can use to play on a media player or share to others. Loading this again to Windows Live Movie Maker will be considered a new video clip, and you cannot make the changes to the individual files compared to saving as a project.

You can also check considerate_guy's post in this thread on how saving as a project and movie works.
