¡firedog say:

The default From address will be the default account address unless (a) the message being composed is in reply to, or forwarding, a message received on a different account, in which case the From address will be that of the account to which the message being replied to or forwarded was received, or (b) a different account or account folder is active in the folder pane, in which case the From address will be that of the active account.

Addresses in the dropdown are in the order of account folders in Explorer. By account folder, I mean a folder containing an .oeaccount configuration file. The naming of these folders is pretty impenetrable, but unless any of the accounts has been renamed, the folder names will normally appear in a format derived from the email address, like Domain (username). It gets a bit complicated if that exceeds 15 characters; if it does, the last four are removed and replaced by a space and three random characters. The name of the account folder for a renamed account may differ, but it's unclear what triggers a renaming of the folder.

 

pa bear - ms mvp say:
Is there any way to change the order of addresses in this drop-down field?


You can prepend the mail account names numbers; e.g.,

  • 1-Hotmail
  • 2-Gmail
  • 3-Yahoo
  • 4-Comcast

Your folders in Folders pane will be in numerical order and the FROM dropdown will be in the same order (without the numbers).

 

Prepend what you consider to be your default account with numeral one (1).

 

Note that if, e.g., the 2-Gmail account is selected/highlighted in Folders pane, the FROM address will be my Gmail address.

I will be glad to help you with the issue you have with the computer. I understand the frustration when things don't work the way it should.

Computer describes any physical space on a hard drive that doesn't belong to a partition as unallocated. This means that no programs can write to the space.

When setting up new computers, the system installation will ask you to partition your unallocated space. This represents the space on your hard drive that has not been assigned a drive letter. Without allocation, you can't use the space on the drive. In some cases, existing computers will also have unallocated space. In Windows, the Disk Management control panel provides tools for allocating this space.

To allocate the unallocated space as a usable hard drive in Windows, follow these steps:

Right click "This PC", select "manage", then select "disk management. Right-click the unallocated volume.

  1. Choose New Simple Volume from the shortcut menu.

    The New Simple Volume Wizard appears.

  2. Click the Next button.

  3. Set the size of the new volume by using the Simple Volume Size in MB text box.

    The size is already preset to equal the entire disk capacity, which is recommended. If you need to set it to a smaller size, do so. The remaining space on the drive continues to be unallocated.

  4. Click the Next button.

    Windows lets you assign the drive a letter, or you can mount the drive on an NTFS volume as a folder. Or, you can do neither, depending on how you fill in the wizard.

  5. (Optional) Choose a letter for the new volume.

    My advice is to use the letter that’s provided.

  6. Click the Next button.

  7. Ensure the option Format This Volume with the Following Settings is chosen.

  8. Ensure that the NTFS format is chosen.

  9. Click the Next button.

  10. Click the Finish button to create the new volume.

  11. Windows prepares the disk by formatting it, laying down the tracks (or parking spaces) for the files. The amount of time taken to complete the operation depends on the size of the volume. Larger disk drives take longer to format.

  12. The display in the Disk Management console shows the drive being formatted; you can watch its progress in the Status column at the top center of the window. The drive isn’t assigned its new letter until after it’s formatted.

  13. When the operation is complete, you can close the Disk Management console.

The newly created disk drive appears in the Computer window. It’s immediately available for use.

Hope this information is helpful. Please post in case you have any further issues, we are happy to help you further.

It will scan and try to fix any corruption or bad sectors on your hard drive and mostly remove that as a potential cause.
