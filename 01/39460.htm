I fixed it by creating a new save, hitting the wait button to create an autosave over the corrupted file, quitting, and then restarting from the autosave. The game worked fine afterwards. It also helps to not be in action (pulling a bowstring, smithing, shouting, etc.) when you save your game. Also, saving in graphic-intensive environments, such as falmer dungeons, can cause problems. It really comes down to the amount of data your xbox can process at a time. Good luck!
