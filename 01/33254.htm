    burrwalnut say:

    On the Windows 7 computer, switch on the printer, go to Control Panel > All Control Panel Items > Devices and Printers > Double-click the printer icon > Customize your printer > Sharing Tab > Share this printer and type its name. > Apply, OK, etc.

    On the XP computer, go to Control Panel > Switch to Classic View > Printers & Faxes > Add Printer > and follow the wizard to connect to a network printer. Bear in mind that the printer and the PC that it’s connected to must be switched on to be able to be accessed by other computer(s).

    If you have a wireless printer, the procedure is different, so post back here.

    realrobtaylor say: Thanks, Carlos. You did indeed manage to set set me off in the right direction. I think I've fixed it and here's what I found.

    It seems that the problem was not a compatibility issue with the drive itself but an issue with Windows 8.1 and USB3.0 drivers. (The drive is connected by a USB3.0 input.)

    After following this route, I found a lot more info on the problem here: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows8_1_pr-hardware/after-installing-windows-81-usb-30-ports-dont-work/6da9d02d-f49c-4416-bca2-a5fd01df3583?msgId=3abae810-aa8a-41a8-b936-8e50f920d243

    FYI, I'm working on an Asus machine with an AMD motherboard. Here's a summary of how to replicate what I did:

    1. Go to Settings > PC Info > Device Manager
    2. Uninstall the two faulty xHCI drivers. (Right click on each; select uninstall.)
    3. Go to https://downloadcenter.intel.com/Detail_Desc.aspx?DwnldID=21129
    4. Download and re/install the drivers.
    5. Uninstall the Asus AI Suite II.
    6. Go to https://downloadcenter.intel.com/Detail_Desc.aspx?DwnldID=19880
    7. Download and install.
    8. Reboot for good measure.
    9. Go back to Settings > PC Info > Device Manager and scroll down to the bottom.
    10. Right click on each of the the two new items (I forget what they're called but you'll know them when you see them) and select reinstall drivers.

    And - hey presto - my drive reappeared.

    I'm well aware that adjusting more than one variable at a time isn't an entirely scientific approach. I didn't note down what I was doing as I was going along (I'm no IT pro) so there may be one or more steps here which are unnecessary. Someone smarter can probably clarify.

    But it's what worked for me. I hope this can now help someone else.
