Hi Margie,

I'd like to clarify my understanding of the problem: it sounds like the Photos app on the full-screen Start menu of Windows 8 is showing pictures on your computer that you don't want it to be showing. Is that correct?

If so, you can fix that by removing that folder from the Pictures library (different from the Pictures folder). To do so:

1. Launch File Explorer (Winkey+E).

2. Click the View tab at the top of the window.

3. Click the Navigation pane button and make sure the "Show libraries" option is selected.

4. Below, in the Navigation pane, expand the Libraries tab.

5. Under the Libraries tab you will see the Picture library. Right-click it and click Properties.

6. In the Pictures Properties window, select the folder containing the x-ray images you don't want showing up, then click the Remove button. Then press OK.

This should remove any pictures from that folder showing up on the Photos app tile on the Start menu. You may still see the pictures from that folder up to 24 hours after you do this, but launching the app after that time will reset the images shown. If you want to make the update instantaneous, you can perform the following:

1. Launch the Photos app by clicking on the tile on the Start menu.

2. Bring in the Charms bar (Windows key + C) and click Settings.

3. Click Options.

4. Toggle the Shuffle photos on the Photos tile to Off, then On again.

If you have any problems, or if this does not work, please respond back to this post!

vijay a. verma say:
Attach this drive in another computer and verify whether the drive is working or not, If working or you are sure that drive is allright -

Assuming that there is no hardware problem with your drive -

1. Right click on Windows Start > Device Manager > Click the topmost entry i.e. your device > Click Action in Menu > Click Scan for hardware changes
Now you should be able to see your USB drive

2, Right click on Windows Start > Device Manager > Expand Disk Drive > Locate the Drive > Right click on this > Uninstall > Reboot your computer
Now it should be detected

3. Whether the Drive is assigned a Drive Letter or not

Right click on Windows Start > Click Disk Management > If you see your Drive is listed there but there is no Drive Letter > Right Click it > Click Change Drive Letter and Paths > Add > Click Assign the following drive Letter > Click in the Drive Letter List Box > Choose an appropriate Drive Letter > OK > OK

4. Use the Hardware & Devices Troubleshooter

Type Troubleshoot in Windows Start Search box > Click Troubleshoot > Click Hardware & Devices > Run the Troubleshooter
