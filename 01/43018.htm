Bear in mind, this is what worked for me and my particular Laptop Computer...  this may or may not work for you. 

After MONTHS of not being able to maintain Wireless connectivity for more than ten minutes to an hour or so, I now have a strong and stable Wifi connection that no longer suddenly disconnects from Wifi or goes "Limited".... even after sleep. Rock Solid. Like every other Computer & wireless WiFi Device in my House ... as it should be. 

Device Manager>Network Adapters>Roll back or install Qualcomm Atheros AR956x Adapter to Version 10.0.0.263 - (In MY case, this was a 'roll-back')

>Device Manager>Network Adapers>Uninstall the Bluetooth Wireless Adapter

(The only Network Adapters I have left are my Qualcomm Atheros Adapter for Ethernet and my Wireless Network Adapter)

>Disable the function in the Adapter's Power Management "Properties" - "Allow the Computer to turn off this Device to save Power"

>network and sharing center. >Click on your WiFi (blue link of your broadcasting Wifi Connection) to open WiFi status window >Click wireless properties >Check the dialog "Connect even if the network isn't broadcasting it's name (SSID)" - Be CERTAIN the "Connect automatically when this Connection is in Range" is also checked!

I also uninstalled Google Chrome and re-installed it, but this time I did not sign in to my Google Account. Now I honestly don't know if this made a difference or not, but I read elsewhere on a Thread within this Forum that this is what did it for another User experiencing these issues, so I figured, "What the heck, why not?"... but to be honest, it didn't 'fix' the issue until I performed the following;

"Windows Key" & "Q" to open 'Search >Type "Command Prompt" then Select "Run as Administrater"> After Cmd Prompt enter;
