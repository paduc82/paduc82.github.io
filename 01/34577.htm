In most objective gametypes, EXP from killing someone is halved from 100 to 50, which is a major point for my opinion... you're swapping EXP from killing people for EXP from picking up tags/capping flags/so on. Team Defender's Double Points for holding the flag? Doubled from 50 to 100, which means that much of the time in that gametype (assuming teams of equal skill) you're losing half the EXP for a kill. Domination? Same thing. That's fine if you can do one of the following:

.

a) Double your kills.

b) Hold onto the Flag (Team Defender) for long enough or earn enough additional points from objectives (Domination)

c) A combination of the two.

.

Now, the OP said they were a fairly casual, more chilled out player, so I was thinking more along the lines of reliable, straight-up EXP. If you kill someone in Team Deathmatch, blam. That's it. That's all your EXP. If you want to earn as well in Team Defender, you'd better make sure the above conditions are being met. If you want to earn the same in Kill Confirmed, you'd better make sure you confirm all your kills, which doesn't happen often, or that you compensate for it by denying kills or picking up your own tags. This is easier than Team Defender, certainly, comparable to a good run in Domination. Kill Confirmed, if you pick your tags up twice and confirm or deny a kill for every kill you get, isn't a bad idea and is certainly going to be better in the long run. If you're aggressive and successful at doing so, you can kill players, confirm the kill, enter a battle, deny your team's tags, kill and confirm more kills --or just confirm kills-- AND move on, but you've got to be successful. If you're not, you're only undercutting yourself. If you're not but you are collecting your own tags, well... you'd better hope you can do it one, two or three times each game and in my experience, that isn't overly likely. If you're a good rusher, it's a profitable gametype.

.

Even in Domination, you won't necessarily make many kills that qualify for Defend a Flag or Attack a Flag (or whatever... I can't actually name it correctly at the moment). Capping a flag is fairly good EXP, but that's only enough to compensate for the halved EXP of three kills. Not a problem, because between captures, defends/offenses and a moderate/low killcount, you're certainly able to balance out the loss, but again, it isn't certain.

.

If you're really not an effective killer/not into killing, then capping and making worthwhile kills is a good way to get EXP, particularly if you feel you need a break, but unless you're well positioned (for the bonus kill EXP) or actively capping, again, you're losing out on EXP that kills should be giving you as standard. You can certainly get good EXP out of Domination, but you'd have to be playing properly (or doubling your killcount in the same amount of time as a comparable Team Deathmatch game (or two, since Domination is often longer)).

.

I just feel that getting all your EXP from one standard source is more reliable, therefore more regular. If you're having a bad time shooting, then, well, you might have to change to get decent EXP for obvious reasons, but otherwise I think it's a good shot.

________

.

Demolition I don't like in the slightest, but there are a few problems with this as an example of an excellent gametype to play for EXP.

.

1) EXP is halved, as is standard. You need twice as many kills in Demolition as you do in Team Deathmatch. That's 40 for 20, obviously, and 30 for 15. It's harder to achieve than it seems, particularly since as you get better, you've only got to double a larger killcount.

.

2) Killstreaks are limited in this gametype... Assault Killstreaks are not going to be called in often. Support is fine, obviously, but Support doesn't pay out much anyway.

.

Specialist will get you somewhere with EXP... but you won't be stringing them together very well, which limits EXP gains. I think if you're good you can obviously kill people going for the objective, but you still have to double what you can get in Team Deathmatch and I'm not assuming the OP is better than everyone else, because EXP wouldn't likely be a problem if that were the case.

patrick m. martin say:

It is definitely an interesting discussion!! :)

 

One thing to keep in mind with Kill Confirmed is that you get points for confirming the kill regardless of whether you or your teammate grabs the tag (if you grab it 100 points for you, teammate grabs it 100 points for you and 100 points for them). So even non-rushers can benefit from the extra points that a confirmed kill gets.

 

In Team Deathmatch the game ends after one team gets 75 kills. That's a total of 7500 points that could be earned by players on the winning team. In Kill Confirmed, the game ends at 65 confirmed kills. That's a total of 9750 points that could be earned by the team (65 * 150 points/confirmed kill). This doesn't take into account that kills can be denied or neither confirmed or denied. It also doesn't take into account that if kills were always confirmed by a teammate (as opposed to the actualy killer) the total number of points per confirmed kill is actually 250. These additional factors means that in most, if not every game, the total number of points to be earned by players on a winning team in Kill Confirmed will far exceed the total number of points that could be earned by players on the winning team in Team Deathmatch.

 

Finally, one thing that hasn't been mentioned yet is air support. Regardless of what game type you're playing take some extra time to shoot down UAVs/Choppers/etc... is a great way to boost your XP.
