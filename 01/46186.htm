While there are some well known persons that can live from gaming content like live streaming their gameplay or posting their Let's Play videos on YouTube, it's a very difficult to get noticed on streaming portals or YouTube. 

Unless there is something very special about you that differentiates yourself from the already established entertainers, I would first concentrate on a more classic approach to making money in the games industry. 

Go to a college or university and study a programming related course. Learn Computer Science, and if your school offers specific gaming related courses, take them. 

Make yourself known in the industry by publishing something that people like, and in your spare time, start streaming.

Continue to get better, and look for open jobs in the game industry. Or monitor how well you do with your streaming or YouTubing. 

Then decide if you wish to continue the classic approach of having a job you love in the industry, or if streaming is something that can pay your bills. 

But be aware: Streaming can be a short lived career. Games you like to stream or are good at, can become unpopular, and so will your channel. Or you will grow older, and you can't or don't want to keep up with the younger streamers. 

So make sure that you have a Plan B to finance yourself, when streaming doesn't work for you anymore.
