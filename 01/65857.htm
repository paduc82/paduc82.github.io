milespruett say:
Hi.
My name is Miles.

If your game is running with an integrated card instead of the dedicated one you will notice less fluid performance or problems when running the game. The solution is usually to switch to the dedicated card.

Most laptops (and, sometimes, desktops) have more than one GPU: one integrated and one dedicated. The integrated part of the processor and ensures that your PC makes use of the screen, but has no dedicated GPU. However, some more demanding tasks require a more powerful GPU, which in most cases is not integrated.

If you are not sure if your PC has more than one GPU, you can easily check it by opening the Device Manager and navigating to Display Adapters. Your GPU or GPUs will be displayed there.

Toggle to the dedicated Nvidia card:

• First, open the Nvidia Control Panel. One way to do this is by right clicking on an empty area of your desktop and then choosing Nvidia Control Panel.

• Navigate to the 3D Settings> Manage 3D Settings.

• Open the Program Settings section and select the game from the drop-down list.

• Next, choose the preferred graphics card for this program in the second drop-down list. There you should see your Nvidia card as a Nvidia High-Performance Processor.

Finally, save the changes. Now your game should run with your dedicated Nvidia card.
