I guess I should have performed more of an investigation before I posted but I was able to fix this after reading your post. I took a look at my laptop that had the icon running and I could see an entry for OneDrive within the startup tab in MSConfig but it wasn't in the startup folder. I ran AutoRuns from Sysinternals and on the logon tab I found an entry for OneDrive in HKCU\software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run and I then fired up Regedit and found that key value in the registry. I then went back to my desktop PC, fired up Regedit and created a new string value, renamed it to OneDrive and entered the following in the Value Data field: "C:\Users\[MyName]\AppData\Local\Microsoft\OneDrive\OneDrive.exe" /background to match what I had on my laptop. Upon exiting from Regedit I rebooted and found that OneDrive did start, the cloud icon was in the system tray and I could find the OneDrive entry within MSConfig. Perhaps this might help someone else who has the same issue.  Thanks again.

I also have a Pace STB, and the Xbox One hates it.  The two constantly battle.  When I first received the STB, I had nothing but trouble getting the Xbox to work with it.  I finally figured out that what was happening is the Pace STB will auto-detect the TV resolution and aspect ratio.  If left in Auto mode, and connected to the Xbox, the STB will continually cycle through aspect ratios and resolutions, never settling on one, and this causes problems.

To resolve, I have to connect the STB direct to my TV and manually set the aspect ratio and resolution.  Once set, disconnect the HDMI cable from the TV and reconnect to the Xbox One.  Do not disconnect the HDMI cable from the back of the STB because if you do, it will go back to Auto.  Do not power off the STB, or it will go back to Auto.  Once connected to the Xbox One, wait 1-2 minutes and everything will work.  Good luck though because you will be repeating this anytime the Pace STB powers off, or the HDMI cable is disconnected from behind it.
