ty_adv say:

Do you have HitmanPro.Alert installed on your computer by chance?

We have noticed that a recent update (as in the past week) has caused several of our computers' keyboards to stop working.  You may get to type one key or phrase, but then it just stops working. 

If that's the case, Uninstall HitmanPro.Alert, reboot computer, and see if you're all set.

We also had this issue in the past.  Things that resolved it:

1) Uninstalling all graphics drivers/software.  You wouldn't think ATI would stop the keyboard but....

2) Reinstalling the .sys files for the keyboard.  Steps:

         - Delete keyboard in device manager

         - Open admin command line and do a 'Takeown /f {file}' on kbdclass.sys and kbdhid.sys in c:\windows\system32\drivers. 

          - Change permissions on those files and then rename them to .old

          - Run a sfc /scannow from admin cmd line

          - Reboot and the keyboard drivers should reinstall themselves

And if all else fails, you mentioned that the keyboard works in safe mode.  Run msconfig and disable all non-Microsoft services on startup and then reboot.  See if your keyboard works.  If so, start going through the services and you should hopefully find your culprit. 

Hopefully something here helps.  I had to troubleshoot over 12+ hours to get these results.  Hopefully this will pay it forward.

Possible Fix ->This email is part of a reserved domain. Please - Microsoft Community

ap-[mpn] say:

Possible Fix ->This email is part of a reserved domain. Please enter a different email address ...

After spending 5 hours chatting with some nice people with MS Tech support, I thought I would post this. The other questions and discussions I searched and read were not helpful but maybe this will help others' that have the same problem as I did.

I have a microsoft account I created when they upgraded my work laptop from windows 7 to windows 10. My daughter created a microsoft account when she received a new laptop with windows 8 a couple of years ago. When I tried to upgrade our media center pc from windows 7 to windows 10, it allowed me to use my microsoft account but would not let my daughter create her account. My work owns and runs their own email server. It handles my work domain and it hosts other domains that are free as part of other services my work offers. When I created my account I used my work email address for registration. My daughter used her email address that is setup in the free domains given to other people from work. The strange part is windows 8/8.1 either of us can login with our microsoft account login using our thrid party email addresses just fine but only mine works with windows 10. Come to figure out any email address from the free domains is automatically blocked through validation with the error "This email is part of a reserved domain. Please enter a different email address". 

The first tech person brushed off the issue saying limited support and repeated the error message. The second tech support person was much more helpful and even when so far as remote controlling the pc but in the end gave up saying I'd have to contact domain accounts. In a very nice way, I was told MS has certain rules to follow and I'd have to contact them for support. The only important and critical information I was given by the second tech support person was my account might have been created as a business account. Which got me to lookup exactly what domain account services really is. This led me to https://www.domains.live.com/ . By the way, my work has never and will never subscribe to any cloud services including Office 365. Other than hosting websites and email services, the domains are not used internally nor do they use Active Directory Services. No one could offer an explanation why the domain was reserved by Microsoft after that information was shared.

Long story short if you use a third party domain email address as a username to create a microsoft account or an email address that is not one of the major players, they assume it is part of the domain account services even when it is not.

The solution is to login to your microsoft account through mail.live.com.
Click on the gear symbol or settings and click options.
Click on create an outlook.com alias. Verify your account.
Now you will have *** Email address is removed for privacy *** tied to your microsoft account as an additional username.
Now, you can add the existing microsoft account using *** Email address is removed for privacy *** as the username using your old password.
Since it is still your account only a different username, it will pull down your system settings that was tied to your account.

If your going to add your Microsoft account login to more Windows 10 computers I'd advise you keep the alias just to keep from having to perform all these steps again but if not then you can remove it.

I hope this saves someone else the time and aggravation this problem has caused me today. If anyone else has a better solution other than Microsoft should fix the windows 10 "add account email validation form field", feel free to respond. I'd really like to hear information regarding how Microsoft classifies a domain as reserved.
