frederik long say:

Your dad is probably a two-finger typer. Looking at the keyboard he wanted to type a capital A but did Ctrl+A, thus marking the whole document. The next keystroke replaced everything he had written. When your mother hit Save she completed the disaster.

Since there are no restore points it is not possible to retrieve the file in its entirety. Some undelete tools (which you can find with Google) might find fragments but they would require a binary editor to extract the words.

I'm afraid this is yet another case of someone finding out the hard way that daily or weekly backups of important data to an external storage medium is not an option but an iron rule. We see this all the time in this forum. The cost of a couple of backup flash disks is next to nothing. Creating a shortcut that would do the backup automatically would take less than five minutes.
