arminatorx say:

Since the wording on the other posts point in the right direction, it's worded "backwards". 

You don't set an account or Gamertag as your "Home", but it's the other way around.

Your adult account "owns" the Fortnite game, and by going to the console settings while you're signed in, you can have your account set the console your son is playing on as your accounts's "Home Console".

That way, your account will share the ownership of the games and Xbox Live Gold memberships with this specific "Home Console", which means that everyone (including your son) can play your games on that "Home Console" of your account. 

So you're setting the console as your account's "Home Console". 

Be aware, that your account can set a Home Console only five times during 365 days. So do not repeatedly set and unset the Home Console setting on the console your son plays on, and do not move the Home Console setting to other consoles unless absolutely necessary (e.g. your son got his own console, or that console had to be replaced for repairs).

  1. In the Search box, type Control Panel and press Enter.
