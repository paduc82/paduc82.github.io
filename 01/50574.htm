erika aro say:

Hi,

The registration and activation depend on what type of license used in activating the operating system. If your Windows operating system is an original equipment manufacturer (OEM) version, then it's embedded to your mainboard. It means that changing the mainboard will remove the licensing and Windows will think that you don't have a license. Therefore, if it was not linked, it cannot retrieve a license to verify Windows.

Linking or registering it to your Microsoft account serves as a backup of your key for reactivation. However, having a physical copy or a retail version and linked it from a Microsoft account, it will not have any problem activating and verifying the operating system.

As a reference, you can check this article created by Andre Da Costa on how to troubleshoot Product Activation in Windows 10 and the different types of licensing.

To answer your questions:

1. The Activation option will remain in the Windows Settings. Type Activation in the Cortana search bar and press Enter. You'll see the status of your operating system and a way to change your product key.

2. Yes, Microsoft account will only be used to reactivate Windows 10. Afterwards, you can go back to your local profile.
