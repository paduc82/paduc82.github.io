Can't play my Xbox Game on Windows 10 - Microsoft Community

nathan roberts (sunknath) say:

Can't play my Xbox Game on Windows 10

Forum Article applies to: Xbox on Windows & Xbox Console

Introduction

Some people may take advantage of their Gold Membership for Xbox live so they are able to play online or buy games at a discount - with Deals with Gold. People may even download free games with their Gold membership that become available to them each month.

While a lot of these games are available on Xbox Consoles. Some are games which can be played on Windows 10 . However, some people have problems playing these games on Windows 10 and their Consoles. This article goes through some troubleshooting methods you can do to get you back, playing your game. 

Content

Method 1 - Making sure you have purchased the game

Sometimes we have different accounts to keep our gaming stuff separate to our personal stuff. We may even have two accounts because we have an account that was used to set up the Xbox Account for our children or relatives. If you find that your getting an error "You do not own the game" then please go to: https://account.microsoft.com 

You will need to sign into your account. When your signed in, please go to Payments and billing, then to purchases (Which is found on the top navigation). 

This will lead you to your purchase history. It tells you when you made the purchase and what payment method you have used. You will need to scroll down until you find the game you have purchased. 

Can't find the game your looking for? The chances are your signed into the wrong account.

Sign into your other Microsoft Account and see if it is listed on there. Once you find it listed, you will need to sign into store using your other account. To do that, first go to Microsoft Store on Windows 10. Select your profile picture:

If your on Xbox one, then you can not add another account to store. You will have to sign into your Xbox console using the other Microsoft account.

Method 2 - Do you have an active Gold membership for Xbox Live? (Applies if you have bought the game from Deals with Gold or downloaded it as a free game with Gold)

If you have bought a game from the Deals with Gold section of the Store app on Windows and Xbox, and you can not play the game, then make sure you still have a subscription for Xbox Live. 

To check, please go to: https://account.microsoft.com and sign into your Microsoft account. 

When your directed to the Microsoft account overview page, click onto Services and Subscriptions. You will be directed to a page that lists all of your subscriptions attached to your Microsoft account. If you do not see anything under Active subscriptions, then you will need to buy Xbox Membership before you can play the games you have bought from the Deals with Gold page, and the Free Games with Gold. 

To buy Xbox membership, scroll down to Cancelled subscriptions and select renew under Xbox Live Gold. After this, you should be good to go. 

Method 3 - Making sure your computer/device meets the system requirements

The Microsoft store checks your device to make sure your device meets the requirements. If it does not, then the game will not open or download. This is to stop you from running into problems with your game and damaging your computer/device.

To check to see if the game is available for the device your using, please go to the Microsoft Store App on your device. Then search for the game you want to download. Scroll down and check the system requirements.

You will need to check to see if the game is a Game on Windows. If it isn't, then you won't be able to play it. This will be reviled when you look at the game in the Marketplace. The requirements page will look something like this:

cmdrkeene say:
Hey guys, there's info about this on the net (Paul Thurrott's site, WinSuperSite.com, has covered it).

Excel is what you call an MDI interface.  You can read about it on Wikipedia.  Basically, each Excel window is basically the same incarnation that a tab would be in a browser.  I can't think of any other MDI programs that come with Windows anymore, but old examples of similar programs include the File Manager or several examples on Wikipedia.

To get it's child windows (documents) to appear as separate application instances on the taskbar, Excel does some tricky stuff that isn't really optimal.  It's basically tricking the taskbar into showing them.  Because of this, no preview of child documents, unless you launch an entire new instance of Excel (so that the two documents are cousins (or step children?), rather than siblings of the same 'parent' window).

In Office 2010, they fix this issue by using some documented Windows 7 enhancements to how the taskbar works (more info about that on MSDN).

Although I wish they'd just make Excel work the same way as Word: open an entire new instance for each document.  But alas, that would probably break backwards compatibility with some old macros, and they can't risk losing business customers over that.

    bruce hagen ~ mvp say:

    Is this computer at home and wired? If yes, it is likely e-mail scanning causing this. Depending on your AV program, you may need to reinstall it.

    Turn off e-mail scanning in your anti-virus program. It is a redundant layer of protection that eats up CPUs, slows down sending and receiving, and causes a multitude of problems such as time-outs, account setting changes and has even been responsible for loss of messages. Your up-to-date A/V program will continue to protect you sufficiently. For more, see:
    http://www.oehelp.com/OETips.aspx#3 

    Why you don't need your anti-virus to scan your email
    http://thundercloud.net/infoave/tutorials/email-scanning/index.htm

    Note that for many AV programs, it may be necessary to uninstall the program and reinstall in Custom Mode and uncheck e-mail scanning when the option is offered.
