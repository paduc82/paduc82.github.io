mouneshwar r say:

Hi Matt,

 

         Did you try burning the mp3 as an audio CD or data CD?

         When you insert the burnt disc back into the computer, do you still have the track information listed for the files in Windows Media Player?

 

For your information, when you burn the disc as audio CD, then the audio CDs that you burn do not include the media information from your original WMA (Windows Media Audio) or MP3 files. This means that if you burn an audio CD that contains songs from multiple albums and you try to play that CD on a CD player or a different computer, then it might not be able to automatically display the names of the songs on the disc or in other words the track information.

 

As of now, I would suggest you to try the steps mentioned below.

 

Method 1: Check for track information by putting the CD back into the Computer

a.    Insert the CD back into the computer.

b.    In the autoplay button, select Play an audio CD.

c.    Now check if youre able to see the track information

If you re able to see the track information after inserting the burnt CD on to your computer, then the problem might be with the CDJ player. Youll have to check your stand-alone disc player's documentation for details.

Note: If you've burned the mp3 files as data disc, then you should be able to get the track information on your CDJ player only if your player supports it. If your player doesn't support data disc, then you need to convert the files to a different format that is supported by your CDJ player before burning the music files.
