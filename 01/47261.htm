malacath say:

The BT home hub 5's are not fit for purpose.

The UPnP on them is broken. To start with it doesn't have multi-console support. So own more than one xbox and you will have problems.

Secondly it is not compatible with any game made by activision on xbox one, ps3 or ps4.

Games by activision on those consoles use demonware port mapping via UPnP to open the correct ports for COD.

But the homehub 5 totally ignores these UPnP requests meaning you get moderate or strict NATs in any activision game.

The only way to get an open NAT in modern warfare using the home hub 5 is to forward UDP port 3076 to your xbox one.

But as you own 2 xbox ones you will only be able to get an open NAT on one of the consoles because it's impossible to forward the same port to 2 different devices at the same time.

Unfortunately the only way you are going to get round this is to throw the BT home hub 5 in the bin and buy a decent router that has confirmed multi-console support. But if you are with BT infinity this would complicate things as most decent routers don't have built in VDSL modems which would be needed for infinity.
