Unless your tablet has a wired plug and/or you are using a USB to Ethernet adapter or something similar - you don't use wired ethernet.

 
Sounds like you have other issues than just your Internet and such.

 
First - get yourself a decent router to go between you and the rest of the world.  The router helps separate you from the other people using the same Internet Service as you - you end up controlling the network on the other side of that router competely.  Make sure you change the router password to something good (15+ characters containing at least three of the four following character sets: uppercase letters, lower case letters, numbers and symbols) and if it is a wireless router and you want to use it - WPA2 with a password different from the router password itself that follows the same rules already given for a good password.

 
Once you have that - you could probably go wireless with a confidence - as long as you follow a few other common sense computing ideas.  All your local accounts should have passwords that follow the same rules already given.  Same for your online accounts - all of them should be changed to follow as closely as possible those rules.  You may find some web pages won't allow the 15 characters - at least try to stick with the three of the four character types on those sites.  Make sure you have your firewalls enabled on your systems - no exceptions allowed on the firewall settings.  No third party remote software installed if you don't need it (LogMeIn, TeamViewer, etc.)  A good antivirus software installed.  A good antimalware software installed (no - an antivirus and antimalware software are *still* not the same thing as of yet.)  Keep both of those updated - automatically.  Don't install random stuff off the Internet.  Don't open attachments to emails you don't trust - and even if you do trust them - scan the attachments first.  Same with instant messaging.

 
The encryption thing - other than protecting individual files you send out over the Internet and/or protecting some files you have saved on the local system from prying eyes if they get on the local system with some other account than yours (depending on your setup) - that's probably not helping you in the least.

aljon_t say:

Hi

If your Skype account was fully-updated (successfully merged to a Microsoft account), you can either use Skype name or Microsoft email address (the one merged to your Skype account) then its password to sign in to Skype.

On the other hand, if you usually sign in using a Skype username and then you signed in using its primary email address instead, it will automatically create another Skype account under it. The new account has the system-generated username "live:[Whatever word comes before @ in the email]" and can be accessed using the email account credential.

Furthermore, it is not possible to merge two Skype accounts as well as a Skype account to an existing Microsoft account. What possible is to merge a Skype account to a new Microsoft account created within the process of merging.

You may check this FAQ for additional information. Can I merge or unlink my Skype and Microsoft accounts?
