mann_manohar say:

Hello Andrea,

Welcome to Microsoft Community Forum.

As per the problem description, I understand that two of your Windows 8.1 computers are configured with the same Microsoft account and you want to separate them with different user accounts.

If you do not wish to sync your settings on both the computers and want to configure the second computer with a different user account, you can do that.

Follow the steps below to do so:

Step 1: Create a new user account with administrator privilege

Create another user account with administrator privilege on your second (grandson’s) computer, configure it with your grandson’s email address (preferably a Microsoft account), if that’s not a Microsoft account you can convert a local account to a Microsoft account.

You may refer the video in the article below to get help in creating a new user account on your computer.

Create a user account

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/create-user-account#create-user-account=windows-8

You may refer the article below to switch your local account into a Microsoft account.

One email address to sign in everywhere.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-8/microsoft-account-tutorial

Step 2: Make the second account (newly added Microsoft account) your primary account on the computer.

Step 3: Logout from the old account and login to the new user account.

Step 4: Remove the old user account

Follow the steps below to remove the old user account from the computer.

  1. While you are logged in to your new user account (the one that you want to keep on this computer) type “User Accounts” on the start screen.

  2. Now, select “Add, delete, or manage other user accounts” from the available list.

  3. Select the user account that you want to delete.

  4. Click on “delete the account”. 

Note: Once deleted, the user accounts cannot be recovered. Make sure you back up any data if required.

Please do let us know if you need further assistance. We will be glad to assist you.

Thank you for the information that you provided. Using Wake-On-LAN, your computer will listen for a command using magic packet containing MAC address while the computer is in Sleep Mode. Change screen resolution

  1. Open Display Settings by clicking the Start button , clicking Control Panel, clicking Appearance and Personalization, clickingPersonalization, and then clicking Display Settings.

Screen resolution refers to the clarity of the text and images on your screen. At higher resolutions, items appear sharper. They also appear smaller, so more items fit on the screen. At lower resolutions, fewer items fit on the screen, but they are larger and easier to see. At very low resolutions, however, images might have jagged edges.
