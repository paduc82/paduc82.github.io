a. user say:

This is expected. When your computer comes out of hibernation the contents of the hibernation file are being written back to memory.

luka d. say:

a. user say:

To achieve greater power savings through hibernation, the entire contents of RAM are written to the hibernation file so power to the DIMMs can be essentially turned off. But that means all that data has to be restored to RAM when the computer comes out of hibernation.

By contrast, in 'sleep' the contents of RAM are not written to the hard drive. The power savings are not as great, but shut down and resume are faster.

If you have a laptop with a battery that's teetering on the edge of death, or the conductor called your stop in the middle of GTA your work on a big presentation, it makes sense to hibernate.
