zamadatix say: Did some testing, what you found ends up being half (or quarter, depending on how you look at it) of the story.

- Metro apps, regardless of your per monitor DPI settings, always scale correctly on every monitor.
- Legacy apps are rendered at the DPI of your primary display and later resized.
- You need to log out out to fully observe the effects of playing with DPI on legacy apps.

So, with a hunch that the DPI aware legacy apps I had tested were only polling the DPI once I dug into the Win32 APIs to figure out what's up. It turns out [there are 3 levels of DPI awareness](https://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/windows/desktop/dn280512\(v=vs.85\).aspx): Unaware = 0, System aware= 1, Per monitor aware = 2.

Why you might ask? Well level 2 was only introduced to the API in Windows 8.1 while DPI awareness has been around since Windows 7. So I went looking and it turns out [Chrome](https://code.google.com/p/chromium/issues/detail?id=426656) and [Firefox](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=890156) are both working on implementing this as we speak.

So the answer is: DPI scaling works perfectly, the apps you tested with haven't been updated to support changing DPI on the fly based on the monitor they reside on.
