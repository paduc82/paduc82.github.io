If you prefer to wake the computer using the Power button instead of using keyboard, then you can follow the steps and check whether you can fix the issue.

    a. user say:

    Here's one option to try: http://www.mydigitallife.info/2007/05/24/increase-multithread-download-speed-by-disable-vista-auto-tuning-on-tcpip/.

    You are confusing several similar sounding terms. The network utilization that shows on task manager is utilization on your local area network. and will show as a percentage of the Local Area Network speed (usually 100M) This bandwidth is available for traffic between PC's on your local area network.

    Your download speed is generally constrained by the speed of your connection to your ISP. If you run a speed test http://www.speedtest.net/ (ignore all the ads and just run the speed test) and you only see 56Kbps or if you see 437 kbps that sounds very low for a broadband connection then there may be a problem with your internet connection (but it may also show as perfectly fine and maybe even better than average).  If your test shows below average speeds (I mean well below average - not just a tiny bit):

    1) Try a speed test from a wired connection. If this tests ok, the problem is the wireless from your pc to the router.

    2) If the wired connection is slow and you are running DSL, check that each telephone in your home has a filter on its jack and the dsl modem has a splitter.

    3) Call your ISP

    I hope this helps.

    Good luck!
