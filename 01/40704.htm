fmcf say:

I was getting this prompt and what fixed it for me was to remove the account from the mail accounts in Control Panel.

Go to Control Panel, User accounts, and choose Mail (Microsoft Outlook XXX) (32-bit).

Then click "Show profiles..." and remove all profiles that you don't need.

In my case, the password was being asked for one of those profiles. The account had been deleted on the server, so it would not stop asking me for that password. Not sure if that is your case, but I'm pretty certain that this would fix your issue.

Now to the root cause of the problem, it seems that the Search Indexer is trying to index everything that your Outlook client has access to, which would include all those accounts that you ever accessed. If that's the case, then you get yet another reason to do a profile cleanup, especially if you aren't going to access those accounts anymore. It's just wasting resources on your machine.

"How to Refresh Windows 8 and Windows 8.1"

  • The PC boots into Windows RE (Recovery Environment).
  • Windows RE scans the hard drive for your data, settings, and apps, and puts them aside (on the same drive).
  • Windows RE installs a fresh copy of Windows.
  • Windows RE restores the data, settings, and apps it has set aside into the newly installed copy of Windows.
  • The PC restarts into the newly installed copy of Windows.

"When you reset your PC, the drive will be completely reset, and all your apps and files will be removed from it. Reinstall Windows Preinstalled On Computer

OEM computer manufacturers are required to provide at least one way for you to reinstall Windows if you buy a computer with Windows preinstalled - your computer should have included either a restore partition on your hard drive, or Windows install/restore disks. ﻿Most computer manufacturers also have a feature to make your own install disks.

If the restore partition is still there and was not removed, it will reinstall Windows. There is usually a prompt when you first start the computer telling you what key to press to start the restore﻿ - check your manual if you're not sure how to. Or if you have install disks - insert them, restart and boot from them to start the reinstall process.

If you restore it to factory condition, it will be like it was when you first bought it - all preinstalled programs will be installed. Anything you installed after buying it must be reinstalled. This will remove any personal files on the computer, back them up first.


You can contact the OEM computer manufacturer and ask them to send you an install disk for your computer - they may or may not do it. If they agree to send you one they may charge a small fee. Contact information for the manufacturer of your computer can be found here:
