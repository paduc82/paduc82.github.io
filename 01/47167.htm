thenoseknows say:

I found the solution to the "system partition update" problem I had with the installer: https://www.reddit.com/r/Windows10/comments/3a6znd/we_couldnt_update_the_system_reserved_partition/

Basically, I followed these instructions, step by step:

  1. Press win+r and type diskmgmt.msc

  2. Click on your C: drive

  3. Below the list of drives there will be a partition map, the first partition will be Data or some such, listed at 100MB, right click on it and go to change drive letters and paths -> add -> now choose Y: for the drive letter

  4. Right click the CMD.EXE start menu icon and choose 'Run as Administrator'. Or, to open an admin cmd prompt, in win8 you can press win+x and choose command prompt (admin), in win7 you have to create a shortcut for cmd.exe, then go to compatibility in the shortcut properties, and choose run as admin.

  5. Type: Y: <enter> in the cmd window

  6. Run these commands:

    takeown /f /r /d y

    icacls . /grant administrators:F /t <see note below>

    attrib -h -s -r bootmgr

NOTE: for the icacls command you can use your username instead of administrators, to find out your username type 'whoami'

  • Now open explorer (win+e) go to the Y: drive under computer, Make sure hidden files and system files are visible through folder options. Then go into the Boot folder, and delete all languages other than en-US. Languages are in the form xx-XX. Make sure to shift+delete and not just delete so they don't go to the recycle bin. Empty the recycle bin afterwards just in case.

  • now go back to the admin command prompt, and type this command:

    chkdsk Y: /F /X /sdcleanup /L:5000

this truncates the NTFS log to 5MB, it can be very very big, not leaving enough space for the install. At the end of the output it should tell you that you have at least 50MB of free space on the partition

[HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\StorageDevicePolicies]

sionetix say:

'Insanity for Xbox Fitness' isn't 5 workouts from the Insanity DVD set, but 5 workouts specifically filmed for Xbox Fitness. They are very much in the same style as the DVD set though.

New content doesn't appear very often. I created a list of all Xbox Fitness workouts.On the 'workouts' tab you can see the date they were added. Most were there on launch, since then they added new content in a few waves. But recently we haven't seen new content for months. Two months ago they posted a teaser photo saying there will be a new workouts in the coming weeks. But that didn't happen, and nobody knows why. To be honest, I get the impression Xbox Fitness is about to be abandoned as far as new content goes.

Again, check my list to see what they have beyond BeachBody. The free MOSSA content is excellent.

I got my Xbox One specifically for Xbox Fitness. That was over a year and a half ago. Since then I lost 100lbs to come to my ideal weight, did that with Xbox Fitness as my only exercise, but of course a good diet was the most important aspect. Because I achieved my goal getting a Xbox One for Xbox Fitness alone was worth it.

If it will be worth it for you depends and some things you can't control. Xbox Fitness is great when it works, but since all workouts are streamed buffering can be an issue. And you can have a great internet connection, but sometimes the problems are just on Microsoft's side. Although from what I can tell their streaming has been fairly sold over the past months. Some people do have issues with the game taking a long time to start though, you can find some threads in this forum. If you get hit by that issue that would such if your are in a rush.

The other aspect if it is worth it is how much new content they will be adding. since launch they did add some new workout programs, but the rate at which they have been doing it slowed down. Worst case they'll not add any new content at all anymore.

Xbox Fitness is great when it works. And with the current content it will keep you busy for quite a while. But if Xbox Fitness will work well depends on factors which are not in your hand. It is a gamble. If you don't play games at all I'd have a hard time recommending it just for Xbox Fitness. If you do like games, or the media feature, go for it.
