dropwindow say:

I would guess that defragging does not mess with restore points.


What I am finding out, is that removing or adding non-essential hard-drives on the system affects restore points. If you have a number of drives/partitions on your pc and create several restore points over time, removal of one or more of those drives will affect your available restore points. Remove all non-essential hard-drives, and nearly all your restore points may disappear. This is even if restore tracking is turned OFF on every partition except C:. Reconnecting the disconnected drives brings back the lost restore points,.


I guess if you're going to create a restore point, or install/de-install software, you should shutdown, disconnect all non-essential drives first, reboot, do the operation, shutdown, reconnect all non-essential drives, and reboot again. A real nuisance, but this assumes changes to attached drives will not mess with your listing of restore points, PROVIDED said drives are NOT CONNECTED when the restore point is created.


It would seem to me, when only C: is tracked for restore, changes to non-essential drives should not cause loss of restore points, but I guess MS does not design an OS to handle well, hard-drives being taken on and off a system.  GH

YOU WILL LOSE YOUR SINGLE-PLAYER CAMPAIGN PROGRESS, HOWEVER IT MAY WELL BE ALREADY LOST.

  • Single-player progress
