rifdhan say:

All the answers to your questions:

- A slimline PC means that the expansion slot opening at the back is only half the size. This is known is low-profile. Look for graphics cards that are low-profile and come with a low-profile bracket.

- A PC doesn't have a price limit for component upgrades - there is no such limit for the price of a graphics card.

- The GT 210 is OK for entry-level gaming, but don't expect much. It will do better than the 6150 SE, but won't be top-of-the-line. I would consider the GT 430, as it is pretty cheap for good performance (around $60-$70). Also, if you are looknig for high value, try looking at ATI cards - they tend to be cheaper than NVIDIA. I'd suggest the HD 5770.

- Your power supply is pretty much inadequate. Either pick a card that has a supported minimum wattage, or upgrade your power supply too. If you install a higher-wattage card on a low power power supply, you risk blowing it out and detroying your PC. A power supply is not too expensive - around $20-$30.

 

Hope that helps,
