The simple answer to your question is No and increasing the video RAM allocation will have little effect.

The video signal in its digital format will have been encoded using specific standards, H.264 (mpeg4) being fairly common. In order to view the video this needs to be decoded and rendered for display which is a function incorporated into many graphics chipsets. Because the dedicated drivers are not available you cannot access such a function which at best then gets pushed onto the main CPU. In terms of pure processing graphics chips can actually be more powerful than the CPU which is also trying to drive the computer. The net result is the video stream is unreliable and gets held up.
