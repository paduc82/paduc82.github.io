arminatorx say:

You probably won't get the Snowball running in a way that you can talk in-game without party chat (so you can also talk to PC and PS4 players), and Twitch.

While Voicemeeter Banana (a virtual sound mixer) can do a lot of things, there are certain limits due to the way you need to get sound in and out of your in-game chat. 

First of all, the Snowball is an USB microphone, so you can't use it on the Xbox console at all. That means, you can't talk to the in-game chat. You might work around that, by also wearing a regular Xbox chat headset, and thus talking into the Snowball and the chat headset the same time. 

The Snowball will then send your voice to the streaming app to Twitch, while the standard chat headset will send your voice to the regular in-game chat. 

Depending on how you stream you might have to set the sound output for chat sound to "headphone and speakers" or at least "speakers only" instead of "headset only".

The System Restore helps you restore your computer's system files to an earlier point in time. It's a way to undo system changes to your computer without affecting your personal files, such as eâmail, documents, or photos. Sometimes, the installation of a program or a driver can cause an unexpected change to your computer or cause Windows to behave unpredictably. Usually, uninstalling the program or driver corrects the problem. If uninstalling doesn't fix the problem, you can try restoring your computer's system to an earlier date when everything worked correctly.

 

Thus, it is always good to create a System Restore Point before making any important changes on the computer. This is because; upgrading the RAM involves the installation of its drivers on the computer. So, if something happens on the computer (eg: a Blue Screen Error) after upgrading the RAM and if you are unable to boot the computer even after removing the newly inserted RAM chip, then you can easily restore the computer to the last restore point that you have created when everything was working perfectly.

 

So, I recommend you to take a System Restore Point before making any important changes on the computer.
