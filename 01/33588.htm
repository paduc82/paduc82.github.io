Black bars on all sides in any resolution - Microsoft Community

openclosed123zb say:

Black bars on all sides in any resolution

My PC resolution is 1920x1200 and when recording with Nvidia Shadowplay, the recordings are 1728x1080. If I view these recordings in their raw form, they are completely full screen with no black bars anywhere. If I put them into Windows Movie maker, they show black bars in the preview, even when set to widescreen aspect. If I publish the movie, it always show black bars on every side (top, left, right, and bottom). I've tried 1920x1080, 1728x1080, and 16801050 (16:10 downscale) all with the same result. I can't set it to 1920x1200 because the max height it allows is 1080.

If I take this same raw footage and put it in adobe premier with the same output resolutions, I do not get the black bars, so what is going on here?

considerate_guy say:

Movie Maker can output two aspect ratios:

4:3 fullscreen (1.33)

16:9 widescreen (1.78)

But your PC screen resolution is neither of these aspect ratios. You can see by dividing the two dimensions:

1920/1200 = 5:3 (1.6)

Even if it is scaled down to a vertical resolution of 1080p, it is still staying in this aspect ratio and doesn't fill a full 1920 width:

1728/1080 = 5:3 (1.6)

What happens when you put this video into a Movie Maker project, it will fill the frame vertically, but you'll have about 100 pixels on either side that are empty.

Now when you playback this 1920 x 1080 video, it won't fill the full 1920 x 1200 screen so you get 60 pixels of black on top and bottom. You'd have this same problem if you loaded the video on YouTube since it expects 16:9 video.

Unfortunately, there isn't much you can do. You could set your game so it plays in a 1920 x 1080 rectangle and record that. That would at least make it fit perfectly in a Movie Maker project at 1920 x 1080. But then if you play it back on your monitor, you wouldn't have black on the sides, but you'd still have it on top and bottom. It would look correct on YouTube however, or if you put it onto a Blu-ray player and/or played it on an HDTV.

How to perform a clean boot in Windows
