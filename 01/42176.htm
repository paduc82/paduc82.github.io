mouneshwar r say:

Hi TheGreek20,

 

Windows doesn’t have an option to hide the mouse pointer completely. The mouse pointer does exist and its by design.

 

Let me give you an instance why the pointer cannot be hidden completely.

Hovering your mouse pointer: Position the pointer to a specific object without clicking any mouse buttons and without moving for at least a second. Hover isn't supported by most touch technologies, so users with such touchscreens can't perform any tasks that require hovering. Lot of windows application does use hovering feature. This might be one of the reason that the developers at Microsoft did not include the feature of hiding the pointer.

 

However, there are some third party software that can hide pointers completely.
