How to Convert Write Protected WMA to MP3

 

1- Open your preferred CD burning software and insert the rights-protected files you'd like to convert. Depending on the software, you can open the files in the burn pane or drag and drop them from another folder into the pane. You can also reorder the files in the pane as necessary.

In some cases, they might already be free, but in others you might have to pay for the rights. Remember that the software will not burn anything until it makes contact with the digital rights on your computer.

2- Insert your disc of choice (CD-R or CD-RW) into the computer's CD drive. Wait for the software to recognize the CD, and press the "burn" button or command to start transferring your songs. Close out of any other applications, including Internet browsers, email management software and games, beforehand, as they can hinder the quality of the burn process.

When the process is complete, the software usually ejects the disc by default. If it doesn't, manually eject the disc when prompted by the software that it is finished.

3- Put the disc back into the computer and open the ripping software. Navigate to the "Options" menu underneath the "Rip" tab to specify the type of format (such as MP3), the quality file you desire (such as "medium," "good" or "best") and the folder where the songs should be ripped and saved. Your new MP3 files will be rights-free, so you'll be able to burn them to disc or use them on any media player or device.

cclar say:

Merging free space partition with main partition

I recently uninstalled ubuntu and as part of this, deleted the partition for it. Now I have:

-9.77GB recovery partition
-194.68GB main partiton (boot, system, flies. Basically everything)
-28.44GB free space.

How can I get rid of the free space, allocating this space to the main partition (so I would have a main partition of approx. 233GB)? Will windows do this automatically when the main partition gets full? And what happens if I simply delete the (blank) free space partition? This is what I see in disk management:



The DVD in the drive is the windows installation CD.
palcouk say:

No it wont do it automatically

In Disk Management expand C (assuming free space is adjacent to C) if it isnt you will need to use a third party tool eg

http://www.partition-tool.com/easeus-partition-manager/

 

cclar say: Thanks, that worked. For those who have a similar problem, I figured out that you need to first mak this free space into a partition. Do this by right clicking and selecting the top option, to create a partition out of this space. Choose 'primary' as the type of partition. After this is done, right click the partition you want to merge it to and select 'merge partition if you right click the blank partition, this won't work). Tick the partitions you want to merge and continue 

That's it. Make sure you save the changes when prompted on exiting the program.
