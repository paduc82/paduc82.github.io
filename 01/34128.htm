... how do I change this to make it work again? I'm back at my place now. With stable internet connection.

  1. Right-click on the account name in the folder pane and select Properties. On the General tab, deselect Include this account when receiving mail or synchronizing.
  2. Press Ctrl-Shift-T to start the Add your email accounts wizard. Enter your email address, password and display name for outgoing messages, then click Next.
  3. Click Finish.
  4. When your folders have downloaded and been populated (this might take some time, taking possibly more than one sync cycle to complete), move any messages from the old account that aren't present in the new one to the appropriate folder under the new account. This will most probably include Sent items, but perhaps not much else.
  5. When you're sure that there are no messages in the folders under the old account that you need to keep, right-click on the old account name and select Remove account. This will permanently delete the account folders and their contents.

    Alternatively, leave the account in place, but inactive. Then you have it to hand if and when you end up in an Indonesian jungle with a tenuous Internet connection. Just use the Include this account ... option to activate the one account or the other.

Solution Two:

Reason 2: File System errors

Solution: Right click the external hard drive and choose "Properties". In tools tab click "Check now" and check "Automatically fix file system errors". Then click "Start" and wait for the end.
