shah mir say:

What can I do?  PC keeps getting blue screen stop code

Michele this kind of problem occurs when, unfortunately, you install some buggy driver. However, most probably this driver be of network adapters and I would not recommend you to uninstall all the drivers on first attempt.

Just follow the below steps to first resolve the random restart issue

1) Unplug your CPU from power cord and HDMI/VGA/DVI.

2) Remove all the connections to the motherboard, that includes:

   a) Power supply wires

   b) USB 3.0 cables 

   c) Hard disk wire (SATA) from motherboard 

   d) Processor fan wire

   e) Casing fan wire (if any); Leave the fan wires if they are directly connected to Power supply.

3) Remove battery

4) Remove all the RAM and Graphics Cards

*leave all the LED wires connected to the motherboard as they won't make any difference.

5) Now look for the jumper to clear the bios - two pin port on motherboard under which CLR_CMOS must be written.

6) If in you have a jumper already installed, just remove it, wait for few seconds and then reinstall it. on the other hand, If you don't have any jumper, just grab a screw driver and touch both the pins together, again for few seconds.

This will reset your bios and will fix the restart issue

Now reinstall all of the stuff except graphics card and connect your monitor with mother board (VGA/HDMI port)

Start your PC and run safe mode. 

While in Safe mode, go to the device manager ( Right click on This PC and then select properties followed by Device Manager).

While in Device Manager, go to Network adapter and uninstall all the drivers (2 or 3 may be). 

Restart your PC, Windows 10 would automatically install those missing drivers and you will not see that blue window again.
