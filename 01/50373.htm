Restore Point vs Backup - Microsoft Community

grendel_won say:

Restore Point vs Backup

How is a Restore Point different from a backup? I know a restore point is is stored on the computer & a backup should be stored eternally on another drive. What else is different?

andre da costa say:
A restore point goes back to an earlier point in time or at particular. For example, before a program or driver was installed.

A Backup archives files to a storage medium such as your Word documents, pictures, music and other important files to an external drive. Usually, the process is automatic.

ken blake say:

They are two very different things, and what's different about them has nothing to do with where they are stored.

A restore point is essentially a backup of the Windows registry. It lets you put the registry back where it was at an earlier date.

A backup is a copy of what's important to you, typically either everything on your drive or just your data. In some case, it's just a copy of a single file (for example, although I have other kinds of backups too, I create them less often and I back up my Quicken financial data to a thumb drive daily). Although it's stored electronically rather than on paper, it's conceptually very similar to a carbon copy or a xerox copy.

Yes, a backup *should* be stored on external media, but it can be a backup even if it isn't. And the external media doesn't have to an external hard drive; it can be  CDs, DVDs, a thumb drive, another computer on your network, even a server somewhere on the internet.
