(1) At "START, Settings, Devices, Mouse & touchpad", click "Additional mouse settings".

(2) Click the Touchpad tab, then click the "Click to change touchpad settings" button.

(3) If that fails to be a cure, type "Hardware & devices" into the search box for its troubleshooter.

(4) If that fails to be a cure, the Hardware tab of the Mouse Properties, will allow you to update the touchpad driver. Or it can be done in Device Manager...

(a) R-Clk START, & select "Device Manager".

(b) Open the "Mice & other pointing devices" branch,

     & double-click the Touchpad under it for its Properties.

(c) Click the "Update Driver" button, & then click "search automatically..."

(5) If that fails to be a fix, try reinstalling the Touchpad...

(a) Right-click the Touchpad in Device Manager, & select "Uninstall".

(b) Reboot, & it will reinstall automatically.
