Hi Chiara,
This service always starts automatically when the OS starts. Local Session Manager (LSM) is a core Windows service that was created to manage local user sessions. Be notified that you cannot stop or disable this service because it results in the system instability.

RPC Endpoint Mapper.
DCOM Server Process Launcher.

"We can't find your camera"? Error Code: - Microsoft Community

leonardo del toro say:

"We can't find your camera"? Error Code: 0xA00F4244 (0xC00DABE0)

Hi, recently the built-in webcam on my Surface Pro 4 hasn't been working. When I try to use it I get an error saying:

We can't find your Camera

Check to be sure it's connected and installed properly, that it isn't being blocked by antivirus software and that your camera drivers are up-to-date. If you need it, here's the error code: 0xA00F4244(0xC00DABE0)

I've tried numerous methods that I found online, including:

-Disabling my antivirus programs and trying it again (didn't work).

-Troubleshooting Windows Store Apps in Control Panel.

-Uninstalling Intel (R) AVStream 2500 Camera from device manager (I think that was the correct device, as the webcam doesn't show up in Imaging Devices) and restarting my surface. I did this several times.

-Trying to update camera drivers (there weren't any available updates in Device Manager, Driver Booster 4, or Intel (R) Driver Update Utility 2.9).

-I also added the Camera application to exclusions on my antivirus programs so it wouldn't be blocked.

I know that the camera is turned on in the Privacy settings and I reset the application in Apps & Features. I don't know what do do, so far nothing has worked. Searching around in the Microsoft Community I've found very little people with the same problem, and I haven't seen anyone solve the issue. Please help!

leonardo del toro say:

Okay, guys, I might've found a fix, even if it's temporary. I just tried it out right now.

1. I went to Device Manager and uninstalled my webcam (in my case, it's AVStream Camera 2500 and was under "Sound, Video, and Game Controllers").

2. After that, I uninstalled other camera-related devices:

  • Microsoft Camera Front
  • Microsoft Camera Rear
  • Microsoft IR Camera Front
  • Surface Camera Windows Hello

3. After that, I restarted my Surface several times and when I opened the Camera application it worked again.


I've restarted my Surface once more since then and Camera still works. Hopefully, this is more than just a temporary fix, and hopefully it works for others, too!
