Why has an important Win10 "Critical Error" thread been shut - Microsoft Community

turnbuckle say:

Why has an important Win10 "Critical Error" thread been shut down when the real solution has not been found

I believe throwing of "Critical Error" message in Win10 was a mistake.  The presumed fix that Windows is supposed to effect on restart does not usually work.  There is, in my judgment, no permanent fix yet forthcoming from Microsoft.  In this sense, the whole business of operating system reliability is reminiscent of Vista.

Here's a real-time "solution" (but not a permanent) fix.  It works on three of my Win 10 machines.  When the OS throws this error DO NOT FOLLOW THE INSTRUCTIONS IN THE CRITICAL ERROR DIALOG...i.e., don 't allow it to do the restart.  Instead, the trick is to get an instance of file Explorer open.  This can be done while the error dialog is thrown because you still have the ability to launch or open an existing app.  The second step is to open Task Manager (ctrl-alt-del which may be the only thing that consistently works in a Microsoft OS!), and use it to examine the Explorer application process.  If you are lucky, you will find the "End Task" button will instead be reading "Restart."  Just press that, and the OS will fix itself without rebooting and without pulling down your open apps.

And, if the Explorer app in Task Manager's Applications doesn't show "Restart," you can orderly closed down apps until eventually only Explorer is left, and at that point the button in Task Manager will be "Restart."  Of course, you could pull down open apps yourself while the Critical Error message is displayed, and then you could fire its restart button.  This may, or may not, work.  In any event, you are guaranteed to see the Critical Error message again at some point in the future when you try to use Cortana or type in the Start field.

Sure, this workaround is a bit ugly, but at least open processes will in all likelihood be preserved which during a force restart would be lost.

Microsoft, if you are listening, you better fix this Win10 problem.  It's enough to make folks like me switch to something better.  And, don't ever lock up a thread until a problem is permanently fixed.  In this case, a fix in the form of an OS update hasn't occurred.  Otherwise, this nonsense wouldn't be in my face all the time.  Ugly...very, very ugly.
