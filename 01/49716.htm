Hi Jim,

It is possible that not enough power is getting through to the Surface to either charge it or make a complete connection. Here are few things that you can try:

  1. Disconnect the power supply from the Surface and the wall.
  2. Remove any Auxiliary devices that are connected to the power supply brick brick.
  3. Reconnect the power supply to the Surface.
  4. Disconnect the power supply from the Surface and the wall.
  5. Check the power supply cables and bricks for any damage or oddities.
  6. Disconnect the AC Cable from the brick.
  7. Reseat the AC cable into the brick.
  8. Re-connect the power supply to the Surface, attempt to charge.
  9. Check for errors in Windows. The issue could be software driven.
  10. Plug the power supply into another, and separate outlet. Attempt to charge the Surface.
  11. Test the customer‚Äôs charger on another Surface.

If the power supply does not work on other Surface devices, then you need to replace it. You can visit a Microsoft Store and test it on other Surface device.
