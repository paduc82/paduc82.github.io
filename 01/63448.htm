leefrost say:

I try to avoid those large drives as they are unwieldy, difficult to Shadow copy or back-up in general. If they go pear shaped (die), there is so much on there that would be very expensive to try to recover. 

It would be safer to at least keep personal photos and docs. off them and on flash drives and disks. They are great for movies though!

A 1TB HDD that is just 1 partition has the MBR and OS and programs taking  up only about 30GB or less on average. When new, I would have created a 900GB partition and called it D: "DATA" or "STORAGE" leaving only 100GB for the MBR, OS and programs. Easy to shadow copy and save, off the computer!

You could create a 2nd partition of the unused 500GB space of say 450GB (C: would then be 550GB) and start moving your non-operating system folders to that partition and if need be to the USB HDD for even more storage.

The aim is to end up with your OS and programs and any non-OS data still there on C: partition, taking up about 30/50GB.

What you then do is reduce that C: partition to about 60GB and create a shadow copy of it to transfer to an SSD.

I have for years now, used the free EaseUS Partition Master for creating and moving partitions. Simple and great to use. I don't mess around with the W7 partitioning or backup tools, there not good enough in my opinion (sorry Microsoft :-)

A warning:

Don't try and move the OS with whatever partitioning program you use. Leave it where it is, right at the beginning of your HDD along with the MBR, or you really risk trouble and strife!
