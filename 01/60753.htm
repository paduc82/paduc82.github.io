bobinseco say: Greetings, Stardust2000,

There is nothing wrong with Bindu R's answer, but I have some additional tips that may be of help to you.

Are you wanting to put the photos in chronological order?  If so, you might want to consider renaming each photo by the date and time it was taken in the format "YYMMDD_HHMM".  e.g. In that format, a photo taken right now would be named 091219_1722 (here in the mountain time zone).  Named that way, assuming your folder is set to "Auto Arrange" or "Sort by Name" (drop-down menu for "View"), your photos will be sorted chronologically, making it easier to find specific photos in your collection (hopefully, LOL!).  You could do the same thing after they are moved to a different folder, if necessary.

Alternatively, in the drop-down menu for "View", click "Choose Details...", then check "Date Created".  Then, when viewing the files in "Detail" mode (rather than thumbnails), by clicking the "Date Created" at the top of that column they will be sorted by date and time created.

If you are NOT attempting to arrange them chronologically, but would still like to have the ability to sort them "automatically" in a different folder, you could add a letter to the beginning of each filename, beginning with "a" for the first photo you want listed, "b" for the second, etc. (without the quotation marks, of course)

Here's a hypothetical example of that:

You have 50 photos , and they all have filenames such as beach1.jpg, beach2.jpg, beach3.jpg, ............beach10.jpg, beach11.jpg etc.  Because of the way your computer "reads" filenames, if you organize them by name, they will be in this order:
beach1, beach10, beach11............ beach2, beach21..........

Now let's say you want them in THIS order:

beach35, beach 50, beach22

Put your letters before the "beach"..... For example:

abeach35, bbeach50, cbeach22, then click View>Sort by>Name

You can also leave a space between them (to make the filenames easier to read) if you like:

a beach35, b beach50, c beach22

Lastly, you have 50 photos, so you will obviously run out of letters after the first 26 photos, so when you get to the 26th photo, z beach39, add another letter:

za beach, zb beach, zc beach, etc.

There are other many other possible protocols, but hopefully you catch the gist, and I hope I have made that explanation comprehensible.

Please post back and let us know if that helped!

Bob

as mostofi say:

Hi there,

I've had the similar problem after upgrading 4 computers (which were already registered on a Homegroup) with Windows 7, 8 & 8.1 to Windows 10; we all lost the access to the Homegroup that we were a member before upgrading.

It took me about 6 hours on the phone with Microsoft Support Team and with the help and advice from 3 different Technical Advisors, I managed to fix the problem with the suggestion I received from the 3rd one; so I thought of sharing this solution as it may work for the others as well:

  1. Shut down all the computers that are connected to the router on your network.
  2. Switch off the router and wait for 10 seconds
  3. Switch on the router and make sure there are no computers switched on and connected to it
  4. Switch on your first computer and let it come up and connect to the router
  5. Go to your File Explorer, where you will see the Homegroup icon
  6. Click on the Homegroup icon and in the page, you should see "Create a Homegroup"
  7. Click on that and create a new Homegroup, where you will be given the new Password
  8. Turn on the second computer and repeat step 4 & 5 and when you click on the Homegroup, it will show "Join a Homegroup". You should be able to add this computer to the new Homegroup with the password you've just got from the first computer.
  9. Repeat this procedure for the other computers one-by-one

Good Luck!!

5. Back on the property sheet, switch to Details tab. Against Property, select Device instance path from drop-down and note down the Value mentioned there.

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Enum\PCI\<AHCI Controller Value>\Device Parameters\Interrupt Management\MessageSignaledInterruptProperties

sheri-d say:
Hello

Retrieving your Windows product key depends on where you got your copy of Windows.

Choose the correct solution that pertains to your situation below:

1. From an authorized retailer. The product key is on a label or card inside the box that Windows came in. For more info, contact your hardware manufacturer.

2. A new PC running Windows. The product key is pre-installed on your PC, included with the packaging the PC came in or included on the Certificate of Authenticity (COA) attached to the PC. For more info, contact your hardware manufacturer.

3. A digital copy from a Microsoft website. The product key is in the confirmation email you received after buying your digital copy of Windows. If you bought Windows 10, you'll receive a digital license instead of a product key. You can use the digital license for activation. For more info, see Activation in Windows 10 (https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/help/12440) or contact Microsoft Support here https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/contactus/.

4. Free upgrade to Windows 10. If you upgraded to Windows 10 for free from Windows 7 or Windows 8.1, you should have a digital license instead of a product key. For more info about digital licenses in Windows 10, see Activation in Windows 10 (https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/help/12440) or contact Microsoft Support here https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/contactus/.
