Hi,

I guessing that your machine is mostly current hardware... in particular the motherboard...

Newer motherboards are UEFI Bios capable and may be configured that way by default... How you install Win 8-10 varies by whether the motherboard is set for UEFI or Legacy, and also how the flash drive and SSD are configured...

I suspect your used SSD may be formatted as a MBR drive... If so, it will not work on UEFI BIOS... It needs to have everything deleted and initialized as a GPT drive...

The flash drive you purchased should be compatible with UEFI Bios, but again there may be a trick involved... Selecting it as a boot device in Bios may not be good enough...

You may need to select your machines Boot Options at Startup with F2, F9, F11, F12, ESC, DEL, or whatever it uses...

The flash drive may be listed twice... Once as a Flash Drive, and again as a UEFI Flash Drive...

I don't think you have gotten far enough to get to the point you may get an error concerning how your SSD is formatted...

If you were to decide to not use UEFI Bios, but Legacy, you would need to select just the Flash Drive...

If you select the wrong type of flash drive for the way the machine is setup or the Flash Drive is formatted, you will in fact get boot errors...

slance310 say:

Hi,

If your M\B is set for "Legacy" the flash drives won't be recognized properly...

The flash drives for UEFI are GPT drives formatted FAT32 and will not be recognized as a boot device by Legacy Bios...

The flash drives for Legacy Bios are MBR or GPT drives, but are formatted NTFS...

The Bios detects this and responds accordingly as to the nature of the flash drive...
