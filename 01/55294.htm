pashap say:

FIGURED IT OUT! :D

Fix the Store:
1) open cmd.exe (command prompt) as administrator and run

PowerShell -ExecutionPolicy Unrestricted -Command "& {$manifest = (Get-AppxPackage Microsoft.WindowsStore).InstallLocation + '\AppxManifest.xml' ; Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register $manifest}"

2) Reboot,

run wsreset.exe to reset the store (you may not have to do this, i don't remember if i had to, but it won't hurt),

launch the store.
Your Store will now update most of the apps that are stuck in limbo

3) If some apps are still grayed out. Reinstall them:

4) Run

PowerShell Get-AppxPackage -AllUsers > C:\listofapps.txt
Find the the apps you want to reinstall (Camera and Photos in MY case) in that text file.

Use the PackageFullname of the app to uninstall the apps:

PowerShell Remove-AppxPackage Microsoft.Windows.Photos_15.618.18170.0_x64__8wekyb3d8bbwe
PowerShell Remove-AppxPackage Microsoft.WindowsCamera_5.38.3003.0_x64__8wekyb3d8bbwe

5) Now install them again. This time use the app's "Name" not the "PackageFullname".
the Photos:
PowerShell -ExecutionPolicy Unrestricted -Command "& {$manifest = (Get-AppxPackage Microsoft.Windows.Photos).InstallLocation + '\AppxManifest.xml' ; Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register $manifest}"

and the Camera

PowerShell -ExecutionPolicy Unrestricted -Command "& {$manifest = (Get-AppxPackage Microsoft.WindowsCamera).InstallLocation + '\AppxManifest.xml' ; Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register $manifest}"

Voila!

There probably is a DVD player, but because Dell computers are so buyer-configurable, go here and click the "Detect my PC" button:  https://www.dell.com/support/home/us/en/04/product-support/product/inspiron-14-3473-laptop/configuration

If you have an optical drive (CD/DVD), it should be on right side of the laptop.  If it's capable of playing DVDs, there normally should be a DVD logo printed on it.  Push the rectangular button (#5 in the image below) to open the tray. 

Alternatively, click Start and then click Computer.  If you have an optical drive, it should show under "Devices with removable storage."  If you have an icon for a DVD drive, right-click on the icon and select "Eject" to open the drive.

If the drive doesn't open, by either pressing the button or clicking "Eject," see "Use the emergency eject option (tray-load drives only)" at https://www.dell.com/support/article/us/en/04/sln301118/optical-disk-drive-is-unable-to-eject-the-disc?lang=en The paper clip goes into the small hole shown as #6 in the first image above.

canadian tech say: The answer is NO.  Most laptops today do not come with a DVD player.  Problem easily solved.  You can buy a portable DVD player for about $30.  Make certain the one you buy does not have a separate power supply.  In other words, it gets its power from the USB port.  They are light and small and work just fine.
