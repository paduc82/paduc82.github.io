a. user say:

You probably know this, but you don't defragment a SSD.

For an HDD, 5% fragmentation is superb. You don't need less. Zero percent fragmentation is not necessary or possible for more than a split second, since your HDD will immediately begin fragmenting after you are done.

Technology improves very quickly - quicker than people can keep up with it. HDDs today don't need defragmenting beyond what Windows does on its own. With the single act of typing your original post, you will have spent more time than manually defragmenting will save over the life of your HDD.
