james vinyard say:

You can look at the Toshiba DynaDock.  Has a built in ATI video card as well.  Not expensive and designed to work with any computer.

The only other option for something like that would be an Expansion Chassis but finding one that can connect via USB is extremely hard to find.  These chassis are nothing more than a motherboard with up to around 13 card slots of both PCI and PCIe.

The expansion chassis goes for around $3,500 all by itself.  The DynaDock is around $150 or less depending on which model you choose.

As for "gaming chip", there is no gaming chip.  That is the CPU, RAM memory configuration (over clocked), and motherboard chipset combination.  Primarily, it is the CPU and RAM designed for optimum performance.

The Pro 3 (i7 model) has what is needed but that Intel graphics has got to go.  That is where the DynaDock would come in.

There is a wireless version of those where no cables are attached to the computer.  Just get within range and everything comes alive.

Take a look at one of those.  You can get them now and no changes are made to the tablet except a driver/software suite for the docking station.

james vinyard say:

You will always get a slight impact when deploying more devices from the same controller.  USB for instance can handle up to 127 connections - but - like a router, the more you connect, the less bandwidth you will have.

The DynaDock is only one example of docking stations available but the most popular.  I set one up for one guy who had a Toshiba laptop and it was USB connected but the ATI video shorted out and took out the laptop along with it.  Toshiba, no questions asked, replaced both the DynaDock and the laptop.  So, you might have great customer service if there is a malfunction.

Now, they do have a WiFi version where you do not use a USB connection.  You install the software suite and it will configure itself to the network and then control the docking station in that fashion.  All you have to do is be within range of the docking station and it connects and you have video, etc.

The Surface Pro docking station has no video card in it.  Most docking stations don't (the DynaDock does).  The Pro docking station has what is called a 'pass thru port' and uses the existing video supplied by the tablet.

I would say to shop around for "docking stations" on the web and look at what is out there.  There could be others far better than the DynaDock.  Also, check out any reviews you can find on those because there are lemons along with the good ones.
