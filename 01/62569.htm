I thought you had a straightforward single partition Windows Vista installation, but you seem to add additional information after each reply, e.g. Linux.

When you Shrink a partition, contiguous, unallocated space appears to the right of the shrunken partition in the Disk Management graphical layout. It can only decrease the partition size down to the first unmovable/locked system file. Running a defragmentation (not on an SSD) may move some of the ‘unmovable’ files, and, if you’re running System Restore or Hibernation, you could temporarily turn them off. If you still cannot shrink the partition sufficiently, you can disable the Page file. Don’t forget to turn these back on after shrinking the volume.
