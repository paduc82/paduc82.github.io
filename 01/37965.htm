5. Click OK, and then click Restart.

bossdweeb say:

The suggestions in the first two posts were all good -

Memory is often the issue with BSOD crashes. It can just be some dust got into the slot, and this can cause frequent, and random crashes, but so will a weak psu.

It is possible the battery is not  not able to deliver enough Amps, or the psu has lost it's capacity, and still show enough Watts.

 BSOD crashes are most often hardware related. While BIOS can become corrupt, I don't think you have to worry about the BIOS being bothered in this instance.

None of the games you mention are incompatible with Win 7 (well, I can't vouch for Prototype). The minimum spec for these games is just that - minimum - then turn the settings down a bit further because you are on a laptop. Laptops  have a clocked down gpu (to save power and reduce heat), and heat dissipation problems, so PC level graphic settings should not be expected.

The HDD should not suffer horribly either. They make 'em stronger these days. Avoiding moving a running laptop would be more important.

EDIT: If your machine has 'on board' graphics you will not get good performance in gaming. On board gpu's share RAM with the system. The 2Gb is really minimum with newer games, and inadequate when shared with graphics, and remember that Win7 uses lots more RAM than XP. Putting in more RAM will help, but it is still a gpu on a motherboard. Most graphic cards these days are almost half the size of a MB. Then there is the fan and heat sink. Dedicated graphics processing.

With on board gpu turn the graphics settings all the way down, then try tweaking them up until you get problems and tweak back again.
