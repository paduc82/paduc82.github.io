medium bloke say:

To make sure the Kinect doesn't fall off the TV, I would probably put it somewhere closer to the ground, such as on a table or other surface elevated off the ground, but under the TV. Then, the Kinect will be able to see you when you face the TV, without risking placing it on top of a thin TV.

The support article says:

  • Place the sensor near the edge of a flat, stable surface.
  • Position the sensor between 0.6 m (2 feet) and 1.8 m (6 feet) from the floor, the higher the better. Ideally, the sensor should be within 15 cm (6 inches) above your TV.
  • Avoid positioning the sensor in direct sunlight or within 0.3 metres (1 foot) of audio speakers.
  • Place your Kinect as close to the edge of the surface it is sitting on as you are comfortable with, and make sure that no other nearby objects are obstructing the field of view.
  • Do not place your sensor directly on top of the console.
  • Be careful not to drop the sensor.

This issue may occur because of a race condition in the Usbccgp component.

Method 1:Disconnect all the external devices (printers, scanner, USB  (universal serial bus) drives, etc...) One by one (Except mouse and the keyboard), shutdown the computer and the try to figure out the device causing this issue

Install Windows Update

b)      Locate the UsbccgpCapabilities registry entry in the following registry subkey:

c)       HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Enum\USB\<VID&PID>\<InstanceID>\Device Parameters
