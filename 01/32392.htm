The Actiontec appears to be a gateway (modem + router). Thus, your Xbox is behind 2 routers (the Actiontec and the Netgear), which creates a classic double NAT problem. There are a few workarounds I have described in other posts here (search).  One of those options is to disable the router on the Actiontec by placing it in "bridge mode" as detailed here:

You would then do all port forwarding on the Netgear. Or, if the Netgear has a good, working UPnP implementation (not always the case with consumer level home routers), you should just be able to enable UPnP and let the Xbox use it to forward ports, rather that doing so manually.

A. Rollback the Driver
