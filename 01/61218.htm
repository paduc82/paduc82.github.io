aldwin pogi say:

Hello Chewie,

To assist you with your concern, RAM or Random Access Memory is the place where application programs and data currently in use are kept so they can be quickly accessed by the device's processor. RAM is much faster to read from and write to compared to other storage, such as a hard disk drive, solid-state drive, or optical drive. Data remains in RAM as long as the computer is running.  When the computer is turned off, RAM loses all of its data. When the computer is turned on again, Windows and other files are once again loaded into RAM, usually from an HDD or SSD.
