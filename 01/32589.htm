frederik long say:

I feel like there may not be any options aside from losing years worth of information and work. 

-> There is something horribly wrong here. From your report I get the impression that your office has been breaking all the rules for years about safe data storage. Important documents must at all times be stored on at least two independent storage media, and management must have ready access to all business data.

Since by your own admission you say that you are not computer-savvy, I recommend this course of action:

  1. Take the machine to a reputable computer service business.
  2. Ask them to create a snapshot of the hard disk and store the snapshot on a 2.5" disk in a USB case. They can do this without risking anything.
  3. Go back one day later and ask them to open the snapshot on one of their own machines and show you some specific files.
  4. Put the snapshot disk into your briefcase and keep it there. It's your life saver.
  5. Ask them to create two local admin accounts on the problem PC: One for you, one as a spare (to be used in case things go wrong in future with your own account).
  6. Ask them to move the former user's files & folders into your own profile folder.
  7. Implement a robust backup process.

The exercise might cost you $200 but this would be money well spent in view of the data you stand to lose. There are, of course, alternative methods that you could use by yourself but they require a reasonably strong understanding of maintenance techniques.
