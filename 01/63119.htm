    steve winograd say: Windows 7 Ultimate has BitLocker Drive Encryption built in.  Its default encryption method is AES 128-bit with Diffuser.  You can configure other encryption methods -- see How to change BitLocker Drive Encryption method and cipher strength in Windows 7 for details.

    markparris say:

    Windows 7 Enterprise and Windows 7 Ultimate has Bitlocker encryption included.  Windows 7 Enterprise is only available through Volume Licensing.

    To take full advantage of the inbuilt encryption capabilities the desktops should have an TPM module installed, else a USB device will be needed to store the bitlocker key. A Trusted Computing Group (TCG)-compliant BIOS for use with BitLocker on operating system drives and the BIOS setting to start up first from the hard drive, not the USB or CD drives.

    BitLocker supports two levels of cipher strength for BitLocker: AES 128-bit and 256-bit

    I would therefore say that Bitlocker meets your requirements, providing the hardware supports Bitlocker functionality. If you use Active Directory, you can also store your recovery keys in the Active Directory, which leads on to the topic of managability and support - but thats a whole different kettle of fish.
