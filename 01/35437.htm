marriagedr say:

**PROBLEM SOLVED**

I have had this problem off and on ever since the Windows 10 update but I think I have solved it. The problem is not with the Surface 3, nor is it with the charging brick. The problem is with the USB cord. Most USB cords don't carry enough current for the power demands of the Surface 3. Also, USB cords will lose efficiency the longer they get.

The solution is to purchase a thicker gauge USB cord (20AWG) that is not too long. I have 6-foot versions that I use with the supplied OEM charging brick and the tablet charges just fine while in use. If you can't find one, some 22 or 24 AWG cords may work if they are very short (like 6 inches).

Here is a link to the cords I am using: https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B018QNC4R4/ref=oh_aui_detailpage_o00_s00?ie=UTF8&psc=1

I got a six pack of them for $12.

james vinyard say:

For all information regarding OpenGL, go here: https://www.opengl.org/

There are download links but a very big word of caution, OpenGL was designed for Linux systems and made available to Windows couple of years ago.  CPU makers have a license for OpenGL to include with their video drivers and thus is included with them.

I have a serious recommendation of leaving it alone.  There are video cards for desktop PCs that do have newer versions of OpenGL included with them (mostly NVIDIA and ATI) and chances are, the software you want to use is designed for those.

In Linux systems, OpenGL is up to version 4.4 with 4.5 coming out soon.  Shows you that Windows systems are a bit "dated"!
