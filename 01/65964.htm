Oddly enough, one of the first places to start is at the websites of the Web hosting companies.   There are hundreds of these...  

For a fee, they will register your own "domain name" (the thing that you type in the address bar of the browser).   So, let's say your web site will be on cooking.   You'd use their service to pick a vacant domain name (like "MyBestRecipes.com")â€¦ but beware, a lot of the good domain names are already taken.  Once you've picked a domain name, they will register that domain name for you so that others will be able to find your website.

Hosting companies typically have choices on what kind of "technology" is used behind the scenes of their web sites.   The two major choices are the "windows platform" or the "Linux platform".   If you're not a developer, then you can safely ignore this choice...  as your web site will probably not be "talking to the platform" at all.

Next, comes how much "hand holding" they will do for you with regard to creating your web content.   I am currently using a "no frills" web hosting company that doesn't do any hand-holding at all.... they just say "OK, here is your empty web site, you do the rest".   Learning HTML5 and CSS3 is not hard... but it is a serious commitment.   So, unless you plan to write all of the HTML code yourself, then you might consider a service that provides more.   For example, a some of web hosting providers use a product called "wordpress" or "easy website builder" as an alternative to writing your own HTML.

The typical fees for hosting a small web site in a "shared web server" environment is actually very cheap... typically less than $5 a month.

Let us know if you'd like more information....

¡firedog say:

You forgot to mention your program version. I think this only happens in WLMail 2011.

(1)  This weird behaviour seems to occur only with messages whose subject contains an odd number of full stops/periods (CHR46, %2E). When dragging a message out to the desktop, the file will be renamed with the message's subject. It's as if the idea has been to avoid a double full stop/period before the eml extension by converting one of them to an underscore character, but it's the wrong one that gets converted.

(2)  You can avoid it by pressing F12; the default Save as will be .eml, all full stops/periods in the subject will be converted to underscores and the last save location (e.g. Desktop) will be remembered, so in fact it's easier than dragging out.

Windows Live Mail has its own support forums at the Windows Live Solution Center. Please direct any further questions you may have to one of the forums on the right-hand side of this page. You will probably be asked to give your versions of WLMail and Windows so that the answer can be more precise.

To find your program version, in WLMail press Alt-H A. If this doesn't show you the version and build number, press Escape twice, then Alt-F B.
