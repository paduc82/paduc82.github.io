First of all, I doubt that Andrew E will be coming back to this thread, his posts could best be described as minimalist one-offs.

Most modern (flat) screens come with an "Auto" button that, when depressed will cause the screen to auto-detect what your PCs display settings are and adjust to display the whole desktop. If it is not labelled "Auto" (many are) then you will have to consult your screen's manual.

If you have a bulky, older crt monitor then there will be buttons that allow you to move the image around, in your case you need to move it up. You may also need to resize and move left/right too, for which there will also be buttons.

Hopefully you can get to see the mouse pointer if you move it up to a visible part of the desktop. If you do, then move the pointer as far to the top left as you can without resting it on an icon, right click on the mouse, then click Properties. When the Display Properties window appears, hopefully it will all be visible, click the Settings tab and move the Screen Resolution slider all the way to the right if you have a flat screen. If you have a crt screen try moving the slider to 1024 by 768 pixels (or failing that 800 by 600). Click OK and if you see a prompt asking you if you want to keep the new settings click Yes.

If the above does not get you working, it may be that your screen and graphics card are not compatible until such times as you install the drivers for the graphics card. Without identifying the brand and model of the graphics card, assisting you to locate the appropriate drivers will not be possible. Did your graphics card come with a CD? If it did then the drivers will be on this. Insert it and it will hopefully automatically run a program that you can see that will enable you to get the graphics drivers installed. Then, following a reboot, your desktop should be fully visible. If you have no CD, finding out the brand and model of your graphics card might prove troublesome without being able to see the full desktop but we can give it a go.

Even if the initial steps do get your entire desktop visible, you might still need to install graphics drivers, so post back here (with feedback on the above) if you need assistance with this.

If a large number of folders have saved views that are not to your liking, you may want to first use the Reset Folders option on the View tab of the Folder Options dialog.
