Hi Philip

You cannot use a Windows 10 licence key to activate Windows 7

Check the casing on that PC, is there a Windows 7 sticker from a previous Installation?

If that PC was originally Windows 10, then most likely device drivers would not be available for Windows 7, which would mean reverting that system to Windows 7 would not be viable . . .

If there is a Windows 7 sticker on the casing of that PC:

Here is how to revert to Windows 7 . . .

The Windows 7 product key should be o a Sticker on the bottom casing of your PC/Laptop If it is a laptop, or it might be under the battery - you will need it!

Backup all your files

Click this link to download the Windows 7 ISO file
https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/software-downlo...

Click this link to download a small Microsoft utility (Windows USB/DVD Download Tool) to create bootable Installation Media from the ISO

Create the bootable Installation media on a DVD or USB Flash Drive

Insert the bootable media into the PC

Start your PC, on the manufacturers logo screen press the appropriate button to get into BIOS Setup (Usually F2 or Del key)

Move the drive with the bootable media to the to of the Boot order list

Press F10 to save settings and restart

Windows 7 will begin installing

When your system reboots, go into BIOS and set your hard drive as top of the Boot Order

Do not remove the Installation media until you reach the Desktop

That's It!

If that does not work, a sequential power cycle is the next step in clearing connection problems, and the order and timing of the steps is important to success:

 

1) turn off the Xbox and unplug the power supply cord from the back of the Xbox then

2) unplug power from the router (Some folks have a combination modem-router AKA 'gateway' or only use a modem with no router. If either, skip steps 3 & 6).

3) unplug power from the modem.

4) Wait at least 5 minutes then replug the modem/gateway power.

5) Wait 'til the light on the front of the modem/gateway which says BROADBAND, ONLINE, READY, CABLE or DSL is on solid (not flashing), then plug in the router. If you have a gateway instead of a modem and router or a modem with no router, then when that light says it's ready, skip to step 7.

6) Wait until the WiFi light on the router is on; you may not use WiFi, but it's still a good indicator your router is finished booting up.

7) Plug in and turn on the Xbox.

 To get the exact syntax, just type in XCOPY /? and it'll respond with it.  Here's what I believe will work though:

 xcopy c: d:  /s /c /f

 The /s switch tells it to copy subdirectories.  Bear in mind that this is going to take a good while to run because of the volume of data (approx 20 GB if I read right) involved.  The /c switch says continue copying even if an error occurs.  The /f switch says display the folder and file name as it copies.  Note that I haven't tested this command, so if it breaks, all I guarantee if it breaks is that you get to keep all the pieces.  :-)

 

 Good luck.
