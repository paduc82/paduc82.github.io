To set the maximum amount of time that Windows Remote Assistance invitations can remain open, , follow these steps on the computer that you want someone to connect to:

a.     Click on Start button, and then click Control Panel.

b.     Click System and Maintenance, and then click System.

e.     Click Advanced, and then, under Invitations, select the time from the two lists.

tom oviedo say:

I got around this a little bit. Here's what I'm doing...

 

I have a portable HD (250 GB) that I do a full backup on via USB, including system image, once a month. Then use the NAS drive (1 TB) for daily backups, *NOT* including the system image.

 

 It works a lot better (no crashes or mystery errors), faster, takes up less space and its not creating new backup sets nearly as often. I've been doing this about 3 months now and I'm getting a full, new set just once a month right after I backup to the portable drive.

 

Then, if I get a crash, I can restore from the portable drive frist. Its much faster than over the network as well. Then just update any new or changed files via the network backup.

 

Now, just to clarify, you have to change the backup settings to do the local, USB drive backup, then change again once that's done to get it on the network.
