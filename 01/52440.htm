Just to make sure that things are working properly otherwise, disconnect the projector from the computer and start the presentation that your friend wants to show.  It should display on the computer's monitor.  Do the slides advance when you click the left mouse button?

I understand that the User's Guide for your projector says

If necessary, configure your computer to work with an external USB mouse. See your computer
documentation for details.

but I have never seen any system that requires special configuration to use an external USB mouse.  All the ones that I've seen just work as soon as they are installed.  If your remote doesn't function as a wireless mouse, chances are that it never properly installed.

What, if anything, happens when you connect the projector to the computer and turn on the projector?  Do you hear any sound or see a brief pop-up message?  Do you recall if you heard or saw anything the first time you connected to projector to the computer?

If there are no warning icons, do the following (this is a bit of guesswork on my part, because I don't have a projector like yours):

  • Click on the triangle to the left of "Human Interface Devices" to expand the category.  Report the complete names of each item in this category
  • Click on the triangle to the left of "Mice and other pointing devices" to expand the category.  Report the complete names of each item in this category
