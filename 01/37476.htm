Junk e-mail (or Junk email) folder is native to Windows Live Mail. Unless you take the necessary steps to inform the accounts that these two folders have the same function, you'll end up with two folders whose function is indeterminate. 

Deleted or the one called Deleted items?

I might well have been the one who questioned your use of IMAP for an Outlook.com account in Windows Live Mail and suggested that DeltaSync was better. IMAP was only introduced because several clients, notably those in iPhone and iPad, will only work with IMAP and not POP3. DeltaSync is at least an order of magnitude more efficient and effective than IMAP in Windows Live Mail.

To correct the errors caused by the mis-configured IMAP account,

1.   In Windows Live Mail, right-click on the IMAP account name in the folder pane and select Properties. On the General tab, deselect Include this account when receiving mail or synchronizing

Junk email) should not be there, so if there are any messages in them that you want to keep, move them to the equivalent one-word folder. Then delete the two-word folders.

Send/Receive once or twice to restore the proper folder structure. If it isn't, you'll have to remove the account, then add it back again. I would close down the program after removing the account and wait a few minutes before re-launching and pressing Ctrl-Shift-T to start the Add your email accounts wizard.
