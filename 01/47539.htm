Connected TV's and Monitors via HDMI, but Audio Playback device - Microsoft Community

steven_b say:

Connected TV's and Monitors via HDMI, but Audio Playback device does not change from the Surface speakers

Greetings everyone,

We're investigating an issue where the Surface with Windows RT is connected to a TV or monitor through HDMI, but the audio playback device does not change to the TV.

I've performed some testing with several TV's and PC monitors that support HDMI input, and found that not all will automatically change the Audio Playback device (found in the Sound option of Control Panel) to the connected screen.

If you have connected your Surface with Windows RT to the HDMI adapter, and then to a TV or Monitor and did not automatically have the audio playback change to it, here is what I found will get the playback device to change.

1. Insert the HDMI adapter into the Surface.
2. Connect an HDMI cable to the TV.
3. Connect the HDMI cable into the HDMI adapter that is connected to the Surface.
4. Press the Windows key to return to the Start Screen.
5. Tap the Desktop app tile.
6. Press and hold on the desktop to simulate a Right Click.
7. Tap Screen Resolution.
8. Set Multiple Displays to Extend These Displays (If it already is, just click OK and exit the screen, skip steps 9-11)
9. Tap Apply.

bossdweeb say:

I agree 50C @ idle is a bit warm for a 3570. Depending on the ambient air temp and the type of

cooler you are using, and if at stock clocks, which I see you are.

I haven't checked my 3570 on a hot day for a while, but believe it hits about 35-40C at idle on the hottest core,

and 60-65 C under full gaming load when ambient air temps hit 30+C (using a  Coolermaster Hyper 212EVO cooler).

A bit lower in cooler months.

- I reckon 70C is a bit low for overheat warning though. 80C would  be more appropriate with an Intel cpu.

( With my old AMD Phenom 965 it was 65C )

If the history is for your cpu to run at lower temps then it may be developing faults, or it's suffering a common fault with Intel cpu's which is thermal paste Intel uses between the lid and the core has developed a very slight gap.

I also have a 4790 which is notorious for this fault, which makes these, and a few other Intel cpu's run a bit

hotter at idle than desirable. Fortunately this does not seem to affect load temps as much as idle.

- My 4790 still idles below 50C on hot days though. Currently @39C on the hottest core w/ air temp about 20+C

(liquid cooling with the 4790, which is really no better than the Hyper 212EVO that it replaced,  btw ).

Have you also checked your fan speed profile has not changed ?

If you have never set up a fan speed profile now may be the time to do so.

I set my fan speed profile so the minimum speed is as high as the fan will go without being too noisy

when not gaming. For me this is generally in the range of 50+ % depending on the fan/cooler.

I start ramping the speed up @50C because the cpu  stays below that when not gaming.

- It is desirable to keep the processor as cool as possible, but in regard to performance a low temp is really

only important when over-clocking as you can obviously use higher clocks if the temp stays within a safe range,

and I wouldn't agree that it costs less to keep  the cpu cool if it runs cool.

The cpu uses more current at higher clocks, but the temp is not part of that equation.

Higher fan speeds are more about too much noise than higher electricity costs.
