shinmila h say:

Hey ananemone,

Welcome to Microsoft Answers.

Try slowing your burn speed. Some lower brands of DVDs function (write better) at slower speeds, especially at the end during finalizing (98.0-99.0%).

Additionally, check with other DVD brand. If this doesn't help, try burning the DVD by putting the system in clean boot state to determine if a third party application/software is interferring with the buring process.

Clean boot process:

   1. Click Start, type msconfig in the Start Search box, and then press ENTER.
      If you are prompted for an administrator password or for a confirmation, type the password, or click Continue.
   2. On the General tab, click Selective Startup.
   3. Under Selective Startup, click to clear the Load Startup Items check box.
   4. Click the Services tab, click to select the Hide All Microsoft Services check box, and then click Disable All.
   5. Click OK.
   6. When you are prompted, click Restart.

Now that the system is in clean boot state, burn the DVD. If it goes successful, we know for sure that a third party application/startup item/service is conflicting with the burning process. Follow the process of elimination from the article below to figure out the culprit item.

How to troubleshoot a problem by performing a clean boot in Windows Vista or in Windows 7
http://support.microsoft.com/kb/929135

Ensure to return your computer to normal startup mode.

1.   Click start, type msconfig.exe in the Start Search box, and then press ENTER.
  If you are prompted for an administrator password or for confirmation, type your password, or click Continue.
2.       On the General tab, click the Normal Startup option, and then click OK.
3.       When you are prompted to restart the computer, click Restart.

If there's no third party conflict, then it could be the problem with the driver or firmware updates.

Uninstall the driver currently being used by Windows and install the one from the Manufacturer’s website.

To uninstall a driver:

1. Go to start orb and type device manager.
2. Select Device manager form the control panel list.
3. Locate the graphics card device, right click on it and select properties.
4. Go to driver tab and click on the uninstall button.
5. Follow the on screen instruction.
Once this is done, download the latest driver for your graphics card from the manufacturer’s website. You can obtain the manufacturer details from the device manager.

Recommended link for driver update:

Update a driver for hardware that isn't working properly
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Update-a-driver-for-hardware-that-isnt-working-properly

For your DVD firmware updates, please contact the manufacturer for details.

See if you find the information helpful.

http://social.technet.microsoft.com/Forums/en/itprovistaapps/thread/5cb0a40d-223c-4aed-87c4-3c63f400ee81

  • In Device Manager, clicked View->Show Hidden Objects, expanded the DVD/CDROM Devices and uninstalled my DVD drive from the list. It never got detected or installed after a reboot - it's gone now, cannot see it anymore.
