Copy the following into a blank Notepad, save it as FIXCD.REG, and then double click on the file to merge it into the registry. Reboot the computer and the drive should be back.

lemp say:
Unfortunately, this question is too vague to answer.

The problem could be failing or overloaded hardware (router or wireless adapter), the need for a new driver for the wireless adapter, local radio frequency interference (RFI), or something else.

Answering the following might give some clues -- but then again, maybe not.

1.  What version of Windows XP are you using, including service pack (right click My Computer and select Properties if you don't know)?

2.  What is the make and model of your router?  When did you buy it?

3.  What is the make and model of your wireless adapter (if you are using a laptop with a built-in wireless adapter, what is the make and model of your laptop)?  When did you buy the wifi adapter (or laptop)?

4.  Have you ever updated the router's firmware or the wifi adapter's driver?

5.  What are you doing when your connection drops (e.g., streaming videos, downloading large files, checking email)?

6.  How far is the router from the computer and are there any walls or floors between the two?  If there are walls or floors between the computer and the router, what type of construction is the building (e.g., wood frame house, steel framed office building, mud hut, etc.)?

7.  Does the problem occur when other electrical appliances are in use, particularly microwave ovens and cordless telephones (not cell/mobile phones)?
