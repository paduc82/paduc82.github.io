    Some devices may lose touch capabilities after waking from sleep while - Microsoft Community

    gerald vit say:

    Some devices may lose touch capabilities after waking from sleep while on battery power.

    After installing the Windows 10 Fall Creators Update (version 1709), a small number of PCs may have touch screens and/or touchpads that no longer work correctly when waking from sleep while on battery power.  Some peripheral devices, including mice and keyboards, may also stop working in the same scenario on some devices.
    ralph san say:
    Microsoft has received feedback that a small number of devices are experiencing this issue, and we are working with device partners to resolve it. We are targeting to have a solution released as part of the next monthly Quality Update, coming on November 14th.

    As a result of this feedback and in order to prevent further devices from experiencing this issue, we are now blocking only the specific few devices that would encounter this exact issue from being offered the Windows 10 Fall Creators Update (version 1709) through Windows Update. When the solution is released, we will update this forum post and remove the compatibility block.

    If you have already updated to Windows 10 version 1709 and are experiencing this issue, you can temporarily resolve it by rebooting the device, or by plugging in the AC adapter or connecting the PC to an external monitor, then clicking the power icon and selecting Sleep, and then waking the system.  However, the issue will return if the system wakes from battery power.

    If the temporary solution is not a good option for you between now and when the solution is available, it is recommended to roll back to Windows 10 Creators Update (version 1703) until the issue is resolved. To go back to the previous version of Windows, go to Settings > Update & security > Recovery > Go back to your previous version of Windows. (This option will not appear if more than 10 days have elapsed since you installed the Fall Creators Update, or if other conditions apply that prevent going back to the previous version. If it no longer available, as a last resort you can choose Settings > Update & security > Reset this PC.)  When the solution to this issue is available the Fall Creators Update will again be offered to your device.

    davem121 say:
    Hi Rick

    1.
    How to disable the Lockscreen:

    Click your Start Button, type regedit and hit Enter to open the Registry Editor

    In the left pane, navigate to:

    HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Policies\Microsoft\Windows

    Right click the Windows Key (folder) and choose New - Key

    Name the key Personalization

    Click the new Personalization key

    In the right hand pane, right click and choose New - DWORD 32bit

    Name it NoLockScreen

    Double click NoLockScreen and set its value to 1

    Close the Registry Editor and Restart Windows for the change to take effect



    2.
    Remove the need for a password to log into Windows 10:

    Open the Settings App
    Go to Accounts - Sign-in Options
    Choose to sign in with a local account
    (in newer versions of Windows 10, this might be on the Your Info Settings Page)
    Enter your old password and leave the new password box blank
    Close the Settings App
