winston m say: Hi,
 
1. What happens when you tried to download pictures from the camera?

2. Do you get any error message?
3. How do you connect the device to the computer? Is it through USB?
 
We have checked and verified that the Canon PowerShot SD550 camera is compatible with Windows7.
Windows 7 Compatibility Center
http://www.microsoft.com/windows/compatibility/windows-7/en-us/Search.aspx?l=en-us&type=Hardware&s=Canon%20PowerShot%20SD550%20&os=32-bit

Method 1:

Step 1: I would suggest you to connect the device to a different computer and check the issue.
 
Step 2: Try connecting to a different USB port and check with the issue.
 
 
Method 2: Run the hardware troubleshooter
Open the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Open-the-Hardware-and-Devices-troubleshooter
 
Method 3:
 
If you are have connected the device using USB to the computer. Ensure that you follow these steps to download pictures from Canon device.
a. Turn the Canon camera on and set it to playback mode by pressing the button with the "play" triangle on it.
b. Plug the smaller end of the USB cable into the camera and plug the other end into an open USB port on your computer.
c. Click the "Start" button in the lower left corner of your desktop and click "My Computer." Double-click on the Canon's icon in the left pane of the "My Computer" window. If another folder exists inside the Canon's folder, open that as well until you reach the image files.
d. Select the files to transfer to your computer. Hold "Shift" to click on two separate files and it will select all the files in between the two. Alternatively, hold "Ctrl" to select multiple files that aren't adjacent.
e. Click "Get Pictures from Camera" if Windows recognized the Canon as a camera to launch the "Scanner and Camera Wizard," which will walk you through downloading your photos to your computer and deleting them from your camera. Click "Copy Selected Files" if "Get Pictures" is not an option, which will open a dialogue box where you should select the location to which you want to download your photos.
 
Let us know the results.
 
Hope this helps.

OK, I'm going to answer my own posing here but I will address only the driver issue, the slow Windows Update issue is still worth leaving on the table.

The driver problem is solved by using a driver backup utility like Double Driver to extract a (backup) copy of the driver from the computer which had a working copy installed on it.  It's a simple matter to run the program, select the driver you want to back up and create a portable copy.  That copy can be installed on the next computer in the normal manner.
