      The currently loaded USB driver has become unstable or corrupt

      Your PC requires an update for issues that may conflict with a USB external hard drive and Windows

      Windows may be missing other important updates hardware or software issues

      Your USB controllers may have become unstable or corrupt

      Your external drive may be entering selective suspend

      Your PC motherboard may need updated drivers.

     

    Do you see any exclamation mark in device manager or do you see any pop-up message on the right bottom side of your taskbar when you connect your USB drives?

     

    To resolve these issues, follow the steps listed in the methods below in the order listed. If the first method does not fix the problem, proceed to the next.

    Method 1: Uninstall and then reconnect the external hard drive

    This method resolves issues where the currently loaded USB driver has become unstable or corrupt.

    a.     Press Windows key + X.

    b.      Select Device Manager.

    c.     Click Disk Drives from the list of hardware

    d.     Right-click the USB external hard drive with the issue, and click Uninstall.

    e.     After the hard drive is uninstalled, unplug the USB cable.

    f.      Wait for 1 minute and then reconnect the USB cable. The driver should automatically load

    g.     Check for the USB drive in Windows Explorer

    Note: Connecting your USB external hard drive into a non-powered USB hub can cause a lack of enough power to operate the external drive. Instead, plug it directly into your computer.

    If this method does not resolve your issue, proceed to Method 2.

    Method 2:

    Install the latest Windows Updates

    This method will install the latest device drivers for your USB external hard drive.

    a.     Press Windows key + X. Select Control Panel.

    b.     Click on Windows Update.

    c.     Click Check for Updates. After the scan is complete, click Review optional updates.

    d.     Click the check box next to the updates, then click Install updates.

    e.     If prompted, review the license agreement, then click I Accept.

    f.      Follow the onscreen instructions to download and install the updates.

    g.     If prompted, reboot your computer.

     

    If your problem still exits, proceed to Method 3.

    Method 3:

    Reinstall USB controllers.

    This method resolves steps where the currently loaded USB driver has become unstable or corrupted.

    a. Press Windows key + X.

    b. Select Device Manager.

    c.  Expand Universal Serial Bus controllers. Right click a device and click Uninstall. Repeat for each device.

    d. Once complete, restart your computer. Your USB controllers will automatically install.

     

    If your problem still exists, proceed to Method 4.
    Method 4: Disable USB selective suspend setting.

    This method prevents your USB external drive from powering down.

    a.     Press Windows key + X.

    b.     Select Power Options.

    c.     Under Choose or customize a power plan, click on change plan settings.

    d.     Then click on Choose a power plan.

    e.     Next to your currently selected plan, click Change Plan Settings.

    f.      Click Change advanced power settings.

    g.     Click the box to expand USB Settings, then expand USB selective suspend settings.

    h.     Click the drop down menu and select disabled.

    i.      If you're using a laptop, click Battery, then click the drop down menu and select disabled.

    j.      Click Apply, then click OK.

     

    Please let us know if the issue persists. We will be happy to assist you further.
