¡firedog say:
it is sent in the sent folder but never arrives at the NC.RR.COM email address.


This would indicate that the message is being blocked at one of the RR servers. If it's in the Sent items folder, it's been accepted for onward transmission by the message submission server. You could examine the message source for clues (select it in the message list and press Ctrl-F3).

First, is there anything injected by security software? Some programs insist on inserting a footer like This message has been scanned by SuperAV and found to be clean, and the injection process itself or the content of the footer may create something that server spam filters don't like.

The same applies to some (HTML) signatures, whose formatting can upset some servers. Can you send a simple one-line plain-text message with no signature to yourself?

It's not inconceivable that your IP address is on a blacklist, which would prevent delivery by some mail servers. I would have expected the RR techs to have checked this, but you can't be too sure. Here's a quick and easy test page: Blacklist Check. It will detect your IP address, and then check it against a number of blacklists when you click Check blacklists.

Just to rule out the really trivial case, please ensure that you haven't blocked your own address or domain. Press Alt-F O S for Safety options, then look at the list on the Blocked senders tab.
