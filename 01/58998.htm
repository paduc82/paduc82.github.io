Hi Lenny, to remove the need for a password in Windows 10:

Open the Settings App
Go to Accounts - Sign-in Options
Choose to sign in with a local account
(in newer versions of Windows 10, this might be on the Your Info Settings Page)

Close the Settings App

Click OK

That's It!

There is no deregistration process so you can just reinstall any Microsoft product which is not an OEM license as you require. Microsoft do monitor the use of a license and can block excessive use altogether.

This is basically what I got to work after a couple of days of trial and error...


1) Unhook the printer from the computer

2) "REMOVE" the printer in Devices and Printers (also the fax if your printer has one)

3) Stop the Print Spooler

4) uninstall the software\drivers

5)  After Reboot, Stop the Print Spooler again.

6) Reinstall Software\drivers (use Compatibility Mode if necessary)...

    Hook up the printer when instructed by the installer.

7) Make sure Bidirectional Support is Enabled in Printer Properties... PORTS tab

8) Enable Bidirectional Support and reboot

9) Hook the printer back up, and it should work


Good Luck...

I have two laptops with the new up to date software and this happens on both of them. It has become very annoying when trying to use my computer.

Many thanks,

robert aldwinckle on forums say:
Whenever I use my mouse to move to the right my computer automatically switches applications such as if I was on the internet and  moved my mouse to the right it would open a previous application and switch to that instead.

This suggests that you actually have a Touchpad with Touch screen emulation features enabled.  If you had a real mouse those symptoms would not happen.  E.g. a real mouse can only access the Modern UI at the absolute corners of the screen.  


A Touch screen user could "swipe" from the left (so be moving right) to draw in a previous app.  That would mean that the App switcher was enabled and its switch automatically option was enabled.  You could turn off both options to see if you like that any better.  Note that when you turn off both, in particular disable the App switcher, you also disable the Win-Tab keyboard command as well, although you could still use Alt-Tab or Task Manager to see all your open apps.


Alternatively, depending on your brand of Touch pad and the level of its software you may be able to disable most Touch screen emulation features to get your Touch pad back to emulating only a normal mouse.  Or just become aware of the Touch screen emulation features and how to control them in order to avoid the problem symptoms you are now having with them.  E.g. instead of completely disabling them you may find that your manufacturer's implementation would allow you to tweak the settings until you were more comfortable using them.

 

FYI
