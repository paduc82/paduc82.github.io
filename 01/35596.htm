    What does the Disk column on the task manager represent?

    What do you think may address this issue? Can upgrading my RAM address it?


    Thank you in advance. God bless

    andre da costa say:

    The Disk column provides analysis of your storage input/output performance. Depending on what applications are running and background activities, you might see constant spikes of 100%, this can include antivirus doing a background scan on files, applications you have running might be writing to disk, live video running in the browser, updates downloading, music playing and other activities.

    You can never have too much RAM, but if this not affecting usability or general performance of the system, I would ignore it.
