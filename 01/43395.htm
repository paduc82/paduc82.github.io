Here's what I learned:

It comes down to digitizer technology, of which there are only a few types on the market. The consumer tablet market is moving towards newer technologies (Microsoft N-Trig, Wacom AES) that have tested out (so far) as having worse jitter than older technology such as Wacom EMS. Also, Microsoft and Wacom seem to have some sort of new compatibility deal which I'm guessing would involve the N-Trig and AES technologies, so I'm not expecting to see this get better in Surface 5 unless they work some magic in software. The older Wacom EMS technology (SP1, SP2, Galaxy Notes, Cintique, etc.) is pretty good in regards to jitter, but is all but gone from new tablets because of the cost, weight, and thickness added by the digitizer layer. The newer technologies are cheaper to integrate into products.

Hi,

4K gaming only applies to Xbox One X.

With regards to 4K for Xbox One X, if a game has been enhanced for 4K, then this is normally included on the disc for current games or as an additional update.  Once the update is done, and the game is running, it won't be using more data bandwidth when you play online.  That's not how it works.  It's not the same as streaming a 4K Netflix video.

For example, you buy Battlefield V on disc.  You insert the disc to install the game.  You will be prompted for an update.  Once you complete this update and play online, you won't be using more bandwidth than other players on original Xbox One or the One S, you'll use exactly the same as they do.  Your 4K enhancement is only about having better graphics and performance, you're not 'streaming' in 4K.

If for example you bought the single player only game like Fallout 4 on disc for the One X, during the install you'd be prompted for the enhancement update.  Once you have the update, that's it.  Unless you buy additional DLC, you won't be using any more

Streaming from sites like Netflix would work differently.  Regardless of your settings, if you watch a film on Netflix that is only Full HD, then it will use less data then a 4K Movie or Episode.

You can adjust the stream quality in the settings within the app.

Note that to get 4K on Netflix you have to pay a higher subscription fee anyway, otherwise the highest possible stream quality would be 1080P.
