I'm trying to open the Microsoft Store app on Windows 10, but whenever I open it, it keeps on hanging, how can I fix this problem?

Here is the solution that worked for me after trying all the others (Wsreset, troubleshooter, PowerShell scripts...).

The problem came from a corruption of permissions on the WindowsApp folder.

Here are the steps to follow:

1. Navigate to C:\Program Files and right-click on the "WindowsApp" folder then "Properties"
2. Click on the "Security" tab then "Advanced" at the bottom
3. Above, in front of "Owner", you probably see "Trusted Installer". Click on "Change".
4. A new "Select User or Group" window opens, click on "Advanced", then on "Find Now".
5. In Search results, double click on your username of your account (on which you are logged in) and then click again on "OK"
6. Before proceeding, be sure that the "Replace owner on subcontainers and objects" box is NOT ticked!
7. Click on "Apply" and wait for the operation to end
8. Still in the same window, check this time the box mentioned in step 6, click on "Apply", wait, then click on OK and close the windows
10. Make sure all windows are closed, and launch the command prompt as administrator (search for "cmd" and click on "launch as administrator")
11. Type the following line, and press Enter :
icacls "C:\Program Files\WindowsApps" /reset /t /c /q
12. Be patient; it may take a few minutes. At the end, you should see the number of modified files (and the number of failures should be 0)
13. Try launching the Windows Store. I hope it helped!

I don't know if this fix is permanent, but it at least allows you to launch the Microsoft Store again.

The command in step 11 resets the permissions. Steps 1 to 8 are intended to get access rights on WindowsApp, otherwise the command cannot be executed on recent versions of Windows 10.
