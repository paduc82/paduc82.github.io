Hello,

There are ways to view your computer's domain name in Windows 10. You can follow the steps below to view your computer's domain name (If your computer is connected to a domain):

  1. Right-click on the Start button and select System.
  2. Click on the Advance system settings link on the left pane.
  3. On the Advance system settings window, you can see the Domain column with the domain name if you computer is connected to a domain or Workgroup if you computer is connected to a workgroup.

To join an active domain, you need to take note of the following:

  • A User Account on the Domain.
  • Name of Domain.
  • Computer running Windows 10 Pro or Enterprise/Education editions.
  • Domain Controller must be running Windows Server 2003 (functional level or later).

  1. On the Windows 10 PC go to Settings > System > About then click Join a domain.
  2. Enter the Domain name and click Next.
  3. Enter account information which is used to authenticate on the Domain then click OK.
  4. Wait while your computer is authenticated on the Domain.
  5. Click Next.
  6. And then you’ll need to restart your computer to complete the process.
  7. When the sign in screen appears, you will notice the DOMAIN\User account is displayed. Enter your password and you will now be logged onto your Domain.

You will notice that once you are connected to the Domain, the About settings no longer list options that were presented before. This is because your computer is centrally managed by the server.

The folders under C:\Users are called "Profile Folders". They contain a user's files and his settings.

When you are logged on under an admin account then you can open any profile folder with a double-click, your own or someone else's.

The simplest way to delete a profile folder is via the Control Panel / User Accounts. While logged on under an admin account you can delete any user account. You get the option of deleting his data (stored in his user profile folder) too.

You write that you have just one account. This is as risky as having a single house key.
