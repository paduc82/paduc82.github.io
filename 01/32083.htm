zachd123 say:

The sub-options for the Lock Screen Slideshow only show up once you flick the "Play a slide show on the lock screen" to On.  Have you done that yet?

The final name for "Let Windows choose pictures for my slide show" is "Only use the pictures that will fit best on my screen".  That is the final name.  All other references are inaccurate and should be updated: Geethu's statement is in accidental error and should be corrected. =]

The "battery power" option of course does not appear on non-portable machines.  That's expected.  =]

As regards Question 2:

Where is this folder located? 

We (slideshow) base our presentation of images off of the images known to the system shell indexer.  Once the indexer has added the folder(s) and image(s) to its database, slideshow will pick up and display what it Should from that list.  If "Only use pictures that fit well" is checked, then we'll skip files that have to substantially resized in order to fit.  If it's not, we'll use anything that's reasonable.  (I can't remember the exact metrics for Absolutely Too Small, but IIRC it's pretty reasonable.)

Note that if you are one of the people who hate indexer and have it forced off, then the slideshow will be expected to fail and there's no point in trying to run it.  Sorry - it would be grievously inefficient for us to have a secondary indexer just for slideshow, and the associated startup cost would have been ludicrous.  (If you paid close attention, the startup time for slideshow went from many minutes in beta to ~20 seconds (conservatively speaking) for RTM.) 

What file types are the images in question?

Are these files located on OneDrive (the current name of former SkyDrive) or are they local files or are they on a network share?  Network files aren't supported: if you hack them into the system library they would be expected to fail.  Local files should work great.  OneDrive files are intricate to handle, but we should do an admirable job of handling them.  (We worked with a variety of teams to get the updates in to make this happen.)  In the worst case scenario, forcing OneDrive folders to be Available Offline should work out any kinks.  Normally for OneDrive we realize that if the folder is offline we're probably going to be data-starved versus our consumption rate, so we quickly enter into semi panic mode and super optimize everything since the actual thumbnail fetches are typically going to need to come from the cloud (and thus are time-expensive, especially depending upon network state).

Since I don't have much information on your system, I have no idea how much if any of that applies to you, but -- that's a basic information dump on this subject that may or may not be helpful.  There's of course a lot more to this subject, but that's a cursory overview that may or may not align to your situation.  If not, an understanding of your exact situation would help me to know which mechanics might be in play.
