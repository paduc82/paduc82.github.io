hellhog say:

You'll need an amplifier for one. I once connected regular headphones directly to the RCA cables using an RCA to 3.5mm jack adapter. The sound was really quiet. If you want to use simple headphones (as in no mic and not a headset), then a simple FiiO headphone amplifier will work for stereo sound. You just may need an RCA to 3.5mm jack adapter. If you want simulated surround sound, then a Turtle Beach DSS is required. You will use the Digital Optical out to connect the Xbox to the digital processor (amplifier). If you want to be able to use a mic and heaphones, then an Astro Mixamp is required. It will allow you to connect a mic TRS 3.5mm jack and heaphone TRS 3.5mm jack into a TRRS 3.5mm jack that connects into the mixamp. From there the Digital Optical from the Xbox connects to the mixamp, and a 2.5mm cable connects between the xbox controller and mixamp. HDMI already transmits audio, connecting the digital audio out on the xbox to the TV will do nothing. Usually, on the TV that is a digital audio out anyway (so connecting to it will do nothing). We need more information on your headphones though. How does the mic connector work? You're best bet may be to just buy the above pictured accessories and a small $15 heaphone amplifier and connect that way.

Hopefully this information is more useful than my last response:

Temp fix via Command prompt:
right click start button
choose command prompt (admin)
type wpr-cancel and press enter

or

Temp fix via services:
Task Manager> Services> Find DiagTrack > right click and **STOP**

both of these require you to re enter the fix after a restart. It will survive a normal shutdown/restart

Permanent fix:

right click start button
choose command prompt (admin)
type sc stop DiagTrack and press enter
type sc delete DiagTrack and press enter

Then open Task Scheduler; under Task Scheduler Library -> Microsoft delete the following items:

Everything under Application Experience
Everything under Autochk
Everything under Customer Experience Improvement Program
Under Disk Diagnostic delete only the Microsoft-Windows-DiskDiagnosticDataCollector

Of course this caveat ALWAYS applies: Make proper backups before applying.

this fix won't fix all cases of 100% disc usage, just the recent one
