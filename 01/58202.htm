Have you gone to the PC or Device's Support Downloads webpage to see if there are newer drivers than those installed in Device Manager on Driver tab? 

The affected driver may be part of the chipset if this is a motherboard with onboard Display, or it may be a separate Display driver for the Video card or chip. 

You did right to first check WIndows Update but if it's not offering a newer driver I'd install Intel® Driver Update Utility

to see what it recommends to download and install.  If this isn't successful then manually search from the specified chipset drivers on the Intel site to see that they're updated.

11.   Under Model, tap your keyboard model, and then tap Next.

12.   On the Update Driver Warning dialog box, tap Yes.

13.   On the Windows has successfully updated your driver software page, tap Close.

14.   To apply the changes, restart your computer.
