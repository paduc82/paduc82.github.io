The problem is and remains that the fs index is corrupted. The difficulty with this is that the index is a sparse B Tree file, that is constituted of fragments that are scattered around the disk. B Trees are fairly efficient search tools.  I hate them - but the reason that people use them is that they are fairly efficient. Their problem is that they must be kept in balance to retain their efficiency.  Therefore, they must be "re-genned" or "re-balanced" every once in a while, usually as a result of adding or deleting significant numbers of files to the disk, as in adding or updating applications or the OS and utilities.  Re-balancing a B Tree file is a "critical path" process, in which any error can be, and often is fatal.  Windows is not, and never has been a robust OS, and the index file re-balance is just one of many things that go wrong, with catastrophic results.

STOP: 0xC000021A  

Can be a difficult problem to solve and you may indeed need technical help from a real computer shop
(not leeks and peeks at a BestBuy or other BigBox stores) or the System maker's Support.


Cause

This error occurs when a user-mode subsystem, such as WinLogon or the Client Server Run-Time Subsystem (CSRSS), has been fatally compromised and security can no longer be guaranteed. In response, the operating system switches to kernel mode. Microsoft Windows cannot run without WinLogon or CSRSS. Therefore, this is one of the few cases where the failure of a user-mode service can shut down the system.

Mismatched system files can also cause this error. This can occur if you have restored your hard disk from a backup. Some backup programs might skip restoring system files that they determine are in use.

Resolving the Problem

Running the kernel debugger is not useful in this situation because the actual error occurred in a user-mode process.

Resolving an error in a user-mode device driver, system service, or third-party application:Because bug check 0xC000021A occurs in a user-mode process, the most common culprits are third-party applications. If the error occurred after the installation of a new or updated device driver, system service, or third-party application, the new software should be removed or disabled. Contact the manufacturer of the software about a possible update.

If the error occurs during system startup, restart your computer, and press F8 at the character-based menu that displays the operating system choices. At the resulting Windows Advanced Options menu, choose the Last Known Good Configuration option. This option is most effective when only one driver or service is added at a time. If this does not resolve the error, try manually removing the offending software. If the system partition is formatted with file allocation table (FAT), use an MS-DOS startup disk to gain access to the computer's hard disk. If the system partition is formatted with NTFS file system, you might be able to use Safe Mode to rename or delete the faulty software. If the faulty software is used as part of the system startup process in Safe Mode, you need to start the computer using the Recovery Console in order to access the file. If a newly installed piece if hardware is suspected, remove it to see if this resolves the issue.

Try running the Emergency Recovery Disk (ERD) and allow the system to repair any errors that it detects.

Resolving a mismatched system file problem: If you have recently restored your hard disk from a backup, check if there is an updated version of the Backup/Restore program available from the manufacturer. Make sure the latest Windows Service Pack is installed.

tricky300 say:

The DG834G supports encryption and mac address filtering. They are different methods of restricting access to the router. My guess is that, if you have a "rogue" connection from an unknown PC, you do not have either type of restriction in place.

 

The easiest protection to implement is encryption. You can set up wireless encryption, which is essentially a password to your wireless network. If both PCs are relatively modern (certainly Windows 7 should be modern enough), you can use WPA2. This assumes default settings for the Netgear DG834G...

  • Open a web browser such as Internet Explorer or Firefox
  • In the address box remove all text and enter 192.168.0.1 and hit enter
  • You will get a logon dialog asking for user name and password, enter admin and password respectivley and click OK
  • In the setup section (down the left hand side) click Wireless Settings
  • Click to select WPA2-PSK(Wi-Fi Protected Access 2 with Pre-Shared Key)
  • Enter your desired password into Network Key (8 ~ 63 characters)
  • Click Apply

You will have to join your laptop to the wireless network again. If you have an older laptop that does not support WPA2 you can use WPA or WEP instead.

 

You already know where all the Ethernet cables go because you can physically trace them. But you do not necessarily know where all the devices that have wireless connections are situated. Every network device has a unique identifier called a MAC address. You can set up your router to only connect to wireless network devices if their MAC address is in the approved list. To find out the MAC address of your laptop...

  • Click Start
  • If the laptop is Windows XP click Run (if it is Vista or Windows 7 just skip this step)
  • Type cmd and hit enter
  • In the resultant black window type ipconfig /all to display a load of information that includes the MAC address

The MAC address in the output is referred to as Physical address. This is what a section of my output looks like (the important bit is the bold bit)...

 

Connection-specific DNS Suffix . :
Description . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : Intel 21140-Based PCI Fast Ethernet Adapter (Generic)
Physical Address. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : 00-03-FF-3B-BD-5F
Dhcp Enabled . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : Yes
Autoconfiguration Enabled. . . . . . : Yes
IP Address . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : 192.168.131.65
Subnet Mask . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . : 255.255.255.0

 

Your output will list all network devices, make sure that you get the physical addess for the wireless device and not the wired device. Now you can enter this as a specific, allowed MAC address in the DG834G...

  • Power up your laptop and wait for it to connect to the router
  • Open the DG834G's admin pages as before
  • In the setup section (down the left hand side) click Wireless Settings
  • Click the Setup Access List button
  • Click to tick the Turn Access Control On box
  • Your laptop should be appearing in the Available Wireless Stations section, you can check its MAC address to be sure
  • Click the Add button
  • Click Apply

Now only your laptop will be allowed to connect wirelessly to your router.
