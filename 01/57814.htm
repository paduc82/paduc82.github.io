judah pereira say:

Discussion on the use of mouse/touchpad while pressing a key on the keyboard

This question has been asked at least once before, in a way that I thought could be asked better/more clearly. Moreover, I think the person who answered the question either misunderstood the question or tried to give an alternate solution. And I don't think that's bad or anything.

MusaYa-Sin asked on May 2, 2013
Q: how to use touch pad while pressing key
It was answered by Tadasha Mishra

The solution offered involved the use of substituting the pointer motion on the keyboard for the touchpad (the 'numeric touchpad').
The person didn't want to know how to use the mouse by using the keyboard, but rather, while.
This solution certainly provides a different flavor for the use of mouse/touchpad, but it doesn't answer the question. And it's not effective in all cases.

I'm quite sure this is a problem most acutely experienced by persons who play video games, and also whoever uses the keyboard and mouse in other equivalent ways.

So the question could be stated more elaborately like this:

When I hold down any key on the keyboard, and when I simultaneously attempt to operate the touchpad, I cannot get the touchpad to move at all.
This holds true excluding three keys- 'Shift', 'Ctrl', and 'Alt' and other keys like the windows-start key, the 'Fn' key. If I hold down these specific keys and try to move the pointer using the touchpad, it's works just fine. But not so with other keys. Is there any way for me to get the touchpad to work regardless of what key I press simultaneously?

Illustration- When I am trying to move forwards in a third-person or first-person adventure game, I press the 'W' key. And when I want to move the mouse around while I am pressing the W key, I can't. I'm a decent gamer. I don't like being trolled like this (JK).

Now there can be at least two ways of solving this problem, to slightly different effects.
(And the exact method of solution may differ according to the laptop model)

1) Reducing sensitivity of touchpad to "Low sensitivity" in the touchpad settings. The obvious trade-off is that while you can now use the mouse and keyboard simultaneously, the mouse won't be as sensitive (unless one can further modify it while playing a game).
This solution worked for me.

2) Modifying the touchpad driver, or installing some other driver instead of the present one.

I use the Elantech SmartPad on Windows 10 (prolly coz it's a default driver associated with the Windows Updates).

Now, for those who are willing to fiddle with the registry, this solution involves modifying the value of a variable/subroutine called DisableWhenType in the TouchPad driver- Elantech sub-settings - t'was in the SmartPad folder for me

(type regedit in start search,

then 

HKEY_CURRENT_USER, then
Software, then
Elantech, then
SmartPad) (or somewhere in Elantech) -

find (Ctrl+F) a subroutine (a thingie) called "DisableWhenType_AllArea"

For me there were 12 DisableWhenType thingies with the AllArea version right at the top. You only need to focus on the AllArea one.

So right click only on DisableWhenType_AllArea and select the Modify option, then change it [from one] to zero (hexadecimal or decimal, doesn't matter).

And then you have to restart your computer.

And this worked for me, too. And I can use a higher mouse sensitivity now.
