rohn007 say:
There are actually 3 types of "graphics ram"

First and "best" is the RAM on an addon video card.
Second is ram "borrowed" from the CPU
Third is "GDI", a small subset of CPU RAM.

In general, I would GUESS that the message means your addon card does not have enough RAM to support what ever you are doing at the time. That means it has to start working around the shortage, usually by "swapping" pieces if Video RAM out to HD. That slows down processing.

Go to start men
search for "System information
Click on "Components"
click on display

The first entry starting with "Name" will be for the built in video. It will probably be something like "Intel(R) HD Graphics ..."

What does it say for "Adapter RAM"?

Scroll down from there looking for another entry starting with "Name" after a couple of blank links. This will be your addin card, if you have one.

Again, what does it say for "Adapter RAM"?
