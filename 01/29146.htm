Hi.

I would recommend you to check your SSD or Hard Disk HDD with chkdsk.
1.- First, close all the programs and files that you have open.
2.- Click with the right mouse button in the Windows Start menu and select Control Panel. Then choose the category System and security. On the next screen, look down Administrative Tools on the right and click on that option.
3.- One of those tools is the Windows Memory Diagnostic. Double click on it to open it.
4.- The diagnosis gives two options to choose from. See what each one does and click on the one you prefer:

Restart now and check if there are problems. It is best to select in most cases. Make sure to close all programs as I have asked before. The PC will reboot on its own when choosing this option. Right after, the check also starts automatically.
Then check the Hard Disk.
1. Open an elevated Command Prompt (Press the Windows + X button at the same time, then choose Command Prompt (Admin))
2. Type the following command, and replace the X with the drive letter you wish to scan: “CHKDSK X:
3. Hit the enter key, Command Prompt will now start CHKDSK.

Hope this helps. Keep us posted on the status for further assistance.

dorklingshot say:

My computer will not fully turn off.

Windows 10 Fall Creators

a. user say:

Aha! That's exactly what is supposed to happen. I'll explain.

When a computer is powered off it is, for all intents and purposes, off. However, there is a tiny amount of power remaining in the motherboard - it's called flea power.

The flea power is needed by network administrators to wake a sleeping computer or to boot a powered off computer in order to perform maintenance on the computers in the network. Basically, the network administrator sends a special 'signal' through the network which instructs the motherboard to power up a sleeping or powered off computer. The 'flea power' is just enough for the motherboard to respond to the network administrator's signal.

Computer manufacturers obviously have no idea where their computers are going to end up, so this feature is enabled by default. If your computer is not part of a network, it doesn't need to have the feature. But there's no harm to leaving it enabled - nobody can use it to break into your computer - and the technicalities of disabling it are such that if you get it wrong you could do serious damage to your computer, even make it unbootable. So it's best to just leave it be.

And what about that light? It's a small LED on the motherboard and its purpose is to warn you to pull the power plug out of the wall socket before you open the case if you want to do work inside your computer. Otherwise you can get quite a shock.
