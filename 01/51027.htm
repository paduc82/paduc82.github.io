canadian tech say:

Following applies to a desktop computer:

Go to the store and pick up a 2032 button battery.  They are stocked in many stores.  This is very easy

  1. Remove the power plug from your computer
  2. hold the ON button in for 7 seconds
  3. Be very careful of what you touch in the computer
  4. Get a small bladed screw driver one not much bigger than one used for those eyeglass screws.
  5. Pry out the existing battery, it should be easily found
  6. Press the new one in
  7. Start your computer, immediately pressing F2 key about once a second.  That should take you to the BIOS.
  8. Change the date and time to current.
  9. It may take a few moments longer for the boot up because the BIOS will be resetting itself.

You should never have to do this again.
