lemp say:
This was a symptom of a bug allegedly fixed by Windows XP Service Pack 2 (see http://support.microsoft.com/kb/839017). 

However, despite the assurance in that article that "This problem was first corrected in Microsoft Windows XP Service Pack 2," it seems to still exist, at least for some.

Assuming that you have installed at least sp2 (if not sp3), one solution is to either disable the Help service or set it to manual.  Setting a service to manual means that it will only start if called for (e.g., you click Start > Help and Support).  Setting it to disabled, means it will never start (and thus you won't have "Help and Support").

To do either:

  • Open Services (Start > Run > services.msc > OK)
  • Scroll down to Help and Support and double-click it
  • On the General tab, click the "Stop" button to stop the service and then select either "Manual" or "Disabled" in the drop-down box next to "Start up type"
  • OK your way out and close the Services window
