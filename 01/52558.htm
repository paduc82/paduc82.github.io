How To FIX a Corrupted TCP/IP (Or if You Can't use the - Microsoft Community

kimani griffin say:

How To FIX a Corrupted TCP/IP (Or if You Can't use the Internet)

NOTE:

-When my TCP/IP was corrupted I was running Windows 8.1 on a laptop where an Ethernet cable was not practical nor even work.

-I've already tried changing my Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IPv4) to follow a DNS server address I found on the internet, but I would not recommend you to try that.

-I've already tried to resolve this problem with 3rd Party TCP/IP Repair programs which did nothing at all since they required internet access. (*Face Palm)

-I've already tried to repair the TCP/IP Winsock through Command Prompt AND I've ran the reset log parameter

-THIS discussion is  ONLY for users that got no success with the listed above options and could not resolve the issue

*Disclaimer: I am not a certified computer technician. This process simply worked for me and I thought since no one published how to resolve the issue through this process it would be best to share it with the public. Be advised that you take full responsibility for any unsaved data losses during this process.

Hi everyone, if you're here because you can't access your internet but your wireless icon is white and lit up as if your machine is connected, and you're running Windows 8 or 8.1, this discussion is for you.

For this you will have to be in Desktop mode

First thing first, check your Media State: (To see if your TCP/IP is corrupted)

1) Go to Control Panel (Right click on the Start icon on the bottom left corner of your screen and click Control Panel)

2) Click Network and Internet, then click Network and Sharing

3) Under view your active networks you will see the name of the network you're connected to and to the right:  Access Type:

                                                                                                                                                                    HomeGroup:

                                                                                                                                                                    Connections: Wireless Network Name

Next to HomeGroup will say Not Connected.

4) Click on the wireless network that you're connected to located on the right of Connections:

A dialog box titled Wi-Fi Status will appear.

On the right of Media State you should see Not enabled or Not connected.

To verify this click the Diagnose tab at the bottom the dialog box and after following its instructions you should eventually get to a point where Windows will try to repair a network problem, however for me this did not work.


Here's what we're doing:

Basically we have to either re-install Windows 8.1 or 8 OR reset it to Factory settings.

BUT if you have a recovery CD then you're in LUCK! (If so then I'm sure by now you know what to do with it)

IF you were like ME, with a laptop pre-installed with Windows 8 and without a recovery disc THEN KEEP READING.

STEPS:

1) You will need to Back up your data. I found that sadly a Windows image File or the backup Windows does for you created an exact replica of my previous system but with the corrupted TCP/IP. So for this you will have to manually back up any files and documents or pictures you want to keep, but sadly software is not salvageable, so take a screenshot of or write down the programs you want to re-install on your rebooted system for later.

2a) Now that you've saved everything, you will have two options: Reset to Factory settings or re-install windows. I didn't have spare cash nor any Windows CDs so I simply reset mine, PLUS IT'S FREE.

2b) To re-install Windows 8 or 8.1 I would seek a technician to ensure that you don't have two instances of the same Operating System running on the same computer (and yes a friend of mine confirmed it was possible... No clue how.). And be advised that you probably will have to buy Windows 8 or 8.1 again as locating your serial key on your current system isn't easy (it requires a 3rd party program)

2c) To reset to Factory settings:

       1) Move your cursor to the top right of your screen and click on the bottom icon called settings.

       2) Now click Change PC Settings

       3) Now on the left panel click Update and Recovery 

       4) Click Recovery again on the left panel

       5) Now choose the middle option, Remove everything and reinstall windows (Click Get started)

       6) A pop-up screen that says Please Wait should appear, followed by another titled:                            Reset your PC 

       7) Click next and click the bottom option, Fully clean the drive.

       8) Then click Reset

       9) After the reset your system will ask you to choose your country, language, and so forth.                    Follow the instructions and provide the information asked to complete the installation.

       10) Now believe it or not the Wireless will be disabled when we sign in. IF it isn't, well hey that's            great news. If not then... To fix this problem move your cursor to the top right of your screen and        click on the bottom icon called settings.

       11) Now click Control Panel.

       12) Click Network and Internet, then click Network and Sharing

       13) Click Troubleshoot Problems, then choose Network Adapter

       14) Click next, Select Wi-Fi, then Click next again

       15) The troubleshooter will diagnose and fix the problem. After that, the wifi icon on your toolbar should appear again but gray. Simply choose a network that you trust and put in the password if it's secure. Once the wifi icon on the toolbar appears white you're good to go!

Good Luck everyone, and thanks for stopping by! I hope this helps. If you have any questions I will try my best to answer them and if not will get the attention of certified technicians who will do their best to simply such subject matters.

Group Policy Management Console

When attempting to copy the contents of C:\Windows\PolicyDefinitions from a Windows 10 client to the central policy store that contains down-level policies, you will see an error dialog stating, "Namespace 'Microsoft.Policies.Sensors.WindowsLocationProvider' is already defined as the target namespace for another file in the store." To address this issue:
1. Dismiss the error dialog.
2. Delete LocationProviderAdm.admx and LocationProviderAdm.adml from the central store.
3. Rename Microsoft-Windows-Geolocation-WLPAdm.admx and Microsoft-Windows-Geolocation-WLPAdm.admx in the central store to LocationProviderAdm.admx and LocationProviderAdm.adml, respectively.
