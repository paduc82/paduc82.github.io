patrick barker say:

Hi Anne,

That is absolutely correct! If you constantly have the charged plugged in, the battery becomes weaker. The reason for this is that each cell within a lithium-polymer laptop battery is charged to a voltage level. With that said, the higher the charge percentage, the higher the voltage level (which means it's under more stress). This stress leads to much fewer discharge cycles.

For example, let's say you bought a brand new laptop and it's at 100%. battery. As the hours go by, you notice it's at 8% battery (hypothetical situation). You plug in the charger, let it charge to 100%, but you leave the charger in for a few hours as it's sitting at 100%. Let's assume this is how you charge the laptop for the next few weeks. Well, if you measured the time it took to go from 100% to 8% the first day you used the laptop, to the next few weeks, it took MUCH less time to go from 100% to 8% battery because you kept the charger plugged in, and waited so long to charge the laptop.

In a perfect world, it's been tested that a good % to charge at is around 40%, and charge it up to around 80% (not 100%). Unfortunately, we are human beings and we forget and this simply isn't something that can be perfectly put into practice.

With all of this said, just do your best not to leave the laptop on the charger when it's not actually needing to be charged, and don't let the battery go low if you're near the charger (ideally lower than 40%).

Regards,

Patrick
