iznougoud say:

"Why is it the Operating System's responsibility to 'support' your printer [or any of the many thousands of potential attachments and peripherals]?"

A number of reasons, but I'll only list the most obvious:

The OS does have a responsibility to be backwards compatible (or clearly state that it isn't backwards compatible) with, at the very least, the previous versions presently used by a majority consumers. This is usually done by following basic guidelines communicated to third party manufacturers and programmers, even before a new OS is planned. That is, the are intended to be relevant at least through the a future upcoming OS. If it were the other way around, developers of third party drivers and other software would need continuous access to the source code and design on a level most likely not comfortable to Microsoft.

A project as complex as an operating system, however, couldn't possibly cover all possibilities, and issues will arise upon release and it is introduced to the consumer market with its large number of hardware devices and drivers and combinations thereof. This is what could be considered the "final testing phase". At this point it becomes part of the commercial survival of the operating system to rectify whatever glitches found (preferrably in co-operation with the party manufacturers involved) - unless, of course, they're not clearly a flaw in the design of a third party product (i.e. a problem not restricted to Windows) .

And, of course, Microsoft could simply state that the new OS isn't supposed to be backwards compatible. But that would most likely be a bad idea from a commercial standpoint.

"Why is it not HP's. and your, responsibility to make sure the printer works with a new OS BEFORE upgrading?"

Of course it is. But again, all incompatibilities are not known, and even less communicated, when most users upgrade. And if they were, many users would probably decide not to upgrade. Which makes us go full circle, and once again state that it is in Microsofts best interest to deal with incompatibilities as best they can, if and when they occur.
