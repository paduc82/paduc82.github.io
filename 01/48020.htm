a. user say:

Is the monitor that color at all times and in every situation?

In that case, there are two possible causes:

1- The video cable - that attaches your computer to the monitor - may be loose, or one of the connectors may be broken.

Power off your computer and your monitor. Detach the video cable from the monitor side and the computer side, and take a close look at the connectors on either end. Do any of the pins look broken or bent? If so, replace the video cable.

If the connectors look fine, re-attach both connectors and make sure they're fully plugged in.

2- The monitor may need to be replaced.

If the video cable is fine, and the monitor looks the same even after you re-attach the cable snugly, you probably need a new monitor.

There are other possible causes, but they're not likely. One is that the color balance settings in your graphics driver are wrong. You would know if this was the case, because you would have made the changes yourself. A video card can't change its own color balance.

Another possibility is that your monitor's color settings are off. But as in the first case,  you would know if this was the case, because you would have made the changes yourself. A monitor can't change its own color balance.
