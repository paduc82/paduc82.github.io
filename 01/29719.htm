lemp say:

Yellow and pink highlights can be deleted.

Exceedingly dangerous advice.

Red (pink) means an unsigned entry.  Although these entries are therefor not Microsoft entries and deleting them probably will not cause your installation of Windows to break, you may very well damage the installation of some program/application you've installed.

Yellow means "missing," but there are some entries that Autoruns indicates as missing (in yellow) that are not, in fact, missing.  For example this:

ie4uinit.exe is, in fact, located in C:\Windows\system 32

Autoruns is likely "confused" by the form of the registry entry:

C:\Windows\system32\cmd.exe /D /C start C:\Windows\system32\ie4uinit.exe -ClearIconCache

Bottom line:  if you are not 100% sure of what an entry is, uncheck it but don't delete it.
