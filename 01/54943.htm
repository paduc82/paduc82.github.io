Your sleep Study report is pretty much useless.  The system is not behaving as a normal modern standby system.

You do not have to turn off fast startup in the registry.  That option is in the user interface on the Power Buttons page.

If you want to really check your system, set the display to turn off after 15 minutes when on battery.  Set the hibernation time to 120 minutes.  Set the network connection to always disconnect while on battery.

Before you quit for the night, pull the external power and leave the system on so the  display turns off.  Then you can check it several hours later and do a sleep study report.  The report should give you guidance as to what is using power until it goes into hibernation.

another folder.  However, if you follow this procedure with Outlook and Cloudmark,

it will reduce your spam emails significantly:

1.  In your outlook.com email home page, look at the top right.  You will see a cog

     symbol.  Click the symbol.  It will take you to the Options section.

2.  In the Options section, click Create New Rules, sometimes it says Manage your 

     Rules

3.  In the New Rules section, at the top, it  says Create a New Rule.  Click that line.

4.  in the new rule, type in the email address of the spam sender, or better yet,

      the sender's name.

5.   Then, click Delete.  That will send the spam email to your Delete folder.

6.   Next, in the  your Cloudmark homepage, for your account, click folders,

7.   In folders, click your Delete folder

9.   Return to your account page, and at the bottom, you will see a line that says:

      Permanately delete the folder information you selected.  Click the sentence.

8.   When information is put in your Delete folder, in your Outlook email account,

      Cloudmark will delete it.  It works about 90% of the time.

if you can't figure out the Cloudmark delete option,  Outlook will automately

delete email in your Delete folder after about one week.
