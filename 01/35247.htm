Hi,

I believe that Windows RT does not support iSCSI, possibly because iSCSI is an enterprise technology, and Windows RT intended for home / consumer use.


Support for iSCSI would be useful in limited cases, but I am not quite sure if it is the right solution for what you want to do:


  1. iSCSI is designed to let one host only access one or more drives very quickly. It is not designed to let multiple hosts access the same drives (or LUs) safely.

    Connecting multiple hosts to the same LU at the same time will cause quick and major data corruption, so storing your personal items there does not seem a great idea;

  2. Windows will drop the iSCSI connection if you use your tablet away from your home network, forcing you to manually reconnect when you get home - far from convenient;


So iSCSI is not the way to go, but unfortunately, I have not found a good solution for this scenario. Personally, I use Skydrive and Dropbox to store a copy of my daily documents, while I keep a copy of everything on the Synology.

I will be meeting Synology on Wednesday for the DSM 5.0 launch and will ask them if they have any improvements planned.
