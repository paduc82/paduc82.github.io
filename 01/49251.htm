You can easily resize the photos but that defeats the purpose of
having a camera that can make bill board sized prints. Truth is...
unless you plan to print lots of huge photos or do lots of cropping
of smaller sections and printing them in a large size...a 14 mega-
pixel camera is really not required. I can print beautiful 8x10
photos with my 5 megapixel Canon.

Also there will be a setting in your camera for medium and small
that will reduce the size of the photos...but here again...you are
defeating the purpose of a 14 megapixel camera.

The following site will allow you to compare images from various
cameras. Maybe it will be useful:

Imaging Resource Comparometer
http://www.imaging-resource.com/IMCOMP/COMPS01.HTM

Anyway to the resizing...

You mentioned wallpaper...if your desktop resolution is 1024x768
(for example)...resize your wallpaper to the same size.

You posted in an XP forum so I'll give you XP instructions:

The following free XP PowerToy can resize
the photos and greatly reduce the file size.

Go to the following link and download and
install:   ImageResizer.exe
http://www.microsoft.com/windowsxp/downloads/powertoys/xppowertoys.mspx
(will not run on Vista)

Then you can open any folder that contains
images and you will have an option to resize.
Just right click the selected image files and
choose Resize Pictures from the menu. You
can select one image or a whole group.

The program will place the resized copies in
the folder with your originals.
(Tip: *Never* overwrite your originals)

To insure that images in both portrait and
landscape orientation are resized to the
same dimensions...go to...Advanced...
and enter the largest number in both fields.
IOW...for 600x800...enter 800 in both fields.

(Tip: even if you resize the Photos to the same
(original) dimensions...the file size will be greatly
reduced.)

a trial version).

Surface Pro 3 Feedback - Microsoft Community

trung d nguyen say:

Surface Pro 3 Feedback

I am posting my comments here mainly because it seemed more complicated to send an email to the Surface Team. I'm assuming at some point, they will come across this post; so here's hoping.

So I got the Surface Pro 3 i7 on the day it became available in the US. Overall, it's been a great tool in my productivity arsenal. I'm a power user and my two top priorities are wicked hardware specs that's portable and power to back up the hardware. It is extremely portable with a nice form factor and great screen real estate with a nice screen size. It is a good compromise overall. The bezel makes me wonder if it would be possible to place a 13 or 14 inch screen in the same form factor; but I know that also comes at a compromise to power consumption. I wish the home button on the screen would be more tactile as it's difficult to find in the dark. The pen is a great addition and works great. The type cover  is a fair compromise. And the Surface Edition Arc mouse is a great addition. The stereo speakers are a delight compared to any other ultra portable I've ever had. The screen brightness has allowed me to work outside for the first time. I think purchasing the Surface Pro 3 has been a good compromise compared to my usual notebook comforts. More than likely; I will be replacing my Surface Pro 3 with another Surface Pro 3 here in about 8 months when it dies on me. This last statement is a compliment! I usually have to replace my computer at least once every 12 - 15 months. Mainly due to stress from the internals heating up. It's the compromise that happens with an i7 under constant load for hours or days at a time.

So here's the reason for my post. The Surface Pro 3 is a good product; but it also comes with compromises and flaws. About 4 hours after I purchased my Surface Pro 3 was the first time the device slipped out of my hands and landed on concrete. Still works! Nothing internally seemed to be affected. The magnesium casing was a little more brittle than I would expect; so a piece of the casing broke off. I've dropped the Surface Pro 3 about 2 more times since; each one being about a 100 cm drop. The machine is still functional and I'm satisfied with the fact that I haven't had to replace the device just yet. The magnesium casing is slightly warped in one corner; presumably where the device landed and absorbed the most amount of shock. My request is that the magnesium casing be designed to be less brittle if possible. The screen is great; but I have begun to notice that a corner of the screen is showing signs of burn where the heat is the greatest whenever the system is under high load. Under the standard power scheme, my average work battery life is about 3.5 hours. I consider this reasonable; as I understand it comes at a compromise with portability. I would love the possibility of having an accessory like a type cover with a battery built in. I know the Surface 2 tablet has one available; but I'm crossing my fingers that such an accessory is already in the pipeline for the Surface Pro 3.

I've had the Surface Pro 3 for almost 2 months now. At first I was very skeptical how it would fair. It's a good compromise. Video over IP / VoIP works great, I've actually used the rear camera to snap a couple of quick pictures and documents. Love having the full power of windows available in an almost tablet form factor. I would love for Microsoft voice recognition to work more fluid; but I'm assuming that Cortana is part of the answer.

Thanks Surface Team! You all made a great product.
