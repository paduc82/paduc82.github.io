Why Scorestreaks are bad for Call of Duty - Microsoft Community

nvo fusion say:

Why Scorestreaks are bad for Call of Duty

/*
Anything I state here is solely my opinion, and is not to offend any person that may, for some reason, be offended. It is also not meant as a hate-speech against anyone who thinks otherwise. This post is only my opinion, but I encourage everyone to reply with their own opinion on the topic.
*/

The Call of Duty franchise has been using Killstreaks and Scorestreaks for many years, and when you really think about it, the concept is the polar opposite of how it should be for a game of this type and even in real life.

Just to get started, if you are getting 25 kills for every 2 deaths, you should probably get an award, but not something as powerful as the 'Ghosts' K.E.M. or even the Goliath in AW. I think you should earn something smaller, something like a small supply crate or a different type of ammunition (Incendiary, jacketed, etc), or even extra exp would be good awards in my opinion (of course they can be improved upon).

On the other hand, if you are getting 7 kills for every 43 deaths, then you are bound to call in assistance. Both in real life, and in game, you would have support sent in to your troops in hope that they can reclaim victory, not let them suffer. I will touch up on this again later in the post.

In a game like Call of Duty, you are also [indirectly] promoting cheating by using the current system of kill/scorestreaks. Kids and adults alike disregard the rules at some point [in time], some more then others, but when those people see that they are running up a deathstreak of 14 they will probably start getting pretty *** off (not all, but most I have come into contact with). The last time I saw a game from this franchise help the losing party was back in Modern Warfare 3, where players dying a certain number of times can receive some sort of support, of their choosing. An example of this is the "Final Stand" deathstreak (MW3) which, after four consecutive deaths, allowed you to take a final stand before dying. This allowed the player to have another chance at life, by having the player fall to the fall as right before they die, with their primary weapon, allowing them to kill the enemy (as long as the enemy doesn't get another shot at you, which hits). You can eventually stand back up as if it never happened if you manage to stay alive and heal yourself.

When a losing player sees the winner gets a Chopper Gunner (yes, it is an old streak, just an example) in the air each time the previous one is called away or shot down, and it keep killing them when they spawn into the game, what chance do they have at winning? None. The system is [indirectly] encouraging people to cheat in any way possible, so they can be less frustrated by what seems to be an endless amount of bullets raining down on them from a gunner.

However, when a losing person gets support, it is a sigh of relief for them, as they still have hope. The, lets just call these things lifesavers for the purpose of this post. Lifesavers should not be too powerful although they should provide some support to the losing player, who may be issued some extra armor, or maybe a drone to do some reconnaissance, or whatever else may be fit.

 

 

I think it would be ideal to cut my story short here, as I don't think anyone wants to read an entire book on how the killstreak/scorestreak system is bad. Please leave opinions in the replies below!

Open Add/Remove Programs in the Control Panel.

Scroll down to the listing(s) of Microsoft .NET Frameworks
