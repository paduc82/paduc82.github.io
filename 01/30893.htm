You have some basic misunderstanding of the function of System Restore and the answer Ronnie Vernon gave you.

 

System Restore is a point-in-time snapshot of the system state.  It's date/time is when that snapshot was made.  It's no different than a photograph taken on 3-22-2013 @ 5PM --> that's the time and date it was taken.  You can have several system restore points (one a day or one is made before certain installations, etc.)  You get to choose one of these when using system restore to go back to that 'point in time'.

 

Just like writing the wrong date on the back of a printed photo doesn't make it true it was taken on that written down date and time - changing the date on a system restore file would do nothing.  If that is even what you were attempting to do - which I am unsure of.

 

Now - System Restore *can* be corrupted for various reasons and not work.  Unfortunately - most people only discover this when they NEED to use System Restore.  :-(


Ronnie Vernon thought you were trying to change the date on a given system restore to another date - which would accomplish NOTHING even if it was possible.  If you were not trying to change the date - then that assumption was incorrect - but based off what you wrote... so that's just a misunderstanding if it is so.

Let's go back to basics again...

 

System restore is like having an archive of physical photographs.  These photographs are stored in a box that, for sake of argument, can only hold (again - this number is meaningless other than an example) 10 of the photographs at any given time.  If you take two photos a day (6AM and 6PM) and store them in the box - in five days, the box is full.  You want newer photos more than you want older (assumption) so you - on the 6th day - remove two photos and replace them with the two new ones from that day.  You repeat this process every day.  In the end you have 10 photos you can review in that box at any given time (and for the sake of argument - you burned the photos you removed from the box immediately.)

 

So - if you wanted to look at a photo from 7 days back - you cannot.  It does not exist.  If you wanted to look at a photo you took 3 days back - that should be no problem - unless you decided one day to take 10 pictures manually and replace all of them in the box with the ones from that day.

 

So - you are interpretting the "any restore date" too literally.  With your interpretation, I should be able to even choose a restore point from another computer. heh  Not likely to happen.

 

If the photo (system restore point) doesn't exist - you cannot choose it.  The number of restore points are limited by many factors (settings you can change, size of your hard drive, realistic things.)

 

If you think going back to Apple resolves your issues without causing any different ones - more power to you.  Having dealt with Windows, Apple and Linux for many years now - I have not found that to be true. heh
