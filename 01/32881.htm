Hi tank,

Windows has a feature called Fast startup (below). Fast startup allows you to start your Surface in less time than is typically required for a traditional, cold startup. A fast startup is a hybrid combination of a cold startup and a wake-from-hibernation startup.

In regards to drivers, a fast startup simply loads the hibernation file (Hiberfil.sys) into memory to restore the previously saved image of the loaded drivers. While a cold startup, enumerates the devices attached to the computer, and loads drivers for them.

After you have updated drivers on your Surface, Restart is the recommend action. Restart isn't affected by the fast startup feature.

Alternatively, if you wish to perform a full shutdown you can do that by running shutdown /s or shutdown /r if you wish to due a full shutdown and restart.

Hope this helps,
