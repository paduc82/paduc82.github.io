a. user say:

Power interruptions/failures are a common cause of systems losing the ability to restart since they can cause corruption of the NT File System (NTFS). 

Usually a chkdsk with error correction (chkdsk  /r) will straighten things out but you are going to have to boot on something in order to do that - either the Recovery Console or something like a Hiren's Boot CD and you may have to run it more than once.

Have you ever heard of a UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)?  You can pick up a decent one for around $50-$100 depending on how much backup power you think you might need (15 minutes, 30 minutes, etc.).  A UPS will protect your system from power outages, brown outs, surges, etc. and keep your system running for some amount of time in a power failure to give you a chance to perform a graceful shutdown.

Most come with software and a serial interface that you can connect to your system (cable usually included) and Windows has a built in Service that can receive a message from the UPS over the serial port when it is close to running out of juice to tell Windows to perform a graceful shutdown if you are not around to do it yourself.

A UPS will also shield your system from quick power outages and glitches which may only cause your lights to blink for a second, but can cause your computer to lose power long enough to "crash" and potentially corrupt the file system.

If you perform a Google search on your error message(s) you can get lots of links to potential solutions to those issues that might work sometimes maybe but you need to fix the problem and not the symptom(s) of the problem that occurs after these incidents.

There is a big difference in fixing the problem and fixing the symptoms of the problem.

The symptom of the problem is after you somehow get your system running again, the console session (and maybe other things) don't work and you have to reload.  You might be able to fix the symptoms sometimes.

The problem (sounds like) your system is having power interruptions that are corrupting parts of your file system and other things and you can fix/prevent that from happening.

To fix the problem you must keep your system from experiencing even minor power interruptions and in the event of a power outage give the operating system an opportunity to shutdown gracefully.

If your system of unspecified make and model has SATA drives, the Recovery Console might not help you since the installation CD does not have SATA drivers on it.
