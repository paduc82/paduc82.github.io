    celebcharas say:

    You'll want to verify that battery saver has been appropriately configured as a starting point. You can just hit the Windows key and type "battery", and the top result should be the system settings for battery saver.

    I configure mine to turn on battery saver when batter falls below 95%, but that number is configurable by you. I also check the box by "Lower screen brightness while in battery saver".

    Doing only these things, I've been able to easily get more than 6 hours of battery life. It depends to some extent on what you are doing.  For me, Photoshop and InDesign tend to chew through battery so I try to limit those activities as much as possible until I'm plugged in.  I've found that general internet browsing (Edge in my experience has been far, far better on battery than Chrome), watching videos, and using Word/Excel/PowerPoint/Outlook all seem to have much less impact on battery compared to PS and ID.

    Microsoft has a decent article on battery saving tips here you can check out too.

    Hope this helps!
