hairyfool say:

1st off I would strongly recommend removing RAID 0 and reverting back to single drives. The speed benefit of disk striping is relatively minimal and as you would lose everything if either drive fails the potential for problems is significantly increased, a rock solid backup routine capable of doing a bare metal restore would be essential. Standard desktop drives are not optimised for block mode transfer, Western Digital have the Red series drive for that purpose. If speed is that critical SSD would be far more effective.

Also bear in mind that Windows cannot "see" the hard disks at all if they are attached to a RAID controller. The controller handles all the communication to the individual drives and presents to the operating system a single 1TB drive of indeterminate geometry or identity. When you do any form of drive diagnostics you are not directly testing the drive. Windows is known to get this wrong but it could be the controller.
