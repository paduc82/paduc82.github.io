groundunder72 say:

For Xbox players, I have found the fix, and it makes sense in a weird

kinda way.

At your Xbox console,

1. Go to Settings

2. Privacy

3. Change Settings

4. Make sure 'Custom' is selected and choose "Customize"

5. Scroll down and select 'Member Content'

6. Change the setting to "Everyone". It *MUST* be at "Everyone". "Friends Only" won't fix it.

7. Back out and "Save Changes"

You will be signed out and back into your profile and 'Contracts' should

now work.

Now...

Before this worked, I will admit I tried a lot of other things which

*might* have also helped. I can't go back and indo those things to

check, so here is what I did right before I spent a while playing with

my privacy settings to REALLY fix this problem.

I deleted all Hitman Absolution Data from my hard drive (Including the

Sniper preview game) and Cloud Storage (yeah, that means I have to start

all over again after finishing in 23 hours).

I went into 'Settings' --> 'System' --> 'Storage' --> 'Hard Drive' -->

'System Items'

I then cleared / deleted EVERY single thing that was listed.

Oddly enough, the Hitman items wouldn't delete fully the first go around

until I cleared what I could here, rebooted, and then deleted them

again.

So if you still have trouble after the first fix (the one that actually

fixes it), maybe give my other steps a try. Those "other" steps btw, are

what Eidos support recommended.

pa bear - ms mvp say:

mendocinosunrise say:

AHA!! I think I got it. I was so busy looking at things like local group policies, that I forgot these are STUDENT computers.

They have a program installed called DeepFreeze from http://www.faronics.com/ - it prevents kids from making "creative improvements" to the computers.

I'm reloading the correct config files now. I'm betting that will solve the problem.
