c:\windows\system32\spool\PRINTERS

davidkentala say:

Another variation of the question:  How do I remove segments within the video while keeping the beginning and end?

considerate_guy say:

First a clarification:

Movie Maker never touches your original source files (photos, videos and music).  When you are creating a movie in Movie Maker, you are telling it to take parts of your existing files and create a whole new edited version of those files in a new movie.  It is up to you to then decide if you want to get rid of the source files and only go with the edited movie.  This is actually the way all video editors work.  You aren't affecting your original files, just creating a new movie.

In order to make edits to a video clip, you can use the trim features (Set start and end points) and that will skip over the parts at the beginning or end.

However, what's usually a much easier way to work is to use the Split feature as noted previously.

Let's say your clip looks like this:
GGGGGBBBBBGGGGG

(G represents the good parts, B the bad part you want to remove.)

Play the clip, or place the playback marker between the first good part and the start of the bad part.  Now use the Split option (M on the keyboard, or Split button on the Video Tools | Edit tab).  The result should be that the clip is split in two parts:

GGGGG|BBBBBGGGGG

(This is a logical split.  Again, you aren't actually affecting the original clip.  And they will still play one after the other.)

Repeat the process by going to the end of the bad portion and making another split:

GGGGG|BBBBB|GGGGG

On your storyboard/project area, you should see 3 clips now.  Right-click on the middle (bad) clip and choose 'Remove'.  The result will be the following:

GGGGG|GGGGG

And when you save that as a movie, it will skip over the bad part and play just the good parts.
