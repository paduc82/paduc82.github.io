techfreak_ say:

Classic Skype 7.x never retrieved anymore than 30 days of chat data from the online cloud. Originally the storage was recent, being a few days or weeks. Anything over ~30 days was stored offline in your main.db file, local cache and simply read by the client; similar to an Outlook PST file. Changes to the cloud allowed this online storage for chats (text, in-line images) to increase to 2 years (30 days to up to 2 years). Any linked cloud data like in-line images referenced by the main.db could be downloaded versus relying on stored cache. The policy on external attachments (files, attached images) is currently only 30 days. I still have conversations from over 10 years ago in my offline main.db files. Some of those files on may machines have become corrupted due to various reasons. My last active one has about 6 years of history due to some partial file corruption. Any information lost was not due to any deletion server-side. If information is missing from your main.db file, it is more than likely due to partial file corruption that was repaired or a deletion that occurred. In the past, if you lost your main.db file you would have nothing more than recent history up to 30 days. If you have no main.db file nowadays, you can still download almost 2 years of data. What was added was up to 2 years of online storage. What was taken away was the ability to have future offline storage without having to perform that task manually.

I suggest you look through any computer you accessed in the past for any main.db files that may contain the history you are looking for. If you have any files present like main.corrupt(.db), that is one indicator some corruption occurred. In addition to the export feature you can use third party programs like SkypeLogView [nirsoft.net] to view the contents for your main.db files or possibly your main.corrupt.db files. If any older data still exists, you may be able to extract it for archival purposes.
