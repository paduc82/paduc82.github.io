james vinyard say:

I want to put an additional note on CPU cooling; the BIOS is designed to read the CPU fan speed when you press the power button and will actually prevent the computer from turning on if the speed is zero or not connected.

If you have a Surface tablet with the i5 or i7 CPU and you do not hear the fan, put your hand over the cooling vents on the upper right side and you should feel a slight "breeze" and that is how it should operate normally.

I have an i3 model which can also overheat at times but rare for the fan to kick in where you hear it.

The system is designed to prevent an "explosion" and the explosion is actually not big but can present a really big surprise if it did happen.

The newer CPUs will clock down and attempt to stall out if the CPU fan fails to also prevent a malfunction.

YouTube has examples of AMD CPUs and what happens to them if you remove the fan during operation.  Intel CPUs have been tested and do better.

The BIOS controls all functions of the hardware and the operating system gets its information from the BIOS.  If that information is not available with built in utilities, there are some third party utilities that you can get if you want to monitor system performance.

Don't worry about it, it can't work without the fan with the exception of the M3 CPU.
