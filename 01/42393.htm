I had the same issue (Lumia 540) too, fixed it by updating the 'Portable Devices' drivers in Device Manager. If Portable devices are not detected in Device Manager, then uninstall all individual devices/drivers of 'Universal Serial Bus Controllers'. Then restart the computer and connect the phone. It will auto install a preliminary driver which will allow the computer to detect the phone as a Portable Device. Once you get that......double click the phones model name under Portable Devices. Go to Driver tab then select 'Update Driver'. Next of the two options choose 'Browse my computer for driver software'. After that choose 'Let me pick from a list of device drivers on my computer'. Then among the list of device drivers in the box under 'Show compatible hardware'.......you should see three names. Of them choose 'MTP USB Device' and click 'Next'. This worked in my case and I am now able to see the personal name of my phone under Portable Devices in Device Manager.

And it is being detected as a hard drive in explorer and also being detected by Windows Phone Companion.

jack burt0n say:

Hi!  Well, since nothing concrete has been mentioned about what exactly will be transferred over from the Xbox 360 to the Xbox One, there have been some general announcements made.  Here's what I can gather from all the actual press releases and interviews with Xbox people like Marc Whitford and Larry Hyrb (Major Nelson):

1) Your gamertag and your achievements will carry over to the Xbox One.  As far as the apps you've downloaded, I haven't heard anything specifically yet, but due to the cross-platform nature of the microsoft app store they've been pushing with Windows 8, I think it's pretty safe to assume that any apps on the 360 will also be available for the Xbox One.  Maybe not at launh, but pretty soon thereafter.  As far as the games, those definitely will not transfer.  Any indie games, XBLA, or Xbox Games On-Demand that you've downloaded will not transfer to the Xbox One, as the architecture of the system is just so radically different, there's no possible way to add backwards-compatibility without raising the price of the system dramatically.  I don't know about songs or anything like that at this time, or the ability to transfer anything from the 360 to the Xbox One.  All of the gamertag/achievements information are stored in your Microsoft/Xbox Live Account, and by signing in on the Xbox One, that's how it will find all of that information.

2) Nope.  If you want to play those 360 games (even the ones you downloaded), you'll have to keep your 360 to play them, unfortunately.

3) You can format the hard drive before you trade it in, wiping any save data and games off of there.  The instructions to do so can be found on xbox.com.  I don't have the link handy right now, but I can dig it up.  I would not trust whoever buys your used system to wipe the info themselves.  That said, they shouldn't be able to access any of your account information without knowing your username/password.  I don't believe there will be any way to transfer data from your 360 to the Xbox One.

Any other questions?  I hope this has been helpful.
