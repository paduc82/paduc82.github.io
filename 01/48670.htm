First of all DO NOT use your email for your son's account!! This will lead to A LOT of trouble later, up to losing your account completely with all your email, One Drive content and other Microsoft Services!!! 

There is absolutely no reason to give a kid access to the adult parent account. 

Thanks to the Xbox One's Home Console sharing, you can do the purchasing with your own account, and share almost everything (Live memberships, game purchases, Game Pass Memberships) with the console, and everyone playing on that console will be able to benefit from that. 

So your son can play with his own email on his own account, with your shared stuff on that console. 

There are only very few things that can't be shared, and that are "consumables" (like virtual game currencies a la V-Bucks, Minecraft Coins and the likes), and some DLC expansion packs for some games. 

So please, as long as you both don't have much game progress on your "wrong way around" profiles, do the following:

Tell your son that he has to use his own account from now on. 

Add your son's account to your parent account family. To do that, sign in on https://account.microsoft.com/family

After you added your son to the family, you can go to https://account.xbox.com/settings and set up the online and privacy settings for your son (for example you can set up time limits how long he can play, or allow him to talk to known people on his friend list ONLY, and prevent from making purchases on the store). 

Then go to the console and sign in with your parent account. Go to Settings, and under "Personalization" make sure that the console is your account's Home Console. That will share Memberships and Games to that console, so your son can play these with his own account. 

Then, if necessary set up a passkey on your account so your son can't (ab-)use your account.

how do i back up my computer. I have a Seagate external hard - Microsoft Community

caz11 say:

how do i back up my computer. I have a Seagate external hard drive?

how do i back up my computer. I have a Seagate external hard drive?

a. user say:

'How to backup' is a huge topic that's discussed in depth on the internet, but I'll give an outline of it here.

There are two types of backup: a file and folder backup and an image. A file and folder backup will backup individual files and folders that you specify. That's a good choice for backing up your personal files (documents, pictures, music, etc.)

An image backup copies your entire hard drive from beginning to end. That's what you do to backup Windows and all your software.

Third-party backup software is the best way to backup. It will help you choose what to backup and it will run your backups automatically.

There's lots more detail and it's all on the internet. Take your time, learn how to do it the right way, and you'll never again worry about losing anything on your computer.
