ken blake - microsoft mvp say:

On Fri, 21 Jan 2011 04:15:27 +0000, Anna's Poppy wrote:

I just rec'd a new cpu with Windows 7 Home Premium. 

Undoubtedly you received a new computer, not just a new CPU.

"CPU" stands for "Central Processing Unit." In modern personal
computers, the CPU is a relatively small electronic chip, an
integrated circuit. It sits on the motherboard, inside the computer's
case. It is not the case itself, nor is it the total computer, which
includes the case and everything within it.
I'm not sure how the incorrect practice began of calling the whole
computer the "CPU," but here's my guess: when you buy a computer, you
normally get the case and what's in it, along with a monitor,
keyboard, mouse, and perhaps a printer. All of these peripherals
connect to the case. Because everything connects to the case, the case
is "central" to everything else and the term "Central Processing Unit"
is sometimes misunderstood and misapplied to it.
I say the above, not to give you a hard time, but because using the
terminology correctly can be very important. It may not happen in this
case, but wrong terminology can get people trying to help you to
misunderstand you, and for that reason give you incorrect answers.

hello Cowell,

There are two types of Windows licence.

1. Retail (which means you buy the product in a box from a retailer.

2. OEM (Original Equipment manufacture) which means windows  comes pre-installed on your PC.

 

If you have type 1 (Retail) you can install it on your shiney new computer. When you activate you may be told that the product key is already in use. Whether this happens or not will depend upon how long it has been since you last activated your copy of Windows. If you have a retail copy and you are informed (when activating over the internet) by the automated activation line that your product key is already in use you simply use the 'telephone' option instead. See this link from my website: http://vistasupport.mvps.org/product_key_already_in_use.htm

If you have type 2 (OEM) then you will not be able to re-install windows on your new computer as the copy will be tied to your old computer.

sheerst say: In reply to deleted message
For those who also want to do this, I found the solution. In task scheduler, the action needs to look like:

Start a program: %windir%\Speech\Common\sapisvr.exe -SpeechUX

%windir%\Speech\Common\sapisvr.exe will be in the Program/script box, and -SpeechUX will be in the Add arguments box.
