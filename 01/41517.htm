    a. user say:

    I hate to say it, but your computer was virtually bricked anyway; the update just pushed it over the edge.

    New versions of Windows are like Judgement Day to computers in trouble. In past, that would mean upgrading from Windows 7 to 8, or 8 to 8.1, etc. Today there is only one Windows but it gets major upgrades twice a year which are basically the same thing.

    Major upgrades make big changes to the operating system, and if the upgrade installer (known simply as Setup) encounters configuration changes it wasn't built to work with the results can be unpredictable.

    Some people in your situation are lucky: Setup throws up its hands and says 'forget it, I'm not touching this.' They're the ones who write and say "my computer hasn't been updated since ..." But for many other people Setup tries to get as far as it can go and damages the computer in the process. But really, the computer was already messed up and had it coming. Maybe you didn't consider it messed up, but Setup expects to find a computer configured as Microsoft designed it to be configured.

    When this happens you typically won't be able to undo the damage. It's very difficult to guess what the original configuration error was and exactly Setup tried to deal with it.

    There is an easy way around this, but unfortunately you are among the majority of people posting here who don't backup on any regular basis, if at all.

    There are at least two ways I know of for you to recover your missing files:

    1- They maybe inside windows.old; or

    2- Boot your computer from any kind of rescue media that has a file manager. Use the file manager to copy your personal files to an external USB hard drive. You won't be able to copy your programs but they can be reinstalled.
