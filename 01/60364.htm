This slowness usually indicates that your computer is busy working on background tasks.  There are many reasons this might happen such as:

  • Too many other programs are running
  • An intensive program (like a virus scanner) is busy scanning your computer
  • Your system is busy working on updates
  • Your system is processing backups or performing other maintenance
  • Your computer is busy indexing your files for the instant-search system
  • A virus has infected your computer

If you have an up-to-date virus scanner and are reasonably sure you haven't downloaded any suspicious programs, then it's probably not a virus.  That's just one of the many possibilities for slow performance.

  • Side note: If you don't have an up-to-date virus scanner (such as you had one, but it is expired and needs a payment), let me suggest that you remove the outdated one and install the free Microsoft Security Essentials instead. It is free and never requires a subscription to keep you protected.

If it's not a virus, I would first restart your computer to see if the issue continues.  If it does, here's some steps you can try.

  1. Turn off automatic startup programs
    Over time as you install programs, some of them will add themselves to your Startup and auto-run themselves when you turn on your computer. This means less working memory is available for the programs you are intentionally using.  Here's how to turn off the automatic startup programs.

    Press Ctrl-Shift-Escape to show the Task Manager. On the Startup tab, disable any programs you don't need auto-running all the time.  You can still use these programs later, they just won't hog up the system automatically all the time.

  2. Update your computer
    Open the "Store" app and install the free Windows 8.1 update, which fixes hundreds of problems that have been reported and found in the past 2 years.
