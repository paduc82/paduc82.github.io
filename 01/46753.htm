lemp say:

Please only ask your question once.  Repeatedly asking the same question will not get you a faster response.

The "selection position" of the standard mouse cursor is the point where the vertical line connects to the line going down and to the right:

Thus, if you want to select something on the right edge of the screen, most of the mouse has to move off the screen in order to get the point at the right edge.  In practice, this usually doesn't really matter very much, because the vertical scroll bar that is typically at the right edge of a maximized window is about the same width as the cursor, so you can position the cursor on the left edge of the scroll bar and see the entire cursor.

This behavior doesn't happen at the left edge of the screen because there is nothing to select beyond the left edge of the screen.

Are you have some difficulty using the cursor at the right edge of your screen?
