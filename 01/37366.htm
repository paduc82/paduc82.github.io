laudablepus say:

The audio processing uses kernel streaming which operates in kernel mode and is part of the operating system.
This sends a digital signal to the on-board audio (Realtek) or your added sound card. If that is screwed up you'll have to reset the whole OS (as a last resort).

Right click on the speaker icon in the notifications area on the taskbar and select troubleshoot sound problems. Click on 'advanced' if you want to apply repairs automatically. Then hit Next.

Does Beats audio show up in programs: >Settings >System >Apps and features? If so, you can uninstall it by clicking on it and selecting uninstall. I'm concerned that there are some components of the program remaining. Anything listed in Programs on the (C:) drive?
