windows7geek say:

Computer Wont Connect To Tv(HDMI)

 I like to watch movies and tv shows on my tv through my computer. I used a HDMI input to connect to my tv, and its worked for a while.But the other day I had to scoot my computer over and I unplugged the hdmi cable from my computer while it was on, and I plugged it in and it wouldnt work anymore. I had already used control panel to try to get two moniters going up (my computer and my tv), but it would recognize my t.v. What do I Do?

samc1 say: Some video cards require you to connect to the TV through (multiple) monitor settings. Until you do this, the TV signal may not be routed over the HDMI output.
With nVidia card, for instance, you have to use the nVidia Control Panel (right click on desktop and then nVidia Control Panel) to set your PC display and TV display whether both should show the same (aka clone setting), independent, or used to extend the desktop. This setting usually stays for as long as the PC is not rebooted even if the TV is switched off. If the computer is restarted you've to re-do the TV settings. The TV detection by video card also varies from TV to TV. Some can only be detected if switched to an HDMI port, while others (in the minority I think) can be detected regardless of their AV settings. This may also be dependent on the video card but my observation is based on at least 3 different models of nVidia graphics cards.

To confirm that your TV HDMI input port is OK, if you can, try with another TV HDMI input. TV HDMI input ports seem to be notoriously unreliable and they can fail. A friend of mine had a similar problem and had his TV and had it replaced for free by describing the HDMI problem while a few months out of warranty. Makes you wonder why the manufacturer was so keen to replace it (but no complaint for the good gesture!). This may not apply to you but that HDMI port on every LCD TV is a relatively new gadget and not well tested over time for reliability under various usage scenarios, and could be the weak link between a computer and digital TV.
