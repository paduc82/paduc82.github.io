arminatorx say:

You are getting the concept of how the "Home Console" feature works wrong. When you say "Game Sharing" you usually mean "I give you my games, and you give me your games, and the 'sharing' is established, and if one side doesn't share, it should automatically unshare the other side too". It does NOT work like that. 

Basically, forget about the "We give games each other" concept. 

If you are unable to play your friend's games, you can't do anything about that. The only person to rectify that, is your friend. 

Your friend has to come over to your console, sign in with his own account, and set your console as his Home Console again. 

It's up to you if you want to do the same. Even if you don't share your games with him, after he set your console as his home console, you will be able to play his games. 

So it's not "Game Sharing". It's the Home Console family sharing.

I FOUND THE ANSWER!!!

You have a SERVICE which is malfunctioning.

Now to fix it.

Go to CONTROL PANEL, select ADMINISTRATIVE TOOLS, select EVENT VIEWER, click on Administrative Events,

Under Administrative Events I FOUND

The HP Connected Remote Service service terminated unexpectedly.  It has done this 2683 time(s).  The following corrective action will be taken in 5000 milliseconds: Restart the service.

I waited for 4 refreshes. I clicked away from Administrative Events, then clicked on Administrative Events The Counter was at 2687 times.

I THEN KNEW I HAD THE CULPRIT. To fix my problem, I had to CHANGE THE PREFERENCES for HP Connected Remote Service. (YOU MAY FIND A DIFFERENT FAULTY SERVICE.)

TO CHANGE THE OPERATION OF THE SERVICE.

Go to TASK MANAGER (Press the keys Ctrl, Alt, Del AT THE SAME TIME. 

Select TASK MANAGER, Choose the TAB Services, Click on the heading NAME, to rearrange the Services by alphabetical order.

Find the FAULTY SERVICE.

RGHT CLICK on Faulty Service, select Open Services

In the SERVICES window, find the Faulty Service again, RIGHT CLICK Service and select Properties. Under properties, Select RECOVERY. Under Recovery I set First Failure, Second Failure and Subsequent Failures to TAKE NO ACTION, Then click the APPLY Button. Now back out OR shut down the Control Panel AND the Task Manager. YOU ARE DONE, No more 8 second refreshes/flickers.
