Yes, it's cheating, and I agree with you that some of us like to play legit.

As for the difference between hacking and modding, I don't know if there's a universally accepted set of definitions that lay out what each act entails.  Modding, from my experience, is about adding abilities or changes to a game in order to alter the details.  It's not always something that gives people an unfair advantage either.  There are some wonderful mods for PC games, including one for Skyrim that changes the first dragon into Randy Machoman Savage.  It's actually quite funny.

Hacking is when one changes attributes in order to gain a specific advantage over others, like unlimited health or ammo, etc.  By this definition, players who do this are more often hacking than modding, even if they claim it's the other way round.

Other definitions of hacking would be going into computer systems that one is not authorized for, while modding would be simply changing the specifications inside the game.  In that sense, one doesn't generally hack a game, but simply mods to get more health, etc.

Realistically, there's no cut and dried standard for what the differences are, except to say that they are both cheating when it comes to PvP (although I'd allow changes, like ones that are strictly cosmetic, that don't actually help a person in PvP are not really cheating).

There are also other types of cheating as well, like lag switching and using in-game glitches.
