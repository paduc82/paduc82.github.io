davidpitlik say:

poor image quality when burning wmv to dvd

I've created a video slide show using Movie maker.  It runs three minutes in length.   Playing back the WMV file, which is at the highest resolution, on my computer looks crisp and sharp.  However, when I try burning this same file to a DVD, play back is poor.  There are vertical lines running through the images.

I'm very confused because I used the very same programs a few months ago to make numerous slideshows and subsequent DVDs and they all worked perfectly.  I can't figure out what has changed.  Interestingly enough, I am experiencing the same problem on my laptop as well.  Any suggestion on how to make the burned DVD as perfect as the WMV?  Both are formatted to be widescreen format.  
considerate_guy say:

On possible contributing factor here is that the DVD format is a standard-definition format.  Though people tend to forget this, DVDs came from an era of analog TVs that had a maximum resolution of 720 x 480 (NTSC).  So the crisp .wmv file, likely in an HD resolution of 1920 x 1080, is going to get changed to an SD resolution for the DVD.

 

As a test, you might try saving to your PC first in a 720 x 480 (or close to that resolution) and see if the same artifacts appear.  The other contributing factor is that the video is switching formats from .wmv on your PC to MPEG2 video (in a .vob container) for the DVD.  That can result in some additional compression artifacts.

 

If you have a Blu-ray player, you may want to consider burning the .wmv file directly onto a DVD disc and playing it in HD resolution instead.  You won't have a menu (and it won't play on DVD players), but you will have HD video this way.  Other options for getting HD video to an HDTV may include putting the file onto a USB drive, or streaming using DLNA or similar to a playback device, such as your Xbox or PS3 console.
