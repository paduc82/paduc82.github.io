You should probably leave the disk space issue until the connection issues are resolved, but see below.

With respect to your hard drive space, your description sounds as if someone partitioned the physical hard drive into a "system" partition (C:\) and a "data" partition (D:\).  If this is done and -- as in your situation -- the system partition was not assigned much space, you have to be rigorous in not storing anything other than Windows on the C:\ partition.  This means, for example, using a "custom" option when installing programs and directing the installation to the D:\ partition (and not all programs have such an option), and re-directing your data folders (My Documents, My Photos, My Music, etc.) to the D:\partition.

At this point, you have some options. 

The first thing you should do is run Disk Cleanup.  Click Start, type Disk Cleanup in the Search box, and press Enter.  You'll have the choice of which partition you want to clean.  The initial Disk Cleanup display will show how much space you'll recover.  You can also click "Cleanup System Files" to see a few more things that can be deleted.  You can click each of the items to see a short description of what you will be deleting.  Disk Cleanup will give you some more space, but likely still not enough.

At this point, you might want to download and run TreeSize Free.  Make sure to right-click and select "Run as administrator" when running this program.  TreeSize Free will show you what files and folders are taking up space on your drive. 

https://www.jam-software.com/treesize_free/

If your data folders take up a large amount of space on C:\, you can easily move them to D:\ as described here:  https://www.techsupportalert.com/content/how-move-windows-7-personal-folders-my-documents-another-drive.htm

Although moving your data folders will relieve some of the pressure on the C:\ partition, ultimately what you probably ought to do is combine the C:\ and D:\ partitions into one.  This is a bit tricky to accomplish.  First, you'll have to uninstall all programs installed on D:\ and later re-install them on C:\.

You then have two options. 

You can copy everything from D:\ to an external backup drive, then delete everything on D:\ and use Windows 7 native tools to merge D:\ with C:\.

Or you can use a third-party disk management tool that merges D:\ with C:\ while attempting to preserve the data.  Notice the word "attempting."  These tools should work, but dealing with disk operations without first making a backup is living dangerously. 

This page discusses both of the above methods:  https://www.easeus.com/partition-master/merge-partitions-windows-7.html  I noticed that the download links in that article are for a "free trial" of Partition Master Pro.  I don't know what, if any, limitations the "free trial" incorporates.  You don't have to buy anything.  Partition Master also has a free (for individual use) edition that will work just as well:  https://www.easeus.com/partition-manager/epm-free.html

Hi Tina101,

Welcome to Windows Vista Answers Forums!

First you need to take ownership of the Folder you need to make changes. You may follow these steps to take ownership of the folder.

To take ownership of a file or a folder

You must have ownership of a protected folder in order to access it. If another user has restricted access and you are the computer administrator, you can access the folder by taking ownership.

To take ownership of a folder, follow these steps:

1.     Right-click the folder that you want to take ownership of, and then click Properties.

2.     Click the Security tab, and then click OK on the Security message (if one appears).

3.     Click Advanced, and then click the Owner tab.

4.     In the Name list, click your user name, or click Administrator if you are logged in as Administrator, or click the Administrators group. If you want to take ownership of the contents of the folder, select the Replace owner on subcontainers and objects check box.

5.     Click OK, and then click Yes when you receive the following message:

You do not have permission to read the contents of directory folder name. Do you want to replace the directory permissions with permissions granting you Full Control?

All permissions will be replaced if you click Yes.
Note folder name is the name of the folder that you want to take ownership of.

6.     Click OK, and then reapply the permissions and security settings that you want for the folder and its contents.

After taking ownership, check whether you can stop sharing.
