jeeped say:

I'm not 100% sure but I believe that you are experiencing a bad 'file extension association'. This is where the default program to open a file is wrong. In the case of your desktop, these are all shortcuts (e.g. .LNK files) and the .LNK association has been mistakenly coupled with Photo Viewer.


I've seen this happen when upon opening a program through a shortcut (e.g. .LNK file), you are asked what program to open it with. After selecting Photo Viewer (or some other program) via the Select a program from a list of installed programs, and checking the Always use the selected program to open this type of file (circled in red below), the document or program referenced by the original shortcut opens in Photo Viewer as it should.

The trouble is that now all shortcuts (e.g. .LNK files) on the desktop or throughout Explorer will want to be opened by could occur if you performed those 'Open With' actions on a .JPG or even an .EXE, but the .LNK explanation is far more likely for items on the desktop.

The good news is that a small free utility has been written to undo the .LNK file association that was created. There is also a registry hack to accomplish this, but I recommend the unASSOC utility for its ease of use.

arminatorx say:

Did you really connect the headset correctly as described in these instructions? 

There are two jacks on the headset itself, one with an oval shaped port, and one with a plug that says "IN" next to it. 

Make sure the Microphone boom is plugged into the hole with the oval shaped port. 

Connect the chat cable to the "IN" Port and the controller. 

Also check steps 9 and 10, depending on if you are using the stereo adapter, or if you plug the headset directly into your controller. 

To test if it's a network issue or a local issue, bring up the messaging part of the Guide menu, and start a new message. It doesn't matter who you send the message to, as you don't actually send the message, you just compose the message, listen to it, and then can discard the message. 

Instead of typing, press the microphone button in the message window, and talk into the microphone. 

When you're finished recording your test message, listen to your message. Do you hear your own message?

If so, then you have a network related party issue, or you have muted (yourself in) the party.

If you don't hear your message, it might be that the headset or the controller are defective.
