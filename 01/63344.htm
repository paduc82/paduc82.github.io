Hi,

I have successfully removed this virus today from XP Home. I would expect the same procedure to work on Vista but cannot guarantee this. The latest definitions for both MSE and MBAM were ineffective (MSE found it but could not get rid of it, MBAM just did not find it) but I was able to clean with SUPERAntiSpyware. However, I do this sort of thing for a living and what I did may not be possible for you...

  • Getting any browser to download anything that might be of use proved impossible so I removed the hard disk and connected it to one of my PCs (that I use solely for occasions like this), so that its boot partition just became a data partition in my PC.
  • I ran SAS full scan on the boot partition (after making sure it was up to date) and it found and removed several items.
  • I replaced the disk in the original PC and the PC booted OK, MSE only found sirefef in restore points so I turned off system restore and then turned it back on again to make sure that all remaining instances of sirefef would be removed.
  • Internet connectivity could not be established, this was becase SAS removed the file netbt.sys as part of its disinfection.
  • I restored this file to C:\Windows\System32\Drivers from C:\Windows\System32\dllcache and internet connectivity returned. This bit will be different on Vista.
  • I removed MSE, downloaded and installed SAS and did a full scan which found no furt

    I hope this helps. If you have any questions about any step please post back.

    It may be possible for you to download SAS at another PC and install it to your PC as an alternative to removing the hard disk and inserting in another PC. Do not attempt to transfer the hard disk to another PC yourself unless you are comfortable doing this. If you do decide to do this you should shutdown both PCs and disconnect at the mains socket and also take precautions to prevent static electricity damage.

    Although my PC was unaffected by this, it does not mean that it will be OK for you - there may be variants of sirefef out there or there may be other malware besides sirefef on your disk.

    By 'WiFi password' do you mean the password for your WiFi network?

    That's a 2-step procedure:

    1- Get into your router's administration console and change the password there. (Don't forget to click or tap the Apply or OK button.) Going forward, every device that wants to join your WiFi network has to supply that password.

    2- (There's more than one way to do this:) Right-click on the network icon in your system tray (which probably has a red 'X' on it now) and choose Open Network and Internet settings.

    • Under 'Change your network settings,' choose 'Change adapter options'
    • Right-click on the icon for your WiFi adapter and choose Properties. That's where you enter the new password
    • Once you've done that, wait for a minute or so. The red 'X' will disappear from your system tray and your computer is back on the network.

    Don't forget to change the password on every other device on your network, like printers and phones.
