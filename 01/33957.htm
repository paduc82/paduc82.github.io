What the console says is correct. 

Your SON bought BO4 with his account. YOUR ACCOUNT does NOT own BO4. 

YOUR SON can sign in on the console YOU want to play on, and set that console as his Home Console, so HIS ACCOUNT can share BO4 to THAT console he sets as his Home Console so you and anyone else playing on that console can play BO4 that your son owns. 

And the other way around: YOUR account has the Gold membership. 

YOU will have to sign in on your SON'S console with YOUR account, and set that as YOUR Home Console. That way you will share the Gold membership from your account to your Son's console, and he (as well as anyone else playing on your son's console) will benefit from your Gold membership. 

It's really quite simple if you just remember this: 

ONLY ACCOUNTS own games and memberships. Accounts automatically take their game and subscriptions with them, anywhere where they are signed in online, on ANY console in the world. 

ACCOUNTS can allow ONE CONSOLE to share that "OWNER ACCOUNT'S" games and memberships to anyone playing on THAT CONSOLE. 

Consoles don't own anything. 

Maybe you mistyped it in your original post, but for me it read as if you were thinking that games and memberships are owned by "consoles", and by setting home consoles all around your house, the consoles will share among each other, which is NOT the case. 

Again:

An account owns stuff. No one else!

The account can allow a console to share this account's stuff to anyone playing on that console. 

As long as you remember these two simple things, the Home Console sharing should be no problem to understand.
