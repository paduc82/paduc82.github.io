a. user say:

Hi CarolChi,
Thank You for bringing up the issue in Microsoft community forum!
As we understand you want to print multiple PDF pages without opening each file.
 
1. What is the make and model number of the printer?
2. How exactly are you trying to drag the PDF files to the printer and print them?
 
Here are a few steps which you can follow:
 
Method 1:
To enable drag and drop printing, all you have to do is create a shortcut to the printer on your desktop
a. Right click on a empty area on desktop, and click on New and Shortcut.
b. Copy paste the below code into the location area, and click on the Next button.
Code: rundll32.exe shell32.dll,SHHelpShortcuts_RunDLL PrintersFolder
c. Type Printers for the name, and click on the Finish button.
NOTE: You can name this anything you would like though.
d. Right click on the new Printers shortcut on the Desktop and click on Properties.
e. Click on the Shortcut tab, then click on the Change Icon button.
f. In the line under "Look for icons in this file", type in the code below and press Enter.
Code: C:\Windows\System32\imageres.dll
g. Select the icon highlighted in blue above and click on OK. NOTE: You can use any icon that you would like instead though.
h. Click on OK.
i. Move the shortcut to where you like for easy use. You can then just drag files over the printer and they will be printed
 
Method 2:
You can also use your own favorite search engine and find an application that meets the printing requirements.
