You have at least two different contacts lists in Windows Live Mail:

 

  • When you're Home tab of the ribbon reads Sign in and the contacts list you see is the default list.
     
  • When you are signed in with a Microsoft Account, the right-most button on the Home tab of the ribbon reads something other than Sign in and the contacts list you see is the one that belongs to the account you're signed in with. The list synchronizes with the one at contacts.live.com. Any change you make to this list in Windows Live Mail will propagate to the online list and vice versa.
     
  • Use the button to change your signed-in state and hence the contacts list you see. If you sign in with a different Microsoft Account, you'll see a different contacts list.
     
  • There is no connection between the different contacts lists you see in Windows Live Mail.
     

As you will by now have noticed, removing a mail account and adding it back again will have absolutely no effect on the contacts list. All it will achieve is permanently to delete all the account folders and all the messages in them. This could well be a costly exercise requiring a re-download of messages from the server and in the worst case it could lead to irrevocable data loss, so it's not to be undertaken lightly - especially when it won't help with the problem you're facing.

Repairing the installation is not likely to help, either; all this does is check that all the program files are present and correct. There is no evidence of anything wrong with your program files. Your problem seems to arise solely from confusion about the purpose of signing in.
