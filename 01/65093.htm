Why doesnâ€™t the battery charge when power level is above 95% with AC adapter connected?
 
This is a normal condition for battery protection, and there is no problem in either battery itself or charging function.
When battery is charged up to 100% , the notebook will automatically stop charging. At this time, battery will self-discharge very slowly owing to the chemical characteristics of Li-on battery even though the notebook doesnâ€™t consume any battery power.
The current design of ASUS Notebook comes with built-in RSOC (Reconfigurable SOC) and the purpose is to prolong the lifespan of the overall battery.  Below description explain this phenomenon:
1. When the battery capacity is below 95% with AC adapter connected, the battery would be charged to 100% then stop charging.
2. When the battery capacity drops from 100% down to 99%~95% with AC adapter connected, the battery would not charge until power level drops below 95%.
3. When the battery capacity remained around 94% to 97%, battery would not charge with AC adapter connected. You can unplug and re-connect the adapter for several times to resume charging to 100%.

a. user say:

The AskSBAR is malware.

The Ask Toolbar is a free browser add-on that allows you to search the Web using the Ask.com search engine directly from Internet Explorer or Firefox browsers.

In the not to distant past (~05/2009), the Ask Toolbar was problematic in that it would conistently delete the following files from your system:

c:\boot.ini

autoexec.bat

config.sys

all folders that start with "@" in its name in the boot drive "C:"

Many a c:\boot.ini was deleted by the Ask Toolbar on the next reboot after the installation of same.  Therefore, I do not and will not ever trust it.

I think the Applian Technologies authors fixed it, but I have not installed it in a while to find out.  Some third party applications you install have a check box to see if you would like to install the Ask Toolbar during their installation.  I would say Noooooooooooo...

I also do not have much faith in McAfee, but at least it seems to have picked up something.  

Since you have had an attack, I would follow up with this and see if McAfee missed anything:

 

Download, install, update and do a full scan with these free malware detection programs then resolve any remaining issues:
