andre da costa say:

With Windows 8, Microsoft has changed from stickers that have the product key that the user has to type in when installing the operating system to new BIOS embedded product keys. The idea is that by eliminating the sticker, you eliminate one of the easier ways for nefarious users to get a legitimate product key. Eliminating the product key sticker also removes any worry that the sticker might get damaged while at the same time eliminating the long and irritating process of typing in various letters and numbers when installing the operating system.

 

If the user has to reinstall the operating system on a machine that came with Windows 8, the installation process automatically grabs the software product key from the motherboard BIOS with no input from the user. This means that those familiar Windows product key stickers will no longer appear on the Windows 8 computers.

internet expert say:

Do you have "stick drift"? This would happen in other games with a movable camera. Oftentimes, it's a subtle, slow movement that moves in a certain direction, even when you aren't touching the thumbsticks. If this is the case, then it would mean that the joystick component in your controller is internally damaged. A possible workaround would be to widen/raise the "dead zone" of your affected joystick, if the option is available in-game.

How do I transfer my music from my HTC Inspire 4G to my laptop? - Microsoft Community

charles lee jr say:

How do I transfer my music from my HTC Inspire 4G to my laptop?

I want to transfer my music from my HTC Inspire 4G cell phone to my laptop and save the music on my laptop and keep it on my phone also. I am not computer savvy, but I want to learn how to do these sort of things. Can someone please help me?
thrashzone say:

Import from cell phone/ sd card or camera,

Click your start button, and click on Computer,

Insert your device, unlock the device if a password is presently locking it, (power on)

Once windows shows the device and loads it's Driver,could be E-F....Removable disk.

Right click the device, (close autoplay if it opens) or you can use autoplay but select Open and View with Windows Explorer.

Click on Open, explore or expand, Open is likely to be an option,

Click on the devices dcim folder until you see Pictures/ videos/ Music, and click on it.(cell phone only)

Scroll the list and select the files you want, (if you can't see images, on the top right there is a symbol to change the view of the page from a generic list to Icons or thumbnails)

Hold the ctrl key and left click them one at a time, or select all ctrl+A

Right click and select Copy,

Open My Pictures or in this case My Music and either make a New folder or right click an existing folder and select Paste, or ctrl+V

Go to the action center on the bottom right and click on the triangle symbol, and right click the safe to remove device Icon and select eject or remove. or right click the drive letter again and select remove/ eject.

What helped me is to uninstall ICloud (unfortunately). 

Ctr+alt+del, then 'task manager', then stop all iCloud services, flickering stops, then uninstall iCloud. 

theblackpup say:

Well, after speaking to three other techs at Microsoft, at the fourth attempt I finally got through to someone who was very helpful. Gualberto, Microsoft Answer Tech, wherever you are, I can't thank you enough for spending over an hour on my computer trying to make it work. I now have Windows 8.1 back and am very happy!

For those of you who want to try uninstalling your update, if you can manage to get the task manager up, then you can do what Gualberto showed me and click file then run new task. Type msconfig in the box (if your screen is refreshing/flickering/blinking like mine was you'll need the patience of a saint for typing...). Hit enter and another window should open headed system configuration. From there you'll see tabs headed General and Boot which will enable you to restart your computer in safe mode. Once your computer is in safe mode (hopefully the screen will have stopped flashing by this point) you can then go to your start menu and settings and find your previous version of windows under the back-up and recovery options. (Forgive me if I haven't remembered all the steps in detail.)
