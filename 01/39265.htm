lemp say:
I assume that you do NOT mean that you have both printer and computer plugged into the same power strip and that both come on when you turn the power strip on.

As near as I can determine from the rather thin material in the User Guide for the Dell P713w, you probably have activated "Eco-Mode" and/or the "Power Saver" mode.  See page 19 of your User Guide (if you don't have it, get it here: http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/systems/PRN_P713w/en/index.htm).

When this feature is activated, the printer goes into a low-power mode when it has been idle for a set amount of time.  When the computer is turned off, the printer is by definition idle, and so will go into low-power shortly after the computer is turned off.  When the computer is turned on, it detects the presence of the printer and connects to the printer.  Apparently, the printer interprets this as "not-idle" and comes out of its low-power mode, which appears as if it is "turning on."

In addition, this printer performs an automatic maintenance cycle every week if it is plugged into an electrical outlet. It will briefly come out of Power Saver mode or will temporarily power on if it is turned off.

Does at least one of the printer's lights pulse on and off when you believe the printer is turned off?  If so, it is in Power Saver mode.

Of course, I could be totally wrong, but it seems to me that if you actually turn the printer off by pressing the Power On/Off button on the screen, it shouldn't be able to turn on automatically (except for that once/week maintenance cycle).  From the manual:

Note: When the printer is on, press [power button] to switch to Power Saver mode. Press and hold [power button] for three seconds to turn off the printer.
