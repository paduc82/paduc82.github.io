Hi again, Tom!

How can I find out if I have the latest version and/or update for Windows Live Mail?


With difficulty.

First, find out which version you have. In WLMail, press Alt-H A. If this doesn't show you the version and build numbers, press Escape twice, then Alt-FB.

Then compare with the version/build numbers on this page: Windows Live Essentials 2011 Application Version Numbers

For the past eighteen months, WLSetup has been calling home twice a day to see whether your version of WLEssentials is current. For those who didn't update to 15.4.3555.0308, this check prompted a semi-automatic update which has caused severe problems for many users.

Incidentally, the four groups of digits mean

15 Major version
4 Milestone within Major Version
3555 Build no.
0308 Date the release was compiled for RTW (Release to Web)
In this case, 8 March 2012

What is the latest version?


For Vista/W7: 15.4.3555.0308
For XP: 14.0.8117.0416

 

Has there ever been any updates specific to Windows Live Mail?


Not that I remember in recent years (i.e. since the invention of Windows Live Essentials in 2008)

Is a replacement for Windows Live Mail forthcoming?


Probably not for OSs up to and including W7. The Mail app for W8 may eventually include some more of WLMail's functionality than it does at present. I'd be happy to be proved wrong.

barb bowman say:

Most customers report having difficulty in situations like this. The North American hardware is different than UK/Europe. They don't have North American devices in the UK/European service centers. WiFi supported channels are different. Customers have found that in most cases they've needed to send their device to someone in the country in which it was purchased to get a support case opened. Those who have persuaded an agent to exchange (these are never fixed, they are always replaced with refurbs) receive a replacement that is from devices sold in the country/region where the service center is located.

USA Surface tablets won't be able to use 2.4 GHz channels above 11, which has been problematic for some traveling abroad. There are similar issues with 5GHz channels.

jokbaskubilius say: Are you implying i would have trouble connecting to the internet outside the US? Any chance you know if this is a problem only with the surface devices or with all laptops and tablets sold in the US?

barb bowman say:
I'm telling you that on the 2.4GHz side that Surface tablets purchased in North America have not connected to channels 12 and above which are in use elsewhere and that there are similar issues with 5GHz. Other vendors may allow drivers to be downloaded specific to the country you are in that allow connections. Or have a setting that enables you to switch WiFi regions. It's called 802.11d (yes "D") - so depending on what channel a router is set to in any particular country..
