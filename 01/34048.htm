For me this problem was caused by the Driver Booster 2 program.  It occurs mainly when you have more

than one version of Driver Booster 2 installed.  When you do an initial scan for outdated drivers, Driver

Booster 2 marks your outdated drivers with exclamation points.  It may also change the program shortcut

on the desktop to include an exclamation point.  The way to remove it is to do scans with all of the installed

versions of the program and update the drivers.  Otherwise, the programs should be uninstalled.

Either way the exclamation point will go away after the scans have been updated with all installed versions

or the programs have been uninstalled.  This triangle is caused specifically by the Driver Booster 2 program

and has nothing to do with the Windows operating system itself.
