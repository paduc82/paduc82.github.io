Step 1: How to identify the services behind a Service Host (svchost.exe) process?

 

a.    Right click on a blank space on Taskbar and click on Task Manager to open up the Task Manager.

b.    Click on Processes tab.

c.    Click on Show processes from all users button.

d.    Click Continue button when prompted with User Account Control dialog.

There will be a long list of svchost.exe shown in elevated Task Manager.

e.    Locate the svchost.exe that you want to find out what’s the services running behind it, such as those with high CPU usage or high memory usage, right click on it, and choose Go to Service(s).

f.     The focus tab will be switched and transferred over to the Services tab, with a few services been selected and highlighted. These are the services that are associated with the particular svchost.exe.

g.    Once identify the services that are possibly causing the svchost.exe to hogging system resources, carefully work out the services that you suspect may cause the issue. Once determined a suspected service, right click on it, and select Stop Service. Before you stopping any service, make sure that no other services rely on that particular services.

 

If you find these steps too troublesome to locate associated services for svchost.exe, then download Process Explorer.

Step 2: Perform an online virus scan

 

Sometimes malware, spyware, adware, Trojan or viruses commonly hide behind the mask of svchost.exe consuming all the system resources. So, I would suggest you to perform a complete virus scan on your computer. Visit the link below for the same.
