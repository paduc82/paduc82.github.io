tryx3 say:

Katrina,

I made an assumption from your question that Canadian tech has not made.

If the computer power ran out at a very fortunate moment [almost certainly only possible right at the start of the process] then it might be the case that the limited set of repairs available on the System repair disk can cope.  But, you can try using the System repair disk as it cannot do any harm.

However, I think it highly unlikely that this happy circumstance will be the case.  Factory restoration is a procedure during which a special utility overwrites the whole disk from a highly compressed bit-by-bit "image" of it.  It does not overwrite one file then the next file & so on.  It overwrites sector 1 of the disk with what sector 1 ought to be then sector 2 with what sector 2 ought to be, ... .  So there is really no moment when the process can be interrupted with anything that resembles a functioning operating system.

That's why I recommend contacting your computer maker or buying Amazon reinstallation disks.  And do not forget to check that you can read the product key on your Certificate of authenticity [many computers have it fixed in such a position that normal usage can result in it being rubbed against until the product key becomes unreadable].

Denis
