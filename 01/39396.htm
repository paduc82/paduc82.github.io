a. user say:

Does formatting and deleting hard disk partitions more often or more frequently degrades its performance? I have Western Digital Blue HDD with 320GB space and Windows 7 SP1 installed.

Formatting and deleting partitions doesn't actually delete data. It only assigns the data to free space. If you want to actually delete data you need to wipe a drive by overwriting it (HDD) or flushing it (SSD) depending on the technology. See here for details:

http://dellwindowsreinstallationguide.com/cleaning-up-a-drive-format-vs-secure-wipe-ssd-and-hdd/ 

Be careful of the differences between a SSD and HDD with this regard. SSDs have limited read/write cycles and the way they assign data is different so conventional means of wiping a HDD don't work correctly for a SSD and should not be carried out.

You can format, delete and wipe (if you use the correct method on the HDD/SSD) as frequently as you want to. I can say that my machines undergo clean installations of Windows much more frequently sometimes up to 5 a day depending on whether I am doing any testing or writing a guide. Today I've actually installed Windows 10147 3 times and Windows 10130 2 times. I am reinstalling Windows 10147 again for another test.

However always expect a HDD or SSD to fail and back up your data to an external hard drive or cloud storage such as OneDrive regularly. This means you are prepared for hardware failure or of a software fault. In reality your drive may not fail for years but if it does its better to be safe than sorry.
