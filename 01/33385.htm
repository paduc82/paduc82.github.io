The Xbox will use whatever connection is provided to it. The Xbox cannot improve the connection that it's getting from your router which is what manages and controls the data to a from the Xbox. If this were a specific Xbox issue, there'd be more posts on this.


The best gaming experience will be with a wired connection, not wireless.


Here are some steps to try but you may need to see about resetting your router back to defaults or connecting the xbox directly to your modem with a wired connection.


Hard reset the Xbox by holding down the power button on the front of the Xbox for 15 seconds. After the 15 seconds let go of the power button then restart the Xbox.

Try out your games.


Power cycle your network by unplugging the power to the modem & router for about 60 seconds. Then plug your router back in and try your game again.

Go to Settings > Network Settings > Advanced Settings > Alternate MAC Address > Clear > let the Xbox Restart?

Try out your games again.


If that doesn't work, I'm guessing you're using a wireless connection to your router.

On your Xbox go to Settings > Network > Network Settings > Forget Wireless Network

Go to Advanced settings > DNS Settings > Manual - Set the Primary DNS as 8.8.8.8 and the Secondary as 8.8.4.4

Go to Settings > Network Settings > Advanced Settings > Alternate MAC Address > Clear > let the Xbox Restart

After the Xbox Restarts go back and reconnect to your Wireless Network and try your game again.

Try out your games again.


If that fails, consider resetting your router back to defaults.
