First.... Nothing to do with 32bit 'legacy' programs. The vast majority of PC games are 32bit, even many (most) recent ones.

The one 'legacy' issue the game would have has to do with Direct Play, which I assume you already have enabled as games

that need it don't generally load if it's not enabled.

"..., eventually, but it is just super slow."

The definition of unplayable.

"I think the problem might be with the DirectX compatibility"

- Win10 has DirectX9,10&11 as well as a couple of mostly unused (only a few newer games use DX12) DX12 files.

Your game is DX7, but DX7&8 will run on Win10. The fact that the game runs at all indicates the problem is not due to DX.

- Yes, your 610m is bottom of that series, but it should have no problem running a game with basic graphics such as this.

What could hang it up is there are no dedicated Win10 drivers on the ASUS download page for your machine so the Nvidia 610

may not be performing as well as it could on Win7/8.1 which ASUS does have drivers for (even though they are five years old).

The Win10 driver from Nvidia may or may not work well, but it's worth a try, if you have not already installed a driver from Nvidia.

The generic driver supplied by Windows Updates would be the least desirable last resort driver for your Nvidia 610.

(final release for this series - March 2018)   NVIDIA Driver Downloads - Advanced Search

- It looks like the original game in the series was released in 2002. While most games from 2002 will run on Win10 some have their

own peculiarities, so you probably need to go back to dedicated Catlan sites to try and find fixes for your version of the game.
