a. user say:

Sleep, Deep Sleep, etc. are just descriptive names. One person's Sleep is another person's Hibernate. Don't let the names confuse you.

Power saving states are actually well-defined once you get past the names, and they run from S1 to S4. They are described by Microsoft here.

S0 means full power - no power saving in effect.

S1 to S4 are the power saving states that are described in the post above.

S5 means no power - what happens when you power off your computer.

S6 means no power and the power cord is removed from the wall socket or the battery is disconnected. I'm not sure if S6 is in official use but it's used to distinguish a computer that's not connected to any power source.
