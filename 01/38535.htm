    Re-installing Windows 11 times is 10 times too many. A far superior method goes like this:

    1. Install Windows.
    2. Install all software.
    3. Install all automatic updates.
    4. Tune the system exactly to your liking.
    5. Wait a week.
    6. Create an image of drive C:. Store it on an external hard disk.

    Next time you wish to nuke your installation, use the image you created in Step 6. It will take you a small fraction of the time needed to go through Steps 1 . . 4.

    About your questions: A new installation will completely replace the various partitions you have. You should leave them as they are. You should also leave the folders under C:\Windows as they are. The "duplicated" files you see refer to different updates and to different CPUs (Intels vs. AMD). You could, of course, delete some of them after doing some research on the net but the benefit would be marginal. If you are tight for disk space, get a larger disk. A 500 GByte disk costs less than $60!
