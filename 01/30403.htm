danielmedrano say:

Hi Shazam

The way the Xbox Family option works is one person has the Master Account (I assume that would be you) and the other 3 would be treated as child accounts.

A.) The Family Option can only work on accounts that are on the current system at sign up. If you would like to include your brother you would have to download his account on to your console

B.) The only abilities to my knowledge that you lose is the ability to purchase Microsoft Points with a credit card. Pre-Paid cards will still work. The way The Family Option works is the Master Account purchases Microsoft Points and can then distribute them throughout the other 3 accounts.

C.) Purchases are not relevant to the Account Status as it is related to the Console DRM System. It will always work on your console, and it will work on any other console providing the account that bought it is there. That being said, Any content bought on your console will work for any account.

C. Licensing of DLC will work the same way. It's licensed to the gamertag that purchased it and the console it was purchased on. Other gamertags on THAT console would be able to use it. Other gamertags on other consoles cannot use that content because they did not purchase it, nor was the content purchased on the consoles they're using.

D. The only thing is that either yours or your brother's account will have restrictions, whichever one ends up being a secondary account. A secondary can't download trailers or demos that are Mature rated. Other content should be fine. And that account cannot make purchases with a credit card.
