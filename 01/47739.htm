Defragmenting SSD - Microsoft Community

nikos lagios say:

Defragmenting SSD

Hello everyone! Should I use the Windows 10 built-in Disk Defragmentation to defragment my SSD? Some internet sources suggest that SSDs should never be defragmented.
bill.p. say: Hi Nikos, I'm Graeme, an independent adviser here to help.

Defragmenting HDDs of the past was done so that files weren't broken up across the surface of the disk, making the mechanical arm that reads the files able to access them faster.

SSDs are an entirely different technology that doesn't have any mechanical parts, so defragmenting an SSD will have absolutely no performance increase. In fact, it's not recommended as SSDs have a limited number of access cycles before they begin to wear out, so defragmenting an SSD will shorten its lifespan for no reason.

You can happily keep using the SSD without needing to defragment it! Let me know if you need further support. Thanks!

If you've already adjusted the volume and boost for your microphone and it's still too quiet, it could be a driver issue.
