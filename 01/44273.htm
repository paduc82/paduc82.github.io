mitch h. say:
Hi Peter,


We understand that you're having problems with your wireless connection.

Let's see the difference between the 2.4Ghz and 5Ghz WiFi bands.

The longer waves used by the 2.4Ghz band are better suited to longer ranges and transmission through walls and solid objects. It is better if you need better range on your devices or you have a lot of walls or other objects in the areas where you need coverage. While the 5Ghz band is less congested whereas you will likely get more stable connections, however, the shorter waves used by the 5Ghz band makes it less able to penetrate walls and solid objects. It also has a shorter effective range than the 2.4Ghz band.

That being said, if you have walls and solid objects around you from the router, it is most likely the reason why you're experiencing the issue.

If Windows 10 fails to boot normally three times over, the fourth time it enters by default in an Automatic Repair mode. Using this mode, you can boot into Safe Mode. To trigger the Automatic Repair mode, you must interrupt the normal boot process three consecutive times: use the Reset or the Power button on your Windows 10 PC to stop it during boot, before it finishes loading Windows 10. If you use the Power button, you might have to keep it pressed for at least 4 seconds to force the power off. When Windows 10 enters the Automatic Repair mode, the first thing you see is a screen that tells you that the operating system is "Preparing Automatic Repair."
