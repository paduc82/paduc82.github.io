a. user say:

Are you possibly using a Microsoft account as your user account? Microsoft accounts are named in C:\Users with a shortened version of your Microsoft account email address. (The part before the '@'.)

I suppose Microsoft did this because there's no need to use the entire email address for the user folder name, or maybe the '@' symbol would cause confusion. In any case, the name of your user folder in C:\Users is merely an internal identifier - not something you show other people - so it shouldn't really matter what it's called.

That said, you can rename your user folder, but keep in mind that it's only a cosmetic change. Inside Windows you will continue to be known as AMARE.

Unsolicited advice #1:

For security, you really should be doing your day to day computing in a Standard account. You should only sign into your Administrator account for tasks that need an Administrator account.

Many people would agree with you that Windows 10 was rushed out the door. Over time, however, it is being rapidly updated and now it's a more competent OS than it was before. More changes are coming.
