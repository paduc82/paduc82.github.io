What is the differences between 'Bad Sectors' and 'Bad Blocks' on HDD?

What is the differences between 'Bad Sectors' and 'Bad Blocks' on HDD?

bossdweeb say:

The terms are confused, and confusing, and often used interchangeably.

- Block instead of Sector is common when referring to an SSD because SSD's use cells vs a platter.

When cells die in an SSD the data is moved to another cell, which is one reason they suggest keeping

20-25%  free space on an SSD.

- Then there is the concept of the data being in Sectors, which may be repairable,  vs physical Blocks,

which means damaged platter, and not repairable, but with possibility of data being moved to another block.

- With either type of error on an HDD it may be time to think about getting a new HDD.

The article below may provide some more clarity -

http://www.sevenforums.com/hardware-devices/344327-bad-sector-false-positives-bad-blocks-caused-write-errors.html
