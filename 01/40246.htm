I was able to resolve the issue. Lenovo has a utility called Lenovo Vantage ( Formerly called Lenovo Settings). This utility has the ability to adjust certain hardware settings, including it's own camera privacy setting. This overrides windows camera settings, and unless you open the Lenovo Vantage tool, there's no indication that it's been set.

Lenovo Vantage received an update recently, which coincides with the start of this issue. when the software was updated, the Lenovo camera privacy setting was reset to the default (no apps are allowed to access the camera). I understand that you want to move the audio files from your USB recorder device drive to your Windows 8 computer. You may follow the steps mentioned below.

Note: Follow the steps from Step 5 mentioned below on how to move the audio files to your Windows 8 computer.

If you wish to copy the audio files from a source computer and transfer it to a USB recorder device and then upload the same to a different computer, please follow the steps:

Step 1:

Insert your USB recorder device into the source computer's USB port. If this is the first time using the drive on your computer, Windows will take a few seconds to automatically install drivers.

Step 2:

Click "Open Folder to View Files" in the AutoPlay window to open Windows Explorer with a view of the flash drive's contents. This makes identifying its drive letter easy. Alternatively, press Windows Key + E and under Devices and drivers the USB recorder device drive will be listed under "Removable Storage."

Step 3:

Locate the files you wish to transfer in Windows Explorer. Drag and drop these files onto the USB recorder device drive. By default, this copies the files, so the originals still reside on your hard drive.

Step 4:

Right-click the USB recorder device and select "Eject" after copying is completed.

Step 5:

Remove the USB recorder device and insert it into the destination computer's USB port.

Step 6:

Open Windows Explorer again via the AutoPlay window or going to the following File Explorer Windows Key + E and under Devices and drivers.

Step 7:

Locate the files you previously copied on the USB recorder device, right-click the files and drag them to the destination computer's hard drive. When you release the mouse button, select "Move Here" This will copy the files to the hard drive and delete the copies from the USB recorder device.

Step 8:

Right-click the USB recorder device drive and select "Eject" after copying is completed. You can then safely remove the USB recorder device.
