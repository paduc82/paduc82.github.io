I installed Windows 7 on my laptop and downloaded the new driver for my Canon BJC 80 printer.  The computer seems to recognize the printer but it doesn't print.  I don't understand why my printer is listed as the default printer and appears to be properly installed but doesn't print.  My printer previously worked without problems with XP and Vista.  Is there anything I can do to get the printer to work?  Or is my printer not going to work with Windows 7?  Thanks.
efkelley say: Just got Canon BJC-80 working on Windows 7 Sony Vaio Laptop:  USB-Parallel interface didn't work. Purchased and installed StarTech 1 Port PCI Express (x1) Base Parallel ExpressCard ($70+) that supports bi-directional hardware. Removed previous BJC-80 installation and reinstalled printer on LPT3 (was on LPT1). Worked perfectly. Hope you have an ExpressCard interface to use; if not, perhaps there are other bi-directional solutions that offer more robust LPTx port handling. Found this solution from amateur astronomers who were trying to get older CCD cameras to work through the printer port. They found the USB-Parallel interface to be incomplete and needed bi-directional handling. Nice to have this little printer with me on travel now. Hope this helps.
