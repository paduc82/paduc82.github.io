Above you indicate that "the credit card page is set to the US", which implies that the credit card information for your own Microsoft account and so that account itself may have been created in the US.

The following is the description of the purpose behind this charge as it relates to US customers.

Why does Microsoft charge me when I create an account for my child?

"Microsoft and Family Safety comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) regarding online account creation for children under the age of 13. To verify that an adult is giving a child permission to create a new Microsoft account, COPPA requires a small amount be charged to the adult's credit card. We charge 50 cents for this verification. This charge isn't refundable."

However, searching a bit more I discovered the following articles at Microsoft and a 3rd-party lawyer's site discussing the  General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), which is a European privacy law affecting those in the EU.

The EU To Adopt COPPA Framework in 2016

Microsoft Trust Center  General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

Though the articles themselves touch on different elements of the GDPR and indicate different dates (2016 and 2018) when it takes effect, The Article 8 section discussed at the first URL above covers the GDPR equivalent to the US COPPA regulations.

So I'd assume that Microsoft has simply started performing the same age verification in the EU as in the US in order to be in compliance with the GDPR once it truly takes effect.

As for your specific problem, I'd first try to confirm that your own Microsoft account is truly set up properly in the country in which you reside.  Then I'd make certain all of your Windows operating system language, regional and time zone settings are appropriate for that location as well, since both Windows and the Microsoft Account system are highly sensitive to these.

If all of this is correct, I'd confirm that the child's account is correctly set up in that same location.

If all of this seems correct, but the credit card payments page still switches to the US, try manually changing this to the correct language-country combination (for example: en-gb for UK) and see if it holds, since this is typically selected based on the Windows settings I mentioned above.

The reason it's difficult to find a proper forum to discuss this is that the Microsoft account information is spread across the MS account help page, as well as the individual product support for Windows and each of the individual applications and products like Email, OneDrive, Skype, Windows Store and others that require their use.

Remember, it's your original adult account that the prompt is trying to verify, while the action of creating the child's account is simply what's generating the request.
