cmdrkeene say:

Those are just design principles.  Windows 8 was called "Metro" because it has big plain and simple icons that look like the icons you would see in a metro train station, bus station, or airport to guide your way.  It used the design principles of Swiss classic design.

Windows Vista and 7 used the Aero design principle, which was supposed to be Authentic, Energetic, Reflective, and Open.   That's why it used simple and large fonts, lots of white space, and was "authentically digital", for whatever that means in marketing mumbo-jumbo.

Windows XP's graphics were the same as Windows 95 up to 2000.  They changed the colors and rounded the edges, but they did not have a massive marketing push on a new design, and it had no specific name.
