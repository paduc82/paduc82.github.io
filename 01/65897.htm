You can use the Disk Management tool (diskmgmt.msc) to add, remove, shrink, extend, or delete a volume. You need to be signed in as the administrator in order to use this tool.

  1. Click the Start button.
  2. Type diskmgmt.msc and then hit Enter.
  3. Select your local disk (C:).
  4. If you're planning to add a secondary partition in the future, you'll need to shrink the current volume. Right-click your local disk (C:) and then select Shrink Volume.
  5. Enter the desired amount of space to shrink and then click Shrink.
  6. A new Unallocated volume will show up in the Disk Management tool.
  7. Right-click the Unallocated volume and select New Simple Volume.
  8. Follow the on-screen instructions, set the desired volume size and assign the drive letter.
  9. Leave the Format Partition to default.
  10. Once done, Windows 10 will detect the new drive.
  11. Click Format disk. Enter desired Volume label and then click Start > OK.
