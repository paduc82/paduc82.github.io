rey g. santos say:

This usually happens on front loading printers usually those all-in-one printers, where the paper loads from the front of the printer and exits to the front. The problem starts as the printer gets older, accumulates dirt on the rubber rollers or the sometimes the roller becomes slippery as it hardened due to age. That's why I prefer those back loading printers where the paper easillly travel almost in a straight path from the back to the front (exit) unlike a front loading printer where the paper would have to navigate a 180 degrees turn inside the printer.

You can try cleaning the rollers or replace the rollers.
