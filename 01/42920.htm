I want to change the sound that is played when Outlook 2016 receives a new e-mail.   I can change the sounds in Windows 10 sounds settings, e.g. "New Mail Notification".  I have tried a dozen different ones of these, any one that could be even remotely relevant.  But to no avail.  Windows 10 still plays "pling" when a new mail arrives.

Anyone who knows how to configure this?

finn a say:

The link points to an article that proposes the same steps that the previous response also suggests.  As I already responded, I already did all this.  It has no effect.

However, meanwhile I have found the cause of the problem   Windows 10 has a setting to control if it should "get notifications from these senders".  This setting was set to on for "Outlook 2016".   As long as this setting is on, Windows 10 seems to override whatever settings Outlook has (including the "new mail notification" sound), "eat" the notification and always play its own Windows notification sound.

Turn off the "Outlook 2016" in the Windows notifications from these senders settings, and the "New mail notification" sound will be played as expected.
