You can shine a bright light on the label or take a digital photo and blow it up.  Or if only one or two of the 25 characters are not legible, you can use trial and error.  Not all 36 letters and numbers are used:

The 24 used are:
2346789BCDFGHJKMPQRTVWXY

The 12 unused are:
015AEILNOSUZ

If there is a COA sticker on your laptop, it came with Windows pre-installed.  Most computer manufacturers that pre-install Windows provide a means to restore the computer to ex-factory condition by using a hidden partition on the system's hard drive.  Doing this does not require the use of a product key.

If you can't use this built-in mechanism, your computer's manufacturer may  -- but probably will not -- be able to provide you with a set of "recovery disks" that you can use to reinstall Windows.  You should not need the product key in connection with using these disks.

ackis say:

In the top left corner, there's three horizontal lines with Menu under them. Click that, and go to game options at the top. Scroll down to the bottom of the options and you should see choices for "Remember Deck Difficulty" - just change that to Off.

Then I think you need to play another game, and then go back and change that to Yes - assuming you want it to default to Expert.  Not 100% sure about this part though.

mistress sara say:

Hi there,

I can confirm what Ackis said to turn off "Remember Deck Difficulty". Launch a new game,  then select the difficulty you want.

If you want to stick with that difficulty moving forward each time, go back into the menu and turn "Remember deck difficulty" back on. The game will launch at that difficultly the next time you play. If you want to choose the difficulty each time, leave it off.
