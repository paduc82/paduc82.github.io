Windows 10 digital entitlement ( the license for the free upgrade ) is "valid for the lifetime of the device" i.e. is restricted to the computer it was actually installed on. It cannot be transferred to another computer.

As the Windows 7 came preinstalled on the original computer it was most likely an OEM license. As such it too cannot be transferred to another computer. Check the COA label for the description of the license.

Only if your original Windows 7 was a Retail license can that be transferred to the Mac onto parallels and then that installation can be upgraded to Windows 10. The license key for Windows 7 was integrated into the recovery image and a second key would be on the COA label on the computer.

Otherwise you will need to purchase Windows 10 to run on the Mac

jimwynne say:

Why do I need to call someone to reactivate it if someone else said it will automatically pick up my license when I log in with my microsoft account? Everything I've been told conflicts with one-another 

There is confusion here.  I'm assuming that you have a retail license, as opposed to OEM.  If this isn't true, and it is an OEM license, it's not transferable.  Your retail license isn't "associated" with your Microsoft account.  What's there is a record of your purchase.  The confusion stems in part from the "digital entitlement" that was granted with the free Windows 10 upgrade offer.  This has nothing to do with transferring a retail license.  When you reinstall Windows 10 using the retail license key, it might be accepted without a phone call and you'll be done.   The worst thing that can happen is that it won't be accepted and you'll have to make the call.  In other words, the phone call might not be necessary, but the fact that there's a record of purchase in your Microsoft account won't have anything to do with activation.
