Q. If my customer asks me to upgrade a PC with new hardware components, when is a new operating system needed? When would the PC be considered "new"?

A. Generally, an end user can upgrade or replace all of the hardware components on a computer—except the motherboard—and still retain the licence for the original Microsoft OEM operating system software. tangerla say:

I took a look at the manual for this headset. If I am not mistaking, you have the 3.5 stereo jack to Red and White RCA males pluged into the femal sides of your "piggy back" RCA to 3.5 stereo jack that came with your headset. The male ends of the "piggy back" cord can sometimes produce feedback, especially when they come in contact with something else that conducts electricity (i.e. water, moisture and/or radio waves emitted in the air, metal objects, humans, etc.). You can test this by listening to your buzzing sound while touching the "piggy back" connector. If this is the case, I would suggest getting a stereo jack to stereo jack cord to replace it and see if it stops. As far as the sound being controlled by the chat volume wheel...the Xbox One controller puts out both chat volume and game volume through the headphone jack in the contrtoller. The chat converter has a few buttons on it to control this. The two symbols on the left (controller symbol and person symbol) adjusts the volume between chat volume and game volume. If you want to only hear chat, keep pushing the person symbol while listening with your headphones until you hear a beep. That should eliminate the game volume through the controller. The middle button mutes your mic and the other two buttons on the right ( + and - ) control the level of chat volume. These will beep also when max or min volume levels are obtained from pushing the + or the - buttons.

Hope I was able to help.

tangerla say:

Another note on the 3.5 stereo to 3.5 stereo male to male cord...

Make sure that the stereo jack on the TV is the same size as the stereo jack on your headphone "in-line" volume controller. Some headphones use a 2.5mm instead of a 3.5mm. The TV is most likely the 3.5mm size.

Compare the one plugged into the TV with the one plugged into you headset "in-line" controller. If they are the same, you will have no trouble finding a 3.5 to a 3.5 (The jack on the Xbox One controller chat converter is 3.5mm also).

If one is thinner than the other, you will have to find a 3.5mm stereo male jack to a 2.5mm stereo male jack. Where I live, the only place I found one was online (eBay is where I got one).
