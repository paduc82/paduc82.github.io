klokkwork say:

So, a couple things.  Yes, any time you purchase a membership through the Xbox or website, using a payment option, or initiate a free trial, or promotional membership offer, it will be set to automatically renew at the end of that cycle, usually into a full price version of that same membership type (1 Month, 3 Month, 12 Month).  Now, Xbox doesn't want this to come as a surprise, so they lay out these terms on the same page you confirm the purchase.  It's in the opening lines of the paragraph located on that page, in friendly bold text.  Nothing hidden away in fine print.  Be sure to look for them whenever you sign up for anything.

-

Also, the whole expired credit card thing... totally standard practice.  Credit Card companies and banks will often honor a subscription that was set up prior to the replacement or expiration of a debit or credit card.  The subscription is considered grandfathered in, so they'll allow those charges through, but they'll block out any new billing agreements, such as point of purchase charges, or new subscriptions.  Xbox sent you an email stating that they recognized your credit card as having expired, and that you should update to avoid any interruptions that could occur.  They're not talking about interruptions on Xbox's part, but initiated by the bank refusing to process charges on an expired card.  Xbox doesn't know if the bank will allow it to go through, so by default, they give you the warning email.  The system will still try to bill the card, and if the charge goes through, your service continues on without incident.  Xbox will still periodically remind you to update your info.

-

Sorry for any bad experience from this whole situation.  It sounds like you'd called in to request a cancellation, and that didn't happen.  I would definitely recommend contacting Xbox support to address the issue.  If they say they're going to cancel it, then it should cancel.  Also, you can turn auto-renew off anytime you want right from xbox.com/account.  Hope this information is helpful.  Have a great day, and happy gaming!

lotsofcams say:

I tested in 32-bit 7 and 64-bit 7. Using 64-bit 7 is my only option as that is what I have a valid license for, and I have more than 4GB total system memory and am not willing to limit myself.

The manufacturer advertises this camera as Microsoft UVC 1.0 compliant - needs no drivers to download. This ends up being true for all 32-bit Windows operating systems. I have even tried this on 32-bit Windows 10 and the camera/microphone works fine. The problem exists in the supposedly 'Universal' Microsoft UVC driver in 64-bit versions of Windows. Something in it breaks compatibility with many UVC 1.0 devices. They fail to detect as a USB 2.0 device (while the camera's mass storage is detected properly and operates as USB 2.0 in all supporting versions of Windows) and will not send video. The audio works fine, however.

So basically, 2 out of 3 functions (USB Mass Storage and Microphone) work in 64-bit, but the primary function, video, does not. All three functions work in 32-bit.

I ask that you ask your engineers to examine the difference in the usbvideo.sys/.inf/.pnf files between 32 and 64-bit version and see if you can provide a UVC 1.0 fix since support for Windows 7 is still active. Some manufacturers, like laptop manufacturers, are much more easily pressed to issue their own driver, while a company making accessory hardware and solely relying upon built-in standards might be less-inclined to do so, preferring to defer the issue to the company responsible for the standard they are relying upon, which in this case is Microsoft.

This issue affects a lot of hardware, not just some pair of camera glasses. Many laptop users are still without working webcameras because of something having changed in the UVC 1.0 implementation in 64-bit Windows, it is a common problem I come across during my computer repair gigs and probably the only truly annoying one. Most of my customers end up just buying a new webcam instead, some just return the laptop and get one with a functional webcamera. It's pretty bad and it affects all of your 64bit operating systems.
