You'd think so, would you?

When you install any of those applications, several changes can be made to your Automatic Updates (AU) configuration:

  • AU will be reset to the Automatic option (it is wasn't already);

  • Microsoft Update (i.e., "Give me updates for Microsoft products") - versus Windows Update - will become your default update source; and

  • The "Give me recommended updates the same way..." option will be enabled (if it wasn't already).

 

That being said, there are many other reasons your AU settings are changed back:

  • You may have a third-party application installed which disallows the changes you made (e.g., SpyBot Tea Time; Ad-Aware Ad-Watch; various & sundry anti-virus applications/security suites;

  • "Leftovers" from a previously-install (or preinstalled by OEM) Norton and/or McAfee applications (both of which are notorious for not uninstalling (or upgrading) cleanly;

  • Injudicious use of registry cleaners; and/or...

  • On going "hijackware" infection - or worse!
