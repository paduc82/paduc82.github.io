jack mvp say:

Hi

Start from the beginning, get rid of the Bonjour.

Put the laptop near the Router and...

Check the Device Manager for valid Wireless card entry.

http://www.ezlan.net/Win7/net_dm.jpg

If there is No valid entry, delete any Bogus Entry, and re-install the Wireless card's Drivers.

Check the Network connections to make sure that you have a Wireless Network Connection Icon/Entry, and that the Properties of the Icon (right click on the Icon) are correctly configured with TCP/IPv4 protocol in the Network Connections Properties.

http://www.ezlan.net/Win7/net_connection_tcp.jpg

Make sure that if there is a vendor's Wireless Utility the utility is Not Running together with the Windows native Wireless utility.

Make sure that No Firewall is preventing/blocking the Wireless components to get to the Network.

If everything above is OK and it is not connecting to the Wireless router. Log from any computer that is, or can be connected to Wireless Router with a Wire, disable the Wireless Security, make sure that the Wireless broadcast is On, and try to connect with No Wireless security.

When working turn On the Wireless security.

Note* People sometimes make mistakes in writing and retyping the security passphrase.

Alternative way is to copy it from the Router's menu, and then paste when asked by the Wireless card.

a. user say:

Hi,

Connect your iPhone to your PC using the connection cable provided by Apple. Check your iPhone screen now. You might see a notification with Trust option. Tap Trust option to continue. The moment Windows recognizes your iPhone, you will see a notification on the screen asking Tap to choose what happens with this device.

Click or tap the notification to see three options: Import photos and videos, Open device to view files, and Take no action.Click or tap Import photos and videos option. This action will open all photos on your iPhone in Windows 10’s Photos app.

Select photos and videos that you wish to import and then click or tap Import button to start copying all photos from your iPhone to PC. Once Photos app completes the transfer, you’ll see an option to open up the folder to view imported photos. You can also close Photos app if you would like to view photos later.

bryan millet say: Palcouk,

Thank you for your assistance in this matter!

Yes, I did contact the PC maker and the printer hardware makers support site for the software, both are HP.  I also tried installing both the basic printer driver and the full software, but neither attempt would fix the problem.  These attempts did include a complete removal of the software from my system, rebooting and all.  I tried by not connecting the printer until instructed to do so, and I also tried to connect the printer, using the plug-n-play so as to have Windows find the correct drivers, but neither attempt would work.  Those are great ideas!

The solution ended up being a removal of the printer driver for another printer that was previously installed when I was using Vista.  This driver was not compatible with Windows 7 and was causing the "Print Spooler" to stop and to cause any other installation to fail.

The help desk at the university said that they ended up deleting the other printers from the computer system;  then restarting the Print Spooler by clicking on the Start button, searching for "Services", opening the program, scrolling down to "Print Spooler", selecting it and clicking on "Start the service"; and then to install the printer software as normal.  The printer is working just fine now!  Yippee!!
