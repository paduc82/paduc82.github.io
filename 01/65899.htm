a. user say:

Are you certain that there is no Windows 10 driver support for your hardware for version 1803 and later?

You really ought to be sure about this. It's small trouble to find out: just contact the hardware manufacturer's technical support and they'll tell you exactly where you stand.

The truth is, when hardware becomes unsupported, no one really knows whether or not it will continue to work properly. The hardware might continue to work perfectly, or it might not. And if it continues to work properly, it may not after some future update.

This is so because 'unsupported' means that the hardware manufacturer has stopped updating the driver (to keep up with changes to Windows) and has not tested the existing driver with newer versions of Windows.

I've got hardware, like my graphics card, that became unsupported years ago and still works very well with the newest version of Windows 10. On the other hand, my inkjet printer only 'sort of' works with Windows 10, and my original WiFi adapter had to be replaced after it lost manufacturer support.

I have two suggestions:

1- If the hardware manufacturer has their own community forum, as many do, ask your question in that forum. You're likely to meet someone else with the same computer as you have, and you can ask about their experiences with Windows 10. You could benefit from some great advice that will keep your hardware going.

2- You could let your computer update to the newest version of Windows, which for the moment is v.1809, and then check for problems afterward. If you want to try this, be sure to make an image backup of your C: drive before you start. That way you can easily go back in time to the way your computer was before the update.
