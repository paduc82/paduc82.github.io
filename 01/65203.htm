Hi

 

You cannot simply add up all of the space used by folders and files on the C: drive and get an accurate result. Even if you are adding all of the hidden folders and files. This is the wrong way to determine the space being used.

 

In the NTFS File System, there are many super hidden items that can only be seen using special developer tools.

 

The accurate way to determine the space being used and free space is to look at the properties of the C: drive. When you do this, all of these super hidden items are included in those numbers.

 

Right click the C: drive and select Properties. This will show the accurate numbers.

 

There is a good article written by the Microsoft Developers that explains this.

 

Where Did My Space Go? - Ask the Core Team:

http://blogs.technet.com/b/askcore/archive/2013/03/01/where-did-my-space-go.aspx

 

 

Here is the best way to recover much of the space that is being used on the C: drive.


 

1. Press the WinKey+Q to display the

 

2. Type  cleanmgr.exe. In the Results, right click the Cleanmgr.exe and select the Run As Administrator option on the bottom toolbar.

3. In the drop down menu, select the drive you want to clean up and click OK. The drive will be scanned.

4. In the next window, select the items that you want to delete. Click the More Options tab. In the
5. Click OK.

 

6. Confirm the Delete Files.



This will remove all but the latest System Restore Point and the other files you selected.

 

After this completes, open File Explorer right click the C: drive and select Properties to see the results.
