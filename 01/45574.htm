brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
You have an entry in your registry that points to a program no longer on your PC.  Using regedit, visit the registry key HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\Session Manager.  You'll see a "BootExecute" entry there, in the right=hand pane.  It will contain a string starting with "autocheck autochk" and it will also contain "PD BOOT.EXE".  Double-click BootExecute and either put quotes around "PD BOOT.EXE" or remove it entirely.

I'm adding to this.  If "PD BOOT.EXE" doesn't exist on your PC, then you should remove the registry entry.  If it does exist, it's likely the registry entry is spelled wrong and then correcting the spelling and, perhaps, the quoting in the key will help.

andre da costa say:
Do you have enough Skype Credit or a suitable subscription?

You need to have some Skype Credit or a calling subscription to forward calls to a mobile or landline number.

Check your account balance (https://support.skype.com/faq/FA36) to make sure you have enough Skype Credit to forward your calls. If you have a subscription, make sure that it covers the number you want to forward your incoming calls to.

Have you set up call forwarding correctly?

Check our guide to setting up call forwarding (https://support.skype.com/faq/FA12258/). Make sure you’ve entered the forwarding number correctly, with the full international dialing code. We recommend you make a test call (https://support.skype.com/faq/FA20/) to the number you’ve entered to confirm it’s correct.

Have you checked your privacy settings?

You may have your privacy set so that only contacts can call you. Check your privacy settings (https://go.skype.com/support.privacysettings) and if you need to, change them to allow anyone to contact you on Skype.
