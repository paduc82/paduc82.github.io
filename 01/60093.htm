This is an old thread, but while I agree in principle to the fine old adage 'I if ain't broke....' , like many

things PC this does not always apply, and drivers that are not up to date can lead  to 'broke'.

- When not using a machine to play games, particularly high end (AKA - AAA titles) older graphics

and audio drivers are  almost always good enough.

- When using an older graphics card (three or more series back from the latest series) older drivers

are generally fine, with the exception of when the drivers address a bug in a specific game.

However, when using a recent series graphics card from either Nvidia or AMD and playing recent

high AAA titles a newer driver (not necessarily the most recent) is generally best if you want the

best performance from a graphics card.

- Newer driver versions tend to focus mostly on optimisations for the current series graphics cards in

relation to recent games, so older cards often see little benefit from the latest drivers.

Basically what happens is the new series cards are being tweaked to their best performance via

driver updates. For the most part this has already been done for the older series cards with drivers

released in the year, or perhaps two after their release.

Audio drivers are far less likely to require an update (and are updates are far fewer), but there are some games that will not run properly if the audio drivers are too old.
