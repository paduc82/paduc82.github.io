wungraileng vashi say:

Hi,

Thank you for posting your query in Microsoft Community.

To make sure I understand correctly, are you referring to having to sign in every time you lock the computer or wake up from sleep?

If you are referring to the sign in requirement in the lock screen, please be informed that as long as you sign in to Windows with a Microsoft account, you will be required to  use a password. Your password helps keep your account secure, no matter what PC you sign in to or what apps, settings, and services you use while you're signed in.

However, if the computer is in sleep mode, you do have the option to skip the sign in Window when you wake it up from sleep. To disable the sign in option, follow the steps below:

1. Press Windows key + R

2. Type powercfg.cpl and press enter to open power options.

3. Click "require a password on wake up" on the left pane.

4. Click "change settings that are currently unavailable".

5. Check "don't require a password" and save the changes.

Hope this helps.
