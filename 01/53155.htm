With end of support Microsoft is not telling you that your operating systems won't work.

What they are saying is that after a certain date they will no longer support them.

Whether they still work or not with particular programs is up to the particular program manufacturers.

    • Open a command prompt and run the following command to run an energy report for 5 minutes. You should use the device like you normally would, during that time:

EPSON 1660 SCANNER IS COMPATIBLE WITH WINDOWS 8 AND WINDOWS 8.1 - Microsoft Community

quinko say:

EPSON 1660 SCANNER IS COMPATIBLE WITH WINDOWS 8 AND WINDOWS 8.1

The Epson 1660 IS compatible with Windows 8; HOWEVER, you need a different scanner's driver.
Here is what you do:-

1. Unistall completely the Epson Perfection 1660 Photo Scanner driver from the Control Panel.

2. You need to download the Epson Expression 1680 Professional Scanner Win 7 64-bit driver from Epson's website. Here is a direct link:

http://www.epson.com/cgi-bin/Store/support/supDetail.jsp?oid=27543&infoType=Downloads&platform=OSF_W_7-64

3. Now make sure that your scanner is connected (click cancel on Windows driver installation), Go to the folder location where you downloaded driver--epson12203.exe-- to extract it (remember the extraction folder). Install the driver by double clicking on "Setup".

4. After installation, on the desktop, you should find an icon titled "Epson Scan". Restart your PC, go to the desktop and double-click on "Epson Scan" to confirm that driver has been installed to the 1660 scanner.

5> Go to the Control Panel, Click >View Devices and Printers, right click on the scanner then select properties (not scan properties--just properties). Then, click on the Hardware Tab. Now, Click on "Change settings" and go to the driver tab.  Now click on "Browse My Computer for Driver Software". Here is the tricky part>>>> do not browse the directory--instead, click on "LET ME PICK FROM A LIST OF DEVICE DRIVERS ON MY COMPUTER".

 

A list of devices will pop up--click the mouse inside the list  but away from the drivers, then on your keyboard press "i" to get to the drivers whose titles start with "i". Now, scroll down to "IMAGING DEVICES" and  double click on it. Another list will open with both the manufacturers name on the left side and the model on the right side.

In the manufacturers list, scroll down and click on "Epson", Then in the model list, Scroll down to Epson Expression 1680 and click on it. Now, click on NEXT--an "Update Driver Warning" window will pop up--in it, click on YES to install the driver.

 

Now close all windows and restart your PC.  Now you'll see in your start menu both Epson Scan Settings and Epson Scan. Epson Scan will also be available on your Desktop. Both your scanner and related twain driver imaging software should be working fine now.



You can scan using Windows fax/scan,  Epson Scan  from the desktop, or through any photo software that uses a twain driver. I prefer Epson Scan because the options and preview on it fits my needs. Good luck.
