Press the key firmly and quickly before the screen goes away. If you do not react quickly enough, you will miss the window of opportunity to enter the BIOS - this can be as short as 2 seconds on some models.

Step 3:
Navigate your BIOS configuration screen to the 'Boot' tab. I

Step 4:
As the first boot device, select 'USB,' or 'USB HDD'.

Step 5:
Press the button for "Save and Exit" in your BIOS.

Once this has been done, the laptop should restart and then try and boot from the attached USB device. You should then be able to reinstall Windows by following the on-screen prompts.
