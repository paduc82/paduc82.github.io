Hi,

 

There is no list of Product Keys that you can do checks against.

You have to install using the Product Key to see if it is genuine/correct.

 

And as the Operating System was pre-installed and is OEM Licensed, Product Key Finder Programs will not find a Product Key from the pre-installed and already activated Operating System that is useable for re-installation purposes

 

 

You needed the COA sticker with the Product Key, but it is faded away.

 

Your choices are now limited as to what you can do without having to buy a new Product Key/License.

 

 

 

As Windows 7 was preinstalled:

 

You can contact your Laptop Manufacturer and ask them to send you a set of Recovery Disks.

They should do that for a small cost.

 

 

You don't say where that Windows 7 disk came from.

 

If you never received a Recovery Disk when you purchased your Computer there should be a Recovery Partition on the Hard Drive to reinstall Windows 7 back to how you bought your Computer.

The Recovery process can be started by pressing a particular Key or Keys combination at Boot. (Power On / Startup)

It may be F10, F11, Alt + F10, etc depending on the Manufacturer.

Ask them for the correct Key sequence.

 

The above reinstallation methods, using either the Recovery DVD or Recovery Partition, do not normally require you to supply a Product Key during the Installation Process as the Recovery Process uses the Factory Installation Product Key to automatically Activate during the reinstallation process.

 

Cheers.

naman r say:

Hi Veteran1111,

If your computer is having problems printing, try using the Printer troubleshooter to fix the problem. It makes sure that a printer is connected to your computer, and it checks for common issues such as whether the printer has enough paper and toner to complete printing your document.

Open the Printer troubleshooter by clicking the Start, and then clicking Control Panel. In the search box, type troubleshooter, and then click Troubleshooting. Under Hardware and Sound, click Use a printer.

Refer the link: Open the Printer troubleshooter: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Open-the-Printer-troubleshooter

If you are unable to print, try restarting the Spooler service and check.A print spooler is software that temporarily stores print jobs on the computer hard disk or in memory until the printer is ready to print them.

If you've been waiting an unusually long time for a document to print, or if you see an error message about the print spooler, you might need to restart the print spooler on your computer.

To restart the Print Spooler service

You must be logged on as an administrator to perform these steps.
1. Open Administrative Tools by clicking the Start, and then clicking Control Panel. In the search box, type administrative tools, and then click Administrative Tools.
2. Double-click Services. If you're prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.
