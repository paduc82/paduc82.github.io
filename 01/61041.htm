davem121 say:
Hi faallisonng, this is very common, do not worry, it is not a virus

Click your Start Button, type regedit and hit Enter - this will open the Registry Editor

In the left pand, navigate to:
HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE - SYSTEM - CurrentControlSet - Control

Then in the Right Pane double click WaitToKillServiceTimeout and change the value to 1000

Click OK

Then back in the left Pane, close up all those folders (keys) again (click in the left pane and press left arrow on your keyboard a few times)

In the left pand, navigate to:
HKEY_CURRENT_USER - Control Panel - Desktop

Then in the right pane, right click in the blank space and choose New - String Value

Name the String Value WaitToKillServiceTimeout

Then double click WaitToKillServiceTimeout

Set its value to 1000

Save and close the Registry Editor

That's It!

defrag C: /H /U /V

and press enter.

Once running wait until the flashing cursor reappears to show the process has finished and then you can close the window.

It might take 20 minutes or more first run depending how defragmented things are.

Depending on the laptop computer that you are using, it is possible that your device may not have the appropriate Bluetooth driver installed which is why it produces the Device does not have Bluetooth error message. It is also possible that the Bluetooth is not enabled on your computer and the wireless speaker is not in pairing mode.

Please make sure that the appropriate Bluetooth driver is installed in your computer, the Bluetooth is enable and the wireless speaker that you are using is set up in pairing mode before attempting to pair them. Please refer to your device manual for the steps on how you can set up your speaker.

As for the information on how you can enabled the Bluetooth on your computer, please go to this link for the steps. Toggling the Bluetooth off and on may also help.

If the issue persists after making sure that the devices failed to pair, please provide the following:

  • What is the make and model of the laptop computer that you are using?
