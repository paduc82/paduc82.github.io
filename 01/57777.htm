Hi,

It may be that your are not booting properly with the repair\installation media...

My experience with the Access Denied message you describe happen when a UEFI machine is booted with media in a "Legacy" mode, or vice\versa... Especially if using a bootable DVD...

What worked for me was to use the specific type of repair media for your machine... A DVD does not usually qualify for that, but can sometimes be used...

My workaround was to access Boot Options at the Manufacturer's (POST) screen...

F9, F11, F12, or whatever your machine uses, and look for Specific boot devices...

For example... "UEFI Optical Drive" or simply "Optical Drive".... "UEFI Flash Drive" or simply "Flash Drive"...

If using UEFI Bios, select the "UEFI" device, and if using Legacy Bios, select the standard device...

Here are the commands I use from the command prompt available on the bootable Repair\Installation media...

BOOTREC /FIXMBR, and then press ENTER.

BOOTREC /FIXBOOT, and then press ENTER.

BOOTREC /REBUILDBCD, and then press ENTER.

It would be best to unhook any additional drives other than the OS drive, but not absolutely necessary...

BTW... Do not reboot between commands... run all three before rebooting...
