john_barnett say: Hi Coolnewyorker,

Your question on 'who scheduled them' - the answer is Microsoft.

Amongst the scheduled tasks you will find System Restore, Windows Defragmenter, Windows Troubleshooter, Windows Customer Experience. You will also find tasks to check your PC's health. That is just to name a few.

As for disabling them not making any difference you may find that not a lot, if anything, is actually scheduled to work when you are actually on your PC. In many cases most  of the schedules run at around 2am or, in the case of everything Microsoft has scheduled on my machine, it doesn't run at all simply because my system is shut down when most of the application are scheduled - or at least it used to be. I systematically went through them and changed the time to when I was online so that the information Microsoft needs is uploaded - for example files that are created when the PC crashes etc.

Regarding MSE, like most applications that run on a schedule there is a heavy reliance upon the PC being idle for so many minutes before the schedule starts. If you are running another program, such as WMC in recording mode the system is not idle, it is recording; therefore, yes, WMC recording could affect the MSE running.
