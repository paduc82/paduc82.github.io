abizarvakharia say:

Solved the problem after reading the "Can a Surface Pro 3 with docking station replace your desktop PC?"  article on ZD Net.

I was using Mini DisplayPort to HDMI Cable to connect the TV to my Surface Pro 3. As the article states the cable has to be HDMI 1.2 compliant - it was not. So, I tried using an HDMI 1.2 compliant cable with a Accell B086B-007B UltraAV Mini DisplayPort 1.2 to HDMI 1.4 Active Adapter; presto-bingo! 

Everything now works.  I am able to get 3840 x 2160 and am able to use it as a primary monitor.

karen_[01] say:

Hello

Upon checking here, it appears that all the calls to your Skype number are being rejected because of the privacy settings.

To ensure that the privacy settings is configured, please follow the steps below:

NOTE: Privacy settings for Skype number is not yet available in the latest versions of Skype and cannot be access on mobile, tablets or iPads, you'll need a desktop or laptop. Also, to configure it, you will need to install the classic Skype version.

  1. Log on to www.skype.com
  2. Click on Downloads.
  3. Click on the dropdown from the blue button.
  4. Click on Get classic Skype.

After installing the classic Skype, follow these steps:

Skype for Windows

  1. In Skype select Tools > Options
  2. Click the Privacy tab, then under Allow calls to my Skype Number(s) from… select anyone, and click Save.

If the privacy settings were already configured like this, complete the two steps below to refresh the settings. 

  1. Go back to the Privacy tab by selecting Tools > Options and change the privacy settings to allow calls to Skype Number(s) from phone numbers in my Contact list only and click Save.
  2. Wait approximately 5 minutes then open the privacy settings once more and change the setting back to anyone and Save.

Skype for Mac

  1. In Skype, go to Skype > Preferences > Privacy
  2. Under Receive calls to my Skype Number from, select Anyone.

If the privacy settings were already configured like this, complete the two steps below to refresh the settings.

  1. Go back to the Privacy tab by selecting Skype > Preferences > Privacy and change the privacy settings to allow calls to your Skype Number from Only people in my contact list
  2. Wait approximately 5 minutes then open the privacy settings once more and change the setting back to Anyone.

Make sure to save the changes and then try calling your number or have someone test it for you.

Let me know if the issue persists.
