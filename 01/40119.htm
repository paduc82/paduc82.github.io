goodthings2life™ say:

I think what you're describing and what you're expecting are different.

 

Xbox SmartGlass allows you to essentially use your phone or tablet as an enhanced remote control for your Xbox. It doesn't really "project" or "sync" anything so much as instruct it to display something and subsequently use it as a keyboard/control to operate it.

 

For example, I'm using Surface to surf the web or watch a video. I have SmartGlass installed, so I use the option to "Play to Xbox 360". The Xbox will receive a command to display the content I'm looking at on the Surface. If that content is video or music, the content will jump right to the point you were at on the tablet and then the tablet begins to display information about the content on the tablet. You then have playback controls on the Surface to pause, skip, etc. Likewise, with a website, your Surface or Windows Phone will allow you to surf the web using it as a controller (navigation, typing, etc.).

 

It does NOT however, allow you to synchronize files between devices. It also does not support Wi-Di to use the Xbox as a wireless projector. Indeed, you will need a VGA or HDMI cable for that. I believe that the Surface Pro will allow Wi-Di though, but we won't know for sure until it's released.

 

In the mean time, you can sync your content directly through SkyDrive, which I feel is probably the best solution. Actually, come to think of it, if you use SkyDrive for your personal content, you can use SmartGlass to surf to your SkyDrive account on the web and control it that way for your projecting needs. It's a work-around that also gives you a convenient on-line backup for your data. :)
