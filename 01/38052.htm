As it turns out, adding an SD card to your Surface Go doesn't increase the capacity of the existing C: drive (where by default files go, since this is the default installation location for Windows 10 S / Home [Depending on if you chose to switch to Windows 10 Home from stock Window 10 S with your Surface Go]).  

You didn't actually mount something within the C drive - but in parallel to the C drive, or alongside it?  In regards to how the computer sees it, you now have two storage devices, the original C: built in eMMC / SSD storage for Windows and anything else, and now the SDXC card. 

You would need to actually move  files to the mounted drive letter location (usually going to be D / F / E / G or something for a USB drive or an SD Card) with Windows Explorer. 

Adding an SDXC card is similar to just adding a USB external hard drive / thumb drive / SSD.  It's just that: extra storage mounted alongside your internal storage (C: Drive).

How do I change the screen idol time before it goes black? - Microsoft Community

kimberlyjennings say:

How do I change the screen idol time before it goes black?

I need to know how to change the time limit that you set for when your screen goes black as a security measure for when you are in a public place and walk away to go to the restroom or something. Where do I go to change the time limit so I don't have to move my mouse while I'm watching a movie to keep my screen from going black??

john_barnett say:

Hello kimberlyJennings,

 

You can change the time taken for the display to turn off by visiting Power Options.

 

1. Open Control Panel.

2. Click on the Power Option icon.

3. In the Power Option window, click on the Change Plan Settings under the Balanced section (assuming you are using the Balanced plan) of the preferred plans.

4.  In the Change Settings for plan: Balanced window you will see an option to 'turn off the display'.

5. Click on the down arrow in the dialogue box to the right of the turn off the display option (it should read 10 minutes or 20 minutes).

6. From the drop down box select Never at the bottom of the list. You can, if you want select anything in between but I prefer the display to never turn off.

7. Click the Save Changes button.

8. Finally exit from the power options window and then close Control panel.

9. Now your display will never turn off.

 

Please note that this also applies when you leave your PC to visit the restroom; your display will never turn off unless you select another time option.
