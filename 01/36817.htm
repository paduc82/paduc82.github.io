canadian tech say:

One of the most common failures is the DVD/CD drive. 

 

If yours is a laptop, the problem is availability and price.  It is an OEM part available only from the manufacturer of your PC.  Cost is likely about $100, but usually quite easy to install.

 

If yours is a desktop, the problem is simple to solve and low cost.  About $20.  Remove all power,  Hold the power ON switch in for at least 7 seconds.  Lay it down on its side with the wires on the bottom. Take the side off.  You may have to remove the front panel as well.  The DVD drive is held in place usually by 2 easily accessible screws.  You have to remove 2 cables from the back.  Pull the old one out.  Slide the new one in its place.  Attach the two wires, they only fit one way.  Put the 2 screws in.  Put the side and front back on.

 

Before you close it up again, if you have one of those cans of compressed air, blow out the dust, focusing on the fans and in particular that finned thing on top of the processor with a fan on it.

 

There is an easier alternative.  It is a portable DVD drive that connects to the computer via a USB cable.  Some get their power from that cable.  Cost closer to $25, but you then have a portable device you can use on any computer.  However, this has a drawback when it comes to re-installing the operating system.  You will have to have a driver CD available at that time.

Whether you can burn it to a recordable CD, DVD, or Bluray Disc depends on your disc burner and the type of discs it can burn, the size of the disc image file, as well as the device on which you plan to use the disc.

 

1) Insert a recordable CD, DVD, or Bluray Disc into your disc burner.

2) Drag the mouse to bottom left corner and right-click on the Start icon, and then click on Computer.

3) In Windows Explorer, find the disc image file, and then double-click it.

4) If you have more than one disc burner, from the Disc burner list in Windows Disc Image Burner, click the burner that you want to use.

5) (Optional) If you want to verify that the disc image was burned correctly to the disc, select the Verify disc after burning check box.
