Regarding your comment -  "VSync, which will make your game a lot slower and your mouse will feel sluggish."


You may already be aware of this, but for other who may read this...

In some instances, yes VSync can cause mouse lag, but it shouldn't normally if a game is running over 60 fps.

Mouse lag should normally only happens if the game is running well below 60 fps with VSync enabled, as VSync is fairly system intensive.

Enabling Triple Buffering can help reduce mouse lag when VSync is enabled as well.
I believe VSync had more impact on games in the past, but I still only enable it when there is tearing. Not something I see
often see on a 144Hz monitor though.

While still a fairly system intensive feature, I don't reckon modern VSync is as likely to make games 'run a lot slower' as it once

may have done. Not if the cpu/gpu are up to the task of running the game in the first place.

Machines with Integrated Graphics are still likely to suffer under VSync though.

Having said that, VSync seems to affect some game engines more than others, and in different ways. It's one of those settings

I reckon has to be dealt with on a game by game basis.

I have a couple of games that get vertical tearing, (as opposed to the traditional horizontal tear) if I don't enable VSync, even with the game only running around 40fps, but it still does not affect the frame rates or mouse response when enabled in those games.
I have had a couple of games in the past that introduced general lag as well as mouse lag when Anti Aliasing was set over 2X as well,

but this was linked to a big drop in fps when using 4X AA.

- I believe a lot of people are not aware that mouse (input) lag can also be caused by the

can be quite high (8+ ms) on many monitors that are not intended for gaming. Particularly monitors with IPS screens.
Monitors designed for gaming generally have a response time below 4ms, with many have as little as 1-2ms.
