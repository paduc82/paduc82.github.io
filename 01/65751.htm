It's possible your screen is dimming due to the Adaptive Brightness settings in Windows.

See if you can turn it off by following these steps:

1. Open the advanced Power Options for your power plan.

2. In your current active power plan's (ex: High Performance) advanced settings, expand open Display and Enable adaptive brightness. (see screenshot below step 3)

3. Turn on or off Enable adaptive brightness for what you want, and click/tap on OK. (see screenshot below)

From: https://www.tenforums.com/tutorials/70157-enabl...

Otherwise, see if you need to disable a scheduled task for adaptive brightness:

1. Search for Task Scheduler in Cortana. Right-click the result and select Run as administrator.

2. In the left pane in Task Scheduler, navigate to Microsoft > Windows > Display > Brightness.

3. In the right pane, Double-click BrightnessReset to open Properties for the task.

4. Click the Triggers tab.

5. Select the “At log on” trigger and click the “Edit…” button to open the edit page.

6. The Enabled radio button at the bottom of the page should be checked. Click on the radio button to clear it.

7. Click OK on the Edit trigger page and then click OK on the Properties page.

From: https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/for...

      =============================================
      Video encoding is CPU intensive and overheating is a definite possibility
      and it sounds like you have done everything to address that issue.

      Look closely at the fan on the CPU...sometimes it gets clogged between
      the vanes almost solid with dust. I use a small brush that came with an
      electric razor and a vacuum cleaner reversed to the blowing end to clean
      mine.

      And...it goes without saying...be really, really careful.

      Clogged Example:
      http://www.ourcurioushome.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/S6301111.jpg

      Also the fan on the back of the case that cools the power supply can
      get clogged.

      Examples:
      http://www.gurulounge.net/gurublog/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/clogged_fans.png

      There is a slight chance that disabling the filters as described in the
      following article could help:

      How to Burn DVD's with Windows DVD Maker
      http://www.7tutorials.com/how-burn-dvds-windows-dvd-maker
      (scroll waaay down to ...What To Do In Case Of Issues)

      creating video DVDs from various video files.

      Freemake Free Video Converter (freeware)

      (Windows XP / Vista / 7)
