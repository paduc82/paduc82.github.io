preciousleb say:

Hi Martin,

It could be the Windows Backup trying to back up files in the LocalLow, Contacts, and Searches folders or these folders are not present on your device. In order to prevent backup from failing, you can use one of the following methods to eliminate this error message during future backup operations:

Method 1:
Create the missing folder C:\Windows\System32\config\systemprofile\new, as listed in the error message.

Method 2:
Exclude the folders from the files to back up in the Backup settings, and then include those folders in their original location. To exclude the folders in the Additional Locations and to include those folders in the original location, follow these steps:

  1. Under the Control Panel, open Backup and Restore.
  2. In the Backup section, click Change settings.
  3. In the Set up backup dialog box, click Next.
  4. In the What do you want to back up dialog box, click Let me choose then click Next.
  5. Expand Data Files, expand <var><your user name></var>Libraries, expand Additional Locations, and then click to clear the AppData folder, Contacts, and Searches check boxes.
  6. Expand Computer, expand the item for your system drive (for example, expand Local Disk (C:)), expand Users, expand <var><your user name></var>, and then click to select the AppData,Contacts, and Searches check boxes if they exist.
  7. Click Next, and then click Save settings and exit.
  8. In Backup and Restore, click Back up now to perform a new backup. The backup operation should complete without errors.

Try this out.

Next time your computer sleeps and then wakes up, immediately open a command prompt and type:

powercfg -lastwake

a. user say:

One of the security issues that's popping up with a lot of Windows machines lately is when your machine gets botted, then turned into a Botnet server relay.  If your machine keeps waking up even after putting it manually into sleep-mode then it's possible you have a rootkit virus which has installed itself and is using your machine as a relay server.  This is achieved by creating a hidden Bit-Torrent installation on your machine.  If disconnecting your network cable, or disabling your wireless connection eliminates the waking up problem then you may have a botted Windows PC.
