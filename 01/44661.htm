Are you sure it is being emptied on startup? Only a third party app can do that. You can bypass the recycle bin, but that should keep deleted files from going there period, not empty it.

  1. Right-click the Recycle Bin, and then click Properties.
  2. In the Recycle Bin Properties dialog box, click to select the Do not move files to the Recycle Bin. Remove files immediately when deleted check box.
  3. Click OK.

Details here.

HOW TO: Bypass the Recycle Bin When You Delete Files and Folders in Windows XP
http://support.microsoft.com/?kbid=320031

If that box is not checked, then you have a third party program similar to CCleaner doing it.

You may also want to move the slider higher than the default 10%, but only files exceeding the alloted space are automatically deleted, not the entire bin.
