111 people found this helpful

a. user say:

There is no way to charge a Surface Pro 4 from a USB port due to the voltage requirements.  SP4 needs 12 volts and USB provides only 5 volts and limited current.  Depending on class of service, airline and route there can be a 110v socket at your seat along with the USB ports.  Before you book your flight check Seat Guru to see the characteristics of the seats on a given airline, aircraft, and flight.  Sometimes the only way to charge your device is to spend time in the lavatory.  They all have 110 volt outlets.

When you click this, another window will appear. This should have a list of functions. You can click each to enable or disable them. Kaboom! Click apply and voila!


METHOD TWO

look in the bottom right of your screen. On the taskbar, There are little white icons (for me they're white). One of which will be a little arrow pointing up.

Click on this to show the hidden icons. There should be an icon that looks a little bit like a touchpad (might be box with little boxes under it, or manufacturing company's icon). Click on that once.

Then click 'tap to click'. If a tick shows the left, it is enabled, but if there is no tick, tap click is disabled.


The second thing might not be the same on your laptop, as they are different brands.


METHOD ONE, MADE EASIER


Go back to the little arrow and touchpad icon on the taskbar. above where it says 'tap to click' it should say in bold text: 'pointing device properties'
