This worked... No more freezes. Of course this is useless since the system isn't really running anything with all the services and processes stopped. So I went through the entire list and isolated the issue.
It is the Workstation service that's causing these freezes (at least on my machine). I disable it, and the system runs fine for as long as I've had the time to test it. Turn it on after system startup (i.e. turn it on manually) and the system freezes after a random time. Set it on to to Manual or Automatic and the system freezes within a minute or two of startup again.

I've been trying to find a way to fix the service, but so far I haven't found one.


Is there a way to repair (or maybe remove and reinstall) this service, short of reinstalling the operating system?
quixand say: Solved it.

Went into Windows\System32, took ownership of the wkssvc.dll file, the .dll corresponding to the Workstation service, and renamed it to wkssvc_CORRUPT.dll.
Then I went to start, typed cmd and ran it as Administrator, wherein I ran sfc /scannow to scan and fix bad/missing system files. This replaced the now "missing" wkssvc.dll with a clean copy from the repository, and after rebooting with Workstation enabled (set to Automatic) in services, VOILLA! No insta-freeze on start up.
I can't report on how long this non-freezing will go, but since the system would freeze within a minute of startup, and having changed nothing else apart from the wkssvc.dll, with the result of no more freezing on startup, I think it's a safe bet this fixed the Workstation issue at the very least.
