a. user say:

Hi TheRealAlgernon,

Thank you for contacting Microsoft Community.

I can understand the frustration when things don't work as expected.

WIA drivers and the Scanner and Camera Wizard support the actual optical resolution that a scanner is capable of. Optical resolution represents the amount of detail that is visible in an image before any software manipulation occurs. Third-party programs and drivers can support interpolated resolution. Interpolated resolution is software-enhanced resolution, and because of this, interpolated resolution may exceed the actual optical resolution that a scanner is capable of.

Let's follow the methods and check if it helps.

Method 1:

To enable your scanner to use the interpolated resolution that is provided by the software that came with the scanner:

  1. Click Start, click Control Panel, clicking System and Security, and then, under System, click Device Manager, and then locate your scanner under the Imaging Devices branch.
  2. Right-click your scanner, and then click Update Driver to start the Hardware Update Wizard.
  3. Click Install from a list or specific location (Advanced).
  4. Locate and click the manufacturer-supplied drivers, and then click Finish.

Note: After Method 1 if still issue is not resolved then Uninstall and Re-Install the Drivers from the Manufactures Website.

Additional Information:

Update a Drivers for Hardware that isn't working properly

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-in/windows/update-driver-hardware-isnt-working-properly#update-driver-hardware-isnt-working-properly=windows-7
