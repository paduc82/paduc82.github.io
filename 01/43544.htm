goodwinmf say:

Thanks Frederik,

The solution I used was as follows . . .

1. Open Windows Power User utilities (Win key & "X")

2. Select Disk Management (as you previously suggested)

In the lower pane, observe Disk0, Disk0, Disk2 etc with their already assigned letters

3. Select the Drive with redundant Letters e.g. Disk 4 Removeable (H:)

4. Select Change Drives Letters & Paths

5. Chose Remove

6. Chose Y to accept warning

Repeat 3-6 as required

No more redundant drives showing in Explorer!
