Easy transfer - Microsoft Community

whatupdawg say:

Easy transfer

So, I understand there is no easy transfer for Windows 10. How do I transfer my data from my old computer running Windows 7 to my new computer running Windows 10?

a. user say:

1- Obtain a USB hard drive; connect to your old computer.

2- Copy and paste your personal folders (Music, Documents, Pictures, Videos ) to the USB drive.

3- Connect the USB drive to your new computer.

4- Copy and paste from the USB drove to the new computer.

You can reduce this process to a matter of minutes with backup software.

The only 'gotcha' with this process is that your personal files belong to your user account on the old computer, and now you're moving them to your user account on the new computer. Since user accounts must be unique, your new user account may not be able to open the files from your old user account.

There is an easy solution to this: Your new user account must 'take ownership' of your folders. To do this, simply do a web search for: take ownership of folders in windows 10. Instructions for Windows 7 and 8 are the same.

vibroplex say:

Some NAT problems are subtle and don't appear in the Test Xbox Live Connection test. Most likely there's a port blocked... ports can be blocked in your ISP's gateway, or at except on the Xbox.

 

A sequential power cycle is the first step in clearing a port blockage, and the order and timing of the steps is important to success:

1) turn off the Xbox then

2) unplug power from the Arris gateway

3) Wait at least 30 secs then replug the gateway power.

4) Wait 'til the light on the front of the modem (or gateway) which says ONLINE, READY, CABLE or DSL is on solid (not flashing), then turn on the Xbox.  

 

For more background on Ports, see whatismyipaddress.com/port and the Firebind test at www.firebind.com/.../xbox
