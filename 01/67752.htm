tharp94895 say:

First make sure that you have them installed the correct way if you look at the stick of RAM you'll see a notch so make sure they line up with the motherboard.

Once you line up the notches on both make sure the RAM is seated in the slots this can cause it to not boot.

If it still won't boot then try these trouble shooting steps to check the sticks of RAM and the slots on the motherboard:

Try this to be sure the RAM that was sent to you isn't defective:

- Take the 2 sticks of RAM that is already in your computer and take them out and note the slots they are in.

- Put the 2 new RAM sticks that you just got in place of the 2 stick of RAM you just took out.

- Try to boot the computer

If this works and it boots up then that means all 4 sticks of RAM are good and the sticks of RAM are not allowing it to boot.

 

Try this to check if the slots of the motherboard are defective:

- Take all of the RAM out of the motherboard

- Take one stick of RAM that you know was good so preferably one of the sticks that were already in the computer.

- Install the one good stick of RAM in one of the slots and boot up the computer and repeat this for the other slots.

- If the computer doesn't boot then the motherboard is defective.

 

Reply back to let me know how it go's.
