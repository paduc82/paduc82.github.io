As I suspected, your video card has determined the "native resolution" of your monitor and set itself at that resolution (1920 by 1080).

Although setting the resolution to the value that the display "wants to have" gives the best results as far as "sharpness" goes, the higher the resolution, the smaller the images (icons and text).  That can cause a problem if you have less than perfect eyesight.

Fortunately for you, your monitor appears to be able to display an acceptably sharp display at lower resolutions.  And lower resolution means bigger perceived size of icons and text.  See this page (10) of a review of your monitor
(images deleted to keep this post short; click on the link to see original):

The LG W2261VP also interpolates low resolutions excellently. The lower the resolution, the weaker the effect the sharpness adjustment has. Even a resolution of 1.024 x 768 or even 800 x 600 pixels can be displayed well by the W2261VP.

[image]

The display of non-native resolutions is flawless in games as well. We looked at a scene from "Dead Space" in various resolutions. Hardly any details disappear and the display only becomes coarsely pixelled at a very low resolution. At the LG W2261VP‚Äôs native resolution of 1.920 x 1.080 pixels, this no longer surprises us.

[image]

Image sharpness

The LG W2261VP offers the option to adjust the image sharpness in steps of one to ten. The factory setting is five. If the W2261VP is run in its native resolution, no adjustment is needed, since if the value is changed, the image is not sharp enough or is too sharp. However, the sharpness regulation has a visibly positive effect on interpolation resolutions, as we have already mentioned under "Interpolation".

At an image sharpness level of zero, the native, but also every other resolution, is displayed as blurred or less sharp.

[image]

The middle and also the factory sharpness setting of five is the ideal selection for native resolution in our opinion.

[image]


Increasing the sharpness of ten is not very useful at the native resolution. This causes the image to become excessively sharp and text takes on a yellowish-green shadow.

My suggestion to you is to move the resolution slider to the left.  Try 1600 by 900 (which fits the aspect ratio of your monitor) or 1360 x 768 or even 1280 x 720.  Adjust the "sharpness" setting to counteract the blurring.  If you don't have the owner's manual for the monitor, you can download it here --> http://www.lg.com/sg/support-product/lg-W2261VP-PF#manuals_panel

lemp say:
It sounds like you've changed the resolution of your display.

Assuming that you have installed the drivers that came with your new video card, right-click on an empty spot on the Desktop and select Properties.  Then click the Settings tab.

In the lower left of the window, you'll see a slider under "screen resolution."  Moving it to the left decreases resolution and increases the size of icons and text.  Moving the slider to the right does the opposite. 

Note that if you have a flat screen LCD monitor, the display driver may have picked the monitor's "native" resolution, which is usually the best choice -- although the icons may be too small for you.  LCD monitors can technically support lower resolutions than their native resolution, but the image might be small, centered on the screen, and edged with black; or the image will look stretched.

Windows System Image recovery files not showing in recovery file list - Microsoft Community

rj_1112 say:

Windows System Image recovery files not showing in recovery file list

Windows 7 Professional - all up to date from Windows update. 

I have just created a system backup image on an external USB Hard Disk Drive. Looking at the USB drive the directory for the backup directory shows as there - there is a folder with the machine name present. 

If I then try to restore the backup to the same disk, either from the Control panel GUI or from a Windows recovery disk the recent backup does not show in the list of available images to be restored.  Other image backups from different machines that have been saved onto this drive do appear in the list!!

Any Ideas?

rj_1112 say:

Problem resolved.

 

The USB External drive had a failed backup on it.  If you carried out a DOS directory list of all the image backup files on the external drive you would see all the backups but system image restore only showed those up to the failed/corrupt backup.

Deleting the failed backup directory tree then allowed System Image restore to list all available backups on the external drive.

 

Thanks to all those who looked at this and helped.
