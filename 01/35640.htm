File encryption is a two-edged sword. While it is designed to make it hard if not impossible for unauthorised people to open an encrypted file (which is the reason why its owner used it!), it works just as effectively against the owner of the file unless he takes great care to preserve the required keys. Translated into real life this means that encryption should only be used after

  • Performing some trial decryption runs under the assumption that the original PC is inoperative/stolen/corrupted.
