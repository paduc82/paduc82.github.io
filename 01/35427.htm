ron sommer mvp 2009-2017 say:

All email goes to email servers.  Some servers can be accessed with a browser.  When accessing with a browser, you must be connected to the Internet to read and compose emails.

Most email servers can be accessed with an email client (program).  When using an email client, emails are downloaded to your computer.  You can read and compose emails without being connected to the Internet.

There are different methods for email clients to access email servers.  Servers can be pop3, imap, http, or an exchange server.  Some servers can use more than one method.

An exchange server usually requires Microsoft Outlook.
Pop3 only downloads emails from the server.
Http and imap access will sync messages and folders with the email server.

Some servers can send and receive emails from other email accounts making it unnecessary to have the emails forwarded.

Login to your university and work accounts.  See what options are available.
