Once connected, your laptop may automatically switch to the new external display or you may be required to press a sequence of keys in order to switch from the laptop to the external display.   

For example, often either Fn + F3, Fn + F5, Fn + F8, or Fn + F9 is pressed to switch between alternate displays. So if your laptop was Fn + F3, pressing and holding both Fn and F3 at the same time would switch between displays. Often, the alternate key pressed in conjunction with the Fn key will be labeled as CRT/LCD and/or have a picture of a monitor on the key or close to the key. Unfortunately, the keys pressed to switch displays is dependent on the model and manufacturer of the laptop. If none of the above key sequences work, consult your laptop documentation or consult your laptop manufacturer.

Some laptops also have the ability to have the display on both the laptop and external display and/or one or the other at a single time. Laptops with this ability can switch between these modes by continuing to press the same function keys until in the mode the user wants.

Hi blaze!  

Not only can I explain a bit more about each of these steps you had questions about - I found some links for you here on the Xbox.com site that explain a bit more about it!

.

Checking for corrupt/incomplete date:

Corrupt files will have a yellow warning symbol next to them, letting you know something's up! You can navigate to the hard drive, Games & Apps folder, and check in each one.  Usually these type of files should be deleted (they're no good anymore), but if you get in there and find a LOT of them (or even all of them) are corrupted, don't delete just yet - it could be a problem with the profile itself and redownloading that might make all those warning symbols disappear (see below).

You can find more info about corrupt/incomplete files here (under the 'I can't play a downloaded game section'): support.xbox.com/.../games-media

.

Clearing the system cache:

The console cache stores temporary files that the console uses. Clearing it can help with certain performance issues. You'll have to download game updates the next time you go to launch games that have them, since clearing the cache removes those updates.

Here's how! support.xbox.com/.../clear-system-cache

.

Deleting and redownloading your profile:

This one can seem intimidating, but it can also fix certain stuff!  There are two different ways to delete your profile - by deleting just the profile only, or by deleting the profile and items.  If you pick profile and items, you will delete EVERYTHING associated with your profile (game save data, achievements, etc) and we don't want that!!

By deleting the profile only, it's more like you're removing the access to the profile (because you won't be able to find it on your Xbox to sign in or anything).

Once you redownload a fresh copy of the profile from the Live servers, you will be able to sign in and have access to your account again.  And deleting the profile doesn't change your actual account in any way (like your membership, your friends list, stuff like that). 

This link explains more about deleting/redownloading: support.xbox.com/.../profile-move-delete

You will need to know your Windows Live ID and password in order to redownload it, so make sure you know it (or find it on the console) before you delete your profile!  Otherwise, you can call support to have them help you find it.

.

I totally understand not wanting to try any troubleshooting steps until you have all the facts, so if you have any more questions, don't hesitate to ask!!  :)
