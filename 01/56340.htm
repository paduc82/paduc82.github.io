Things I like:

  • Textures are very recognizable so no re-learning
  • Chests are awesome
  • Workbench and Furnace are much improved
  • Glass is easier to see through (both blocks and panes)
  • Wood barks as well as planks are really nice and I like the blonde color of jungle bark.
  • All the ground textures are very good looking and blend well
  • Gravel, well, damn, it looks like gravel again!  Really.  (I liked the old stock gravel before they changed it, TU7 or TU9 I think)
  • Skeletons, gotta love em now.
  • Both wood and metal doors have large windows to see through now.
  • Metal bars look great
  • Stone textures (like from the strongholds) are really distinctive now.
  • Netherrack is less noisy now.  Not all good, not all bad.  Just different.
  • Cows are different but not better.  Faces not so great.
  • Chickens are brown now.
  • Ores are really cool looking and distinct from stone (iron used to be hard to see in dim light, now easy)
  • Diamonds are white :)  Its a good thing.
  • Cactus.  Yea, they look like cactus now!
  • All the redstone devices and rail items look much better, especially tracks and pistons.
  • Water looks more realistic now
  • The moon!  Its round now!
  • Sugarcane, well, it looks like sugarcane!
  • More detail in spider webs as with most other blocks like grass and flowers.
  • Torches are a little better.  Like the ones from ME pack better but the rest of the ME pack sucks for me.


Things I don't like:

  • Sheep.  They look ratty.  Yes, I know they look more like this than in the original but I am still not on board with the new look.  Its hard to tell white, from light gray, or dark gray sheep.
  • Pigs.  Too white.  Again, yea, I know they are not really as pink as the original color but now its hard to see them at times
  • Spiders.  Yea, they look brown and fuzzy now. and pretty realistic (given the squareness of the game)  Look good!  But, without the red glowing eyes, sometimes they are real hard to spot in wooded areas until they are right on you.
  • Wheat.  Very cluttered looking now
  • Pumpkins.  Seriously.  Why does everyone want to put a face on a growing pumpkin?  Wish they were just orange, no face.  Jack-o-lanterns can have faces, good with that.
  • Watermelon (whole).  Their color is off.  Think its got a touch of cyan now.  Not bad but the color on the originals were better.
  • Lava.  Meh.  Think I like the original better.  Maybe its me.
  • Water.  Yes its listed here too.  Its a little harder to tell flow direction with the new texture under some light levels.
  • Mushrooms.  They do look quite a bit more realistic but, its hard to tell red from brown at a distance now.  Before, it was very easy to tell red from brown.
  • Tree leaves.  Seriously, when is someone going to make a texture where you can tell the difference between the birch and oak leaves?  Still, when two trees are next to each other, its hard to tell one leaf block from another.
  • Wood logs.  While I love the new bark,I hate the cut ends.  All the wood ends (inner core) are almost the same color ends.  Kinda ruins some patterns made using the old wood textures.  There should be more of a difference in core colors than there is here.
  • Bookshelves not as vibrant as before.  Jury still out on this one
  • Clay.  Good grief.  Its still hard to see.  If a brick is orange/brown, so was the CLAY it was made from.  Can we get more color in clay so you can see the stuff when its next to sand please?  Not as orange as dirt but not as light as sand.  Please?



Overall, the new Natural Texture Pack is a good improvement over the stock Minecraft textures.  A welcome change but not enough to make you re-learn blocks.  Its well worth the $1.00 IMHO.

davem121 say:
Hi Cm2-d, this must be done via the Registry . . .

Click your Start Button, type regedit and hit Enter to open the Registry Editor

In the left pane, navigate to:
HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Lock Screen

Click that reg key

Name it SlideshowDuration

Please note 1000 = 1 second, 2000 = 2 seconds and so on

Restart your PC for the change to take effect . . .
