Realtek HD Audio Hearing Loss Nag Screen Workaround - Microsoft Community

neuromancer_ say:

Realtek HD Audio Hearing Loss Nag Screen Workaround

This post is to help people struggling to get rid of the extremely annoying 42% volume limit on machines running Windows 10 that have a jack plugged into the headphone port. When a jack is plugged into the headphone port, if the volume is turned down below 42% and then you attempt to increase it above 42% you first have to dismiss an awful nag screen that looks something like this:

This issue is the result of terrible Realtek HD Audio drivers. It's extremely annoying since while it MIGHT make sense for headphone users (but please don't tell me what to do, k? thanks), it's terrible for those who use the headphone out as the link to an external amplifier and speaker system. The solution involves reverting back to Window's default HD audio drivers which work fine and don't cause this problem. Here's how to do it:

  1. First you need to uninstall your Realtek Audio Drivers. Right click the Windows icon in the task bar and, select Programs and Features.
  2. Scroll down until you find Realtek Audio Drivers or something similar.
  3. Uninstall this nonsense -- good riddance!
  4. Now you need to prevent Windows 10 from forcing the 'Best' drivers on you automatically via Windows Update. If you do not disable this, Windows will automatically reinstall the Realtek drivers the next time you reboot.
  5. Right click the Windows icon in the task bar.
  6. Click System.
  7. Click Advanced System Settings on the left of the window.
  8. In the System Properties window that pops up, click the Hardware tab.
  9. Click Device Installation Settings.
  10. Click on No, let me choose what to do.
  11. Click Never install driver software from Windows Update.
  12. Click Save Changes.
  13. Reboot your system.

Windows should now automatically revert to it's generic audio drivers which work just fine, thanks. The trade off is that you will have to manually keep on top of your driver updates. To me it's worth not having to deal with a ridiculous nanny screen. Thanks, mom, I'm old enough now to manage my volume!

If anyone has a better solution, I'd be happy to hear about it! Hope this helps some of you.

Best,

NM_
