How to make a filter - Microsoft Community

bernard liengme say:

How to make a filter

My employer uses Microsoft Exchange for email and we read our email on Outlook, a webmail site or using Windows Live Mail. Many years ago I set up a filter such that mail from certain people automatically got switched into a folder specif to that group.

Some of my colleagues are misusing the email and posting about horrid cats: cat looking for home, cats in need of daycare, etc. How would I set up a filter to search for CAT in the message and send it to the Junk folder?

Many thanks

hansv mvp say:

In Outlook 2010:

Select File, then if necessary click Info.

Click Manage Rules and Alerts.

Click New Rule...

Under Start from a blank rule, click Apply rule on messages I receive.

Click Next >.

Under Step 1, tick the check box With specific words in the body.

Click on the link specific words that appears under Step 2.

Type the word cat, then click Add.

Click OK.

Click Next >.

Under Step 1, tick the check box move it to the specified folder.

Click on the link specified that appears under Step 2.

Select the Junk E-mail folder, then click OK.

Click Next >.

If you wish, you can specify exceptions to the rule (for instance, if you would like to receive messages about cats from a specific sender).

When done, click Next >.

Under Step 1, specify a name for the rule (or accept the one suggested by Outlook).

Under Step 2, make sure that the check box Turn on this rule is ticked.

Click Finish.
