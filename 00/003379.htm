A quick method is:

 

Create a .cmd file in an easily accessible folder, for example MpScan.cmd, containing:

 "C:\Program Files\Windows Defender\MpCmdRun.exe" -Scan -ScanType 3 -File %1
 PAUSE

Create a shortcut to this .cmd file on the Desktop

When you want to virus check a file just drag and drop it over the shortcut icon just created

A Command box will appear, scan is performed and reported, and the PAUSE command stops the command box disappearing before result can be read.
Press Enter to continue

Posting a screenshot of your phone - Microsoft Community

ray hipkiss say:

Posting a screenshot of your phone

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary

Details

If you've ever wondered how some users post screenshots of their phones on the forums then here is how it's done.



First you will need to install a screen grabbing app onto your phone.

 

For Symbian ^1 and Symbian ^3 devices, you can get the free Best Screen Snap from the NOKIA STORE

 

For the Nokia N9, a good app is SCREEN CAPTURE

 

For the new Lumia 920 and 820 (and other WP8 phones), this functionality is built into Windows Phone 8. Simply press the Windows key and power button simultaneously and a screenshot will be taken and placed in Screenshots under Photos > Albums.

 

For Asha and other s40 devices, it isn't possible on the phone, but you can take screenshots using your PC. You need to use Nokia Screen Dump for this purpose.

Now you will need a photo sharing service such as Photobucket or Pixelpipe. My preferred method is to get a free Dropbox account and share photos directly from my desktop.


Now, follow the instructions in Best Screen Snap for Symbian and Screen capture for Meego to take a screenshot and transfer that image to your PC. Assuming you have chosen Dropbox, copy the image to the Public folder and wait for it to synchronise. Once synchronised, right click on the screenshot point to Dropbox then select Copy public link.



To insert that screenshot into your post, simply click the fifth icon along the editing toolbar (the one that looks like a tree next to the chain link icon) and paste the Public link you have just created into the Image URL box. Then select an option from the Alignment box depending on how you want your screenshot to appear (I usually select Middle) then finally click Insert.



And that's it. You can now post screenshots to better explain your problem, rather than having to describe it.

scottgem say:
So you have two of your tables to start with. You should add Autonumber Primary keys to each table. Employee name should be broken out into at least first and last names. Generally I use 5 fields for people names (Salutation {Mr, Ms, etc.}, First, Middle, Last, Suffix {Jr, Sr, etc.})

The department field in the Employee table should be linked to the PK field in the Department table. 

Your department table should have a Subdepartment field that is linked back to the DepartmentID.
