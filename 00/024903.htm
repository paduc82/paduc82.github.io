    Here is an example for a single sheet.  It inserts two blank rows above row #715. This pushes row #715 down to row #717.  The macro goes to row #714 and copies each formula in that row down to the next two (newly inserted) rows:

     

     

    Sub I715KM()
    Range("715:715").Insert shift:=xlDown
    Range("715:715").Insert shift:=xlDown
    For Each r In Intersect(Range("714:714"), Cells.SpecialCells(xlCellTypeFormulas))
        r.Resize(3, 1).FillDown
    Next
    End Sub

    andreas killer say:
    The percentage depends on the perspective... If we assume that we have 2 values A and B, and A=5 and B=4 we can can ask 2 questions:

    How much of B is in A?

    Which means A is the maximum and thus is associated with 100%, therefore the calculation is B/A = 4/5 = 0.8 = 80%

    How much is A over B?

    Which means B is the maximum and thus is associated with 100%, therefore the calculation is A/B = 5/4 = 1.25 = 125%

    So we can answer your formula is =E2/G2 or =G2/E2

    In my example, A and B have the same unit, but your values do not. Of course both are $ amounts, but one is "Sales" and the other is "Balance"...

    I can sale goods for $1 and my bank account shows $1.000.000 or
    I can sale goods for $1.000.000 and my bank account shows $1
    There is no logical dependence between the two, right?

    From that point of view: You could also have asked how much your Christmas gifts weigh, we can not answer that, sorry.
