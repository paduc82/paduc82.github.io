tom_bpc say:

Comments from the gallery:

1. As far as I can tell, setting the baseline for a linked master does not set baselines for the associated sub-projects.  (You need to open those individually and set the baselines.)  That's why you see "NA" in the subproject baseline fields.

2. Critical path and schedule variance / Earned Value analysis are not mutually exclusive.  In general, both are necessary for adequate schedule visibility and control, though I would suggest the first is more important than the second for that purpose.

3. Although you can easily generate a filter of "Critical" tasks, Project has no output called "Critical Path."  There is a not-so-subtle distinction between the two terms that is unfortunately overlooked by most users.
