jay freedman say:

The only other program that might have an effect on margins in Word is your printer driver. Before we get to that, though, try starting Word in Safe Mode, by holding down the Ctrl key while starting Word. Continue to hold the Ctrl key until a message box appears asking whether to start in Safe Mode, and click the Yes button.

Safe Mode loads Word without the Normal template, without any add-ins, and without most of the customizations that are stored in your Registry. Note that Safe Mode is a troubleshooting tool, not a permanent solution for any problem.

While in Safe Mode, look at the margins of a new blank document and of a document created from the Labels dialog. If they're still too large, it's time to examine the settings of the printer driver. If they're back to the regular sizes, though, there's something wrong in Word itself.

To check out the printer driver, click the Windows Start button and type printer. When the Devices & Printers entry appears in the menu, click it. Right-click your printer and click Printing Preferences in the menu. This opens the same dialog box that you would see in Word if you go to File > Print and click Printer Properties. Each brand of printer, and sometimes each model, has a different dialog box, so I can't tell you exactly what to look for, but somewhere in the dialog you should find settings that deal with paper sizes, printable areas, and other measurements. Look for anything that could affect the top margin.

If Safe Mode eliminated the large margin, first shut down Word, rename the Normal.dotm template as OldNormal.dotm, and restart Word. (If you don't know how to locate the template, see http://www.addbalance.com/word/normaltemplate.htm.) If the margins are the regular size now, keep the new Normal.dotm that Word creates, and copy any customizations from OldNormal.dotm to Normal.dotm (http://addbalance.com/word/movetotemplate.htm).

If renaming the Normal template hasn't fixed the problem, try disabling or uninstalling any add-ins, one at a time, to see whether one of them is the culprit. Use the troubleshooting steps in http://support.microsoft.com/kb/921541 -- but leave the registry changes (steps 3 and 4) until after you've tried all the other steps without success.

a. user say:

Hi,

The Windows Phone operating system of your Lumia can only be changed by over-the-air updates from Mircrosoft which come from secure servers direct to your phone or during a repair process. There is no way that code from any other source can get into the phone's core operating system. Furthermore, any app that you install has it's own secure area of memory where it and its data are stored. This "sandboxed" memory cannot be accessed by any other apps, and apps also cannot make changes to the OS, so your Lumia device is well protected against the threat of viruses.

Regarding the phone slow booting you may try to perform a soft reset. Press and hold the volume down and Power button until your phone vibrates and restarts. Note that you should not touch the device screen during this process (not even when the 'slide down to power off' prompt appears). After performing a soft reset, try to check if the issue persists.

Special security codes being asked by your bank app is a part of the security of your account. We suggest that you contact your bank regarding this matter.
