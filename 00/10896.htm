bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Further to what Charles replied, there is much more to the Normal template than most users realize... That's why you can't effectively just save a new one by using that name. A valid Normal.dotm template must be generated by the program. The template also stores a variety of operational information, such as custom keyboard shortcuts, Styles, & other document defaults. Often the user takes actions which affect the Normal template without even realizing it :-)

One way to avoid the notification is to visit Word> Preferences - Save to clear the check for Prompt before saving Normal template. Most experienced users will advise against doing so, though... The belief is that it is better to be notified in order to avoid saving unintended changes. I agree, but it's an available option.

If you're getting the notification even when you're certain that nothing has been done to affect the template it may be due to any of several issues, including a corrupt template. It seems that this is rather common due to the variety of changes made to the program since its introduction in conjunction with Apple's sandboxing requirements. In order to test, you can do either of 2 troubleshooting steps:

  • After Quitting Word, rename your current Normal.dotm [something such as oldNormal.dotm is fine] then launch the program to see if the notification appears when you Quit. Word will generate a new Normal.dotm template. Or,
  • Create a new User Account on your Msc. Log in as that User then launch Word to see if the notification appears when you Quit the program.

Let us know the result & we'll go from there.
