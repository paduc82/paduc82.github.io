"You've lost access to your email

You haven't lost access to the other services you use with your Microsoft account. However, you must choose a new primary alias to continue."

 

This issue may occur if your email address is part of custom domain being managed by a domain administrator. Domain administrator has a several ability to manage an email account, these are; adding/deleting a user or resetting a password.

 

A user may encounter this error if the email account was deleted from the administrator panel. The system will recognize the account but since it is no longer available on the admin panel they will be asking the option to create a new primary alias or use an existing account.

 

If your domain administrator recreated your account and you are still encountering the error, you may need to verify the password you are using as you may need to use the new password.

 

Let me provide an example:

 

Your domain admin created your account as "Account A" and your logging in with the password: AccountA123.

 

Then, your account was deleted and you get the error above.

 

Now, your account was recreated as exactly the same with your first email address with the password: AccountB123.

 

If you will sign into https://outlook.com with the email address and you will be using the password: AccountA123, there's a big possibility that the system will be getting the old information from your first account thus getting the error.

 

Please use the new password for your email address provided by your domain administrator.

 

You can reset your password through this link or contact your domain administrator to reset your password.

 

Probably the reason, why you are able to access those email account via mobile is because you are using the latest password.

 

If you are still having an issue with your account even after resetting the password, we would like to ask your affected email account so we could investigate the issue.
