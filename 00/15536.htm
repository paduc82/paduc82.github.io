Deleting conversation history on one device does not delete the history from any other devices. Each of the devices Skype is installed on has it's own local history storage and the history must be deleted locally on this particular device/Skype installation.

Microsoft Word Push in a line.

When reading a book, and a new line begins it will often be pushed in a few spaces to mark that a new line isn't just a continuation of an old line. I'd like to know if there is a way to do this automatically or if I'm forced to add a few spaces before every line manually?
jay freedman say:

You can modify the style used for your text (usually Normal style, but it could be Body Text style or something else) to have a first-line indent.

To do that:

  • Click in any paragraph of that kind of text.
  • Look at the Styles gallery on the Home tab of the ribbon. The style applied to the paragraph you clicked in will have a gray box in the ribbon.

  • Right-click that box in the Styles gallery and click on Modify in the menu.
  • In the Modify Style dialog, first click the "New documents based on this template" option to make the change apply to all new documents as well as the one that's now open. (It won't change anything in existing documents.)

  • Next click the Format button and click Paragraph in the menu.
  • In the Paragraph dialog, in the Indentation section, set the Special box "First line" and set the box to its right to  the amount of indent you want, such as 0.25" or 0.64 cm.

  • Optionally, check the box for "Don't add space between paragraphs of the same style".
  • Click OK in both dialog boxes.
