Hi, John Doe User.

Thank you for posting your query on Microsoft Community.

With Automatic Rules, you can have Cortana turn on Quiet Hours automatically at specific time (say, from 10:00 A.M. until 5:00 P.M., for example), or turn it on for you whenever you’re in a meeting that’s marked “busy” on your calendar.

To Turn it on, launch Cortana> Click on the Hamburger Menu> and then tap Cortana’s Notebook> Quiet hours, then click on Automatic Rules. Now select the day, and time you want it to turn on automatically.

    

Rules to Break Through

Follow the same steps as above until you reach the Quiet Hours window. Under Break-through rules, you will find Edit Break-through list, click on that if you want to add some exceptions like your family member. Adding your contacts in the list is very simple. Just click on Edit Break-through list> Tap on “+”> Select people you want to break-through. Now, these very people can call, or text you, even when Quiet hours in On.

More Rules to Break Through

Now, what if somebody makes a call from a number which is not in your contact list, and its important. Usually, in these situation, the person would call you multiple times to get your attention, and this is where the rules kicks in.

Anything you then type into the cell before the first of these characters will push the rest of them along, with the result that any 'overflow' ------ characters will simply be pushed out of view below the bottom cell boundary.

If you use unjustified text and fixed column-widths for the cells, you could pre-fill the cells with a string of ------ characters followed by a paragraph break at the end of each line. If you then give all the paragraphs in the cell a negative right indent equal to the cell width, anything you type on a given line will cause any 'overflow' ------ characters will simply be pushed out of view behind the cell right boundary.

Once you know how much text there is for a given row, you can set its 'exact height' to match. You can then fill the remainder with dashes.

For the dashed line option, one doesn't really need to know what the row height is beforehand, but somewhere along the way you'd need to fill from the last-used line to the bottom of the cell with dashes.

The following macro will pad all paragraphs and cells in all tables in the document with n-dashes until the end of the paragraph's last line or the end of the cell, as appropriate. It is assumed: all rows in a given table have the same number of columns; all columns have the same number of rows; and the paragraphs are justified. No attempt is made to pad individual lines. If you only want to process one or more selected tables, change the first reference to ActiveDocument to Selection.

Sub TableFiller()
Application.ScreenUpdating = False
Dim Tbl As Table, Cll As Cell, r As Long, c As Long, p As Long, sTmp As Single, sHght As Single, bBrk As Boolean
For Each Tbl In ActiveDocument.Tables
  For Each Cll In Tbl.Range.Cells
    With Cll.Range
      Do While .Characters.Last.Previous.Text Like "[ " & Chr(11) & Chr(160) & vbCr & vbTab & "]"
        .Characters.Last.Previous.Text = vbNullString
      Loop
    End With
  Next
  With Tbl
    .AllowAutoFit = False
    .PreferredWidthType = wdPreferredWidthPoints
    For r = 1 To .Rows.Count
      With .Rows(r)
        bBrk = .AllowBreakAcrossPages
        .AllowBreakAcrossPages = False
      End With
      sHght = 0
      For c = 1 To .Columns.Count
        With .Cell(r, c).Range
          sTmp = .Characters.Last.Previous.Information(wdVerticalPositionRelativeToTextBoundary) - _
          .Characters.First.Information(wdVerticalPositionRelativeToTextBoundary)
        End With
        If sTmp > sHght Then sHght = sTmp
      Next
      For c = 1 To .Columns.Count
        With .Cell(r, c).Range
          For p = 1 To .Paragraphs.Count - 1
            With .Paragraphs(p).Range
              .Characters.Last.InsertBefore Chr(32)
              sTmp = .Characters.Last.Information(wdVerticalPositionRelativeToTextBoundary)
              Do While .Characters.Last.Information(wdVerticalPositionRelativeToTextBoundary) = sTmp
                .Characters.Last.InsertBefore Chr(150)
              Loop
              ActiveDocument.Undo 1
              '.Characters.Last.Previous.Text = vbNullString
            End With
          Next
          .Characters.Last.InsertBefore Chr(32)
          Do Until .Characters.Last.Previous.Information(wdVerticalPositionRelativeToTextBoundary) > sHght
            .Characters.Last.InsertBefore Chr(150)
          Loop
          ActiveDocument.Undo 1
          '.Characters.Last.Previous.Text = vbNullString
        End With
      Next
      .Rows(r).AllowBreakAcrossPages = bBrk
    Next
  End With
  DoEvents
Next
Application.ScreenUpdating = True
End Sub
