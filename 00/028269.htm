You can try the following steps to share a file with others without granting Delete permission in your OneDrive for Business:

2.  From PERMISSIONS tab, click Permission levels>Add a Permission Level.

a. user say:

Hi Nibes,

Thank you for posting your concern here in Microsoft Community.    

I reproduces the issue and found that while creating a group you can add the contact that are already present in your contact list.

When you copy and paste a large number of contacts from a list, only those (from the large list)get added to the group which are already existing in the outlook.com contact list.

Here are a few suggestions:

1. Add all the contact (that you want in the group )manually to outlook.com contacts and then create the group.

Check the option 'Add a contact'

Contact list management in Outlook.com

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/outlook/manage-people-contact-list#contact-add

2. Import the contacts from outlook 2010 configured on your computer to outlook.com contact list and then create the group.

Check the option 'Import contacts from another email account or app'

Contact list management in Outlook.com

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/outlook/manage-people-contact-list#contact-add 

Hope this information is helpful. Feel free to get back to us if you have any more questions.
