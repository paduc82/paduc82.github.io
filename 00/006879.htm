vincentchoy say:

Here are my thoughts -

Yammer is a social communication platform, different from email, or a structured intranet like Sharepoint.

Just like Facebook is an excellent tool to be in touch with friends and family when there is no time to physically meet, so is Yammer an excellent tool to be in touch with projects and colleagues when its hard to get everyone together for a physical meeting.

Yammer contextualizes discussions into Groups. A group can be liken to a collection of discussions and information on a particular topic. The content in this group can be chats, photos, files, notes ..etc. The content can be further sub contextualised into #topics (hashtags) - e.g. #notcompleted #completed #safety #safetyissues.

Alternative to Yammer can be Email. However email is useful for one to one, or one to many communication, but once you have many to many, and a serious discussion develops, it can be hard to follow all the emails.

Alternative to Yammer can be an intranet. Intranets can be useful for placing forms, announcements, documents, information, or getting things approved via workflows. However, it does not communicate like "Facebook" does.

Alternative to Yammer can be chat tools like WhatsApp. However, they don't store discussions very well. The discussions that go on in Yammer becomes knowledge to the company where others can go and look for information.

What are some possible use cases? Depends on the needs of your company. Until you figure these out, Yammer will not be very useful to you.

1. Does your company have high turnover of staff? If you do there may be a few use cases 

- New staff on boarding groups, where friendly seniors would be there to "chat" and answer newbie questions as and when they come about.

- Social groups (e.g. sports clubs) where new staff can participate and get into the spirit of things

- Motivational and feedback groups (why are you having high turnover, can a social platform help with morale?)

2. Is your company embarking on any major change?

- A group to discuss and explain those changes could make sense

3. Does your company engage with outside partners?

- Yammer allows External Networks where you can have more than one Yammer network to engage with your clients. The Office365 ITproNetwork is one such example.

4. Does your company provide internal helpdesk services?

- Yammer can be an alternative to the internal helpdesk service where employees volunteer to help answer questions, thus improving response time and lightening the helpdesk load. An example is here (but it doesn't use Yammer) where myself and David answer your questions and we are volunteers. A global pharmaceutical company saw their help desk tickets drop significantly when Yammer was implemented because of this. We didn't implement for them but we were friends with the implementation team so this was their story.

This article might help too - 

http://www.themalaymailonline.com/tech-gadgets/article/fact-being-social-at-work-amps-productivity

The way forward -

1. What are the companies goals and challenges business wise?

2. Who is responsible to execute this goals (e.g. We will be closer with our customers)

3. How will the person execute his goals (e.g. hire more sales, work harder, response faster...etc)

4. How can a social tool help (e.g. Sales team discussions, Sales Feedback, customer networks..etc)

5. Who is owning it and what are the objectives and measures for success

6. Launch the service with much training and encouragement

7. Measure and adjust for success.

lz. say:

Hi Aldo

  • Value in A2 is a copy/past from your file. As you see in B3 the number of chars (of A2) is wrong (10 vs 8)
  • D1:M1 extracts each char. from value in A2. As you can see D1 and M1 contains "nothing" (the invisible/unprintable char.)
  • D2:M2 gives the numeric code of D1:M1. So theoritically we could do a SUBSTITUTE(A2,CHAR(63),"") to remove these 2 offending chars. But for some reasons this doesn't work - I must admit I'm not sure why but found a number of cases where people had problem removing it (not only within Excel)

So, in your Sheet2 I took a different approach to "construct" column CLEAN_SKU. Instead of trying to remove these 2 chars with code 63 (after checking all your SKUs in column M had these 2 offending chars) I used the MID function to extract all chars from the 2nd to the last one -1. With the above example the formula extracts 100054MX.

Now re. the formula in column P (SKU DESCRIPTION):

  • As I explained earlier the SKUs in Sheet2 are stored as TEXT values (and the MID function also returns a TEXT value). But in sheet ALL the SKUs that consist of digits only (ie 100916) are NUMeric values. And a VLOOKUP/MATCH of a TEXT value within a range of NUMeric values doesn't work ==> #N/A
  • So, for the formula to work in column SKU DESCRIPTION it needs to consider 2 things: CLEAN_SKUs that consist of digits only and CLEAN_SKUs that consist of a mix of digits/alpha chars

Taking the 1st formula as example:

  • The IF will execute VLOOKUP(N5,ALL!$B$14:$C$30194,2,0) OR VLOOKUP(--N5,ALL!$B$14:$C$30194,2,0) depending upon the evaluation of the 1st part of the formula: ISERR(--N5)
  • When we have a NUMeric value stored as TEXT there are different ways to convert it to a NUMeric value: use the VALUE function; multiply that TEXT value by 1; add 0 to that TEXT value; constrain it with a double negative sign (the fastest approach), ie.: --N5
  • In ISERR(--N5) we have 2 things: 1) Contraints N5 to its NUMeric value; 2) ISERR that returns TRUE or FALSE if --N5 raises an error (that would be the case with a CLEAN_SKU such as ‚Ä≠100186M‚Ä¨ as it doesn't consist of digits only and therefore can't be converted to a NUMeric value)

So the formula does:

IF there is an ERRor trying to constrain the CLEAN_SKU to a NUMeric value

THEN execute VLOOKUP(N5,ALL!$B$14:$C$30194,2,0)      ' tries to match the TEXT value

ELSE execute VLOOKUP(--N5,ALL!$B$14:$C$30194,2,0)     ' tries to match the NUMeric value

Hope this helps
