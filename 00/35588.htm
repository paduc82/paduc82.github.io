5 years of operation, it may be just about time to replace the battery. It should be CR2032.

I am not sure if your issue is caused by depleted battery, but your problem description seems to be caused by dead battery on the motherboard.

If the battery is dead, your motherboard cannot retain the BIOS settings, RAM timings, etc. So when you first power up your system, all settings try to come to life to BIOS under full mains power. But it is too late for Windows 10 to figure out what settings to start with. In your second attempt to restart, all settings for BIOS are there now and Windows 10 knows what to do and how to configure devices. So your computer fires up.

Therefore, I suggest battery replacement. It is not rocket-science to replace the battery, but you should be very careful not to cause static electricity discharge on any of the devices.

Before attempting battery replacement, remove power from your computer and press and hold the power-on button for ten seconds to completely discharge power on the capacitors. Also refer to the motherboard manual for the location and type of battery.
