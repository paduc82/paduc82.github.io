bill mosca say:

That "Insert row" button is for inserting a new field in the table, not a row in the data. It's actually misnamed. Relational database tables display rows according to indices, one of which is a metadata index where you can sort by a specific field. All of this is only for human eyes. In reality the rows are not ordered.

Fields, on the other hand, are ordered. In design view a table shows the fields vertically. That is just to make it easier to add a field, its data type and other properties.

If you want to insert a row between records you will have to rely on an index. You still have to insert a new record after the last existing record, but refreshing the view will move it to the right place based on the sort. All in all, the order is just for aesthetic reasons and has nothing to do with the physical position.

john w. vinson say: Access tables are NOT spreadsheets; they do not have row numbers . A table should be considered as a bucket of records in no particular order, and you should not use it directly. Instead, use queries and Forms to  interact with data. Forms and queries can be sorted and edited.

john w. vinson say:

Forms are just WINDOWS, not data stores.

Data is stored in Tables.

It is selected, sorted, and arranged in Queries.

It is displayed and edited on Forms.

Right tool for the job!

thedbguy say:
In reply to deleted message
Hi Kaylynn. Database tables are not particularly ordered in any particular way. You could think of tables as buckets and records as marbles. When you drop a marble into the bucket, it will fall and settle in the bucket at no specific spot. Tables are the same way. The database engine just adds the record to the table and does not care where they fall within the table. When you want to see the data in order, you specify the sort order to the database engine. You do that using a query's ORDER BY clause. Can you tell us why you need this functionality, so me might be able to offer a possible solution? Cheers!

bill mosca say:

Hi Kaylynn - Using a form to present records for manipulation (editing, adding, deleting) is the best way to do it. You can put a sort on the form to show the records in the order you wish. You will still need to add new records after the last row but it will be resorted once you close the form and open it again.

Forms can show one record at a time or many records depending on the form view you choose.

As to adding an index, open the table in design view and you will see an index button in the Ribbon. Click that and a dialog box will open where you can select the field(s) you want to include in an index.
