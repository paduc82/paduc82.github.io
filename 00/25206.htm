Hi Jon,

It is not feasible to add a shared personal OneDrive folder to OneDrive for Business.

The Shared with Me folder on your OneDrive for Business is not the same as the Shared List folder on personal OneDrive.

When you open a personal OneDrive link, the feature to add the folder to the Shared List is only available when you log in using a Microsoft account, which means that it can only be added to another personal OneDrive account.

The Shared with Me folder in OneDrive for Business automatically shows content shared with you from other OneDrive for Business accounts or SharePoint Online.

brianthor say:

I FOUND A SOLUTION!!!!

This is not pretty, but it works.

First, make your document as a booklet. In my case, I made a blank document and then when to the "Page Design" tab and clicked on the arrow next to "Page Setup" and change "Layout type" to Booklet. This set the page width to 5.5x8.5, which is what I want for a letter-sized document folded in half in landscape orientation. Click OK. Publisher will ask you about inserting more pages and click Yes.

Second, go back into Page Setup. Change "Layout type" to "One page per sheet." Change width back to what you had set above. In my case, I changed width back to 5.5 and height back to 8.5. Click OK.

Third, design your document and make your mail merge (if you haven't already).

Fourth, when you are ready to print, adjust your printer settings so that your printer will create the booklet. Publisher will send one record at a time to the printer and each booklet will be stapled individual.

If you are printing to a Ricoh copier, choose these settings under Printer Properties (assuming you are starting out with the Basic Setting):

1. Change "Document Size" to match the paper size outlined above. In my case, I changed it to 5.5x8.5.

2. Change "Print On" to the actual paper size. In my case, I changed it to Letter.

3. Change "2 sided" to Off.

4. Change "Booklet" to Magazine.

5. Change "Staple" to 2 at Center.

6. Leave "Orientation" as Portrait (this may vary depending on the type of booklet you are making)

7. Under the tab "Detailed Settings" and the menu "Basic" change "Reduce/Enlarge" to Off.

8. (Optional) After verifying that this works, save these printer settings by going to print and clicking on Printer Properties. With a Ricoh, choose "Register Current Settings" and give it a memorable name. Click OK. Then click Manage to rearrange your presets if you want this setting more visible.

Finally, a note when adding pages. If you created a document in Publisher with booklet mode on, Publisher automatically adds 4 pages to the document to keep the booklet even. When you add pages under the settings above, Publisher will add 2 pages. Make sure the total number of pages is divisible by 4 in your document. Also, make sure the first and last pages remain a single-spread page and the rest of the pages in the middle are either single-spread or double-spread (but not a combination of both).

This should work for you. I hope it helps. I have searched and searched and searched for solutions and have found people asking for the past decade for a solution. I never found any answers. I suppose people gave up. Here's the solution.
