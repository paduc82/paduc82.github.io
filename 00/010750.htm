adnoh---01 say:

Screen resolution

Not really a problem here but something I'm hoping someone can clear up for me.

I have a 6500c, it has a 2" screen with a resolution of 320x240 pixels. Fonts are crisp and sharp.

The N85 has a 2.6" screen with a resolution of 320x240 pixels. Now the same resolution stretched over a larger area should become jaggy and pixelated should it? Yet this does not happen on the N85 screen.

So my question is, how come even though both screens have the same resolution but are different sizes, the larger screen is not pixelated?

Thanks

mccbleue say: No, the issue is no different than a 15" and a 20" TV screen compared.

grschinon say: I think the phenomenon you're referring to, when fonts become blurred and blocky, only occurs when the fonts are rendered at one resolution and then upsized to another. You'd see it frequently on older laptops when you had, for example, a 1024×748 panel but you set the desktop resolution to 800×600. The fonts were rendered to be good on an 800×600 screen but the whole thing was blown up in order to be plastered on a screen actually running at 1024×768.

Given that both screens are the same resolution, 240×320, no extrapolation is taking place and the fonts still look crisp.
