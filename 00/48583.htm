It's a physical issue. It has nothing to do with the power saving settings. The computer is shutting down because the CPU temperature is reaching critical levels and BIOS/UEFI does this to prevent thermal failure and possible motherboard failure as well.

This is often caused by dust and crud blocklng the fan port(s) and also by obstruction or impending failure of the CPU fan. It may also be due to settings having been reset from an OS version upgrade or some updates. You should always check your settings after an update, or at least if you suspect the update/upgrade changed settings to a default state, no matter what the issue is.

The reason I say this is because we had a karaoke DJ using a Dell Dimension E310/3100 series, where the vent is up front in the form of a large perforated grate in an opening  behind the front panel and the CPU cooler is directly behind it. I researched the problem as I have the same chassis style and he came to that same conclusion before I told him and blew it out.

Problem solved for him. I suggest that much the same problem is happening to the OP. Blockage, or obstruction that doesn't allow for air flow. The solutions are to clean out the computer (blow it out/dust it) and keep the vent(s) free of restrictions that limit proper air flow.

A fairly common problem, actually.
