dana.d say:

Hi.  As a general idea, here are some programming thoughts to consider.

When the title is "Listing All Combinations", a good general question to ask on these types of problems is "Why?"  Why list millions of possible outcomes, and is there a much simpler way to do what we want?     What would be the purpose of listing all 3.80E+09 combinations, and then limit the list to 5,000?  It would appear then that the list may not actually be important.

In example #4, if we are given 1 line of input, and want to compare it to the "Big List", the solution could be nothing more then counting the number of n's in the input list.  If the sum is 7 n's, then the input is valid.

In example #4, the total count was

=COMBIN(10,7)
120

To generate your particular list, you don't have 10 items, and it's not size 7.

From an algorithm point of view, what you really have is an integer partition of the number 7 with at most 4 numbers.  Each list padded with blank (for size 4), and then take the Permutations of each group.  For this example, the integer partitions of 7 with at most 4 are:

(A 7 represents 7 n's, 6 represents 6 n's, 1 equal 'n,   etc.)

{7}
{6,1}
{5,2}
{5,1,1}
{4,3}
{4,2,1}
{4,1,1,1}
{3,3,1}
{3,2,2}
{3,2,1,1}
{2,2,2,1}

You are then taking the permutations of each group at size 4 for a total of 120.

Although this is fast, I don't see the purpose of this.

The number 22 at most 15 has 972 partitions.  I would work with this much smaller number if that would help instead of all 3.80E+09 permutations.

HTH  :>)

Dana DeLouis
