cbarnhorst say:

Your other laptop was a registered Window 10 device, meaning the unique identifiers of the device were registered on Microsoft's servers.  With Windows 10 there is no activation data stored on the hard drive.  In W10 the licensing software submits the device's unique hardware identifiers to MS for verification of the device's registration and an affirmative response enables activation or the continuance of activation.  This is why no product key is needed when reinstalling Windows 10 on a device.  The free Windows 10 upgrade was good for the life of the device but it does not apply to a new device.  That is what Joe means by his equation.

A new motherboard has always meant a new system for activation purposes going back to XP so there is nothing new in that pertaining to W10.

Changing motherboards means a new set of unique identifiers like the mobo's serial number and these are what are not registered with Microsoft.

You cannot install an insider build on the new-old computer without purchasing a product key for Windows 10.  In fact it does not matter whether you use installation media for build 14393 (the Anniversary Update) or build 14905 (the current insider preview build).  Activation will require a retail product key for both.
