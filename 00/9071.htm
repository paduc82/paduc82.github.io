  1. Sign in to Outlook.com.

  5. On the Connect your email account page, enter your full email address, and the password of the email account you want to connect to Outlook.com.
  6. Choose OK.
  7. Select Options to return to your Inbox. Your connected account will be included in your folder list.

Problem resolved.

Even though it does not show up in the list of add-ins, ABBYY FineReader software apparently still manages to interfere with Excel 2016 operation through some other mechanism.  When it is uninstalled, Excel 2016 now works properly.  This should be particularly noted by Epson printer users, since ABBYY software has come with many Epson printer models. 

Upon further investigation, last month ABBYY came out with Update Part Number 58250 which allows Epson users to retain the FineReader functionality (most notably the conversion of scanned documents to PDF files) without interfering with Office 2016 (at least upon initial assessment).  If you still need this functionality, the upgrade is a preferable approach to a simple uninstallation.

scottgem say:
Frankly, I do not recommend using the Attachment data type. While it works better than using BLOB (Binary Large Objects) it still is more cumbersome and not scalable.

The way most professional Access developers do this is to have a field that stores the location of the JPG image in a text field in your table. You would then use a form bound to your table and use an Unbound Image control with the controlsource set to the text field containing the image location.

As you cycle from record to record the image control will display the image.
