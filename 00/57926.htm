arnold001 say:

By now you've likely purchased the Pro 3 or made the choice not to.  I'd just like to give some info to some people who may still be on the fence.

1) The Pro 3 has sufficient power to handle PS CS5 and Diablo III.  I am running both now.  There is, however, a caveat.

2) The Pro 2 actually runs Diablo 3 with better graphic settings than the Pro 3.  This is likely due to a) the more aggressive throttling settings on the Pro 3 and possibly b) the more robust cooling solution on the Pro 2.

If you are like me, and you use your Surface for work, you won't notice any issues.  It's with gaming that the Pro 3 is actually less useful than the Pro 2.  There are some ways to fix the issue - look up USB cooling fans and CPU throttling in the forums - but they aren't exactly elegant.

I'm actually using the i7 w/ the Iris pro graphics, and I can say that the Pro 2 with the i5 is smoother in games than the i7 Pro 3.  I kind of wish I hadn't upgraded...but the larger screen and thinner size is just so nice.  It's impossible to go back to the Pro 2 as a result, despite the poor performance in gaming.
