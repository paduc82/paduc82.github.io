matt mickle say:

The #REF Error is produced when a formula refers to a cell that's not valid..... therefore, what you are experiencing is to be expected.  When you create a new sheet with the name you mentioned the formula in the cell will have to be confirmed again with the enter key in order to update the value.

Once, you have created your new sheet when you double click into the cell and confirm with enter the cell will be recalculated.


A better option may be to do the reverse....create all of your sheets and then insert all of your formulas.  This would prevent the manual portion of the process.  This could be done using a macro, which would eliminate manual entry.

myall_blues say:

Hi

Depending on how many formulas you have on your master sheet this may or may not be practical, but you can do what you want by using INDIRECT.

If you create a column of the expected sheet names on your master sheet, and then refer to those using the INDIRECT formula, you can avoid the REF errors and the formulas will populate correctly when you create the sheets.

See image below. In my example, Sheet1 and Sheet 2 already exist, sheets 3-5 do not. The formula in B2 is 

=IF(ISERROR(INDIRECT(A2 & "!B1",TRUE)),"",INDIRECT(A2 & "!B1",TRUE)) and copied down.

Strictly speaking you don't need the INDIRECT and could just enter the IF formula to a non-existent sheet like so

=IF(ISERROR(Sheet1!B1,"",Sheet1!B1)

but doing it this way is a pain to enter because Excel will prompt you for a filename to reference all the time.

Hope this helps

beth melton say:

If Word crashes each time you use Bullets/Number then you may have a corrupt key in the Registry. To correct this issue:

  1. Click Start and then click Run
  2. In the Run dialog box, type Regedit and then click OK or press Enter
  3. In the Registry drill down to:
    HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\11.0\Word\List Gallery Presets
  4. Right-click List Gallery Presets and then click Delete

In this situation you may want to use the Registry/Export Registry file command and export your selected branch. That way if you find the change wasn't necessary or incorrect, you can merge the deleted branch back to the Registry by double-clicking the resulting file.

~Beth Melton Microsoft Office MVP
