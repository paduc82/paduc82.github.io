Few audio sources actually have any rear speaker signal, the main one that does is surround sound Video. This means that natively any standard playback will not produce rear signal such as MP3 etc.

 

By using the "Fill Speaker" option you are instructing the sound card to produce a rear signal when there isn't one. In most cases it is just the front signal with the frequency envelope shifted slightly to reduce the bass and at a lower level so the balance is biased forwards.

 

The lack of a middle speaker has always been a problem even with just stereo speakers. You would often find the dialogue in film playback very low because the audio mix in the recording loads the actors primarily to the centre speaker. Quite often in games if the protagonist was talking in whispers if your character looked at them you could not hear anything, if you turned the view so they were left or right screen they would be much clearer.
