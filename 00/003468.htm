brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

> I have Office 365 and I do have the ads in outlook.com. So... does

> Microsoft intend to remove them for Office 365 subscribers or

> not?!?

Office 365 Home and Office 365 Personal subscriptions both utilize Outlook.com, which you can also use without a subscription. As a result, Office 365 Home and Personal subscribers will see ads when using Outlook.com from a web browser. In order to not see ads when using a web browser, you need an Office 365 for Business subscription or you need and Ad-Free Outlook.com or Outlook.com Premium subscription. The latter two do not require an Office 365 subscription; they are separate offerings.

The result is that Office 365 Home and Personal subscribers and users of the free Outlook.com service will see ads. Subscribers to Ad-Free Outlook.com, Outlook.com Premium, and any of the Office 365 for Business offerings will not.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

> A family member and I signed up to outlook.com and got ads on

> the web interface. We then bought Office 365 Home to get rid of

> the ads.

An Office 365 Home subscription won't affect the ads you see when using the web interface. An Office 365 Home subscription uses plain vanilla Outlook.com for the mail engine, just like a free Outlook.com account does. You need Ad-Free Outlook.com or Outlook.com Premium (totally separate from any Office 365 subscription) to remove ads.
