xero90 say: Hi 6280,

To clear up what the symbols mean....

A G means that GPRS has been requested and signal is available.

A G with and square around it means GPRS connected (so during internet browsing etc)

A G with a line through it means GPRS not available this normally happens during a call or when you receive a text message.

If there is no G it means that GPRS hasn't been requested and you will need to do this again manually.

It may be that you lose your GRPS signal when at home because your house is in a GPRS blackspot or is caught in between two GPRS areas.

I recommend trying your sim card in another phone which has the same network provider as you. If there is an improvement in signal quality I would contact a Nokia Service Centre... If there is no difference I would contact your network provider and find out the reason why signal is so poor.
