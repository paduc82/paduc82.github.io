jeeped say:
SUMPRODUCT((Detail!$J$2:$J$5732="D")*(Detail!$D$2:$D$5732="IA")*(Detail!$AH$2:$AH$5732="Decr")*(Detail!$AJ$2:$AJ$5732))

If you absolutely cannot get rid of the text in column AJ, try this formula instead,
=SUM(IF(Detail!D:D="IA",IF(Detail!J:J="D",IF(Detail!AH:AH="Decr",Detail!AJ:AJ))))
... which is an array formula and needs to be entered with Ctrl+Shift+Enter rather then just Enter.

The above formula uses SUM() which steps over text entries when totaling numbers.SUMPRODUCT() attempts to process them and throws out of the calculation on the first error.

herb tyson say:

In addition to what Tony said, you can control the format by creating an AutoText (gallery) item named AddressLayout, which contains the format you want to use. Ignoring the Word 2000-specific instructions, the layout formatting stuff still applies to Word 2007 and Word 2010 in this article:

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/212345

So, for example, where I live, the following generally works for residential addresses:

{<PR_GIVEN_NAME>} {<PR_SURNAME>}<Press Shift+Enter>
{<PR_STREET_ADDRESS>}<Press Shift+Enter>
{<PR_LOCALITY>}, {<PR_STATE_OR_PROVINCE>} {<PR_POSTAL_CODE>}

Note: <Press Shift+Enter> means that your press Shift+Enter, rather than type <Press Shift+Enter>--this is important to prevent extra line spacing from being appearing in the address. Everything else is typed verbatim. The article cited gives the names for other address fields so you can design the format you need.

So, once you have your format, select it, and in the Insert ribbon tab, click Quick Parts, Save Selection to Quick Part Gallery. Set the Name to AddressLayout, set the Gallery to AutoText, and click OK.

Then insert an address to see if you get what you want. If not, then you might need to experiment a bit (I did) to get exactly what you want.
