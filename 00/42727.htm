dreadnaughts say:

I just have this problem and came across your post. Thought I would give you an answer finally, of sorts...

Anyway, it was obvious that Grzegorz didn't understand your problem exactly but I do.

Symptom:

you extend your desktop (usually to the left for this to happen) and any icons you have on the far right of your main screen will nudge to the left about an inch for no apparent reason.

why?

well it seems that it happens with the second monitor having a lower resolution, and windows being a bit funny about that situation with some alignments.

solution:

Easy. Using the windows resolution applet, drag the second monitor from the top left of the main screen, to the bottom left of the main screen and apply. For some reason using the bottom left alignment does not result in the icons being moved and still allows you to have your extended monitor on the left.

I hope this makes sense. When you first move the second monitor over to the left side, it sometimes defaults to the top left position (i.e. with the tops of both screens running in a line), you need to move the second monitor down so that the bottons are in a line and the problem is solved.

hope this helps you and antone else who has this unusual issue.
