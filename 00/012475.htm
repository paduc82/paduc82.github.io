Its the basic concept of OneNote (on any system) to hold all files in an offline cache copy. This is important for offline availability and the sync protocol.
That cache is cleared when a notebook is closed (it's "main version" still saved on OneDrive). So the only way to prevent OneNote from using up to much memory is to close all notebooks you do not need in constant access.
Maybe you could consider a change in your notes organisation; having just one (or few) notebook(s) which need(s) to be available all the time on your mobile devices.
