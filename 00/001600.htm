suzanne s. barnhill say:

For the margin issues, see http://WordFAQs.mvps.org/BottomLine.htm.

For the spacing between paragraphs, keep in mind that a certain amount of space is automatically added in the form of "leading" (line spacing) that is part of the font. This varies from one font to another. As an example, however, the default line spacing of Times New Roman is approximately 120% of the nominal point size. So there will be 12 points between the baseline of one line of 10-point TNR and the baseline of the line above or below it. Most of the space will be above the line. You'll need to factor that into the space between paragraphs.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

It's extremely rare that an image would display in the Print dialog's Quick Preview [I assume that's what you mean by "print preview" since Print Preview as a feature in Word was eliminated as of O2011], but not actually print on paper. The reasoning behind that is that what's displayed in the Quick Preview actually is generated by the printer, itself. If this truly is the case I'd suggest that you confirm that everything is fully updated, including;

  • Whatever issue of OS X you're using
  • Your installation of Office 2016 is up to date[15.15 is the current version]
  • Check the printer mfr's web site for the latest driver/firmware updates
  • After applying any updates, Restart your Mac... If using OS X 10.10.5 run Disk Utility to repair disk permissions before restarting.

OTOH, if you mean that the images appear in the document while using Print Layout View, that's a different story... have a look in Word> Preferences - Print to confirm that the boxes are checked for both Drawing Objects & Background Colors & Images.

steve winograd say: The problem is that the version of Microsoft Word on your computer is newer than the version of Microsoft Word on your recipients' computers.

The solution is to save your documents in a format that earlier versions of Word can read:

4. Click Word 97-2003 Document (*.doc) .
