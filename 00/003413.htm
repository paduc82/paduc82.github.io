Replace 3 with the number of the volume which is assigned a drive letter. (In hundreds of MBs) 
remove letter="D" 
Replace D with the Recovery drive letter.

jim gordon mvp say:

I prefer simple steps. Sometimes going slow results in less time consumed altogether.

1. Restart your Mac holding the SHIFT key down. This is called doing a Safe Boot. It takes longer to boot this way than normal, so be patient. One of the things a Safe Boot does is prevent any applications from running that normally get started when you start your Mac. This way there's nothing to turn off.

2. Make sure your Mac has a live internet connection

3. Open any Office application. Go to the Help menu and choose Check for Updates to launch the AutoUpdate utility. 

4. Use AutoUpdate to install the updates.

5. After AutoUpdate has installed all the updates, Restart your Mac the normal way.
