New computers that come preinstalled with Windows often have what is called a recovery partition. This is used to reinstall the operating system in the event of a system crash. To access it, you will need to boot into when you start your computer by pressing a function key. This can be either F1, F2, F9, F10, F11, F12 or even DEL or Tab key. Consult the owners manual that came with your PC for instructions about how to reinstall Windows.

If your recovery partition is not available or damaged, you should contact the manufacturer of your computer and request a recovery disc set you can use to reinstall Windows 7. They might charge a small shipping and handling fee.

Unchecking both of them caused the fan's turn-off behavior to be restored to the old box's one (instant stop when console shuts down as expected)

daft badger say:

That feature has been there for a few months now.

 In instant on it will download any console,game or app updates while the console is off.

In energy saving mode,if you have the 2 boxes ticked it will download console updates while the console is off & download any other updates in the background when you turn your console on.

When you turn your console off it uploads saves etc. with the cloud & checks for updates which is why the fan runs for a couple of minutes after you've turned the console off & is perfectly normal.

Click Start > Computer
Right click the Flash drive if it shows up there
Click Format
Click in the File system list box and select FAT32

If that does not work:

Click Start, right click Computer
Click Manage

Expand Storage > click Disk Management

Select the thumb drive in the Volume list
Right click it and click Format
