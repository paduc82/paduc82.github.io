a. user say:

Well, that sounds like we are on the right track then, knowing what the problem is generally helps solve the problem ;)

There are two solutions that occur to me, and both require a second computer you can open the file on (or a friend/co-worker on a different computer to do this for you on their machine).

(actually one solution is just a more vigorous version of the other).

1.  On the other computer open the file, then "save as," and make it into a new file with a new name.  If your excel is latched onto the name, this will solve the problem (I don't think it will though).  If instead it is something in your file, you will have to go with option 2.

2.  On the other computer, open the file, select all sheets, move/copy, new workbook.  (note, do not click copy, you specifically want to move the sheets).  This will create a whole new workbook, with none of the attributes of the old book, that just happens to have exactly the same data, formulas, formatting, etc.

If you have macros you will have to copy them separately, sorry.

I really hope that works for you,

Aaron

PS, the reason to move the sheets instead of copying them is that if you copy, any links between sheets in your workbook will remember that they link to the other sheet in your old workbook-which means going through and fixing all the links.  If you move, those links have to change to your new workbook because that is all that is available to them...if I remember right anyway.  Hopefully you won't have to fix links, I hate having to do that!

Hiding Built-in styles in Word. - Microsoft Community

momtov say:

Hiding Built-in styles in Word.

I would like to remove -- or at least hide -- the built-in styles from my style sheet. Is this possible?

 

rohn007 say:

Yes, you can do more of it in 2007/2010 than in earlier versions. What "style sheet" are you referring to.

Home tab / Styles group / Dialog launcher button (that itsy bitsy, teeny weeny, stupid little button on the group name bar) / to display the Styles Pane / at the very bottom of the styles pane there is an "Options ..." hyperlink / displays Style Pane Options dialog. In there you can select styles to show in the pane: In current doc/ recommended / in use / all styles. And you can select how they are sorted.

If that is not what you want to control there is another dialog. In the Styles pane, just to the left of the Options hyperlink is a Manage Styles button. This will allow you to control what is shown in the style gallery on the Home tab.
