ozrob---01 say:

 

nickchai13 wrote:

I'm using the same firmware and my battery can last for 24 hours only, before update this firmware I still have to charge it after one day. Maybe e71 battery life really low.

 

No. This is not the case. The E71 has one of the longest battery life of any smartphone on the market. Those whose phones only last one day on a full charge either have a faulty battery or phone, have everything possible switched on at the same time, or have some unknown background app sucking power, usually from a poorly conceived app they have installed or some stupid carrier-specific firmware setting.

 

To find out what is going on, download and install an app called Energy Profiler. Run the app for an hour or so in the background and then look at what's happening with the power draw. If there's lots of repetitive big spikes then there is most likely a background process running that is connecting to the internet frequently to monitor something. Press and hold the Home key for a few seconds and you'll get a list of all running apps. If there's nothing listed except for Home Screen, then it's most likely a hidden background task.

 

Common culprits are early versions of Garmin XT, which automatically runs a background service that kills battery life - upgrade to the latest version where background services can be switched off - and carrier-specific firmware that maintains a constant connection to the carrier's online portal - google your specific carrier to see if problems (and solutions) have been reported.

 

Other common batter killers are having your phone continually scan for wifi networks in the background. Turn off the automatic feature and just manually scan when you need to. Also, have sensible settings for things like email scanning - scanning every minute really isn't necessary.

 

If Energy Profiler shows a constant high power drain, then you could have a faulty battery. If under warranty have it checked out.

 

Also, to the OP, if you imported your phone settings from a backup after installing the new firmware, then do a full factory reset and MANUALLY re-enter the phone settings, rather than load them from backup. Restoring phone settings from an OLD firmware to a NEW firmware is problematic as the new firmware may have new/different values for some settings and restoring can cause unpredictable results. Restoring contacts, calendar, etc. if fine, just don't restore phone settings.

The "+B2/10^5" part in the criteria formula is to introduce a fractional tie-breaker which brings in the seniority rank. Stick to using this, do not change the " /10^5"

For the top row formula which extracts data corresponding to the smallest number within the criteria col, you should always use ROWS($1:1). You don't need to change this

When you adapt it to suit where your 1st row of data runs down, just ensure that all the cells above the 1st cell of the criteria formula are empty, ie ensure X1:X5 are empty

 

max, singapore say: Here's a non-array formulas set-up which could achieve the auto-sort by a 2 cols criteria that is sought (It takes care of tie breaks as well)
Assume your source table as posted is in A1:C8
Put in E2: =IF(C2="","",C2+B2/10^5)
Put in F2: =INDEX(A:A,MATCH(SMALL($E:$E,ROWS($1:1)),$E:$E,0))
Copy F2 to H2. Select E2:H2, fill down to H8. Hide away/minimize col E
Cols F to H will return the desired results, ie an auto-sort firstly by "Hours" in col C, then by "Seniority" in col B (for tie breaks)

RunDll32.exe shell32.dll,Control_RunDLL hotplug.dll
