    larryyu_msft3110 say:

    Hi PaulClaude,

    After removing the users license, the user cannot access the mailbox anymore. If you just need to access his mail, you can turn the mailbox into an inactive mailbox by placing it on In-Place Hold or Litigation Hold and then remove his license. More information about the inactive mailbox can be found in the article below:

    https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/dn798632(v=exchg.150).aspx

    Note: Your subscription needs to contain Exchange Online plan 2.

    If the user needs to access his mail as well, you may need to export his mail data to a PST file and then remove his license. Here are two articles for your reference (Export/Import mail data to a PST file through the Outlook client):

    https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Export-or-back-up-messages-calendar-tasks-and-contacts-d791fe88-5c67-44c5-a3f6-a484508082a1
