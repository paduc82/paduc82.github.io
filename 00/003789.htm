Exchange e-mail address stamping policy for Exchange Hybrid - Microsoft Community

exchbin say:

Exchange e-mail address stamping policy for Exchange Hybrid

Hi,

I'm planning a multi-ad/exchange forest to O365-single tenant migration for one of the organizations. The problem we find ourself running into quite often is that when an user account exists with the same UPN on two or more domains.

For eg., let's assume a user *** Email address is removed for privacy *** whose AD UPN is *** Email address is removed for privacy ***. So apparently, his cloud identity gets another e-mail address which is *** Email address is removed for privacy *** which is cool.

Let's assume a different user in domain 2 whose e-mail address is *** Email address is removed for privacy *** whose ad UPN is *** Email address is removed for privacy ***. Naturally, Exchange decides to stamp this user too with the same additional SMTP address for routing (*** Email address is removed for privacy ***) and this user no longer syncs via AD connect to Azure due to a pre-existing conflict with another user in Domain 1.

I'm quite stumped on how to handle this. I changed the default e-mail address stamping policy on on-premise Exchange servers to add domain1/domain2 to their UPN. (i.e., *** Email address is removed for privacy *** / *** Email address is removed for privacy ***). But the exchange won't stop dishing out an extra SMTP address to add an extra alias to whom it thinks does not have it in the right format. I'm not quite sure where. Any insights to solve this deadly problem without the needing to change the UPNs of about hundreds of thousands of users would really be useful.

exchbin say:

Never mind. I figured it out myself.

Amend the address policy on on-premise Exchange server to stamp an unique domain.mail.onmicrosoft.com address.

So instead of *** Email address is removed for privacy ***, users can either get *** Email address is removed for privacy *** or alias1.d1@domain,mail.onmicrosoft.com and the users in domain 2 would get *** Email address is removed for privacy ***. The cloud-on-premise e-mail flow works on this domain.mail.onmicrosoft.com e-mail address, so as long as the object has one and is synced to Azure AD, all is well.

P.S.: There was no invisible force that adds the extra smtp address of domain.mail.onmicrosoft.com. Editing the on-premise address stamping policies was sufficient. Although cloud users get a separate domain.onmicrosoft.com which is unique irrespective. The ones I've seen are the residue of older address stamping policy.

a. user say:

Hi,

Sorry for the late reply.

“By upgrading to Outlook.com, you are only upgrading the user interface of Hotmail to the new user interface; hence all your emails should still stay in your Inbox. However, if your emails were over 270 days old, you may have lost them due to inactivity in account even before you renamed the account. If you had Self-recovery of emails turned on in your account before rename, you can try to recover some of the deleted messages yourself (Link to recover deleted messages is under DELETED folder), refer: link .

 

If you chose to rename your account to @Outlook.com, a prompt would have asked you – ‘Where do you want to save your emails?’ You will have the option to create a new folder for your old account or save the emails in the Inbox of your renamed account.

 

If you chose to create a new folder, the default name of the folder will be the name of your old account and the folder will appear in the left pane under ‘Folders’. And all your old emails will be stored in this folder.

 

Another reason why some users aren’t able to see old emails after rename is when their account was hacked and the hacker had already deleted emails in their account before their renamed the account.

 

Thanks,

kyr'bes say:

SOLVED: Windows 10 Mobile, Error Code 0x80246007 Lumia 535 & 640Xl wont download updates

I recently updated both Lumias from 8.1 to 10.
I did a hard reset after the update and it all works fine, but when I try to download the next OS update (14393.1066) it downloads the update, but won't install it... After "preparing" the update it gives the error code 80246007 :'(
I did a soft reset and hard reset, still nothing....

Can someone help please?

SOLVED: I used the recovery tool to revert it to 8.1, and did a hard reset after.Then I downloaded Windows 10 Mobile via the Upgrade Advisor, after installing the update I alse did a hard reset.
Then the next update downloaded without a problem :D 
For the best update experience hard reset after every major update (I think it's best not to use a backup)


In short:

8.1 - hard reset before downloading the upgrade advisor and Win 10 Mobile
Download Win 10 1151 then hard reset
Download Win 10 1607 then hard reset

Download Win 10 1708 then a final hard reset and you're all set :D

It takes a lot of time but it works :D
