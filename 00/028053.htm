john korchok say:

The default paste for Visio is as a Visio Drawing Object. There isn't much you can do with this in PowerPoint, you need to change it in Visio. 

However, if you click on the bottom half of the Home>Paste button and choose Paste Special, you can paste as a bitmap or a metafile. The bitmap choice will let you alter the chart using PowerPoint's Picture Tools, giving you overall Color changes or Artistic Effects.

If you paste it as a metafile, you can subsequently right-click and choose Edit Picture. You'll be asked if you want to convert the image to a Microsoft Office drawing object. This turns it into native autoshapes that you can reformat like any other autoshape in PowerPoint.

With any of the Paste Special options, you're turning a chart into a drawing, so of course you won't be able to edit it as a chart any more.
