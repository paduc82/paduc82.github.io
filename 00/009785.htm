How can I set up a formula for an entire column? - Microsoft Community

pinz711 say:

How can I set up a formula for an entire column?

I know this is a simple question, but I can't figure it out.  I want to subtract the numbers in one column from those in another, i.e. A-B =C.  I can do it cell by cell, but what's the trick to doing it once for the entire column?

 

Thanks!

t. valko say:

I know this is a simple question, but I can't figure it out.  I want to subtract the numbers in one column from those in another, i.e. A-B =C.  I can do it cell by cell, but what's the trick to doing it once for the entire column?

 

Thanks!


You simply enter the first formula then you copy it as far down the column as needed.

Let's assume you have numbers in the range A2:B10.

Enter this formula in C2:

=A2-B2

With cell C2 still selected, in the lower right corner of the cell you'll see a little black square. Hover your mouse over that square until the cursor changes to a "skinny plus sign". When that happens left double click the mouse and the formula will be copied down to the end of data. That little black square is called the fill handle.

Or, when the cursor changes to the skinny plus sign, left click and hold ("grab") the fill handle then just drag down the column to the end of data and the formula will be copied.
