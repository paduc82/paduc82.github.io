stevem26 say:

Those duplicates are because each time you take one or more photos using Office Lens, it first saves them in a batch.  When you tap save to OneNote, the original and processed image (a pair for each shot) is saved to the camera roll, and the processed copy normally is then saved to OneNote.

Since the save to OneNote process fails, the batch doesn't complete so is left in the Lens app.  If you try again, more copies are saved to the camera roll since it never completes.

The workaround for now would be to take your shots, attempt to save to OneNote.  Now grab the processed copy from the camera roll and add to OneNote manually.  Then back in Office Lens, you can safely delete the current batch of pictures (you can only have five at a time anyway).

A rigmarole I know, but it's keeping me going whilst they fix this.

***SOLUTION FOR ME*** 8/4/14

It is a simple imbedded cookie issue which prevents (not causes) the Gateway or Portal server redirect to "T" (this being the opposite of what I thought from my first post day one here) and its actual proper name is changed every time its deleted and redownloaded (see 3 examples of the same "msn.com" cookie using a formal name in pic below) however the cookies generic name is simply msn.com but its stored/hidden/imbedded in some part of the INetcache which you cannot see or find by looking in the shell:cache as I thought it should/would be.
