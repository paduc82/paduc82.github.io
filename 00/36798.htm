Name: Vampire Slayer FPS

Review Bit:  So recently I’ve been on a sort of weird asymmetrical multiplayer kick and not using the Wii U. Or a true example of the problems with getting this thing called balance all MLG players run their mouths about how the rocket launcher is OP because you killed them with it from the other side of the map yet the sniper is a finely balanced gun because it can kill in 1 hit or 2.

Ok that’s enough of me ranting about modern gaming and time to look at possibly the most generically named game I’ve come across in XBLIG. Now being a fan of the concept of vampire slaying in media, especially if it happens to be slaying vampires called Edward this game managed to almost instantly appeal to me. Sure Night of Blood might have made a better title but the core game concept still appealed to me as I did enjoy watching Buffy the Vampire slayer, and not just because I thought Willow was hot. For the record I watch supernatural too before I get accused of simply watching the show with the hot women in it and not actually enjoying the supernatural elements, though quite how a giant talking hamburger fits it I don’t know.

 

You get put in a map and play a sort of team death match mode with one side taking the role of the vampire and the other the slayers each with their own set of characteristics. The Vampires move silently and get a pounce ability to close the gap on players at the expense of their stealth as the leap is pretty loud, you can kill a slayer in 1 hit close range and feed on their corpse to regain some health if you were injured. Vampires also have one extra trick up their sleeves in the form of resurrection, if you lose all your health you drop down, however after a certain amount of time you will auto revive at 50 health and gain a short period of immunity. The human team get ranged weapons, differing for the two characters except for the all important stake and a torch. The priest gets a powerful shotgun and the defensive crucifix, the female survivor gets an uzi pistol and a stake firing crossbow.

The problem is already while it’s not designed so each side is equal there’s a little bit of imbalance in favour of the humans. You see the crucifix can effectively be spammed and it grants immunity to vampire attacks and burns nearby vampires. Meaning any player who isn’t feeling sportsman like will just spam this all match or expect defensive walls of immune priests with the female survivor behind them using the stake crossbow, that kills in 1 hit if you headshot with it. The main way to kill vampires in the game is to down them then run over and quickly stake them to finish them off.

The game does come with 2 maps however they feel like they haven’t been used to their full potential. While the humans get torches you barely have to use them as from playing it I could mostly see without the need, this hampers the vampires ability to get close at times as you have to rush at a player who will know you’re coming, if the team is defending well. The game does have bot support and this is where an unfortunate issue surfaces as the bots are ok enough. Verdict: It feels like a game that’s almost there, I’m sure if you can get some friend together and play it will be fun enough and you’ll be able to fill in numbers with bots along. You’ll also have a laugh at the ragdoll death system which after a few seconds locks the corpse into its death position, leading to some very comedic positions and opportunity to create some rather rude looking moments. OK it’s a multiplayer XBLIG game and those have the big issue of needing an online community for them (PC games that let anyone host personal servers don’t get this issue so much). With XBLIG it almost requires a perpetual online community hosting and searching for matches and it’s something very few games can manage to do, even bigger games have issues with maintaining an community on console. So for the fact you won’t get to enjoy this game much as its 1 player offline and requires system link or online for multiplayer then I can’t recommend especially as it opens some elements up to very easy abuse online as mentioned. 

Name: Project Gert Recon

Cost: 80

Review bit: Due to a Minor accounting error on Microsofts side I’ve been credited with extra points somehow. As they haven’t fixed it, Microsoft’s loss becomes you the readers gain as I decided to open up the digital moth infested cloth sack I call my feedback inbox. In there I found one of those sort of half request emails I get from time to time asking me to apply for a review code. I decided to try the game as if the trial did enough for me to want to see the game progress then I’d use these extra points on the game.

Well the trail did just enough and I bought the game. I have in fact finished the game on normal.

 

Ok so enough putting it off I have to review this one. Project Gert recon is the Sequel to Project Gert Moonbreaker, remember that one ? No me neither nor could I find anyone else who’s reviewed the damn thing so I can’t tell you if this sequel is improved or not. I can however tell you the game while a unique concept for a puzzle platformer, having platforming and puzzles separated out essentially into different sections. The game starts with telling you the platforming basics, A to jump, X to attack, you can’t attack and jump and A twice to double jump. Shame this tutorial kind of forgets some bits. So let’s begin with the laundry list of issues considering I bought this one. Firstly the box art, if you view the trailer below you’ll see some quite nice impressive art, so quite why they went with the box art they did is a mystery, is it meant to be sexy ? Is it meant to be a subtle parody of XBLIG box art ? I’m not sure, either way I know it looks to me like She Hulk about to transform or something which when your character is called Gert conjures up images of Body building women, not my thing fine if it’s yours but it’s not the universally sexy look I think they were going for. 

 

So onto the game and well considering the previous game boasted 50 puzzles, if you were a fan of the previous game then the 15 puzzles in this one won’t have you too excited and the platforming is only 5 levels. There are additional difficulty levels but for me I’d had enough by the time I finished it once.

The Platforming feels unfinished and like it lacks any direction. The Initial tutorial insists on you doing the basics like jump, double jump and attack then leaves you the fact you can wall jump, which is pretty vital later on. The gameplay itself doesn’t feel like its settled on a platforming genre with the controls allowing for fast precise movement but attacking and combat feels like a snail’s pace comparatively yet its two hits and dead. Essentially the game doesn’t know if it wants to be in the super precise fast twitch area of platforming or the slower approach other games take with attacks and enemies. Speaking of enemies they come in two varieties, K9 from Doctor who after having an unfortunate accident and flying sphere things they randomly attack you and fly off out of range making them a pain to deal with. The idea of these tiny enemies is hinted in the game to be designed to force you to avoid killing enemies but in some locations its near impossible to do.

 

Next onto the puzzle element. There is no tutorial for the puzzles so it’s mostly guess work, which considering the previous title was out in February in 2011 means it’s a bit of a stretch to expect people to know what to do instinctively, especially as this isn’t a generic FPS. So for lack of a tutorial I’ll explain. You have to get the gold block onto the platform with the green on it. You do this by using the various characters abilities, none of which are explained before hand to you really to move the block, as each characters abilities differ rather significantly it means you get some nice cheap failures every time you get a new Moonbreaker ability. You gain points by having other blocks remaining on the level, doing the level quickly and not using all the abilities up to finish a level. What do points mean ? Bugger all as there’s no online leaderboards or ability to challenge friends.

Now onto the further issues of the puxxle mechanic. Peggle is a good game, I like Peggle, I’d say Peggle takes a combination of skill and luck that makes it enjoyable and even failing you enjoyed the time. The problem is with the Moonbreaker puzzles they rely as much on luck sometimes as Peggle. You’re reading someone who spent 30 minutes solid on the Cherry Poke Prison toaster puzzle, you know what. I gave up on some of the Puzzles in Project Gert, I skipped them on my first run as puzzles working so much on pure luck shouldn’t be done, ever. I did go back and complete them later just to prove they were possible to myself so I wasn’t illegitimately saying the game had broken impossible puzzles. Luckily on normal you can skip the puzzles with minor penalty so its fine. The puzzles however unfortunately spotlight another issue in the game. The physics engine. You know what’s great about physics ? It allows you to predict fairly well what will happen, sure anything can happen at any moment for no apparent reason according to the laws of physics but it doesn’t. So to have chaos theory in a game where reliable repeatable physics is essentially is a real pain. I didn’t even have to try to break the game sometimes, it did it for me, and not just in silly ways, in ways that completely break the laws of Physics. Scotty once said, “Ye canne change the laws of physics Jim” yet this game seemingly does exactly that with moments of Solid objects passing through one another and not simply the moving shapes, oh no I’m talking the shapes falling through the non moving platforms. I’m talking Gert sliding backwards off platforms without me touching anything on the controller. Initially I thought it was just me as I tried one of the later abilities, the Vortex and instead of pulling all the blocks in, it fire them outward like a cannon, but no. Part of the luck in the game is to be lucky enough for the damn physics engine to decide to work well enough for you to finish the “puzzle”. Tell me the puzzles are all about timing all you like but one puzzle in there is almost pure luck, relying on a shape turning the precise amount in the air to be able to fit into a certain spot.

Oh and the voice acting is pretty bad, and by pretty bad I mean I’m calling it bad when I found Trailer Park King to be passable voice acting wise or at least on par with some bigger games. For me to call voice acting bad means it was all too obviously read from a script on the take that was recorded. The story isn’t that great either relying more on external events to add story elements then leaving it with no real conclusion, most likely open for a sequel.

 

So I’ve just slated the game for what 750 words straight or so, was there anything I liked about it ?

First the concept is good for the puzzles, the execution not so much but the idea is there.

Second the non combat platforming works with the controls being sharp enough to stop that many silly deaths (even if there is some blind jump platforming involved).

Thirdly, Um er, the concept art stuff / custscene art looks nice

Verdict: Project Gert: Recon is a game that needs to decide what it wants to be as it’s neither good as a puzzle game or a platformer. If the sequel picks one and works on it then I hold out some hope, or even if they split it into two different games and have an intense action platformer and a physics based puzzler. As is Project Gert: Recon has the issue of having the platforming be more of a puzzle and the puzzles contain more action sequences. I can’t in any good conscience suggest this game is worth your time or money. All I can say is it might be worth downloading the demo for Project Gert : Moonbreaker instead and if you enjoy that you’re getting 3 times more puzzles than this version. Also did I mention how annoying it is to have a killer snowman in the game with no indication its harmful ? Well it is as is the lack of subtitles.

Name: Comet !

Cost: 80

Review bit:  It always seems strange when I come back to a game from the beginning of XBLIG’s days and this is one of those strange moments. What’s stranger still about this title is I actually kind of knew the developer. You see long ago the developer for this game along with myself were regular posters on a forum for Kodu gamelab. If anything this games existence suggests Kodu gamelab can inspire people to give creating actual games a go.

Now having known the guy who made this isn’t however going to stop me being fair an unbiased here as the game while fun and functional it’s a flawed experience.

In Comet! You play a spaceship with your objective being to intercept comets falling to earth. To do this you press the colour of the comet you wish to go after and your ship homes in on them. You’re given a set colour series to acquire in that order gaining your extra points and / or more time. The problem is a minor flaw means if you acquire the same colour as the starting comet in the sequence twice it resets entirely meaning you need to get a 3rd version of that colour to begin the sequence.

The controls for the ship also don’t help as while your ship homes in on the target it will automatically fly through other coloured comets, now with colour comet chains giving you points changing your ships colour or hitting another comet breaking the chain is rather annoying, the solution is you have to use the analogue stick to control and avoid obstacles. This however brings up the question of why not just fly the whole game with the analogue stick, and not require the having to press the buttons to set the ship in motion.

My final Minor complaint is that the space junk randomly coming across the screen at you doesn’t stand out anywhere near enough so often deaths to it feel rather cheap.

On the brighter side the game does have nice visuals, a charming fake retro B movie art style to titles and into and voice acting present which does work for it.

Verdict:

Honestly the presentation feels like it should be there for an entirely different game and not a rather simplistic and flawed game. While fun and functionally solid I can’t recommend it due to what is essentially flaws in some of the core concepts of the game unlike glitches they don’t break the game as they are part of it and as such lead me to say the game is flawed. I will suggest you try the demo though as while a flawed concept it is something different and who knows maybe you’ll find your views differ greatly from mine.

 

Name: cylinder

Cost: 80

Review bit:  So a tile rotating game hhhmmm. It’s pretty hard to say much about Cylinder as really there’s not a lot to the game. It’s a standard tile matching game. You pick a section of 4 tiles in a square and rotate them to try and match up blocks of the same 4 tiles. It’s very much like Hexic HD but without the whole flow and star stuff. The gimmick as such here is the game is played with the blocks in a 3D cylinder you rotate round when you move the selection to the sides.

To the games credit the 3D effect looks good and despite the tiles being coloured I think due to the designs on most of them they might have managed to make it possible for colourblind gamers to also play this one. To the games determent though it’s just not that fun there’s no real action to it as such and it just all feels rather dull to do with no gimmicks itself beyond the 3D cylinder so no real hooks to keep you going gameplay wise. 

Verdict:

I recommend this title only to those hardcore Hexic fans who are fed up of hexic. Otherwise due to how quickly the game feels like it’s becoming stale and dull while playing I say you’re best avoiding this one.

 

Name: Guppy Collect 2

Cost:  80

Review: I must admit I downloaded the trail for this game a long while ago and intended to write this then. Unfortunately for me and fortunately for this game other things came up and my brain refused to let me play this one.

I miss my brain protecting me like that as I finally decided to play this game and realised why I was so apprehensive about playing it. You see Guppy Collect 2 is at its core a quick time event, or more correctly a series of quick time events presented as a game. You play as the guppy, you have a time limit, you have objects infront you, to progress you press the button/s shown on the item and collect it moving forward. If you press the wrong button you lose a cat (life) and the same if you run out of time. The incentives to keep going are higher score, which isn’t save anywhere and you can’t compete with friends or others via any online high scores system, and extra skins / costumes for your guppy. The environments are fairly nice and the items at least have a little variety but  theres really no escaping the fact this is just one giant QTE and nothing else.

Verdict:

I can only recommend this to people who love quick time events in gaming and really want to see a game with nothing but those essentially. Otherwise guppy collect is a pretty basic game with little interesting to it beyond its style. The only real longevity in the game would be collecting the costumes and I have to question if it’s really worth slogging through a game that’s quite boring and repetitive just for a few different appearance options , which you won’t use due to being fed up with the game by then.
