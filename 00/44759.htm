pranavmishra5582 say:

Hi Paul,

I suggest you to boot to safe mode and check if you are able to boot. If the operating system is able to boot successfully and allows you to log on to your computer with an administrator account, entering into Windows 10 Safe Mode should be easy (and so would rectifying the issue). Boot the computer to Safe mode and check if it persists. Follow the steps given below to boot the computer in safe mode.

 

  1. Press Shift Key and then restart your computer,
  2. Windows 10 will reboot and ask you to select an option. Choose Troubleshoot.
  3. Then, on the Troubleshoot screen, select Advanced options.
  4. On the Advanced options screen, choose Startup Settings.
  5. Windows 10 notifies you that you can restart your device in order to change advanced boot options, including enabling Safe Mode. Press Restart.
  6. After Windows 10 reboots, you can choose which boot options you want enabled. To get into Safe Mode, you have three different options.
  7. To enable Safe Mode press the F4 key on your keyboard.

You could face this issue due to corrupt or outdated display driver. You can also try to download the latest driver from the manufacturer's website.
