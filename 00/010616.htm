ossiemac say:

There are different types of DropDowns. I am assuming by DropDown that you are referring to Data Validation Dropdown. Is this correct? If so, if you only have the 5 options of 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 then you can just insert the numbers all separated by a comma in the Source field of the Data Validation Dialog instead of using a range on the worksheet.

You can also place the list of numbers on another worksheet and the Source field then has the worksheet name and range like example below. However, the easiest method of inserting a range in the Source field is to click the icon at the right end of the field and you will get a new popup field. Then change worksheets and select the list and the range will be inserted for you. After selecting the list, click the icon at the end of the displayed field to get back to the dialog box.

=Sheet2!$A$1:$A$5

The worksheet with the DropDown list can then be hidden. Just right click the worksheet tab name and select Hide. (Unhide again by right clicking any worksheet tab and select Unhide and follow prompts to complete.

If you are not using a Data Validation Dropdown then please get back to me and explain how you created the DropDown.
