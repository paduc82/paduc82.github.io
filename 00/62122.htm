how to identify usb port

a. user say:

When you plug in the flash drive to any USB port, it will appear in Windows Explorer with a Drive letter (you may have some installation software to run first and you may need to format the drive - it depends on the drive and how it came).  That's all you need to access the drive.  You don't need to identify the port to use the flash drive.  The port itself is not really identified except in Device Manager where you could disable one of the ports and then see which one no longer worked and then that would be the port (but that would just be used to repair the port or re-install drivers if necessary).

I hope this helps.

tryx3 say:

Those entries would be perfectly normal if it were not for that strange symbol.  Authenticated users, Administrators and Users are simply groups of users on your computer.  Since you are logged on using an Admin account you are a member of all three groups.

You would normally expect to see COMPUTERNAME\Administrators and COMPUTERNAME\Users.  If your computer name is XEAL followed by a symbol that Windows cannot easily cope with then perhaps that explains what you are seeing.

- - - - - - - - - - - -  - - - -

About the "ConsentUI" [also known as "Secure desktop"] dialogs you are seeing -

they are also perfectly normal.  The way Windows works is that, as an Admin, are still logged in with "Standard user" permissions and the ConsentUI dialog is seeking your agreement that you wish to invoke your Admin status for the specific task identified in the dialog ["Show more details"].

You will always get such ConsentUI dialogs for tasks that affect the computer more widely than just within your own user account.  You can change the level of alerts you get by altering the "UAC" setting at

Start menu,

Windows system,

Control panel,

User accounts,

Change User account control settings.
