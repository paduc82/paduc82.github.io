Blocking access to office 365 from vpn service providers - Microsoft Community

david feifer (dfeifer) say:

Blocking access to office 365 from vpn service providers

We had an account hacked today and the hacker was using a consumer vpn service out of new York to bypass geofencing. Is there a way to block this type of service all together short of inputting all the ip's in a deny access list?
marvin ma msft say:

Hi David,

Based on your description, an Office 365 account was hacked and you'd like to block users access by VPN. As far as I know, it's not available to directly achieve this. What's more, to set up related access polices in your organization, you may need an on-premises environment with Windows Server 2016. So for this issue, we suggest you reset the hacked account password and block this account accessing for a while, then set up multiple-factor authentication (MFA) for your users to avoid users directly signing in by user credentials.

1. Use admin account to login Admin portal, then navigate to admin center > Users > Active users.

2. Click More > Multifactor Authentication setup.

3. Select all the users and enable MFA.

After that, it's necessary for users to access Office 365 with another authentication method except for user credentials. For your reference, you can make a view of Plan for multi-factor authentication for Office 365 Deployments. And if there's any difficulty in the progress or you have any further question on this issue, feel free to let us know.

Regards,

Marvin
