BIOS is firmware to perform hardware initialization during the booting process and for pre boot configurations. CMOS is a battery that stores you BIOS settings. If you remove the battery and power off PC, the BIOS is reset to default. The battery prevents this from happening when unplugged from A/C power.

CMOS is a coin battery that you see in watches, car fobs and many other gadgets and devices, but these are a little larger. Usually CR2032 battery is used on PC's.
