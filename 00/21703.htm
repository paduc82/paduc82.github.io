techie chick say:

Two things to understand first.

1. Email folders listed on your Gmail web account are on the Gmail server.

2. Email folders listed under "On My Computer" are on your computer hard drive.

If you want your folders to be in sync on Gmail server and on other devices like your cell phone and Mac computer, then yes folders need to be created in Gmail and it will sync back to Outlook on your Mac.

But if you want to create folders more freely and directly on your computer hard drive under "On My Computer", you can do so.  Just know it won't be synced back onto the Gmail account for access by your other devices.

When you are on your Macbook and want to clean up mail out of your Outlook Inbox,

  1. drag and drop them into your folders under "On My Computer", effectively creating a copy of them
  2. check the folder to make sure it made it in there
  3. go back to the Inbox and delete those emails

Again, just remember

  1. those that you move over to "On My Computer" folders will not be visible by your other devices, just via your Macbook computer on Outlook.
  2. those that you leave on the Gmail server folders are synced and visible to all devices.

Did this clarify things for you?

issue with lookup table in a hidden sheet -

I created a spreadsheet for a client to use for some monthly reporting.  We have an extract function in our system to pull data, but it only pulls codes, not the associated descriptions, so I added a lookup table in a separate sheet, and a formatted "report" in a 3rd sheet. 

 

I was hoping to hide the lookup table to prevent any confusion for the users (who aren't well-versed in excel).  When testing, I was getting all "N/A"'s on the report.  As soon as I un-hid the lookup table, it worked fine.  Does this seem right?  Is there any way to reference a hidden sheet as I'm describing?

jllatham say:

Actually this doesn't seem right - you should be able to hide the sheet with the lookup table on it with no trouble.

If you were using some type of code to retrieve the data from the hidden sheet that required that the sheet and/or cells on the sheet be .Selected then I could understand this.  But a simple formula such as (just made up for example):

=VLOOKUP(A3,MyHiddenSheet2!A$3:C$14,3,FALSE)

should work just fine.

Be sure that in your VLOOKUP() formula that the lookup array is referenced using absolute row numbers as I've shown above - if you don't, then Excel will increment the row numbers as you fill the formula down the sheet and eventually will increment beyond the table area and you'd legitimately start getting N/A errors.
