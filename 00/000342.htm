macuser_110 say:

I figured it out! For anyone who upgraded to High Sierra and is now having this weird Helvetica/Times New Roman issue, here is the solution:

  1. Make sure your version of Word is the latest (15.41).
  2. Then, open a Word document, blank or existing, doesn't matter.
  3. Go to Tools > Auto-Correct > AutoFormat as You Type.
  4. Check the box in the Replace as You Type section for "Straight quotation marks"with "smart quotation marks."

This will create curly quotation marks for TNR in Word.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Log into your Yahoo account at http://mail.yahoo.com/ using a web browser so you see your Inbox there.  IOn the left-hjand pane, click the word "Contacts".  Then click the word "Tools" that appears in the menu bar of the web page (not the browser's menu bar).  Click Export from the drop-down menu.  Next to the words "Microsoft Outlook", click "Export now".  That will generate a CSV (comma-separated values) file that you can then import into http://people.live.com/.  Log into your People page and click Manage>Add people.  Click Import from file, click Browse to locate the CSV file you save, select it, click Open, then click "Import from file".
