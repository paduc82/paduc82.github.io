Welcome to Microsoft community.

 

We understand you're getting a lot of email spam on your account. There are many possibilities why we acquire a huge number of junk mails, some of the following are:

 

  • Senders email address is not added in our contact list resulting that their emails are filtered as junk.
  • Your address is associated to phishing sites.
     

To resolve this you can try the following solutions:

 

  • Add the email addresses that are known to you in your safe senders list.
  • Block emails from potential spammers.
  • Set your Junk Mail filter to Exclusive.

 

You can follow the steps below on how to add email address in safe senders, block spammers and setting your Junk Mail filter to Exclusive.

 

Add in safe senders list

 

1. Sign-in to your account.

2. Click the Gear Icon located at the upper-right side of the page.

3. Select More mail settings.

4. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

5. Click Safe senders.

6. Enter the email address or domain that you want to add in your safe senders list

7. and click Add to list >>.

 

Blocking emails / contacts

 

Note: You can skip step 1 and 2 if the emails that you want to block are in the Junk folder. 

 

1. Choose the emails that you want to block in your inbox, by clicking the check box on their left. 

2. Tagged them as Junk by clicking Junk located at the top beside "Delete". 

3. Go to your Junk folder, choose all or the preferred emails that you want to block. 

4. Click Sweep and click Block from.

5. A pop up window will appear to confirm that "You're about to block the following senders" 

6. Click Block all. 

 

Configuring junk mail filter

 

1. From your Hotmail inbox, click the gear icon then More mail settings

2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting

3. Under Choose a junk email filter, select Exclusive.

Note: When you set your junk email filter to Exclusive, any emails that are NOT in your contact list and safe sender list will be automatically sent to your junk email folder so you should occasionally check your junk email folder to make sure that good messages don't get put there by mistake.

Example

Contents

Just wanted " im writing a sentence" in the TOC and not the rest of the header 2.

john korchok say:

Select the paragraph that you want to have only the first sentence appearing in the TOC, and apply some other style that is not in the TOC. (Normal will work for a demonstration). Then select only the first sentence and apply Heading 2. By default Heading 2 is both a paragraph and character style, so you can apply it to a portion of a paragraph. Then update the TOC. Viola! Only the sentence formatted with Heading 2 appears in the TOC.

Probably, you don't want a mismatch in appearance between the first sentence in Heading 2 and the following text. So make a new style that has the same characteristics as Heading 2, but call it something else (could be "Not in TOC") and in the Paragraph settings for the style ensure its level is Body Text. Apply it to the paragraph, then apply Heading 2 to the first sentence. Now the appearance matches, but only the first sentence appears in the TOC.

stefan blom say:

You can insert a style separator by pressing Alt+Ctrl+Enter and then you can apply a non-heading style, such as Body Text, to the portion of the paragraph following the style separator.

When working with style separators, it is best to display nonprinting marks. Click the button on the Home tab. You'll find that an ordinary paragraph mark is represented by a pilcrow, , while a style separator is represented by a pilcrow surrounded by a dotted line.
