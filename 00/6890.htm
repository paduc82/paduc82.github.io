    jay freedman say:
    Since you copied from a Word 2007 document, you probably brought along the paragraph style that was applied to the text in the table. The default in 2007 is line spacing of 1.15 lines within the paragraph and 10 pt of Space After, compared to the Word 2003 default of 1.0 line spacing within the paragraph and no Space After.

    If that's the cause, select the whole table and go to Format > Paragraph. Set the line spacing to Single, and put zeros in the Space Before and Space After boxes.

    That's right but only if your Wheel click is defined as Middle-click.  E.g. when I use a mouse which is not defined that way I often get some unexpected alternatives.   ; )



    [image of Tab Settings shows  Always switch to new tab when they are created is checked (a default) and other settings which will ensure no new windows are opened unless explicitly requested]

    Then with those settings to open a new tab in the foreground you would press Ctrl- and click.   Middle-click would probably have the same effect.   If you wanted to open a new tab in the background you would press Ctrl+Shift- and click.  Those procedures have been defined since IE7 when tabs were first introduced.  A related feature which was not well documented is what happens if  Always switch to new tabs when they are created is unchecked.  In that case the meaning of Ctrl- click and Ctrl+Shift- click are reversed.


    Otherwise the consequences of just clicking on a link depend on the coding of the link and some of your other Tab Settings.  E.g. if the coding is designed to open the link "in a new window" or as a popup window then your settings ensure that they should open in a new tab, still in the foreground.  However, you can always override that action by pressing Shift- (only) while clicking.  Again, this is nothing new, unchanged since IE7.
