In general, an individual file has no sync/move to options via UI in OneDrive for Business libraries as it is a view not an actual library storing the files. You can use the following workaround to move the files to your OneDrive for Business.

 

1.   Make sure you share a folder instead of a file.

2.   To sync a folder, click on the folder in "Shared with Me" and let it will forward you to its original location.

3a. You can then press Sync button to sync the folder to local, and then move to your own OneDrive sync folder.

3b. You can also select the files, and then press Copy to/Move to to move them to your own OneDrive for Business

john mcghie say:
Gidday Lou:

Daniel's correct: you apply the language to the "Text" not the "Dictionary".

Typically in Australia, the dictionary should be set to "No language" so it can store words from any document, no matter what the text is marked as.

If you set your Custom Dictionary to English AUS, then you have to create a new custom dictionary for each one of the 29 different flavours of English you could encounter, otherwise you will not be able to add words when you get text from the internet in a language for which you do not have a custom dictionary.

If you want all your documents to spell in English AUS, in the Blank document that appears when Word starts, go to Tools>Language and choose English(AUS).  Then click the Default button, and say Yes to the prompts.

If that doesn't work, come back, you need to do a slightly different procedure.
