hansv mvp say:

When only one sheet is selected, Activate and Select have the same effect. The difference comes into play when multiple sheets are selected, and you activate or select a sheet within the group of selected sheets.

 

Let's say that your workbook has sheets Sheet1, Sheet2, Sheet3, Sheet4 and Sheet5.

Select Sheet1, Sheet2 and Sheet3, either by clicking on the sheet tab of Sheet1, then Shift + clicking on the sheet tab of Sheet3, or by executing

 

Worksheets(Array("Sheet1", "Sheet2", "Sheet3")).Select

 

You'll see [Group] after the name of the workbook in the title bar, and the sheet tabs of Sheet1, Sheet2 and Sheet3 will be white (using the default formatting).

 

Sheet1 will be the active sheet within the group of selected sheets:its name is bold.

 

The line

 

Worksheets("Sheet2").Activate

 

will leave Sheet1, Sheet2 and Sheet3 selected (you'll still see [Group] in the title bar), and make Sheet2 the active sheet within the selection: the sheet name of Sheet1 will be unbolded, and that of Sheet2 will be bold.

 

But the line

 

Worksheets("Sheet2").Select

 

will ungroup the sheets (you won't see [Group] in the title bar any more), and leave only Sheet2 selected.

 

Remark: if Sheet1, Sheet2 and Sheet3 are selected, the lines

 

Worksheets("Sheet5").Activate

 

and

 

Worksheets("Sheet5").Select

 

have the same effect: the grouping is undone, and Sheet5 becomes the only selected sheet.
