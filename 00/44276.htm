Are you doing anything to your original files on the desktop?  When you "add" your pictures (or videos or music) to a Movie Maker project, you aren't adding a copy into the project file.  You are just adding a reference to that file, wherever it resides on your computer.

 

The project file is akin to a recipe, where it lists the ingredients and how to put them together.  But it still needs those ingredients to exist when it later goes to create the movie.

 

In other words, if you get rid of the source files (photos, videos, music) that are stored on your desktop, you'll end up with a project that points to nothing.  This is different than PowerPoint or Word, where the actual content of the picure gets embedded into the save file.  With Movie Maker (and video especially), the design is to just refer to the source files on your computer.  So you must keep both your source files and the project in order to work.
