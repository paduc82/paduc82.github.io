vijay b say: Hi Peter,

Method 1: I would suggest you to run network troubleshooter.
a. In the start Menu Type “Identify and Repair Network Problem” and hit enter.
b. Follow the Onscreen instructions to complete the wizard.

Method 2:  Firware reinstall - this would set your Duo back to automatically getting an address from the DHCP server of your router, just as your macs are setup. Alternatively:

Note: Make sure that you re-install the firewall as soon as the troubleshooting is completed.

Method 3: . Direct connect to the Duo and reconfigure the IP settings - to do this, temporarily setup one of your macs ethernet port to have the static address 10.0.0.1, connect a cable from the ethernet port of the mac directly to the ethernet port of the Duo (ie no router inbetween). Then access Frontview at https://10.0.0.50/admin and update the IP settings to either be a static address (eg 192.168.1.50), or to automatically obtain the address. Then reset your mac settings back as they were and plug the Duo back into your router.

My recommendation would be to set the Duo address to be automatically obtained. If you check out your router settings, you will probably find that there is an option to reserve IP addresses - this means that, even though addresses are assigned automatically, you can ensure the Duo always gets the same address. The reason this is beneficial is that you gain all the benefits of auto setup (and avoid this specific issue in future should you make further network changes) while still effectively getting a static address, which is of course often needed

For sidelongmjp and anyone else experiencing this issue, I did finally find a resolution after trying all of the potential solutions in numerous threads around the web.


Can't guarantee this will work for everyone, but it did work for me and was relatively painless, and I can now see my NAS Shares in Windows 8 on both my wired and wireless connections as expected, just like in Windows 7.


1) Go into your Network Connections

2) Right-click on your network adapter and choose Properties

3) UNINSTALL the Client for Microsoft Networks (usually the first item)

4) Immediately REINSTALL the Client for Microsoft Networks item


This is all it took for my NAS Shares to reappear, so it seems like a setting gets corrupted somehow in Windows 8, and reinstalling the default networking protocol fixed it for me.

I managed to solve this problem.  I have no idea if it is typical, or what may have contributed to it.  It was a new Surface, trying to attach to an established Win8 Homegroup on a common wireless lan workgroup.

 

The problem was two-fold. 

1) Go back to your Homegroup machine and VERIFY that in Control Panel, for Private Networks (assuming yours is) that Sharing is indeed turned on.  For some reason, mine had been turned off.

 

2) I found on my Surface that the Microsoft Windows "Services" utility, had been turned OFF. Why? Who knows! But it was and I didn't do it.  I figure Msft troubleshooter might have..  To find that 'Service', I was into Computer Management, and was looking at what was enabled and disabled under Microsoft.  I turned it on, and BANG, I could access shared machines via Network, and Homegroups.

 

NOTE! Homegroup can take a while, and even a reboot before it suddenly seems to see 'everything'.  Maybe it is a slow indexer. I don't know.  But if you can see Homegroup folders but can't access them, give it a day and a reboot before you throw in the towel.
