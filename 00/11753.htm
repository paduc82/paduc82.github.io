inipo say:

Emails disappearing when moved to folders in Outlook 2013

I have found similar reports but not this particular issue. This is my first post, I hope it helps!

One of our customers had IMAP email and we moved them to Office 365 and Office 2013. Since, she has run into issues where she will create new subfolders in folders, she had when she was on IMAP, and then cannot see any email she files in these. She can however find them when doing a full mailbox search.

It turns out that exchange flags these folders as IMAP and automatically filters them, hiding read mail for whatever reason. You can remove the auto generated filter by selecting the folder > Select the View tab > Click View Settings > Filter > Advanced > Highlight all and click remove.

Solution:

Option #1: Change mail to keep offline to "All" by going to File > Info > Account Settings > Account Settings > Email Tab > Double click the account with the issue > Check "Use Cached Exchange Mode" and move the slider all the way to the right.

Option #2: Recreate the folder structure and move any emails already in the IMAP folders to these. To do this you need to start with the highest level folder that is not IMAP and recreate from there.

Option #3: (I have not tested this) Export all email to a PST and reimport it. From what I hear this converts any IMAP folders to Exchange format.

Note: You can tell a file is IMAP quickly by right clicking on it and you will see the option that says IMAP.
