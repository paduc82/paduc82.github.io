Assumption:  Grandparents purchased a full retail license kit for Office Home and Student 2007.  This would have been purchased at a local store, and the package would be a clear plastic one rounded corner container, with a pivoting disk holder tray.  On the outside top of the container is the Certificate of Authenticity (if retail it does not have the product key printed on it).  On the back of the pivoting disc holder tray is the yellow-orange Proof of License label with the Product Key printed on it.  The paperboard liner with the product name and such has a light to medium yellow coloring.

 

If you have the above you have a full retail license.  Para 16 of the Software Licensing Terms (SLT) ( http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/products/microsoft-software-license-terms-mslt-for-the-2007-microsoft-office-system-HA010210317.aspx )allows you to transfer (aka sell) the license to someone else:

 

16. TRANSFER TO A THIRD PARTY. The first user of the software may make a onetime transfer of the software, and this agreement, directly to a third party.
