vanessa ouyang msft say:

Hi Jimmy,

Yes, it is achievable. For the first question set online archive after 12 months as the default one for new users, you can run this script in Power Shell:

Set-RetentionPolicyTag "Default 2 year move to archive" -Name "Default 1 year move to archive" -AgeLimitForRetention 365.

Before that, use an admin account to connect Exchange Online with Power Shell:

https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/jj984289(v=exchg.160).aspx .

In this way, all new users are assigned the default policy about online archive after 12 months.

For the second question assign another archive policy to users in Group 2, you can use an admin account to log into Exchange Admin Center> Compliance management> Retention tags> add a new personal tag> add a new retention policy> add the retention tag created above to this retention policy> we can apply this different policy to a single user or some users according to their own attributes such as old policy.

Here is an article for your reference: Apply a retention policy to mailboxes.

steve rindsberg say:

Saving as PPTX should solve the problem (although it won't help if people open the presentation in earlier versions of PPT).

Here's the problem:

In PowerPoint 2007 and up, each PARAGRAPH can have its own tab settings.

In earlier versions, the paragraph settings apply to the entire text shape and all the paragraphs in it.

When you save from PPT 2007 (PPTX) to PPT 2003 (PPT) format, the text box gets its tab settings from the first paragraph in the text box.  When you reopen the saved PPT into PPT 2007, all of the paragraphs in the text box inherit the text box's tabs.
