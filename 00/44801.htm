Be aware, however, that tracking down the causes may be more difficult that it looks like. For example, your game needs 512 MB RAM - how much is installed on your PC? If it's >= 2 GB, the game's probably not the culprit. You may have to look for other programs or processes running at the same time (try the "Determine if a program overuses memory" section of the above link). Sometimes you may run out of disk space instead (as it is used to expand your virtual memory and can give a "low memory" alert, too). Sometimes broken installations of something can cause this error; if you can identify an out-of-control process, all may be resolved by an uninstall/re-install of the corresponding software. - The easiest thing you can try: When the issue occurs again, open Task Manager, kill the process "explorer.exe" ("end process") and restart it by "New Task (Run...)" in the "File" menu of Task Manager. This alone helps surprisingly often.
