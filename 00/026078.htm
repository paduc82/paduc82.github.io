suzanne s. barnhill say:

Although a macro is quick, it's probably just as easy to do this with Word's Replace feature. With the insertion point in a footnote (or viewing the Footnotes pane in Draft view), search as follows:

  1. Press Ctrl+H to open the Replace dialog.
  2. Click More>> to expand the dialog.
  3. In the "Find what" box: type ^f (the code for a footnote reference mark).
  4. With the insertion point in the (empty) "Replace with" box, click Format, then Style..., and choose Footnote Reference.
  5. Click OK to close the dialog, then click Replace All.

With respect to the page numbering note the following from http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/CustomizeNormalTemplate.htm 

Changes that are not recommended

Because the Normal template is the basis for labels as well as ordinary documents, it is strongly recommended that you not add a header or footer (even something as simple as a page number) to the Normal template. This will adversely affect the layout and alignment of labels.

What you really should do is create a separate template for your client that has the required features in it.  To facilitate it's use, you could put a button on the Quick Access Toolbar that runs a macro to create a new document from that template.

hansv mvp say:

Try this:

 

Private Sub cmdOpenReport_Click()
    Dim strWhere As String
    If Not IsNull(Me.txtStartDate) And Not IsNull(Me.txtEndDate) Then
        strWhere = "[DateField] Between #" & Format(Me.txtStartDate, _
            "mm\/dd\/yyyy") & "# And #" & Format(Me.txtEndDate, _
            "mm\/dd\/yyyy") & "#"
    ElseIf Not IsNull(Me.txtSpecificDate) Then
        strWhere = "[DateField] = #" & Format(Me.txtSpecificDate, _
            "mm\/dd\/yyyy") & "#"
    Else
        MsgBox "Please fill in either start and end date or specific date"
        Exit Sub
    End If
    DoCmd.OpenReport ReportName:="rptMyReport", View:=acViewPreview, _
        WhereCondition:=strWhere
End Sub

 

Substitute the correct names for the command button (cmdOpenReport), the date/time field (DateField) and the report (rptMyReport).

what does it mean that the 2 download disk is for 1 user, 2 pcs? - Microsoft Community

t. ferg say:

what does it mean that the 2 download disk is for 1 user, 2 pcs?

Whatdoes it mean that the 2 download disk is for 1 user, 2 pcs?  I have a small office with 4 computers and each computer is used by a different person.  Can I buy 2 of the 2 download disks or do I need 4 of the single download disks?   Thanks
dan at it associates say:

To be in full compliance with the letter of Office 2010 Software Licensing Terms (SLTs), under the circumstances you describe,  you would need one license per computer, either the 1-User 1-PC type or the 1-User 2-PC type.

 

The 1-User 2-PC type would not help "stretch" the licenses so that only two 1-User 2-PC licenses would be needed.

 

The way to decide is to consider whether any of your users would need to install and activate Office 2010 on another computer they use (say, a laptop), or whether your office computers are going to be retired and replaced with new computers in the near future.

If your users have additional computers then that's a strong case for the 1-User 2-PC license.

Because the 1-User 1-PC license is not permitted to be moved to another computer (while the 1-User 2-PC license is permitted), if the office computers are XP or Vista computers, you have to think they they are at or within a couple of years of retirement, so being able to move the Office 2010 license to a new computer would be a reason to go with the 1-User 2-PC license.
