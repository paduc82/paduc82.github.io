uninstalling windows 10 and reinstalling on another computer. - Microsoft Community

robertjmunro say:

uninstalling windows 10 and reinstalling on another computer.

can i move windows 10 from one computer to another?

cmdrkeene say:

That depends on your license agreement (how you obtained Windows 10 in the first place).  If you bought it yourself at the store (on a CD for example), then it's yours to do with what you please and you can remove it and put it on another computer as you wish (as long as you follow the rules and remove it from your old computer).

However, if you got Windows 10 as a free update on the 1st computer, then it's non-transferable.  Same story if Windows 10 came with the computer pre-installed from the factory. 

robertjmunro say: how do i uninstall windows 10? when i put the cd into another computer it does nothing. do i have to uninstall before reinstalling?

cmdrkeene say:

Typically you'd remove it by installing a different operating system onto the computer and erasing the hard drive as part of that process.  However you can also do this:

  1. Go to the built-in Settings app, and choose "Update & Security" from the bottom
  2. Choose "Recovery", then click the Get Started button under "Reset this PC"
  3. Choose to "Remove Everything"

This process will take about 10 minutes or so, and at the end the computer will be just like it came from the factory (or after a fresh install of Windows 10, depending on your situation).  At this point, it will be ready for you to agree to the license terms of use.  Simply turn off the computer. As long as no one has accepted the license terms of use, the product is not being used and is inactivated.

Now you can legally install your purchased copy of Windows onto another PC.  Just put in the drive and run the setup program.
