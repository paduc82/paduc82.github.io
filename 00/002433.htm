Inserting graphs into Word causes lines on graph to disappear - Microsoft Community

tylersmith6 say:

Inserting graphs into Word causes lines on graph to disappear

I am inserting many graphs into my word .doc (the graphs to the left of each red and blue circular image). But when I insert them and resize/crop them, there is always an issue with resolution. Particularly, the x and y axis disappears on several graphs. On other graphs the black horizontal lines get thinner or lighter. I can't publish a paper with graphics that look this bad. Any help as to why Word butchers graphics would be great?

jay freedman say:

When the resizing causes a line to become one pixel (or less) thick, it can't be shown on the screen unless it happens to fall within the location of a physical pixel. Typically, printing the document allows the lines to appear, because the printer's resolution is much higher than that of the screen. Changing the zoom factor in Word can also let them appear (although others may then disappear).

It may work better if you do all your cropping and resizing in a dedicated graphics program, in which the resizing algorithm takes account of adjacent pixel colors. The resulting file can be inserted in Word without further resizing.

One other thing you can try is to go to File > Options > Advanced > Display and change the check box for "Optimize character positioning for layout rather than readability". That may or may not have an effect on the graphics.

Lumia 550 micro sd card

Hi,

I have a brand new Lumia 550 phone.

The phone was working fine until I inserted a brand new Sandisk 32gb class 10 micro sd card. The phone recognises the card as normal. The problem arises when I try and store any content on the card. This could be moving an app from the phone to the card, or downloading a new app from the store to the card, or putting a photo/document on the card. The phone just stalls/freezes/continually tries to install the file but never finishes its cycle. The only way to resume normal phone function is to turn off and remove the battery and card, then reboot the phone with no card inserted.

I have tried formatting the card in both my pc and phone but it makes no difference.

I have tried the card in my other lumia phone and it works correctly so I'm assuming the card is not at fault and it may be the interface/driver on the phone itself? In which case I'm assuming the phone itself is faulty and should be returned for a replacement?

Thanks in advance for any advice given.

David

sven johannsen say:
I might suggest doing a factory reset on the device, and/or use the Windows Device Recovery Tool to bring the phone back to what should be a known good state. Avoid adding anything you don't have to before trying the SD card again. If that doesn't provide more satisfactory results then I would agree that the unit might need to be replaced. I do note that a firmware update for the 950/XL was recently released which addresses some SD card functions as well as other items. It is possible a 550 update may be coming. With seemingly few other incidents of your issue though, it would seem more like a hardware anomaly if the reset(s) don't clear it up. If the reset(s) don't show that it is functional, I wouldn't wait to see if an update is imminent.

daibutton say:

Before I first used the card in my phone, I created some folders on the card using my laptop and stored some photos on it. When I then inserted the card into my phone I can only assume this was the cause of the fault, either that or the card itself was faulty.

In short, I bought a second new card and inserted it into my phone and did a format. Then I started created my own folders, etc. The phone and card have worked faultless since.

Whatever the cause of the fault was, moral of the story is when using a card for the first time always insert it into your device and format it before doing anything with it.
