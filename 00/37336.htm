what is the difference between surface RT and surface windos 8 - Microsoft Community

veronicamalone say:

what is the difference between surface RT and surface windos 8

I want to buy a Microsoft Surface but would like to know the difference between surface RT and surface windows 8. Please help.
Veronica
andy rathbone say:
Microsoft explains the differences here to help you choose between the two:

http://www.microsoft.com/Surface/en-US/surface-with-windows-rt/help-me-choose

But I'll try to sum it up here:

The Surface RT can't run traditional Windows desktop programs. Instead, it's limited to apps you download from the bundled Store app. The Surface RT also includes versions of Microsoft Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and OneNote. For many people, that's all they need.

What the Surface RT lacks in horsepower it makes up for in extended battery life: Mine typically runs two or three days if I'm just using it a few hours a day.

The Surface Pro, by contrast, is like a desktop computer in a tablet package. It can run both traditional desktop programs, as well as apps. All that power comes at a price, though, as it lacks the battery life found in the Surface RT. Its battery probably won't last more than four or five hours. (Microsoft hasn't yet released battery life figures.)

So, when choosing between the two, decide what's more important to you: battery life or traditional desktop programs. Then choose the Surface that best meets your needs.

mattschubeck say:

Microsoft's update KB3176493 and KB3176495 for Windows 10 resolved some security issues in IE10 and IE9.  Thomson Reuters accounting programs that are affected are using an older IE7 API upon startup to display current content from their own servers inside deprecated frames - TOP.HTML, SEPARATOR.HTML and CONTENTS.HTML.  Because IE7 is not supported by Microsoft any longer, the corresponding files did not get updated while the newer files did.  This breaks rendering the T-R webpages in the frames where they are called, and is what is causing the error messages to occur.

The problem is with T-R using the outdated & unsupported API with their accounting packages.  At this time, we do not anticipate Thomson Reuters will update their Creative Solutions program because it has been termed end-of-life and will not be supported at all after June 1, 2017.
