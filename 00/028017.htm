Current recommendations for minimizing junk mail in Outlook.com.
1. Sign in to Outlook.com.
2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on
More mail settings.
3. Under Preventing junk mail, click Safe and blocked senders.
4. Under the Safe and blocked senders, click on Blocked senders.
5. In the Blocked senders list, add the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as outlook.com) that you don't want to receive mail from, and then click Add to list >>.

You can create rules to delete incoming junk mails.
1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on More mail settings.
2. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
3. Click New and then under Step 1, click the drop down arrow on the first box and choose Sender's address.
4. Under Step 1, type in on the third box the email address.
5. Under Step 2, choose Delete these messages—don't even put them in my Deleted folder.
6. Click Save at the bottom of the page.

You may also set your Junk email filter to Exclusive.
If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will be sent to the Junk folder.
By default, the Junk folder will automatically delete messages after 10 days.
This is more convenient as we don't have any control if senders are randomly changing their address.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Select the «race» mergefield and then press CTRL+F9 to surround it with a pair of field delimiters { } and then add the following

THe checked symbol is Wingdings 254 and the unchecked symbol is Wingdings 111 inserted via the Insert Symbol dialog.

Press ALT+F9 to toggle of the display of the field codes.

For the line issue, it is probably best to layout that part of your document in the form of a table and add a bottom border to the cell.

macropod say:

Another option would be to use a field coded as:

{SYMBOL{IF«race»= "racetype" 82 163} \a \f "Wingdings 2"}

or:
{SYMBOL{IF{MERGEFIELD race}= "racetype" 82 163} \a \f "Wingdings 2"}

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above example are all created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Nor is it practical to add them via any of the standard Word dialogues. Likewise, you can't type or copy & paste the chevrons (i.e. '« »') - they're part of the actual mergefields, which you can insert from the 'Insert Merge Field' dropdown. The spaces represented in the field construction are all required.
