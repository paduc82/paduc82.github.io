I have an email account with an ISP that previously only supported POP then IMAP and most recently Exchange Activesync. Exchange Activesync is most desired because now my mail and calendar is synced across all my devices (desktop, laptop, tablet, phone).

I successfully installed my email account as an Exchange account in Outlook on one PC. On another PC my email account would not install as an Exchange account within Outlook. Outlook returned an error that there was a problem.

To cut to the chase, I spent about 30 mins on the phone with MS support. At first she told me that my ISP only supported POP and IMAP but by this point she had full access to my machine using an MS support app that I downloaded and installed and I showed her the successful Exchange account on my other PC. She tested it and was satisfied that my ISP did indeed support Exchange. That led to a few quiet stretches on the phone with her poking around Outlook and consulting with her coworkers. Finally she came back...

Solution:

- Open the Control panel and type "mail" in the search box.

- Click on Mail (Microsoft Outlook 2016)

- In the box Mail Setup - Outlook that appears, click Email Accounts

Now you are looking at a box that looks identical to the one that would appear if you were adding a new email account within Outlook, only this time adding my email account as an Exchange account through the Mail applet worked flawlessly without any issues. The new Exchange account tested without any new issues. The Calendar for this new account syncs across all my devices.

Clearly I still have an a issue within Outlook 2016 itself on one PC but thankfully MS support gave me a complete working solution.

shane devenshire say:

Here are two equivalent approaches:

=SUMPRODUCT(SUBTOTAL(3,OFFSET(C13:C18,ROW(13:18)-13,0,1)),--(C13:C18="Red"))

 
=SUMPRODUCT(SUBTOTAL(3,OFFSET(C1,ROW(1:19)-1,))*(C1:C19="Red"))

These formulas work with filtered lists to give a count of the number of items in the filered list range that contain Red in column C.

shane devenshire say:

I'm going to assume the data goes from E1:E100

In sheet2 enter the following formula in B4:

=SUMPRODUCT(SUBTOTAL(3,OFFSET(E1,ROW(1:100)-1,))*(Sheet1!E1:E100="Daily"))

or if you want Daily to be more flexible enter Daily in C4 of sheet2 and use

=SUMPRODUCT(SUBTOTAL(3,OFFSET(E1,ROW(1:100)-1,))*(Sheet1!E1:E100=C4))

Then you can change the contents in C4 to Weekly or whatever.  Also, if you are going to copy this formula down for any reason make the references absolute as appropriate.
