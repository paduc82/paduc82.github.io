suzanne s. barnhill say:

Draft view is the new name for Normal view. I do see dotted lines indicating page breaks in Draft view, with or without nonprinting characters displayed. I don't see them in Outline view, but they are not displayed in Outline view in Word 2003, either.

Note, however, that these are just the "soft" page breaks that Word inserts automatically; they cannot be deleted. If you are referring to manual page breaks, then it is true that they cannot be seen in Draft view without displaying nonprinting characters--at least not in Word 2010 (*.docx) documents. If you open a document created in Word 2003 or earlier, or if you save your document in Word 97-2003 (*.doc) format, then you will see the page breaks in Draft view regardless of display status.
