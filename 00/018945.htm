As you've discovered, the cell has automatically become a hyperlink (more on this below). If you want direct access to the cell, click and hold on that cell for a couple of seconds and it will not launch the hyperlink.

Alternately, select a non-hyperlinked cell near it and use the arrow keys to navigate to the cell. Tap F2 to edit the cell's contents or Delete to remove them.

stefan blom say:
If the document was originally scanned, you often need optical character recognition (OCR) software to convert it into editable text. Otherwise, Word sees the content as (a sequence of) pictures.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

The anchor suggests that the text you are viewing is actually a picture, inserted with text wrapping. The anchor shows that it is anchored to a text paragraph. As Stefan says, this may be the result of scanning a document. In any case, you will need an OCR application to convert the picture to editable text.

OneNote contains very rudimentary OCR capabilities. You can paste the picture into OneNote, then right-click and choose Copy Text from Picture. The result will likely be pretty rough and need a lot of clean-up.

seamster say:

WHY CAN'T I LOG INTO MICROSOFT WORD

EVERYTIME I TRY TO LOG IN, IT TELLS ME ANOTHER ACCOUNT IS ALREADY LOGGED IN, BUT THERE ISN'T ANOTHER ACCOUNT.
tisky zheng msft say:

Hi Seamster,

 

We would suggest you try the following steps:

 

1. On the Accounts screen, click Sign out.

2. Under Connected Services, remove all the services for the existing account.

3. Click Start, click Run, type regedit, and then click OK.

4. In Registry Editor, locate the following registry subkey:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\16.0\Common\Identity\Identities

5. Select the Office account that you want to delete, and then click Delete.

6. Exit Registry Editor.

7. Open Control Panel, and then click Credentials Manager.

8. Under Generic Credentials, locate the account that you want to remove, and then click Remove.

9. Log off, and then log on to the computer.
