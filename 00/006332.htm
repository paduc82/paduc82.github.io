Hi WCarp,

The same "size" on the screen is relative. If you notice on my images above, the reason why the image appears blurry in the business card is because the image was first reduced to only only 2x8cm from the original 66x200cm and blown up (magnified) 300%, which is essentially stretching the image out instead of making it smaller.

Regarding .jpeg image quality degradation, this has nothing to do with Publisher. It is a byproduct of a lossy image. However, after further testing, I can confirm that the blurry image is more related to the fact that your image which should only be a few centimeters in size is stretched almost 500% larger as seen in the screenshot you provided.

To summarize, what happens is:

1. You insert the image to Publisher's business card.

2. As the business card's size is small, Publisher reduces the size of the image.

3. After reducing the size of the image, the business card is magnified to show a clearer layout as the business card at 100% size is too small to view on a screen.

The best way to make sure that the image looks sharp is to insert a high resolution image so that the effective PPI is higher.

First off, if you are linking to a z:\ drive then everyone need to have that drive mapped for this to work.

  • Create an Access Web App (AWA) rather than a Desktop Database
  • Use RDP, CITRIX, Terminal Services, ... a technology to remotely access the Access db
  • use another WAN technology, such as: MySQL/PHP/.Net ,...
