twozillas say:

Pause between tracks when burning CDs using WMP.

When I burn a CD that has songs that flow into each other (ie: live music), I notice a brief pause between tracks.  How can I avoid this?
manjit kaur say:

Hi twozillas,

Some CDs, when some of their songs are ripped, may include one or more seconds of silence in the ripped music file after the music ends. Other CDs may include silence gaps at the beginning of some songs. Many CDs mark the beginning and ending of songs with the introduction and end of audible sound on each track. The Player automatically adds a two-second gap between songs when burning audio CDs to keep the next song from beginning immediately after the end of the previous song. The extra silence gaps included in the music files from the ripped CDs can create long, inconsistent pauses between songs. Since the Player's two-second pause cannot be turned off, the only way to resolve this issue is to use an audio file editor to remove the silence gaps in the song recordings themselves.

considerate_guy say:

No video editing program can add looping.

It's the *playback* software that determines whether it loops. For example, in Windows Media Player, Ctrl+T toggles the function where your movie either plays once, or it loops indefinitely.

If you are making a DVD, the authoring software can either have the video loop back to the menu or loop back to the start of the movie. But again, it is the playback software, not Movie Maker that does the looping.

    andre da costa say:

    You don't get one, you get a Digital License which is attached to your computer/device.

    When you upgraded from a previous version of Windows or receive a new computer preinstalled with Windows 10, what happened is the hardware (your PC) will get a digital entitlement, where a unique signature of the computer will be stored on Microsoft Activation Servers. The Windows 7 or Windows 8 genuine license you were previously running will be exchanged for a diagnostics key.

    Anytime you need to reinstall Windows 10 on that machine, just proceed to reinstall Windows 10. It will automatically reactivate.

    So, there is no need to know or get a product key, if you need to reinstall Windows 10, you can use your Windows 7 or Windows 8 product key or use the reset function in Windows 10.

    If you are prompted to enter a product key, click 'I don't have a key' and 'Do this later'

    Hi, I would like to ask the following:

    1. My laptop came with Windows 10 Home (digital license), and I have a key for Windows 10 Pro from Microsoft Imagine (Dreamspark) as allowed for by my school. If I do upgrade my laptop from a clean install of Win10 Pro, can I re-activate my laptop with the Win10 Home digital license it came with with a clean install [of Win10 Home]?

    No, Windows 10 Home cannot activate Windows 10 Pro. The Windows 10 Pro license is its own full license. If you perform a factory reset, it will restore the system to its original Windows 10 Home install, which will be automatically activated. Of course, the system will now have a Digital License attached it because it was upgraded to Windows 10 Pro.

    2. Does the Windows 10 Pro from Dreamspark expire once I graduate from school, or can I use it for good?

    No, it does not. You can use it forever. Its similar to a retail license. Don't lose the key.

    davem121 say:
    Hi Azam,

    Honestly there is no point in creating a partition and backing up onto that - a lot of users do that.

    The problem with that is your data and your backup will be on the same physical disc and if the hard drive breaks, all your data and your backups are gone, which defeats the purpose of a backup

    You should backup to an external hard drive or USB Flash Drive, then if your hard drive ever fails, your data is safe . . .

    Windows 10 Backup and indeed File History are fine for now, but will be retired in the future . . .
