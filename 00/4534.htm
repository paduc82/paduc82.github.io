sven johannsen say:

A link would be nice. I expect it's not going to work. I found numerous chargers with micro USB plugs that list the 2520 as one of the compatible devices. The 2520 does not charge off of USB. As has been mentioned the 2520 charger is 20v, USB is 5 volts. Your car incidentally is 12v. Takes a bit of electronics to upvonvert either 5v or 12v to 20v. It can be done, but the market for that is small enough it wouldn't be profitable for a reputable company to do it.

 

Incidentally the micro-USB 3 socket on the 2520 is not an OTG socket. it is a normal Host USB port with an unusual size. On-The-Go is a feature which allows what is usually a Client port, such as a phone charge/sync port, to also act as a Host, so you can attach a client to it, like a USB drive, or keyboard. The port on the 2520 is a host, it does not act as a client. You can grab a USB 3 cable intended for a pocket hard drive and hook the Lumia to a PC. Nothing happens. You are hooking a host to a host. Were the Lumia able to act as a client, it should mount as an external device of some sort on the PC.

 

The challenge at this point is to find an appropriate adapter cable. The ones with the right ends are typically OTG cables, and they do work fine. You can in fact use any number of USB2 microUSB dongles to hook a USB thumb drive, or a keyboard, or a phone to the 2520. You just need a microUSB, 2 or 3, male to USB socket adapter. Typically thos are listed as OTG adapters.

 

Back to the auto adpter though. You aren't going to find a generic one of these. It will need to be built specifically for the 2520 and convert 12v to 20v. Or use the inverter and the AC adapter.

sven johannsen say:

Hate to be a killjoy here, but I'd bet against finding an adapter that fits the coaxial plig for the charger and adapts to the Lumia. You'd likeluy have to make one, in which case cutting off the current one and soldering on the right one for Lumia would be easier, assuming you have a soldering iron, and a voltmetter to get the polarity right. That's after you acquire the right plug.

 

I'd suggest an inverter actually. That's a device that turns your car 12v DC into 110 AC. ( I assume 220 ones exist if that's your normal standard.  Here's a search at Amazon.  Mount something like this in your car and you should be golden, for about anything. You aren't running your girl friend's hair dryer off it, but you could charge your Lumia.

 

Just a rough rule of thumb. volts times amps equals watts. ( yes, there are power factors, inductance, yadda yadda, but basically...) So the output for the Lumia charger, 20v * 1.5A is about 30 watts. The input to the charger says 100-240v @ 0.65A , so that is 65 to 156 watts. Why the big difference? Well the input is what you need to ensure the source to the charger (the wall outlet) can deliver, so the charger is guaranteed to pull less so you don't run the risk of burning up the source. Typically that is overspec'd for safety. (The say they draw more than they expect so if you meet it you are safe) The output of the charger is what it can safely deliver, underspec'd for safety. That is, if you draw every bit of that, you can't hurt it.  There are some losses in the conversion so what comes out is always less than what goes in. In any case, an inverter that promises 200w is more than enough. I am a little hesitant about the size of the 12v wire on that thing, because to get 200W you'd need to draw 16Amps, and that suggests house wiring wire sizes to me, but hey, what do I know, just a BSEE. So, I wouldn't plug three Lumias into it, but one and a Windows Phone or two on the USB, is reasonable. There are lots of options. I would lean towards the ones with one or two AC outlets and a metal shell. Better for heat dissapation.
