Try this

1. Press Ctrl+F3 > New

2. In the Name box, type sheet_names

3. In the Refers to box, type

=GET.WORKBOOK(1+0*NOW())

4. Click on OK

5. In cell K41 of the Nov 2014 sheet, enter this formula.  This will return the tab name of the previous worksheet

=IF(MATCH("*"&K42,sheet_names,0)=1,"",IFERROR(RIGHT(INDEX(sheet_names,1,MATCH("*"&K42,sheet_names,0)-1),LEN(INDEX(sheet_names,1,MATCH("*"&K42,sheet_names,0)-1))-SEARCH("]",INDEX(sheet_names,1,MATCH("*"&K42,sheet_names,0)-1))),""))

6. In cell K42 of the Nov 2014 sheet, enter this formula.  This will return the current tab name

=MID(CELL("filename",O1),FIND("]",CELL("filename",O1))+1,256)

7. In cell J37 of the Nov 2014 worksheet, enter this formula and copy down till cell J39

=E38+INDIRECT("'"&$K$41&"'!J"&ROW())

8. Now right click on the Nov 2014 tab and select Move or Copy > Create a Copy.  Rename the tab to Dec 2014

9. Enter your data on this new worksheet and all formulas should automatically recalculate to show you the correct result,

N82 seems to be a nirvana of hardware and connectivity but it requires all sorts of extra work and purchases in order to get the benefits.

The best charging solution (most compact/easy to carry and with the widest available main power source) is via USB. The phone comes with an USB cable but it's not capable of charging via that same cable. I would've understood that, even though we are in the age of iPods and iPhones which are both capable of charging through their data cable, but Nokia didn't even bundle with the phone the special cable (CA-100) that allows charging from the USB port. Instead, Nokia has the nerve to charge a hugely unjustified price for this ridiculously simple cable (15cm USB to N82 power port converter that costs twice the price of a fully blown charger).

The best Internet connectivity method is via WiFi but this method also has some serious drawbacks. The main problem is the fact that you can't get through WiFi the same local PC data connectivity (file transfer, PC Suite, etc.) that you get from using the USB data cable.

The wisest way to connect to the Internet would've been via the USB data cable that you carry around anyway. You wouldn't need to go hunting for WiFi APs and at home, you wouldn't need to pay extra and setup a WiFi AP just to connect you phone to the Internet. I don't think the phone is capable of ad-hoc WiFi connections so you will need to be very careful when buying a WiFi solution at home because you need to setup a WiFi AP or WiFi router (a simple USB WiFi dongle for your PC probably won't be enough).

The worst of the worst is what you have to carry around in order to use the phone on a day to day basis. 1st you need to have the USB data cable "just in case" you need to transfer something off the phone. 2nd, since the battery is drained fast when using all the features of the phone, you need to carry around a charger and for the best charging solution you need to buy the superoverpriced USB charger.

Adding pain to sorrow: S60 is slow. I heard it was slower in the past but I can't imagine that since I find it sluggish as it is right now. I'm afraid S40 is better than S60, at least when it comes to command response time.

The S60 UI is not at all thought out to be modern and ergonomic, it just gives the feeling of a '99 era phone, with 16M color icons on a bigger screen and the same endless and illogically arranged tree style menus.

The applications are not well designed to work together and they load slooooowly. Unforgivable is the lack of integration between Contacts and Calendar and the totally impossible To-Do List functionality (you can't add entries fast, you can't attach voice records to entries, etc.).

The music player is a joke. The keyboard caused me severe knuckle pain after three days of typing in dozens of contacts manually because my previous Nokia phone only had infrared and no data cable.

The N82 is the same Nokia story that we keep getting since ~2003. A lot of very good hardware running software that is severely out of phase with the times.

Nokia you really need to start firing managers ( 1st out should be the guy who thought a bar code scanner is something anyone wants in a high end phone, 2nd the idiot who placed the media key between the "right function key" and "C" ) and start hiring UI experts to boss around your software developers. You are very close to being as bad as Windows Mobile and if your idea of high end phones are not the gay-approved 8800 then you need to separate the teams who make "flashlight enabled" phones from the people who would give you a chance in the coming war against Android and iPhone2.
