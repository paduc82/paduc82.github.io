ken sheridan say: If you are restricting the form's recordset on the basis of a parameter on one column, and using the combo box to navigate to a record on the basis of another column, then you should not need to reference the parameter in the combo box's RowSource.

For example you might restrict an employees form on the basis of a parameter on a Department column, and then use a combo box to navigate to an employee.  In this hypothetical scenario the form's RecordSource property might be the following query:

SELECT *
FROM Employees
WHERE Department = [Enter Department:];

The RowSource property of the unbound navigational combo box would then be like this:

SELECT EmployeeID, FirstName & " " & LastName
FROM Employees
WHERE Department = Form!Department
ORDER BY LastName, FirstName;

i.e. the parameter in the RowSource property to restrict the list of employees to those in the selected department is a reference to the form's  Department control, not to the original parameter.

If the original parameter is a reference to a control in another unbound dialogue form, which is a better approach, (or even another bound form) from which the employees form is opened, the parameters in both queries can be the same provided that the calling form remains open, e.g.

WHERE Department = Forms!frmEmployeeDlg!cboDepartment;

The above is a hypothetical example of course and probably bears little relation to your actual set-up, but if you are attempting something along these lines then I don't think the control wizard would be able to handle it.  You'd need to hand-craft it.

If the above does not point you in the right direction, post back with a clear description of what you are attempting to do in real world terms, rather than how you are attempting to do it in database terms.

x-apple-msg-attachments and ATT000xx.htm files - Microsoft Community

michele& say:

x-apple-msg-attachments and ATT000xx.htm files

I'm using Outlook for Mac 2016 and I am receiving complaints from my email receipients that my emails with attachments also include these additional attachments x-apple-message-attachment and/or ATT000xx.htm.  At first I thought it was the logo in my signature but I have removed the logo and these attachments still appear.  I have tried changing the message format to plain text and that did not work.  If anyone has a resolution to this problem I would greatly appreciate it.
talking moose say:

This happens when your email client (and it can be any email client not just Outlook for Mac) intermingles text and attachments:

Hello!

Here’s a picture:

<insert picture here here>

Here’s some more text.

<insert another picture>

Goodbye!

<insert email signature>

It often occurs with email signatures because they are inserted into messages automatically instead of being typed into the continuing body of the message.

You can mitigate this by doing some of these things:

  1. Delete your email signature completely and recreate it with just text. Don't insert any graphics (those only consume more space anyway).
  2. As you reply to messages, delete everyone's email signature from the message but your own. Outlook may display them inline but your recipient's mail client may translate them as attachments and cause this issue. This also makes the thread of messages much easier to read.
  3. As you reply to messages, delete any unwanted attachments. Other folks in the email chain should already have them anyway.
  4. If you attach more than one file to a message, add them as an attachments. Don't place them in the middle of your message.
  5. You can send as plain text not HTML.

Hope this helps!
