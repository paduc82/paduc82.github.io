Ok, got a fix that has at least worked for me. 

Type Troubleshooting into the Cortana/Windows search box

Click Hardware and Sound

Click Hardware and Devices, then Next

On my system Windows Update was set to not install the latest drivers and Troubleshooting fixed that

Now open Device Manager and click to uninstall the MS Bluetooth and Bluetooth LE Enumerator

Choose to uninstall the Atheros Bluetooth driver, but on the uninstall dialog check the checkbox to delete the drivers from the system.

Run Scan for hardware changes and it should pick up new ones and install everything correctly

Bingo, mouse and sound are back up and running.

I wouldn't concern myself with it as games will put more onto the hardware than say aps will. The fan is designed to work harder and as a result will get louder, sometime jet engine loud while expelling the excess heat generated by the hardware while under load.

electroflying say:

No, I wouldn't "recommend" anything other than what the system came with.

The fan is designed to expel heat. Games are very demanding, and put a lot of stress on the system which then generates heat. When it does the fan will speed (more RPM) and may even sound like a Hair dryer at times, but it's nothing to worry about.
