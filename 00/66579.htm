When you say "badly contaminated" you also have to make the distinction between high-risk malware like viruses, trojans, worms, ransomware, etc. that can damage your operating system versus lower-risk PUPs (potentially unwanted programs) like browser hijackers and adware.  Most malware removal specialists recommend that users run an antivirus in real-time protection mode to protect against high-risk threats and supplement their antivirus with the occasional on-demand scan with an anti-malware/anti-spyware scanner like Malwarebytes Free to look for lower-risk PUPs and other threats that might have been missed by their antivirus - see the Lifehacker article The Difference Between Antivirus and Anti-Malware (and Which to Use).  On my Vista SP2 computer I use Norton Security v22.15.1.8 for my real-time protection (I pay under $15 USD each year for a retail box set from Staples Canada and use the product key to to renew my annual subscription) and then run an on-demand Threat Scan with Malwarebytes Free v3.5.1-1.0.365 every 3 or 4 days to look for any lower-risk threats that might have been missed by Norton.  My Norton security software includes multiple layers of protection, including Intrusion Protection against known vulnerabilities in my unsupported Vista SP2 OS and Firefox ESR browser that are vulnerable to attack by zero-day exploits.
