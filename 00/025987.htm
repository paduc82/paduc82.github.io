ericccccccc say:

Can we get a smart watch that everyone needs?

There are many different types of smart watch on the market. The problem is, we don't really need them. They cannot change our life, and most of us don't need to record our heart rate / blood pressure / location all the time! For most of the Apps, mobile phone is a much better choice than the tiny "smart watch".

However, there are some features that everyone needs, are perfect for smart watch.

All current smart watches are developed in wrong direction. Watch is very small, but most vendors want to copy the features from smart phone to it! This attempt is doomed to fail from the very beginning.

From my point of view, a smart watch everyone needs is a simple one with the features below. No fancy appearance, and it is not going to compete with smart phone.

1. Identity verification.

I don't want to carry keys, security passes, membership cards, discount cards, library card, drive license, credit cards, transportation card, etc. And, I even don't want to carry mobile phone everywhere. Smart watch is a perfect device for identity verification: light, handy, not likely to left in car / office / home / hotel, or get stolen. When working with fingerprint scanning, it's much more secure against hacker's attack or robber or thief.

At checkout desk of supermarket, no need to take credit card / cash / smart phone out of pocket any more, just raise your hand, put your finger on the watch, "Beep", done.

Do we still need wallet? I doubt it.

2. Receive ‚ÄúCheck Code‚Äù

How many passwords do we need to memorize? How complicated each one need to protect you to resist attacks? With the help of this watch, all online system can turn into "passwordless". Want to log on email system? Please enter your email address, then click "submit" button, wait for a few seconds later, enter the "Check Code" just received on watch, then click "submit" again, done.

3. Receive notification

For any important action online, such as online payment, no matter it's through credit card or PayPal, or just getting the salary of last week, we can get notification in almost real time.

So, if anything goes wrong, we can respond in short time.

4. Long lasting battery

We don't need to keep the watch running all the time. It should be "wake up" instantly when making payment, and then go to sleep again. So, we may only need to run it for a few minutes a day, which means, it can last for months in one battery charge!

When the battery needs recharge, we should get notification on the watch.

So, the OS is completely different from any existing OS.

5. Remote control / No keyboard.

We should not be able to enter any text on the watch. So, all configuration is done through computer or smart phone, remotely. That's one of the major differences between Watch and Phone.

6. Tough

Dust proof, water proof, shock proof, and works well in extreme temperature.

7. Enclosed environment

This watch is critical to user's daily life, so, security gets highest priority. To minimize risk, it should not allow any third-party software.

8. Date / Clock

Only provide most basic information.

9. GPS track (optional)

No map needed, but it's nice to know that we can always get help, anywhere, anytime.

10. Privacy

OK, this is the inevitable drawback of this watch. But, as it doesn't collect information more than current mobiles, I assume it's fine.

Currently, Android and iPhone can do these job already, but "watch" is much more elegant, and safer.  Actually this watch can improve the security of all mobiles, a lot!

To live without smart phone is all right. To live without this smart watch, it will be miserable.

ossiemac say:

If there are no spaces in the worksheet name then the single quotes around the sheet name not required.

=VLOOKUP(A6;Klantensheet!$A$1:$E$6;3;FALSE)

Tell me what occurs when you use the formula. Is it telling you there is an error in the formula or just not finding the value.

I have assumed that in your region you use a semi colon delimiter between the arguments in the formula and not a comma as is used in other areas.

The value being looked up (A6 value) must be in the first column of the range Klantensheet!$A$1:$E$6

The 3 indicates to return the value in the 3rd column of the range Klantensheet!$A$1:$E$6. You do not use the sheet name with the 3.

The dollar signs make the range absolute. ie. if you copy the formula down to the next row then the lookup table range will remain as $A$1:$E$6. If you don't use the $ signs then if you copy the formula to the next row then the lookup table will become A2:E7 and then the following row A3:E8 and so on and it is not looking in the correct range.
