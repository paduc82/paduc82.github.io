Hi Leonard,

Usually when emails sent are landed to junk folder is because the SPF record is not valid. You will need to set up the SPF record on your Public DNS provider.

There are some external tool that help to generate a valid SPF record.

https://mxtoolbox.com/SPFRecordGenerator.aspx
https://www.kitterman.com/spf/validate.html

However, I suggest you contact your register domain for support in this matter.

Note: the links above are from third party tools and are not provided by Microsoft.

Outlook.com to Office 365 Migration - Microsoft Community

joey pender say:

Outlook.com to Office 365 Migration

Outlook.com Custom Domain registrations have been shut down as most people are already aware.  MS has recommended that those of us who need the flexibility of custom domains migrate to Office 365, however after comparing the two for a few weeks, I find that I would be losing functionality by making the migration.

I'm not a small business with 5-10 employes - i'm just an individual, a freelancer, who wants to have a custom domain with a single (or 3 at the most) email address.  Office365 seems to be way beyond what I would need compared to the offerings of Outlook.com:

IM:

Again, as an individual, I do not use Lync Online.  No one I know uses Lync Online.  The office where I work at also does not use Lync.  However...I have 10+ contacts who use Skype, and double that amount of people who use Facebook and Google Talk.  Outlook.com's Messaging sidebar is super useful as it integrates all three of those networks, and allows me to chat with them directly from the webmail client within Outlook.com.  In OWA in O365, the only integrated chat is Lync Online, with barely any Skype integration (from my research, it looks like to communicate with my Skype contacts in Lync Online, I would have to re-add them as contacts...there is no integration with my existing Microsoft Account that already has my Skype contact list), and non existent integration with Google Talk or Facebook.  This makes OWA's IM feature set useless to someone like me, and degrades the webmail experience i've been used to since using Outlook.com.

OneDrive:

OneDrive Pro looks overcomplicated compared to the standard OneDrive.  As far as I know, it doesn't work with the iOS apps for OneDrive, for the OS X desktop app, Xbox One, nor has the excellent file viewing interface as the Outlook.com version.  I don't doubt its very useful for large companies, but for me, or even for a small company of 2-4 people, its seems over the top, and again, we lose functionality.

Inability to use a O365 Account as a Microsoft Account

My current Outlook.com account I use for signing in to all Microsoft Account services, as well as Windows 8.  If I were to migrate to O365, this would no longer be possible - i'd have to maintain two separate accounts, one for Office 365, and the other for the rest of the MS services.  Another degrade in experience.

So to gain custom domains by being forced to switch or migrate to Office 365, I'm losing many other features i've come to love from Outlook.com.  If anyone from MS is reading, do you think its possible to keep the Outlook.com custom domains feature but just as a paid service?
