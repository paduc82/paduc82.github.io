jllatham say:

What are columns B and C?  Wins / Losses  or the other way around?  Based on the question, I'm assuming that's the way it is: 1st column (A) = conference ID, 2nd column = wins, 3rd column = losses.  If it were the other way around, answer is zero.

 

The conference (almost) doesn't play into this because of your requirement of "from the "BE" conference OR the "B12" conference"  and all entered are from only those two conferences, but we will assume that somewhere along the line another conference identifier could be introduced.

 

Now, there are a total of 6 entries with wins greater than 25.

There are a total of 9 entries with losses less than 10.

 

But once again the requirement is OR for the scoring.  I believe the answer should be 9 from observation and interpretation of the requirements.  If the scoring had said >25 wins AND <10 losses, then the answer would be 6:

32 & 2, 27 & 7, 27 & 5, 26 & 6, 26 & 7 and 26 & 9.

 

Actually, I believe zorvek's formula returns the correct answer of 9.  I rewrote it based on A=conference, B = wins and C = losses like this:

=SUMPRODUCT(((C2:C100<10)+(B2:B100>25)>0)*(((A2:A100="BE")+(A2:A100="B12")>0)))

and it shows me 9 here.

 

Of course this hasn't taken the HINT into consideration at all; just say you ignored the hint and used an array function [SUMPRODUCT()] that internalizes the IFs. :-)

 

Now the long-winded proof of this would be something like this formula:

=COUNTIFS(A:A,"BE",B:B,">25")+COUNTIFS(A:A,"B12",B:B,">25")+COUNTIFS(A:A,"BE",C:C,"<10")+COUNTIFS(A:A,"B12",C:C,"<10")-COUNTIFS(A:A,"BE",B:B,">25",C:C,"<10")-COUNTIFS(A:A,"B12",B:B,">25",C:C,"<10")

 

Where we count the rows where BE is combined with wins >25, add in the rows where B12 is combined with wins >25, add in rows for BE with losses <10 along with rows for B12 with losses < 10 ... that gives you 15 (6 >25, 9 < 10) but is incorrect because it is counting the ones that are 'paired' twice, so the last two pieces subtract out those rows where both OR conditions were met: again the result is 9.
