john_barnett say:

Hello sil-va,

You are not going to be able to move the contents of the C: drive to the E drive. Doing so will make Windows inoperable as Windows is looking for the operating system files on drive C:

As drive E: is so large I would be inclined to move the contents of E: to a newly created partition, thus rendering E: empty, then, in disk management,  Delete the E: drive to create unallocated space, then finally right click on the C: drive and select the Extend option to extend the C: drive into the unallocated space.

At least you will then have enlarged the C: drive to give you more room.
