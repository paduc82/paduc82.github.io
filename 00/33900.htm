davem121 say:

This is caused by the 'Wake on LAN' function being turned on. The PC is seeing a LAN event and turning on . . .

  1. Go to your BIOS settings on the PC and see if Wake on LAN is enabled, if it is turn it off
  2. Then in Windows, open Device Manager (accessible from the right click on your Start Button)
  3. Expand Network Adapters and choose the Network card
  4. Right click and choose Properties
  5. Click the Advanced Tab
  6. Select 'Wake on Magic Packet' and set the value to 'Disabled'
  7. Click the Power Management Tab
  8. Uncheck the 3 boxes on that Tab
  9. Click OK, close all open windows and reboot the PC
