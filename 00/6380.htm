don cuthbert say:

Unless one account is an alias of the other, the accounts should be able to be dealt with individually. Login to the business account online via a web browser. https://account.microsoft.com/profile/ Check what aliases exist for it under the link Manage your sign-in email or phone number . Do the same with the personal account. That way you know for sure whether or not they are connected at the account level at Microsoft. Even if they are connected, you may be able to remove one from being an alias of the other, although I don't think that you can have multiple Microsoft Accounts connected these days, as used to be an option.

Once you confirm that each account can be administered separately online, you can switch the Windows login to use only the personal account. First switch to a Local Admin Windows login. Then switch to use the personal MSA as your Windows login.

I have multiple Microsoft Accounts and Google accounts and have had them connected or not in various ways over the years. More than one of my Microsoft Accounts has been used with the Insider Program from time to time.  Having access to more than one account via an email App does not connect them at the account level, as the email app logs in separately to each account.

More info on account aliases: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/12407/microsoft-account-manage-aliases
