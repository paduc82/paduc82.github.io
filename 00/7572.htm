As the link Joe provided to my answer states, the issue is 24-bit versus 32-bit BMP image requirements. On the PC, formats other than BMP are allowed but sadly that is not the case on the Mac version of Office.

Something you could try however is to create your command bar on the Mac but instead of inserting the 24-bit BMP files, open via Preview, any other image type that is sized properly for a command bar button and then use the good old Copy/Paste method to insert it into the button. It involves a bit of labor, but it might be worth a try. I know it's accepted on a Mac (i.e. opening a PNG image and copy/pasting via Preview) a screen clip of the image and pasting it into a buttonâ€¦ I just don't know if the PC will accept that same button image.

My final suggestion is to have two command bars inserted in the template, 1 for the PC and the other for the Mac. Then depending on the platform, automate which command bar is displayed. You can do this using the Application.Version property. Both Office 2010 and Office 2011 are version 14, however, on the PC the property returns 14.0 and on the Mac the full version number is returned, which is currently 14.3.9, so that can be your trigger (one of a few) to determine what platform you are running on.
