john - project say:

ChrishawnSpackman,

If you're looking for some automated way to have Project allocate resources to fill the "gaps" then I'm sorry but that won't happen. Project just doesn't have that level of automated scheduling.

However, your description sounds very detailed, (e.g. resource effort back and forth across multiple tasks), and your best bet might be to scale back the detail and take a more general approach. Perhaps a better approach is to generalize tasks within a module such that they are fixed duration and run in parallel. Assign resources as appropriate but forego the detail of exactly when a particular resource will be working on a particular task, it's likely not relevant. The only concern is that the task be completed in the given time (i.e. duration). This may require you to increase a resource's Max Units such that overallocation doesn't cloud the issue, or set the leveling option to look at larger "chunks" (i.e. month by month).

Many years ago I was faced with a similar development scenario wherein there was a series of develop, test, fix, test, fix, etc. task elements. Attempting to track the full spectrum of detail was simply not worthwhile, so we backed off to a more general approach.

I'd start by determining exactly what you NEED from Project. Then set up the plan to meet those needs. If for some reason you truly need all the detail you describe, then bite-the-bullet and be prepared to spend a lot of time detail managing the plan.

Hope this helps.

John

I am currently having an issue with my Outlook 2010.  The hard drive in my laptop went bad, but most of the data was still OK.  I installed the new hard drive and copied over what I needed from the old drive, including my Outlook PST files.  With the new hard drive, I set up my email account in Outlook and then did an import of all the messages, calendar events, and everything else from the PST into Outlook.  It all appeared to go smoothly, however any meetings that I had set up with meeting requests being sent out are uneditable by me.  Instead, when I open the appointment in Outlook, I see the following message: "This meeting was copied to your calendar and will not receive updates. To receive updates, contact the organizer, <me>".  I am the meeting organizer, but it doesn't know it.

Anyone know what I can do resolve this situation?  Thanks for your time!

Dave
brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Do not import the old PST.  Just reuse it.  Use the Mail applet in Control Panel to add it to the mail profile and make it the default data file, specifying its Inbox as the delivery location for your default account.  Start Outlook and it should look just like it did before the hard drive replacement.
