Cannot burn a CD from a mono audio recording because error says it - Microsoft Community

caropat say:

Cannot burn a CD from a mono audio recording because error says it does not recognize the length and determine whether the recording will fit on the blank disc.

I know the recording will fit because I know the length.  I have played the recording as suggested with the hope that MediaPlayer would recognize the length with no success.  How can I burn the CD?

neil smith say:

Hi -

 

You may find the mono audio came from a voice recorder or similar. Many of those devices only record the audio at 11khz as anything with a higher sample rate isn't needed for voice (though music would sound terrible if limited to this frequency !) For comparison, CD audio is recorded at 44khz.

Media player won't BURN to CD anything (mono or stereo) below 22khz, so this is a likely explanation for your problem.

To get round this issue, you can convert the file to 22 or 44khz sample rate in many common audio editors, and add it back to media players library.This process is called "upsampling"

Media player should then burn succesfully. You won't add any quality by doing this step, but it shouldn't lose any either.

Audacity http://audacity.sourceforge.net is a free and powerful program for converting audio* and it makes it simple to "upsample" from 11 to 44khz for example :

In Audacity, open your existing file, then click Tracks -> Resample menu, and choose 44100 from the dropdown menu.

Finally, choose File -> Export , saving as the default WAV (uncompressed audio) should be enough to get you running once you've added the WAV file to media player's library so it can be burned to CD.


HTH - Cheers, Neil

 

* Once you've tried that, have a look at the Effect menu for a wide range of tools you can use to muck about with audio - change pitch (mickey mouse), Phaser and Vocoder are fun tools to play with, but there's a lot more serious stuff in there like fade-in/out, click removal (think vinyl tracks) and more.
