If you click on Add to Document in the Envelopes dialog, does the delivery address appear in the correct place?

It helps perhaps if you turn on the display of non-printing characters by clicking on the Show\Hide ¬∂ button on the Home Tab of the ribbon.

john korchok say:

This is poor interface design on Microsoft's part, not an indication of a problem. Normally in a preference pane, the control settings indicate your previous option choice. But the Compress Pictures (Reduce File Size in 2011) dialog is not part of Preferences and doesn't act like a normal dialog. It doesn't mater what you set that dropdown to, when you return, it is back at 220d.p.i. It provides misleading feedback, even worse than no feedback at all.

If you don't open that dialog, pictures do not get automatically compressed. If you open it, change the dropdown to Keep Current Resolution and click on OK, pictures do not get compressed either. Only if you open it, change to 220, 150 or 96 d.p.i. and click OK, do pictures get downsampled.
