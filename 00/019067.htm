albert d. kallal say:

The limits on  row counts returned will depend on the particular hosting or server environments you have.

So if this is your own internal SharePoint site, then often you can have the administrator remove or increase some of the limits that access web services has set by default.

I believe for office 365, the tables can be quite large, but I believe you're restricted to returning 5000 records at a time in a query.

You don't mention how large your tables are now.  Office 365 currently does not have reports, so it is not clear if you're attempting to launch a form with a filter, or report.

If you can open the form via browse two with a where clause and restrict the records to less than 5000 records then you should be ok.

Also, keep in mind that AFTER you publish then you want to take a look at the web compatibility and errors table as there is a good many error and messages you'll find that are NOT caught by the compatibility checker. (so file->, View Application Log Table) or simply open up the USYSApplicationLog table assuming you have "show system objects" checked for the navigation pane options.

So, either the queries are not compatible, or they are pulling in too many reocrds. Keep in mind that without indexing to child tables and using joins will ALSO cause the record limit to be exceeded. I explain this issue in this post:

http://social.msdn.microsoft.com/Forums/en/accessdev/thread/90c24c11-7846-433b-b532-4e0739c66786

 

The above also mentions some issues with setting indexing for office 365 that you may have to take into account.

The instructions on how to uninstall depends on the device (Android, iPhone, Mac, Desktop, etc.) that you are using.

5.   After installing Skype, please do another restart before signing back in.

2. Uninstall Skype by opening your Applications folder and drag your copy of Skype to the trash.
3. Then open ~/Library/Application Support * and drag the Skype folder to the trash. The ~ sign represents your home folder. To find your home folder, open Finder and, from the menu bar, select Go > Home
4. Visit www.skype.com and install a fresh copy of Skype.

For iOS:

  1. Tap and hold Skype icon until it shakes.

  2. Click on the X button that will appear on the upper left corner of the Skype icon.

  3. Tap on the X, then tap on â€œDeleteâ€ to uninstall Skype.

  4. Reboot your device. Reinstall Skype from the Apple Store.

  5. Reboot once again your device before signing back in to Skype.
