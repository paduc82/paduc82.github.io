a. user say:

The transmission speed of your network adapter is measured in Mbps, while you say that the download speed of your internet connection is measured in mbps. There's a massive difference between M and m. Better check your internet speed.

In any case, the rated transmission speed of your network adapter (which can't be increased) and your internet connection are both theoretical maximums measured under laboratory conditions. What you actually experience in the real world is usually much slower.

There is no standard for measuring download speed, so ISPs are free to use whatever creative method suits their advertising. You will probably laugh when you see how differently your download speed is measured by different websites (unless you cry.)

Most people pay for much more download speed than they need. Unless you have a special reason for needing more, anything beyond 50 Mbps isn't adding anything other than money to your ISP.

Finally, the download speed only measures how quickly the electrons can travel to your computer once they reach the internet. If a web server or a relay is overloaded or misconfigured, or if a page is poorly coded or filled with advertising you will still have to wait.

canadian tech say:

Ken Morley of this forum has come up with the solution to this problem:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_7-update/windows-update-remains-at-checking-for-updates/ff3c442e-79a6-40d3-ac35-29e130cebef1

The following procedure was successful for me and I repeated it again just to be sure:

1) Download KB3083710 and KB3102810 in advance.

2) Disconnect the computer from the Internet so Windows Update cannot automatically update itself to 7.6.7600.320.

3) Perform a fresh reload from Recovery Media or Windows 7/64 with SP1 RTM.

4) Manually install KB3083710 and restart - WU components will now be version 7.6.7601.19016 dated 9/25/2015.

5) Manually install KB3102810 and restart - WU components will now be version 7.6.7601.19046 dated 10/20/2015.

6) Restore the Internet connection and launch Windows Update as normal.

7) After a few minutes, you should have plenty of updates to download and install.

I am not sure that both steps 4 and 5 are required, but I didn't want to mess with success.  I hope this saves someone else some time and effort.

Ken Morley
