graham mayor say:
You would need macros and you have no way of enforcing their use, but that aside, the following macros in the form template should do the trick. In this case the value of form field 'Text1' is used as the name for the document.


Sub FileSave()
    If Len(ActiveDocument.Path) > 0 Then
        ActiveDocument.Save
    Else
        If ActiveDocument.FormFields("Text1").Result <> "" Then
            With Dialogs(wdDialogFileSaveAs)
                .Name = ActiveDocument.FormFields("Text1").Result
                .Show
            End With
        Else
            MsgBox "Complete form field before saving!"
            ActiveDocument.FormFields("Text1").Range.Select
        End If

    End If
End Sub

Sub FileSaveAs()
    If ActiveDocument.FormFields("Text1").Result <> "" Then
        With Dialogs(wdDialogFileSaveAs)

            .Show
        End With
    Else

        ActiveDocument.FormFields("Text1").Range.Select
    End If

nathan roberts (sunknath) say:

Playing your songs offline on Groove Music

Technical Level : Basic

Summary

Groove Music allows you to download your purchased songs so you can play them Offline. This is easy to do, with the current version of Groove Music - 3.6.2304.0. So if your wanting to download your Music onto your device and move it to a new location, you can. This article shows you how.

Note - You won't be able to download your songs onto the Xbox console's (Xbox 360, and Xbox One), instead you can save them to OneDrive, but you will still need to stream them.


Details

Downloading music onto your device

You will first need to open Groove Music on your device. Once Groove Music is open, you will be presented with a screen like this.

You will see the navigational menu on your left hand side, which will show you the songs you have, either though Music Pass or purchased songs or any other songs imported onto Groove Music from other providers. You will need to scroll down to and click onto 'My Music'.  - as seen below:

Once you click 'My Music' you will see this page. 

If you click onto a song you wish to download, then you will be able to see an option 'Available Offline'. If this is set to off, then the music will need to be streamed. If you do find that it is off, you can turn it on by pressing onto the slider and the song will begin to download onto your device.

Downloading playlists onto your device.

As well as downloading music, you can also download your playlists. To view your playlists, you will need to click onto 'Playlists'. When you have, you will be shown a screen like this.

Your able to see all of the playlists you have created. If your wanting to downloading you will need to right click onto the playlist your wanting to downloading. An option should appear like the one below.

Once you see the option, click 'Download' and the songs/playlist will begin to download onto your device. The songs from the playlist will be downloaded onto your device. Although, the playlist it's self will not be downloaded. But all the songs within that playlist will play when your offline.

Finding your downloaded music on your computer/device.

Once you have downloaded the songs, then you will be able to see the songs on your PC/device as shown below.

Once you have found the downloaded music, you will be able to move it to another location on your PC or even to another device.
