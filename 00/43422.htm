Try these steps, specifically, and please let us know whether the Auto-Hide option remains enabled.

1.  Right click the Taskbar, choose Properties, and enable the Auto-hide the taskbar option (i.e., put a checkmark in the box).
2.  Log off (note, specifically do NOT shut down here, but just log off).
3.  Log back on.
4.  Right click the Taskbar, choose Properties. Is the Auto-hide the taskbar option still set?

The various applications that show up in the System Tray (and in the hidden portion of same) can cause the Taskbar to unhide, even when the Auto-hide option is enabled.  If you are seeing the Taskbar come out and stay out even when the Auto-hide the taskbar option is checked, then it is likely one of these tray apps is doing it.  Before Windows 7, the way to notify the user of a problem or important message was to unhide the Taskbar and display that message.  Now, with the try apps hidden, one of them may be trying to do this but you are not seeing it.  To overcome this, click each one of them and check their individual settings.

Good luck.
