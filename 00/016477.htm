a. user say:

Since this issue appeared when you have upgraded to Windows 10, and clearly you're able to identify the problem was not with the charger.

Let's try these troubleshooting steps to rectify that:

Soft Reset

The soft reset is the same thing as pulling your battery (on devices where that is possible). This method will not remove anything at all from your phone and is completely safe to attempt in any problem situation.

You perform the soft reset by having your phone turned on. Then press and hold the volume down and Power buttons until your phone vibrates and restarts. Note that you should not touch the device screen during the process (not even when the 'slide down to power off' prompt appears).

Example of situations where the soft reset should solve the problem:

  • An app that usually starts won't start
  • The camera won't open
  • The device starts using too much battery
  • The device starts behaving oddly

Hard Reset

The hard reset will bring your phone back to factory settings, the same state as when the device was first turned on. All apps, messages, media, personal settings, etc will be removed from the phone. If backup is turned on under Settings, you can restore your apps and settings after performing a hard reset (as long as you initialize the phone with the same Microsoft Account). Note that resetting your device will not remove any OS or firmware updates. You will remain on the same version of Windows Phone after the reset.

If you can turn on your device you can reset your phone under Settings: Settings  > about > reset your phone
This procedure is described in more detailed in this official how-to article.

If your phone is unresponsive and you can't reach Settings , you may be able to reset your phone using the hardware buttons:

  1. Press and hold the volume down and Power buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10â€“15 seconds).

  2. When you feel the vibration, release the buttons, and then immediately press and hold the volume down button until you see a large exclamation mark.

  3. Once the exclamation mark appears, press the following four buttons in this order: volume up , volume down , Power , volume down . Your phone should now reset and restart itself. (It might take a while for the reset to finish.)

The hard reset operation will take a while to complete and will show spinning gears before it reboots your device to factory settings. Usually it will finish within 30 minutes but can, in some rare cases, take up to a couple of hours. Some older Nokia Lumia handsets have a bug where a reset never seems to finish. If that should happen to you, check this wiki article for a solution.

Example of situations where the hard reset should be used:

  • The soft reset didn't solve the problem
  • You want to switch to another Microsoft Account
  • You want to reset a device that you will no longer use (this enables someone else to initialize the device with their own Microsoft Account)
  • You want to restore a backup

If Soft reset and Hard reset didn't worked at all, then it's time to rollback your phone to its previous Windows version by the use of Windows Device Recovery Tool. This will revert back your phone to it's original factory settings, meaning it will remove everything else, including your apps, games, texts, call history, music, and photos.

Regards.

Whats the formula to convert a number into a random letter - Microsoft Community

whipidhinio say:

Whats the formula to convert a number into a random letter

In Excel I have managed to make a row of random numbers. Each cell contains a random number from 2-33. I would like to know if there is any way of formatting it so if the random number is above 9 it will convert automatically to a letter. I tried making an IF statement but found it too hard to do so.

If there is anyone that can tell me the formula to do so (if its possible) it would be much appreciated.

rich007a say:

You can use =RANDBETWEEN to make a random number between two values.

You can use =CODE to turn a letter into a number, A=65, Z=90

You can use =CHAR to turn a number into a letter

Putting that all together:

=CHAR(RANDBETWEEN(CODE("A"),CODE("Z")))

or just:

=CHAR(RANDBETWEEN(65,90))

will return a random letter between A and Z.

 

From your question it looks like you want to generate a random character from 2 to 9 and A to X (since 10 would be A, 11 would be B...... and 33 would be X, so no Y or Z).

If you want that to happen you could use this formula which will give equal chances to all possible results:

=CHAR(IF(RANDBETWEEN(2,33)<10,RANDBETWEEN(CODE("2"),CODE("9")),RANDBETWEEN(CODE("A"),CODE("X"))))

or

=CHAR(IF(RANDBETWEEN(2,33)<10,RANDBETWEEN(50,57),RANDBETWEEN(65,88)))

Make sense?

Hope that helps.

Cheers
Rich

EDIT: If you want the numeric results to come out as numbers rather than text, this would also work:

=IF(RANDBETWEEN(2,33)<10,RANDBETWEEN(2,9),CHAR(RANDBETWEEN(65,88)))

SUM FORMULA IN WORD TABLE - Microsoft Community

renegr say:

SUM FORMULA IN WORD TABLE

Good day, could someone please guide me in how to add R #,##0.00;(RS #,##0.00) to the number format drop down menu in a word table column.  Thank you.

jay freedman say:

Word uses the Windows setting for regional currency to determine what appears in that dropdown. 

In Windows 7, go to Control Panel > Region and Language > Additional settings and click the Currency tab in the dialog. Change the currency symbol to R (just type it into the box if it isn't in the dropdown). The negative currency format will be (R1.1) rather than (RS1.1), and there's no way to change that.

In Word, the table formula number-format list will now include R#,##0.00;(R#,##0.00). If you require RS in negative amounts, right-click the field inserted by the formula dialog and click Toggle Field Codes. Type the S in the correct place. Then right-click again and click Update Field (or press F9).
