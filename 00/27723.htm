It's like the Mary Celeste in here.  Well, still haven't found a decent solution for multiple email addresses.

Aliases:
I tried to set up aliases for each email address.  The first problem you encounter is because your MX records now direct all the email for your domain to the Outlook.com mail servers, when you go to setup an alias it will ask you to verify the alias by responding to the email it sends to that email address.  But you can't verify the alias because there's no way to receive the verify email, as the email address doesn't yet exist.

So, what I done was to re-direct the email for my domain back to my original (non-Microsoft) mailbox so that I could receive the verify emails and therefore verify the aliases.  However, it was a waste of time anyway because although you've now set up an alias in Outlook.com and verified it, Outlook.com still won't allow you to receive email for that alias (all you get is a 550 mail delivery error if you try sending email to that address).  Yeah, stupid I know!  And it's not really any good for sending emails from either, because all it does is send the email with a 'sent on behalf of...' message on it. 

Mailboxes:
So, next thing I thought I'd try was to set up a seperate mailbox for each email address, then just forward the email to my main account.  Well, not only it is a lot of messing around setting up whole new Microsoft accounts for each individual email address, it turns out it's not very practical anyway.  You see, Microsoft don't allow you to turn off the SPAM filter, and the filter is applied before it forwards emails.  Therefore, if Microsoft deem an email to be spam (even though quite often it's a false positive and not spam at all), the email will get put in to the junk email folder instead of forwarding it to the forwarded email address.  So, it's inevitable that you will end up missing emails due to them ending up in the junk email folder of your non-main mailbox, instead of being forwarded to your main mailbox.

And obviously (as mentioned above), you also have to make sure you sign into each mailbox every 270-365 days to make sure the mailbox doesn't get removed.

Alias Identifiers (using a 'plus' symbol):
I tried using a '+' identifier (such as name+facebook@mydomain[.]com).  The problem is a lot of companies don't recognise it as a valid symbol in an email address and therefore show an error saying that it's not a valid email address.  If you look at the following screenshot, Microsoft themselves don't even recognise it as a valid email address.  You really couldn't make it up!

Conclusion:

So, I've got to the point now where I'm considering just ditching the idea altogether and just re-direct my domain back to my original (non-Microsoft) mailbox.  I'm still running a local account in Windows 8 rather than a Microsoft account anyway, so it's not such a problem as it's only email.

If Word is correctly installed, there are two things that can go wrong:

  • Your Normal.dotm template
  • Your personal preferences and settings

There is no way to bypass one or the other with Shift, so you need to do this manually. It involves some detective work. You can try the following steps in sequence, or do them separately or in reverse. The idea is to identify which is corrupt: your Normal template, your preferences or both.

1) Bypass the Normal template
  • Quit Word
  • Go to the Finder
  • Select Go > Go to Folder
  • In the field, type this: ~/library (don't forget the twiddle ~)
  • Locate this folder: Application Support/Microsoft/Office/User Templates
  • Drag file Normal.dotm to the Desktop (in case something goes awfully wrong, you can place it back)
  • Start Word and test
If Word works OK, you are done. If not, the fault should be your preferences, although there is a chance that both parties are involved. So, even though at this point you could restore your Normal template to its natural folder, better leave it on the Desktop for now.

2) Bypass your Word preferences
  • Repeat the first four bulleted steps from before
  • Locate this folder: Preferences
  • Drag file com.microsoft.Word.plist to the Desktop
  • Start Word and test
If Word works OK, you are done. If not, try dragging other preference files, such as com.microsoft.office.plist. You can drag anything starting with com.microsoft… out of that folder, but the less things you drag out, the less your current preferences will be changed.

Once you get Word running (and you will), you can try restoring Normal.dotm to its former glory in its original folder and check if Word still runs.

Finally, dispose of the Desktop files that caused the problem.

PS (April 14, 2015): User philmcmahon states the above deletions did not suffice to make Word start in a stable manner. He advocates a further deletion that solved his problem:

  • ~/Library/Saved Application State/com.microsoft.Word.savedState
