Need Help to Change my outlook calendar to my new address

tammy.m say:

Hi JScheffler,

It sounds like you may have created a what is called a new Profile.

I am assuming you changed your Internet Service Provider. I am also assuming that you want to get your calendar appointments from your old profile to your new profile.

With that said let me fill in with a little more information.

In a simple Profile you have services such as:

(Service 1) Account - Contains your internet service provider information needed for you to send and receive email.

(Service 2) Data file also called Personal Folders - Contains all your data including Inbox, Contacts, Calendar, Outbox, Sent Items, Notes, Journal, etc. Your data file or Personal Folder can be used with multiple profiles.

Here is an acronym I use to help explain how Profiles and the associated services work.

Think of your Profile as a drawer in a filing cabinet.

With your filing cabinet you can only have 1 drawer open at a time. It is the same with a profile, you can only be using one profile at a time.

Your filing cabinet contains 2 drawers. One drawer is your old profile and the second drawer is your new profile.

Your filing cabinet drawers have folders in them.

In drawer 1 (Old Profile), You have a folder labeled Account this is your old service provider and you have a second folder labeled Personal Folders which contains all your data including your appointments.

In drawer 2 (New Profile), you have a folder called Account with your new service provider information and you also added and created a new folder called Personal Folders which contained nothing except possibly new email but not your appointments. 

What you need to do is open your old Personal Folder and drag the appointments to your new personal folder.

To drag the appointments from the old calendar to your new calendar, you will probably need to change the view in Outlook from a calendar view to something like Active Appointments and then drag all the appointments to your new Calendar Folder in your new Personal Folder.

I hope this helps to understand how Outlook works.

The following article explains how to manage your Personal Folders.

Create and use .pst data files in different versions of Outlook

The simplest fix for issues with anything under the WindowsSelfHost Registry Key is to simply delete the entire WindowsSelfHost Key. It will be rebuilt. Reboot computer.

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\WindowsSelfHost

You may want to double check your Insider settings to make sure it is on Fast, it has been known to change to Slow sometimes.
