You might notice that the last balance is not zero.  This is because I round PMT (B6 and B7). It should be rounded because it is a real-world amount, in contrast to interest, principal and balance.  Lenders have varying ways of dealing with this.  Some do not give it any thought because most loans are closed before they go to term.  If you remove ROUND(...,2), you will see that the final balance is nearly, but usually not exactly zero due to binary arithmetic anomalies.

Some of these 'errors' that show up in the Design Checker have no impact on the page. If you can edit, save and print the page successfully then it is probably safe to ignore. You certainly would not want to 'reload' or 'reinstall' the program.

Your 'error' might be that you have a design element that is encroaching on the top margin for the page and this might impact the printed results. Note the distance from the top of the page to the first element on the page. Go to View > Rulers if you don't have the rulers in view on the side and top of the page. Now go to Page Design and click the down arrow beside Margins and take note of what the margins are for the page. For example if the design element is touching the top of the page and the page has a .5 inch margin then the design checker would probably generate a "top margin has space above it" error.

One thing you can do that might give some insight as to what the Design Checker is talking about is to change the default boundary setting. Go to View > Check Boundaries . Now you will be able to see the text box boundaries even when you have not selected them and in your case might see where a text box boundary is encroaching on the top margin for the page. This is also especially helpful if you happen to have any empty text boxes on the page. I don't know why this would be left unchecked in Pub 2010 as I can't imagine being able to layout my page without seeing all the boundaries of all the elements on the page.

When every other way of figuring out why something is wrong with formatting, etc. this is the approach I take.


Open your publication and drag all the design elements off the page into the scratch area around the page. Then run the DC and see if the error is gone.
