hiya... i posted a few posts up, but i wanted to post and say i found a resolution, so hopefully this will work for you, too.

 

i noticed in the registry (after Sanmartin pointed out what the version should be) that i was missing quite a few entries.  i asked to see the registry of a friend who was running windows 7 under direct X and could play civ 5 perfectly fine, and low and behold they had 2 items in the registry that I did not.  turns out i *did* have the most updated version of direct x, but somehow the part in the registry where it tells the computer what version you're running got misplaced... so that's why steam would say i didn't have the most recent version - because there was no way for the computer to tell steam what version it had.  anyway, here's how I fixed it:

 

- pull up the registry (regedit in the search box)

- go to HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE > SOFTWARE > MICROSOFT > DIRECTX

- if you have a friend who runs this fine, you can just have them copy their registry files for directx onto a disc for you and place them into your own directory.  if you don't:

- right click on the directx folder, click on NEW > STRING VALUE.  where it asks for data, put "4.09.00.0904" (without the quotation marks)

- right click again on the directx folder, click NEW > BINARY VALUE.  where it asks for data, put "00 00 00 09 00 00 00 00" (minus the quotation marks but INCLUDING THE SPACES)

 

make sure it saves you work, close the registry, and open up steam again.  should let you play now!  at least it worked me, anyhow
