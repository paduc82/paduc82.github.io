haaklong say:

FEEDBACK: Bing Audio/Bing Music + Bing Smart Search

I want to suggest a feature that has been on Windows Phone for years, the ability to search music in Zune/Xbox Music by letting the device listen to surrounding music and finding it in the Zune Marketplace/Xbox Music store, I've already made a concept, one is integrating it directly into Windows' Bing Smart Search.

In this version you can simply open Bing Smart Search, and click on the Arrow and select music (which will be listed with Everywhere, Settings, Files, Web Images, and Web Videos), and it will search for the song in Xbox Music and allows the user to play it with the Xbox Music Radio (formerly SmartD.J.), another suggestion might be to include 3rd party software platforms if/when they're installed like Spotify, iTunes, Google Play Music, Pandora, Etc.

The image of the band is used to represent a song playing either on your P.C. or near your P.C. which Bing Audio/Bing Music will hear with your P.C.'s microphone.

My other suggestion is...

integrating Bing Audio/Bing Music directly into Xbox Music (and maybe also Xbox Video to search for music video's), and maybe also integrating this directly into Internet Explorer as an add-on.

Here are my reasons why it would be better suited in Bing Smart Search:

Integrating it in Bing Smart Search will boost the usability of the built-in Bing applications and search capabilities, and will make it more competitive compared the built-in search functionality on other platforms. Bing Smart Search for Windows and Bing Smart Search for Windows Phone should also resemble each other more, and will give a more seamless experience between both platforms. :-)

Here are my reasons why it would be better suited in Xbox Music:

If integrated with Xbox Music it could be more easily ported to the Xbox Music apps on Android and iO.S. where it could gain ground on the competition by offering a service that most others don't. Bing Audio/Bing Music in the Android and iO.S. apps of Xbox Music will also eliminate the need for other music recognition software. Another advantage that Xbox Music offers over Bing Smart Search is that updating Xbox Music can go directly from the Windows Store, Google Play, Apple App Store, and Windows Phone Store, while Bing Smart Search is updated via Windows and less frequently, thus allowing bug fixes and improvements to ship easier.

How do I download a file?

How can I download the object file so I can remix in my own tool outside paint 3D?

[Moved from: Windows Insider Program / Apps on Insider Preview / Remix 3D / Getting Started]

devlin sco say:

Hello,

Are you referring to exporting your project to a 3D file? You can simply save your project in 3MF (.3mf) format that can be opened on different 3D editing tools.

  1. Open your project in Paint 3D.
  2. Click menu at the top-left corner of the window.
  3. Select Save as > Export.
  4. Select the save location for the file.
  5. Enter the File name.
  6. Make sure to save the file as 3D - 3MF (*.3mf).
  7. Click Save to finish.

Let us know if you need further assistance.

Just to clarify, are you referring to downloading a project from Remix 3D? If you are, you will still need to have Paint 3D in order to open the project. You can have the project exported to 3D - 3MF (*.3mf) once its opened on Paint 3D.
