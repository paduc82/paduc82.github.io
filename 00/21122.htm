f.     To change the view in the right pane, right-click anywhere in the pane and choose from the View options.

I think the way Dropbox works is that it saves a temporary file on your local computer.  It doesn't actually save back to Dropbox until you close the file.

So sadly, your saves went to the Mac temporary file, not to the Dropbox.

I wouldn't edit anything big or for long to Dropbox (or any other cloudy solution).  The inner tubes are just not that reliable.

I would also strongly recommend that you go to Word>Preferences>Save and enable "Always make backup".  When you do, the previously saved version of an open document is preserved in the same folder, labeled "Backup of..."

Not only does it enable you to go back to the previous save when Word blows up, it makes Word less likely to blow up.

Hope this helps
