1. Instead of deleting all IMAP accounts, a better option is to unsubscribe from the All Mail folder (Tools > IMAP Folders > Select Folder > Unsubscribe)

2. For Gmail accounts, Microsoft recommends using the "Don’t store a copy of sent messages" in the Account Settings. Microsoft actually added this preference specifically for Gmail. This is the best way to avoid duplication, and not having to store Sent Items somewhere else

By default in SP2 IMAP operates as "push mail". It always has an IMAP IDLE command outstanding against the server and will download new email the moment it arrives on the server.

mike_myers_n8 say:

Suggestion - you can try the same thing I did. I was expecting to have Nokia Care Center re-flash the phone. This obviously means *everything* on the phone would be gone. So, you've got nothing to lose!

Do several backups, on the memory card, on your PC, using Nokia Suite, and also PC Suite. One of those should bring back all your data.

Next, back up your contacts and photos someplace safe, using any method you want. Check out "PhoneCopy" as one place to store a backup.

Now, look at all the programs you've got installed, and start removing them, one by one. Get rid of anything you don't need first. You can do this all at once, or in steps.

Remove the memory card from the N8. All you've got left now is your phone, no cables, no memory cards, nothing.

Turn off bluetooth, and try to make sure everything is at the default settings. No Wi-Fi, no nothing.

 

Look at the list of installed software, etc. If it's not absolutely necessary, dump it. Change back to a standard "theme", and standard everything-else. Look around until you are satisfied that you're back to a new phone, un-modified. You'll never get all the way there, but maybe you won't have to.

Long before I finished removing software, my N8-Belle got over whatever was ailing it, and started behaving properly once again.  Maybe you too will be lucky enough to remove whatever it is that's messing the phone up.

 

(I suspect it's not Belle, but rather something else on the phone that doesn't play nice with Belle.)
