Is your OS Windows 10 Anniversary Edition? In all likelihood it is by now. Miracast is broken in that platform at a WiFi adapter device driver level for Surface Pro 4.

Basically, whenever you try a wireless connection to a display/TV/projector, you invoke the WiFi Virtual Display Adapter to establish the connection with your device.

Try this for yourself: Open a 'View Network Connections' window on your Surface, and then try connecting to your wireless display just the way you would normally from Settings -> Display...

Keep an eye on the network connections window while you're doing that - you'll see another WiFi connection's icon flash briefly in that window. The very fact that it dies soon after it is spawned is what causes the display connection to fail.

If you dig deeper into the properties of that failed WiFi connection, you'll see an error message saying 'Device not migrated'. Essentially, telling you that when the automatic update to Anniversary Edition happened, this device's driver was not updated successfully.

There's no fix issued by MS as yet. Wait for them to show us some mercy :-(
