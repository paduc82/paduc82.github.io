This happens if your secondary hard drive loses its letter / path, for whatever reason, after installing Windows 10. Try the below steps:

  1. Press Windows + R keys on the keyboard to open run command prompt. (Run)

Q) "What is it? I've never heard/read anything about "uudial.com".

A) uudial.com is the enterprise dialup service of Verizon Enterprise / Wholesale formerly MCI Communications.  Actually MCI still exists and Verizon just added a DBA (doing business as) when they acquired Verizon, they just repainted the street labeling fiber and MCI was painted/labeled referring to MCI - MFS/WorldCom Metro Fiber and or the MCI - UUNet/AlterNet Backbone.

UUNet was really known back in the 1990's as they were the first commercial ISP in the USA, and if you wanted to get online, and be connected to everyone and everything, you had to get a T-1, ISDN, Dialup through UUNet, although there were many resellers of UUNet dialup such as EarthLink in the beginning, as well as Mindspring, and AOL.

how important UUNet was:

I was a network engineer working for a small ISP with about 5,000-10,000 business/enterprise/government customers and we sold dialup through our own POP, and sold T-1's to all the cities in the area, as we were the first organization in 1994 to have a handful of DS-3's - From our office we had one DS-3 to PAIX (pronounced PAX by most) at 529 Bryant St in Palo Alto where we had our Main backbone Network gear, our dialup POP as AT&T/SBC/PacBell central office was next door, a DS-3 from 529 Bryant Palo Alto to UUNet PAO1 for internet, with a shadow DS-3 to SJC7 at 55 Market ST in San Jose incase of PAO1 going down or we needed extra bandwidth, and a second DS-3 to UUNet SFO4 for Internet Access from the Office itself, office colo, office aggregated T-1's from clients who connected at the office, we tried to get them to the colo at PAIX, but some refused, so we would do it, it would just cost them $1,000 more per T-1.  We used to sell consulting services, a T-1, and Colo for $10K a month.

At the time it was huge as PacBell installed a OC-12 into our office, and the only other fiber customer was Alta Bates hospital, which was hooked up at the same time as us, as they were a couple of blocks away.

Why Microsoft has parts of uk.uudial

I believe Microsoft bought an old datacenter from MCI in 2004-2005 that under WorldCom in the 1990's acquired PipeX.  At the time PipeX was acquired they were a network service provider and hosting provider.  So Verizon ditched some datacenters as they have thousands, and in the UK PipeX was taken by Microsoft since Microsoft already had used 3/4 of the datacenter anyway. 

Verizon should actually move the nameservers for uudial.com off Pipex.net as Verizon got rid of the name, and still has the nameservers for UUDial.com using PIpeX.net domain name.  The name servers should be renamed to uudial or preferably, ns-X.uu.net as you never want to have the same domain name for the nameservers as you have for your domain due to DDoS attacks.

Verizon should also re-assign the netblock for reverse DNS so Microsoft has control of it.  Most of the time, Microsoft leaves IP's without reverse DNS, but that is their choice.

davem121 say:
  1. If you cannot see file extensions already, go to Windows File Explorer, on the View Ribbon, check the box marked ''File Name Extensions', close Windows File Explorer
  2. On your Desktop, right click an empty part and choose New - Text Document, name the document XXX.exe and make sure you remove the .txt file extension
  3. Open Control Panel, click the Default Programs Applet
  4. Click Associate a file type or protocol with a program and let the list build
  5. In the left list scroll down to .srt, select this entry and click 'Change Program'
  6. In the resulting list, click 'More Apps' and at the bottom of the list click 'Look for another app on this PC'
  7. Navigate to the file you created on your desktop (XXX.exe)
  8. The list will rebuild and the association will be made
  9. Close all Control Panel windows
  10. Delete the file you created on your Desktop
  11. Now .srt files will revert to 'Unknown file type'

Please Note: If you need to do this again in the future for another file type, use a different name for the file on the Desktop with the .exe file extension . . .

Also Note, using the Settings App to change the file association, instead of Control Panel in Windows 10 does not work!
