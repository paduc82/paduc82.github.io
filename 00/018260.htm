john mcghie say:
Hi Greg:

See here:  http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/mac/forum/macoffice2008-macword/a-footer-section-is-outside-of-a-printable-area/0891f06a-5826-4203-a76f-249c75528c11

In the Word Help, read the topic "Use headers and footers".

The issue you have is one of the more complex problems in publishing: the difference between the "Sideways page" and the "Landscape Page".

A "sideways page" is where the headers and footers remain in portrait mode, and the content of the page is rotated.  That's the one you want.  In a Landscape Page, everything including the headers and footers are rotated.  That's not what you want this time.

If you take a portrait page and set it landscape, the headers and footers rotate so they remain at the top and bottom of the text. But that edge of the paper is wider, so you have to re-set the tab positions.  If you change back, the headers and footers are narrower, and again you have to reset the tab positions.

Setting up a proper sideways page is complicated, and you're out of time.  So I suggest that you simply REMOVE the header and footer from it.  That looks OK, the readers won't miss the header and footer and page number for one page, it doesn't look like an accident, and it avoids your having to deal with complexities about how to rotate the text.  Just take the headers and footers off.  While you are at it, set the top and bottom margins to the same dimensions as the portrait left and right margins so the print image lands in the same position.

As I am sure you are aware, it's not the program that is making you late.  Some of the things we have to do in business are complex, and require powerful tools that take some learning to master.  Word is one such tool.

#1 - First and most importantly, OneDrive has nothing to do with Outlook or to put this another way, you cannot use Outlook data files stored on OneDrive.

#2 -There is no restriction on what email accounts (email address) can be used in Outlook, just a matter of configuring the email account.

#3 - What you can and cannot "sync" in terms of "Outlook" between devices depends on the protocol used when configuring the account. In your case, it's going to be whether you configure the account using <Imap> or <POP3>. Each device must be configured accordingly. 

In a nutshell - every device connects to one or more <email accounts> using an <email client> which in turn requires each <email client> to be configured using a specific <email protocol>. <Email protocols> are industry standards not specific to an <email client> and what protocols are supported at the server level is up to each ESP (email service provider)

Understanding Email Accounts and Email Clients
