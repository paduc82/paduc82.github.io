john mcghie say:
The formatting is doing what you told it to do.  On your new computer, the screen width (and maybe the zoom level) is different.  You are seeing the result of the formatting settings when the window size changes.  This is what is likely on your customer screen too.

The key here is to understand the effect of the formatting options so you can take control.

When you right-click a text box, you see a "Wrap Text" option.  In there are several options:

The options are illustrated here:
http://office.microsoft.com/en-au/images/use-text-wrapping-options-to-move-a-clip-within-a-document-HA001083136.aspx

The most important thing to understand is that Microsoft Word is designed to reflow text, and it will always do so unless you prevent that.  The easiest way to prevent that is to set the wrapping option to "In line with text", which treats the text box as a very large single character.

To use any other wrapping style, you must know that you are then programming Word to move things around.  In which case, you must be in control of the paper size, margins, window width and zoom ratio.

The easiest way to do that is to print the document to PDF and send the PDF to the customer.  Then no matter what size the customer's screen, your components can't move.

Hope this helps
