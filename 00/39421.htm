hairyfool say:

The label is exactly that, a device that has been given that label. It would only be applicable to you and nobody else.

The 1st place to look is the router or whatever device acts as your DHCP server. It will show you all devices it has issued addresses to and in some cases also other units on the network that it has seen and not had to allocate an address. Associated with that should be the MAC Address and you can use the 1st 3 pairs of the MAC address to look up the network chip manufacturer which may identify the device.

You can't "remove" devices, they are items found to be on the network and you need to identify the device. Don't forget they include games consoles, tablets, phones and satellite tuners (channel xxx?). Arris manufacturer routers etc.
