jay freedman say:
For the styles that are in the Quick Styles gallery (that's what Word calls it) that you don't want to see, just right-click each one and click "Remove from Quick Styles Gallery".

For styles that aren't there but you do want to see, first open the Styles pane by clicking the tiny arrow in the bottom right corner of the Styles group on the ribbon (or press the shortcut Ctrl+Shift+Alt+S). Right-click the style's name there and click "Add to Quick Styles Gallery".

Two complications may occur:
  • By default the Styles pane shows only "Recommended" styles, which may not include the styles you created. Tofix that, click the Options link at the bottom of the pane and choose one of the other items in the "Select styles to show" dropdown.
  • When you add a style to the Quick Styles gallery, the order in which it appears depends on a priority that may not be what you want. To change that, click the Manage Styles button (just to the left of the Options link) and click the Recommend tab of the dialog. Click one style at a time and use either the Move Up/Move Down buttons or the Assign Value button to change the number to the left of the style name.
Depending on how you work, having the Styles pane open all the time may be preferable to using the Quick Styles gallery. For one thing, because it sits along the right side of the document, it's often less mouse travel to get to the Styles pane than to the ribbon.

Another alternative, if you prefer keystrokes to reaching for the mouse, is to assign a keyboard shortcut to each of your styles. You can do that through the Options dialog: click Customize Ribbon, then the Customize button to open the Customize Keyboard dialog.

tom ogilvy say:

I am not sure why you are rebuilding your list in another location.

Just build a formula like

M2:  =Index($B$2:$B$302,row(A1),1)      and drag fill it down to pull over the names.  Then to the right of that (N2) put in an index and match formula to pull over the items from column 1

N2:  =Index($A$2:$A302,Match(M2,$B$2:$B$302,0),1)

ashish mathur say:

Hi,

Assume your source data is in range A2:B8 of sheet1.  Headings are in A1:B1.  In cell B2 of sheet2, enter this array formula (Ctrl+Shift+Enter) and copy down

=IFERROR(INDEX(Sheet1!$A$2:$A$8,SMALL(IF(Sheet1!$B$2:$B$8=Sheet2!A2,ROW(Sheet1!$A$2:$A$8)-ROW(Sheet1!$A$1)),COUNTIF(Sheet2!A$2:A2,Sheet2!A2)),1),"")

Hope this helps.
