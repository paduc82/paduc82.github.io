I was able to work around this by modifying /etc/smb.conf to turn off posix locking for the share by adding:

posix locking = no

Samba should continue to manage locks, it just won't take an OS lock, so you need to avoid writing files on that share from anything other than the SMB mount or the locks won't be honored. I designated a share specifically for OneNote which should do well enough for now.

Use at your own risk though. As Bernd mentioned, a Windows share is your safest bet.

If you verify again that it is definitely not there, go into the Control Panel, Programs and Features and make sure Microsoft Office is listed.  For example, I have Microsoft Office Ultimate.  When I go into Programs and Features, I see the office icon with the name: Microsoft Office Ultimate 2007, the date it was installed and the size of the file.  You should see YOUR version listed in this folder.

 

If you do not, then something is out of wack ... I would reinstall Office.  In fact, if it is listed and you definitely DO NOT have that folder mentioned above, I would uninstall, reboot then reinstall accepting ALL THE DEFAULT locations.  WMDC is looking for Outlook to in a specific folder, if it can't see it, it will not synchronize with Outlook ( and I do not believe that it will search to find it in another location - although that is just a guess on my part).

 

On one of my computers, there are 12 folders in C:/Programs Files that have as their first word, "Microsoft".  Like Microsoft Office, Microsoft Reader, Microsoft SQL Server etc.  On another computer, there are 8 folders with the name Microsoft.  I would expect that you will see at least 1 or 2 and you definitely should see Microsoft Office if it is installed..

 

Like you , I DO NOT have a folder that is just named Microsoft in C:/Program Files/
