Hi Joel,

You can do this with a macro and keyboard shortcut.

Assign Ctrl+F to the following if you want: 
Sub EditFind()
'
' Find with Match Case and Whole Words Macro
' Charles Kenyon 1 June 2018
'
    With Selection.Find
        .ClearFormatting
        .Forward = True
        .Wrap = wdFindContinue
        .Format = False
        .MatchCase = True
        .MatchWholeWord = True
        .MatchWildcards = False
        .MatchSoundsLike = False
        .MatchAllWordForms = False
    End With
    Application.Dialogs(wdDialogEditFind).Show
End Sub

Removing Parent Account

Hi. I have a parent account attached to my account that was used by someone else. I'm not sure how to change it or take it off, It has me labeled as a child on the account. It is prohibiting me from doing some of the things i would like to do.

a. user say:

Hi Caesha,

If you are under a Microsoft Family as a Child account, you may have to contact the owner of the Parent account to have your account removed from the Microsoft Family. After contacting the owner of the Parent account, have them follow the instructions below on how to remove your account:

  1. Log in to Family, and sign in with the Microsoft account of the Parent account.
  2. To remove the a Child account, find their name, then select More options > Remove from family.
  3. For adults, select Remove from family under the name of the person you're trying to remove.
  4. If they're the only adult in the family and wish to leave, they'll need to remove all Child accounts, then find their name and select Leave family.

Note: Before removing anyone labeled as a Child in a Microsoft Family, make sure to turn off their family settings, or they still may apply.
