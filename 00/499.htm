You can fix the row heights, but that won't stop users from entering more text than fits in a cell - the text will just disappear below the bottom border of the cell.

Click anywhere in the table.

Activate the Row tab.

Enter a number (if you wish, you can specify a unit, for example 1cm or 0.5" or 30pt).

Select "Exactly" from the "Row height is" dropdown.

Click OK.

charles kenyon say:
Tabs are a paragraph formatting feature. They are best set, IMO, as a part of your Style definitions.

With your insertion point in the paragraph where you want to set the tabs you can click on the dialog drop-down in the Styles group (or press Ctrl-Alt-Shift-S). Find the style in use, right-click on it and select modify.

Click on the Format button at the bottom left and select tabs. Set your tabs.

OK to go back to the style modify box. If you want this change to apply to all future documents based on your template, check the box to do so. (This will change your template. When you close Word or your document you may be asked if you want to do so. Tell Word "yes.")

If you just want to set tabs for a single paragraph, put your insertion point in the paragraph and set them on the horizontal ruler at the top of the page. (No ruler? Go to the View tab and check the box for it.)

Word's help has an article on setting tabs. Just search for Set Tabs.
