<Original title - Movie Maker Not Working  >

considerate_guy say:

What you are seeing in Movie Maker is a *preview* of what will be created when you save your final movie. When you are adding clips (photos, videos, music) to a Movie Maker project, you aren't actually embedding any media. Instead, the project is just remembering where all those source files are. When you click the play button to preview the movie, Movie Maker dynamically loads each of those clips to give you a hopefully real time preview.

When you save your *project* using File > Save project, you aren't creating a movie. You are just having Movie Maker remember where it is in your editing session. It will remember all the source files, what edits you've made, their order, effects applied, titles added, etc. The next time you reopen the project, it will look in the previous locations for all your source files and will get ready to let you edit again.

When Movie Maker can't find a source clip, it will instead show you a yellow triangle with an exclamation point. That means you have either moved, renamed or deleted the source files. To fix this, either restore the files to their original locations, or double-click on one of the missing clips (yellow triangle) and navigate to the new location. That should fix the references in the project.

When you are done with your editing, you should always generate your final *movie* using File > Save movie. At that point, Movie Maker will take copies of all the necessary parts and put them together into a single audio/visual (a.k.a. movie) file. You can then share that movie with others without needing to return to Movie Maker or without needing the original source files be present.

So the short answer is you must have affected the original source files so that Movie Maker can't find them. For example, you might have copied the project from one PC to another. Or perhaps you were cleaning up space and deleted them accidentally. Or maybe you decided to rename some folders or move media files around. Do you know which one is the case for you?
