peterjchandler say:

Persistence of status display is a bug that have reported quite a few times over the last 10 years, it appears to apply to all platforms, but is erratic, sometimes the status can be updated quite quickly.  See it often here as have several Skype accounts for different contact groups, although in practice the Linux computers do almost all of the communication.  This does mean that I know exactly when an account goes offline, with the only computer signed into that account (account never used on mobile phone / tablet) then being shut down, yet the status shown in my other accounts can take hours to change to reflect the changed status.  At one stage this persistence of online status was seen by Skype developers as a positive feature for mobile phone based Skype accounts with the erratic nature of cell phone connection, although they may have moved away from that position now.

Perhaps this is an issue that Skype should put more attention on as the issue of persistence of both online & offline status display has led to ... emotionally difficult moments here, and am sure for many many other Skype users

In earlier versions of Skype, chat messages were date stamped with the received time, not sent as now, this then showed if the displayed status is correct, with chat messages shown as pending if the recipient was offline yet showing online, although the reverse did not differentiate between online but status not updated and invisible status, both showing the time that the chat message was received.  Invisible was useful for a single account and only wanting to be available for a part of the contact group, a far simpler solution is additional Skype accounts with different contact groups relating to the setting they are used in, work, sport, social, holiday, etc. etc. contact groupings for example.

    clairemarie-peterson say: Oh, it was definitely corruption, but there were dozens of paragraphs affected, so I doubt that copying and pasting everything but the final paragraph mark would have worked.

    I isolated the problem in the body style. It was a ghost--not an intentional setting.
