sven johannsen say:

Unfortunately TRRS just means Tip, Ring, Ring, Sleeve. It says nothing about the wiring for those connections. Unfortunately that is not quite an industry standard. You can see the issue in the TRRS table at Wikipedia. While the table indicates that most things should be relatively compatible, that table looked different not to many years ago. Your splitter may just not be compatible.

Just as an observation, the sleeve on the picture you included seems awfully short. May just be the picture, which may not even be exactly what you have. Usually all four connection lengths are pretty similar in size. If it is short, the mic and common connections may both be contacting ring 2 in the socket. That would short out the mic regardless of the standard. That is what using just a headphone (no mic) three connector plug does on purpose. No harm, just no mic.

Hello summerbj,

 

For your problem, please go to Outlook > File > Options > Easy of Access > check the Show Read Aloud option

  1. File > Options > Quick Access Toolbar
  2. Set the “Choose commands from” drop down list to: Commands Not in the Ribbon
  3. From the list of commands select; Speak
  4. Click the button: Add > >
  5. Click OK.
