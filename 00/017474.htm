Assuming you are creating an instance of Excel 2003 it sounds like the system also has the Compatibility Pack installed which converts the file to 97-2003 format. How do I sum cells of a column in alternate rows? - Microsoft Community

bradvan cuyk say:

How do I sum cells of a column in alternate rows?

I have a table of targets and results in bowling chart format by months and project and need to sum the results for the month.

I have included a small example below.

Jan Feb
Project 1 Targ 50 50
Project 1 Act 48 58
Project 2 Targ 68 68
Project 2 Act 75 76
Total Act 123 134

I need a formula to sum the cells in the alternate rows for a table of ~300 rows. 

What function or combination do I use?

amit tandon say:

Presume your data is in cells A2:C5 with the month mentioned in the first row.

In case you wish to total all values which end with "Act" in column A, use this formula in B6 & copy to the right:

=SUMPRODUCT((RIGHT($A$2:$A$5,3)="Act")*(B$2:B$5))

In case you wish to total all values which end with "Targ" in column A, use this formula in B6 & copy to the right:

=SUMPRODUCT((RIGHT($A$2:$A$5,4)="Targ")*(B$2:B$5))

You may change the range reference in accordance with your data.

Amit Tandon
