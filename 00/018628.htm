Hi,

 

Most likely the problem that you're having is that you want to rename back  the alias as your primary account. Based on what you have stated on you post, it seems that you renamed your account from @outlook.com to another @outlook.com email address.

 

To clarify this one, when you decided to rename your account,  which is your first @outlook.com, it will became an alias of your new account (your second @outlook.com). You can use your alias to send and receive emails but can't use as account log in. You need to access your new account in order for you to use or access that alias. That's the reason why you can still receive emails from your old @outlook.com email address.

 

If you want to make your alias (your first @outlook.com email address) as a regular account, you need to remove it as an alias first. Please be informed that the recently removed alias would become unavailable for registration for 30days. Only after the 30-day waiting period that the account name could be registered as new Microsoft account. 

 

Please refer to these steps on how to remove it:

 

1.  Sign in to your Microsoft account using this link: https://account.live.com/ManageAssocIds

2.  Under “Other email addresses” click the Remove link below your previous address.

3. Click the Remove button to confirm the message.

4. You should now see a message on the page stating, “You've removed an email address from your Microsoft account.”

5. Select the Save button.
