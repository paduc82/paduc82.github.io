Uninstalling Windows Apps or programs you may have installed yourself does not really free up much space.

eg- I have 38 various programs installed on my D:\ partition and the total space used is only 3.7GB.

If you are desperate for more drive space moving your personal data - video, music, photos, in particular, to another

HDD is likely to free up more significant space than uninstalling the pre-installed Windows Apps (and new bloatware that comes

with Win10 version updates), which by the way don't really get fully removed from your system. They are just made unavailable.

The main benefit of 'uninstalling' Windows Apps is to de-clutter the Start Menu.

The main problem with trying to free up space is it becomes and endless management chore. The most viable solution in most cases

is to get a larger HDD/SSD with enough capacity to allow for a minimum of 10-15% free space at all times.

Unfortunately, if you are one of those poor souls who got tricked into buying a tablet/notebook with a criminally inadequate

32GB 'SSD' , you will need to use an external drive to store most of your data on as many (all ?) of these so called 32GB SSD's

are actually just a chip soldered into the machine, so you cannot upgrade them.

( Not positive, but some of the 64GB 'SSD's' may also be soldered in chips.)
