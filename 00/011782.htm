Word documents traditionally reopen at the same window size & position they had when last saved & closed... assuming that some type of change had been made to the document other than just the document window. However, you're right that Maximized/Full Screen is the exception. I'm not sure why it's designed that way, but in the past there has been a distinction between Maximized & Full Screen... That's no longer the case.

As for the difference between programs, all I can say is that (a) they're different type of environments & (b) the Office 2016 programs are still "works in progress". All are in the process of being rewritten in new code compliant with Apple guidelines & developments in the operating system, itself. Don't hesitate to use the ‚ò∫Ô∏é at the right end of Word's Ribbon to submit your feedback to the development team.

There's also the possibility that others may work with the document as well. If they save a changed window size, that's how it will open for you the next time you access it.

You can fake the program out by resizing the window manually by stretching it to the size you want, making some sort of change to the structure/content of the document, then Save & Close. The document should open to the same window dimensions - not Full Screen, but close based on the size you stretched it to. Similar changes can be made to your Normal.dotm template in order to generate new documents at a larger window size, but it appears that there may be a restriction. Full Screen for new documents may require a VBA solution.

There also are VBA options, but personally I find the built-in keyboard shortcut for Full Screen [Command+Control+f] to be quite sufficient. for me, keyboard shortcuts also facilitate other window changes as well.
