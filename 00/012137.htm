simply powerful say:

1. This is not normal, even if the phone is getting hot.  It is not normal for the phone to get noticeably hot during a call.  (Warm maybe, not hot.)


Some cases can prevent phones from dispelling heat, and I would consider another case if you are noticing your phone getting noticeably warm during phone calls.

The first thing to do is remove any case you have on the phone and see if you can still replicate the problem. 


The cable you describe in #2 could also have damaged the phone, meaning the heat relation is an effect of the problem and not the cause.


If the problem still happens, contact Verizon and get the phone replaced.

 

2) Some Micro-USB cables do not provide the proper power or can handle the power rating for the phone.  Some cables are not well made and the connectivity can also be suspect.

Without enough power or a solid power connection, the phone can randomly connect/disconnect, even after seeming to work properly other times.  A bad signal connection from a poorly designed or damage cable will also exhibit the same problems.

There are good 3rd party cables, but ensure they approved for your specific device.

The Nokia 928 in normal charging can use up to 1500 mA, 1.5A.  Most USB ports provide 500-900 mA, and newer USB ports can provide up to 5000 mA, 5A when they detect they are charging a device.  Even a constant 500mA draw is more than some of the cheaper cables are designed to handle.

Cables are cheap and not worth damaging a device, throw your cable away.

Excel VBA to Format Shape based on Linked Cell Formating - Microsoft Community

rc1409 say:

Excel VBA to Format Shape based on Linked Cell Formating

Hi,

I have couple of shapes in a worksheet and they are linked to Cell A1 & A2 respectively. Can I have a macro so that what ever is the format I assign to Cell A1 and Cell A2 can become format of their respective shapes. At the moment it reflects the values that are contained in those cells.

Many Thanks & Regards,

RC1409
tasos_k say: try this code..
(i assume that value in A1 <> value in A2)
 

Sub abc()
Dim r As Range
Dim ws1 As Worksheet, ws2 As Worksheet
Set ws1 = Sheets("Sheet1")'source
Set ws2 = Sheets("Sheet2")'linked
Application.ScreenUpdating = False
For Each r In ws2.UsedRange.SpecialCells(xlCellTypeFormulas)
If r.Value = ws1.Range("A1").Value Then
ws1.Range("A1").Copy
ws2.Cells(r.Row, r.Column).PasteSpecial xlFormats
End If
If r.Value = ws1.Range("A2").Value Then
ws1.Range("A2").Copy
ws2.Cells(r.Row, r.Column).PasteSpecial xlFormats
End If
Next r
Application.CutCopyMode = False
Application.ScreenUpdating = True
End Sub
