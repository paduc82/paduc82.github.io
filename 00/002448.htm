Actually, the Normal template must be a .dotm (Macro-enabled template) as must any other templates that contains macros. Other templates can (and should...) be .dotx.

Word 2016 does not overwrite the template folder, but it does seem to copy existing templates from Application Support into the Group Containers location.  That is required for sandboxing.

This is possible only in Word, the other applications won't do this.

john korchok say:

Your old Normal.dotm has not been over-written. Word 2016's Normal.dotm is stored in Users/YourUserName/Library/Group Containers/UBF8T346G9.Office/User Content/Templates, while Word 2011's (your previous version) is to found in Users/YourUserName/Library/Application Support/Microsoft/Office/User Templates.

To make your user Library folder visible, hold down the Alt key while clicking on the Go menu and choosing Library. You can simply copy your old Normal.dotm from the 2011 folder to the 2016 one, or:

  1. In Word choose Tools>Templates and Add-Ins, then click on the Organizer button.
  2. Click on the left-hand Close File button, then on the Open File button.
  3. Navigate to Users/YourUserName/Library/Application Support/Microsoft/Office/User Templates and select your previous Normal template.
  4. Using the tabs at the top of the dialog, copy over any Styles, AutoText, Toolbars or Macros you want to keep.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Expanding on John's reply, you did not "update" to Office 2016 -- It was a new installation of a different version of the program. Traditionally, new installations do no overwrite anything from a previously installed version. Likewise, installation of a new version does not automatically import anything (e.g., Macros, AutoText, etc.) from a prior Normal.dotm or anything else.

I certainly don't mean to dissuade you from making backups... In fact, you're to be commended for doing so. However, unless you explicitly removed your prior version & its support files you should find that your previous Normal template remains in its original location. Word 2016 uses a different Normal.dotm which, of course, doesn't contain your macros, but you can use the Organizer to import them from the old Normal template if you still have it. Where it would be located depends on which older version you were using before installing Office 2016... The path John cited is correct assuming you previously were using Office 2011.
