For this, it sounds like something is wrong with the sensor. With the new Xbox, there's those pressure sensors instead of buttons that do the work for you. To fix this, turn the Xbox off and get a Q-Tip soaked with isopropyl alcohol, also called "rubbing alcohol" (not water so you don't screw the system up, alcohol evaporates and doesn't conduct electricity), and clean out the sensor area, making sure that there isn't anything stuck in there, like dust or food, that could be tripping the sensor. If that fails, there is probably a problem either with the button on your controller (which would make it eject/close) which could also be fixed a similar way.
