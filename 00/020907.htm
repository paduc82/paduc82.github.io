macropod say:

Having 980 (or whatever) empty but active rows below your data is the root cause. The 'correct' way to handle this is to delete those rows. That benefits your Excel workbook as much as it does your mailmerge. You could apply a named range to the relevant range in Excel, but I suspect you'd end up with either:

‚Ä¢ empty rows in that range because someone had deleted their contents instead of deleting the rows (ie the same issue you're now having); or

‚Ä¢ rows not being picked up because someone forgot to expand the range.

 

As a failsafe workaround, you could add a SKIPIF field to the mailmerge main document to test a given field that must always have data. You'd code such a field like:

‚Ä¢ {SKIPIF{MERGEFIELD MyField} = ""} for text; or

‚Ä¢ {SKIPIF{MERGEFIELD MyField} = 0} for numbers,

where 'MyField' is the required field.

Although this will suppress the output of the empty records, your mailmerge process is still going to require the processing time it takes to evaluate them.

 

Note: The field brace pairs (ie '{ }') for the above examples are created in the body of the document via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac) - you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message.
