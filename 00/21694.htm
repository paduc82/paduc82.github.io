hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:
If you delete an email from the server and it deletes from your Outlook Inbox, you have an IMAP account.  IMAP is different from POP, which, it appears you used to have, in that it keeps everything on the server so it can be synchronized across all your devices.  IMAP is newer than POP, it was created in response to the emergence of smartphones, laptops, and tablets, all of which folks wanted to keep in sync with each other.  So, this is not an Outlook issue - any email client that is setup as IMAP will respond exactly the same way.  You can always re-create the account in Outlook as a POP account to return to the old way of doing things, or keep the account IMAP, create a local .PST archive file, and move data from your Inbox into it.  This would leave it available in Outlook, but remove it from the server.

Microsoft and NordVPN seem a bit clueless on this issue.  Mac Wheeler's solution to this annoying Office 365 issue seems to work

(https://www.macwheeler.com/windows-10-office-365-cannot-connect-over-openvpn-fixed/)

  1. Go to your Network & Internet Settings in the Control Panel. 
  2. Click on Network Connections. 
  3. Select the connection related to your VPN (e.g "TAP-NordVPN Windows Adapter").  Click on Properties. 
  4. Click on Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IPv4). 
  5. Select Properties. 
  6. Click on Advanced. 
  7. Click on Add under "Default gateways".
  8. Enter the IP address of your router (e.g. 192.168.1.1 is the typical default gateway).  Make sure "Automatic metric" is checked. 
  9. Click OK.   

That should do it.
