andre da costa say:
Create a bootable Windows10 drive(cd/pendrive) via MediaCreationTool. Boot from that drive. Pop-up appears, press "No"(like fresh install). Choose language, edition, architecture and press "Next". Choose "Repair Computer", left lower corner. PC will restart.

Now, we are in recovery manager. We are looking for "Use another operating system". There would be 2 or more instances of Windows 10. Choose the second or the third, machine will restart. Don`t boot again from recovery drive, you can pull it out. Your computer should start properly without any errors. Now we have to make a fix in bootloader. Start cmd as administrator and type:

bcdedit

Here you can find all your windows instances with identifiers. Find a registration with path \Windows\system32\winload.exe and check out the indentifier, it`s probably {current}. Type this, ID is your registration identifier:

bcdedit /default {ID}

For current you type:

bcdedit /default {current}

Restart your PC. Boom, it`s working.

You can also delete corrupted instance, there should be "$" in its path. Use this:

bcdedit /delete {ID}


Restart then check if you can boot.

bill.p. say: Hi there, thanks for your question.

There appears to be an issue with support for Xbox 360 controllers in Killer Instinct for Windows.

Firstly, I'd try updating or reinstalling the drivers for your controller:

- Press Windows Key+X and choose Device Manager.
- Find your Xbox 360 controller in the list.
- Right-click it and choose Update Driver.
- If that doesn't work, try uninstalling it and then scan for hardware changes to reinstall the driver.

Otherwise, try booting into Safe Mode to see if the problem persists:
https://support.microsoft.com/en-au/help/12376/...
