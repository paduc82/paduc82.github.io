You won't get decimals using QUOTIENT function.

joeu2004 say:

The correct answer is =D3/C3, or more correctly: =ROUND(D3/C3,2).

But note that 0.82 is probably an approximation.

When I use the Google currency calculator now (of course, currency conversion changes often), 1 euro is 1.23 USD, and 1 USD is 0.82 euro.

But 1/1.23 is 0.813008130081301, which does not round to 0.82.

And 1/0.82 is 1.21951219512195, which does not round to 1.23.

Consequently, it is not surprising that according to the Google currency calculator, 54.46 euros is 66.80 USD, but in Excel,

ROUND(54.46/0.82,2) is 66.41.

According to http://www.x-rates.com/calculator/?from=EUR&to=USD&amount=1, 1 euro is currently 1.226566 USD.

Thus, =54.46*1.226566 is 66.79878436, which does round to 66.80.

And 1/1.226566 is 0.815284297787481, which does round to 0.82.

And note that 1.226566 rounds to 1.23.

All are consistent with the Google currency calculator.

Conditional Formatting Two Date Columns - Microsoft Community

catrinaduty123 say:

Conditional Formatting Two Date Columns

This is probably super easy but I can't seem to get it just right...I have an Excel spreadsheet that has a list of invoices with purchase order dates (column F) and invoice dates (column G).  I need the following conditional formatting for each row: if the date in column F is after the date in column G, both cells for that particular row fill with red.  Can you help me write this?
hansv mvp say:

Let's say the dates are in F2:G100. Select this range. I will assume that the active cell within the selection is in row 2 (it doesn't matter whether it's F2 or G2).

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > New Rule...

Select 'Use a formula to determine which cells to format'.

Enter the formula

=AND(COUNT($F2:$G2)=2,$F2>$G2)

Click Format...

Activate the Fill tab.

Select red as fill color.

Click OK, then OK again.
