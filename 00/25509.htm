paulcordts say: You can both a formula and conditional formatting for a cell.  You may want to double check your conditional formatting.  If you were copying or moving cells it is possible that you inadvertently altered your formatting.

I would image you used a formula for your formatting in H5(=G5="Yes").  This in no way is altered by any formula you would put in H5.

You did not give much detail but it may be one of these two things

If you copied and pasted the formula into H5 from another cell, you also copied and pasted any conditional formatting, thus eliminating what you had set up.

You conditional formatting formula may be an absolute reference (ie =$g$5="Yes"), when copying this formatting the reference does not change, just as when copying cells. It is harder to see this because when you look at the formula in the conditional formatting section it does not change.

If you are setting up the format formula and pick a cell by mouse clicking, the absolute references appear in the formula (ie $g$5).  This can cause issues if you are copying the formatting or using the applies to range.

If you put in the formula =g5="Yes" as the format condition formula in H5 and extended this range to cover down to H7, when you would check the conditional format for H6 you would see =G5="Yes", but because of the relative reference for H6 this is treated as G6="Yes" (ie the cell one column to the left of this cell).

If you had put in =$g$5="yes" and done the same extension you still see the same formula but because of the absolute reference, all the cell are tied back to G5.

amit tandon say:

Enter below formula as an array formula (Ctrl+Shift+Enter):

=SUM(IF($A$2:$A$100<>"",IF($B$2:$B$100=C2,1/(COUNTIFS($B$2:$B$100,C2,$A$2:$A$100,$A$2:$A$100))),0))

This will COUNT the unique values in column A where column B = C2. So duplicate values in column A will be counted only once.

The sender will not be notified if you will put them in the blocked senders list, not even a bounce back error message will be received by them. Follow the steps below to block a sender:

 

1. Sign in to your account. Go to inbox.
2. Click the gear icon located at the upper-right side of the page.
3. Select More mail settings.
4. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
5. Click Blocked senders. 
6. Enter the email address or domain on the box labeled Blocked email address or domain: and click Add to list >> or <<Remove from list
