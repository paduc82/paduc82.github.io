Regarding your "bitness" problem, unless you are working with ginormous spreadsheets in Excel there is no advantage to install a 64-bit edition of Office, and Microsoft recommends that for best results you stick with the 32-bit edition.

 

As other posters have said, even though you purchased the Product Key Card "at retail" it does not confer a full retail license, rather, you get a Product Key Card license which as has been posted is only valid on that first computer and when that first computer dies, so does the license.  That's why these PKC licenses are about 33% cheaper than the full retail license.

 

Full retail licenses come with traditional packaged discs that you'd buy at a store, and with the product when it is purchased and downloaded directly from the Microsoft Store online, www.microsoftstore.com.
