The problem occurs because the font characteristics in the list template have become corrupted.  It can be fixed by running a macro containing the following code:

Sub Fixit()

Dim templ As ListTemplate

Dim lev As ListLevel

For Each templ In ActiveDocument.ListTemplates

    For Each lev In templ.ListLevels

        lev.Font.Reset

    Next lev

Next templ

End Sub

robert aldwinckle on forums say:

Verizon FiOS, will not allowed allow me to access www.marlboro.com.

Using Firefox no problem


called Marlboro  and said problem not on their end

Actually who you probably should have called is Verizon.  Is it their DNS that you are using?  Trying a different DNS may help.


FWIW my DNS is showing that the host name that you are trying to use is an alias for an Akamai caching server.   You need addressability to that one in order to get addressability to the alias.   Otherwise that could explain your symptom.  The fact that other browsers work then could just be a consequence of different timing in that procedure.


For example, do an nslookup www.Marlboro.com (e.g. in a cmd window) and see something like this:


<cmd_output command="nslookup www.marlboro.com">

Non-authoritative answer:
Name:    e219.b.akamaiedge.net
Address:  184.85.17.7
Aliases:  www.marlboro.com
          www.marlboro.com.edgekey.net

</cmd_output>


If you tried to open that "canonical" host name and had connectivity to it, it would give you an HTTP 400 response, indicating that it didn't want you doing that and didn't know what it meant exactly in any case.   (In fact, some caching servers respond differently and actually help you get where you want to go, so you shouldn't just ignore this possibility just because it fails so often.  Similarly some respond well when you enter just the IP address, thereby at least temporarily avoiding a DNS lookup issue completely.)


If you did an  ipconfig  /displaydns  after trying to open the canonical host name you might see that its DNS record was cached.   That record would stay there for the number of seconds indicated by its  Time to Live


<cmd_output  command="ipconfig /displaydns">

C:\WINDOWS\system32>ipconfig /displaydns

Windows IP Configuration

    e219.b.akamaiedge.net
    ----------------------------------------
    Record Name . . . . . : e219.b.akamaiedge.net
    Record Type . . . . . : 1
    Time To Live  . . . . : 16
    Data Length . . . . . : 4
    Section . . . . . . . : Answer
    A (Host) Record . . . : 184.85.17.7

</cmd_output>


So then when you made IE actually request  www.marlboro.com  it could make use of that cached IP address.


BTW an alternative workaround (which in the past has been my only suggestion) would do most of the work getting the lookup cached by using  ping  commands.   E.g. notice that


ping -n 1  -w 1  www.Marlboro.com 


only caches the CNAME record but identifies the next step in the chain.


Doing it that way means that you don't need to do an initial  nslookup  to see what needs to be done.   I actually used that technique to get the example I showed above cached.  Unexpectedly it did not get cached when I tried it for an HTTP 400 case and I am sure that I did my  ipconfig /displaydns  well within a 16 second window.   So perhaps a  ping  command generates a longer  TTL  than we get normally?   Then that would be further justification for my original procedure, though, as I said, since the other one works better occasionally and is easier to use, I have to mention it in case you would prefer to try it first.


Another thing to be aware of is that IE does its own DNS caching once it has done a successful lookup, so, if it has an IP address that doesn't work, e.g. one which doesn't have good connectivity it may be a good idea to close the browser, and make sure that all instances of  iexplore.exe  are terminated before trying again in hopes of finding a better one.


 
Good luck

 
Robert Aldwinckle
