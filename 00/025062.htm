I want to take the equation of a line of best fit from a graph and use - Microsoft Community

adamparr01 say:

I want to take the equation of a line of best fit from a graph and use it in a calculation. How do I do this? I have seen it done.

I want to take the equation of a line of best fit from a graph and use it in a calculation.  How do I do this?  I have seen it done.

Imagine I have a linear relationship, e.g. the race times of an athlete against their distances.  I get an equation for this graph using Excel.  How do I somehow get it into a cell or some sort of format so that I can automatically predict another time given any race distance?  Bear in mind that the user will be inputting this data and so I don't want them having to manually substitute in their information.

Alternatively, if there was a formula that gave the same results as the Excel graph, I would use that quite happily if it can do the above.  In other words, I don't need to take it directly off the graph, because I could source them from the same data (so the graph in essence is just a nice way of checking the linearity).

Any help would be appreciated!  I'm happy to clarify anything that appears confusing!
hansv mvp say:

You can the LINEST function for this. For its simplest use, select a range of 2 cells next to each other (i.e. 1 row by 2 columns).

Enter the following formula as an array formula, i.e. confirm it with Ctrl+Shift+Enter instead of just Enter:

 

=LINEST(range of y-values, range of x-values).

 

The formula will return the slope of the line in the first cell, and the intercept in the second cell.
