How can one get Word to check for a period at the end of every - Microsoft Community

bbran say:

How can one get Word to check for a period at the end of every sentence?

I can't find a setting for that anywhere in options.  Only to require two spaces at the end of a sentence.
jay freedman say:
The expression [ ^13]@ will match any sequence of one or more spaces or paragraph marks.

For a full discussion of wildcard syntax, read http://www.gmayor.com/replace_using_wildcards.htm.

jay freedman say:
I can't see any way to do it globally or automatically, because you'd get too many false positive hits in most documents.

The machine-usable criterion for "the end of a sentence without punctuation" can't be made any more specific than "a lower case letter followed by one or more spaces or paragraph marks, followed by an upper case letter". However, any use of a proper noun in the middle of a sentence also matches that criterion. Distinguishing between correct and incorrect use of capitalization within a sentence requires an understanding of the meaning of the text.

Still, doing a wildcard search for that criterion can at least reduce the number of places to look. To do that, open the Advanced Find dialog, click More, check the box for Use Wildcards, and enter the following expression in the Find What box:

[a-z][ ^13]@[A-Z]

Then you can click Reading Highlight to highlight all occurrences, or click Find In and choose Main Document to select them all, or click Find Next repeatedly to step through them one at a time.
