Yes and no.  View Previous in Acrobat is kind of like the back button in a browser.  

Example:  if you view pages 1, 2, 3, 4, then 5, the Previous button takes you back up the stack:  4, 3, 2, 1

If you do the same thing in PowerPoint and get to slide 5, the Last Viewed Slide link will take you to 4, 5, 4, 5 etc, ad infinitum until you get tired and give up.

It means "last viewed" quite literally.  If you're on 5 and move to 4, 5 is the last-viewed slide.  Move back to 5 and 4 is the last-viewed slide.  Back. Forth. Back. Forth.

Acrobat doesn't have anything quite the same, so there's nothing to "map" PPT's last-viewed action to in the Acrobat feature set.

5.  Click on Add button.
