mathewpiedoux say:

How to get rid of those files that you know do not belong

We al have those files that we want to delete to free up some room on our drive. Some may be programs that we cannot install because we do not have permission.... Some are locked and we cannot get into them.....Some processes are viruses and you want to delete them...

Well, here is how you fix this to take control of your computer...

  1.  Right click on the item that you want full control over.
  2. Click on Properties (all the way at the bottom)
  3. Click on the security tab (the one that says "security")
  4. Click the advanced buttom
  5. click on the owner tab.
  6. Click on the edit button if you are not the owner shown in the owner box. If you need to plug in a admin password, do so.
  7. a. Click on your account from the box shown, and check off the box that says to replace the current owner.                                  b. If your account is not shown, click add, and type in your account and click the look up button. If the name matched an account, then it comes up.    If you want everyone to be the owner type in users.
  8. You will the need to exit out of it and reenter the properties in order to do anything else. So click ok until you are out.
  9. Re enter into advanced security properties and stay on the permissions tab.
  10. Click change permissions button
  11. Click on the account you want to change, and then click edit. If you do not see your account then, click add. type in your account, then click check names. There are times when you need everyone to have access so that you can do anything to the item, in which case, "users" needs permission.
  12. Once you have found the account, and are into editing it, check the first box next to full control, and then everything will be checked off. this will enable you to have full control once you hit apply.
  13. Click ok to everything and voila you now have full control over the item.

Now you have the knowledge to get rid of stupid programs that"trustedinstallers" needs to give you permission.

Just be careful that you do not delete something that enables the good running of important things.

To delete processes that are running, just open up the task manager and find the process and follow these steps.

  1. take ownership and allow yourself full control over the item.
  2. Right click the item, and click delete. DO NOT EXIT OUT OF THE DELETION IF IT ASKS YOU TO TRY AGAIN if it is running somewhere else.
  3. Open the task manager by right clicking the bottom bar of the screen and choosing start task manager.
  4. Go to the processes tab
  5. click show processes from all users.
  6. right click the process that the deletion says is using the item you want to delete
  7. Select end process tree.
  8. IMMEDIATELY after go back into the deletion access denied window and click try again.
  9. It works.

chiefjoboo say:

I can not find much info on that TV but it seems most likely due to the price, that it's using fake 10 bit color in what's called "8Bit + A-FRC" to get that many colors. This is not technically 10 bit color but 8 bit color that has a screen feature that blinks between two colors in a pixel to approximate the color that 8 bit can't show.


On the other hand, some TVs actually have to make you enable UHD color on each HDMI input before it will show 10 bit color if it's true 10 bit color. My Samsung required me to do that. Look around in the TV options with your remote to see if that's the case in the menu, then reboot your console by unplugging it for 30 seconds. It should show 10 bit after you enable that option, if there is one.
