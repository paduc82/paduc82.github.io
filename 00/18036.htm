rob koch say:

9 times out of 10, weird random opening of programs or moving of the cursor are simply due to either stuck or shorted keys on the keyboard, mouse buttons or the touchpad when a laptop is involved.

This may not always seem to make sense, but to understand the specific actions this can cause you need to fully understand which keys or other inputs are involved, since they aren't always caused by a single key.  In fact, it's possible that the driver for the device itself might be involved, in which case simply pressing all of the individual keys and buttons looking for a sticking key may not find it.

I've seen many of these in these forums over the years, though most are more clearly obvious when they describe a single letter or symbol repeating, often in either the login dialog or when an editor like notepad is run.

In the case of the first video (the second is misnamed and so I can't open it), it appeared that the icons along the bottom of the screen were being cycled through, starting initially with Cortana and then later the individual applications in the Taskbar.  The sequence seemed to thus include a possible Tab character followed by an Enter, since the particular item highlighted was then being selected.  I can't determine how that sequence could occur, but it was otherwise so relatively predictable that I truly suspect it was related to the keyboard somehow.

The reason most people don't believe this is possible is because they aren't aware of how fast a keyboard repeats and that in most cases the PCs operation slows this action down to some extent.  So even though it appears that things are happening very fast, in reality they are much slower than the key inputs are actually occurring.

One of the most common causes of these sorts of problems is spilling something on the keyboard or touchpad, since a sticky fluid will typically start to cause these issues days after the initial spill.  Even if you think you've fully cleaned the fluid off, it always gets into the crevices between the keys or under the edges of the touchpad, eventually causing them to stick or otherwise act in very erratic ways like causing the cursor to move randomly around the screen.

I know all of this from lots of personal experience, with both other people's systems and my own.  In my case I've spilled lemonade on laptops twice, with keyboard replacement the solution for the first and flushing out with water under a sink (keyboard removed of course) the second time.  I still have one slightly sticking down arrow key, though fortunately it only makes a snapping sound rather than sticking in a pressed position.

Can't say if this might also have been the cause with the phone, since those are typically a sealed screen with no buttons today.  However, if anything gets under the edges of a smartphone display then the same sorts of flaky issues occur as with a touchpad, since that causes the same sort of false apparent pressing in areas near the pressure.  In some cases a plastic screen guard or even the edges of a shock protecting case can cause these false "touches" of the display, the first of which I've had happen myself when the screen guard was slightly misaligned.
