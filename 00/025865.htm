bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

You can hyperlink to an image external of the document & you can hyperlink to an image embedded in the document, but neither gives the result you claim to want: There is no provision in Word for making an embedded image or object invisible so that it appears only on execution of a mouse action or any other type of trigger.

If the image is set to In Line with Text it can be formatted as Hidden, but a hyperlink to it will not cause the object to become visible... Clicking the hyperlink only takes the insertion point to the location of the object, it doesn't remove the Hidden attribute. Any objects or text formatted as Hidden display only when non-printing characters [¶] are visible & displaying non-printing characters reveals all Hidden content at once.

Your idea of hyperlinking to images stored externally on a web site is probably the best approach. As John indicated, though, the hyperlinks have to be discernible in some way, other wise the reader has no way of recognizing them as hyperlinks.

jack cook - aka help_line say:

I don't have an iPhone so I have never had to deal with that Anthony but this article may help:

MSFTJoaoRibeiro did this piece on how to Copy contacts from iPhone 4 to Windows Phone 7

 

The article can be found here:

http://blogs.technet.com/b/jribeiro/archive/2010/11/23/moving-contacts-from-iphone-4-to-windows-phone-7.aspx

 

 

This is a three step move, with several minor details.

Let's begin :

1 - Start by install the iTunes on your PC :

http://www.apple.com/itunes/download/

Next, connect your iPhone to your computer and then wait for it to show up as a device in iTunes.

Once connected, click on the "Info" tab and go down to "Contacts". Select to sync contacts with Outlook and then sync your iPhone.

This operation will copy all contacts from your iPhone (including contact pictures, if you use them) to the Outlook Contacts folder.

Just need to make sure that the contacts were sinchronized to the Outlook.

2 - The next step is to install the Outlook Hotmail Connector, depending on your Windows Version, 32 or 64 bit :

Download details: Outlook Hotmail Connector 32-bit
http://www.microsoft.com/downloads/en/details.aspx?FamilyID=39db2b89-af2e-41f9-a175-f93e1377959f&displaylang=en

OR

Download details: Outlook Hotmail Connector 64-bit
http://www.microsoft.com/downloads/en/details.aspx?FamilyID=8a31fbf4-f4bf-4751-954b-5b36d80ec375&displaylang=en

You must restart Outlook and configure a new account, Hotmail, using your Hotmail username and password.

At this point, you are connected to the Hotmail.com service using Outlook, meaning you can send and receive e-mails from Outlook and to synch contacts ;)

3 - You can now start the next step.

In Outlook, scroll to the Contacts of your Usual Mailbox and select all, by pressing CTRL+A and hit CTRL+C.

Scroll to the Contacts inside the Hotmail Mailbox on your Outlook and press CTRL+V.

Outlook will copy all the contacts to the "Cloud". Your Hotmail contacts folder online is the destination.

On your Windows Phone 7 device, click the Right Arrow ( à ) and then choose "Hotmail".

Press the Sync button and that's it J

I hope this helps someone, because it would have helped me a LOT

João Ribeiro
