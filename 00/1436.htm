anoop k m say:

By default, Outlook signatures will be located in the folder ‘C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Signatures’. Once a signature is set, it should be available for all the profiles.

 

Open a new email in Outlook, click on Message tab > Include group > Signature > Signatures. On the Email Signature tab > click the drop-down on the right side of New Messages > select the signature that you need to apply > click OK. To include the signature in replies or forwards, click the drop-down on the right side of Replies/forwards > select the signature > click OK.

 

Outlook 2007 is not available in 64-bit. 32-bit Outlook is compatible with both 32-bit & 64-bit operating systems.

roberttolz say:

I have a clue for you, which I just figured out yesterday.  The culprit is likely to be the Intel Anti-Theft service.

 

I have a Sony Vaio Pro Ultrabook, so given the family tree of our two computers, there's likely to be a link in the common problems we have (had) and the solution that I have very accidentally found.

 

I was having installation problems, not only with Office 365 (couldn't close that "System" service) but also with two other programs (Steam and Kaspersky Internet Security).

 

I had tried virtually everything, without success.  When I unloaded Kaspersky and then tried to put it back, I found out that it wasn't reinstalling properly.  Went to Kaspersky's site and downloaded the 2014 version and tried to install that, without success.  Nor could I reinstall the 2013 version.

 

Now that my computer was unprotected, I went to BitDefender to download their trial version.  When it started installing, it identified the Intel Anti-Theft service as being incompatible and demanded that it be uninstalled before installing BitDefender.  I bet you have the Intel Anti-Theft service on your Sony Duo 13, right?

 

I uninstalled Intel Anti-Theft immediately.

 

But instead of going forward with BitDefender, I tried once more to install Kaspersky Internet Security 2014.  Voila!  Installation without a hitch.

 

I went back and opened up Steam to check if the problem there was fixed.  Yes, it was!  Interestingly, in Steam's extensive troubleshooting tips, one of the things that they point you to is a long list of antivirus programs that could cause incompatibilities (https://support.steampowered.com/kb_article.php?ref=9828-SFLZ-9289).  The most commonly used antivirus programs were on the list, but Intel Anti-Theft was not. 

 

As for Office 365, I had previously installed the current version (through a workaround of uninstallation and re-installation), so I'm completely up to date.  The automatic update function is set to run in the background - though it would not function before.  I bet that it'll work now that I've uninstalled Intel Anti-Theft.

 

So, you might want to either uninstall Intel Anti-Theft completely (via Control Panel) or possibly use MSCONFIG to prevent it from starting up.  I strongly suspect that will solve your problem.

 

One caveat about the uninstallation through Control Panel.....  I called Intel to let them know of the issue.  The tech asked me whether the program was "Sync"-ing before I uninstalled.  Since I could not recall, he asked me first to go back to the McAfee website and reinstall (it was during this re-installation that I saw that it appeared to have some anti-virus operations of its own - no wonder it interfered with Steam and other programs), then start the Anti-Theft service and click on the "Sync" button, then go to Control Panel and uninstall.

 

I chose to completely uninstall rather than use MSCONFIG for a temporary fix.  My trial period for Intel Anti-Theft ends in about 19 days, and I don't think it's an important enough addition to value for me to keep it running, especially in light of its interference with the rest of my computer.
