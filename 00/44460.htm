Hi Michiel,

OS_BOOT_FAILURE occurs when your system is unable to find your operating system or some system files that are required for your PC to boot is either damaged, corrupted, missing, or encrypted/blocked. Usually, this BSOD happens because of these factors:

  1. SATA cables are either loose or disconnected-
    This usually happens on desktops. When SATA cables are loose or disconnected from your hard drive, your PC will be unable to find your operating system, leading to either OS_BOOT_FAILURE BSOD or INACCESSIBLE_BOOT_DEVICE error. If you're using a laptop, it could be incurred by instances like dropping your laptop.
  2. Bad sectors-
    Bad sectors are data clusters on your hard disk that is unreadable. If your hard disk incurred a bad sector on a disk space where some important system files are stored, then OS_BOOT_FAILURE BSOD or other BSODs may occur on your PC and only resolvable by reinstalling your operating system.
  3. Dismounted system partition-
    Some applications could dismount system partitions which could cause OS_BOOT_FAILURE BSOD or INACCESSIBLE_BOOT_DEVICE error.
  4. S.M.A.R.T. drive reporting is enabled
    S.M.A.R.T (Self-Monitoring Analysis and Reporting Technology) drive reporting is an outdated standard early warning system that detects hard disk issues. In some way, it sometimes causes OS_BOOT_FAILURE BSOD.

So far, there are no other reported users who encountered this error after installing Windows 10 Fall Creators Update. Windows Updates does not corrupt system images too. If an update fails to install, Windows will simply undo the updates as revert back to the previous version, and it will not corrupt any system files. cmdrkeene say:

Microsoft facilitates it but they don't really do it completely directly on behalf of those other companies.  For programs that come from the Windows Store catalog for example, Microsoft runs the infrastructure that allows those companies to push down updates easily and efficiently.

The "old way" (which is still the most popular way right now but it's on the way out) is when your computer has a separate updater for every vendor.  So every time you login, there's background work for things like the Adobe updater, Google updater, Apple and iTunes updaters, software checks from the computer manufacturer and dozens more -- all running simultaneously, all trying competing for resources and making the computer slow and sluggish.  It's a big reason why a computer starts to feel slow over time, because all this junk starts piling up and running all the time.

The modern way is to use packages where the platform handles all the updates through a common infrastructure (in this case, the Store).  So no matter how many programs you have, there's only 1 thing that handles the updates.

    Hi

    Question 1: I'm confused on what pose to happen when I make a Skype call from laptop to laptop. First, is the number I'm calling suppose to ring on the phone? And if so, do I need to answer it to get Skype to work?

    Answer: If you will call a number using Skype, yes, it will ring on their mobile device or landline depending on what number you are calling. Or if you will call another Skype account, the call will ring depending on the device that the recipient of the call is using.

    Note: Calling mobile and landline numbers require a calling subscription or Skype credits to cover the calls.

    Question 2: If I'm using two laptops, does everything happen in the Skype window?

    Answer: You can use Skype in multiple devices, if you are referring to Skype calls, you can only see the call window open on the device you have used to initiate or receive the call.

    Question 3: When I run the video test, I see me in window, but when making call the video button in Skype window has a slash through it. How to I get that to turn on?

    Answer: Please be guided that you cannot initiate a video call to mobile or landline number. It is only possible if you are initiating Skype to Skype call. If so, you can enable the video by tapping the video icon.
