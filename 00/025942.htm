Client was not authenticated to send anonymous mail through Office 365

When you send using Office 365 SMTP servers, you may receive error messages. 

"Cannot send the message. Verify the e-mail address in your account properties. The server responded: 530 5.7.57 SMTP; Client was not authenticated to send anonymous mail during MAIL FROM [*.PROD.OUTLOOK.COM]"

If you receive this error message when you are adding the account to a POP or IMAP client, you need to confirm the settings in More Settings, Outgoing Server tab. Tick the option to use the incoming username and password for sending mail. 

This may be trying to use Direct Send. 

When you use Direct Send, you can send mail without authenticating. This is useful for printers, scanners, other devices, and applications but is not recommended for Outlook or other email client.  When you use Outlook, you should use auto account setup and set the account up as a Microsoft Exchange account. If you need to set up a SMTP account in Outlook, you can find the correct the POP/IMAP and SMTP settings to use in Outlook on the web's Settings.

jllatham say:

I suspect what you did was to fill using a double-click.

Assume your formula is in a cell in a column right next to a long list of continuous data.  You select the cell with the formula in it and move your mouse pointer to the lower right corner of that cell until it it is over the small square box and changes from a fat cross to a small + sign.  Then you double-click and voila! the formula is automatically filled down that column for as many cells as have entries in them in the column next to it.

By the way: that little square is called the "fill handle".

More info: you can also just click on the fill handle and drag with it in any direction to 'fill' based on that cell.  You can drag up/down/left/right - just not diagonally.  Also, you can select several cells on a row or in a column and then the fill handle will be at the lower right corner of the cell that makes up the lower right "corner" of the group and then fill by click-and-hold-and-drag to fill that entire group horizontally or vertically.
