What's best will depend on your circumstances.

Will you be projecting the presentation or only using it on your computer (for display or printing)?

If your PC's screen is a 4x3 aspect ratio and you won't be using a projector, I'd stick with 4x3.  Likewise, if you have one of the newer widescreen laptops, I'd go with the widescreen option.

Between the two options, one means "Take everything that's on the current slide and make sure it ALL fits on the new size" That means that it will "letterbox" the presentation ... add black to fill the areas that don't match in proportions. 

The other option is liable to crop parts of your presentation in order to make it fit.  This may or may not be ok, depending on the content of your show.

I'd make two copies of the original presentation and try both methods of resizing, see which suits you best.

A reason why pasting to a blank word document to another may work for you, is pasting the text to a blank doc either strips the style/formatting on the text so when you paste into the other document it doesn't 'freak out' over the style formatting conflicts.


I recommend trying changing some of the Cut, copy, and paste settings:

  1. From the ribbon, go to File > Options.
  2. In Word Options, in the Cut, copy, and paste section, from the Pasting between documents when style definitions conflict drop down select Keep Text Only.

This should stop Word 'freaking out' and freezing when pasting from other documents. This may also help with word crashing when pasting from other applications as well, so if you're experiencing problems pasting from say a webpage, selecting the Keep Text Only option for Pasting from other programs may help as well.

Hi,

I can understand that must make you feel upset. We will work toward fixing this situation for you.

When you first configure the email account, Outlook would download all data from the webserver and internet usage might depend on the amount of data you have on the mail server which could amount to an increase in bandwidth.

When you work offline, Outlook connects to the mail server only when you choose for it to do so. When offline, Outlook does not try to connect to your mail server to check for new messages, download headers that you marked for download, or send messages.

1) Lets change the automatic Send/Receive duration for Setting for group "All Accounts" and When Outlook is Offline from 5 minutes to 30 minutes in and then verify the data usage.


2) Make sure your computer is updated till date with all the available updates installed for Office 2013.

3) You may also start the computer in clean boot state and verify the result. This is to determine if high internet usage is due to 3rd party program integration with the email client.
