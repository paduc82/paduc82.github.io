Any legitimate alert from your AV app, or from your OS, will always appear in either a notification area pop-up, or an independent window (outside of your browser window) ‚Äì never as a pop-up in your web browser. So whenever you see one of these security alerts pop up in your browser, it always means that somebody out there is looking to scam you, and that they‚Äôre using this fake alert just to lure you into calling their ‚Äútech support‚Äù number where they‚Äôll try to sell you some software, or some malware-removal service, that you really don‚Äôt need ‚Äì and then charge you an exorbitant price for it. These Tech Support Scam pop-up messages (dialogs) come in lots of familiar themes: virus detections; hacker alerts; firewall intrusions; hardware error reports; system error reports; and what have you ‚Äì but all of these web-based warnings are fakes, and you just have to ignore the content and close the page.

 

And of course closing the malicious page is usually the problem, because most of these scam sites try to coerce you into calling their tech support number by preventing you from closing their webpage. They do that with a dialog loop, which is just a script that reloads the fake pop-up alert (dialog) every time you try to close it. So in order to escape from one of these malicious pages, the first thing that you have to do is try to close the alert. john p spencer say:

Soundex usually returns an encoded version of ONE string and does not generate a comparision between two string.  For Instance spencer, sponsor, spencerian, and spenser would all be encoded as S152.  ANd emails like abcdefzz @ SomePlace.Org and abcdefzz @ NeverNeveland.com  would both be encoded as A123

So you would be comparing two Soundex codes to see if they match. 

Your major problem is that you need a way to match each record in table 1 with one (or more records) in table 2.

You probably need to use Tom van Stiphout (SIMIL Utility) since it does return a number that indicates the degree of matching.

SELECT AVDC.First_Name, AVDC.Last_Name, AVDC.email AS AVDC_email, SFDC.firstname, SFDC.lastname, SFDC.email AS SFDC_email, Soundex([AVDC].[email],[SFDC].[email]) AS Soundex_Score
FROM Contacts_AVDC_FINAL AS AVDC, SFDC_GoodCntctList_MASTER AS SFDC
WHERE fnSimil( AVDC.First_Name & AVDC.Last_Name,SFDC.firstname & SFDC.lastname) > 0.98

Do not join on the two tables together based on first and last name fields.  If you do you are going to end up with an exact match (1) or no match (0).

SELECT AVDC.First_Name, AVDC.Last_Name, AVDC.email AS AVDC_email, SFDC.firstname, SFDC.lastname, SFDC.email AS SFDC_email

FROM tmp_Cntct_AVDC_FINAL AS AVDC

INNER JOIN tmp_SFDC_GoodCntct_MASTER AS SFDC

AND Left(AVDC.FIRST_NAME,1) = Left(SFDC.firstname,1)

I am assuming that your phone comes with a removable SIM.


1. If it is really jammed, best to let the store/authorized repair folks do it. That way, if something breaks, it is on them.

2. If you want to give it a go, dry the surface of the SIM and your thumb. Press on the SIM, thumb flat on the surface and push it out. The phone has PUSH AND REMOVE mechanism for SD card. For odd reasons, the same mechanism is not included for the SIM tray. It is odd.
