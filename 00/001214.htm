Word Margin Borders

On older version of Word, "Show Text Boundaries" used to show the Margin only where text could be placed, which was very useful when designing a word layout, including tables and shapes.

All later versions shows the boundaries for text that has been entered including blank lines, BUT not for the whole page.

This means I cannot layout shapes / images unless I do 'Carriage Return' to the bottom of the page, and this looks awful.

This has been a problem for many years forcing me to use Word 97-2003 compatibility for Word 2010.

jay freedman say:

This unfortunate change was introduced in Word 2013 and continues in Word 2016. Please visit http://word.uservoice.com/forums/304924-word-for-windows-desktop-application/suggestions/9253599-bring-back-classic-text-boundaries and add your vote. The issue is already marked as "under review" by Microsoft, but the more votes it gets, the more likely it is to be scheduled for a return to the old behavior.

By the way, in Word 2013 and 2016, you can use the Alignment Guides feature to place a picture at the bottom of the page without all the extra paragraph marks (carriage returns). When you insert a picture, the Picture Tools ribbon appears. Click the Align button in the Arrange group, and make sure there's a check mark next to Use Alignment Guides. If the picture has Square text wrapping (or any other wrapping except In Line with Text), a horizontal green line will appear when the bottom of the picture touches the bottom margin of the page; it will disappear when you release the mouse button. Vertical green lines will also appear when the picture touches the left or right margin and when the picture is centered between the margins.
