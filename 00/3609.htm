Pictures in Header/Footer shrink in conversion to PDF - Microsoft Community

conlormar say:

Pictures in Header/Footer shrink in conversion to PDF

I have a picture in both the header and footer of an Excel spreadsheet.   The pictures are the company logos.  When I convert the spreadsheet to a PDF to send to a customer, the picture in both the header and footer shrink to half the size in the PDF document.  I am using Adobe Acrobat X, Version 10.1.6 as the PDF converter.  This has been driving me to distraction for quite some time.  I've Googled it and I see others have the same problem, but no resolutions.  Can anyone shed some light on this.  Does this PDF converter not talk well with Excel 2003?  How can I resolve this?  Connie
conlormar say: It actually has nothing to do with Adobe, I found out.  I have found the answer through other technical support and will share it here for anyone who may be interested.  The problem is with Excel 2003.  Unlike the newer versions, i.e. 2007, 2010, etc., it has no "scale with document" option where you can remove the check mark.  So, the work-around is this:  in Excel, go to page setup>Header/Footer>Customer Header>Click in the picture section>Click on the Format picture icon>Scale height and width to 100%>OK>OK>OK. Do the same for the footer.  This is a nuisance if you have many Excel spreadsheets to be continually converting to PDF, but you can create a macro to do it for you by the click of a button, which is what I'm going to do until we upgrade Excel.
