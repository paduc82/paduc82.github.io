If you want the first formula to give the same result as the second formula, you might write:

=ROUND(G6*6%,2)*1824

But that begs the question:  which is the correct answer?

Really, only you can answer that.

The issue is rounding:  whether to round, and when?

Note that 31.22*6% is 1.8732, and 1.8732*1824 is 3416.7168.

ROUND(31.22*(1+6%),2) would be the correct new hourly wage, since unit wages must always be rounded to the cent (US) at least.
