jllatham say:

Not necessarily the first cell of  the new column, but on the same row with the first phone number.    If your phone numbers are in column B and begin on row 7 and you create a new column C, then you would put the formula into C7, editing it to point to B7 as:

=SUBSTITUTE(SUBSTITUTE(SUBSTITUTE(SUBSTITUTE(SUBSTITUTE(SUBSTITUTE($B7,"+",""),"(",""),")","")," ",""),"-",""),".","")

 

Fill the formula down to the end of your list of phone numbers so now you have a list of compressed phone numbers in column C, beginning in C7 in this example.  Let us say it went down to row 104, so you have compressed phone numbers from C7 down to C104.

Now select all of those cells from C7 down through C104 and COPY them.

Job done!
