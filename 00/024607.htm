susanmo'brien say:

Took me some digging but I figured out how to change this.  

Go into "Settings" then into "Manage Accounts".  Select the email account that you want to see ALL of your emails displayed in your inbox (if you have more than one email account, you need to do this separately for each account).  Another window will open - select "Change Mailbox Sync Settings".  You will see a dropdown menu called "Download Email From".  Click on this and choose "any time" and save your settings.  Then go into your inbox for this account and click the sync icon and it will begin syncing ALL of your messages.  Depending on how far back this goes, it will take some time but all of your messages should finally be visible in your inbox.

Hope this helps everyone!  I thought I was going mad until I figured this out.

Although I think it is very rare, it is not impossible that the problem is a known one, and the link was to a Microsoft Knowledge Base article about the problem. This was the original idea of setting up Office applications so that problems could be reported to Microsoft (this is a feature that you can opt into or out of--if you opt in, whenever Word has a problem it can't handle, you'll be asked if you want to report the problem to Microsoft). If enough users report the same problem--in technical terms, if enough reports fall into the same "fault bucket"--then the Microsoft team will investigate the problem, come up with a solution or workaround, and publish an article describing the problem and solution/workaround. Eventually a patch will be created and included in an update, or a Fix it may be included in the article. Users who subsequently experience the problem will be given information, probably in the form of a link such as you experienced.

I'm not saying you weren't right to be suspicious, but if the link was to somewhere at office.com, it might have been worth investigating. There are three ways to access your email when travelling:

  1. receiving a code to a phone or email address
  2. take a trusted device for accessing emails
  3. have an authenticator app on a phone

Option 1 requires having a phone that has service or an email address that is accessible when travelling.

In order to have access when travelling, one of the listed options must be setup before you travel.

Making multiple attempts to access your account when travelling could cause your account to be locked.
