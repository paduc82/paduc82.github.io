Function CopyFile(strSource As String, strDest As String) As Boolean
On Error GoTo CopyFile_Error

    FileCopy strSource, strDest
    CopyFile = True
    Exit Function

CopyFile_Error:
    If Err.Number = 0 Then
    ElseIf Err.Number = 70 Then
        MsgBox "The file is currently in use and therfore is locked and cannot be copied at this" & _

End Function

Restore data from my phone service - Microsoft Community

ak014 say:

Restore data from my phone service

In my old WM i have lost contacts. How do I restore it from my phone service? Can I archive and then restore? I want all the contacts to be restored in one go. Even if I select all contacts only those selected on the displayed page get archived.
julie fka profjulie say:

My Phone will restore items that are not archived to your phone.  If you've synced data to My Phone and then you lost contacts on your windows mobile device, when you sync to My Phone again, the contacts will be moved from the active contacts to the archive.  If you want to sync all your contacts, both the active and archived contacts to your phone, you should:

Stop the existing My Phone service between your phone and My Phone.  This will remove the syncing relationship between your phone and My Phone, but will not delete anything on the My Phone web page.

Delete the PIM.vol file on the windows phone.  You can find the PIM.vol file by opening up File Explorer on your phone, then navigate to the root folder.  Scroll down the items there until you see PIM.  You can delete the file or rename the file to something else.  Renaming or deleting this file essentially removes all PIM-related data from the phone (calendar items, contacts, and tasks).

Move all the contact information that is currently located in the Archive on My Phone back to the active contacts.

Then Open up the My Phone software on your phone and set up a new My Phone partnership, selecting the data you want to sync from My Phone to your phone.

My Phone will view your phone as a new device and sync all the data that is not in the archive to your phone.
