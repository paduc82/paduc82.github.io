More likely than not, a combination of steps located here [physical link at bottom of post] will be required.

Steps I Had to Take to Fix This:

  1. Close OneNote Desktop  and completely exit out of [force close] OneNote Android on all devices you have your problem notebook synced to.
  2. In Credential Manager, delete the "MicrosoftOffice15_data" user
  3. Open OneNote Desktop and sign out of any account logged into OneNote Desktop
  4. Restart computer [not sure if this is needed, but I wanted to make sure all cache was cleared]
  5. Upon logging back in, open OneNote Desktop and sign into your Microsoft account (this is maybe all that's needed to fix your issue, however if still receiving the same errors, proceed to step 6)
  6. Create a new section at the top of one your section groups and name it "1"
  7. Copy [not move, but copy via "move or copy..."] pages from your trouble section to "1" in small increments (I selected ~25% of  page newly created "1" section... this is not for OneNote Desktop's benefit, but OneNote Android's)
  8. Sync your changes within OneNote Desktop, and when it is done syncing all changes to your OneDrive account, open up OneNote on Android and sync the problem notebook.  (This will take a bit [10 - 30 min, maybe longer], but before going further you must make sure all pages in section "1" have synced to OneNote Android)
  9. Repeat steps 7 and 8 until 100% of the problem section's pages have been copied to section "1" and all pages copied to section "1" have been synced to OneNote Android
  10. Once you've verified all pages have been synced to OneNote Android, delete the problem section from OneNote Desktop and sync the change to OneNote Android
  11. After verifying the problem section has been deleted on OneNote Android, you can rename section "1" on OneNote Desktop and sync the change to OneNote Android
  12. Repeat for any other sections you may be having issues with

jw0914 say:

After some further experience with this OneNote for Android issue, my prior post needs to be amended:

  • It appears that only steps 6 - 12 need to be performed

The cause of the issue is definitely due to large section sizes and OneNote for Android's inability to properly download and recompile the downloaded section fragments that reside within the ./data/data/com.microsoft.office.onenote/files/temp directory [my guess is there's a token and/or timeout issue at play here].

If you experience the issue, as long as you move ~75% of the problem section's pages to a temp section stored locally (on your HDD, not OneDrive) and sync the pages in ~25% increments to OneDrive, this resolves the issue.  It's important to allow for all synced changes on OneNote Desktop to finalize before syncing the section on OneNote Android, as failure to do so will most likely result in a sync failure on OneNote Android. 

These failed syncs come with their own set of issues, as once a sync fails, OneNote Android doesn't usually cleanup it's temp files, thus resulting in massive amounts of storage being used by temp section fragments [OneNote Android doesn't download sections as one complete file, but as a hundred or more smaller fragments, recompiling all fragments into a completed file once all parts have been downloaded and their hashes verified].  To provide a real world example, my Main Notebook is ~1.5GB in size, and after two hours of failed syncs on two separate sections [due to their size], OneNote's storage was up to just shy of 6GB for a notebook only 1.5GB in size.  Due to the location of these temp files, only users with root permissions [equivalency of "run as administrator" on Windows] would be able to delete these system files, hence creating a major issue for the vast majority of Android users.  The only way of deleting these temp files [if Android doesn't after a reboot] is to delete all OneNote data, resulting in having to redownload your Notebook(s) and sections.

    scottgem say:
    Yes it can, if its done properly. 

    You do NOT want to update the main inventory table. As a general rule we do not store calculated values and stock on hand is a calculation.

    You need at least 3 tables. Your customer table with info about your customers, a product table with the description of your products and a transactions table, which records all movement of stock in and out, this table will include a field to indicate the direction of the movement. 

    From there you calculate the stock on hand, by adding the incoming transactions and subtracting the outgoing ones.


    ken sheridan say: You'll find a very simplified example of the sort of thing Karl and Scott have described as Inventory.zip in my public databases folder at:

    https://skydrive.live.com/?cid=44CC60D7FEA42912&id=44CC60D7FEA42912!169

    I should point out that this little file was created specifically to demonstrate the basic methodology for computing opening and closing daily balances, and current stock in hand per stock item in response to another post here some time ago.  It was not intended as a working application by any stretch of the imagination.  It does however illustrate the basics.

    The main thing to look at is the qryTransactions query:

    SELECT InventoryTransactions.*,
    NZ(DSum("QuantityIn - QuantityOut","InventoryTransactions","ItemID = " & [ItemID] &
    " AND TransactionDate < #" & Format([TransactionDate],"yyyy-mm-dd") & "#"),0)
    AS OpeningBalance,
    DSum("QuantityIn - QuantityOut","InventoryTransactions","ItemID = " & [ItemID] &
    " AND TransactionDate <= #" & Format([TransactionDate],"yyyy-mm-dd") & "#")
    AS ClosingBalance
    FROM InventoryTransactions
    ORDER BY InventoryTransactions.ItemID, InventoryTransactions.TransactionDate, InventoryTransactions.QuantityIn DESC;

    This is the RecordSource for the form and computes the balances per item by calling the DSum function.  Using this function rather than a subquery makes the query updatable and thus suitable for the RecordSource property of a form in which transactions can be entered.

    The query could also be used as the RecordSource for a report, but as the database does not include a Customers table this would need to be added and joined to the InventoryTransactions table (to which a foreign key CustomerID column would be added) in the query to be able to group the report by customer.

    The current stock in hand per item is computed by calling the DSum function in the ControlSource of a text box control in the form, not in the query, using the following expression:

    =DSum("QuantityIn-QuantityOut","InventoryTransactions","ItemID = " & Nz([ItemID],0))

    Background image in Excel 2016 for Mac - Microsoft Community

    errolkonat say:

    Background image in Excel 2016 for Mac

    I have a full color picture as a background image set for a cover page.

    In 2 cells I put text that's above the picture. If I want to print this cover with photo, I get a blank page in preview, when I print, I get a blank page aswell. Does anyone have an idea how to print such a page with backgroundimage and text.

    bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

    It isn't clear how you've gone about doing what you've done since Excel really doesn't support "cover pages", but...

    Background images added by using the Background tool on the Page Layout tab are for on-screen display only. They do not print, nor can they be constrained to a single page... they tile the entirety of the worksheet. Excel has no feature for including printable content behind the data on a worksheet. Also, content cannot be added "above" the background, so I'm guessing that you may have taken a different approach.

    Working in Normal View, what you can do is place the image on the sheet & reduce its Transparency if necessary to allow the underlying worksheet content to show through. [IOW, the image overlays any cell content.]

    If you actually have included the image on the worksheet but it isn't printing, the first place to look is the Properties of the image:

    1. Right-click the image
    2. Select Size and Position
    3. Expand the Properties section on the Format Picture pane
    4. Confirm that the Print object box is checked

    If that isn't the solution, please provide complete descriptive details including the exact steps you've taken as well as the image file type. Also, please indicate your present update level of both Office 2016 & your version of OS X.
