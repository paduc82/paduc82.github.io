gpb-90 say:

How to solve Bose Soundlink Mini II connection / stuttering issue when Bluetooth paired with Windows 10 Surface Pro 4

Having not found any online, Microsoft or Bose Support answer to the stuttering problem I encountered, after connecting my shiny new Surface Pro 4 and Bose Soundlink mini 2, I thought I'd post what I think to be the answer to the issue which makes the speaker produce inaudible sound, no matter what kind of content you attempt to play.

(presumably the same issue is experienced with the rest of windows 10 devices when connected to any iteration of the Bose Soundlink range that have the same extra Bose feature)

The problem seems to be with the with the Bose' extra feature of its 'Hands-free Telephony', which causes the issue. Once turned off, the speaker resumes to its normal function of producing exceptional sound no matter what content you're listening to.

Here's how I went about fixing this issue:


1. Ensure your speaker is connected and paired to your Surface by Bluetooth. You can check to make sure this is the case by going to Bluetooth settings.

2. Go to Control Panel > Hardware and Sound > Devices and Printers


3. Right click your Bose Soundlink Icon and click 'Properties'. Click the 'Services' tab and then de-select the 'Hands-free Telephony' option. Click apply and then Ok.
Restart whichever software you were using to play audio content - this should now play without issue :)
