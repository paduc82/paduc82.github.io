I had the same issue when I first installed this printer. Under normal conditions the 64-bit driversDID install successfully on your machine. I have been using mine for the the last three months withNO ill effects. I have used all functions selected during the installation, except the fax; even though the fax test was successful.

The solution was the following:

1) DO a clean install as you would normally would, including selecting/deselectings features you want to install.

2) As long as the device is functioning correctly (print, scan, copy, fax) - IGNORE the device manager - will explain later.

3) DO NOT ATTEMPT TO TROUBLESHOOT, this will disable what drivers WERE successfully installed.

4) You should be able to view the device under the DEVICES & PRINTERS panel, which is accessable via the Control Panel.

The problem is a communication malfunction between HP and Microsoft. HP originally notified Microsoft that the 32-bit drivers were compatible and Microsoft's compatibility page shows this. The mis-communication is that HPDID NOT notify Microsoft that the 64-bit drivers were also compatible, or they did notify Microsoft and Microsoft has not taken any action.

Because of this Microsoft has not updated the DEVICE MANAGER software to recognize the drivers. This is why youMUST IGNORE THE DEVICE MANAGER FOR THIS DEVICE AND NOT ATTEMPT TO RUN THROUGH THE TROUBLESHOOTING dialog. The device manager will show the printer under two different devices (multifunction & other devices). They will have a yellow triangle over the device. If you attempt to update the drivers via the device manger or the troubleshooting dialog, the drivers that did successully install will be disabled and the only way to use the device is completely uninstall the device, recycle the machine, clean the registry, and then do a clean install.

I have been in contact with HP's product support, who had no idea that this has happened. A product support manager contacted me and after explaining in detail what is happening, he assurred me that it will be taken care of as soon as possible. He is getting together with product and software engineers to find out what did/did not occur concerning the compatibility issue. It will not happen over night, but he assurred me that he will be pushing for an escalatiopn in the matter. It could be hurting their sales.

It doesn't matter. Both the HDMI audio output and the optical audio output provide the same audio signal. While one is optical and one is electrical, both are digital and contain the same information.

 

The only thing you should worry about is if the receiver introduces any latency in the HDMI video path.
