You have 3 options.

Email (though not calendar/tasks/contacts) shouldn't be a problem if you have IMAP enabled on the Notes server (i.e. Lotus Domino), as WP7 lets you sync your email via IMAP. Alternatively, though with less functionality, you can use POP3 if your Domino server has that enabled. Check with your IT department whether either of these is possible.

For full integration with the 'phone's built-in email, People hub, tasks, and calendar, IBM's Lotus Notes Traveler product is currently available in beta for Windows Phone. Get your IT department to register for the beta: go to http://www.edbrill.com/ebrill/edbrill.nsf/dx/lotus-notes-traveler-and-windows-phone-7, go right to the bottom of the comments (14 Sept 2012 comment), and contact Ed Brill as he suggests. It's very clear how much difference Nokia's WP7 'phones have made to the perception of Windows Phone in this segment of the market - I suspect most of the posters there inhabit some sort of small to medium enterprise.)

The third option: have a look at http://www.commontime.com/, e.g. http://www.commontime.com/msuite/corporate-email/. Despite the name this does appear to include calendar, tasks, and contact synchronisation as well as email. Their 'individual' Notes email solution doesn't list Windows Phone as a supported device OS, but the 'corporate' one does have support for Windows Phone. Again probably best to get your IT team to have a word with CommonTime.

Please post back to let us know how you get on - I am keen to ensure that Notes users coming to this forum for help get the most up to date evidence - let's get these two great products working together properly!
