    After you start the recorder, select "Use relative references", then select the upper left cell of your target tag prior to performing any other steps. All your movements and steps will be done relative to that first cell: you will get code like

     Sub NameYouGaveInRecordDialog()

    vincentchoy say:

    From the way you described, you are already licensed for Office 2016 via an alternative licensing method (MVL).  Unless you have violated your existing licensing contract, you will still have usage entitlements until such a time your licensing conditions lapse.

    Without understanding the specifics of your existing licensing plans and situation, it would be difficult for anyone to advise if you are compliant. The best is to talk to a Microsoft licensing specialist in your location.

    A slight correction, Microsoft has a Business and Enterprise family of products. E3 falls under the Enterprise family. The equivalent product under Business family is Business Premium. E3 comes with Office Pro Plus, whereas Business Premium comes with Office 'standard'. In your post you did not mention Pro Plus, so I am unable to gauge you are on Business Premium or Enterprise E3. There is no business E3.

    To give you an example of what I am talking about -

    If a particular customer has recently invested significantly to purchase Office 2016 licenses either on Open or Select, his management would have  significant reservations signing up for E3, given that the cost between E1 and E3 is USD8 and USD20 respectively, so potentially wasting the earlier Office 2016 investment or the Office Pro Plus that comes with E3.

    If that particular customer was on Office 2016 due to EA Agreement with CoreCAL, then the transition from EA to Office365 requires certain licensing steps, including the acquisition of a CoreCAL bridge, to compensate for the customer's commitment of Windows Server CAL and SCCM CAL which the E3 does not provide.

    If that particular customer was using some sort of traditional subscription based plans such as SPLA or Open Value, and you intend to stop the subscription after getting Office365, then the licensing of your previous Office stops and logic will dictate that you need to install the version of Office you have signed up for.

    So whether you are compliant in what you are doing depends on what was your initial agreement entered with Microsoft. Without which it would be impossible to advise you.

    Hope that gives you some idea on how to proceed with this question
