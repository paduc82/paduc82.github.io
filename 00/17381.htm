Bing crawlers never drop crawling a non-existing webpage that returns - Microsoft Community

lee the bean say:

Bing crawlers never drop crawling a non-existing webpage that returns 404/410 - even after 8 years

BING, MSN (and YAHOO) crawlers continue crawling deleted webpages that were deleted over 8 years ago.

There are several problems identified with bing crawlers that make the use of bing webmaster facilities such as Reports & Data -> Index Explorer unusable from a users perspective.

1. Page fetches that return 404 or 410 are never dropped from the Bing crawlers.
2. None of the deleted pages have in-bound links as identified by BING webmasters (BWM).
3. Any deleted page that BWM Index Explorer drops from its list returns to the list once a re-crawl attempt is made.
4. Excluding the deleted pages by adding into robots.txt file temporarily stops the crawling (have experimented with a 2 year exclusion), however once the pages are removed from robots.txt the crawlers start crawling them again.
5. Some pages that the crawlers attempt to fetch may have never existed, but simply have been a broken link on another page which was fixed within an hour.
6. Some pages that the crawlers attempt to fetch may have never existed, but were simply made up by BING (e.g. Bing changes capitalisation in webpage URL).

7. BWM Tools maintains a list of URL parameters (Ignore URL Parameters). Crawlers seemingly randomly append parameters where none should be. This act must create duplicate content counts as the real page (without an extra parameter) and the crawled page (with BINGs extra parameter appended) will point to the same single page.

8. Using the Bing content removal tool doesn't remove the non-existing pages from the crawlers (not that it should as the pages don't appear anywhere in BING search results).

Reporting these problems via Webmasters Support hasn't provided any fix to this issue 12 months on despite assurances that the problem is a known problem to be resloved:-

jmh3143 say:

The possible reasons why e-mails suddenly disappear.
1. Messages were manually deleted either due to accidental deletion or were moved or deleted due to your account filter settings.

2. Messages were deleted due to account compromise issues where a hacker might have gained access to your account and deleted the emails.

3. You are using an e-mail client or a third party spam filtering software to access your account.
This often happens when using the Pop3 protocol to retrieve your mail and the option Always leave a copy of the message on the server is not selected.

4. The account was marked as Inactive." Accounts become inactive if you dont sign in regularly via web browser

5. If the feature Conversation Threading feature is turned on.

6. If you use Sweep to schedule a cleanup.

7. If you rename your account to @Outlook.com a prompt would have asked you Where do you want to save your emails? You will have the option to create a new folder for your old account or save the emails in the Inbox of your renamed account.

8. Did you try to restore deleted email messages in Outlook.com?

You could  contact customer support. 

9. Without a back up of your own to retrieve the data from, your chances of finding  anything 2 years old are next to zero.
