Limited or no connectivity on Windows Vista on my wireless network.

Before a couple of days ago my laptop has had no problems connecting to the wireless internet in my house, from the same location in my bedroom.. I live with three other people and they are all able to connect with no problems. But mine will now say its connected, but with "limited or no connectivity" and I can't get online! What should I do?

jack mvp say:

Hi

This usually means that your Wireless card sees the signal and attempts to connect but cannot obtain a valid IP.

Sometimes Rebooting a busy Wireless Router can help.

Check your Firewall and other security application to male sure that nothing changed and it block the capacity to connect locally on the Network.

Assuming that the signal is strong at your computer’s location, that there is No electrical noise around (like 2.4GHz telephone), the Wireless Router Broadcast its SSID, and the DHCP has enough IPs to assign to all computers.

Use a computer that can connect to the Router, temporarily disable the Wireless encryption, and see if you can connect.

If it does Not work well without security, borrow a PCMCIA or USB card and try it. If it works OK you, might have “quirky” Wireless card.

If it does work well without security, uninstall the card and reinstall it with the newest drivers that you can find on the vendors support site.

 

Jack-MVP Windows Networking. WWW.EZLAN.NET

jaredhay say:  I work for a large ISP, and we have the same issue with Vista machines, and occasionally Win 7 machines doing this often. I have found that Windows does not always ask you, when connecting to a new network with WiFi, for the location. Home, Office or Public.

 When I find a customer who has troubles with Vista and can see the wifi SSID's but is not given a valid IP (169.254.x.x), the Location settings are always set to Public. Changing to home or Private, allows the wifi connection to pull an IP from the router as it should. Something in the FW settings used in a Public network is blocking the DHCP connection from working correctly.

Hope this helps!

JSH
