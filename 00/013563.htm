Arrgh! Where did &F and &P in headers go - Microsoft Community

helpingwithw8 say:

Arrgh! Where did &F and &P in headers go

I have a document that I want to have the header of the second and following pages to show the filename on the far left, and the page number on the far right.  I cannot figure out how to do this now.  I thought I used &F and &D in MS Word of old.

Help?

Thanks.

hansv mvp say:

Codes such as &F are used in the header and footer in Excel, not in Word.

In Word, you can use fields to insert elements such as the filename and the page number.

On the Insert tab of the ribbon, in the Header & Footer group, click Footer > Edit Footer.

This automatically activates the Design tab of the ribbon.

Press Ctrl+F9 to insert field braces { } (do not type the braces yourself, that won't work).

Type the word filename between the braces, then press F9 to hide the field code and to update the field.

Click after the field that now shows the filename.

Press the Tab key twice to move to the right margin.

Press Ctrl+F9 to insert field braces { }.

Type the word page between the braces, then press F9 to hide the field code and to update the field.

In the Options group of the Design tab of the ribbon, tick the check box Different First Page.

The footer text will disappear from the first page, and only be displayed on the second and following pages.
