Hi Kyle G,

Firstly, I‚Äôd like to share with you the difference between Authoritative and Internal relay.

Authoritative ‚Äì Selecting this option means that email is delivered to email addresses that are listed for recipients in Office 365 for this domain. Emails for unknown recipients are rejected.

Internal relay ‚Äì Selecting this option means that recipients for this domain can be in Office 365 or your own email servers. Email is delivered to known recipients in Office 365 or is relayed to your own email server if the recipients aren‚Äôt known to Office 365.

Since DDG chooses recipients according to the filters that we configure, if we select Authoritative and there is an unknown recipient in the DDG list, this issue will occur. In this case, we have to set the domain to Internal Relay to rectify the issue.

In your situation, there should be some differences between DDGs in different Office 365 organizations and I suggest you enable Internal Relay for the domain in Organization 2 to get the service working. greg maxey say:

The correct way to make it work is to bind the content controls to the a customXMLPart in the document.  The content control toolkit does that, but I think this does it better:  http://gregmaxey.mvps.org/word_tip_pages/cc_var_bm_doc_prop_tools_addin.html

 

Once a CC is bound to a data node in the CustomXMLPart, any copy of that CC is also bound.  So:

1.  Bind the control

2.  Copy it.

3.  Paste it where you want the text repeated.

 

See: http://gregmaxey.mvps.org/word_tip_pages/repeating_data.html for other alternatives.


I'm not certain you really need a protection mode.  Unless you want to use a password, you can achieve your goals with group or richtext controls:  http://gregmaxey.mvps.org/word_tip_pages/content_control_grouping_and_navigation.html
