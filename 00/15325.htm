kosh vorlon say:

Hi Gary,

 

Steve is correct.  BUT, there is a way out of the box (so to speak) though it is radical and not selective.  If you don't mind losing the entire history of updates in WU (not the list of installed updates in Programs and Features or the updates themselves, but the list in Windows Update) - and not just definitions but ALL of them (clearing the slate) and also losing it in some other places (like MSE may not initially report the date it was last updated until you next update and begin recreating the WU history list), it can be done as follows.

 

Please note that I do not recommend this as that information can be valuable and this procedure is a troubleshooting tool to resolve update-related problems and should ONLY be used for that - but it's your computer and the decision is yours.

 

First, reboot to clear out and finalize any updates that may be in process.

 

In C:\Windows\SoftwareDistribution, find and delete the contents (but not the folders themselves) of ONLY the following two folders: Downloads and DataStore.  You may have to do some permissions work to be allowed access and to do this.  Also, it may tell you it can't be done because it is in use.  If that occurs and you are not actually updating, then reboot again and that should clear whatever is using it and eventually allow you do so after the boot process is complete.

 

Again, this takes up little space (even if it looks long) and if space if a concern, post back and I'll give you some far better methods to improve space than resorting to something like this (which will be of little value anyway).  Though the information isn't critical, it can be valuable when troubleshooting problems and removing it unless forced to do so to resolve an update problem is not recommended.  Plus there's no way to selectively remove just the definition updates and leave the Windows and other updates - it's all or none.

 

I hope this helps but also hope you decide not to do it just to clear the list.

 

Good luck!

 

Kosh
