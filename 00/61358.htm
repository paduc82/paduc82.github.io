  • You’ll get a single password to sign into all Microsoft apps and services – one less thing to remember!

  • You’ll get a better account recoverability experience; in case you lose access to your Skype account (like if you forgot your password).

For these reasons, we strongly recommend that if you have a Skype account that you combine it with your Microsoft account. You can do this by adding your Microsoft account email address to your existing Skype account, or by adding another email address if you don’t already have one.

Hi, nutmix

You could consider creating a brand new Microsoft account, with its own new email address, to use for linking (you might see it referred to as "merging" elsewhere) your Skype account.   You would still be able to use your Skype account name to sign on, and also take advantage of two-factor authentication as well as other Microsoft account security enhancements. 

If you choose this path, create the new Microsoft account with its own new email address first, prior to the merging process.  The Skype account "technical ID" assigned automatically by the system would be replaced with your Skype account name when the merging process is complete.

Hint: Microsoft is bound by what is known as Children's Online Privacy Protection Rule (COPPA) requirements, so be extremely careful not to enter a date of birth that makes your account look like you are younger than 13 years old!

Server Error: 0x800CCC90
That only means that the server didn't understand the response from Windows Live Mail. It might mean that the username or password was not found in the server's mailbox database, but it might mean something else completely. Windows Live Mail interprets it - rightly or wrongly - as 'wrong password.'
Server Response: -ERR [SYS/TEMP] Server error - Please try again later.
On the other hand, this response indicates a temporary system error at the server. We really have no idea what it means in fact.
 
I understand that you have generated an app password and pasted it into the password box. Try with a new one: copy the new app password, then in Windows Live Mail right-click on the account name in the folder pane and select Properties. On the Servers tab, clear the password box and paste the new password in. Click Apply and wait until you can click OK. Then close down Windows Live Mail and wait a few minutes for the program to finish its housekeeping and store the new password. Then re-launch the program and try send/receive again for this account. Paste any new error message into your reply.

a. user say:

Hi,

 

I see that this is your first post in this forum, welcome to the Microsoft Community forum.

 

When you refresh your Windows 8 computer, all the apps and other third party applications that you have installed would be removed. Windows puts a list of the removed apps on your desktop after refreshing your PC. You will have to reinstall them again to access these apps/programs. Refer to these steps to reinstall the apps and the third party programs that were removed.

 

To reinstall the removed apps:

 

a)      In Windows store, right click on an empty space.

b)      When you get the app bar on the top, click on Your apps.

c)      Click on an app to highlight it and then press Control key +A to select all.

d)      Now click Install on the bottom app bar to install the apps.

 

To reinstall the removed third party applications:

 

You will need a CD/DVD from which the apps were originally installed to install them back on the computer. If you don’t have an installation media, then you can visit the software’s official website and download the applications and install it.
