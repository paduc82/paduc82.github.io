I would like to help you.

There is a setting within the Outlook email for clients that, if enabled, will cause your mailbox to receive duplicate messages. Removing duplicate messages from your email box does not stop your arrival. The only way to do this is to modify the configuration of your email account.

Step 1
Open the Outlook email for clients and click on "Tools" in the menu bar.

Step 2
Select the "Accounts" option.

Step 3
Select your email account in the "Email" tab.

Step 4
Click on the "Properties" button to access the mail properties of your account.

Step 5
Click on the "Advanced" tab in the "Properties" window.

Step 6
Uncheck the option "Leave a copy of messages on the server."

Step 7
Click on the "Apply" button to save the changes.

Step 8
Click "OK" to close the mail properties window. Click again on "Accept" to close the accounts window.

Step 9
Click on "Tools" in the Outlook menu bar. Select the "Send / Receive" option.

Step 10
Select "Receive everything." This will remove all duplicate messages from the email server and from your inbox.

Step 11
Remove all duplicate emails that have appeared in your inbox. After this final deletion, you will no longer have to worry about how to delete duplicate messages.

john korchok say:

There is no need to change the TOA to text. If you turn off automatic field updating, the TOA will only update when you right-click on it and choose Update field. Here are the steps:

  1. In Word, choose Tools>Options.
  2. Choose the Print tab.
  3. Uncheck Update fields.
  4. OK Out.

Now the TOA will remain stable.

Then create a series of bookmarks in the document:

  1. Select an authority.
  2. Choose Insert>Bookmark and name the bookmark. The name cannot contain spaces or non-alphanumeric characters.
  3. Click on Add.
  4. Repeat for all authorities.

Next create the hyperlinks:

  1. Select a line of the TOA.
  2. Choose Insert>Hyperlink.
  3. Choose Place in This Document. You'll a window showing Headings and Bookmarks, with lists under them.
  4. Select the bookmark for the current line.
  5. OK Out.

You can Ctrl-Click on the hyperlinks to test them.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
Some fields update even when automatic field updating is disabled. The TOA field is likely not one of them, but a better approach (which doesn't affect other fields that one might want to be updated) is to lock the TOA field using Ctrl+F11. This doesn't unlink it--it can, if necessary, be unlocked with Ctrl+Shift+F11--but it protects it from updating.
