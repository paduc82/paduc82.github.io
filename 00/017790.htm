Private Sub cmdAppendRecords_Click()

Method 1:

1.       Exit Internet explorer 8, if it is running.

3.       On the Display Properties, We need to Navigate to the Themes tab.

1.       Exit Internet explorer 8, if it is running.

4.       In the Display Tab, check the box Use High Contrast and Click on Apply.

5.       Now the Entire Computer screen will go Blank and White in Color, Now we need to Uncheck the Option Use High Contrast and Click on Apply on the Display Tab.

john w. vinson say:

I second the suggestion of checking out Crystal's tutorial.

 

You are making the VERY common mistake of "thinking spreadsheet". Spreadsheets are wide and flat, with many fields (CampFee, BusFee and so on); databases are tall and thin, with many RECORDS. Rather than storing any fees at all in the campers table, you would instead have THREE tables:

 

Campers

  CamperID (Primary Key)

  LastName

  FirstName

  (other biographical and contact data)

 

FeeSchedule

  FeeID (Autonumber primary key)

  FeeType (e.g. "Bus")

  Fee (Currency)

  EffectiveDate (date/time, when this fee went into effect)

  (any other fields needed to specify whether the fee is due)

 

CamperFees

  CamperFeeID (autonumber primary key)

  CamperID (link to Campers, who was charged this fee)
  FeeID or possibly a currency Fee field, if you want the ability to use the Fee Schedule as a default but to also be able to edit the fee actually charged

 

Your Form would be based on Campers, with a subform based on CamperFees; you could calculate the total fee due in the subform Footer, for example.
