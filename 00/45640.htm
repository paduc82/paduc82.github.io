icestorm iii say:

As I said before, the only way MW2/MW3 are "faster" when at 480p is if the MW2/MW3 engines are shifting to rendering internally at 640x480 instead of a fixed resolution.

 

Normally, 360 games render internally at a fixed resolution. The Xenos GPU scales that output to match whatever the display resolution is. The scaling is "free" in terms of resources.

 

For a game to perform better at a lower resolution, the game must be changing the rendering resolution.

 

Replacing the HDMI cable won't make any difference. You're hitting a limitation of the game.

cmdrkeene say:

When viewed on-screen, the font sizes are not very meaningful.  For example, you could be using a size 6 font (not that I think you should have to either), but then pinch-and-zoom (or ctrl+scroll on the mouse) and it would appear far larger, but then print at the original scale.

The opposite is also true.  You could use a larger font, 16-18, but be zoomed out so it looks smaller.  But the zoom scale based on your pinch-zoom (touch screen) or ctrl+mousewheel zoom (non touch-screen) is only used for the display, not the print.

I would suspect that it's this display zoom that's causing what you see.

Also -- you are absolutely correct that Office OneNote has far greater capabilities than the app. And it always will.  The app is meant for fast light use on a phone or tablet.  Office OneNote will always be the fully featured powerful program for serious users.

Marking off bad sectors on a hard drive takes time so be patient. Marking off does not repair a bad sector. It places pointers on the drive telling the system not to read or write to those sectors which have been damaged.

If the number of bad sectors continues to increase after you have run the procedure above several times then you should replace the drive. If an important system file is written to a bad sector you can corrupt registry hives and lose the whole contents of the drive.
