System tray, date and time missing from Tasksbar in Windows 10, SOLUTION

I had this problem after updating in windows 10 - my time and date were missing and nothing I tried helped, even after trying several ideas on this forum that helped some but not others (strangely).  

So I experimented myself and did the following which helped me- hopefully it will help others as it is a SUPER EASY fix. 

1) Right click the system tray at the bottom of your screen where your time and date are missing.
RESULT: The settings menu opens. 

2) Select Taskbar Settings from the menu (on my settings menu it was the very last option).

3) Navigate to the field titled: Taskbar location on Screen. (On my settings screen it was the first field beneath all the ON/OFF button options somewhere in the middle of the screen - I had to scroll down just a bit). 

4) Click the drop-down arrow to the right of the Taskbar Location on screen box and change the taskbar location from Bottom (or wherever you currently have it located- most people have it on the bottom) to Left.  (Note: You can change it to anything other than what you currently have -  If you currently have it on the Left, change it to something else). 

Result: The system tray appears on your screen in the location that you selected. (Note: When I did this, I could see my time and date again on the left side of the screen and was thrilled it was still there.)

5)  Change it back to Bottom.  

Result: The time and date should now appear on the bottom of your screen in the system tray.
