Hi Paul

Open File Explorer and navigate to that folder

In the address bar, just type cmd and hit Enter

That will open a Commend Prompt in that folder

Paste this into Command Prompt and hit Enter

dir /s/b > AAA.txt

Wait for that command to complete

dir /b > AAA.txt

There was no way that any manufacturer could predict Microsoft's future operating systems, let alone guarantee that their laptops will continue to work seamlessly with post Win 7 OSes.

In Windows 10? Yes, there is.

You can prevent Win 10 from updating any hardware driver using the following method:

  1. Open up Control Panel and select Devices and Printers.
  2. Right click the icon representing your laptop and choose Device Installation Settings.

This should ideally be performed as soon as you have successfully upgraded your laptop to Win 10 or at least, temporarily disconnect your machine from the Internet. bill.p. say: Hi Alvaro, I'm Graeme, an independent adviser here to help.

I'd recommend two troubleshooting steps which may resolve the problem you're having accessing Google sites:

Firstly, flush your DNS:
- Click Start, and type "cmd".
- Right-click Command Prompt and run it as administrator.
- Copy and paste the below one line at a time, pressing enter each time:
netsh winsock reset - and press Enter.
netsh int ip reset - and press Enter.
ipconfig /release - and press Enter.
ipconfig /renew - and press Enter.
ipconfig /flushdns - and press Enter

Secondly, try resetting your hosts file. Here are instructions:

https://support.microsoft.com/en-au/help/972034...

After doing these steps, you should be able to access the sites again. Let me know how you go! Thanks!

I'm Independent Advisor and Microsoft MVP, here to help you with your question.

Make sure you've enabled TLS 1.0, 1.1, 1.2 protocols:

1. Press Windows Key + R and type inetcpl.cpl in the Run dialog box, to open Internet Properties.

2. In Internet Properties window, switch to Advanced tab. Under Settings, scroll to Security heading. Beneath it, you need to enable these options:

Use TLS 1.0
Use TLS 1.1
Use TLS 1.2

3. Click Apply followed by OK to save your settings.
