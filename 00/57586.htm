john john __ say: Is this XP Home or XP Pro?

Don't listen to those who tell you that you can just willy-nilly delete folders in the Documents and Settings folder, if you do you will probably end up damaging your Windows installation.  Furthermore, if you want to delete unused user accounts you don't do it by deleting folders in  Documents and Settings, this will not remove the account (aka the Security Principal).

Typically a Windows XP installation will have at least six folders in the Documents and Settings:

Administrator
All Users
Default User
LocalService
NetworkService

On single user XP Home installation users often run with the built-in Administrator account (a very bad practice) so there will typically be five folders instead of six as
John

Hey there Marie!

Sorry to hear that you're dealing with this issue trying to access the rest of your console.

If power cycling did not help resolve the issue, then I would suggest trying a soft reset through the startup troubleshooter.  Try bringing it up by following these steps.
  1. Unplug the network cable if you're using a wired network connection.
  2. Power off your console, and then unplug the power cord to ensure that the console is completely powered off.
  3. Wait 30 seconds, and then plug the power cord back in.
  4. Press and hold the BIND button (located on the left side of the console) and the EJECT button (located on the front of the console), and then press the Xbox button on the console.
  5. Continue holding the BIND and EJECT buttons for 10-15 seconds.
  6. Listen for two power-up tones a couple of seconds apart. You can release the BIND and EJECT buttons after the second power-up tone.

After you get the Troubleshoot menu, select reset this xbox and keep my games & apps.
Just a heads up though, this will remove the accounts on the console, so you will have to redownload them with the email and password after.

Give those steps a shot and let us know how that helped.
