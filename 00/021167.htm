The necessary commands are:

for disable standby

powercfg -change -standby-timeout-ac 0

for enable standby

powercfg -change -standby-timeout-ac X

where X is the wait time before standby in minutes.

    Hello YousefSahib,

     

    Thank you for contributing to the Microsoft community!

     

    When a Windows Phone is turned off during an update, it can be that the software on the phone gets corrupted.

     

    Is your Windows Phone responding at all?

     

    Have you already tried removing the battery, waiting a couple of seconds, and re-inserting it, to check whether your phone will respond again?

     

    If your Zune software still recognizes the Windows Phone when connected to the PC, you may want to try to restore it to the previous update version. You can do so within the Zune software in Settings -> Phone -> Update.

     

    If that does not work, you could also try to reset your phone, if you can still turn it on, in Settings -> About -> Reset your phone. But please be aware that this will erase all your personal data off your phone (music, pictures, videos, etc.).

     

    If your Windows Phone does not respond at all, you may want to try a heard reset via the following button combination:

     

    1.       With the phone turned off, press and hold the Volume Up and Down buttons, and then briefly press the Power button.

     

    2.       Wait for the screen to show the instructions on how to do the factory reset, and then release the Volume buttons.

     

    3.       Follow the instructions on screen to factory reset your phone.

     

    If your Windows Phone does not respond at all to the above mentioned troubleshoot steps, you will most likely need to send in the device for repair.

     

    For further information for a replacement or repair service, you will either need to contact the manufacturer of your phone or the retail store you purchase the phone from.

     

    Cheers!
