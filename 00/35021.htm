7. Make sure the controller is not full of dust in the headset port (you can use compressed air to clear the dust). 

8.  Make sure controller is fully charged/ change battery type. 


9. Use a headset adapter or standard headphones (Main fix)

10. Soft or full console reset. (may not work all the time) 

Hope this helps. Let us know!

NOTE: I have an Alienware X51 R2 Desktop, so some things may not work the same.

------- I advice readers to not follow these unless you have a good grasp of Windows settings and are willing to risk trying this on your computer.

------- I am not a professional and am only sharing my findings, trying to help others with this same problem.

-------Systems are different and have different ways of getting to certain options. If your PC/ laptop requires F8 instead of F12 to get to certain options then substitute those keys.
