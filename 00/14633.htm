Hi Donna,

If you are charged monthly, it could mean that you are subscribed to Office 365 Home. The charge would depend on the plan that you have subscribed, i.e. monthly or annually. If you want to switch subscriptions, you may do so by following the steps below:

  1. Sign in to the Services & subscriptions page with the Microsoft account associated with your Office 365 subscription.
  2. Find the subscription you want to change, and choose Switch plan under Billing.

If you have enabled automatic renewal, you'll be billed for your subscription on your renewal date. However, if automatic renewal is turned off, your subscription will expire on the date shown, and you won't be billed in the future. Here's how to turn off automatic renewal:

  1. Go to the Services & subscriptions page.
  2. If prompted, sign in with the Microsoft account email and password associated with your Office 365 subscription.
  3. On the Services & subscriptions page, locate your Office 365 subscription.
  4. Do one of the following:
    • If you see a Turn off auto-renew link, choose it to turn auto-renew off.
    • If you can see a Cancel link, choose it to cancel the subscription. You might be given the choice to get a refund for unused time. If you choose to get a refund, the subscription will go into reduced functionality mode immediately. Otherwise, you'll have access to your subscription until it expires.
    • If you see a Turn on auto-renew link, it means automatic renewal is already turned off.
    • If you don't see an option for Turn off auto-renew or an option for Cancel, and you see Paid with and None, your subscription will end on the date specified in Expires on.
  5. Follow the prompts to turn off automatic renewal. You'll have access to your subscription until it expires.

If your subscription has expired or you didn't renew it, you won't be able to use Office and Office will eventually run in reduced functionality mode and will show unlicensed product notifications. lemp say:

What is the full path to SDXHelper.exe?

If it's in ...\program files\microsoft office\... it's probably "used when later Office versions are installed in parallel with an earlier version so that they can peacefully coexist."

As to the IP address warning, that's almost certainly innocuous, unrelelated to SDXHelper, and would be quickly and automatically resolved. 

Most home users have a router with multiple devices that connect to it from time to time.  Typically, all of the devices are configured to automatically obtain an IP address from the router.  Some devices, however -- particularly printers -- work better if they are assigned a fixed or "static" IP address. 

If you configured one or more devices with static IP addresses but were not careful to select an address outside the range that the router uses to assign addresses automatically, the conflict described in the error message may arise.

Usually, the router will re-assign the problem device -- e.g., your computer -- a new, non-conflicting address.  If the conflict does not disappear, the following should resolve the problem.

On your computer, open a Command Prompt window (click Start, type Command Prompt, and press Enter).

Type the following in the black Command Prompt window and press Enter after each line

     ipconfig /release

     ipconfig /renew

If you still get an IP address conflict, turn off your router, wait a couple of minutes, and turn it back on.  Wait another couple of minutes before trying to connect.

You need to make adjustments to your code to reflect the correct row and columns, I think I have done that with the following modifications:

Sub PrintJobSummarySheets()
'
' PrintJobSummarySheets Macro
'
' Keyboard Shortcut: Ctrl+r
'
Worksheets("Menu").PrintPreview
    Bot = Worksheets("Cab Components").Range("M65000").End(xlUp).Row
