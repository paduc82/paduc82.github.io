When I give command like "play music", the response I get is "I can't seem to find any music locally or in your collection." How can i fix this?

I am able to repeat the same issue and it appears to be a problem with Cortana's programming code for voice recognition commands to perform music operations. As you may already know, Cortana is associated with the Groove Music app, regardless of which music application you choose as your default music player.  This appears to be intentionally programmed by Microsoft perhaps to integrate the two programs.  In any event, there is an alternative voice command you can say to Cortana that will recognize your music as long as you have added the location path in Groove Music so that the app can access your digital audio files.  Once that's confirmed, you can say the command, "Hey Cortana...Play me any song" and Cortana will load Groove Music and begin to play your music.  If you want to narrow the command a bit, you can also say, "Hey Cortana...Play some music from the 1980s genre" and Cortana will play music in your collection from the 1980s as log as your ID3 tags include date information.  I recommend that you edit your music collection to update your ID3 tags to make sure all of the information is in the file to identify it for Cortana.  I use tagscan which is the best ID3 tag editor that's been freely available for many years now, and is always kept up to date by the developer.  Keep in mind also that you can make variations of my examples and Cortana may work with your variations.  Finally, don't forget that Cortana uses AI to enhance your user experience, so be sure to let the voice assistant learn from your voice and it will eventually start recognizing words like "genre" which it may not know the first few times you say it.  Cortana will learn any word as long as you repeat it enough.
