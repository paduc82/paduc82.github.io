Why not?   PowerPoint won't treat their presentations any differently that it'd treat your own.

A little squiblet of VBA can remove the hyperlinks for you:

Sub DeleteHyperlinks()

    Dim oSl As Slide
    Dim x As Long
    
    For Each oSl In ActivePresentation.Slides
        For x = oSl.Hyperlinks.Count To 1 Step -1
            oSl.Hyperlinks(x).Delete
        Next
    Next
    
End Sub

macropod say:

The complication arises from the fact that the selection of images is variable. There are six possible jpg's,and the selection of the appropriate image is driven by a series of nested IF(THEN statements. In that case you might code the field as:

{IF{INCLUDEPICTURE {IF TRUE "C:\Users\My Document Path\Pictures\{IF«Condition»= 1 "Image1.jpg"}{IF«Condition»= 2 "Image2.jpg"}{IF«Condition»= 3 "Image3.jpg"}{IF«Condition»= 4 "Image4.jpg"}{IF«Condition»= 5 "Image5.jpg"}{IF«Condition»= 6 "Image6.jpg"}"} \d} {INCLUDEPICTURE {IF TRUE "C:\Users\My Document Path\Pictures\{IF«Condition»= 1 "Image1.jpg"}{IF«Condition»= 2 "Image2.jpg"}{IF«Condition»= 3 "Image3.jpg"}{IF«Condition»= 4 "Image4.jpg"}{IF«Condition»= 5 "Image5.jpg"}{IF«Condition»= 6 "Image6.jpg"}"} \d}}

But, if the images and conditions are both numbered (and only the number varies) as per the above, you could also use:
IF{INCLUDEPICTURE {IF TRUE "C:\Users\My Document Path\Pictures\Image«Condition».jpg"} \d} {INCLUDEPICTURE {IF TRUE "C:\Users\My Document Path\Pictures\Image«Condition».jpg"} \d}}
