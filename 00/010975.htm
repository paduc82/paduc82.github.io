The usual cause for what I *think* you're seeing is font problems; if you're using a font that they don't have on their computer, their computer will substitute some other font.  Sometimes this works well enough that the result isn't even apparent; other times ... well ... I think maybe you KNOW what other times can look like.

Check that they have the fonts you've used on their system.  For example, give them the names of the fonts, have them start PowerPoint up from scratch, start a new presentation, then try to format some text in each of the fonts you've used.  If the font doesn't appear on their font menu, you have your answer.

Note: Make sure they DO NOT open the file you've sent and check the font list.  PowerPoint has become idiotic in its dotage and will include on the font list both the fonts on the system AND the fonts your presentation asks for, present or not.
