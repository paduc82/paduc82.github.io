Hello!

A notification banner is a box the will pop up in your bottom right taskbar when you get a notification.

Notification in action center is what shows a notification in the pop out menu when you click the "notification" button in the bottom right corner.

Notification is simply getting notifications at all for any reason.

Notification banner Those notifications that pop up on your screen when a notification first arrives are called banners.

thedyzaster say:

Just to let you know, most routers split the bandwidth up and can actually specify that one device gets faster speeds then another, and it can actually reserve a specific amount of speed towards a device.

Best way to fix slow throughput is:

Disconnect ALL devices Connected to the internet. Either turn Wifi off, or turn the entire devices off themselves. After that, clear the cache on your Xbox (Hold the power button on the front of the console for 10 seconds, it should shut off, keep it off) Now look at your router. Are any lights flashing? If so, then you get to play hide and seek with whatever device is still connected to the internet. Make sure NO lights are flashing, that means there is no current connectivity which leads us to believe no devices are set to connect automatically. Once you see all the lights stay solid for 1-2 minutes, then pull the cable out of the back of the router, and keep it unplugged for 5 minutes. We want the internal battery to die and the RAM to clear that way it can forget all devices it had leased out.

After that 5 minute wait, turn the router back on, wait 2 minutes, all lights should be solid and green like normal. If ones flashing, go play hide and seek for wireless devices again because you missed one, otherwise, you're good to turn the console on and connect. After that do a speed test on the console and see what you get, now your console is the primary device and other devices should receive slower speeds

KEEP IN MIND: You will NEVER receive the promised speeds on a wireless connection. Its just not going to happen (At least not in the next 10 years). There is a distance between the signal and even if its only an inch, its distance. For gaming we require wired connections as that allows for the fastest possible speeds as well as its more direct. Most ISP's will not give you your full speed, and with Cable providers speeds go down depending on how many people in your neighborhood are using the internet. You share with your neighbors, with DSL, its more like "You get what you pay for" Though there still might be a small difference.
