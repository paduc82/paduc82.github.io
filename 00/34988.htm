a. user say:

Most people have what is called a Dynamic IP. This means it changes every time you connect to the internet or reset your router. This will give you a new IP address.

In some cases you'll need to contact tour ISP in order to get it changed. If your IP address remains the same no matter what then you have what is known as a Static IP. These aren't usually issued to home customers but to people and companies who run their own websites.

DDoS attacks are therefore far more of an issue for the latter.

Edit: I know that is a simplification but it holds true for most people.
