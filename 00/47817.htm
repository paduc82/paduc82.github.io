You can reach the Services control panel a few ways, but here's one quick option:

1) Press Windows+R to show the Run box

Hello,

I know its a long time, but I had the same problem with Windows 10 Creator Update, and game dvr : I've only Intel chipset (HD 620), recorded in 1080x60p in high quality, on the old game Re-Volt running with Vsync ON 60fps, fan-ported and running actually on OpenGL. I experimented different settings, and found a great alternative to recover some fps, before you try to lower your settings for the recorder.

I just disabled the sync in game, but if I record with sync off, the result is awful like 4 fps or 40fps with Game Mode off (probably because it prioritize more the game than the recording).

So, I capped my game in 60fps sync off with Rivatuner statistics server. And bingo, I recovered some perfs, enough to reach the stable 60fps in recording.

So, you can try this: 

- Vsync off

- Cap 60fps in your graphics settings (if you can), or with a tierce software like Rivatuner Statistic Server.

- If you don't reach enough, consider to lower the capping to 50 maybe, for a stable 50fps recording, or disable settings in your game, like antialising (which is very demanding in ressources).
