Check in your network settings your NAT status. 

If it's not open, ask your friends in the party what their NAT status is. 

Opening a NAT requires you to make adjustments on your home router. Either by enabling UPnP, or if that doesn't work, manual port forwarding. 

Also, if your Internet Provider uses CG-NAT and DS-Lite (or similar tunneling technologies), you will have to contact your Internet Provider and demand a public IPv4 address. 

Some Providers will give you a public IPv4 for free, others want you to pay a monthly fee for that, and others simply can't or won't do it. In that case, your only option is to change your Internet Provider to a Provider that can give you a public IPv4 address. 

For further advice on how to open your NAT, we'd need to know exactly how your network is set up. 

What router you use, and if applicable, which broadband modem you use, if router and internet modem are two separate devices.
