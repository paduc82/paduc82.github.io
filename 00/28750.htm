bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
With the understanding that Style management in Mac Word leave much to be desired :-) knowing a bit more about how it does work may help...

The built-in Styles are categorically grouped based on a Priority Level which is automatically assigned. There is no provision for assigning a Priority Level. Any user-define style is assigned the same specific Priority Level.

Within Priority Level, styles are listed in the Styles Pane of the Toolbox in ascending order based on their name. You have some degree of control based on how you name your styles. Keep in mind that you can contrive your own set of prefixes (including numbers) in order to 'force' a style position within the User-Defined group. [I.e., H1Red Head would be listed above H1Blue Head followed by H2Red Head.]

User-defined styles are listed first in the Styles Pane of the Toolbox. If you get in the habit of using it rather than the older Styles dropdown of the Formatting Toolbar you should be able to select styles more readily... that tool lists all styles in ascending order by name regardless of Priority Level.

You might also benefit from the use of Custom Quick Style Sets as described in Word Help:  Apply, change, create, or delete a Quick Style set

You can create as many Quick Style sets as you wish, include the same style in more than one set & switch from one set to another as often as necessary.

=IF(M2<=DATE(12/01/2017), "register","")          Incorrect

=IF(M2<=DATEVALUE("12/01/2017"), "register","")      Correct

suzanne s. barnhill say:

In the "Book fold" settings, do you have "Sheets per booklet" set to All? When you print, are you using manual duplex? How many pages does the booklet have? Is the number a multiple of 4?

The inability to print specific pages of a booklet seems to have been introduced in Word 2010. In Word 2007 and earlier (which have the classic Print dialog rather than the Print tab in the Backstage), that control is still available (though results are always unpredictable). I think you'll have to give up on printing the remaining pages and concentrate on trying to print the entire booklet at once.

An alternative that I find works very well is to change the setup from "Book fold" to "2 pages per sheet." This does not change the layout at all; you won't be able to tell the difference since Word displays even "Book fold" documents in page number order. Save that as a PDF and use the booklet printing option in Adobe Reader to print the pages as a booklet.

OneNote 2013 and OneNote for Windows 10 are two different apps. One app may have a feature that doesn't exist on the other, such as your concern.
