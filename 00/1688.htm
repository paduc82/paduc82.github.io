bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Hey Bob -- I don't have any answer, but I can see where the problem is. The formula only works for dates less than a year old. Adding 365 to anything older returns a negative result when the current date is subtracted from it. E.g., June 1, 1985+365-4/10/2011 is going to return a meaningless negative value regardless of formatting :-)

My impression is that Al wants to know how many days it will be before the next occurrence of that original Month/Day regardless of year. IOW, if the original M/D is June 1, 1985 the formula needs to return the number of days it will be until "June 1" arrives again regardless of which Year that might be -- How many days until the next occurrence of the individual's birthday.

It seems to me that Al needs something a bit different [such as the absolute value of subtracting the current M/D from from the original M/D, maybe?]

bobgreenblatt say: The answer is correct! There have been 26,119 days between today and your actual day of birth in 1942. In your original post, you wanted to know the number of days between now and your next birthday. That formula will provide that answer. If you want to know something else, then state precisely what you are looking for.

bobgreenblatt say:

The original post asked to calculate the number of days until his next birthday, and stated his last birthday was in A1. To do that more precisely, and not caring when his last birthday really was and just calculate the number of days until the next occurrence of that particular month and day, then....

Still assume the birthday is in A1. The formula is:

=IF(TODAY()<DATE(YEAR(TODAY()),MONTH(A1),DAY(A1)),DATE(YEAR(TODAY()),MONTH(A1),DAY(A1))-TODAY(),DATE(YEAR(TODAY())+1,MONTH(A1),DAY(A1))-TODAY())
