steve rindsberg say:

Gloria, Nick:

First off, a possible fix:

Select both the photo and the shape with transparency, copy/paste them to a new slide as PNG or JPG, or at least as some kind of raster format.

OR

Export the slide to PNG/JPG/etc, then insert the exported image onto a new slide.

Either method flattens the transparency and preserves the effect in a single image, which will print the way you expect it to rather than relying on the creaky PDF-making apparatus to have enough steam to carry the job out properly.

I suspect this problem is related to the one reported in a different discussion:

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/msoffice/forum/all/powerpoint-export-to-pdf-transparent-items/0b0ab92b-3263-435e-afcf-3d2d89d5c90a

I'm betting that it has to do with transparency in gradients, which appears to be what you're using here, Nick (please correct me if that's not the case).

Mac PPT, as far as I know, relies on the print to the (free) PDF feature built into MacOS, which isn't as flexible or sophisticated as Adobe's (paid for) product and doesn't give the Mac Office developers the degree of control that the Windows folks have, since they wrote their own PDF making feature a few versions ago.

Fingers crossed that as the Mac and Windows versions converge regarding features and code, this situation will improve.
