Are iMacs that inefficient?  (Real question, not sarcasm). For the last few years Apple has been using the same intel hardware that PC's use.  So a very ball-park estimate of performance based on the hardware is not (totally) unreasonable.

I did a quick google. All of the iMacs that come with 8 GB of RAM are i5's and i7's.  Those are not trivial CPU's and with 8 GB of RAM should be much more than capable to run any number of applications without perceivable lag even with the built in graphic chip.

Strange, that with 8GB (that really is a significant, and reasonable, amount of RAM for the 'average' consumer) your system is resorting to a swap file. 

Take a look in the resource manager. Is the system actually using all (or most) of the 8GB of RAM before resorting to the swap file? If not, it could be a sign of bad/obsolescent program code in your applications.  In that case you have an opportunity.  You could define a "RAM" disk in the unused RAM and set your system to point to the RAM disk for the swap file. That would speed things up.

Another thing you could try is ask your boss to upgrade to a SSD type drive if you don't already have one. That would speed up access to the swap file. SSD's are orders of magnitude faster than spinning disk HDs (close to RAM speed) and their cost has now come down to entirely reasonable price points.  You can get a reasonable sized one in the $50-200 price range.

Hi,

 

You can adjust the font size of the letters by adjusting the message font settings of your account.

 

Please follow these steps to locate and adjust the message font settings:

 

1. Sign in your account.

2. Click the gear icon in the upper right corner of your homepage.

3. Click More mail settings.

4. Under Writing email click Message font and signature.

5. Under Message font click the icon between Aa and A adjust the font size.

6. Click Save.

 

After saving, the letters should now be set to your preferred font size when composing an email.
