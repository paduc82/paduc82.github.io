After the initial sync of a connected account, attempts to sync will only occur once per hour.

If a connection is not made, another attempt to connect will wait until the next hourly attempt.

After the initial sync, only new emails are downloaded.

There is no syncing of read status, email deletion, or movement of emails to other folders.

My business email going into my personal Junk Folder - Microsoft Community

brendanbehan say:

My business email going into my personal Junk Folder (Hotmail/Outlook)

Hi There,

My business emails are going into Microsofts junk folder. Every other email client such as gmail are going into the inbox folder. Is ther any way of getting on a whitelist for microsoft to change this?

mydooms say:

okay, let's see if I can explain this better: the fact that your emails go to Microsoft's junk folder is due to a spam detection. Email providers detect spam in basically three ways:

1) check spam databases

2) parse the content

3) rely on other users marking that sender as spam

If you're sending emails from the server, and those emails bounce in at least one provider (hotmail, for instance) then it is time to check if the server has been blacklisted.

The fact that gmail doesn't mark the email as spam isn't a good index of anything: one of my shared servers got listed in a spam directory once. I realized this because I replied from one of the server's email addresses to a friend who had previously written to me, but that precise email bounced.

If I had listened to gmail, I wouldn't have noticed this: gmail kept displaying the emails from that same server, without any problem, in the inbox folder (not in the spam one) and without any notice (nothing like "this message could be spam").

Other factors

a) You use HTML in your emails. If you use HTML for pretty emails, keep in mind that HTML is more subject to end up in the spam filter.

b) Your emails aren't opened by the majority of recipients

c) You have "spam" keywords in the text

d) You have too many links in the email, or link to directory with strange names 

e) You are sending out promotional content to people who didn't subscribe (that would be spam, in fact)

f) other hypotheses here

I wouldn't expect Microsoft to adjust the spam filter... You can adjust your emails and policies (or check if the server is fine) to cope with the filter. If you make a search for "why do my emails end up in the spam filter", you should find some information on how to adjust things and get out of the spam filter. Personally I don't send emails from the server, unless it's to confirm a sign up, or with an alert. I've given up newsletters, because nobody reads them (and you end up in the other folder). Hope this helps.
