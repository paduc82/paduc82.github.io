suzanne s. barnhill say:

If you're using Level 7 for the A, B, C headings, then you use Level 8 for A.1, A.2, A.3. Create the numbering as follows:

  1. Clear all numbering from the "Enter formatting for number" box.
  2. Under "Include level number from," select Level 7. This will put an A in the "Enter formatting for number box.
  3. Type a period after the A.
  4. In the "Number style for this level" box, select 1, 2, 3. This will enter a 1 after the A.
  5. Make sure that Level 7 is linked to Heading 7 and Level 8 to Heading 8.
  6. Make any desired changes in alignment, indent, etc., then click OK.

neal b say:

For upgrades to the phone's O/S you will have to look to your carrier.  If updates are going to be made availible for your model phone, the carrier is who these will come from, due to licensing and such.

 

With Activesync, it will matter how you have this setup on your work computer - you can have it set to sync your outlook info with the PC, or exchange server but not both (in PC mode it cannot sync over the air using your data connection, it will only sync with the outlook client when plugged into your PC) but not both.  You sound to have this setup for sync with Exchange, in which case, the phone will by default try to use your PC's internet (and in this case work network) connection to get out to the internet and then to your Exchange server.  Since it cannot tell that the PC it's on might be on the internal network with your Exchange, it will always attempt to use the internet connection.  This means you will have to setup on your phone a proxy server most likely, as nearly all businesses us some form of proxy for internet access. 

 

The steps on a Smartphone (I am not sure if they are different under Pocket PC or not) are:

 

Look under Settings -> Connections -> Proxy and you can add a new proxy to enter the name of your companies proxy server and then your credentials if you are required for proxy use, and it should get rid of the error message.

 

To sync subfolders on your mailbox, you need to be looking at the list view of your mailbox your phone (where you can see the inbox, outbox, etc), and the right softkey should have Menu above it.  Choose menu, and "Manage Folders" this should show you all the subfolders you have on your exchange box, and allow you to select which you want to add for sync.

 

 Neal
