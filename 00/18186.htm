kouzelna say:

OK, last one.

As I said above, I didn't want to reset my Chrome as I did with IE to solve the problem.  So here's what I did instead, and it solved the problem in Chrome as well.

I went carefully through all of the cookies in Chrome.  I used CCleaner to locate them all first, and then I used Chrome itself. 

First I went through all the cookies in CCleaner for Chrome and deleted everything related to Microsoft, Outlook, Live, Bing and Skype.  CCleaner is easier to use than Chrome's new "mind-bogglingly-frustrating" cookies list, plus it allowed me to avoid deleting IE cookies by mistake because it identifies each cookie by browser.  Then I went through it again, removing all "saved" cookies (right panel).


Then I went through Chrome itself and did the same thing, for anything I missed, or for any cookies that CCleaner identified as belonging to both browsers. (Again, so I didn't have to delete IE's cookies).

I then closed Chrome, and restarted.  Opening Chrome I logged into Outlook.com using my 2-Step Verification, and making sure to check "keep me logged in" on both screens.  (The initial login screen, and the 2-Step screen).  I checked mail, answered some emails, and then closed Chrome.  Then when I restarted, everything was great: it logs straight into my Outlook.com InBox automatically.

I guess for some reason the cookies related to Outlook/Live had to be deleted and reset.  But at least this method, while tedious, prevented me from having to reset Chrome entirely.

Take care, and spread the info around so others can be helped.
