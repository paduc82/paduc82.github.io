I suspect the ones you cannot open are corrupted files.  How they got corrupted is the question.  Because you are getting duplicates, I am going to take an educated guess that they are being corrupted at your internet mail provider or enroute to your computer.

Log into your internet email provider's webmail.  Then delete either all or most of the content of your inbox on the webmail server.

  1. In Windows Live Mail, right-click on the name of your mail provider (or your account) in the left column, choose Properties
  2. Click the Advanced tab
  3. At the bottom of that window choose when and how Windows Live Mail will tell your web mail server to delete email from ITS inbox.

Next, run a chkdsk on your computer.  chkdsk is a tool built-in to Windows 7.  It searches for a variety of integrity violations across all files on your hard drive and in almost all cases corrects it.  It will take from 1 to 3 hours to run and your computer will not be usable while it is running.

  1. Start globe (bottom left)
  2. All programs
  3. Accessories
  4. Right-click on Command prompt, choose Run as Administrator
  5. In the black window that pops up type:
  6. chkdsk c: /r
  7. Then y for yes
  8. Then exit

Restart your computer

When chkdsk is complete it will simply return your normal starting screen.  There is no easy way to get a report.

vijay a. verma say:
There can be several causes but to start with, you can try following. Please try these options one by one and check after each option.

1. Troubleshoot Power
Type troubleshoot in Windows Start Search box > Click Troubleshoot > Scroll down > Click Power > Run the troubleshooter > Follow on-screen direction.

2. Restore Default for Power Settings
Right click on battery icon in system tray > Power options > Click Change Plan Settings > Click Restore default settings for this plan

3. Disable Fast Startup
Right click on battery icon in system tray > Power options > Click Choose what the power buttons do > Click Change settings that are currently unavailable > Scroll down to Shutdown settings and uncheck Turn on fast startup > Click Save changes

4. Uninstall / Install Battery Driver
Shut down your computer > Detach power chord > Remove battery > Attach power chord > Reboot your computer
Windows Key+X > Device Manager > Expand Batteries > Right-click Microsoft ACPI-Compliant System > Uninstall > Shutdown the computer > Remove the power chord > Attach your battery > Attach your power chord > Reboot the computer to automatically install the driver
