joe_967 say:

Ok, this issue was solved. Here's what happened.

  • I went to the support page and tried the suggestions made by the virtual agent. Those didn't work. 
  • Then I entered a chat with a live agent. The first agent said s/he was from "billing and accounts." That person was of no help and transferred me to a real tech support agent.
  • That person remoted into my machine and tried all the things I listed above, then ran a script called scrub.vbs. That didn't solve the problem, either.
  • So the agent looked around the registry, but didn't do anything.
  • Then, the agent downloaded RevoUninstaller and ran it. Revo found registry keys and lots of Office related files, which the agent deleted. ...That solved the problem!  Then Office could be installed. 

The agent believes previous installations had problems (in the reg keys, extraneous files, etc) that prevented this update from installing.

Thanks MS tech support!
