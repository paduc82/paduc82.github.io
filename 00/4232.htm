Sub Unzip1()
    Dim FSO As Object
    Dim oApp As Object
    Dim Fname As Variant
    Dim FileNameFolder As Variant
    Dim DefPath As String
    Dim strDate As String

    Fname = Application.GetOpenFilename(filefilter:="Zip Files (*.zip), *.zip", _
                                        MultiSelect:=False)
    If Fname = False Then
        'Do nothing
    Else
        'Root folder for the new folder.
        DefPath = "C:\MyDownloads\"
        'DefPath = Application.DefaultFilePath
        'If Right(DefPath, 1) <> "\" Then
            'DefPath = DefPath & "\"
        'End If

        'Create the folder name
        strDate = Format(Now, " dd-mm-yy h-mm-ss")
        FileNameFolder = DefPath & "MyUnzipFolder " & strDate & "\"

        'Make the normal folder in DefPath
        MkDir FileNameFolder

        'Extract the files into the newly created folder
        Set oApp = CreateObject("Shell.Application")

        oApp.Namespace(FileNameFolder).CopyHere oApp.Namespace(Fname).items

        'If you want to extract only one file you can use this:
        'oApp.Namespace(FileNameFolder).CopyHere _
         'oApp.Namespace(Fname).items.Item("test.txt")

        MsgBox "You find the files here: " & FileNameFolder

        On Error Resume Next
        Set FSO = CreateObject("scripting.filesystemobject")
        FSO.deletefolder Environ("Temp") & "\Temporary Directory*", True
    End If
End Sub

techfreak_ say:

1. Adding a subscription simply adds another additional subscription to your account that follows its own cycle. If you want to stop your other subscription, you can cancel your existing subscription to stop its future renewal. The subscription will still complete its cycle, so you will not be credited anything. 

2. All that information is available on the Skype Rates page.  Simply select a country and look under the Skype credit option under "See rates".

3. Credit and a subscription are two different things. If you dial a call covered by your subscription, no credit will be required or deducted. If you dial a number not covered by any of your active subscriptions, credit will be deducted or prompted for, if no current balance exists.

4. I am unaware of any pausing of the a subscription being available. Due to this, you would have to plan out your purchases/subscriptions in advance to align it with your future schedule. If you do not want to commit to a year, you can purchase a lesser interval subscription commitment that will lock you into that time frame, rather than a year.

Be aware that all subscriptions have a coverage area and no special, shared-cost, premium, non-regional numbers, or SMS texts are covered by any subscription.  Those will still prompt for or deduct credit, even in your coverage area. The Skype Rates page provides you with the coverage area of the North American and Unlimited World subscriptions.

julie fka profjulie say:

Is xBox Music and Groove somehow intertwined?

YES, Groove is essentially the same thing as xBox Music (xBox Music rebranded for Windows 10 as Groove).

If I have xBox Music, do I need to have Groove?

No, they are the same thing....at least for the time being....the Groove music player app is only available on Windows 10 devices. Windows 8 use the xBox Music app.

Can I use X-Box music to download music onto my Zune 30 ?

No, once the Zune Subscription is completely discontinued, you will not be able to play any subscribed music on any Zune Player, including the Zune HD, Zune 120, Zune 30, Zune 8, etc.  Subscribed music is encrypted with DRM and the DRM rights must be renewed every month.  The Zune Music subscription service uses a form of DRM that is unique to it and the Zune Player is designed to be compatible ONLY with the Zune Music subscription service DRM.  xBox Music/Groove Music use a different form of DRM encryption services which is not compatible with the Zune Player. 

Furthermore neither the xBox Music or Groove Music app has any compatibility with Zune players and doesn't provide any way to transfer music from a computer to a Zune Player.  .

The only music you will be able to play on a Zune Player is music from your personal collection that you have ripped from CDs or purchased and downloaded from most any music website (this type of music is usually in an mp3 format).  Music websites include the old Zune Music Marketplace, Google Music, etc.

I've been mainly purchasing music through Google Music Play, and using the Zune software to add the songs onto my Zune.  Can I continue to use the Zune software to load songs to my Zune ?

Yes, you can still use the Zune desktop software to sync your personal music collection to a Zune player

Can I move songs purchased through Zune or X-Box into Google Music ?

I don't use Google Music Play, so I don't know what your options are.  But if you can upload music you have ripped from a CD to your Google Music Play account, then you should be able to also upload any songs you purchased through Zune or xBox, since this music will be in the MP3 format and DRM free.

bill mosca say:

Amy

I'll bet anything that the control Company_Name has a controlsource that uses a lookup field. Lookup fields show you one thing when they are really tied to a field in another table. You are probably getting the Company ID. Open the table and look at the primary key field. See if 21 is the number for the company_name you are expecting to get. If it is change the code.
strCriteria = "[YourPrimaryKeyGoesHere] = " & ctrl

You won't need the double-quotes around ctrl if the PK is a number data type.
