kyddynomyte say:

The controller has a 2.5mm female stereo jack, you need an adapter(s) that will switch that into 2 mono plugs. One of them will be the mono mic input and the other will be the mono chat audio output. I don't use the chat audio output on the controller because I figure it's got to save the controller battery not to use it, less cables, and you can get better voice audio output sent to the speakers in the xbox preferences.

Most guides online show you how to buy some Radio Shack parts and cut them up and solder them back together. If you are like me and have boxes of misc. cables and adapters you can hack something together with no soldering. I was lucky and had these parts already,  a male 2.5mm stereo to 2 female RCA mono adapter plugs into a 2 male rca to male 3.5mm stereo adapter that plugs into a male 3.5mm stereo to 2 female 3.5mm stereo splitter that I just used the 2 female plugs as a coupler that the PC mic plugs into. Of course there are many different types of different adapters you can link together to accomplish the same thiing. Good luck.

kyddynomyte say:

Sorry just read your post again, I was thinking you were using a stereo headset. Sounds like you are using the single headphone standard headset. To do it like you describe, you need the 2.5mm male stereo to 2x 2.5mm female stereo splitter, plug the headset into one and then split the other female stereo into the mono channels like I described above and run the output one to the computer and leave the input one unused. If you don't split it out to the two mono plugs I think the input and output will be shorted out and the mic will no longer work.

It might be easier and sound better if you instead have audio out on you tv  (or tap into whatever type of soundsystem you are using) that can be hooked up to your computer and change the xbox preferences to play the voice chat through both the headset and speakers.
