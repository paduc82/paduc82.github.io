vijay a. verma say:

In Mike's formula, you need to replace 1 with 3 and < with >.  3 will calculate 75 percentile and > will consider only those values which are above 75 percentile. Above 75 percentile means top 25%.

Hence, the formula will become

=SUMPRODUCT((B3:B102>=QUARTILE(IF(E3:E102="ABC",B3:B102),3))*(E3:E102="ABC")*(C3:C102>B3:B102))

As Mike mentioned, it needs to be entered with Ctrl+Shift+Enter to make it array formula.

Problem burning CDs with Windows Mdia Player - Microsoft Community

jim m. say:

Problem burning CDs with Windows Mdia Player

Hello RicW-nw and others reading this,

I read through several of the posts in one of the Microsoft (MS) community forums, over which MS moderator RicW admitted having a diminishing role in moderating, the questions posed about the problem of burning discs with Windows Media Player (WMP), after I recently began having this issue of burn failure myself. I was at first having difficulty burning a CD in such a manner as to play the tracks in the order I want them to be played, then was unable to burn at all and kept receiving error messages.

I thought I had found a method to do what I wished, by joining all the .mp3 audio files into one super audio file with a media conversion program (Freemake, if that matters) and then plotting that file into WMP's burning process. But that will not really allow for individual playing of each track, just a continuous playing of all the tracks combined; although, granted, the songs are at least in the order one wishes.

So the CDs I tried to burn, with WMP and a few other programs (Nero was one of them), all produced two basic types of disc--one called an "audio CD" that played the tracks in alphabetical order (I think) and one which may or may not play the tracks in the order I wish by virtue of writing as a "data disc." I need to try that latter method's end result out on a friend's automobile CD player, as my only boombox CD player doesn't work at the moment except for the AM/FM radio functionality. The discs I tried to burn will sometimes play on my laptop's disc drive and sometimes not. I really began to be confused about the whole process after awhile and so just gave up. But I am left with four piles of discs, one pile in which the discs seem to play the tracks in alpha order, one pile which may play in desired order, one which won't play at all, and one pile with another variation I cannot recall. The last few attempts produced error messages, implying either something is failing in the WMP program or mechanically with the disc drive itself. I would like to know if there is at least some way to tell which is the problem--software or mechanical failure.

To veer off the subject at hand slightly, since RicW's replies mentioned the eventual obsolescence of media players, I have also read a few posts and on other online sites which state that media players (including Windows Media Player) and the hard copies played through such will soon be going the way of the floppy disc and audio cassette. But still, for those wishing to listen to their collections of CDs and DVDs, I would assume those devices for playing them will continue to be manufactured for the foreseeable future, as media companies continue to release products in CD and DVD (Blu-Ray included) formats as well.

The problem is these media companies never figured out (or simply don't care to make the investment) a way to market pre-loaded flash drives (or flashsticks or "thumb drives") with files of films and music albums, to replace CDs and DVDs. If they had bothered, presumably these businesses would have also replaced or supplemented (with an eye to eventual obsolescence) the CD and DVD players with a FlashBox or some other such commercial contrivance. And most persons of a certain age (and born after that) prefer, it would seem, to listen to and view their mass-produced media through streaming technology and apps and services such as  Amazon, Apple Music, iTunes, Napster, Pandora, Spotify, etc. anyway, thus obviating the existence of media hardware devices and the hard copy media played through such.

While one may certainly listen to and view audio and video files through the USB ports of desktops, laptops and smart television sets, as well audio files through iPods and .mp3 players, there is still a need (ever decreasing, granted) for the hardware to play CDs and DVDs. So CD/DVD players and even turntables and speakers will still be with us all for some time to come, I think.

Meanwhile, for that matter, vinyl recordings and so-called "record players" have enjoyed a resurgence in recent years, egged on (I think) by disc jockeys who think that aural method superior, as well by those with a retro-chic curiosity factor.

But to get back to the subject at hand, my last few attempts at burning a CD with WMP produced error messages which said the disc failed to burn. I tried two methods of burning--as an audio CD and as a data disc, the latter which I understand from a friend will play in most modern automobile CD players (to the extent the car will even have a CD player at all, as many automobile manufacturers now assume more people will listen to audio/music using Bluetooth or iPod/.mp3 players). But only the data disc method would seem (in a preliminary step) to allow me to burn the tracks in the order I desire them. And as I said, I still have to check to see if the data disc method worked, by listening to the final product on a friend's car CD player.

My main problem now is the last few CD burn attempts produced error messages and quick ejects of the discs. I tried all of the recommended solutions I found in this MS forum, including wiping off the dust gently (with a soft brush) from around and on the optical lens inside the disc drive of my laptop, but all to no avail. So, HELP, please.
