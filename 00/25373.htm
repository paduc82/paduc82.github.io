If your question is

Again to stress, the issue applies to <any> field that contains "multi-line" information and is not something restricted to the Address field.

mem-tek say:

Some AV programs, after you manually disable them, automatically re-enable themselves when you reboot. Even if the AV program is disabled, its low level I/O driver or drivers most likely still load during a reboot. So my first suggestion is to fully uninstall the AV program. Don't assume that the AV software is fully uninstalled simply because you uninstalled it. You may have to download a "full" uninstall utility from the AV manufacturer in order to fully uninstall every last remnant of the AV program. The same goes for third party computer backup programs. Five years ago I used to use Acronis backup software. I discovered that after uninstalling Acronis, the uninstaller still left the low level I/O drivers installed on my computer. I had to download and run a separate utility to remove those low level drivers.

You also might want to uninstall any USB3 drivers. I make this last suggestion since some USB3 drivers are a bit twitchy and can interfere with USB2 devices.

With regards to other drivers which you might want to uninstall before upgrading, think I/O. As mentioned, third party backup programs install low level I/O disk access drivers which of course could interfere with the upgrade process. Unneeded yet attached USB drives also have I/O access. So unplug any unneeded USB drives and peripherals such as printers and the like.

If you still have problems after all of the above, consider that your video driver might be causing problems and uninstall it.

Hopefully these are helpful suggestions. I am sure that others will chime in with specific third party software and drivers which need to be uninstalled before trying to install the Win10 Preview.
