I want set it so whenever I press ENTER to get Times New Roman at five points.

I would deeply appreciate your help in this matter.

hansv mvp say:

I cannot imagine why you would want that, but you can do the following:

 

Press Alt+F11 to activate the Visual Basic Editor.

Click on Normal in the navigation pane on the left hand side.

Select Insert > Module.

Copy the following code into the new module:

 

 

Sub InsertPar()
    Selection.InsertParagraph
    With Selection.Font
        .Name = "Times New Roman"
        .Size = 5
    End With
    Selection.Collapse Direction:=wdCollapseEnd
End Sub
 
Sub ChangeEnter()
    CustomizationContext = NormalTemplate
    KeyBindings.Add wdKeyCategoryMacro, "InsertPar", wdKeyReturn
End Sub
 
Sub RestoreEnter()
    CustomizationContext = NormalTemplate
    FindKey(wdKeyReturn).Clear
End Sub

 

 

To set Enter to insert a Times New Roman 5pt paragraph mark, run the macro ChangeEnter. You have to do this only once.

To restore the normal behavior of the Enter key, run the macro RestoreEnter. You have to do this only once.

stephen boots say:

There's a slight wrinkle...

In your scenario, on Day 1, it updates shortly after 7 am - probably due to the catch-up check that happens about 10 minutes after the PC boots.
On day 2, since you booted the PC at 7 am again, and the last check for updates was within 24 hours *and* assuming that the regularly scheduled check for updates was missed, it will not perform the catch-up check.
On day 3, if you did not manually check for updates and the scheduled check was missed again, it will check for updates about 10 minutes after booting.

The key here is that any check for updates - shceduled, manual or catch-up - within the past 24 hours will cause the catch-up check to not happen in the 10 minutes after boot timeframe.
