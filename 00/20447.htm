tom van stiphout say:

ULS was removed when the ACCDB file format was introduced, with A2007. I believe the main reason was that it was hard to apply correctly, and most users only needed a small subset of its features.

That subset was the use of Groups or Roles, whatever you want to call them, and assigning rights to parts of the application based on that. There are plenty of alternatives to using ULS for this purpose. Some of them are discussed in my blog: http://www.accesssecurityblog.com/

As a general rule you should expect software to evolve, and backward compatibility with versions that have been out of mainstream support for several years (https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/dd873564(v=office.12).aspx) is not a major design goal.

    v_agarwal say:

    If your range M2:M210 contains only numbers or Blanks coming from formula then you may use this formula,

    =COUNTIFS(M2:M10,"<>*",P2:P10,"Yes")

    In case your range includes only Text and Blanks, then you may use

    =COUNTIFS(M2:M10,"?*",P2:P10,"Yes")

    In case it includes both Numbers/Text and blanks then use sum of both formula like this.

    =COUNTIFS(M2:M10,"<>*",P2:P10,"Yes")+COUNTIFS(M2:M10,"?*",P2:P10,"Yes")

    Hope it helps.

    amit tandon say:

    Using below will not count "blanks" or "blanks as a result of formula" in column M. It will count if column P contains "Yes" & column M has some value (text or number).

    =SUMPRODUCT(1-(M2:M10=""),--(P2:P10="Yes"))

    Thank you for your help.

    fst1 say:

    hi

    special sorts almost always use a helper column.

    in a blank column to the side of your data, you could enter a formula like this.....

    assuming your sort column is A....

    =if(A2="Ralph Lauren",0,1)

    copy down the helper column to the end of your data.

    use the helper column as the primary sort and your data column(A??) as the secondary sort.

    this should bring lines with Ralph Lauren to the top and sort all else normally.
