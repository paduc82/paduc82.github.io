ronrosenfeld say:
On Mon, 27 Dec 2010 18:09:12 +0000, bjaney74 wrote:
 
>
>
>Green >= .7 did not work (still have the same percentages with different colors) ex: 70% is green and Yellow like before.  What is causing the same percentage to fall into to more than one category?
>
>Yellow >= .51 did not work, it started at .51 where it was suppose to but ended at 70% again (70% is green and Yellow like before)
>

Your problem, most likely, is that the cells you think contain 70% really don't.  They just display that way because of how you are formatting the cells.
Formatting does alter the contents of the cell, and formulas work on the actual contents of a cell; not on how it happens to be displayed.
 
Your "fix" will be to either round the formula that you have in the cell to your desired limits of precision; or select from the Options "Precision as Displayed" which will alter the values, irretrievable, of everything on your worksheet.
 
So, if your percent cells have a formula  =A1/B1 or similar, you could change that to =round(a1/b1,2) ; and your traffic lights should then display as you seem to want them to.
