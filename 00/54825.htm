considerate_guy say:

How long are each of the pictures on either side of the transition? And do you have a transition between each of the photos?

Think of a transition (animation) as an overlap when one image is fading out and the other is fading in. If you have a 7 second photo next to another 7 second photo, but with a 5 second overlap (transition), that leaves 2 seconds on each photo when it isn't transitioning.

Now if you then want to transition to another photo after that, there isn't enough time. You need time added to account for a transition at the beginning and the end.

So if you wanted the photo to have 5 second transitions at both ends and appear with no transition for 2 seconds, you would need to make the photo duration equal to 12 seconds. If you want the picture to appear for longer than 2 seconds, you'd want to add more time still.

Basically, make sure you account for a transition at both ends of the photo.

a. user say:

The firewall is the wrong tool to use, and you can seriously impair your computer by altering the firewall incorrectly.

A better tool to use is a DNS service that offers whitelisting. Or you can use your router, which probably offers URL whitelisting by keyword.

But with both those choices you'll run into a few problems:

Like most large websites, Facebook owns hundreds of thousands of unique URLs and IP addresses, and creates and modifies new URLs regularly.

If you block all other URLs and IPs, virtually every Facebook page would cease to work. 

Finally, it's not within your ability to block all other inbound and outbound connections, as a casual session with Wireshark will demonstrate.
