You need a printer that is capable of printing to the edge of the paper or you can fake it by specifying a larger (custom) page format, print the document and manually cut the "excessive" areas around the page.

charles kenyon say:
Stefan has given you your options. Word believes what the printer driver tells it about what can be done with your printer. Many printers, especially ink jet printers, have a fairly wide area at the bottom that cannot be printed on.

macropod say:

While searching on net i found there is something called non printable area. I tried checking to reduce that non printable area to zero. Let me know if that is the issue or something. 

Just to add to what Stefan and Charles have said, the non-printable area is determined by the printer hardware. Nothing you do in software can get around that. Word will warn you when you set the margins to narrow for what the printer reports as its non-printable area, but you may be able to crib a bit more than what the printer reports it can do.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
To add to what the others have said, some printers have a "borderless" setting, but it's generally intended for photo printing and may be limited to sizes smaller than US Letter or A4 or may require a special paper carrier. Often it can't be used to ordinary documents. If you can deceive your printer into thinking the paper is larger than it is (Legal rather than Letter, for example), you might be able to trick it, but the downside is that you may be damaging the printer by releasing ink into the works through the required paper handling.

jean-yves tfelt say:

Hi,

 

To my understanding, when a date (text string looking like a date) is entered in a cell, Excel will try to evaluate it and try to recognise, or not, depending on what a date should look like, as defined in you pc's regional settings. two things here :

What is the sequence of data : dd/mm or mm/dd

What is the date separator : /  or -

So when you dine a date, in the regional settings, to look like dd/mm/yyyy but you entered a value in a different format, excel will try to swap these value (day and month)

If the slash, /,  was defined as date separator, entering 01-03-2013 will not allow Excel to recognise a date format and will threat the entry as a text string. 

 

Formatting a cell with a particular format will not change the way dates should be entered but only the way they are displayed in the cell, i.e. cell text. Note the possible difference in the formula bar.

 

Having defined a particular date format, you STILL have to enter the date AS specified in the regional settings to be valid. Note that 01 March 2013 will allways be correctly evaluated.
