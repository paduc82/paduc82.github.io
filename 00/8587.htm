From a generic trouble-shooting standpoint the reality is this

#1 - It is QuickBooks that is invoking the sending of an email using whatever the configuration parameters have been set as per documentation that should be included with Quickbooks

#2 - <IF> the support folks deem it to be an <Outlook> problem then it is they that should indicate why and at he very least confirm all the applicable settings. To restate point #1 a different way - you don't launch and use Outlook to send the QuickBooks invoice <from> QuickBooks - you invoke that process from QuickBooks to use whatever email setting it's configured for (am not a QuickBooks Pro user so have no way to determine what settings are involved).

If any support person from <any> vendor simply states that "it's an Outlook problem" without providing any information for anyone to go on then what essentially is being said is "have no clue as to why it's not working - it should and since it can't possibly have anything to do with us - go see the other guys"

#3 -are you saying that QuickBooks is invoking a WebMail client when you send an invoice via QuickBooks?

There are 3 configuration areas at play - (1) default email client -(2) MAPI setting - (3) Mailto setting. There's no way for Outlook to know what every 3rd party program does or needs.

Finally - always helpful to provide specific details concerning "I am unable to send invoices......." in terms of what actually occurs (or doesn't as the case may be) with any messages that appear exactly/completely as those messages are displayed.
