That's a good start.

Your system may appear to hang on mup.sys, but it is actually what comes after it that is the problem.  The mup.sys load is long gone.

If your system has a chkdsk on reboot pending, it will also appear to hang on mup.sys until the chkdsk completes. Lots of disk flashing makes this obvious, but it can fool you when the last thing you see is mup.sys...  (be really patient).

The next thing to load after mup.sys is usually i8042prt.sys which involves your mouse and keyboard driver.  Since you say they are USB and neither work, that is even more suspicious to me.

Are your KB and mouse wireless and can you use a wired PS/2 KB device for a test (nothing USB if possible).  If wireless, your wireless drivers may not load in Safe Mode.

Unplug the mouse and all other USB devices temporarily (you only need a keyboard to boot in Safe Mode) and if still no go, is is possible to use a wired PS/2 keyboard for a test.

Anything new with the mouse - new mouse, wireless mouse, etc.?
