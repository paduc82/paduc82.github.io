How to get Word Autotext from one computer with 2007 to another with - Microsoft Community

cmsaunders say:

How to get Word Autotext from one computer with 2007 to another with 2010

I've got autotext entries in my computer using word 2007 and I want to move them onto another computer using word 2010 - is this possible? If so, how?
jay freedman say:
AutoText entries in both 2007 and 2010 are "building blocks" that are stored in templates. To find out which templates, open Word 2007 and click Insert > Quick Parts > Building Blocks Organizer. In the dialog, click the column heading of the Gallery column, which will sort all of the AutoText entries to the top. Look at the list in the Template column to see where those entries are stored -- most of them will probably be in Normal.dotm, but some of them may be in Building Blocks.dotx.

Now prepare to copy the template(s) from one computer to the other. Close Word. Then go to the Templates folder. If you haven't made any changes in the File Locations dialog of the Options, the Normal.dotm template will be in the folder
   C:\Users\<your name>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Templates
Because the AppData folder and everything below it is marked as Hidden, you may have to change an option in the Windows Explorer's dialog at Tools > Folder Options > View to show hidden folders. Copy Normal.dotm to a USB drive or disk, or use a network connection to the other computer.

If any of the AutoText entries are in Building Blocks.dotx, you'll find that file in the folder
   C:\Users\<your name>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Document Building Blocks
or one of the folders under it. Also copy that file to the USB drive or disk.

On the computer with Word 2010, paste the copied file(s) into the folder
   C:\Users\<your name>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Document Building Blocks
and restart Word.

doc convert to docx or docm in VBA - Microsoft Community

juwen.z say:

doc convert to docx or docm in VBA

I have some "*.doc" files,I want to convert to "*.docx"or "*.docm" with Word 2010 VBA,how to do?
doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Use

Dim fd As FileDialog
Dim strFolder As String
Dim strDoc As String
Dim DoctoConvert As Document
Set fd = Application.FileDialog(msoFileDialogFolderPicker)
With fd
    .Title = "Select the folder into which the documents will be saved."
    If .Show = -1 Then
        strFolder = .SelectedItems(1) & "\"
    Else
        MsgBox "The documents will be saved in the default document file location."
        strFolder = ""
    End If
End With
strDoc = Dir$(strFolder & "*.doc")
Do While strDoc <> ""
    Set DoctoConvert = Documents.Open(strFolder & strDoc)
    With DoctoConvert
        .SaveAs strFolder & "Processed\ & strDoc & "x", wdFormatDocumentDefault
        .Close
    End With
    strDoc = Dir
Loop

dan at it associates say:

These new computers came with Office Starter 2010, which itself is a subset of the Office 2010 Single Image Installation.  The Office 2010 SII is a special installation of Office that contains all of the bits/code needed to install four different Office 2010 editions onto the computer (Office Starter 2010, Office Home and Student 2010, Office Home and Business 2010, and Office Professional 2010).  Which edition gets installed depends upon the product key entered into the main screen when you click on the Start Menu link for "Microsoft Office 2010."

 

So in your case, there is no need to uninstall Starter on the computers that have not yet been deployed to end users, because technically until you make your choice at the main Office 2010 screen, no installation of Office 2010 has been done yet.

 

What can happen is that when a computer is being prepared for an end user, the IT person can click on "Microsoft Office 2010" in the Start Menu, and they will get a screen that gives them three choices:

A.  I already purchased Office 2010

B.  Go online to purchase Office 2010

C.  Use Office Starter 2010

 

The IT person should choose "I already purchased Office 2010" and then on the next screen there will be a place to enter the product key for Office 2010.  When you enter one of the product keys for Pro out of the batch of 40 you purchased, the Office SII installer will see that it is an Office Professional 2010 product key and Office Professional 2010 will be installed by the Office SII installer.

 

Now to answer your question, just Ghost an undeployed computer and you will have what you need.

The original SII that was included on the Office 2010 Preinstallation Kit (OPK) disc, and I think two subsequent versions of the OPK, contained the bits to "install" Starter, and Yes it originally was to be a product license that the system builder was supposed to pay Microsoft for.  However there is no accounting mechanism built anywhere into the product so I doubt if MS ever collected 5 from a local Mom-n-Pop system builder.

 

When MS announced it was discontinuing Starter 2010, the OPK download available on the systembuilder website was pulled and replaced with a modified version that no longer offered "Use Office Starter 2010" as a choice when Out-Of-The-Box-Experience was run.

 

The Microsoft-manufactured OPK disc that I got at an Office 2010 launch event for systembuilders has proven invaluable when the somewhat finicky Office Starter 2010 click to run vapor-locks and even a repair won't fix it.  It's so much easier to reinstall using the OPK disk than being at the mercy of a royalty OEM whose only method for cleanly installing Starter is to run the full cycle of the manufacturer's recovery procedure.
