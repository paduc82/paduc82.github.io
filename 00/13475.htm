Start by changing the path separator. In OS X, it's a forward slash (/), not a backward slash (\) as you have it now. In older versions of Office you'll need to use a colon (:).

LocalSeparator = Application.PathSeparator

And then just use LocalSeparator whenever you need to use /  ,  \    or    :   .

Thank you for posting on the Microsoft Community.

Uninstalling the Zune software won't make you lose anything.  Zune itself is more or less just a window to what is on your PC, so uninstalling it just closes that window, without removing the content itself.

The first thing you'd want to do is uninstall the Zune software completely.  I recommend using this utility to do this, to ensure it's completely removed from your PC:
http://support.microsoft.com/mats/Program_Install_and_Uninstall?wa=wsignin1.0

Save this to your PC and then run it.  During the setup steps, it's going to ask if you are having issues Installing or Uninstalling an application.  We want to select Uninstalling, and then find Zune in the list of programs, and follow the rest of the steps in the utility.  You might find more than one item named Zune, usually Language Packs.  We just want the one called Zune by itself.

Once that's completed, you'll want to clear your temp folder on your PC.  To do this, press Windows+R and put %temp% in the Run box, and hit Enter.  Then press Ctrl+A to highlight everything in the folder, and hit Delete.  Skip anything that it gives you an error with.  After that, empty your recycle bin, located on the desktop.  When that's finished, reboot your PC.

After you're signed back in, download Zune 4.8 from here to reinstall:
http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=27163

Let us know if you keep having issues after this reinstall!

Sounds more tedious than it is but this is s straightforward method...

To insert a TC field;

  1. Select & copy the content to be included in the TOC
  2. Place the insertion point at the right end of that content
  3. Go to Insert> Field
  4. From the Categories select Index & Tables, then choose TC from the 3
  5. OK out of both dialogs
In the document you'll see {TC "HeadingText" \l3 } . You can use one to create an AutoText enttry with a name such as l3head, then you'll be able to insert them faster & simply replace the "HeadingText" portion as appropriate.
