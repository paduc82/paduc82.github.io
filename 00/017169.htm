steve rindsberg say:

Thanks for posting the comparative images, but looking at images in the browser isn't very helpful in this case.  It would be better to be able to see the original PPT (a one-slide version of it would be fine) and the PDF produced from it.

A couple of thoughts:

Changing the compression option only affects what happens to images you insert after making the changes.  Once the images are inserted, the damage, if any, is done and can't be reversed.

The compression options no effect on images you insert into content or image placeholders.  They're *always* compressed.  It's a bug.  And if you insert an image onto a slide that includes an empty content or image placeholder, PPT 2007 will pop the image into the placeholder rather than inserting it as an image shape.  The trick is to delete any empty placeholders of this type before inserting images, OR add some content to them so they're not empty, insert the image, then delete the dummy content from the placeholder.

In some versions of PPT, applying certain effects or borders to an image can cause it to go soft.  See what happens with plain images, no effects added.

Make sure that the High Quality option is selected in the print dialog before you print to PDF.

And when using Acrobat, check it's job options settings.  If you're setting it to make the smallest possible PDFs, that'd likely be the source of the unwanted compression.

john korchok say:

Here's my standard Acrobat setting note I send to my clients to eliminate downsampling in PDFs:

Windows Using File>Print>Adobe PDF Printer
1.  From the Start Button, choose Settings/Printers and Faxes, then right-click on Adobe PDF and choose Properties. 
2.  Under the General tab, click on Printing Preferences. On the Adobe PDF Settings tab, click the Edit button to the right of Default Settings. 
3.  Choose the Images tab. 
4.  Under the all three groups (Color, Greyscale and Monochrome) set Downsample to Off. 
5.  Click on the Save As button and give your new Job Options a name like BestQuality. 
6.  Click on OK. Check that Default Settings shows your new settings. OK Out.

Windows Using Acrobat tab>Create PDF Button
1.  From the Acrobat tab of the Ribbon, choose Preferences, then click on Advanced Settings.
2.  Choose the Images tab. 
3.  Under the all three groups (Color, Greyscale and Monochrome) set Downsample to Off. 
4.  Click on the Save As button and give your new Job Options a name like BestQuality.
5.  Click on OK. Check that Default Settings shows your new settings. OK Out.

Mac
1.  In your Application folder, look inside the Adobe Acrobat folder for Acrobat Distiller and open it.
2.  In Distiller, choose Settings>Edit Adobe PDF Settings.
3.  Click on the Images tab
4.  Under all three groups (Color Images, Greyscale Images, Monochrome Images), set Sampling to Off.
5.  Click on the Save As button and give your setting a name like BestQuality.
6.  Click on Save. Check that Default Settings shows your new settings. Close Distiller.
