doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Use:

Dim t As Table
Dim rngt As Range
For Each t In ActiveDocument.Tables
    Set rngt = t.Range
    t.ConvertToText vbTab
    rngt.ConvertToTable vbTab
    With rngt.Tables(1)
        .Columns(1).Width = InchesToPoints(5.31)
        .Columns(2).Width = InchesToPoints(1.5)
        .Rows(2).Cells.Merge
    End With
Next t

A bit more code to re-instate the original formatting will probably be required, but without knowing how the original table is formatted, cannot give you that.

Programmatic calls to Outlook almost never work the way you would want them to. Most calls to Outlook are made with simple MAPI which will only open a stub of Outlook but with no mail transports. The most you can ever do that way is create a message that will remain in Outlook until the next time you fully open the program at which time you may or may not be able to send the message (there's a good chance the message will still have been created incorrectly).

Although I absolutely don't advise it, you can remove the entire Styles group as follows:

  1. Right-click on the QAT and choose Customize the Ribbon.
  2. In the "Customize the Ribbon and keyboard shortcuts" dialog, under Main Tabs, select Home to expand it.
  3. Select Styles and click Remove.

Be aware that this will remove not just the Quick Styles gallery but the entire Styles group, and it will not affect the amount of space consumed by the Ribbon itself. If you want to do that, you can minimize the Ribbon by double-clicking on any tab. Click on any tab to open it temporarily for use, or double-click again to expand the Ribbon.
