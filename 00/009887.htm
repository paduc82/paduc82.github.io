john mcghie say:

OK, you can do this with an Advanced Search/Replace, but you need to be careful (very careful!).  It is a LOT quicker to do this than it is to read about it.  But you need to read carefully, because you are about to perform a series of replacements using Word's little-known regular expression ability.

  1. Make a copy of the document and save it (if this goes wrong, you lose all the text, so let's not take risks with the original, work on a copy!)
  2. Go to Edit>Find>Advanced find and replace
  3. First, we need to "mark" the paragraph marks, because we do not want to lose them. It helps greatly to turn on your non-printing characters for this exercise, so you can see what you are doing.  Click the Â¶ on the Ribbon until the blue paragraph marks appear.
  4. In the "Find what" box, type ^p  -- that's a caret symbol and a lower-case letter p.  Must be lower case. No spaces.
  5. In the Replace with box, type $^p -- a dollar sign and a caret and a lower-case p. The dollar sign could be any other character you like, so long as it is NOT a digit and does NOT appear in the unwanted text (a-z, A-Z).
  6. Click Replace All.  This marks each paragraph mark Find what box, paste [!0123456789$] exactly like that, including the square brackets. The exclamation point tells Word to find any character that is NOT in the brackets, except itself.
  7. In the Replace box, click Delete until you are sure there is nothing in it.
  8. Check the "Use wildcards" box.  The use wildcards box will not be visible unless you click the  âˆ‡ checkbox at the bottom left of the dialog.
  9. Click Replace All.  All the text will be removed apart from the dollar signs and numbers.
  10. Go to Edit>Find>Advanced find again, and this time type a $ sign only.
  11. In the Replace with box, type ^p
  12. CLEAR the Use wildcards checkbox.
  13. Hit Replace all.
  14. Now we are left with the numbers, and lots of blank paragraphs.  To get rid of those, go back to the Advanced Find dialog.
  15. In Find What, type ^p^p -- two carets and two lower-case p
  16. In Replace with, type ^p -- replace two paragraph marks with one.
  17. Hit Replace all several times, until the number of replacements it shows stops decreasing. Typically, it will get down to 2 or 3 and be unable to go below that.

There you go, job done.  And as I said, a lot quicker to do it than to read it!  For more, see https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Use-wildcard-characters-to-find-or-replace-text-610e37dc-bb2f-4a8b-8fa5-aa991160eafb

grovesp601 say:

Here is what I did to correct the issue being unable to change the Primary Alias.  Your mileage may vary and know that you are proceeding at your own risk. :)

This is a two-step process - create a new alias for the account and ensure it works.  Then, visit the Windows Live Admin Center and delete the account associated with the external domain.  This will "break," the link between the external domain account and the newly created alias.  You're then free to make the new alias the primary one.

  1. Sign out of everything and clear your browser's cookies. Microsoft recommended using Internet Explorer several times in case there was an issue with the browser settings.  I'll follow suit.
  2. Visit the Microsoft Account Center at account.live.com and sign in.
  3. Click on Aliases under the Account heading in the left menu.
  4. You should see your Primary Alias, with a message that indicates it cannot be changed.
  5. Click Add Alias and complete the form.  I used a Microsoft Outlook.com account, but you are free to use any alias outside of the domain in question.
  6. Click Add Alias at the bottom to finish the process.
  7. Confirm you have a Primary Alias and the new alias you've just created.  Now, click Sign Out.
  8. Attempt to login to your account with the new alias and identical password.  If this does not work, repeat steps 2-7.
  9. Now, visit the Windows Live Admin Center at domains.live.com.
  10. You should see a list of domains associated with your Windows Live account.
  11. While there may be only one, be sure to click the one that matches the domain of your account.
  12. Click on Member accounts in the left menu.
  13. You can view or delete accounts from this listing - click the Red X across from your account under the Delete heading.
  14. You'll receive the message, "Deletions Are Permanent."  Try not to panic - remember you can still access the account via the alias you just created.
  15. Your browser may redirect to an accounts page to reset the Primary Alias.  If this does not occur, click Sign Out, and login to account.live.com using your alias.
  16. You will have the option to add a new alias or assign an existing - use the one you just created.
  17. Voila. If you visit the Aliases link in the Microsoft Account Center, you will now see that your newly created alias is the Primary Alias.  You can subsequently add additional aliases and use the Make Primary Alias option to change it.  (Up to twice per week.)

I am by no means an expert, and your account issue may or may not be related to the domain association.  Feel free to ask any questions and I'll answer if I'm able.

Good luck.
