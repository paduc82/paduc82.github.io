rob koch say:

Since the IoT version is based on the Windows 10 IoT Enterprise version, these consumer focused community forums aren't the correct place to ask your question.

You'd be better off asking this in the TechNet forums where other OEM and commercial customers ask technical questions such as this one relating to; Windows 10 IoT enterprise (LTSB) deployment

Simply click the highlighted forum name near the top of that thread in order to view that and other forums in which you may start your own thread.

That said, considering the work that might be involved to configure the Defender components within IoT in order to accomplish what you desire, I'd first take the time to analyze the true risk your particular dedicated purpose application might include.  If you've already locked down your IoT installation to include only the operation of the particular application required to operate your machine, with limited access by operators to download files and the removal of general web browsing abilities, then your risk of an attack should be minimal.

Adding the complication of Defender or other security applications to this type of limited embedded system installation can be counterproductive and in fact typically indicates that the initial design of the system may not have been thought out completely.

In most cases, it's best to simply remove all Windows components not absolutely necessary to the core application operation in order to limit such risks, in which case the hardened operating system often contains few if any of the vulnerabilities, especially those relating to human social engineering, that are the most common points of entry used to attack systems today.

Deleting skype account

kenneth_f say:

Hi

We, at Skype, no longer have an option to delete accounts. Microsoft aimed to bring convenience on your end and that means you do not need to contact us to process the cancellation. You may click this link for the step by step process in deleting your account:  How to close your Microsoft account

To set your expectation, if you proceed to delete your Skype account, the associated Microsoft account will be closed as well. This is due to the full migration of Skype to Microsoft and one credentials to all Microsoft Products. If you are using other email domains such as Yahoo, Gmail, etc., you can still use the email for signing in to any other email account but can no longer be used to sign in to any Microsoft Products.

Your account will be closed after 60 days, but you if you change your mind anytime during the 60 day period. All you have to do is sign in to your account again to cancel the closure.

The 60-day waiting period helps keep hackers from changing an account’s security info as a way to completely take over that account. This waiting period gives the real account owner enough time to be alerted of the change and to cancel it in case they didn’t start it. Let me know if you still have questions.
