If the problem turns out to be a corrupt user profile, an in-place upgrade will not solve it and you need to move the folders under your present user account to the new one and change the sign-in to your Microsoft account.

How do I add more than 1 mobile number to a contact? - Microsoft Community

aaronbenjamin say:

How do I add more than 1 mobile number to a contact?

How do I add more than 1 mobile number to a contact? I'm only able to send a text message to numbers stored as mobile numbers...but contacts are limited to one mobile number each. This is crazy annoying. 

Help?
entegy say:
I tried, but it seems my developer screenshot app isn't working. Until I get that fixed, I'll try to give more detailed instructions and use the generic screenshots from the Windows Phone help website.

  • On the person's contact card, add the second mobile number under any of the headings. Use "work" for example. So now, your person has two numbers: mobile and work.
  • On the person's contact card, tap the "text" option. (It may also be called "text + chat") You will be taken to a screen from which to compose text.
  • When a person has multiple ways to reach them, a switch button becomes available when viewing their messaging thread. See this screenshot from Windows Phone help. Notice at the bottom of her messages, there are four buttons: send, attach, speak and switch.

**NOTE** whenever you don't know what a button from the app bar at the bottom of the screen does, tap the ... at the bottom right of the screen. This will reveal the button names.

  • Tap the switch button, then tap more phone numbers. You can select which number to send messages to.
Chances are you never saw the switch button because you never added more than one number to that person's contact card. It's a lot of words, but once you notice that switch button, it's very easy.
