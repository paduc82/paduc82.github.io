Uh, no it does not.


Look closely at the package...the footnote you quoted applies ONLY to Office Home and Student 2010, it does NOT apply to any other edition of Office 2010.


The edition of Office 2010 you have, Professional, was sold either as a "full retail license" (license designation FPP [which means full packaged product]), or as a "product key card" license (license designation PKC).


The full license is permitted to be concurrently installed and activated on up to two computers owned by you, and you are permitted to move the licensed activation(s) to new/other computers, as long as you only have two installed and activated at any one given moment in time.


The product key card license is much more restrictive.  You are permitted to install and activate the PKC license on one computer, and that is where the license lives and dies...there is no permission to move the license to any other computer, even if the computer dies.


The fact that you purchased at a major retailer and your purchase included a disc leads me to believe that you have the FPP license.  If so, then you should be able to install and activate on your new computer, so it's likely you have a technical issue rather than a licensing issue.

See this article to uninstall Office 2010:  http://support.microsoft.com/kb/971179/en-us
