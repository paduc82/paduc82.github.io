Most people who do RAID mirroring or a PC do it because they think
it's like backing up and it protects them against data loss. That is
*not* what RAID mirroring is. RAID mirroring is used by large
companies who can't afford to have any downtime, and that's the
purpose of RAID mirroring. It keeps you up seamlessly even if one
drive fails.
 
But it doesn't protect you against the things that backing up protects
you against. It leaves you susceptible to simultaneous loss of the
original and backup to many of the most common dangers: severe power
glitches, user errors, nearby lightning strikes, virus attacks, even
theft of the computer.
 
In my view, secure backup needs to be on removable media, and not kept
in the computer. For really secure backup (needed, for example, if the
life of your business depends on your data) you should have multiple
generations of backup, and at least one of those generations should be
stored off-site.
 
Companies that use RAID mirroring almost always also have a strong
external backup plan in place. The are both needed. Very few home
users have any real need for mirroring.
