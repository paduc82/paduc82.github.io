"I'm not a gamer, but Sibelius behaves similarly in some ways..."

In what way is that (I'm a gamer, and don't use Sibelius, but I'll waffle on anyway) ?

If you scroll through your scores a lot you may notice "tearing" (a faint wavy horizontal line)

@ 60Hz when scrolling at speed, but this should not be a problem, as at this speed you can't

read the score anyway.

It's this tearing when playing a game at a frame rate higher than 60fps that is a problem for gamers.

This is commonly fixed by using V-sync, but V-sync is not always effective and can cause other problems, so some gamers prefer 120Hz monitors, which are overpriced for what they do.

[ Says the man using a 120Hz monitor, and who finds tearing when scrolling anything annoying  ;) .]

As Mike said, LCD panels that have a theoretical 81Hz refresh rate can  rarely be set to

use that refresh rate, unlike 120/144Hz models (and CRT monitors), but with LCD 60Hz does not

cause eye strain, as did CRT.

"The u2010 (the one I'd really like, because it's is IPS) "

Yes, the U2010 is legendary, but...

The two main advantages with an IPS monitor are a wider viewing angle without colour distortion,

and better overall colour accuracy.

The new VA, and similar panels still have good viewing angle, but less accurate colour.

Do you need either with a music score ? Maybe....

- The U2412 & U2414 have a coarse (aggressive, according to TFT Central) anti-glare coating,

resulting in a somewhat grainy picture. I've read a few complaints about this with the 2412m in the past, and it could effect text, so it may be a good idea for your needs to find which monitors have the clearest text vs the most accurate colour.

The colour is not as good on the 2412m as on the 2410/2413 either - 6bit vs 8bit, and lower colour gambut of only 71% (U2410=98%, U2413=99%), which is similar to some of the better new TN panels.

The new VA, and similar new style panels, do have the better viewing angles of IPS though, and

I expect that, in addition to sharp text, the better viewing angle may be what you need for your circumstance.

- TN panels commonly have a viewing angle of around 170/160 degree (160 being for the vertical)

vs 178/178 IPS, so using the monitors in Portrait mode would require a more direct view than when in Landscape mode.

If you both stand and sit while working with your music, but remain fairly well centred on the monitors, Portrait mode would have an advantage over Landscape mode on a TN monitor (tilt helps too ;) ).

It mostly depends on how much you move about while you view the screen, and if others also

need to view the screen.

- Another tricky part with TN panels is finding a monitor with a rotating stand ( The 120Hz 'gaming'

monitors do rotate).
