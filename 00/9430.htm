jeeped say:

I believe that standard accrual accounting practises would dictate that column D (aka credits) should be negative numbers to start with so that the simple sum of debits (column C) and credits would produce the outstanding amount. Column D's SUMIF() could be written as one of the following,

=0-SUMIF(<range>, <criteria>, <sum_range>)

  ... or even more simply as,

=-SUMIF(<range>, <criteria>, <sum_range>)

Addendum: of course, my solution above depends upon the source of the data. If it was coming from a general ledger that was already in accrual format, the SUMIF() numbers should be coming in as negatives without changing their sign.
