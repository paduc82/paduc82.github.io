Inserting graphs etc in text box to occupy full page; how to get - Microsoft Community

gail writing up phd say:

Inserting graphs etc in text box to occupy full page; how to get narrative text to flow around

I am trying to insert graphs, or pictures with captions into a document with text. What I want to do is to be able to have these on a separate page within teh narrative text, but to able to format it so that when I amend or add text above these graphs/pictures, the text continues after without inserting extra blank pages. Currently I have the graphs in the right place, but if I add in lines on the previous page, the text doesn't shift down after the graph/picture text box, but adds in a new page before, with the resulting large gap, before the graphs on the next page, then continuation of the narrative on the the page after that. I am usifn Word 2007.
jay freedman say:

That's the way Word works, and has always worked. All graphics are "anchored" to some specific paragraph of text, and must always be on the same page as that paragraph. If editing of the text that precedes the anchor paragraph causes the paragraph and graphic not to fit on the current page, both are forced to the next page. The text from the following paragraphs cannot flow backwards to fill the empty space, any more than they could do so if the graphic wasn't present.

Often this is expressed as "Word is not a page layout program". What that means is that Word treats its text, and everything embedded in the text, as a stream; it makes pages as necessary to contain that stream by constantly recalculating the layout. Pieces of the stream can't flow backwards. In contrast, a page layout program such as Publisher considers the pages as fixed containers, and "pours" the content into them. Some objects such as graphics may be fixed to the pages, and the text flows around them.

The bottom line is that Word can't do what you propose. The only alternative is to completely finish editing the text and then -- and only then -- position the graphics where you want them. In the meantime, ignore the layout.
