Word 2010 macro error - Microsoft Community

cynthiabenner say:

Word 2010 macro error

I have been trying to create macros using a text file that has been created by another user.  I have had several errors but the current one is "Compile error: Invalid outside procedure."  I am not comfortable using VBA so any assistance would be great.

macropod say:
Without seeing your code, it's impossible to provide a solution. Even so, the implication is that you're trying to do something outside a Sub or Function than can only be done inside one.

jay freedman say:
Expanding on macropod's response a bit:

A procedure starts with "Sub <procedure name> ()" or "Function <procedure name> () As <data type>", and it ends with the matching line "End Sub" or "End Function". Almost everything else must be between those lines, except for lines starting with the words "Dim", "Const", "Public", and a few others.

If you have a line that tries to set a variable's value, or that calls another procedure, and that line isn't between Sub and End Sub, or between Function and End Function, then you get that compile error message.

The usual fix is to move the line inside a procedure where it belongs.

Adding your own music to your Xbox Music collection, on Windows 8 and - Microsoft Community

nathan roberts (sunknath) say:

Adding your own music to your Xbox Music collection, on Windows 8 and 8.1

Technical Level : Basic

Summary

This Wiki Article is here to help you add your music, from your device, onto your Xbox Music collection. Some of the steps in this article have been suggested by Mr Ohnaka on this thread on the Xbox Music forums on the Xbox.com.

This article has been updated, so it includes three methods on how you can add Music from your device into your Music collection stored on Xbox Music. These are:

  • Through the Import option on your navigational pane
  • Using the "Change where we look" option
  • Adding music through your file explorer, on your device

Note: By adding the music to your collection, on the app, this will not allow you to play the content on other devices. It will only allow you to play the music you add to your collection, on that device only.


Details

If your Xbox Music application can not pick up your songs within your Music Library that is stored on the Computer. You will need to add them in manually. You can now do this in three ways. You can add them using the "Change where we look" option; the Import option; and through your file explorer on your device.

Adding Music using the Import Option in your navigational pane

If you have music saved in your Music Library, on your device, then you can use the Import option on your navigational pane. If you cant find it, then look at this picture below to see where it will be.

 

Clicking on this option will display a message, telling you that the App is searching your Music Library. Once it has searched your music library, it will then add the music into your collection. This can be music from iTunes or just music that is stored on your device.

Adding Music using the "Change where we look" option

When you first open the Xbox Music App on your device, it should load up your collection. You should also see a notification telling you that all of your music has been added from this device. This is what it will look like on your screen:

 

You can add music to your collection, by clicking on the green hyperlinked text, which will bring up a pop up window that looks like this:

 

If you click on the + icon, then you will be able to add more locations that the app searches in to add your Music from your device. This will only make them available on the device only.

Adding music through your file explorer, on your device

If you can not find the notification, mentioned above, then you can add them through your file explorer. If you want to do this, please follow these steps here:

  1. First of all, you will need to go to your Windows 8 Start Screen
  2. Click or Tap onto the Desktop tile, this should open the classic desktop view
  3. Then from there you will need to click onto the File Explorer  (the manila folder icon next to the Internet Explorer icon).
  4. Now, under Libraries, tap or click onto Music
  5. Tap or click Manage on the ribbon.
  6. Tap or click Manage Library.
  7. Tap or click the Add button.
  8. Tap or click the folder where your music is stored, and then tap or click Include folder.
  9. Tap or click OK, wait for the changes to be applied, and then restart the Music app to make the new files appear.
