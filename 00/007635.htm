a. user say:

Hi Erwoin,

 

Thank you for your contribution to the forums. When it comes to connection errors, if you've already tried all the steps in that article we're probably looking at an issue with the phone itself. There are a few things we can take a look at however.

  • Does your phone charge when it's plugged in? If not, this is almost certainly an issue with either the USB port, the sync cable, or the phone itself. In this case, you'll want to first see if any other USB device works with the PC, and progress based on that result.
  • Is it detected by the PC at all? For example, when following the steps about updating your driver, did you see the driver in Device Manager? If it charges but isn't detected at all, we might be looking at an issue with the USB drivers on your PC, or possibly the USB port itself. The best thing to try in this scenario is another PC.
  • Are you able to try plugging your phone into another PC? This is probably going to be the most helpful thing to take a look at, because if it's not detected on another PC we can pretty safely conclude that it's an issue with either the cable or the phone.
  • Do you have another sync cable available to try using? This would be the second most important step if possible, because if you get the same results with a different cable(especially on another PC), then it's probably an issue with the phone.

If we can conclude the issue is with the phone, I would suggest backing up what you can via SkyDrive, and then performing a factory reset of your phone. This erases all data, but will allow you to reinstall the firmware that dictates how your phone syncs with your PC.

Sam P.

The museo_sans300 portion is a font commonly used for Internet web pages.
