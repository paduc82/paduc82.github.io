a. user say:

You should uninstall programs from Start / Control Panel / Programs and Features - then select the program you want to delete, right click on it and click on uninstall or delete - otherwise pieces of the program stay in various places throughout the operating system and in the registry - there to cause you problems later on and taking up space for no good reason.

If you really want to delete this folder take ownership of it. 

To fix this problem with the CA folder, take and then click the name of the person you want to give ownership to (you may need to add it if it isn't there - or it may be yourself). If you want it to apply to sub-folders and files within that folder, then check the box for replace owner on subcontainers and objects and click OK.  Back out and now there's a new owner for the folders and files who can change permissions as needed.  You can now change them from read-only (even if the main folder says they still are read only - you can have access yourself as the owner).  You can now delete the files and folders.  Here's more information on taking ownership of a file or folder: http://www.vistax64.com/tutorials/67717-take-ownership-file.html.. If taking ownership doesn't work, then someone has placed a deny access to the Everyone user somewhere in security - you need to find that entry and delete it and then you'll be able to delete the files and folders.

Good luck and I hope this helps!

a. user say:

Your describing what sounds like an overheating problem.

Watching videos and other "heavy" things put more demand on your components and if they get to the point of a thermal event your system will just switch off to prevent permanent damage.

There might not be any good clues about such things in the event viewer logs or a crash dump if there is no time to write that information out.

Why don't you download something like the free version of Speccy:

https://www.piriform.com/speccy

Then launch it and you can see what the temps are (green is good, red is bad) then minimize Speccy and you can still see the overall temp in the notification area next to your clock:

Then do whatever you do that recreates the failure condition while keeping an eye on the temp and at what point does it get to where the system shuts off.  Look for a pattern of failure.

Leave your system off long enough to cool down and repeat and see if you can spot a pattern of failure.

Your crash dumps indicate you have a Compaq 610 and if you perform a google search on something like:

compaq  610  overheat

You will find many links and videos that show you how to do some maintenance to clear out dust/debris that may have built up over time preventing ventilation to keep the system at a good temperature.  Other advice...  use your laptop on a flat surface (not in your lap), etc.

Some instructions are more complex than others depending on your comfort level about such things but you can at least get a can of compressed air and blow the dust out of your system vents and fan and monitor the temps again to look for an imporvement.
