Chart showing rank - Microsoft Community

tezmac say:

Chart showing rank

I need a chart to show our ranking in an annual competition. eg last year we came 20th out of 53, and this year 15th out of 71. The number of entries each year is always different and we are aiming to come first. Any ideas?
tushar mehta say:
I need a chart to show our ranking in an annual competition. eg last year we came 20th out of 53, and this year 15th out of 71. The number of entries each year is always different and we are aiming to come first. Any ideas?


This evokes some interesting issues.

For something that happens once a year, is it even worth charting?  Worth reporting, yes.  Worth charting, not so sure.

But, let's assume you do want a chart.

If you just plot the rank, then improvement in performance will create a downtrend as the rank goes from 20 to 15 to 10 to 1.  While there's nothing wrong with that, it will require some education so that the reader knows that down is better.

How do you want to handle the number of competitors?  Is 15 out of 53 better than 18 out of 71?  One option might be to see how you did relative to the only those who were better than you in the previous year.  Or it might make more sense to compare performance relative to those who were in the previous competition.

Ignoring the subtleties of comparing with only previous competitors...

One way to factor in a changing number of competitors would be to scale your rank to a number between 1 and 100 depending on the number of competitors and track this.  A simple way would be your-rank / number-of-competitors * 100.

Then, if you want a positive trend to appear climbing up in the chart, reverse the display of the y-axis values.

You can use a Line chart but I would be more inclined to go with a Column chart.
