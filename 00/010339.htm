timzimpfer say:

Find most repeated value based on a condition

First, thank you in advance. 

I am trying to determine what my most repeated value is based on the time of day.  For example, what was my highest product type (column M) at the rounded time of 12:30:00 AM (column U)?  I have 235,000 rows I am trying to compare so it is a lot of data.

amit tandon say:

Refer below image:

Enter below formula as an array formula (CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER) in cell V2 & copy down:

=IF(MAX(COUNTIFS($M$2:$M$100,$M$2:$M$100,$U$2:$U$100,"="&$U2))=1,$M2,IF(OR($U1=$U2,MAX(COUNTIFS($M$2:$M$100,$M$2:$M$100,$U$2:$U$100,"="&$U2))=0),"",INDEX($M$2:$M$100,MIN(IF(COUNTIFS($M$2:$M$100,$M$2:$M$100,$U$2:$U$100,"="&$U2)=MAX(COUNTIFS($M$2:$M$100,$M$2:$M$100,$U$2:$U$100,"="&$U2)),ROW($M$2:$M$100)-ROW($M$2)+1)))))

This will give the most repeated item in column M, corresponding to the time in column U. The most repeated item will appear only once on the first row when the time changes, and if all items in column M appear only once for specific time then each of them will be listed.

This formula can be customized in case your requirement varies.

Hope this is your requirement.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Select all of the text that you want to appear on a landscape orientated page and then from the Page Setup facility, select that orientation.  Word should automatically insert the necessary Section Breaks before and after that page.
