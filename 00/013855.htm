ianrg say:

In many situations you can do an upgrade that will retain your programs and files.

Alternatively you can do an upgrade that will retain your files only at you can do a complete clean install.

However, regardless of which one you use, you should back up your files anyway. 999 times out of a thousand this ends up being not necessary but how much comfort will that give you if yours is the one that goes wrong? - Good back ups of your files is also the best defence against ransomware or hardware failure.

With the three options (Retain everything, retain files only or clean install) there will always compromises.

  1. In retain everything it is possible that after your install your PC will not perform as well as a clean install because of the old programs, drivers and settings you retain. Some programs may be lost due to their incompatibility.
  2. With retain files this will be less of a problem but you will need to reinstall your programs.
  3. With a clean install there is the added investment in time and the chance that the install may give problems with older equipment or where the drivers in Win 10 are not as good as drivers you have now - I have found that the Asus touch gestures affect a clean install. And you have reinstall your programs and restore your files from your back up.

Most people have had no problems regardless of the method. Those that have had problems generally have fixed these by re-running the install but this requires a download of the install .iso on a USB etc. This is nothing new for major OS upgrade, especially one where it is relatively early in release. Many here have been doing this with quite a number of pre-release versions with little or no problems each time - some habitually clean install everytime, some upgrade retaining files and programs most of the time.
