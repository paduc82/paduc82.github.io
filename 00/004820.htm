How to Recover from a Crash and possible data loss - Microsoft Community

renee culver say:

How to Recover from a Crash and possible data loss

I hate to say this but BE PREPARED.

Before loading a technical Preview, when you have the system the way you like do a full image backup. The solution to this is Windows version sensitive: For Win 7 you may chose backup restore in the control panel. On later systems the control panel option to choose is file history. At the left bottom you will see System Image as a choice. You need a storage device that is savable. After having made the backup-tuck it away in a safe place.

2. Insure that you make an adequate recovery mechanism. It will be bootable this is a constant, however the remainder is not. After booting the recovery SSD or DVD for version seven becomes part of your recovery system. Practice restoring your system disk until you can safely recover. But be careful here because it's quite possible to wipe out other partitions on a disk but there is a fairly low probability of doing that. Remember that the disk or SSD may have other logical partitions which are the same as other disks and these are to be preserved. The safest method involves dual booting or having a native bootable system on the disk or having an alternate disk.

In case of a crash and there will be crashes, you can quickly restore your last backup or your original by booting your recovery device and restoring your backup or booting your other bootable partition if you have one. You will lose all new data since the backup BUT you will have a working system that you are familiar with. Practice backing up and Restoring until it's automatic. Always backup a comfortable working system.

My system has recovery built in.... Im usually prepared for a crash.

Renee
