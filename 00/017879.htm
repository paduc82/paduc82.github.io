Drive letters are normally assigned by Windows at installation with the OS partition as C:, every other hard drive and partition in somewhat physical order, CD/DVD/BR drives installed, then any other partitions/hard drives/removable drives added after installation.  Windows will sometimes "remember" different removable drives, so it assigns one USB drive to a letter, then a different USB drive to another letter.  Windows may also perm-assign a drive letter to any internal memory card device or another hot- swappable drive permanently installed in the system, even if a card or drive is never inserted.

To change drive letters in Disk Management, select the partition or drive you want to change, right click it, then select Change Drive Letter and Paths.  Remember, you can only assign a drive letter than has not been assigned, so you may have to start with assigning your Win10 partition to H:, then assign each disk by one, until you have the drive letters assigned the way you wish.

under Windows Vista and 7. The template will then be loaded as an add-in in Word.

Normal.DOTM is a remnant of the very original design of Word. When computers were standalone, non-networked, DOS machines.  That was unshakable design assumption.

Select A1:E6. I'll assume that A1 is the active cell within the selection.

Select Format | Conditional Formatting...

Select "Formula Is" from the first dropdown.

Enter the following formula into the box next to it:

=MIN(IF(A1<>$A$1:$E$6,ABS(A1-$A$1:$E$6)))<=7

Click Format...

Specify Bold.
