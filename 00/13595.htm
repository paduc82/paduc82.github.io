Thank you for reaching out with the community team. Apparently, Wi-Fi drivers that we have for windows PCs are designed to get signals in a particular range. Apparently there are no means to not see the wireless networks available especially if they are in range. The Wi-Fi is designed to see all available networks in the area once it is turned on. 

 In terms of having the devices connected to unwanted networks, you can try this: go to Settings > Network & Internet > Wi-Fi > Manage known networks > Choose the other networks that automatically connects > Properties > Switch off the Connect automatically when in range button.

Please let is know if this works.
