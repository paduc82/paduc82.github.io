I found on-line an obscure reference to McAfee being a problem in some cases, specifically with my computer (Dell Studio XPS 1645).  So I reviewed the programs installed on my computer and noticed that one McAfee program (I have two installed) was installed on October 15th, which is around the time I started experiencing my Internet access issue.  I initially thought it was my wireless router because I had also just managed to get it working properly again around the same time; hence, the reason for any confusion on my part.

Lesson learned...when you have issues always check your software installation dates to see if any of those correlate to the time you began experiencing your problem :-).

andre da costa say:
CPU Not Compatible

To install Windows 10 on your PC, the processor (CPU) must support the following features: Physical Address Extension (PAE), NX, and SSE2. Most CPUs have support for these features, so if you receive this error, it is likely because the NX feature is not enabled on your system.

You can uninstall Family Safety by signing in to the Administrator account of your computer and follow the steps below:
