vincentchoy say:

Office365 is a family name for Microsoft's suite of online products. It is not a single product.

Please do not confuse Office365 to MS-Office. MS-Office is the desktop productivity product of Microsoft, and Office365 is a broad name that describes Microsoft's suite of online productivity tools.

The exception is Office365 Personal  which is essentially MS Office for consumers via subscription, with some additional consumer features.

Office365 for Corporates comes in two families (Business and Enterprise), and are basically a whole suite of hosted "Server" products plus many business apps. Eg Exchange Online, SharePoint Online, Skype for Business Online, One Drive for Business, Yammer, Planner, Teams...etc. All these work as a suite to provide a seamless collaborative experience to the user.

Since all your requirements require on-prem, you cannot use Office365 as these are Microsoft's Online versions of their Server Products.

For your other questions, you really need to sit down with a Microsoft expert reseller to explain to you how Microsoft suite works, and how Microsoft licenses its products. You really need a A-Z lesson, which a forum doesnt do very well. A forum like this helps to answer any specific questions that you might have. In your case you really need to be briefed in detail across Microsoft various products. 

For example you talk about SharePoint. I am not sure if you are aware of the pre-requsites of SharePoint for its various use cases? 

You mentioned the cost of SharePoint Server license. Please also remember to include Windows Server License, SQL Server Licenses, and its corresponding CALS as well? Not sure also if you know that Office Online for on-premise also requires a Office Online Server. Coupled with this is also costs of servers, 2 tier or 3 tier design, backups etc.

In short, I would suggest working with a SharePoint consultant on your needs. Costs vary by country and expertise, so we can't help here.

If you intend to self study, a good place to start would be Microsoft's services descriptions which describe, almost down to the bone, the details of the various products. However, without a guide to help you navigate this, it might prove too complex to understand.

If so, it is possible that you are experiencing this issue because your account's auto-complete suggestions feature is not enabled. Kindly refer to the steps below on how to enable the auto-complete suggestions feature in your account. You can choose which suggestions appear, whether from contacts only in your list or from contacts and non-contacts.

 

To enable auto-complete suggestions for everyone youve communicated with:

  1. From your Inbox, click the gear icon on the upper right corner of the page beside your name.
  2. Select More mail settings.
  3. Under Customizing Outlook, click Advanced privacy settings.
  4. Under Auto-complete suggestions, select Give suggestions for anyone I've communicated with (contacts and non-contacts).
  5. Click Save.

To enable auto-complete suggestions only for people included in your contact list:

  1. From your Inbox, click the gear icon on the upper right corner of the page beside your name.
  2. Go to More mail settings.
  3. Under Customizing Outlook, click Advanced privacy settings.
  4. Under Auto-complete suggestions, select Only suggest people in my contact list.
  5. Click Save.

Hope this helps. Let us know how things turn out.
