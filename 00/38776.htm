If your QL is stacked on top of your open program(s) or the other way around you will not be able to shrink the Taskbar by dragging the top down until they are not stacked on top of each other.

If you do not see the vertical dotted separator to the RIGHT of the QL bar, that means it has been drug (or is it dragged) too far to the left and what is supposed to be two separators are now on top of each other.

Make sure the Taskbar is unlocked and grab and hold (with left mouse click) the separator to the LEFT (immediately to the left of the Start button) of either the QL or your open program(s) and slowly drag it to the right keeping it very horizontal towards the Notification area (clock) all the way until whichever you are dragging is on the same level as the other and the suddenly you can see two vertical separators and they are not on top of each other (keep trying) then release.

If the dragging of one doesn't make the second separator appear, try the other one.  Keep trying alternating between the two until it works and you see the two vertical separators.  Try one, they try the other until it does work, the release the left mouse button.

Then when the two are no longer on top of each other, you can drag the top of the Taskbar down to a single line and you should see the two vertical dotted separators - you may have to try it a few times, but it will work eventually.

Be careful when dragging the separator so that it stays horizontal and try not to let the mouse go up and down vertical when dragging the separator.

If you need to move the QL or open program area area around to get the in the left to right order you prefer, drag it around using the left separator bar - not the right.  The right bar resizes and you need to move things - not resize.

Then when you get things the way you want, Lock the Toolbar.

Hi I ran into your posts while considering trying to create VHD space on one of my hard drives.

There a way to fix this as long as you have your original windows installation disc, not just the repair disc. 

I you wish you can convert the drive to dynamic rather than basic in Win7xxx.

Make sure you have either another way to get at the data still there and that it wont matter if you can then not boot to that drive.  My computer has two drives and I boot from the other one and can then access data still on the dynamic partition.  So you open up the drive to have a look and you see the current VHD sector and the other sectors.  If you convert the sector that has your boot area and op system and is active to dynamic, it will then be able to be shrunk to just over what is on there.  You can not boot this any more but you may access it from either another bootable drive in your computer or later from what follows.  So once that section is dynamic and shrunk.  You then convert the VHD section to dynamic, and any other sections.  You then expand all of those sections excluding the one where your data is still remaining.  After this the sections can be formatted back to Basic Drives and as they are all already empty they will merge into one larger area. 

This results in you having data in one shrunk dynamic not bootable area.  But you regain your remaining hard drive area as the other partition on that drive.

Then install windows whatever on that partition.  You will then be able to boot from there, pick a drive letter for it and access the dynamic data in the part you left behind.

This assumes you didn't want to use a ton of dvd's to back everything up first.

Good luck with it.  You should still save anything important first externally in case.
