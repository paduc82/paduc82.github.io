Can I protect an email I send to someone from being forwarded and - Microsoft Community

canon1013 say:

Can I protect an email I send to someone from being forwarded and printed

I have a very sensitive email I would like to send with an attachement and I would like to restrict this email and the attachement from being forwarded or printed by the receiver.
graham mayor say:
You won't be able to stop someone forwarding the attachment - it's a file like any other - but you could create a protected PDF file from a graphical image of the document i.e. use an application like SnagIt to create bitmapped images of the pages - preferably with a 'busy' text watermark over the full area of the document to make it difficult for OCR, then use a PDF creation tool that allows security to be set to prevent printing and copying against a password. PrimoPDF will do that and of course Acrobat.

It will not stop someone determined from copying or from printing, or from cracking the password, but the extra trouble may be enough to deter them.

If the content is so valuable that you need to control access then don't send it by e-mail and number each document and get recipients to at least sign the paper copy of the document on receipt and make each copy subtly different so that you can tell later who has passed it on, should a duplicate come to light.

If it is more important than that, get specialist advice, but the fact remains that anything that can be seen can be copied.

If you no longer have the phone number associated with your Hotmail account, you can use an alternate email address instead. Follow these steps:

 

  1. Go to the Password reset page.
  2. Select the reason you cant sign in.
  3. Click Next.
