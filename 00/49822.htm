crdavis53 say:

Reader app, how does one change from full screen to a smaller window so that other open apps are still visible

Original title: Change readerr widow size

When uses Reader to view pdf files, how does one change from full screen to a smaller window so that other open apps are still visible?

cmdrkeene say:

You can't do this with the Reader app, at least not floating around inside it's own resizable window.  But you can "dock" the reader to the side of the screen by dragging it down from the top and then to the left or right, then open something else next to it (or even the desktop next to it), but the reader itself is a tablet app, so it can't be infinitely resized like regular programs.

Alternative: You could use another PDF program like Adobe Reader (or others, there's many choices and actually many a lot better that Adobe's, even though theirs is the most popular).

Windows 10 also brings full-resize to all apps, so even things built for tablets (like the Reader app) can look and work like regular old programs.  But maybe you're not willing or desire to upgrade to 10 yet, I'm just pointing out as an option.
