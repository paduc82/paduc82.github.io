robert aldwinckle on forums say:

went to a black screen and nothing happened.

Do you have a second monitor you could attach?  Black screen can mean that the OS has decided (inexplicably) to use a secondary monitor and hence switch the Display configuration to Second Screen Only.  If you are past the Lock screen and at least as far as the Login screen you can use Win+P, CursorUp, Enter.  In the case of the above hypothesis that would switch it to Extend which might then show you something on your laptop screen.

 

You can also try starting Narrator even at the Lock screen by pressing Win+Ctrl+Enter.  Then try pressing Ctrl+Alt+Delete to try to get past the Lock screen.  Listen to Narrator and hear what it thinks is possible.  Press Tab to navigate.  If you get past the Lock screen a possibility would be to get to the Power button's menu for its Restart button at which you could press a Shift- key with Enter or Spacebar.

As for your booting from an alternate device tactic, it's not clear if you have changed the device boot order (e.g. via BIOS settings) in order to make that work.
