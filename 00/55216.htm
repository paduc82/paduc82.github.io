glópez say:

Unfortunately, this seems to be some sort of bug.  In my case, VIA has not released a driver that can solve this situation, although my speakers work fine.  There's a way around this, however.  When you intend to use headphones connected to the computer's front jack, try the following:

  1. Locate the volume icon on the taskbar (that little speaker on the bottom right corner of the screen) and right-click it
  2. A menu will appear, select "Playback Devices"
  3. You will see a list of the sound devices connected to your computer.  If your headphones are connected properly, you should see a "Headphone" icon below the "Speakers" icon.  Right click on the headphones.
  4. Select "Set as default device".  Sound can now be heard through your headphones.  IMPORTANT: Doing so will disable sound from your speakers and re-route it thru your headphones, as intended.
  5. Please note, this is not an actual fix, is a workaround.  Once you unplug your headphones, your speakers WILL NOT WORK as usual.  You'll have to repeat the same procedure again, but this time select the speakers as your default device. 

Yes, this is a hassle, and yes, you'll have to do this every time you switch from speakers to headphones and vice-verse, unfortunately.  This is just some workaround for people who use headphones on their PCs and are struggling to make them work until a fix is issued.  Hope you find this helpful.
