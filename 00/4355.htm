rich michaels - mvp (greatcirclelearning.com) say:
Also as a follow-up take a look at this information from Word Help about Changing Spacing.

Change the spacing between lines or paragraphs
Show All

You can control the vertical space between the lines of text in your document by setting the line spacing, such as double space. Or, you can change the vertical space between paragraphs in your document by setting the spacing before or spacing after paragraphs.

 Line spacing

 Spacing between paragraphs

Do any of the following:

Change the spacing between lines
Change the spacing between paragraphs
Change the spacing between lines or paragraphs for all new documents

When you create a new document, Word uses the Normal template to determine the settings, such as the spacing between lines and between paragraphs. If you change the settings in this template, all new documents that you create will use the new settings.

  1. On the File menu, click Open, and then double-click /Users/username/Library/Application Support/Microsoft/Office/User Templates/Normal.dotm.

    box, press COMMAND+SHIFT+G, and in the Go to folder box, type ~/Library.

  2. Double-click Normal.dotm.

    The Normal template opens.

  3. On the Home tab, under Paragraph, click Line Spacing  , and then do any of the following:

    To

    Do this

    Change the spacing between lines

    Click the line spacing that you want.

    Change the spacing between paragraphs

    Click Line Spacing Options, and then under Spacing, in the Before or After boxes, enter the paragraph spacing that you want.

  4. Save and close the Normal template.
