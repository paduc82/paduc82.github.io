cmdrkeene say:

Companies that send out solicitations are required by law to honor unsubscribe requests.  Unfortunately, enforcing that is difficult.

Less-than-honest companies and spammers even use your click of the "unsubscribe link" as a sign that your email address is real, and so they send even more spam to it.

So unless it's a reputable company's message, the best advise is to click your email program's "junk" or "block" button, and don't click anything in the message that might tell the spammer that you saw the message.

Download and install (free) IrfanView.  This is probably one of the most popular image viewing utilities on the Internet, and it can do a lot more than just view images.  https://www.irfanview.com/

Once IrfanView is installed, right-click any photo file and select Open with > Choose default program...   In the window that opens, select IrfanView and make sure the box at the bottom to "Always use the selected program to open this type of file" and click OK.  This will open the photo in IrfanView from where you can print it.

Do the above for each file type you use for photos (mostly, photos will be *.jpg files).

Now when you want to print a photo, double-click it and it will open in IrfanView and you can print it from there.

******************************************************

You can probably change this behavior by editing the Windows Registry.  However, I don't have time to experiment with that at the moment and I don't want to suggest a change that I don't know will work.
