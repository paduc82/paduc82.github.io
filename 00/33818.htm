a. user say:

To add on to what others have said:

The issue is not whether a computer or any given hardware is compatible - it may well be. The issue is whether it's supported.

If Acer supports your computer on Windows 10, that means Acer has tested your computer on Windows 10, and has either deemed it compatible as-is or has provided updates, such as BIOS or drivers, that make it compatible.

As you might imagine, this is not a simple task. It requires an investment of time and money, particularly if Acer has to produce updates. Like other OEMs, Acer has to make a business decision about whether its investment is worthwhile.

If an OEM doesn't support its hardware on Windows 10, that means you have to do all the research and testing yourself. That's beyond most users' technical abilities, so lack of support is typically the end of the story.

On the other hand, if you're an amateur geek like me, and you just love to immerse yourself in all the details, you might be able to make an older computer work on Windows 10. My seven-year old Dell desktop works really well on Windows 10, even though Dell wouldn't touch it with a ten foot pole.
