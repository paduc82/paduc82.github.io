It seems to me that a more efficient approach would be to use an algorithm, recognizing that:

  1. The A weeks and the I weeks alternate; i.e. they have a natural date index of 14.
  2. The date in question will be either an A or an I depending on which date series has the lower differential from the closest but not larger series element.

Define the following variables:

  • thisC =Sheet1!A1                                 the active cell
  • thisR =ROW(thisC)                               the index into the dateQ column
  • thisQ =INDEX(dateQ,thisR)                 the date being considered
  • rootA =DATE(2016,1,4)                        the base date from which all A periods are scheduled
  • rootI =DATE(2015,12,28)                      the base date from whi

    Define the column in which you will put the dates to be categorized as: dateQ =Sheet1!$A:$A if its column A, for instance.  Then in the adjacent column put the formula "=AorI" in each cell opposite those cells holding a valid date.

    zorvek say:

    Before continuing much further, let's review what, exactly, you are trying to do.

    If you are trying to generate an "I" for every second week and an "A" for every other week. If so, then use this formula:

    =CHOOSE(MOD(TRUNC((J2-2)/7),2)+1,"I","A")

    This formula generates the exact same result as you have listed in your table.

    Here is the formula you say you want:

    =INDEX($H$2:$H$54,MIN(IF(($A$2:$A$54=J1)+($B$2:$B$54=J1)+($C$2:$C$54=J1)+($D$2:$D$54=J1)+($E$2:$E$54=J1)+($F$2:$F$54=J1)+($G$2:$G$54=J1)>0,ROW($A$2:$A$54)-1,"")))

    Enter by pressing CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER.

    Here is a formula that assumes the result will vary week by week:

    =INDEX(H2:H54,MATCH(J1,A2:A54))

    To remove an account, follow these steps:

    1. Open Outlook for Android.
    2. Go to Settings, then select the account you want to remove.
    3. Select Remove Account.
    4. Select From Device, (removes the account, but keeps the data) or From Device & Remote Data, (removes the account and all the information related to that account).

    To add an account, follow these steps:

    1. Open Outlook for Android.
    2. Go to Settings, then select the account you want to remove.
    3. Tap Add Accounts, then select Add Email Account.
    4. Enter your full email address, then tap Continue.
    5. Enter your email account password and tap Sign In or Next.
