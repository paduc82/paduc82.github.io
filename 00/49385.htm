james vinyard say:

If you bought it from Best Buy, that is the only place you can take it to for refund.

Micro-SD cards and USB pendrives; you have to buy those on your own.

When it comes to internal, primary storage, all tablets have small capacity SSD cards because the intent was to store just a few files and access the rest from the "cloud".  Of course, the Internet is not available everywhere and when off-line, that is when you usually find out that, that internal storage is just not enough.

If you don't have much data on the tablet, you can perform a RESET which reformats the free space and reinstalls the system and try again.  There is a possibility that the File Allocation Table on the SSD card is corrupted.  That is the only way to fix it.

I have the 32GB RT and moved all files onto a micro-SD card which I keep in the tablet all the time.  All documents (except a few), pictures, music, and four movies are all on it.  Not taking up any space on the tablet.  A good 32GB or 64GB micro-SD card (they come with an adapter to fit in a SD card slot) are pretty cheap and you can get a handful of those and you have the potential of unlimited storage.  You can also get an external hard disk as well.

You are not going to be using all files at the same time and only put on the tablet what you want to use at any particular time.  Some people want everything they have on the tablet and that is the wrong mindset for a tablet.  You can do that with a laptop but not a tablet.
