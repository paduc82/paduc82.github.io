photo blurry in Word

the troubleshooter say:

I use MS Word 2007 and found this glitch very annoying, but there are 2 workarounds. I saved a small 3 inch JPG in a photo editing program at 4.4.4 subformat with 100% quality and 96DPI so lines were crisp with no pixelation. I did not drag and drop or paste directly into Word, but used the 'Insert Picture' option. The glitch shows up immediately without needing to save or reopen the document. Anyway here are two workarounds:

 

Option #1- Select the picture, go to the 'Picture Tools' option that appears at the top of Word. Click 'Compress Picture', and click 'Options' on the pop-up.Uncheck 'automatically perform basic compression on save' and I also made the DPI match the inserted picture so I selected 'E-Mail (96PPI). I selected 'Apply to selected pictures only' and clicked 'OK'. This does work around the problem but sort of recompresses the picture a 2nd time. While it retains much of the original crispness of the picture, you will get a little pixelation (at least on the JPG I used). This will probably work for most people since the pixelation was only slightly noticable and then only on a crisp and shaded graph with simple colors. Photos will probably be difficult to notice this.

Option #2- Select the picture, go to the 'Picture Tools' option that appears at the top of Word. Select either 'Brightness' or 'Contrast'. In either case, highlight over any option that will either increase or decrease, but DO NOT CLICK to make the change! You should notice the picture tone will change but otherwise look like it should be. Now, hover back over '0% - Normal' and click that. There should be no pixelation and look like the original. This is the best and quickest workaround I have found.

I could not find anywhere in Word 2007 main options for a default picture/compression setting and you cannot adjust more than 1 picture at a time. There may be a better option but after alot of toying around these are all I could come up with. 

Also, not every "Picture Border' option keeps the original clarity. For basic color borders (non artsy) I have found border thicknesses that are in multiples of 1.5 kept the picture clarity... so 1.5, 3, 4.5, and 6 thickness will not cause the picture to degrade. Hope this helps

Heuristic scanning methods vary depending on the vendor. Some claim to allow emulation of the file's activities in a virtual sandbox. The disadvantage to using heuristics is that it is not as reliable as signature-based detection (blacklisting) and can potentially increase the chances that a non-malicious program is flagged as malicious.
