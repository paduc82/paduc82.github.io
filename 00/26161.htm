heidi B.

steve rindsberg say:

You'll get borders at sides or top/bottom whenever the aspect ratio of your slides doesn't match the aspect ratio of the screen you're displaying your presentations on.  

Aspect ratio is geekspeek for "Width divided by Height".  

So for example a 4:3 slide's aspect ratio is 4/3 or 1.333333

1280 / 960 also = 1.33333 so if you can choose a slightly different resolution as your final target, use 1280 x 960 and start with a 4:3 ratio presentation.

If you're completely locked into the other resolutions you mention, there may be other ways of making the video match up, but we'd need to know exactly how you're recording the video.  

For example, if you're using a tool that lets you choose the area or the window to record, that might help.
