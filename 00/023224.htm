bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Based on another interpretation of your question...

I get the impression that you may be expecting Word to actually scale the document content based on a different paper size. If I'm wrong, please disregard, but if I'm right, that isn't going to happen :-)

For example, let's say the document dimensions are currently 11"x8.5" & that there are 5 pages. You can change the dimensions as the others have described, but that will not have any affect on the content of the document. Font size, spacing, the size of images/graphics, etc. all will remain the same. As a result, the document will be forced to generate more pages to accommodate the content. Additionally, everything will be forced to reflow -- most likely having an adverse affect on the structure of the document. Also, any graphic objects larger than the new paper size may disappear altogether.

kosh vorlon say:

Hi Zwanzer,

 

Open MSE and click on the arrow beside Help and then click on About Security Essentials.  Check the Security Essential version number.  if it says 2.0.657.0 then you have the latest version.  If not, you should upgrade to that version.

 

To upgrade, simply install the most recent version.  To install, go to http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security_essentials/default.aspx and click on languages and locales at the bottom of the Download button and then choose your language and the appropriate version for your operating system.  When you download, save it to the desktop (rather than running it).  When finished, right click on the installation icon and click run as Administrator (even if you’re logged in as an administrator) as I’ve found this sometimes helps.

 

I hope this helps.

 

Good luck!
