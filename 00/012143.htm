cbarnhorst say:

Hard disk: Samsung DDR3 160Gb - C Drive 50Gb - D Drive 100Gb

How are you using Drive D:?

Partitioning is not often useful with NTFS formatting.  It was a method often used in the FAT32 days.  FAT32 used a fixed number of sectors and partitions above 32GB lost efficiency as the partitions got larger.  NTFS does suffer this problem and does not benefit from partitioning.

Also, partitioning and using C: for programs and D: for data on the same physical drive is inefficient because there is only one set of read/write heads and they have to travel more on average to read and write.  On the other hand, with two physical drives read/writes can occur simultaneously because the read/write heads of the two drives are independent (asynchroneous reads/writes). 

I would consider at a minimum repartitioning into a single volume.

Since you are using a tower another answer is to purchase a new, larger drive and transfer the system.  Most retail drives (not OEM) come with the software to do it.

If you decide to repartition or change drives, don't use the Disk Cleanup technique I suggested at this point because you should rollback to your previous OS and then make the hardware changes.  Then upgrade again.

(DDR3 is a memory specification, of course.)
