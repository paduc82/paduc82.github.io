Privacy in Windows / Office is tricky.  Setting up Windows to avoid OneDrive is a good start.  Buying One-time payment Office rather then Office 365 is another good step.

Note: Windows 10 and Office 2016/2019 have been set up to treat OneDrive as the primary location to save files. It takes some effort to break them of that habit.

In 2010 the Office license was primarily linked to the hardware it was installed on.  The email account is required to link your Office 2019 / 2016 / 365 license to your email account.  After Office 2019 is activated and installed on your computer, you no longer have to log in to the email account in Office. I never do.

No you cannot (easily) change the email account that your Office license is linked to. I have seen mentions of an "alias" process (basically what you described) to change your email account, but I have not tested it personally.  Basically, when MS designed it's ID system and linked Office licenses to it, apparently then never considered the possibility of someone wanting to change their email account.

Not you cannot disable Office updates. They are independent of Windows update, so those blocking techniques won't work.  Office update is run through the "Click to Run" environment that Office runs inside of.


I have not seen confirmation one way or the other that your email account is not embedded in your files. I don't think it is.  You could check for yourself.  Save a file to OneDrive, then "unzip" it.  Just rename the file to add the ".ZIP"  suffix to the end of the file. Then you can navigate through the various sub files and folders that create the file to see if you can find your email address embedded in any of them.

Update:

For anyone asking the same sort of questions, here are at least some answers and more definite info.

1) The MS account email will only be stored in the file's metadata (as far as I know) if you use that email as your Windows sign-in account I.E. use all the cloud-based stuff that they're pushing so hard these days. If you use a purely local account for Windows (I.E. the account is local to your computer only) and are signed out of your MS account online, then the file shouldn't have any trace of the email address. The file will have Author (Windows username) and other info (quite a lot of info) on the file, but anything that Inspect Document picks up can be cleaned out before you save. Alternatively you can run Inspect Document before you email the file to anyone.

I have no idea if there's anything else buried so deep in the file that Inspect Document doesn't show it, and MS isn't telling!  ¯\_(ツ)_/¯

Moral of the story:

Become better acquainted with Inspect Document (see link below) and when you install Office, click File > Options and go through every single blessed one to config things the way YOU want.

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Remove-hidden-data-and-personal-information-by-inspecting-documents-presentations-or-workbooks-356b7b5d-77af-44fe-a07f-9aa4d085966f

Also Google it.

2) Andre is correct, you can actually disable updates for Office 2019, and doing so in one program takes effect in them all. Click on File > Account to enable/disable. This is also where you can check whether or not you are signed in to your MS account online.

3) Yes, you can change/remove the email assocated with your MS account, but it's important to do it right... otherwise you might lose access to stuff afterwards. Follow the procedure in this article (it worked fine for me):

https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/help/12407/microsoft-account-change-email-phone-number

Pay attention to the section towards the bottom titled "I want to completely remove an email address or phone number" and note the warnings in the yellow box - if you have any MS service which is registered to the address you're about to remove, manually update that service with an alternative, alias email first!
