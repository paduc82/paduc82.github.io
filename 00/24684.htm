Purely for the sake of accuracy, you don't add a contact item to the AddressBook. If you add a new contact that appears in the AddressBook - it means that you've added the contact to a contact folder that is a member of the AddressBook.

The AddressBook is a specialized view of contact folders that have been designated to be a member of the AB. From your description, you imported your contacts into a contact folder which (1) was not your "default" contact folder and (2) the contact folder is not a member of the AB

karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

..................

However, on import of the .csv file produced I get the following result in Outlook:

All field names from the first row of the file are appended to one long string separated by semicolons on the left hand side of the dialogue window "Custom mapping of fields".

.........

I have no explanation for this which is why I ask for your help.

Means that the list separator character for your region is a semi-colon which is what's uses as the "field delimiter" when Excel saves a .CSV file. However, Outlook requires that the field delimiter be a comma.

Simplest solution

#1 - From Control Panel Regional Settings --> change your region to any country that uses a comma as a list separator (i.e. US)

#2 - Save the Excel data as a CSV file again

#3 - Import the file into Outlook

#4 - Change your regional settings back to what they were

You may see some people suggest editing your existing CSV file replacing all semi-colons with a comma - highly ill-advised and a great way to create all kinds of needless problems.
