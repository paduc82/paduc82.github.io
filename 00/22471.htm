mike middleton say: An excellent source is Excel chart guru Jon Peltier's web site, peltiertech.com, starting at http://peltiertech.com/Excel/Charts/ChartIndex.html

For your specific question, see his TechTrax article, Surface and Contour Charts in Microsoft Excel, at http://pubs.logicalexpressions.com/Pub0009/LPMArticle.asp?ID=447

Note the limitations of nearly-true 3d charts in Excel. The X and Y axes are category axes, so to avoid distortion the X and Y values must be equally-spaced. The data for the chart must be arranged in a grid (see Jon Peltier's TechTrax article).

After selecting the data, including the empty cell in the top left corner, in Mac Excel 2011 choose Charts > Column > 3-D Column, or Charts > Other > 3-D Surface, or Charts > Other > Wireframe 3-D Surface.

I've been running Office 2016 Preview (e.g. Outlook 15.11) and I got the same error myself today and any repeated attempts, whether ignoring or trying to activate Outlook/Office, would result in the crash. I got Outlook to work again by deleting the cached preferences via the following steps.

1. Open Terminal

2. Execute the following command to delete the preferences (without quotes): "defaults delete com.microsoft.Outlook"

3. Execute the following to delete the cache: "killall cfprefsd"

4. Relaunch Outlook. It will begin the steps to create or import account. I simply choose to skip this step and all my previous accounts and emails displayed again. You will have to go through Outlook preferences (Outlook --> Preferences...) to reset your customized settings, though.
