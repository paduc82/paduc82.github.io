- I was worried about the warning message at Step 3, but was reassured that I would not lose any of my email since the warning message onlyapplies to aliases on a Microsoft account with no hosted mailbox (ex: yahoo.com or gmail.com). Since my current inbox resides under hotmail.co.uk it was not affected by the deletion of the hotmail.com alias which forwarded email to hotmail.co.uk.

Good luck and I really do hope you get things sorted. I know how frustrating and worrying it can be.

1. In your browser, navigate to https://account.live.com/ManageAssocIds

2. Sign in with your new @outlook, @hotmail or @live account. (if you are already signed in with youroutlook.comaccount this step will not apply)

3. Under “Other email addresses”click theRemovelink below your previous address.

4. Click the Remove button to confirm the message.

5. You should now see a message on the page stating, “You've removed an email address from your Microsoft account.”

5a. Temporarily change your country to United States at https://account.live.com/EditProf.aspx(otherwise hotmail.com will not appear in the drop down in step 8)

6. Navigate to https://account.live.com/ChangeId.aspxin your browser.

7. Verify that “Create a new email address” is selected.

8. In the New Microsoft account box, enter your previous address and select your previous domain (hotmail.comorlive.com).Note: You must enter your previous address or the process will fail.

9. Select the Savebutton

Access Convert regular case to uppercase in all tables. - Microsoft Community

timothy (live.com#tim)1 say:

Access Convert regular case to uppercase in all tables.

I have several tables and need to convert the text from standard text to uppercase in all tables. it has been several years and I have forgotten more than I can remember about Access. Can someone help me with this problem?

john w. vinson say:

There's a builtin VBA function UCase() which will convert a lowercase or mixed case string to upper case.

However... this is a) a destructive change, since you can go from "Hans deVries" to "HANS DEVRIES" but you can't reliably go back; and b) it's probably not necessary! If you just want to DISPLAY data in upper case on a Form or Report, you can simply use a Format property of

">"

on the Textbox displaying the field; this will show it in upper case whatever the case in the table.

If you do want to permanently change the case, simply run an Update query updating each field to

=UCase([fieldname])

using your actual field name in square brackets. (If you leave off the brackets it will update your data to the literal string FIELDNAME!)

Welcome to Microsoft Community. I understand that you want to have a feature wherein specific emails will be moved to directly to your preferred folders.

 

You can create an email rules to work this, wherein certain emails from specific people or emails containing the same subject line will be moved automatically to your desired folder once your account received it. Here's how to do this:

 

If from specifif senders:

 

1. In your Outlook.com Inbox, click the gear icon  beside your Profile name.

2. Click More mail settings.

3. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.

4. Click New.

5. Under Step 1. Which messages do you want this rule to apply to? Select the Senders address, then is, type the email address of the contact which you want to receive email from in the text box provided.

6. Under Step 2. What action do you want to apply? Tick on Move to Inbox, then select the folder you want to receive the emails.

7. Click Save.

 

If you are going to use this email rule, you have to create email rule to each of your contacts to send their emails directly to the folder.

 

If emails have the same subject line or content:

 

1. Under Step 1. Which messages do you want this rule to apply to? Select the Subject, then contains word, type the common word/s that you are receiving in the text box provided.

6. Under Step 2. What action do you want to apply? Tick on Move to Inbox, then select the folder you want to receive the emails.

7. Click Save.

 

Note: You can change the email rules to your preferred options.
