Microsoft Access Binding Form field to table field - Microsoft Community

michael nesom say:

Microsoft Access Binding Form field to table field

I have a form which uses a calculation to produce a total the report prints out which becomes the customer receipt. The source for the table is the formula in the form to produce the total. I want the total to record in a table field but when I try to bind the table field to the form field there is no data source for the table and the data source for the form is the math formula to provide the calculation. I don't want the table to perform a calculation just record the end number of the total of the form

john w. vinson say:

Ummm...

No. You DON'T want the table to record the end number of the total.

Storing a total just leaves you open to data anomalies. If one of the underlying values in the table is changed, your total will now be WRONG, with no obvious way to detect that it is wrong, or what would be right.

Simply put the same expression in the control source of a textbox on the Report and redo the calculation. Computers are good at doing calculations; that's what they're FOR. Store the underlying data and do the calculation when you need it. If the form will always be open to the same data when the report is printed, you could also just use =[Forms]![YourForm]![YourCalculatedControl] as the Control Source of a textbox on the Report; but it's just about as easy to copy and paste the expression.

The one exception would be if you have a demonstrated - not assumed! - major performance problem doing the calculation (e.g. if it's a complex multirecord total requiring a lot of disk thrashing to get the values to sum). Even then, storing the total should be a last resort.

Ummm 

You are right, I don't, which is why I designed the data base to not calculate anything in the tables but rather to do all calculations in the forms, however, Revenue Canada does need me to be able to store the totals in a table so I can calculate how much money I have grossed. Which is why I want the table to store the NUMBER not as a calculation but just as a number

john w. vinson say:

Well, you can calculate your gross from the raw data stored in your table at tax time. But I agree, it may not be practical!

In that case, you can use a suitable Form event (such as the AfterUpdate event of a control which fills the last control needed in the calculation. Have a textbox (which can be invisible, if you wish) bound to the table field, txtStored let's say, and "push" the calculated value into it:

Private Sub controlname_AfterUpdate()

Me!txtStored = Me!txtTotal

End Sub

davidf2 say:

Try selecting the text in the text box > Home tab > Paragraph group and click the down arrow to see the Paragraph dialog. Note the line spacing settings. Most older versions of Publisher were set at 0 before and after paragraphs and 1 sp between lines. Many Pub 2013 templates now have defaults set at 1.14 or 1.19 sp and sometimes 9 pt after paragraphs. Try changing the setting to 1 sp and 0 before and after and see if that makes your spacing look better...or more normal to your eye.
