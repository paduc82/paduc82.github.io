Well, when you able to make it work on the first two sheets, I assume you are experienced enough to make it work on the 3rd sheets.

The logical conclusion is that not the method is wrong or that you do something wrong, the issue is in the data.

The formula above compares I39 with a date and a date in Excel is a number. But it is also possible to write a string into a cell that looks like a date, but in fact it isn't. I guess that is the issue on the 3rd sheet.

Go into the 1st sheet, select a cell with a date, press CTRL-1 and choose the General format, you'll see a number in the Sample field as in this screenshot:

Click Cancel, go into the 3rd sheet and try the same. I guess you'll see "a date" as sample:

That date isn't a date, it's a text. That's the reason why you failed.

You have to convert all date strings into real dates. Use a formula like
=DATEVALUE(A1)
into a helper column to get a real date from the date string, then copy and paste as value into the date column.
