john w. vinson say:

Take a look at the VBA help for the DateDiff() function. To get years of service it would be

DateDiff("yyyy", [Start Date], Date())

as a calculated field in a Query or as the control source of a form or report textbox.

Strictly speaking, this will get the number of year boundaries (midnight January 1) between the start date and today's date, so if you use it on January 3 for an employee hired December 29 it will show 1 year; post back if that's inappropriate.

Just for what it's worth, a Date/Time value is just a double float number - a count of days and fractions of a day (times) since midnight, December 30, 1899. Your subtraction will get the number of DAYS the employee has been in service.
