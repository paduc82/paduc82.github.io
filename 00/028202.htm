Welcome to the Microsoft community! My name is Alessandro, I'm an independent advisor and I'm here to help you!

I'm very sorry for this problem and I know what it is! For your account you are experiencing a "Spoofing" which is an improved technique used by current spammers who send emails forging the message. This is due to an attack on a possible vulnerability in the SMTP protocol (which is responsible for sending e-mail), although it is safe to say that the vulnerability allows anyone with an internet connection and a special program to send messages by changing the field "From" (sender) such as: You can receive a message with the sender "*** Email address is removed for privacy ***"

I will list here some causes of this problem:

1 - Computer infected by Worm virus

It may be that your computer or someone who has your email in the address book is infected with a Worm, which is a type of virus that automatically sends emails to all contacts in the program's address book and -mail, mainly Microsoft Outlook and Outlook Express, in order to try to multiply. Variations known as trojans may also behave similarly. Other types can even search for addresses typed in your Webmail, via temporary browser files. This allows the virus to create a contaminated message forging to be its own. To resolve, you need to scan your computer to find and eliminate the virus. We also recommend that you change your mailbox password and no longer use the email on your computer until you are sure it is "clean".
However, if your machine is not infected and you still receive returns of unsent messages, it is very likely that the problem is on the computer of someone who has your email registered in their address book, and there is not much what to do, because the solution will depend on that person.
To prevent and combat this problem it is recommended to keep your antivirus always up-to-date and active (recommended antivirus and antimalware). Certain antivirus and firewall programs detect and block when messages are sent with many destinations, and even many messages sent at one time.

2 - Your computer was attacked by Spoofing

Of course, it is possible that somebody sent a mass message as a direct mail that characterizes SPAM and took advantage of this strategy to disseminate the Spoofing mentioned above, which is to forge the sender of the message using any email address as a response to the possible errors. So Heidihi these Spammers use this sophisticated technique camouflaged and full of error messages with the sender containing your email address or the address of any account of your domain. This is because every time we send a message to an invalid / non-existent address, a bounce message is returned, and as Spammers typically fire messages to a huge email list, it is common for many of the addresses in this list to be invalid.

So to prevent this messages from being returned to the sender's own server it sets up any email to receive them, and you may have had the misfortune to be chosen.
Here are the top three situations where there is spoofing:
 - If you receive return messages from emails that appear to come from your account;
 - If you find spam messages from where the sender is your own email address
 - Receive responses from a message you never sent.

3 - Identifying whether the cause is Worm or Spoofing

 You will know and you will be sure after analyzing all the possibilities mentioned above, but to know where to start, notice the full header displayed in the contents of the error message received.
Reading the header from the bottom up, see if the first "Received:" has an address with the domain "xxx****". If it does, go to the DOS prompt and ping that address and ping the pop address [yourdomain.com].
Compare the IP addresses shown in each ping and if they are the same, it means that the message was triggered by your mail server, meaning your machine is possibly infected. If the IPs are different, it may be some vulnerability related to the allow_url_fopen directive, so it's worth checking the schedule for your site.
Otherwise, it is very likely that you have suffered a spoofing or that someone you know caught some virus that is trying to multiply.

How to solve this problem:

To reduce the chances of you experiencing this problem is the possibility that you do not activate the nickname * (asterisk) or catch-all, as this causes your account to receive messages sent to any electronic address of your domain, even if it does not exists.

To minimize receiving these messages in the inbox we also suggest enabling the SPAM tools on your domain accounts by following the steps in the link below:

Private Sub UserForm_Activate()
 'Set some of the properties for the ListView
       'Set some of the properties for the ListView
   
   
    With Me.ListView3
        .Gridlines = True
       
       
        .View = lvwReport
    End With
   
    'Call the sub to fill the ListView
    Call LoadListView
   
End Sub

Private Sub LoadListView()

    'Declare the variables
    Dim wksSource As Worksheet
    Dim rngData As Range
    Dim rngCell As Range
    Dim LstItem As ListItem
    Dim RowCount As Long
    Dim ColCount As Long
    Dim i As Long
    Dim j As Long
   
    'Set the source worksheet
    Set wksSource = Worksheets("Data")
   
    'Set the source range
    Set rngData = wksSource.Range("A6").CurrentRegion
   
    'Add the column headers
    For Each rngCell In rngData.Rows(2).Cells
        Me.ListView3.ColumnHeaders.Add Text:=rngCell.Value, Width:=60
                
    Next rngCell
   
    'Count the number of rows in the source range
    RowCount = rngData.Rows.count
   
    'Count the number of columns in the source range
    ColCount = rngData.Columns.count
   
    'Fill the ListView
    For i = 6 To RowCount
        Set LstItem = Me.ListView3.ListItems.Add(Text:=rngData(i, 1).Value)
        For j = 2 To ColCount
            LstItem.ListSubItems.Add Text:=rngData(i, j).Value
        Next j
    Next i
   
End Sub

You could try a factory reset and if this doesn't work go for the hard reset (try thr phone without any apps for a while before intalling one ap at a time)
