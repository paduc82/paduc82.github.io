Just a few thoughts...

Might be worth a try to check your Printer Preferences from Control
Panel very closely...there may be more than one place to set the
default paper size. Check all the options that say...Default, Advanced,
Preferences, Properties, etc...

The Printing Wizard should recognize the default paper size if all
the settings are the same.

Maybe the documents are being auto-cropped to fit the templates
in the Printing Wizard. This can occur if the aspect ratio of the scan
is not equal to the print template. If your Photo Wizard has an option
to print a 'Full Page Fax Print'....it may be worth a try because it
should not auto-crop.

Some programs have a 'Shrink To Fit' or 'Scale To Fit' option that
will force the complete photo to print even if the aspect ratio is
different. (FWIW...the freeware Picasa has a 'shrink to fit' option)

If all else fails...you may need to update your printer driver.

The question I have is this: each laptop will be re-imaged many times before it's taken out of service - does every single activation of the exam image use one of our 500 up? Or does the MS activation server remember each laptop and so re-use the same activation? Also, once the laptop has been taken out of service, can the activation be reset so that it can be used with the new laptop purchased to replace it?

Thanks,

Tim

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
If the hardware and software is the same, I do not believe that multiple activations are different than a single activation.  However, the Activation Server does keep track of the frequency of reactivations and can balk if too frequent (although what constitutes "too frequent" isn't publically defined).  If I were you, I'd speak to the school's Microsoft liaison and obtain a suggestion on the best approach to this issue.  I'm sure the school must have one if they are a volume license customer.

donpick tls say: KMS is not dependant upon AD domain membership - so you can do this just fine on your workgroup machines.
If you do choose to use MAK instead of KMS, yes, every MAK activation will consume irreversibly one of your 500 activations. MAK activations are never reclaimed/returned.
MAK activations are different from Retail activations, in that Retail activations permit re-activation on the same hardware numerous times.
