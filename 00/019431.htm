Are we talking about footnotes or footers? In either case, the likelihood is that a negative left and/or right indent has been added to the footer/footnote paragraph or style. The styles in question are Footer and Footnote Text.

Right-click in the affected paragraph and choose Paragraph. If the Paragraph dialog shows a negative indent (left or right), you can correct it in a given paragraph, but a better choice is to right-click in the paragraph and choose Style, then Modify, then Format | Paragraph. Your computer is nothing more than an organized pile of metals, oil and sand without an Operating System.  You have to buy one, such as Windows. All consumer computers come with one installed at the factory. You have paid for it in the price of the computer. less than if you bought it retail.

The operating system includes some additional "free" features beyond what is required to simply make the computer run. Windows has included programs like NotePad, WordPad, Minesweeper.

Most computer builders include "free" programs collectively and colloquially known as "crapware" on the computer. Some of it is their own custom programs, much of it is a "time-limited free trial" that the manufacturer pays to put on the computer. That commonly includes antimalware (like Norton Anti-Virus or others) and MS Office.

Any other specialized programs you want on the computer have to be paid for and installed. That includes MS Office. 

IF the ad for your computer says it "comes with" MS Office, read the fine print.  99.99% of the time the fine print will say that it is the free time limited trial.

If you believe you already paid for a full license, take a close look at your invoice/bill. Office would be listed as a separate line item.
