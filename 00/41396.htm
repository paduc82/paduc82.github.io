Hi Sheng,

The Unmountable Boot Volume error on your computer could be due to errors on the hard drive. Such errors can be fixed by running the CHKDSK command. CHKDSK is a Windows utility that can check the integrity of your hard disk and can fix various file system errors. To do this, follow the steps below:

  1. Press and hold the power button for a few seconds, laptop would shut down.
  2. Remove the battery of your laptop. Make sure to unplug the power cord before doing this.
  3. Turn on your laptop and ensure that the USB is already connected to the device.
  4. Check if you can boot from the Windows Recovery Environment.
  5. If you were able to boot from the USB drive.
  6. Click on the Repair your computer option located at the lower-left corner of your screen.
  7. On the next screen, click on Troubleshoot.
  8. On the Troubleshoot Screen, click on Advanced options.
  9. On the Advanced Options screen click on Command Prompt.
  10. Once you are in Command Prompt window, type chkdsk c: /r /f and press Enter.


Note: Wait patiently, as the CHKDSK function checks your C: drive for errors. Remember, this can take a long time and you need to give your computer all the time that it requires to fix the errors on its hard drive. After the chkdsk function is completely finished, restart your computer and see if you can boot directly from your hard drive.
