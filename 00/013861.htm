danida_u say:

Hello DaveOrlando,

Skype doesn't offer subscriptions for SMS. Sent SMS will be charged with your Skype credits and will not be covered by a calling subscription.

If I get your concern correctly, you want the IM you sent from your device to be receive on your daughter's device though you and your daughter are logged in with the same account, please correct me if I am wrong. We do not advice two users to be logged in to one account especially if the two users are residing on two different locations. Skype might detect that as an unusual activity and therefore temporarily restrict the account.

It would be better if each one of you have separate accounts. Also subscriptions cannot be shared or transfer from one account to another and it is subjected to a fair usage policy.

Thanks!

Danida_U

daveorlando say: Thanks for your reply, Danida. You've answered my question! :)

For anyone finding this post in the future, this is what I did without knowing any better. I signed up with my name & login then purchased the unlimited calling plan thinking I'd be able to communicate with my daughter while she was in Europe all on that account. I would send a test invite from her Europe phone (an unlocked GSM phone with a European SIM card in it from Telestial) to my American phone (which was also logged into that same account), and that invite would show up as a notification on her phone but not mine. It turned out that I was receiving the invites on my phone (since we were logged into the same account), but my notifications went to text and automatically showed up as "read". Tapping on the link on my phone got me nowhere.

I created a separate account for my American phone, logged into that, then sent an invite from her phone to mine, and it went through as expected. What I didn't bother to do (and should have) was to create a third Skype account so my wife could be her own entity in our 3-way conversations. This means that when I was logged into my account on my phone plus that same account on my wife's phone, anytime either of us would sent a text to our daughter, it would show up on each of our Skype apps, and vice-versa. Not so much of a big deal, but it did get confusing for her to know who was talking to her.

As Danida pointed out, it's best for each person to have their own account to avoid all this confusion. Incidentally, because I signed up with my information & paid for the call plan on my account on my daughter's European phone, anytime she used Skype to speak to anyone, my name showed up instead of hers as the caller/texter. A little weird, but we just rolled with it. But it's another reason why everyone should have their own account, and the account you put a plan on should be in the name of that person.

_elaine_ say:

Hi, DaveOrlando

To clarify and avoid any further confusion:

It is not only best practice, but also absolutely imperative that each of your family members have their own Skype (Microsoft) accounts.  Skype does not work like the party telephone lines of the 1950's and1960's!  People can not speak with each other or send messages back and forth with one another using a single account.

Next tip: All calls between Skype users are free of charge, so if everyone is on a Wi-Fi network, there is no need for a subscription or credit.  However, sending text messages (SMS, as opposed to instant messages within the Skype app) requires Skype Credit.  Reply SMS (text messages) will be received by the Skype accountholder's mobile number.

Incidentally, because I signed up with my information & paid for the call plan on my account on my daughter's European phone, anytime she used Skype to speak to anyone, my name showed up instead of hers as the caller/texter.

My only concern here is that you set up a new Microsoft/Skype account using daughter's mobile phone number, therefore that number is tied to a Microsoft account in your name.  As you said so well, roll with it for now and sort the details with editing the account details after your daughter returns home.

I am sorry the process of setting up new accounts and adding contacts was not clearer from the start.  Thank you for sharing your story and for your feedback!

Kind regards,

Elaine
