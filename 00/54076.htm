ethernet vs wifi - Microsoft Community

marielisabrown say:

ethernet vs wifi

hi there cant seem to get an answer from vm but, should my wifi security work on ethernet? everything seems good to go, just not sure about my security?, in my device manager it has a yellow key beside my security? but erm I think this should be ok if im on vm security?

a. user say:

For security, Ethernet is far superior to WiFi. However, WiFi beats Ethernet for convenience.

It is possible to secure WiFi against all but the most determined, sophisticated attacker, but that requires a level of technical knowledge that most people don't have. Nevertheless, it's not rocket science. The average person can learn how to protect a WiFi network if they have the desire to do so.

There are three elements to protecting a WiFi network:

1- Give your WiFi network an uncrackable password.

2- Use the strongest possible method for encrypting the data in the radio signals. Currently the strongest encryption method is known as WPA2-AES. The WPA2 component will soon be upgraded to a newer method called WPA3.

3- Password-protect the router's menus (known as the 'administration console') with a different, uncrackable password, so no one is able to change your network password or switch to a weaker encryption method.

But even with all this, it's possible to unwittingly compromise your WiFi network's security by making an unwise change to your router's settings. That's why you need to understand your router's many configuration settings well enough to avoid putting your network at risk.

Fortunately, most people's WiFi networks are just not that interesting to an attacker, so they're not worth the effort for an attacker to crack them. That's what makes WiFi secure enough for most people to use, if they know how to take the right precautions.

Ethernet is very different. Your data isn't being transmitted through the air in radio signals for anyone to receive. It's all in wires (Ethernet cables.) Unless an attacker is somehow able to connect their device to your network with an Ethernet cable, your network is completely safe without passwords or encryption (although it's always a good idea to password-protect your router's administration console.)

If you're concerned that an unauthorized person (e.g., roommate, house guest) might try to attach their device to your wired network with an Ethernet cable, password-protecting the network with an uncrackable password will stop them.
