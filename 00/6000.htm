ynnhoj gnahz say:

Hi Mark,

Thanks for the reply.

Here are the detailed steps:

1. Deactivate the AAD Connect for the old domain. Then the accounts will become Office 365 accounts and you are manage them directly in Office 365.

2. Add and verify the new domain in Office 365.

3. Change the primary address of the account to the new domain. For your reference:

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Change-a-user-name-and-email-address-in-Office-365-fb5ac074-e203-4e1f-9843-b9d1a3e03297

4. Create the user in your AD with the address your change in step3.

5. Install AAD Connect with the new domain and use SMTP matching to match on-premises user accounts to Office 365 user accounts for directory synchronization. For your reference:

https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/2641663/how-to-use-smtp-matching-to-match-on-premises-user-accounts-to-office-365-user-accounts-for-directory-synchronization
