Hello Season_Hou

A very general explanation is that every MS Account has an Outlook.com email account associated with it. Things get shared among devices from an <Email Account> or more specifically, when it comes to People (Contacts) and Calendar, an <Email Account> that is configured as an Exchange account (which is the default for an Outlook.com account). However, the <Exchange> part of this explanation only applies if you were to add the Outlook.com account to the Outlook desktop program which is part of the Office 365 suite. So with all that extraneous information, the answer to your questions are as follows:

Re: #1 - In terms of People and Calendar, any items added under the MS Account are stored locally only, in other words, just on your PC. If you save the contacts/calendar items to "Outlook" - those would be accessible by other devices as long as the Outlook.com account as added to the device.

Re: #2 - Items stored on Web only when saved to "Outlook" when in People/Calendar

Re: #3 - You can't, you need to save the items to "Outlook" and then on your Android device, you need to add the Outlook.com account by entering the email address/password for the account which, in this case, is the same password as you enter for your MS Account on your PC

Would suggest that you add Outlook from the Google Play Store to your phone and then add the Outlook.com account to the Outlook app.

Please feel free to ask any clarification questions you may have if any of the above is not clear.

steve rindsberg say:

I think you're on the right track, but watch out for shape outlines. 

1) Make sure the background shape you're adding has no outline

2) When adding the background shape, compensate for the outlines of any shapes in the group.  See, the outline of a shape sits half inside, half outside the shape itself (ie, if the shape is 100 points wide and has a ten point outline, it'll actually appear 110 points wide on the slide).  Try making the outlines REALLY big on the shapes in a test group ... 10 or 20 points ... then run your code to add the background shape.  See if that doesn't make the "AHA" light go on and twirl your solar powered propeller beanie.   ;-)

steve rindsberg say:

Please re-read my last reply.  It's the line width of the individual shapes within the group that I'm referring to, not the group itself.

If you have a 100 pt square starting at 0 left, 0 top and another starting at 100, 0 (left,top)  and both have a 10 point outline, when you group them, the group will be at 0 left, 0 top, 100 height, 200 width but the shapes within will be 5 points additional in each direction.  That can make your exports weird if you put a background shape in at exactly the coordinates of the group shape.

Try it manually to see what I mean.
