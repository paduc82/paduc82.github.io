You certainly can do that ... and it's a great idea because you can backup C: and D: independently.

Let's do Documents, as the same procedure applies to Music, Pictures and Videos

  • In File Explorer navigate to C:\Users\ <your account>\Documents
  • Right-click on the Documents icon and select Properties
  • Switch to the Location tab
  • Move the default Documents folder to D:

You got the rest.

Your personal files won't generally benefit from the increased speed of a SSD, so you won't lose anything by way of performance by taking them off the SSD.
