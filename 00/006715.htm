=SUMPRODUCT(MAX((M:M<>"")*ROW(M:M)))

=OFFSET(G1,SUMPRODUCT(MAX((M:M<>"")*ROW(M:M)))-1,0)

If that is correct it is a nifty little "feature" of Windows 8 Refresh.  Out of the box it uses a backup that does not include any of the desktop applications you installed. Refresh is designed to 'ignore' Metro apps (don't ask how, I haven't seen any explanations). 


The immediate fix to your problem is simple. Re-install Office 2007, and any other "old" desktop apps.


The longer term fix is to learn about "RecImg".  The MS has included a tool called RecImg to fix the 'design flaw' in refresh. But the brain trust in marketing didn't bother mentioning it to the general public.


The issue is that the new Win8 Refresh uses a "hidden" backup image to restore from.  Right now, the image it is working from is "factory fresh" without your installed desktop applications.  RecImg creates new image files for Refresh to use that include you installed desktop apps.

yoga yao msft say:

Hi David,

 

Based on your description, you would like to select multiple worksheets in Excel for Mac. You can follow the steps below to achieve it:

  1. To select two or more adjacent sheets: Click the tab for the first sheet> hold down SHIFT and click the tab for the last sheet that you want to select.
  2. To select two or more nonadjacent sheets: Click the tab for the first sheet> hold down COMMAND and click the tabs of the other sheets that you want to select.
  3. To select all sheets in a workbook: Hold down CONTROL and click a sheet tab, and then click Select All Sheets on the shortcut menu.
