Not a problem if it goes green.  The network connection coming up late is also not a problem.

Perform these checks:

Verify that you are using version 2.0.657.0 of MSE (as of 2010-12-16).

    Publisher inserts text as Calibri or changes to Calibri - Microsoft Community

    danielhollaz say:

    Publisher inserts text as Calibri or changes to Calibri

    Can someone tell me how to stop Publisher 365 from changing the font to Calibri?? I use Times New Roman generally, or Book Antiqua. 

    Often times I paste text into an open publication in Times NR, but the program inserts it as Calibri.

    Other times, it will insert it as Times New Roman, but when I start typing after the inserted text Publisher will have switched to Calibri font for some reason.

    Then I have to stop what I'm doing, highlight the Calibri characters, then change them to Times NR or whatever. Lots of wasted time! 

    Thanks for any help. 

    davidf2 say:

    Many of the templates in Publisher have Calibri as the default font. If you want the pasted text to keep the original or source font then there are several ways. Publisher has a wealth of pasting options. You just have to invest some time learning them.

    Home tab > Paste and click the down arrow > Paste Special and opt for formatted text to retain the font. Opt for unformatted text to take on the default font of the text box.

    Right click > mouseover the different paste options.

    Go ahead and paste and look for the clipboard icon with (ctrl) beside it on the border of the text box > click the down arrow > Keep source formatting to keep the font of the text unchanged, or keep text only to take on the default font of the text box.

    You can also just Ctrl+A to select all in the text box and then change the default font so that when you start typing again it will be your preferred font.

    You can also save a text box that is formatted with the font you prefer and then in the future insert it into any new publication. Right click the formatted text box > Save as a building block. Then to insert that text box go to Insert > Page Parts.

    There are even more options but play with these and you will find a best way for your work flow.

    DavidF
