sivashankarsuganthi say:

How to terminate word process by deleting temporary files

After closing the word document, i still find the temporary file(Example : ~$60021-USERMANUAL_Rev1.docx) present. How to remove these temporary files? Can they be deleted? If deleted will the original document get affected?

macropod say:
Some such temporary files will remain until you both close the document and exit Word. Indeed, you may find it impossible to delete those files until you exit Word.

charles kenyon say:
If you find such files after you have closed Word (not just the document) you can safely delete them --

UNLESS you are sharing the workspace with another use who may have documents open.

This is not the Word "process" but a leftover file, usually from an unexpected termination of the Word process. These are 'locking" files intended to prevent the same document from being opened simultaneously by multiple users. I understand that Word 2013 actually locks paragraphs rather than documents but have not experimented with that.

Word usually terminates its process handily, even if it leaves such a file. If the Word process, itself, is open without a Word window, the only way I know of to terminate it short of restarting the computer is to use the Task Manager in Windows under the process tab. This is very unusual, though.
