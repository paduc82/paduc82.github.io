Originally I had it like this

=IF(D15=0,"$0.00",IF(E15<=TODAY(),C15-D15,"$0.00"))

which was fine but the conditional formatting didn't recognize it as a "0", so I changed it back.

Is there a way to have the cell convert the result "0" from the formula to "$0", or will I have to just change the conditional formatting to look for "$0.00"?

bernie deitrick say:

Formatting applies to numbers, not strings - with your formula, you are returning strings.

Try, instead, this formula, with the cell formatted as currency:

=IF(D15=0,0,IF(E15<=TODAY(),C15-D15,0))

Also, have the CF look for 0, not "0"  - in Excel the number 0 is not the same as the string "0" - formatting is rarely needed in a function unless you are looking for a specific string.
