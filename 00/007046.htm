You're in one of the numerous situations for which a saved disk image backup of a previous build made by a 3rd party imaging program (and saved on a different drive) can get you back in the game in a few minutes, since the bootable rescue medium doesn't depend on having a working OS.

It only takes a few minutes to make such an image, and only a few minutes to restore to the previous working OS build.

When one is working with insider builds, good practice requires making and saving an image of the current public release as well as each of the previous one or two successful insider build(s). Then, when a hard drive fails or an installed new build goes sour, it's no big deal; simply boot the rescue medium and restore the desired OS build (including all of the programs that were installed when the image was made).

(Think of the former test pilot who insisted that he didn't need a parachute, and right up to, but not including, his final flight, his stance proved to be correct.)

I presume you've tried booting with nothing but a known good keyboard (substituted) plugged into a USB port? Example: Recently, a USB wireless adapter caused such an issue, and when removed, allowed normal USB operation for the mouse and keyboard. 

Although you didn't specify, I presume you don't have any 3rd security program installed, since Microsoft discourages installing any 3rd party active security program in the insider builds, because the developers of them cannot keep up with insider build changes to insure compatibility. A 3rd party program that seemed to work in one build might not work in the next one, and some users get tripped up by that.

An SD card becomes a part of your phone. It is meant to installed and left that way.

A Microsoft account gives you OneDrive storage. You have access to the storage but it is not part of the phone storage. It is not storage space on your phone.

Always use a name brand SD card like SanDisk, they are not expensive. SD card shopping is full of cheap and counterfeit cards that fail quickly.

Get a class 10, UHS-1 card.  A 32GB or 16GB card can probably do what you need. Do the math.

SD card and apps - System apps can not be moved to an SD card. Other apps you currently have installed can be moved to a new card. The data for those apps will be moved also. This is why you use a name brand card.
