apollo p say:

I believe the equivalent in Word 2007 to Quickwords in Word Perfect would be AutoText.  The AutoText feature has changed from Word 2003 to Word 2007 so this wouldn't apply to both versions.

Here is how to add this type of text to AutoText:

- Select the text you wish to save in AutoText

- Click on the Insert Tab, then click on the Quick Parts drop down

- Click on Save Selection to Quick Part Gallery

- When the Create New Building Block box opens, click the drop down for Gallery and change it to AutoText.  You can change the name to whatever you wish.  Then, click OK.

Ok, now the AutoText is saved.  I find the easiest way to organize it is to add the AutoText drop down list to your Quick Access Toolbar.  You can do this by clicking on the Office Button, then click on Word Options. Once in Word Options, click on the Customize section on the left side.  From here you can click the drop down for "Choose commands from" to choose All Commands and then scroll down until you find AutoText.  Once you select AutoText, click the Add button to add it to the Quick Access Toolbar.

This sounds like alot, but you only have to add the AutoText button to your Quick Access Toolbar once.  You will see the icon on the top row next to the save, undo and redo buttons (looks like a hand over a keyboard).  You can click on the button and choose from all of your autotext entries.

marvs barce say:

Hello Nancy,

Thank you for sharing your concern to the Microsoft Community. We recommend that you follow these steps in changing the language to default for the pages of the browser:

  1. Open Internet Explorer.
  2. Click Tools then click the Internet Options.
  3. Click Languages, under the General tab then click Add.
  4. Select the language that you would like to add.
  5. Click on OK.

Also, you can move the priority of the recently added message. Simply, move it to top of the list in the displayed languages. Follow these steps:

  1. From within the Language Preference window, select the language you would like to have priority.
  2. Click Move up repeatedly until the language is displayed at the top of the list.
  3. Click OK.
  4. Close Internet Explorer.
  5. Open Internet Explorer.

Let us know how it goes.
