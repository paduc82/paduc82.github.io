ghill say:

I have a Samsung Blackjack, my PC is running XP with SP2. What I had to do was go into the Windows registry and change a few values. As usual, going into the registry is AT YOUR OWN RISK!!! DO NOT DO THIS IF YOU ARE NOT COMFORTABLE WORKING IN THE REGISTRY! ALWAYS BACKUP THE REGISTRY BEFORE CHANGING ANYTHING! (File, Export, Save As...)

 

Getting into the registry:

 

From the desktop of your PC, click Start, then Run, then type in "regedit" and click OK. Left-click once on My Computer to select it (if not already). From the menu at the top, click Edit, then Find. In the "Find What" box, type in the name you wish to change (that annoying name in Active Sync you wish to get rid of). Type this name EXACTLY as is displayed in Active Sync. For instance, mine was "WM_HP_Administ1". Make sure that the Keys, Values, and Data boxes are all checked and the "Match whole string only" box is not. Then click Find Next.

 

Once it is done searching the registry, there will be a registry item that is highlighted. Right-click this and choose "Modify". Here, you can change that name to something like "My Blackjack" or whatever you want to display as your phone name in Active Sync. WARNING: Be sure to change ONLY that portion of text and nothing else! For example, mine said: "\Microsoft\ActiveSync\Profiles\WM_HP_Administ1" and I changed it to say "\Microsoft\ActiveSync\Profiles\My_Blackjack". You have to include the "underscore" symbol instead of a space. Don't ask why, I just figured it out by trial and error.

 

There will be other places you will need to do this. Press the F3 key (find next shortcut) and it will go to the next item. Do the same thing as described above. I only had to change the name in 3 places of the registry, should be the same for you...

 

Turning off Active Sync on your phone:

 

On my phone, I went Start, Settings, Connections, USB. There I could switch between Active Sync, Mass Storage, or Modem. Here you would select Mass Storage. If you choose this method, it will not allow you to access your removable storage device such as an SD Card. You have to go into Active Sync and click Explore.

 

You can do that if you want, but you could also do this in the connection settings in Active Sync. Click File, Connection Settings, then uncheck the box that says "Open ActiveSync when my device connects". Or, you could go into Tools, Options, then uncheck every item.

 

Once everything is done, make sure your phone is disconnected from the PC. In Active Sync, click File, Delete Mobile Device. When you reconnect your phone, it should take you through the setup. If it asks you to give your phone a name type in what you changed it to earlier. Mine was "My_Blackjack" (without the quotations, obviously).

 

GOOD LUCK!

el_loco---01 say: Resolutions:

Not all resolutions are supported. The ones I know that should work:

QCIF 176x144
Sub-QCIF 179x96
CIF 352x288
QVGA 320x240
VGA 640x480 (But only for MPEG4 codec)
Some of these seem to work in MPEG4

320x112
640x396

RealVideo only QVGA and QCIF are supported and only 128 kbps, I think that 256 kbps starts to have problems.

H264 (MPEG4 AVC) only QVGA resolution is supported and it has to be Baseline profile is supported. Many encoders only support Main profile and that's why they don't play. Also some videos for Ipod use the Sorenson codec which is not supported.
See here:
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sorenson
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H.264/MPEG-4_AVC

MPEG1/2, Quicktime MOV, WMV and Flash Video (FLV) are not supported.

Further as korngear said you can add DIVX and xVID support by downloading the DIVX player or purchasing CorePlayer or Smartmovie.

el_loco---01 say: I forgot to mention the bitrates and framerates.

In general you can trade off bitrate for framerate and viceversa. You can get up to 30 fps working in MPEG4 if the bitrate is not too high.

I use 25 fps and about 1Mbps bitrate for MPEG4
25-30 fps and 750 kbps for H264 (QVGA)

Also h263 is supported as a codec in the 3GP or 3G2 container.

Extensions have to be:

.3GP
.3G2
.RM
.RMVB
.MP4
.M4V
.3GPP
.3GP2

But you have to use the above mentioned codecs, resolutions, bitrates and framerates. AND not all possible combinations work.
