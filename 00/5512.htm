conradargo say: I doubt that this is possible seeing as how your Profile seem to overrun any other settings. If you have your phone set to Silent or Meeting or whatever - then it's going to stay quiet regardless of who calls you.

gordonshowers say: Hi Jangle, yes you can.

1) Create a contact group. You do this by running the Contacts app, then pressing the right joypad button. Call the group whatever you like, but something like "VIPs" would do!

2) Add the person (can be more than one if you like) to this group.

3) Create a new profile called something like "VIPs only" and set it to vibrate & ring however you want it when the special people call you. The important setting is near the end. Set "Alert for" to be just the group called "VIPs". Make sure that the "All calls" box is NOT ticked.

When you set your profile to "VIPs only" then the phone will be completely silent and will not vibrate for everybody, except those in your "VIPs" group. For them, the phone will behave how you set the profile up.

Please note that the phone will still tell you that there is a call for everyone that phones you, but it will be silent.

charles kenyon say:

Diego means well. Neither safe mode nor repairing will help with this. I suspect he does not understand the relationship between recorded macros and the Normal template. Starting in safe mode starts Word without your Normal template and without any other files that might contain macros. Repairing fixes problems with Word's installation files but does nothing to fix any missing customizations.

You may or may not be able to get your normal template back. Regular backups of this template are a very good idea. I usually do one a month.

You can, and should, store your precious customizations elsewhere. The only advantage to storing macros in the normal template is that they are easily editable. Otherwise, storing them in a different Global Template will make them safer and more portable.

Moving (Sharing) Customizations in Microsoft Word

How to try to find your template:

That said, with Word closed, take a look in your user templates folder.

Where is the Normal Template / How to Find the Normal Template

Look for any file with Normal as part of its name, including normal.wbk. Observe the dates.

Try, one at a time, renaming a copy of any other file with "normal" in the name normal.dotm. Open Word and see if you have any luck. Start with the file with the latest modification date first.
