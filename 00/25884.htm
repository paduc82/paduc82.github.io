    For starters, your criteria line should be

    IS NULL

    rather than an empty string "". NULL is a funny beast: it means "no value, undefined". A literal "" is not the same - it's precisely defined, as string value of zero length.

    If File_No is not a Primary Key (or otherwise has a unique index) in tble_files, you'll need to change the JOIN to join the two tables on some unique key (which might consist of two or more fields). How are your tables related in the Relationships window? What are the two tables' Primary Keys?

    I am unable to minimize my document. - Microsoft Community

    marydaz say:

    I am unable to minimize my document.

    When I click on the box to minimize the document it  moves slightly and then two boxes show up. 
    suzanne s. barnhill say:
    When the middle button shows the two boxes, then its function (as shown by the ScreenTip) is Restore Down. What this actually does is change from a Maximized window to a Normal window. If the Normal window has been sized to fill the screen, then you won't see any difference. But when the document is restored down, you can drag the edges to make it smaller.

    suzanne s. barnhill say:

    I think you're confusing the Minimize button with the Maximize button. You should see three buttons in the top right corner. The first, which has just a line (like a minus sign) is for Minimize. The second is the button that toggles between Maximize and Restore Down. The "two boxes" you're seeing are the Restore Down button. If you didn't see much difference between the normal ("restored") window and the maximized window, then you probably had the window dragged to cover most or all of the screen. If you click the first button, you should Minimize.

    Alternatively, you can just click the Taskbar button of an open window to minimize it and again to restore it.

    beth melton say:

    When you change your Word options, it's important to make sure you have a single document window open. When there's more than one, the last document window you close when you exit Word is the one that updates the Registry. In other words, if you have two documents open, make your Options changes in one, close it, and then later close the other, that's the one that makes the updates.

    Try making your change again with only one document window and then exit Word. Then start it again and verify the change has stuck this time.
