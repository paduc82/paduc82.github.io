Outlook.com does not appear to offer an option to disable the display of ads on the right pane of the Outlook email page.

 

When you read email in your Inbox, information about the sender is often displayed in the right pane (people pane) instead of ads if that person is on Facebook, Twitter, or other social networking services and  the information is publicly available via the sender's email address. In other cases, the sender's contact information from your People page will be displayed.

 

If you connect your Facebook, Twitter, or other social networking accounts to outlook.com, then additional information about the sender may be displayed instead of the ads.

 

Internet Explorer 9 and 10 have a feature called "Tracking Protection" that some claim can be used to block ads using tracking protection lists available for download from the Internet. You can search the Internet to find more information.

 

Other browsers offer add-ons that are designed to block ads using regularly updated blocking lists. You can search the Internet to find more information.

 

I am not stating that either of my above suggestions will or will not block ads on outlook.com, but are simply options for you to research further if you wish.

 

As always, be very careful when visiting websites you do not know and when downloading any programs or files from the Internet.

 

That being said, I too have used ad-blockers on sites that display obtrusive graphical ads, over-use ads that slow down my browser, or display ads that may link to malware. Outlook.com is using text based ads, they do not seem to slow down my browser, and they don't distract from the primary web product (email/messaging).

 

Websites and free email services display ads to generate revenue to pay the costs of operating the service (and occasionally actually make a little profit). I don't begrudge them this as long as it does not ruin my browsing experience. I think outlook.com's approach to ads is much better than Hotmail's and other major free email providers.

 

This is just my opinion. I'm sure many others will disagree with me and they will be right (for them).

 

I hope this helps or answers your question.
