cmdrkeene say:
Sharing and Security Permissions are basically the same thing.  If a folder has more restrictive permissions that its parent folder, and it's either shared or in an already-shared hirearchy, meaning it's "less shared" than the parent, it will have a padlock icon indicating that it's private.  Every folder that's private won't have a padlock, (subfolders of the private folder, even though they are also private, won't show padlocks, because then the screen would be full of lock icons).

It's indeed weird and not very intuitive at all.
