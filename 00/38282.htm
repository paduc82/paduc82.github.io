Where to find audio track? - Microsoft Community

Where to find audio track?

considerate_guy say:

Can you explain what it is you are trying to accomplish? Then I can give you a more comprehensive answer. Also, which specific version of Movie Maker are you using?

In the "classic" versions of Movie Maker (up through Windows Movie Maker 6.0) there were separate tracks for video, a sub-track for its audio and another track for added music.  These all appeared in a timeline along the bottom of the Movie Maker window.

In the newer versions of Movie Maker (called Windows Live Movie Maker through 2011), you don't have a timeline.  Instead you have a wrapped storyboard/project area to the right.  The video track is shown as one strip (aka track) and below that you'll find the added captions as another strip and the added music as yet another thin strip.  But you couldn't see the audio that was part of the video track.

When Movie Maker 2012 was released, it fixed this by showing the audio waveforms within the storyboard strip for your video.  It appears as a squiggly line within that video track.  You can't do much with that audio (except change the volume) since it is tied directly to the video.

Movie Maker also added the ability to add another track of audio (in addition to the added music) in the form of added narration, added sound effects or even the sound from a video file.  That appears as a separate strip below the others in the storyboard.

Hopefully that is enough to go by,  but if you still need help, provide more details on what you are trying to do and why having access to the audio track is needed.

It depends on how Windows 10 was originally configured on your computer.
