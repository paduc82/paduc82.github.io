brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Outlook 2013 (or any Outlook version) doesn't really have an "address book".  Instead, it has contacts folders and you can create as many of those as you wish and collect contacts in them in whatever manner seems best to you.  You'll always have at least one Contacts folder that Outlook will create for you.

The address book itself is just a view of those contacts folders you have elected to show in the Address Book interface.  That interface also allows Outlook to translate the contact names to addresses.  By default, any folder you create in Outlook to contain Contact items will be enabled as an address book, if your mail profile is working correctly.  So, click the Address Book icon in the ribbon, then click Tools>Options.  You should see contacts folders in the dialog.  Do you?

To create a contact folder, click the Folder tab on the ribbon, then click New Folder.  Give the folder a name and choose "Contact Items" in the "Folder contains" drop-down.  You can make any other folder the new folder's parent (it doesn't even need to be the main Contacts folder) or you can make the root of the tree the parent, which will put your new folder on the same level as the default Contacts folder.

Another approach that may be better in Outlook is to have a single Contacts folder, but assign your contacts categories to collect them in the relationships you want, as opposed to collecting them in multiple folders.
