      Sub MyErrorRoutine ( ErrorCode, SubName , SendMail )

      Call MyErrorRoutine ( Err.Number , "MyMacro" , True )

cth6 say:

It is actually very simple.  I dont know why MSFT doesn't post how to do this on their FAQ.

Your mail hosting at Zoho will not go away when you migrate to Premium.  It will stay right were it is until your billing period expires.  Let me explain.

At GoDaddy today you have an MX record that says send all of the email for mydomain.com to Zoho.  Zoho receives all of your email and stores it on their servers.  Before you go any further, goto the DNS management page at GoDaddy and take a picture of your current DNS settings.  

During the registration Microsoft will add three DNS entires to your GoDaddy domain settings.  Basically what this does is says 'Hey GoDaddy you need to send mail to Microsoft now'.  Zoho is none the wiser.  They just think that you have not received any new email.  You can still log into Zoho's servers and see your email box.  You wont see anything new and you can still actually send from that account.  At the point that Microsoft changes your DNS entries your email will now goto Microsoft.  

If you ever want to send email back to Zoho all you need to do is remove the three DNS settings that Microsoft added.  Now to your question about the actual migration.

The easiest way is to open your mail client (outlook, mail,...) on your computer that you use to check Zoho.  Your email may be stored on their servers or downloaded on your mail client.  (This removes the complexity of knowing if you have pop, imap or exchange on your Zoho account).  Now just add your new premium email account information to that client.  Once it is added you will have both old and new.  Just move the mail from the old inbox to the new inbox.  Do this for each remaining folder (Sent, Archive, custom folder,...)  Once you have moved everything from Zoho to Microsoft, you will need to leave your computer connected for awhile so that the mail folders sync back to the outlook.com servers. 

If you ever decide to go back to Zoho, just delete the MX records and move the mail back.  This is why it is important to take a snapshot of your mx records before microsoft changes them.
