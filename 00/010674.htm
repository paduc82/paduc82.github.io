bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
I don't believe you screwed anything up :-) it's just a matter of how that feature works. Technically, the Footnotes are a separate, subordinate 'plane' of the document. If the insertion point is in a footnote when you do the search the search is limited to that plane. Likewise, if the insertion point is in a Header or a Footer only Headers or only Footers will be searched. If the insertion point is in the body of the document the entire document -- including the footnotes, Headers & Footers -- will be searched.

You can also take advantage of the Edit> Find> Advanced Find & Replace dialog rather than the Search in Document device. Assign a keyboard shortcut to the EditFindDialog command for convenience if you wish.
