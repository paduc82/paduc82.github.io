If you have an Office 365 account for companies with a vanity domain and you have access to the administration center what you should do is the following.

1- Enter the Office 365 portal with your administrator user (http: portal.office.com)
2- Go to the administration center> active users.
3- In active users look for your user> click on it.
4- In the new window that opens> Username / Email Aliases> Edit.
5- Establish your company's email address as the main one.

If you do not have access to the Office 365 Administration center, contact the office manager of your company or organization.

By setting your email address as the main one you can send emails and create calendar events with this email address.

peterjuuls say:

Chaotic device password dialogue on WM6.5

My default Exchange 2007 SP1 ActiveSync policy requires device passwords and it works fine on WM6.1 devices.
Last week I connected my first HTC Touch Pro2 WM6.5 mobile to Exchange and the ActiveSync policy was enforced onto the device. The result was a chaotic password dialogue when I restarted the device, and it seems to repeat itself each time I restart the mobile. A prompt for simcard pincode, mixed with several device password pop-up prompts and a set-up-your-first-device-password wizard. When I finally get passed all these prompts, the device works okay. But the next time I switch the device on, this chaotic dialogue starts all over. (I've entered only passwords, that meet the password requirements in the ActiveSync policy)  If I wipe the device and remove the requirement for a device password in the Active Sync policy, the problem disappears. If I wipe the device without setting up a connection to Exchange, the problems disappears

How can I enforce a device password via ActiveSync policies and avoid this annoying password dialogue?

peterjuuls say: Eric, thanks for your posting.

Devices in my setup connect "over-the-air only", ie. all devices dial up via a GPRS/3G modem-connection to the ISP. Other device connection options, like WiFi or cable or infra-red, are disabled or blocked by the ActiveSync security policy. So, the only permitted communications on devices are the modem-connection to the ISP and a Bluetooth connection to a head set. The setup is strict, to protect devices from any kind of security threats, but the setup works fine with WM6.1 and WM5.

I'm glad you confirm, that SP2 is irrelevant in this matter.

The ActiveSync policy requires a simple password, 8 characters long (to permit users an 8-digit pincode). I tried setting up another policy, that requires a complex password, and it had no impact on the symptoms. What helps is disabling the password requirement completely.
