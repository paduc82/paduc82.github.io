Hi Kara,

Yes, you can use the cmdlet Search-Mailbox to search and delete messages.

Below is an example to delete all meetings recevived in a specific time. For more information, you can refer to: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/ff459253(v=exchg.150).aspx

Get-Mailbox | Search-Mailbox -SearchQuery 'Received:01/01/2010..01/01/2015 kind:meetings' -DeleteContent

Note: When creating the tag, please select applied automatically to a default folder and then choose the calendar folder.

Get-MailboxFolderStatistics -Identity *** Email address is removed for privacy *** -FolderScope Calendar | FL FolderPath, ItemsInFolder

bernie deitrick say:

Assuming your headers are in row 1, and you want to sort ascending on both, this will sort the entire sheet based on those two columns:

 

Cells.Sort Key1:=Range("1:1").Find("Customer Name"), _
Order1:=xlAscending, Key2:=Range("1:1").Find("Order Number") _
  , Order2:=xlAscending, Header:=xlYes

 

If your headers are in row 2, then you could use

 

Range("2:" & ActiveSheet.UsedRange.Rows.Count).Sort _
Key1:=Range("2:2").Find("Customer Name"), _
Order1:=xlAscending, Key2:=Range("2:2").Find("Order Number") _
  , Order2:=xlAscending, Header:=xlYes
