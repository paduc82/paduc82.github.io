charles kenyon say:

First, if you save a blank document with keyboard shortcuts as your normal.dotm template, you will disable certain word functions. You cannot create a functional normal template this way. You may never notice the difference, or you may be perplexed for a long time by doing this. Only Word, itself, can create a fully functional normal.dotm template.

Instead, at this point to recover your shortcuts, close Word. Rename your normal.dotm as normalnot.dotm. Download the keyboard shortcut organizer from Chris Woodman. Search Other Downloads for Woodman the link. You can use that to transfer the shortcuts you stored from normalnot.dotm to a new normal.dotm when you restart Word.

As I said before, sometimes the simplest thing to do is to save your customizations to a file other than normal.dotm and use that as an Add-In. Instead of renaming your (defective) normal.dotm template to normalnot.dotm and then transferring the shortcuts from it, you could name it MyShortcuts.dotm and simply put it in your Word Startup Folder.

Next time you want to be sure of saving something in the normal template, open that template for editing, so that the name in the title bar says "normal.dotm" instead of "Document 3" or the like. Then make and save your modifications (no text!) and save the template. Then close it.
