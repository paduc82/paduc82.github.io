Fundamental difference: Save As creates a different file, Save updates the current file. Notice that when you Save a new file for the first time you are prompted but there is no further need for a prompt unless you actually close the file.

Other than that there is no real distinction. Some of what Phillip refers to may have been fairly accurate in the days of binary file formats, but doesn't apply to contemporary OOXML files types... they are handled in a completely different manner.

On the same subject: It sounds like you may be looking for some sort of confirmation or 'reassurance' that the Save occurred. If so, note the leftmost of the 3 stoplight buttons in the upper left corner of the document window. If a dot appears within the button the document contains unsaved changes. When you save the dot goes away. This is a standard feature of OS X.

  

Additionally, when you Save glance at the Status Bar to see the progress indicator provided by Word.

dcms371 say:

I am not 100% but it likely tracks your HR and then looks at what HR Zone you're in to determine the percentage of fat vs carbs.

This is actually a pretty common statistic that people look for in workouts.

The recommendation for burning bodyfat is lower intensities (Zone 2) and the harder you go, the more carbs (muscle glycogen) you burn. This is why people that over do a long strenuous exercise like a marathon or a cycling century can "bonk" (run low on muscle glycogen). They have burned up all the energy stored in their muscles because the body is looking for quick energy. Metabolizing fat during a hard, long workout is just too slow.

To figure out your HR Zones, you can go here: http://www.digifit.com/heartratezones/training-zones.asp

For me, at 43, my Zone 2 HR range is 106-123. I tend to want to keep at the upward end of that range. My last workout was 2 hours on the treadmill with an average HR of 119 beats per minute. I actually had a 60-40 breakdown in fats vs. carbs, which is great.

Hope this helps.
