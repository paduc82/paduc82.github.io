a. user say:

You can certainly extend your C: partition, but I would not recommend using Windows' own disk management tool to do that.

Disk management is for simple tasks like extending or shrinking a partition, or for creating a new partition in unoccupied space. You need to do more complex re-arranging and for that you really should use a third-party partition manager.

You can delete the OEM partitions, if you wish, but you have to be aware of the consequences of doing that. OEMs generally place two partitions on the disk that are normally hidden: one contains the manufacturer's factory reset utility and the other contains the manufacturer's diagnostic tools.

Obviously, if you delete the factory restore partition you won't be able to restore your computer to the way it was when you first got it. That may or may not matter to you.

Similarly, you might decide that you can diagnose hardware problems on your own without the manufacturer's diagnostic tools. However, if your computer is still under warranty you should probably keep that partition. If you ever have to contact the manufacturer's technical support, and they ask you to run their diagnostics tools, and you tell them that you removed that partition, you might void your warranty.

FWIW, not that I pretend to be an expert, I got rid of the factory restore partition because I'm fastidious about backing up every day, and I got rid of the diagnostic partition as soon as my warranty was over because I can run third-party tools that do the same tests.
