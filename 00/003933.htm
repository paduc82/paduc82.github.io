john w. vinson say:

I'm not familiar with the NTSC timecode architecture, but I'd have to say - yes, but not directly, and you'll have to probably write some VBA code to interpret the codes from your mpegs. Access date/time values are stored as a Double Float number of days and fractions of a day (times), and can only be manipulated (in practice) with one-second granularity, so getting down to individual frames of a video will require other datatypes. I'd suggest converting the timecode value into seconds and fractions of a second (perhaps in a Double Number field) and do your manipulations with that.
