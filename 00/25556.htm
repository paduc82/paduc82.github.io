Your screen shot suggests that "Track Changes" is activated. You should be able to turn it off by clicking the Track Changes button (on the Review tab) so that it gets deselected. You will then have to accept all changes by clicking the arrow on the Accept button and then click All Changes in Document.

If you can't turn off the tracking, the document has protection applied to it. That means you will have to make use of a trick: Save and close the file. Create a new blank document. luc sanders say:

Als,

Should not that be I finished on 1st (first) place? After a period Word automatically adds a capital letter as the following sentence should begin with a capital letter.

In the autocorrect options settings there is an item 'Capitalize first letter of sentences that is checked by default'.  You could uncheck it.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
Correct English would actually be "I finished in first place." Likely the language setting in this example is not English because recent versions of Word (at least English versions) do not capitalize after a period if the period is preceded by a number. This actually causes more problems than it solves and appears to be an unintended consequence of a change made (presumably in response to user demand) to prevent numbered lists from automatically being capitalized.
