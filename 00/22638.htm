This is a real 320 kbps MP3:

This is a "320 kbps" MP3 purchased from the Xbox Music Marketplace:

You will notice that the lower-quality recording has a cut-off line. A lot of the data above that line is missing unlike in the full 320 kbps file where much less data is missing and the cut-off line is both higher and less significant. This results in a lower-quality music file.

The reason I'm bringing this up is because I like the convenience of Xbox Music as a music player, but the quality of the music is off-putting. By comparison, MP3s bought from the Amazon Music store have real bitrates generally between 224 and 256 kbps compared to 192 or below for Xbox Music. So this is both a question and a discussion: can Microsoft make the music publishers sell music at a quality on par with the Amazon marketplace or other services? And for the community, do people care enough about music quality? I know that I'm a bit more on the technical side of music, but I think all people appreciate high quality music. And if the Xbox Music Marketplace's 30 million songs could all be real 256 kbps or 320 kbps MP3s, this service could have an advantage over other services that offer lower-quality music (like Spotify) or a weaker selection (like Beatport).
