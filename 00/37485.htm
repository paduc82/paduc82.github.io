What errors? How do you know you have them? What makes you think they

If you are considering running a Registry Cleaner, I strongly
recommend against it. Registry cleaning programs are all snake oil.
Cleaning of the registry isn't needed and is dangerous. Leave the
registry alone and don't use any registry cleaner. Despite what many
people think, and what vendors of registry cleaning software try to
convince you of, having unused registry entries doesn't really hurt
you.
The risk of a serious problem caused by a registry cleaner erroneously
removing an entry you need is far greater than any potential benefit
it may have.
Read http://www.edbott.com/weblog/archives/000643.html

And also
http://blogs.technet.com/markrussinovich/archive/2005/10/02/registry-junk-a-windows-fact-of-life.aspx

Let me point out that neither I nor anyone else who warns against the
use of registry cleaners has ever said that they always cause
problems. If they always caused problems, they would disappear from
the market almost immediately. Many people have used a registry
cleaner and never had a problem with it.

Rather, the problem with a registry cleaner is that it carries with it
the substantial risk of having a problem. And since there is no
benefit to using a registry cleaner, running that risk is a very bad
bargain.

shenan stanley say:

... from Microsoft?  Or is there some manufacturer's bloatware running on the system that will supposedly do this for you?

Your audio device (hardware) was not made by Microsoft.  The drivers that may be delivered to your computer may/may not be the latest drivers the actual manufacturer of your audio device has.  It is quite likely they are not.

The hardware in your computer is supported on whatever OS you choose by the hardware manufacturer, not Microsoft (nor the makers of the other OSes you might have on that system.)  If you want to be sure you have the latest drivers installed for your hardware (be it the motherboard chipset, video device, network device or audio device) you should figure out who manufactured the device(s) and visit those manufacturer's web pages to get the drivers you can install to make them communicate with the operating system of your choice.

Now - being that you have a laptop - even though you could go to the manufacturer's web pages for each individual device (and likely get things working just fine) it is better you go to the laptop manufacturer's web page and look up your model there.
