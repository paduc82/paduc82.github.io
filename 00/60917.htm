Neither the Microsoft Office application suite, nor any of its
individual component applications (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Access,
Outlook, etc.), have _ever_ been "part" of *any* Windows operating
system. They are, and always have been, separate applications, that
must be purchased and installed separately.

A full license for Microsoft Office comes pre-installed on new
computers only when the computer manufacturer chooses to offer it, and
the purchaser is willing to pay extra for it.

Additionally, many OEMs offer the trial license for Microsoft
Office, which you can use for a limited number (25, I believe) of times
before you have to purchase it.

As always, though, Windows 7 does include two rudimentary text editors:
NotePad and WordPad.
