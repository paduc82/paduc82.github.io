charles kenyon say:

A page field is never a typed number. It is a gadget/code called a field in Word that gives you the correct page number even if you change the margins and have more or less pages.

If you have a confused document, page numbers can become confused as well. In a document with only one section, page number fields work very smoothly. If you insert a section break and tell Word to not continue (link) with previous headers, anything in them disappears in following pages. The default is to continue them so you (or someone else working on the document) have to have told Word to not do this.

The people at Microsoft work hard to hide the complexity of Word from the end user. For the most part, in simple documents, this is very effective and makes Word easier to use. When documents become more complex, though, it can become a nightmare for the novice. I, and the others answering here, have been trying to give you ways to enter a PAGE field in your document where it is missing and let you get on with your life.

If you are using Word much at all, though, you will be better off, long term, coming to grips with its complexities and learning to really make use of its power rather than fighting against it. That will require some time, effort, and yes, frustration, on your part, but you will find it worth doing.
