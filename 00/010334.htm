diane poremsky mvp (slipstick.com) say:

Office 365 (and outlook.com/Hotmail) instituted anti-spam policies that block sending from other email addresses.  You can't type just anything in the From field and send it - you need to use an address Microsoft knows is associated with your account and you need to do it their way. Many mail providers are moving in this direction to combat spam and email spoofing and to avoid getting placed on RBLs.

 

You need to use a Connected Account (Options > "See email from all your accounts in one place ") if you want to send from other addresses in outlook.  You won't use a SMTP account - you need to use exchange mailbox and enter the address in the From field of the message.  (This is how you use the connected address in Outlook 2013: http://screencast.com/t/C5B5G6QzuOvO)
