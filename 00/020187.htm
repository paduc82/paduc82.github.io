joeu2004 say:

This is simply yet-another example of arithmetic anomalies that commonly arise because Excel uses 64-bit binary floating-point to represent numeric values internal.

For example, IF(10.1 - 10 = 0.1, TRUE) returns FALSE(!) because 10.1 - 10 is 0.0999999999999996.

The work-around is:  whenever you expect a calculation to be accurate to some number of decimal places (2, in your example), explicitly round to

Your sum should be =ROUND(SUM(A1:A11),2) .

These "anomalies" are not considered to be bugs per se, but a limitation of the internal representation of numeric values.  Most computer applications have the same limitations.  (Although some do not because they use a different internal representation.)

In general, the problem is:  most decimal fractions cannot be represented exactly in 64-bit binary floating-point.  And the approximation of a particular decimal fraction depends on the magnitude of the value.

For example, notice the different approximation of 0.1 in the following numbers:

10.1         10.0999999999999,996447286321199499070644378662109375

0.1              0.100000000000000,0055511151231257827021181583404541015625

I use period for the decimal point and comma to demarcate the first 15 significant digits, which is the most that Excel formats, rounded.

If the files were created in any version of Word, you should still be able to open them, but by default, files that use the format used by Word versions before Word 97 will be blocked by Word 2010. In order to change this, you need to go to File | Options | Trust Center | Trust Center Settings | File Block Settings and change the settings to allow you to open files in these formats. Note that, if you want completely unrestricted access to these files, you need to clear the check boxes for them so that whichever of the radio buttons is selected at the bottom does not apply to them. Alternatively, you can leave them checked and opt to allow opening them in Protected View, with or without allowing editing.

jason (office engineer) say:

Hello Aishwarya,

Unfortunately, it looks like that Sway no longer exists.  After a Sway's owner leaves a company and their account is removed, that user's Sways go into a holding pattern for 30 days where they act as if they're deleted.  After that 30 days, they get permanently deleted.  However, the organization admin can transfer Sways in a deleted user's account to another account during the 30 day holding period.  I suggest contacting your organization admin and inquiring about this.  Here is the help article on how to do the transfer:  https://support.office.com/article/Reassign-Sways-from-a-deleted-user-account-Admin-Help-9580E618-3C3E-4D28-A6EF-74C00A997248
