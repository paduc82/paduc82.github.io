For those examples, it is silly to use DATEDIF(...,"d") to calculate either days or weeks.  A better way:

days:     =E9-D9

weeks:  =(E9-D9)/7

On the othe hand, the MSFT article might want to highlight the difference between that and DATEDIF(...,"d") when D9 and E9 contain time of day as well as date.

Specifically, DATEDIF effectively uses INT(D9) and INT(E9), I think.

Of course, we could do the same, namely =INT(E9)-INT(D9).  But arguably, one function call is better than two.

Bottom line:  Change the example to that D9 and E9 contain and display date and time.
