Doug has given you the correct solution, including the link to Shauna Kelly's excellent article. Although you could use a separate multilevel list for your appendix headings, the recommended approach using a list that is already linked to the built-in heading styles will be necessary if you want to incorporate the heading number in page numbers or captions because the "chapter number" that you can add to those numbers must be the paragraph number of an autonumbered paragraph in one of the built-in heading styles.

It won't affect the ability to use it in page and caption numbering.

It's not really malware, it's simply a script that was likely launched from an advertisement delivered to the page by a 3rd-party advertising organization and not in the control of MSN itself.

This is a common issue lately, since the scripts required to perform these are quite simple to create and typically not detected by most antimalware programs.  That's because nothing within them is actually malware, they're just otherwise innocuous scripts and html files.

The problem is that to many consumers they're confusing and not easily removable, so the distinction of malware doesn't make any difference.
