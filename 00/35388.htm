davem121 say:
Hi WRD313,

There is a very big mis-understanding with regards to Windows 10 Licencing . . .

It does not really matter that a Windows 10 Digital licence is attached to your Microsoft account, unless you are running a Retail copy of Windows 10, then it can make activation a bit easier. . .

In the normal course of events, that Windows 10 licence is for the lifetime of the PC it is originally installed and activated on - regardless of the user accounts - say if you sell you PC - that licence will go with the PC, not stay with your user account

When you initially activate Windows 10, a digital signature is generated form your hardware and that is stored on the Microsoft Activation servers in the form of a digital entitlement to run Windows 10

If you ever need to re-install Windows 10, it will be no problem. Windows will contact the Microsoft Activation servers and automatically re-activate
