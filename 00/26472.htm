In Project's definition, a summary task is one that contains subtasks (also called Outline Children). The Outline number or the WBS are show one level higher than the subtasks. Its start is the earliest start of its outline children, its finish is the latest of the finish dates of these children.

A Hammock tasks is one whose start is defined by a date (generally the start) of one task, and its finish is defined by a date (generally the finish) of an other task. This is achieved by paste link of these dates in the Hammock task.

Create a new task. Copy the start of task A and "paste special-paste link" it into the start of your new task. Then copy the finish of task B and "paste special-paste link" it into the finish of your new task. Now your new task is a hammock task.

If you dont see the reason for having one, GOOD! Don't use them. I myself always try to find a workaround to avoid them, and as you remarked, a summary task may be a better replacement!
