Alt 0127 worked for me.

Try this macro:

Sub SplitOn127()

    Workbooks.OpenText Filename:=Application.GetOpenFilename("Text files (*.txt),*.txt"), Origin:=437, StartRow _

        :=1, DataType:=xlDelimited, TextQualifier:=xlDoubleQuote, _

        ConsecutiveDelimiter:=False, Tab:=True, Semicolon:=False, Comma:=False _

Does you printer support 2 sided printing or do you need to put the card stock through separately? It will probably be easiest to do one sided printing and put the card stock through a second time.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Is the information to be printed on the back of each card unique to the information that is printed on the front?

If it is, and the cards are two across, the order of the data in the data source will need to be changed so that it is like this

Side 1 Card 1                   Side 2 Card 2

Side 1 Card 2                   Side 2 Card 1

Side 1 Card 3                   Side 2 Card 4

Side 1 Card 4                   Side 2 Card 3

etc.

If your data is in Sheet1 of an Excel workbook, if you add a second sheet to the workbook and then run a macro containing the following code, it will populate that sheet with the records arranged as required for the reverse side of the cards

Dim i As Long, j As Long
Dim arrData As Variant
arrData = Sheets(1).Range("A1").CurrentRegion
With Sheets(2).Range("A1")
    i = LBound(arrData, 1)
    For j = LBound(arrData, 2) To UBound(arrData, 2)
        .Offset(i - 1, j - 1) = arrData(i, j)
    Next j
    For i = LBound(arrData, 1) + 1 To UBound(arrData, 1) Step 2
        For j = LBound(arrData, 2) To UBound(arrData, 2)
            .Offset(i, j - 1) = arrData(i, j)
        Next j
    Next i
    For i = LBound(arrData, 1) + 2 To UBound(arrData, 1) Step 2
        For j = LBound(arrData, 2) To UBound(arrData, 2)
            .Offset(i - 2, j - 1) = arrData(i, j)
        Next j
    Next i
End With

rohn007 say:

Correct, you cannot simply copy the program files from an existing computer to a new one. You also have to get the appropriate registry entries.  The simplest way to do that is run the installer.

What you can do is copy the installation disk to a USB drive (at least 4gb in size).  Then copy that to your new HD.  You don't want to run the installation from a USB drive because it will be slower AND the installation process creates and deletes a lot of temp files, which wears down the finite write/delete cycles built into flash drives.

Note: at least one person has asked about problems they had installing Office 2K on Win 10.  Please let us know if it works, and what you may have had to do "extra" to make it work.

good luck
