rohn007 say:

Office 2016 Pro licenses are 1-time payment, 1-computer licenses. As far as I know you can activate multiple keys on the same email account because they are associated with different machines.

Office 365 (Personal) is a keep-paying-forever consumer "subscription" for 1 computer at a time. Since 2016 and 365 are totally separate classes of license as far as I know you should be able to use the same email account to activate it as the 2016 licenses.   Sorry to say I have not actually tried it myself.

I do know for sure that there is a problem trying to activate more than one type of Office 365 licenses. MS "designed" the system to support only one 365 consumer license per email account. The last license activated takes control of email account.

So, in theory you should be able to use the same email account for all 3 licneses and therefore to easily share onedrive between the 3 machines.

Worst case, if you don't get "official" confirmation you can trust, you can use a different email account to activate 365. Then in Office use the File menu > Account command > "Connected Services" feature to associate the email account, and OneDrive to your other email account.

Sub convert_textTOnumber(ws As Worksheet)
Dim colLtr As String, testCol As String
'==============================================================================
    With ws
        .Select
        prlCol = .Cells(1, Columns.Count).End(xlToLeft).Column
        prlRow = .Cells(Rows.Count, "B").End(xlUp).Row
        .Cells(1, prlCol + 1) = "TEST"
        testCol = findcolumn("TEST", ws, 1, True)
        
        For Each c In .Range(Cells(1, 1), Cells(1, prlCol)).Cells
            If c.Value = "Project #" Or c.Value = "Existing PO#" Or c.Value = "Purchase Request #" Or _
               c.Value = " Requisition #" Or c.Value = "PO #" Or c.Value = "Line #" Or c.Value = "QTY" Then
                colLtr = findcolumn(c.Value, ws, 1, True)
                
                fillrange = .Range(testCol & "2:" & testCol & prlRow).Address
                .Range(testCol & "2").Formula = "=IF(" & colLtr & "2="""","""",VALUE(" & colLtr & "2))"
                .Range(testCol & "2").AutoFill .Range(fillrange), xlFillDefault
                .Range(fillrange).Calculate
                .Range(fillrange).Value = .Range(fillrange).Value
                .Range(fillrange).Copy
                .Cells(2, c.Column).PasteSpecial xlValue
            End If
        Next c
        fltrCol = findcolumn("TEST", ws, 1, False)
        Columns(fltrCol).Select
        Selection.Delete Shift:=xlToLeft
        .Range("B2").Select
    End With
End Sub

When you rebuild, Outlook takes the active identity and copies all the data, nice and clean into a new one.

The old one is left unaltered with a [backed-up] tag and the data of the procedure (in case you want to revert back to it).

The process is really not dangerous for your data and as I was saying, you can always revert back if anything went wrong.

You can also use the database utility to re-open an older identity and export data from there. It can help recover lost items (reimport in the normal main identity afterwards).
