julie sheets say:
Try a fairly simple experiment:
New project
Start date August 5, 2013
Day = 8 hours

Create two tasks each 5 days in duration. Task Type = Fixed Units (default)
Task 1 - starts Aug 5 ends Aug 9
Task 2 - starts Aug 5 ends Aug 9

Create two resources:
"FT" -  with no modifications to the calendar - works 08:00 - 12:00 and 13:00 - 17:00 Monday through Friday
"PT" - working only 08:00 - 12:00 Monday through Friday.

Assign "FT" to Task 1.  The task duration = 5 days.  Starts Aug 5 ends Aug 9.  Work = 40 hours.
Work is calculated based upon Duration (5 days) * Assignment Units (100%) * Hours per day
40 = 5*1*8

Assign "PT" to Task 2.  Task Duration = 5 days. Starts Aug 5 ends Aug 16 Work = 40 hours.
40 hours of work is still calculated and 1 day still equals 8 hours.  However, because the resource only works 4 hours per day - the finish date of the task is extended.

The non-working time (after 12:00 pm and weekends) are not included in the start and finish date calculation for the task.  The duration is 40 Working Hours for the resource assigned.

I hope this helps.
