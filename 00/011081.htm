techfreak_ say:

The number 1 issue is not being signed into the correct account because multiple accounts have the same primary email address. 

The number 2 issue is attempting to cancel a subscription following the guidance for a different type of subscription/service.

1. Sign out of the incorrect account and sign into the correct account. This correct account will generally be addressed in purchase and renewal statements in your email.

Skype accounts can be under:

1. A unique email address (live: prefix account).

2. A Skype name you created long ago.

3. Your phone number.

4. A friend/family member account authorized (or not authorized) to use your payment source.

If you are still unable to locate the correct account to issue a cancellation, I suggest you contact Skype support over live chat for assistance

2. Locate the correct subscription and cancel it. 

a. If you have a calling subscription, click on the manage button on the account page and select the option to cancel the calling subscription.

b. If you have a Skype Number subscription, click on the Skype Number section of the account page, click settings, then select the option to cancel the subscription.

Starting point when Word does something unexpected

As a general rule, when something seems to be going wrong, or at least not the way you want it to go, in Word, any version, go to Tools and then Options and study all the settings on all the tabs. You may even find some settings that you want to try that have nothing to do with the current problem.

When I was teaching Word and other Microsoft Office programs at a private business college, I had three rules:

1.     Always check Tools Options

2.     When in doubt, right-click on something

3.     If you're wondering how to set up a special kind of material in a document, consider whether Word tables are suitable. The placement officer of the school came to me several times and asked how I thought she should set something up. Each time the answer was, "I'd use a Word table." By the fifth time she had stopped asking--she got the point.
