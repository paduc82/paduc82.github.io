Here's where your problem lies. Your computer has (or had since you've replaced the hard drive) a hidden partition used to recover the machine in the event it won't boot anymore. That's accessible by hitting F9 during startup.

What you've done by replacing the hard drive yourself and then reinstalling Windows using a borrowed CD installer is not going to work simply because the latter isn't programmed to read the BIOS product key.

¡firedog say:

The polling settings are on the General tab of the program options sheet (Ctrl-Shift-O). POP3 accounts will honour the settings you make there. HTTP and IMAP accounts may not, because their aim is to stay in sync with the server. If you don't want this to happen, deselect Include this account when receiving mail or synchronizing on the General tab of the account's properties sheet (right-click on the account name in the folder pane and select Properties).

You should also deselect Send messages immediately on the Send tab of program options, otherwise, a send/receive will be triggered for the account as soon as you press Send. With the option disabled, messages won't be sent until you next use Send/Receive.
