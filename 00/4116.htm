It is really just a coincidence.

These are rounding errors that are inherent in the way that Excel represents numbers and does arithmetic, called 64-bit binary floating-point.  For all the gory details, take a look at http://support.microsoft.com/kb/78113.

(But take the information in KB 78113 with a grain of salt.  It contains many incorrect statements, e.g. that numbers and arithmetic are limited to up to 15 significant digits.)

In a nutshell, most numbers with decimal fractions cannot be represented exactly.  This causes infinitesimal anomalies ("errors"; but that word does not mean "defect" in this context) in arithmetic operations.

These rounding errors are difficult to predict.  Sometimes, just rearranging terms seems to eliminate them.  For a simple example, note that IF(10.1-10=0.1,TRUE) returns FALSE(!).

The work-around is to explicitly round expressions to the desired precision.  For example:

=ROUND(600-39.49-39.32-42.87-1.07-44.12-47.84-40.8-43.77-38.96-39.99-39.13-40.3-44.3-39.27-32.49-26.29, 2)

Preview builds expire.  This build expires next summer.  Of course there will always be new builds, extending the expiration further into the future.  

The release of Windows 10 to the general public was the last of the Big Bang releases.  Microsoft is not planning a Windows 11.  The Insider program is not only a preview of new features, etc., it is a preview of a new way of moving forward with Windows.
