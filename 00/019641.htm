richard127wl say: FIXED!

I had the same problem for days. Nothing I did worked. I kept on getting the dreaded C00D07C9 error. I tried everything on every post and forum - nothing worked. Some posters even said it was an unfixable bug that would only work after a firmware update. I almost gave up. Until... I did this:

Not sure how or why this worked, but it did. Some of the steps might be unnecessary but I'm providing everything I did, just in case.

1. Via the Zune software, gave my phone a new name with no spaces as per Microsoft's advice: http://support.xbox.com/en-US/zune/software/wireless-sync#method4 NOTE: I tried everything else on this page but it didn't work. And, the bit that says to go into advanced options in the Zune wireless sync settings doesn't work. 'Advanced Options' simply isn't there.
2. Closed Zune Software on desktop PC
3. Detached and turned off Lumia 800 phone
4. Re-started phone
5. Entered >Settings >Internet Sharing and set that function up, following the phones instructions. Note: this also turns off WiFi connection on your phone
6. Exited setting
7. Went back to settings and disabled Internet Sharing
8. Went into settings on phone and checked phone had re-connected to my home WiFi (It had, but just manually re-connect if yours hasn't; making sure you have followed step 7. above and disabled Internet Sharing)
9. Attached phone to PC with USB in the usual way
10. When Zune software had booted, I re-tried wireless sync setup and... Success! It took less than a second to find and link the phone

As I say, I have no idea why this should have worked, but it did, after days of trying everything else.

It's worth nothing, however, that you need to have your phone plugged into the mains charger to sync (wtf Microsoft/Nokia - not really wireless then... is it?) and, for the first sync, the phone has to have been charging for at least 15 minutes without being switched on or used. You'll know when it's ready as Windows pops up a bubble alert in the system tray/taskbar to say it has installed new hardware and Zune software will display a black, active icon for your phone in the bottom left-hand corner of the screen, instead of the white, inactive icon. Only God and MS/Nokia know why. I have never encountered anything so ludicrous. I have never had any problems attaching any Phone or MP3 player to any edition of Windows. Attaching a Windows phone to a Windows PC running Windows Zune software should have been easy... Shouldn't it?

Hope this works for some other peeps, I'm sorry if it doesn't. Good luck.
