ronnie vernon say:

I have Acer Aspire 5738. I using windows 7 RTM. Now it is showing a cross icon on battery saying that "Consider replacing your battery" as my battery is giving the same performance.Help me to sort out this issue.


Hi

Here is an explanation of what you are seeing.

The newer Li-ion batteries are different from the older Ni-Cad batteries that you may be familiar with.

As soon as a Li-ion battery is created by the battery manufacturer, it will begin to deteriorate. This deterioration will occur regardless of whether the battery is installed in a device and being used or whether it is sitting on a shelf, waiting to be used.

These batteries typically have a life span of 1 to 3 years.

Each battery will have a Design Capacity and a Full Charge Capacity.

When the battery is created the Design Capacity will remain constant, but the Full Charge Capacity will start falling. This loss of Full Charge Capacity is permanent and cannot be recovered.

The Windows 7 Battery Meter is designed to report the battery status that is reported by BIOS and the Hardware charging components.

When the charging components report that the Last Full Charge has fallen below 40% of the Design Capacity it will display the Consider Replacing Your Battery message. This gives the laptop owner plenty of time to plan on replacing the battery.

Hope this helps.
