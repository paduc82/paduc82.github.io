How can I increase the size of my windows backup drive D? - Microsoft Community

mikemccreary say:

How can I increase the size of my windows backup drive D?

I am getting messages that I don't have enough space to perform another backup.
tryx3 say:

Are you backing up to an internal drive instead of to an external one? 

 

Backups to drives within the PC are more useful than chocolate teapots but only just - if your PC goes wrong then it can take its drives with it.  If you choose to backup to an external drive such as an external hard drive connected by USB then you are much safer.  Whilst I use a third party backup utility, I understand that Windows Backup has [as its default setting] the backup destination on the D:\ drive and that is what has led you into your current situation.

 

To avoid filling up that external drive too quickly, you need to manage it manually.  Generally speaking, what fills the drive up is the whole series of system images that can be included with backups.  They are very useful but are almost as big as the used space on the original drive(s).  You can manually delete older images to create room on the drive for the next one.  I actually keep two recent images but delete the older ones in turn.  If you are interested in doing this then I can give you the details of how I do it.

 

The fact that you say you are backing up to the D:\ drive also makes me think that you have the OS & its applications on drive C:\ and you also have all your own files on drive C:\.  If that is correct then you can also achieve considerable improvements [once you set backups up to use an external drive] by moving your data files over to D:\.  Other than the time it takes to move your files, the setting up that goes along with this change takes less than two minutes.  The  additional benefits of this are substantial.  You can carry on making normal file backups of your own files and you carry on making system images.  But those system images are going to be up to about 35GB and only take 20 minutes to do.  Similarly, recovering from serious system problems by restoring from those images only takes 20 minutes to do.  Because I have my PC set up this way, I can get out of any serious system problems with ease.  If you are interested in doing this then I can give you the details of how to do it.  Somebody else was asking about this only last week so I know that I can find the procedure.
