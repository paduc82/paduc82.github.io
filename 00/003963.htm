Hi Sukie,

Is there any way to loop Guy in on this thread? I believe Greg misunderstood the question and the above thread with pictures would help him out. The intended functionality of Excel is indeed to create a 1-to-1 relationship between the query and data connection. However, the issue here is that Excel is trying to create duplicate connections for each pivot table. This is not enforcement of a 1-to-1 relationship but almost enforcement of a "read only connection" concept where you must duplicate a connection to create a pivot table. By the one to one nature of connections and queries creating a new connection creates a new query.

The functionality that is broken here is the notion of a many-to-1 relationship between pivot tables and data connections, not the 1-to-1 relationship between queries and connections. Without many-to-1 connections between pivot tables and data connections, excel functions such as slicers do not work as intended:
