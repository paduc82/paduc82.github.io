First I would do as chanchan05 suggests. Some application is using power and draining the battery.

Then I would do the usual ''power saving'' settings: packet data=when needed,  3G to GSM (if 3G not needed) ...etc.

Then I would do the battery calibration:

1.Switch off the phone

2.Fully charge it

3.Unpug the charger

4.Remove the battery from the phone

5.Wait 10 minutes

6.Insert the battery back in the phone (don't switch the phone on)

7.Plug in the charger and charge again till the battery meter shows full

 

Switch on the phone and start Nokia Energy Profiler; turn off the screen nad wait 10 minutes. Then check mA graph (cut out the spikes from the start and from the and of the measurement when the screen was on). If the measurement shows more then ~15mA there is something wrong. My Nokia 5230 measures 6 or 7mA in standby.

rich007a say:

I think you need to approach this from a different direction (as was suggested in your other post).  You should not be thinking about copying a row, but insert a row, and build the formulae and formating with code.

Here's something that will get you started:

Sub InsertRowAboveSelection()
' A new line is inserted above the current selection, B:D on the new line are merged,
' and E and F have formula inserted (in this example, they both multiply the cell in that
' row in col A by 17, using the R1C1 and A1 reference styles respectively.
'
    Dim currentRow As Long
    currentRow = Selection.Row 'Get current row number.
    Rows(currentRow).EntireRow.Insert Shift:=xlDown 'Insert row.
    Range("B" & currentRow, "D" & currentRow).Merge 'Merge cells
    Range("E" & currentRow).FormulaR1C1 = "=RC[-4]*17"  'R1C1 style formula
    Range("F" & currentRow).Formula = "=A" & currentRow & "*17" 'A1 style formula
End Sub

Hope that helps.

Rich

rich007a say:

I second what Adreas said; please paste your code.  Also, it will help if you can include the details of which cells are merged or have special formatting, and some examples of your equations.

As an aside, you could quite easily approach it from your original idea of copying the line above (warts, formating, equations and all), and inserting the copied cells as a new row.  You expressed concern that there may already be data in the row being copied.  One would think that cells contain either data OR equations (not both), so you should be able to clear the contents of any "data" cells in the new row.

Here's an example:

    Dim currentRow As Long
    currentRow = Selection.Row 'Get current row number.
    Rows(currentRow - 1 & ":" & currentRow - 1).Copy  'Copy the row above current row
    Rows(currentRow & ":" & currentRow).Insert Shift:=xlDown 'Inserts copied row as new row.
    Application.CutCopyMode = False
    
     'NOW DELETE ANY "DATA" CELLS
    Range("C" & currentRow).ClearContents   'Clears contents of column C in the new row
                                            'but leaves formatting intact.

Take your pick: either (a) insert new row, format the new cells and add equations where required, as in my first reply, or (b) copy a row (including formatting), insert as new row and clear any data from the new row, as in this reply.

Note: in the above example, and the one in my previous post, the "insert my specific data here" parts should be fairly self explanitory.  For example, in the above the line

    Range("C" & currentRow).ClearContents 

clears the contents of the cell in column C (as it says in the code comments).  If you don't want to clear column C, then change "C" for whichever column you want to clear.

Similarly, in my first reply, the line

    Range("F" & currentRow).Formula = "=A" & currentRow & "*17"

puts the formula "=A#*17" into cell F#, where # is the row number.  So in cell F4 you would get "=A4*17".  The multiply by 17 is just an example.  You need to build up your formulae into a string (enclosed by quotation marks), using the variable currentRow inplace of any row numbers.  You can see in the examples how you combine strings in quotes with variables (i.e. the value of a variable, in this case, currentRow)  to make one long string that will be entered in the cell as a formula.  So in the above, we are taking the string "=A" and the value of currentRow and the string "*17" to make "=A4*17" (if currentRow was 4).

It might help you to include some extra lines to help you debug your code:

    Dim myFormula As String   'Add to top of your sub.

    myFormula = "=A" & currentRow & "*17"   'Edit this to make it your new formula
    Debug.Print myFormula
    Range("F" & currentRow).formula = myFormula

Now when you step through your code in the debug mode, you will see the value (or "contents") of myFormula in the Immediate window in the VB environment.  Go View>Immediate Window in VBA to show the Immediate window if it is not already visible. 

Hope that helps.

Cheers
Rich
