A variation - this should sort a comma separated list of words from right to
left
 
With ActiveDocument.Range(0, ActiveDocument.Range.End - 1)
.Text = StrReverse(.Text)
.Find.Execute FindText:=" ,", ReplaceWith:="^p", Replace:=wdReplaceAll
.Sort ExcludeHeader:=False, FieldNumber:="Paragraphs", _
SortFieldType:=wdSortFieldAlphanumeric,
SortOrder:=wdSortOrderDescending
.Find.Execute FindText:="^p", ReplaceWith:=" ,", Replace:=wdReplaceAll
.Text = StrReverse(.Text)
End With

scottgem say:

To expand a bit on Gina's response. Forms can be bound or unbound. A bound form has its Recordsource property set to a table or query. In a bound form Access handles the interaction between the form and the table. So any data entered into the form is automatically saved/updated to the Recordsource. This can be verified by looking in the table directly.

However, a form can have some restrictions on what data is displayed in the form. A filter may only show certain records. A sort order may make the records sort differently so you have to look in the correct order for records. Setting the Data Entry property to Yes restricts the form to entering new records and can't view existing records.
