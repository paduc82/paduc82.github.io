Here are a couple of tips that may be useful:

First, regarding Windows Update constantly asking you to download the Windows 10 upgrade, you can simply right-click the update notification and select "Hide this update".  This will prevent it from appearing again.

Second, regarding Windows 10 and how to control/force updates, now that I am more familiar with Win10, I have found that under Settings there is a "Update & Security" tab.  If you click on this, you will see a message stating that either your system is up to date, or that updates are waiting.  Note that updates normally are not downloaded while the computer is busy.  There is also a "Check for Updates" button that you can click to see if any downloads are available.  So, I am more comfortable with Win10's behavior, although I am still a little irritated when I shut down in the evening expecting to be able to immediately head to bed, but get the "Windows is working on updates--don't power the system off" message.

a. user say:

Backing up your entire hard drive is called imaging a disk, so you'll need disk imaging software. Imaging is not the same as copying because an image includes information about the disk that's not contained in any file. This information is called the metadata and it describes the structure of the disk. Your computer needs the metadata to know how to use the disk...where to find files and how to store information.

I'm currently using Macrium Reflect, but there are other good choices too. They've all got free trials so you can try them and see which you like. (Macrium Reflect even has a version that's free.)

The thing about disk imaging is that it's a little hard to understand. It will take some work to figure out, but the reward for your effort is fabulous.

Let's say I make some big mistakes and completely mess up my computer. (Of course, that never happens.) When I'm ready to give up trying to put Humpty Dumpty back together again, I restore an image made before my experimenting began and in a matter of minutes I've gone back in time like nothing ever happened. That's the power of disk imaging and I am not exaggerating.

Think of the confidence you will have once you realize that you won't ever need to reinstall Windows or your software ever again. Just restore an image and you're back in business.

Schedule your images to run daily, in the dead of the night, and you won't ever be more than 24 hours away from the most recent working version of your computer. Big companies have been doing this for years and we can too.
