Phone screen – Bluetooth troubleshooting - Microsoft Community

lonnie_l say:

Phone screen – Bluetooth troubleshooting

We are excited to provide an early preview into the newest feature for the Your Phone app – phone screen. You can now mirror your Android phone’s screen directly on your PC without having to dig for your phone.   Having trouble connecting with Bluetooth?  Try these troubleshooting steps. 

How do I turn Bluetooth on and off?  

On PC   

1. Select Start > Settings > Devices > Bluetooth & other devices.   

2. Select the Bluetooth toggle to turn it On or Off.

Note: The Bluetooth switch only appears on your system when Windows detects a Bluetooth adapter.   

On your Android Phone   

1. Open your phone’s Settings.   

2. Tap Connected devices (or Connections) > Connection preferences > Bluetooth.   

If you don't see Connection preferences, go to Step 3.   

3. Turn Bluetooth on or off.   

Tip: If you have the Bluetooth icon on your notification center you can tap it to toggle Bluetooth on/off and you can tap & hold to jump directly into Bluetooth settings. More info   

How do I pair or unpair my phone and PC?  

The Your Phone app needs to be connected to your Android phone over Bluetooth to use your PC's mouse and keyboard to interact with your phone screen.   

We’ll try to initiate the pairing automatically for you. If we’re able to find your devices, the only thing you will be prompted to do is to confirm that the passkey or pin on your Android phone is the same as the one on the PC. You will need to select the toast on your PC in order to see it and confirm it.   

If for some reason the automatic flow doesn’t work, you can try to pair your devices manually.   

To pair your devices manually  

Note: You need to unpair on both the PC and the phone side for the unpair to be complete.  

1. Make sure you have Your Phone app open in the phone screen section. Your phone won't be able to see your PC correctly if you're not running the app.   

2. Open the Bluetooth Settings on your Android phone.   

a. Open the Settings App.   

b. Tap Connected devices > Connection preferences > Bluetooth.   

Tip: You can quickly open Bluetooth settings by bringing down the notification center and doing tap & hold on the Bluetooth icon.   

3. Make sure that Bluetooth is turned on.   

4. Find the name of your PC on the list of available devices.   

You may need to tap Pair new device if you're using a different Android version. If required, tap More > Refresh or Scan to refresh the list of devices.    

Important: Make sure you select the one shown with an icon showing 4 items. You may see another entry for your PC with a different icon. Do not choose the icon that looks like a PC or a PC and a phone.   

5. Follow any on-screen steps.   

a. You will get a prompt on the PC to add the device. Select it and then confirm the pin.   

b. On your Android phone confirm the pin.   

c. You should get a success message on the PC that your devices are now paired.   

6. Close the success message on the PC and any other windows that opened.   

7. If the pairing was successful you will see the PC show up on the list of paired devices on your Android phone and showing as "Connected as input device".   

Tip: Whenever your Android phone is connected to something via Bluetooth, at the top of the screen, you'll see a Bluetooth icon.

To unpair your devices  

On your Phone

Before you start, make sure the Your Phone app on your PC is closed.   

1. Open your phone Settings app.   

2. Tap Connected devices > Connection preferences > Bluetooth. If you don't see Connection preferences, go to Step 3.   

3. Make sure that Bluetooth is turned on.   

4. Next to the paired device that you'd like to change, tap Settings.   

5. Select unpair (or 'forget').   

On your PC

Before you start, make sure the Your Phone app on your PC is closed.   

1. Select Start > Settings > Devices > Bluetooth & other devices.   

2. Select your Android phone from the list.   

3. Select Remove device.   

4. Confirm the removal.   

5. If there's another entry for your phone, make sure you unpair it too by following steps 2-4.   

What can I try to troubleshoot Bluetooth issues?  

If you’re having problems getting the Bluetooth connection to work, try the following steps:  

  1. Make sure you have installed any available updates on your Android phone.
  2. Make sure both of your devices have Bluetooth turned on. 
  3. Have your phone within Bluetooth range of your PC.  

  4. Turn your PC’s Bluetooth on then off again.  

  5. Turn your phone’s Bluetooth on then off again.  

  6. Make sure your phone is not in battery saver mode. 

  7. Confirm that your PC and Android phone are paired and connected.  

  8. Restart your PC and Android phone. 

If the above didn’t work, try to get back to a clean state and pair again 

  1. Close the Your Phone app.  
  2. Unpair from both devices.  
  3. Restart both your PC and Android phone.  
  4. Open Phone screen in Your Phone app and go through pairing flow.

Formula to calculate years of service as of today's date - Microsoft Community

scottsemel say:

Formula to calculate years of service as of today's date

I have a list employee start dates and need to know each employee's years of service as of the date that I open the spreadsheet. (I need Excel to automatically use whatever the date is each time I open Excel.)

As a bonus problem, if they have a work anniversary within the current month that I'm running the report, I need to have that highlighted. For example, if their original start date was July 27, 1993, and I am now looking at the spreadsheet on July 14, 2017, I want to know that this month is their anniversary month and I want to know how many whole-number years they've been employed.

Please advise.

Thanks,

hansv mvp say:

Let's say you have a start date in cell D2.

Enter the following formula in another cell, for example E2:

=DATEDIF(D2,TODAY(),"Y")

This can be filled down if applicable.

Select D2 (or the entire range you want to be highlighted conditionally)

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > New Rule...

Select 'Use a formula to determine which cells to format'.

Enter the formula

=MONTH($D2)=MONTH(TODAY())

Click Format...

Activate the Fill tab.

Select a highlight color.

Click OK, then click OK again.

susan_s. say:

Hello,

Your Skype account is the same as your Microsoft account. If you close it, you'll no longer be able to sign in to other Microsoft products or services such as Outlook.com, Office Online, OneDrive, Xbox Live, or Windows.

If the email address associated with your Microsoft account ends in @outlook.com, @hotmail.com, @live.com, or @msn.com, closing the Microsoft account deletes any email stored with it after the 60-day waiting period. During the waiting period, however, your inbox will continue to receive email messages. If the email associated with your Microsoft account is from a different provider like @gmail.com, you can skip this section because your email address won't be affected. 

With that note, I suggest you think twice before disconnecting your Microsoft account to your Skype account.

Let us know if you need further assistance.
