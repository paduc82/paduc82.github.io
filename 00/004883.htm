john w. vinson say:

A Text field just takes up a lot more room in the index used in the join, and is slower to process. I'm not as averse to using them as Scott is, but I would generally use a "surrogate key" such as an Autonumber (joined to a Long Integer foreign key) in queries.

 

You certainly should not be joining on employee names though! I once worked with Dr. Lawrence David Wise and his colleague, Dr. Lawrence David Wise. Larry was a tall, blond, affable chemist; L. David was a stocky, dark, taciturn biologist. Names make LOUSY keys; a key must be unique, should be stable over time, and ideally should be short - names fail on all three counts.

 

It also sounds like you have <shudder> twenty-six fields in your table, one for each week in the half-year. This is "committing spreadsheet" in the first degree! Even if the data comes that way from your accounting software, you should append it into a tall-thin table with one RECORD (not one field) per pay period. Am I misinterpreting?

jauhis say:

A couple of words on FOTA, as you guys are interested.

 

The main difference between NSU and FOTA is the mechanism how the phone, in your case the E52, is updated. In FOTA the update is applied with a differential package, which results in a far smaller update package that you are downloading compared to NSU, where package sizes range from tens of megs up to over hundred megs.

 

Another difference is the way how the update is applied to the phone: in NSU it is actually the PC that writes the SW into the phone. In FOTA the phone "rewrites" itself. This highlights the importance of a solid cable connection between PC and the phone when using NSU.

 

Allthough it is recommended to make a backup prior to a FOTA update, it is in practice not necessary as FOTA is not changing any of the user's data. NSU might, depends on the product in question.

 

kaushalls say: I upgraded my E52 yesterday using the NSU integrated with Ovi Suite 2, on my Vista laptop. It was smooth all along, no issues/errors whatsoever during the update and all user data in the phone were preserved so I didn't have to restore any backups either. Most issues with NSU I beleive is due to USB driver conflicts with other devices and/or interference by antivirus/security software during the update

scottgem say:
Yes, You are using a parameter prompt query. I rarely use them because they are so limiting. 

You can have them open a form instead. On the form you can use a combobox that lists all the valid dates. Then, in the query that is the recordsource for the report, use the syntax:

=Forms!formname!controlname

where formname is the name of the form and controlname the name of the combo.

A value in ANY control on an open form can be referenced that. way.
