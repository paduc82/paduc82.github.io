eli qian msft say:

Hi Filippos,

Thank you for the detailed description.

OneNote client will sync the content from the cloud resource like OneDrive. Then it generates the local cache file of the content in order to back up or recover if required. This is the reason why you still have the local data whilst it prompts the file isn't available after your deletion from OneDrive.

If the local data is necessary for you, you need to recreate a new notebook and re-sync it from the online resource you select. After that, you can use Move or Copy the Section feature to transfer the data. If it's unnecessary, you can just delete them. For the situation you are experiencing, we suggest you create a new notebook and delete the old one. Here is the cache folder of OneNote in ~/Library/Containers/ on Mac: com.microsoft.onenote.mac

Back to the question, OneNote synchronization mechanism is different from the synchronization mechanism of OneDrive. It is independent synchronization by OneNote. You can just consider that OneDrive is the place to store the notebook but notebooks can sync up to OneNote client via another way. This is the reason why you need open your notebook from OneNote desktop client instead of OneDrive sync client locally. So when you create OneDrive sync client in your hard disk, you will see your notebook folder including the extension *.url to redirect you to OneNote Online. We suggest you follow the articles to create and sync your notebooks for your iOS and MacOS devices.

Create a new notebook in OneNote for Mac

Sync the OneNote notebooks on your Mac or PC with your iPad or iPhone

After writing the VBA User Defined Function (UDF) go to the Developer tab and select the Macros button. Since your UDF macro is a "Function" it will not appear in the list of Macros. However, if you type the name of the Function into the "Macro name" text box the "Options" button will enable on the dialog.

Click Options and in the dialog that opens you can enter the Tooltip.

Hope this helps

jim gordon mvp say:

Following up on Rich's answer...

After typing your tool tip into the Description field of the Macro Options dialog, click OK to exist the Macro Options dialog, but then click the Cancel button to exit the Macro dialog box. This is counterintuitive, but clicking the Cancel button actually registers the tool tip.

Save the workbook, and then re-open the workbook and you will see your tool tip in the Formula Builder.
