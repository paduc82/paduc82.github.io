thkn777 say:

@graceboy9

Please have a look at some information about common problems and techniques of modern digital photography. You will find, that image stacking is used for two main reasons: increase DOF (depth of field) and NR (noise reduction).

The standard approach for NR is to take more than 1 photo of the same scenery and combine these (lets say 7 images). As digital noise is kinda random, chances are good, that only some of the 7 images have a noise pixel at a given point, where the other images show the true color/brightness.

Taking several images in a row is easy if you have a static scene with no moving objects and the same lighting conditions etc. - but it's very hard if not impossible to use it for everyday shots of people, sports etc. - and this is, what a camera handy will do: everyday photography. So if you can't take 7*5mp images in a row fast enough (like in a 1/250s, all together), what else can you do?

Nokia's approach is, to take one 35mp image and split it in 7 "grid-images" holding 5mp of data each. THIS can be done in 1/250s. Chances are, that only a few "neighbor" pixels will be noisy and the others show the true color/brightness. Combine the 7 "grid-images" and you get an image with less noise and more accurate color information.

 

So YOU don't have to pay for a 41mp sensor. Choose another phone with a 5mp sensor and blame it for taking noisy images. However, IF image quality is important for you, then Nokias approach might be a reason to consider buying the 808.

 

As for your argument, that images and videos only have to be as big as the output device (i.e. the screen of the mobile phone): well, that depends. IF you only want to show images/video on the phone, you are right. If you plan on printing your photos or postprocess them and show these and the videos on a TV screen, you are wrong.

 

Just give it a thought: why do dSLR cameras take high resolution images bigger than their rather small monitor on the back can show?

mandyshaw say:
i was using my phone. t suddenly switched off and reset the whole phone. i hadnt synced up the phone for a few days and i had some pictures and stuff. how can i restore my phone to a previous used state?

Depends on what data you want to get back.

There are two things that only a restore to your last pre-update Zune backup will replace:
  • text messages and other application data that doesn't get sync'd anywhere (e.g. Office documents that aren't sync'd with SkyDrive), and
  • application and 'phone configuration settings such as accounts, internet sharing, etc.
So the first thing to do is to decide how desperate you are to get those back (assuming that you've at some point updated your 'phone - otherwise this avenue is closed to you anyway - and also, obviously, assuming that the backup isn't too old to be any use).

So first, restore from a Zune backup if you can and want to. If you do, your 'phone will need re-updating, but I'm not going to cover that bit here.

Anyway, whether you've restored or not, now make sure that all your 'phone settings are as you want them, especially your accounts. All the below assumes you use the same main Windows Live id as last time.

You can then start re-syncing.

Email, contacts, calendar, Windows Live tasks, and anything that was sync'd to SkyDrive should sync with the cloud as normal and you should be able to get back to where you were, except for anything really newly entered that was only on the 'phone.

Media should sync with Zune as normal and again you should be able to get back to where you were, except for any photo really newly taken that was only on the 'phone.

If you didn't restore, or if you've bought/downloaded apps since the backup, some or all of your apps will re-install automatically (Zune does this, but it seems that it doesn't always remember all of them). If any app doesn't re-install, go through the purchase process as when you originally bought the app - you won't be expected to pay again.

Now configure all automatically or manually re-installed app(s) as needed.

Any app that has its own sync mechanism should now sync as before.

I hope that covers most of it - if you need more info, just post again.
