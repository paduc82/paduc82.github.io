Changing Footnote Spacing - Microsoft Community

landolphe say:

Changing Footnote Spacing

Running Word 2008 Mac under OS 10.5.8 (PPC):

By default, Word puts a large space between the footnote separator line and the first footnote. Is there a way to change (decrease) this spacing? Tried selecting the line (cannot) and its paragraph mark but that had no effect. Changing the spacing within the body of the footnote is doable but has no effect on the spacing between the separator line and the footnote.

john korchok say:

This is a little complicated by today's standards:

  1. With your document open in Word, choose View>Draft.
  2. Choose the View menu again and select Footnotes. A separate Footnotes pane opens at the bottom of the Word window.
  3. The Footnotes pane includes a dropdown that defaults to All Footnotes. Change this to Footnote Separator.
  4. The Footnote separator line appears. Select it and choose Format>Paragraph. If there is space after, set it to 0. You can also change Line spacing: to Multiple and At: to about 0.5 to reduce the space the separator takes. If you make the At: amount too small, the separator will disappear entirely.
