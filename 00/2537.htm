ronrosenfeld say:

I think what is happening is that your unqualified Cells are getting confused with what worksheet they are referring to.

What about simply:

Option Explicit
Function myAverage(rng As Range) As Double
Application.Volatile True
Dim D As Double
Dim R As Range

Set R = Range(rng, rng.End(xlUp))
D = WorksheetFunction.Average(R)

myAverage = Round(D, 2)
End Function

And make sure you take into account that the VBA Round function is different from the Worksheet Round function.

If you do not have Application.Volatile, then changing a cell other than 'rng' will not trigger recalculation.

Can I sell my product key for Office 2010 for use in another household - Microsoft Community

philayes say:

Can I sell my product key for Office 2010 for use in another household

Can I sell my product key for Office 2010 for use in another household, with another computer??
brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

I see no reason why you cannot sell your Office license, unless it is specifically marked "Not for Resale" (Item 10 in the End User License Agreement).  Here is the section of the EULA governing that:

15. TRANSFER TO A THIRD PARTY. You may transfer the software directly to a third party only with the licensed device, the Certificate of Authenticity label, and this agreement. Before the transfer, that party must agree that this agreement applies to the transfer and use of the software. You may not retain any copies.

It is clear that when you sell the license, you yourself lose any rights to use the software.  If it is installed on one or more PCs, you must remove it from all of the PCs on which the original license allowed you to install it.  You should convey to the purchaser the entire installaiton kit, including the disks, documentation, Certificate of Authenticity, Product Key, etc.  In fact, Item 15 as quoted above indicates you can't sell the software without including the "licensed device" - the computer on which you first installed the software.  If I were selling it, I'd also convey a bill of sale signed by me attesting that I sold it and that I have removed it from all systems where I had installed it.  This, I believe, would convey to the purchaser legal standing to get the product activated on his or her system(s).  I suspect that Microsoft would allow you to sell just the software without including the PC on which it was licensed, providing it's not an OEM license.  For an OEM license, you cannot sell it without including the licensed device.
