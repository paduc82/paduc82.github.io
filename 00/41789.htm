A sudden change in a Router's or Access Point's settings is a sign of outsiders breaking in and taking over your access point- thereby resetting WIFI credentials in the process. You might not notice the change via Ethernet since DHCP is an automatic protocol and doesn't require you to set it up.

next, you didn't mention specifically whether or not the device is a laptop but if it is:

3. Did you double check that you didn't accidentally press the "function key combination" ("fn"+"something") that turns WIFI off on your particular model? The key combination is different on each model and it would explain why a local Bluetooth connection with your phone works and ethernet but not WIFI. If it is a laptop or similar device then the manufacturer's user guide will list the function key combinations and what they control. Some models actually have a physical switch that controls WIFI (I will admit these tend to be older units that have a physical WIFI switch).

I hope one of the things above helps to solve your issue and if doesn't please let me know so I can help get WIFI working for you!

The "adultness" of an account isn't determined by the settings you change. 

During the creation of your account you are asked to provide a birthdate. That birthdate determines the age of the account, and only if according to that birthdate your account turns 18 (or 20 in Japan), your account truly is an adult account. 

Until then your account is a limited Child account, even though your parents might set your account to allow certain "adult" features like talking to strangers or buying stuff. 

Changing your Profile picture is unfortunately a thing, only "real" adult accounts can do, and there is no setting to circumvent that.

My name is Angel. I am an Independent Advisor. Thank you for posting in Microsoft Community.

Let's troubleshoot the issue by performing the steps below:

Method 1: Reset the app settings

1. Press Windows key + I, then select System.

2. Go to Apps & features. Click on the JW Library app.

3. Click on Advanced options, then click on Reset.

Method 2: Run PowerShell

We recommend running the following PowerShell script to re-register the Windows Store.

1. Press Windows key + X, then select Command Prompt (Admin).

2. In the command prompt, enter the following command:

PowerShell -ExecutionPolicy Unrestricted -Command "& {$manifest = (Get-AppxPackage Microsoft.WindowsStore).InstallLocation + '\AppxManifest.xml' ; Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register $manifest}

3. Reboot your computer.
