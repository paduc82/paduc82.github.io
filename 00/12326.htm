Hi Z,

 

To go to the AutoRecovery folder and check the unsaved Word file, please do the following steps:

  1. Paste defaults write com.apple.finder AppleShowAllFiles YES in Terminal (Finder > Applications > Utilities > Terminal), and then press return to show hidden files.
  2. Hold the ‘Option/alt’ key, right click on the Finder icon in the dock and click Relaunch.
  3. Go to ~/Library/Containers/com.microsoft.Word/Data/Library/Preferences/AutoRecovery/ and locate the file.
  4. Copy the Auto-recovery file to desktop.
  5. Right click on the file in desktop > Get Info > under Name & Extension, change the file extension to .docx or .doc. Or, open the file with Text editor, and then copy all text back into the document.
  6. Paste defaults write com.apple.finder AppleShowAllFiles No in Terminal (Finder > Applications > Utilities > Terminal), and then press return to hide hidden files again.

dan at it associates say:

Thanks for posting your report.

 

Your Toshiba netbook has a Volume Licensing (VL) installation of Office Professional 2003 installed on it.  The bolded parts of the extract of the report below are the indicators that tell us it's a VL installation.

 

 

><Pid>73931-640-4339835-57976</Pid><PidType>14</PidType></Product></

 

And...

 

><Product GUID="{90110409-6000-11D3-8CFE-0150048383C9}">

 

 

 

VL versions of Microsoft products are sold to larger businesses, educational institutions, and government agencies that need lots of flexibility to install Office on many computers in their organizations.  VL versions of Microsoft products are different from consumer licenses in that the licenses are not perpetual (meaning the licenses are issued on finite term agreements and can and do expire if not renewed with Microsoft) and the licenses are not transferrable outside of the organization that entered into the VL agreement with Microsoft.

 

The report is telling us that the installation is "Genuine," which means that the organization that originally entered into a VL agreement to use the software has been renewing the agreement with Microsoft.  But that organization is the rightful user of the software installed on your computer.  If you purchased the computer from that organization, the right to use the software did not come with the computer just because the software was erroneously left on the computer...it should have been uninstalled before the sale.  So any use of the software not by the organization that has the VL agreement with Microsoft is considered "nongenuine" use of the software.

 

If you purchased the computer from an individual and not the organization that holds the VL agreement with Microsoft, then that previous owner may have improperly installed the software.  They may have stolen the software from the original organization, or maybe stumbled across a download of it on the internet.

 

So the bottom line is that the Office on your computer is a nongenuine installation which should be uninstalled from the computer.  As you have plans to cleanly install Windows on this computer, the process of cleanly installing Windows will erase the nongenuine Office.

 

For more information see the many posts in this forum:  http://social.microsoft.com/Forums/en-US/genuineoffice/threads

 

If you want to use Office, please purchase a proper consumer license for Office. If your Office needs are modest, you can try the free online Office Web Apps here: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/web-apps/

rohn007 say:

No you do not have to buy a disk. All of the default Office 365 / 2016 installs work from Online downloads (after the initial activation you do have the option of downloading a "full offline installer" file).  Even if you spend extra money to buy an install disk you need the online connection because the install disks now only contain the installer "stub" which depends on online download for the majority of the final install.

There is no discounted "upgrade" from 2007 to 2016/365.  You will have to buy a full license and install it.

Yes, there are financial consequences.

Office 365 is only a "subscription". It is a keep-paying-every-year type license. Depending on the number of computers you are working with 365 will eventually cost you more, much more, than a one-time payment license.  Especially since you are willing to run older versions until they go off support. 

For example, assuming one computer Office 2016 Home and Student will cost you US$150, while 365 Personal costs US$70 per year.  After 2 and a bit years the cost will be the same. At the end of 10 years 365 will have cost you AT LEAST (if not more!) US$700 vs US$150 for Home and Student.

There are a number of significant differences between what you get with 365 subscription vs the 2016 1-time-payment licenses so you have to decide what you want / need to pay for.

This wiki goes into some of the differences:

I Need Office, What Should I Get: Pros vs Cons – WIKI – buy Office

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/msoffice/wiki/msoffice_install-mso_other/i-need-office-what-should-i-get-pros-vs-cons/2e75ea21-f8d0-4cde-9764-13b02cde46a7?tm=1478718433145

You will have to decide for yourself. You can to trade off long term cost and features If your needs are very simple, you won’t need to spend much or work with one of the free options. But if you are a more “advanced” user, using multiple Office applications and sharing documents/files with others you will have to pay for one of the Office 365/2016 bundles.
