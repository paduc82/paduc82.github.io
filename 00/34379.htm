shannonmase say:

Windows uses too much space by keeping old Windows Updates, Hibernation files, hosting a huge Page file, Unneeded Applications, reserved Recycle Bin space, and copies of outdated compatibility files in the massive WindowsSxS directory.

Follow these steps at your own risk (or benefit ;) ) to get this much needed space back in your system.

   1.) On start, swipe up to reveal apps, select the ones you do not want (manufacture apps) ,click on uninstall. Some apps will take you to Programs and Features where you will have to uninstall applications manually. From here you can uninstall other apps not needed. 

    (This will free up space by removing unused applications/programs from the system)

   2.) Swipe from left edge of screen, click search, type in Windows Update, click search for updates, install each update one at a time from bottom up. (windows will brick if you try to install all at once) repeat this step till every update is installed and you no longer can get any more updates. Once all updates are installed including all service packages, open explorer, hold down on the C: Windows directory, Click properties, Disc cleanup, Clean up System Files, check all boxes, click Ok, and finally Delete Files. This will take up to several hours to complete.

     (This will free up space by removing the downloaded update data already installed on your system)

   3.) Open Command Prompt from the app list by holding your finger down and clicking Run As Administrator. Type in the provided commands (spaces and all) and hit enter one at a time.

Dism.exe /online /Cleanup-Image /StartComponentCleanup

Dism.exe /online /Cleanup-Image /StartComponentCleanup /ResetBase

Dism.exe /online /Cleanup-Image /SPSuperseded

powercfg -h off

Next go to advanced settings in power options and tell it to shut down when critical, not hibernate.

     (This will free up space by cleaning out the WindowsSxS folder and removing the massive hibernate file eating GB's on your system)

   4.) Click on recycle bin, go to recycle bin settings, lower it to 200mb.

    (This frees up space by limiting the reserved space for deleted files)

   5.) Go to explorer, on left column hold your finger down on My PC. Click on properties. Go to Advanced System Settings. Under Performance click Setting. Click the Advanced tab. Under virtual memory click Change. Uncheck the Auto Manage, click custom size, set minimum to 200mb, and maximum to 800mb.

     (This frees up space by shrinking the 2Gb pagefile down, increase as necessary)

   6.) Buy a micro USB otg cable for a flash drive to connect to the tablet. Buy at least an 8 GB flash drive. (some removable hard drives will not get enough power from the port to operate) Plug the Otg cable into the tablet micro USB port and the USB into the otg cable. Swipe left and click search, type in Control Panel. Open Control Panel and click recovery. Click on Create A Recovery Drive. Make sure the checkbox marked Copy the recovery partit.. . is checked. Follow the prompts until the recovery partition is now on the USB. It will ask you if you want to delete the recovery partition. Click on it and wait for the process to complete. Safely store the flash drive with your recovery somewhere safe.

     (This frees up space by removing the massive recovery partition and giving it back to the main drive)

Congrats you just gained 12 GB (I gained this much) Remember that when you install windows programs in the future to install them to your SD card whenever possible. If you game on Steam, install Steam on your system first, go to the Program Files directory and copy the Steam folder to your SD card, delete everything in that directory except for SteamApps and Steam.exe. Run Steam.exe and allow to update, log in, downloaded games will install on your SD card in the Steam folder. Also make sure you only use a 2 amp charger for the tablet as a regular phone charger will not charge correctly. Enjoy your tablet. :)

Probably either a driver error (more likely) or faulty memory. Start by running the internal memory diagnostic (just type 'memory diagnostic' into the start/search line to begin). If it passes, then we may need to analyze the dump file that is created.

If the error messages are consistent - same verbiage and error code, it's more likely to be a driver issue. If they are always changing, then the problem is more likely to be faulty hardware. Start with the memory, then perhaps a hard drive diagnostic. If these pass, then the next step is to swap in known good hardware starting with the power supply.

At any point in the process you described did you run CHKDSK or SFC /SCANNOW?

1.  Completely uninstall iTunes following the directions here: https://discussions.apple.com/docs/DOC-6562#reinstall (don't reinstall just yet).  Then do the following:

  • Select All (CTRL+A) and press DELete.  Click SKIP for any files than won't delete
  • Navigate to C:\Windows\Temp and again Select All (CTRL+A) and press DELete.  Click SKIP for any files than won't delete
  • Empty the Recycle Bin again

2.  Close all open programs, then click Start, type cmd.exe in the Search box, then right-click on the link to cmd.exe and select "Run as administrator."  Respond as needed to the UAC prompt.  Type the following in the black Command Prompt window and press Enter

     chkdsk c: /f

Type Y to answer the question and then reboot your computer

Windows will check your disk after the restart.  It may take some time, depending on the size of the disk.

Assuming that you elected not to sit and watch as chkdsk did its work, you won't have seen its report.  Follow the directions here (use Option One, but skip steps 5 and 6) to see what happened:  https://www.sevenforums.com/tutorials/96938-check-disk-chkdsk-read-event-viewer-log.html

4.  Reinstall following the last 2 paragraphs of the directions from step 1 (https://discussions.apple.com/docs/DOC-6562#reinstall)

If the above doesn't work, please report at what point the problem happened and include the complete text of any error message.  Read through the document linked at steps 1 and 4 for additional suggestions, including what to do in the case of some specific error messages during the installation process.
