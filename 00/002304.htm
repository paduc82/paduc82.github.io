need to identify disadvantages of using different headers on even and - Microsoft Community

amyuh say:

need to identify disadvantages of using different headers on even and odd pages in Word 2010

Original title: Headers

I am preparing for an exam and need to identify disadvantages of using different headers on even and odd pages in Word 2010.  Any thoughts?  Thanks for your help. 

suzanne s. barnhill say:

If you have a document that requires different odd and even headers or footers, then you will want to enable this option, but it does sometimes have unintended consequences. When you select "Different Odd and Even" (or sometimes even Mirror Margins), Word makes the assumption that you will be duplexing the document. This means that Word will insist that the odd pages be recto (right-hand pages), which will have the following effects:

  1. If you restart numbering at 1 in a new section, yourNext Page section break will be converted to (or treated as) an Odd Page break. If the previous section ends on an odd page, a blank even page will be inserted before the section break.
  2. That in itself would not necessarily cause problems, but if you are using Page X of Y numbering in each section and using SectionPages as the Y, you will find that Word starts counting the pages not with page 1 but with the blank page. This means that the last page of the section will always be Page x of x+1 (for example, Page 6 of 7).
  3. Numbering aside, when Word thinks you intend to duplex a document, it may accommodate the printer (printers are notoriously lazy and don't like to print portrait and landscape pages back to back) by changing your Next Page section break to Odd Page when you have section breaks to change the page orientation from Portrait to Landscape or vice versa. Again, you'll get a blank even page.

The primary thing to realize about Different Odd and Even, however, is that it can't be enabled for specific sections (unlike Different First Page). When you enable it, it is enabled for the entire document.

That said, there is no guarantee that these are the answers your exam will be looking for. If you have a textbook, it would be preferable to refer to it.

rod gill say: I assume by Persian calendar you mean Thursday and Friday non-working?
Select Project, Change Working Time.
Either edit the Standard Calendar or create a new Calendar.

Select the Working Weeks tab in the lower half of the Change Working Time window then click the Details button.
