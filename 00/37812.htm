missing cd or dvd say: Dear Icicle167.


Symptoms - cd.dvd device does not appear on my computer and device manager.  When the computer boots up, the lens arm can be heard. Also, the eject button ejects the the cd tray, and the led light works.

Every device has a chip in it that identifies it to the computer. This is what is seen on device manager.  If the ID chip cannot be read at boot time, the computer will not recognize the device.  This means the data I/O flow circuit of the device is not sending information to the computer.

First, determine if the problem is caused by hardware. To do so, check your jumper to see if it is correctly set. Check that the cable is correctly installed in the motherboard and the cd.dvd device.   Check for bent pins on the motherboard and cd.dvd device. Check the bios setup so that cd.dvd device is set up as the first boot device, then try to boot from a cd that is known to be bootable.  If the boot frorm cd.dvd fails, this means your computer cannot recognize the cd.dvd device. Therefore, pursuing a soultion with software is useless.  At this time, you have verified that the jumper is set correctly, and the cable is correctly installed to the motherboard and the cd.dvd device, and there are no bent pins on the motherboard or cd.dvd device. Now check the integrity of the cable by conneting to another device on the computer that is known to work, such as a hard dirve.  If the other device does not work, replace the cable and try again  Once you have verified the cable works, you have eliminated the possiblity that a connection is causing the problem.  Swap your cd/dvd devices form another computer. Check as follows:

1. My cd.dvd device works on another computer.

2. A cd.dvd device from another computer works on my computer.

3. My cd.dvd device does not work on another computer.

4. A cd.dvd device known to work from another computer does not work on my comuter.

If sutuation 2 and 3 are true, you MAY want consider replacing your cd.dvd device.

If situation 1 and 4 are true,  There MAY be a problem with one or more electonic components on your motherboard that are out tolerance, thus causing your cd/dvd device to not be recognized.  A repair or replacement of the motherboard MAY be required.

I hope this information has been helpful to you.
