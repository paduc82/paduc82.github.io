[How To] Connect to Xbox Live using your Laptop/Computer - Microsoft Community

palihustlaod say:

[How To] Connect to Xbox Live using your Laptop/Computer

Hey guys. I decided to make a video and thread on how to connect your Xbox onto Live. My router doesn't like when my Xbox (or PS3) connecting to it. I have a DLink DIR-655 Router. I found a way to use my laptop to connect to my Xbox and play on Live.


Requirements:
- Windows based computer / laptop
- Ethernet Cable
- Xbox 360

This method works on both the Phat (old model) and the Slim Xbox.

1. Connect the Ethernet cable to the Xbox BUT not to the computer.

2. Click "Start". Type CMD in the search box and press enter.

3. Type ipconfig and scroll up until you see something that says "Wireless LAN adapter Wireless Network Connection:" Leave this open

4. Go to Start -> Control Panel -> Network and Internet -> Network and Sharing Center. Open the left, you will see "Change Adapter Settings". Click it.

5. Highlight "Wireless Network Connection" AND "Local Area Connection"

6. Right click onto one of them and select "Add to Bridge"

7. You will have a new icon that pops up and says "Network Bridge".

8. Connect the Ethernet Cable to your laptop / computer now.

9. Right click "Network Bridge" and select "Properties"

10. Under the "This connection uses the following items:", click on "Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IPv4)"

11. "Properties" is now a selectable button. Press on "Properties"

12. Press "Use the following IP Address" and go back to the CMD prompt (where you typed in ipconfig)

13. Under IPv4 Address, you should see something like "192.168.0.xxx" (xxx is where there are numbers). For example, it may say 192.168.0.108

14. Go back to the "Use the following IP Address" and add 10 to the last number of the IP. For example, if it says 192.168.0.108, change it to 192.168.0.118

15. For Subnet mask, it will automatically say "255.255.255.0"

16. Where it says "Default Gateway", type in 192.168.0.1

17. For "Preferred DNS server" under the "Use the following DNS server Address", type in 192.168.0.1

18. Check the "Validate settings upon exit". Don't close it yet.

19. Turn your Xbox on. Go to Settings -> System -> Network Settings -> Wired Network -> Configure network.

20. Under IP settings, select Manual.

21. Under IP Address, add 1 to the IP address you changed for network bridge. Since we changed it to 192.168.0.118 for Network Bridge, on the Xbox, change it to 192.168.0.119

22. Under Subnet mask, write "255.255.255.0"

23. Under Gateway, write "192.168.0.1" and hit done when you finish.

24. Go to DNS Settings and change it to Manual.

25. Under Primary DNS Server, write "192.168.0.1". Hit done when you finish.

26. When you checked the "Validate settings upon exit". Go back to it and hit  OK on it and hit OK on Network Bridge Properties.

27. Go back and select "Test Xbox LIVE Connection"

28. Let it run the test and you should pass!

Note: When you finish playing Xbox or when you turn it off, you must follow these steps so your computer stops the bridging and allows it to connect back to the internet.

1. Unplug the Ethernet cable from your Xbox and computer.

2. Go to Network Bridge, right click it and press "Disable"

3. Highlight "Wireless Network Connection" AND "Local Area Connection" and right click it. Select "Remove from Bridge"

4. You are done!

Note again if you want to play on Live.

1. Connect your Ethernet Cable.

2. Highlight "Wireless Network Connection" AND "Local Area Connection" and press "Add to Bridge"

3. Right click Network Bridge and press "Enable"

4. You are good to go again!

andre da costa say:

If its an OEM System Builder disc, yes, you can use it. If its a manufacturers OEM Recovery Disc, no, you can't use it.

For the past 5 years Microsoft made it quite easy for users of the Windows 7 operating system to obtain reinstall media. If you lost your retail installation disc or recovery media; either it was damaged or faulty; you could download a copy of the edition you have a license for from an affiliate website named Digital River. Most recently, Microsoft ended availability of reinstall media which you could download as a .ISO file from Digital River, which is a digital replica of a optical disc.
This was especially handy for persons who could not easily obtain recovery media from the manufacturer, did not want to pay the cost required to obtain it or preferred a clean configuration without the manufacturers bundled software or even a non-functional recovery partition. It was easy to use, all you had to do was reinstall and reactivate using the product key located on the certificate of authenticity and download any appropriate drivers from the manufacturers website.
Since this option is no longer available, what are your options? See article for details:
How to: What are my options for obtaining Windows 7 reinstall media?
Make sure to scroll down and read the section:
What to do if you cannot get recovery media from your manufacturer, refuse to use or purchase it or the Microsoft Software Recovery Website is not working?
