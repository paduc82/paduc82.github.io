joel ivory johnson say:

That's strage, I never get that phrase at the bottom of my facebook messages.

It took a while to round up enough Windows Mobile devices to do this, but I did a test and I found a pattern that gives some insight as to what is going on. I sent an e-mail to myself and specified a reply-to address. Some of the devices respected the address, others didn't.

Of the devices that didn't respect the reply-to address the thing they had in common is that they were all made by HTC. The devices I tried from other OEMs didn't do this. I also tried the Window Mobile emulators for both versions 6.1 and 6.5 of Windows Mobile and they respected the address.

I used the emulators because OEMs are allowed to customize their individual Window sMobile operating system builds and the emulators only have Microsoft components on them and are more representational of the OS when it ships from Microsoft to the manufacturer. Given that the mail client on the emulators respects the reply-to address I think that what you've observed is the result of an OEM customization to the mail client.

What type of Windows Mobile device do you have? I used a Toucp Pro II and HD II when the reply-to address was ignored.
