Standard inserting of figures does not work, as they move with the text.


I have used text boxes, this works more or less: While they stay on top as they should, they are still anchored to the text and the text does not follow seamlessly around them (leading to gaps/half empty pages appearing in the Word document before the figures if you change text), and you might delete the figure be accidentally deleting the part of the text where you originally inserted the text box.


Is there a good procedure/method/trick to keep figures in the same position on the same page, regardless of what I do with the text?


Thank you!


bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
Move the Anchor to a paragraph lower on the 'page' containing the image.

The main cause of your dilemma is having a floating object as the first item on a page.

If you continue to revise the document content you'll still have to make adjustments to compensate as the text reflows... If the anchor paragraph is forced to a different page the graphic will go with it. There also may be instances where you'll need to turn off Widow/Orphan control in the last paragraph on the page preceding the graphic.

FWIW, my personal preference is to not even insert floating graphics until the text content is nearly complete. Final positioning of objects is one of the last steps I take. Otherwise you waste a lot of time chasing your tail.

daniel g. grau say:
The display of gridlines through Table > Gridlines is not a document setting, but a user preference. Thus, you cannot control what the next user of the document will see because gridline display information is not saved with the document.

Once you enable this setting, any document you open next will show the gridlines. Likewise if you disable it.

Howeverand this is the confusing aspectif you simultaneously open two documents and set them so that one has them showing and the other not, the next document you open will inherit the gridline display of the frontmost document.
