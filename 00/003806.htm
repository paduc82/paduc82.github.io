ilyasqasim say:

HOW TO CREATE MENU SHEET IN EXCEL 2007

mike h.. say:

Hi,

Hi,

You could re-invent the wheel and create your own with hyperlinks or you could use the existing functionality within Excel.

Just to the left of Sheet1 tab you can see some small icons, right click them and you get a popup of all the worksheet names, simply click the one you want to go to.

jllatham say:

But if you really want this 'table of contents' feature, take the code below and copy it into the 'menu' sheet's code module (Right click on that sheet's name tab and select [View Code]).  This will refresh the list each time you select that sheet, assuring that the list is up to date with the latest content of the workbook.

Private Sub Worksheet_Activate()
  Dim anyWS As Worksheet
 
  Application.ScreenUpdating = False
  Me.Cells.Clear
  For Each anyWS In Worksheets
    If anyWS.Name <> Me.Name Then
      Range("A" & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Offset(1, 0).Formula = _
       "=HYPERLINK(" & Chr$(34) & "#" & anyWS.Name & "!A1" & Chr$(34) & _
       "," & Chr$(34) & anyWS.Name & Chr$(34) & ")"
    End If
  Next
End Sub

After you've done that, identify a cell that is empty on all of the other worksheets and enter a formula like this into that cell.  For this purpose, Sheet1 is the name of your 'menu' sheet:

=HYPERLINK("#Sheet1!A1","Return to Menu")

Now copy that cell to all of the other sheets in the workbook.  If you know how to select all of the sheets (other than the menu sheet) at one time, you can actually do that setup by selecting them all and typing the formula into a cell that is empty/available on all of them.  It will look like a hyperlink on the first sheet only, but it will act like one on all of them.

tom ogilvy say:

ignoring the fact you said vlookup would not work with your data, then just to illustrate, I was successful with this:

=CONCATENATE(VLOOKUP("A",$G$3:$H$7,2,FALSE),",",VLOOKUP("C",$G$3:$H$7,2,FALSE),",",VLOOKUP("E",$G$3:$H$7,2,FALSE))

G3:H7 looke like

A   1

B   2

C   3

D   4

E    5

and this formula produced  1,3,5

You can replace the vlookup with functions that return the appropriate values.
