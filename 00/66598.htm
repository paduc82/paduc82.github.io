a. user say:

Hi Larry,

I would suggest you to try the following steps and see if Windows Updates work.

Method 1:

1. Swipe in from the right edge of the screen, tap Settings, and then tap Change PC settings. (If you're using a mouse, point to the upper-right corner of the screen, move the mouse pointer down, click Settings, and then click Change PC settings.)

2. Tap or click Network, and then tap or click Connections.

3. Tap or click the connection you want to change, and then, under Data usage, turn Set as a metered connection to off.

Method 2:

Clear the DNS (Domain Name System) cache.

a. Swipe in from the right edge of the screen, and then tap Search.

(If you're using a mouse, point to the upper-right corner of the screen, move the mouse pointer down, and then click Search.)

b. In the search box, type “command prompt” without quotes.

c. In the list of results, swipe down on or right-clickCommand Prompt, and then tap or click Run as administrator.

d. At the command prompt, type “ipconfig /flushdns” without quotes and press Enter

 

Method 3:

Try to obtain IP address automatically.

 

Please let us know the results.
