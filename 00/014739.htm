random numbers for given mean and standard deviation - Microsoft Community

petros g say:

random numbers for given mean and standard deviation

I need to produce a series of random numbers given a specific mean and standard deviation. Any ideas? Thanks!

joeu2004 say: Petros G wrote:
The http://sulprobil.com/html/norminv.html addresses the "eaxctly on target issue" although I can not apply it. I tried it and unless I am doing sth wrong it fixes the stdev number but not the mean (could it be because my mean target is small? i.e. 0.1%)

The paradigm worked for me.  I tried it with a desired mean of 0.1% and standard deviation of 0.01%.

Perhaps you got confused by Bernd's use of array formulas, which I believe is an unnecessary complication.  Try the following equivalent approach, which I hope you can expand or reduce as needed for the actual number of random values that you want.

1. Put the desired mean into B1 and the desired standard deviation into B2.

2. To generate 10 random values, enter the following formula ("trial value") into A3 and copy down through A12:

=NORMINV(RAND(),$B$1,$B$2)

Note the use of absolute references ("$" cell names).

If you want to "freeze" those values, copy A3:A12 and paste-special-value back into A3:A12.

3. Calculate the mean and standard deviation of the "trial values".   In A1, calculate the mean:  =AVERAGE(A3:A12).  In A2, calculate the standard deviation:  =STDEVP(A3:A12).

Note:  Some might prefer to use STDEV.  I believe STDEVP is the better choice because the "trial values" represent an entire set of numbers, not a sampling from a larger set of numbers.

4. Enter the following formula ("actual value") into B3 and copy down through B12:

=$B$1+(A3-$A$1)*$B$2/$A$2

Note the use of relative and absolute references; specifically, A3 is the only relative reference (no "$"), which will change as the formula is copied.

If you want to "freeze" those values, copy B3:B12 and paste-special-value back into B3:B12.  Then you can delete the values in A1, A2 and A3:A12.

5. To double-check, calculate the mean and standard deviation of the "actual values".  In C1, the mean is:  =AVERAGE(B3:B12).  In C2, the standard deviation is:  =STDEVP(B3:B12).  (Use STDEV here if you used STDEV in step #3.)

Note:  Do not be surprised if these differ by an infinitesimal amount from the desired mean and standard deviation.  I think it is misleading to expect an "exact" match, due to the way that Excel does arithmetic ("binary floating pointing").  By "infinitesimal amount", I mean:  a visible difference in the 15th or 14th significant digit, or an invisible difference that causes IF(B1-C1=0,TRUE) to return FALSE.
