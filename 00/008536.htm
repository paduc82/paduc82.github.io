The error 0x80070005 means "access denied" which indeed happens in most of the cases when the command isn't run with "admin privileges".

regsvr32 "C:\Program Files (x86)\Internet Explorer\ieproxy.dll"

vincentchoy say:

I am so sorry Matt. Its a real bummer to be in your situation. Let me explain to you what is happening -

Office365 is a subscription license. You pay a monthly subscription to use the products. Once you stop the subscription you can no longer use the product.

Think of it as renting a video vs buying a video.

To download the product, you login to your office365 account to download it.

As it is a subscription license, it doesn't use a product key to determine authenticity. Instead your installed product checks with Microsoft approx once a month (where a internet connection is required) to see if your subscription is current. If it isn't then it will go into reduce functionality mode. It is not a perpetual license (which you had with Office 2010 Pro).
