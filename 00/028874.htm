jllatham say:

Well, I'm not good with tables, and there's probably a neat trick with table layouts that will do it really well.  So, not being good with tables, I'll stick with good old VLOOKUP()

 

With your dropdown on a sheet in cell B1,  and assuming your table is over on a sheet named Ralphs Travel Service,

then in B2:

=IFERROR(VLOOKUP(B1,'Ralphs Travel Service'!B:C,2,False),"")

and in B3

=IFERROR(VLOOKUP(B1,'Ralphs Travel Service'!B:D,3,False),"")

 

 

The IFERROR() should never come into play if your dropdown is getting its list of selections from column B (Product Name) of the table, but it never hurts to be safe.  If no match is found instead of showing "#N/A!" it will simply return a blank stare.

 

Actually, a litttle experimenting shows that if your table is named Table1, then this would work, for the Description field:

=VLOOKUP(B1,Table1[[Product Name]:[Details]],2,FALSE)

and for the 'details'info:

=VLOOKUP(B1,Table1[[Product Name]:[Details]],3,FALSE)

 

Here are a couple of sites showing how to set up your data validation using a column from a table, if you want that also:
