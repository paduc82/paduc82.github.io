steve rindsberg say:

As I recall, this came up once before and we concluded that there's no way to make PPT do this, short of writing and installing an add-in. 

But

Go to Office Button | PowerPoint Options | Advanced

Under Display, remove the check next to "Show all windows in the task bar"

Click OK

 

Now there will be a small black X beneath the bigger red X in the upper right corner of the PPT window.

Click the smaller x when your new blank presentation opens automatically; that'll close the presentation w/o closing PowerPoint altogether.

 

Or just leave the blank presentation open. Normally it's not going to hurt anything or eat enough resources to be worth worrying about.

How do I delete the custom defined bullets that I created in Word Mac - Microsoft Community

harshjo say:

How do I delete the custom defined bullets that I created in Word Mac 2015?

I created 4 different custom bullets with the exact same picture and I can't differentiate between them, so its very annoying to try to select the one I want whenever I need the bullets.
john korchok say:

You can get rid of all of them by following these steps:

  1. Close Word.
  2. In the Finder, hold down the Alt key while clicking on the Go menu and choosing Library.
  3. In the Library folder, open Containers.
  4. Drag com.microsoft.Word to your desktop.
  5. Restart Word. It takes a little longer than usual, but when it does, your custom bullets are gone and you can start over.

While you can't delete existing bullet styles, you can change them to another format that reminds you which one is the wrong one:

  1. In Word, click on the down-pointing arrowhead to the right of the Bullets icon and choose Define New Bullet. 
  2. Select a bullet you created that is incorrect. Click on the Bullet... button and choose a bullet. Your picture will be replaced with a bullet.
  3. Repeat until all your incorrect Picture bullets are replaced except for the one you want to keep. Now your correct picture bullet is the only one left in the Gallery, so it's easy to remember which one to choose.

john korchok say:
Yes, the general idea is still correct. However I did a little further research and found that you can close Word, open Users/YourUserName/Library/Containers/com.microsoft.Word/Data/Library/Preferences and just drag ListGal.dat to the desktop. This will delete only your custom bullets and no other preferences. ListGal.dat will be automatically regenerated the next time you open Word.

karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

Just to ensure the understanding is correct, assume that the folder you are "saving it in" refers to file folder versus an Outlook folder. If that's correct, the <received date> is not <lost> but is part of the message itself like any other information pretaining to the message and is stored inside the file.  <File Folders> only provide dates in reference to when a file was actually created and modified in the folder which has nothing to do with whatever may be contained within the file itself (i.e. <received date>)

Seems a better approach would be to create a new PST file (along with a new mail folder for these items) and save the messages in this PST file. You can then sort/view the messages any way you wish and need only backup up one file (the new PST file).
