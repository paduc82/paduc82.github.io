mikenusn say:

While you may be able to do this as a macro, there is a MUCH easier way.  Your friend probably added "Paste Hyperlink" to your "Quick Access Toolbar" (you'll find this toolbar next to the little office circle in the top left of your screen and it usually houses the "Save, Undo, Redo" buttons).  You'll notice if you tap the ALT button, a number of ghost numbers and letters pop up on your menu bar. 

The letters usually correspond to different tabs (technically called "ribbons" by Office) like the home, insert, and formulas tabs.  The ghosted letter/numbers allow you to navigate the menu bar from your keyboard, for instance tapping Alt displays them and tapping "H", by default, navigates you to the home tab, then typing the letter or number corresponding to any command you want (like '1' for Bold) is the same as clicking "home" then "bold" and if the command has two letters (Like "AL" for Left Aligning text) tapping the "A" selects ALL of the 2 letter commands beginning with A, then typing the second letter selects the command itself.

So how can the ghost letters help you with Pasting a Hyperlink you ask?  Simply do one of the following:

  1. Right click in the menu bar, and you should see the "customize quick access toolbar" option pop up
  2. Click the little "DVD Eject" looking button to the right of the little bar with ""Save, Undo, Redo" buttons and you should see the option for "More Commands..." under the customization drop down menu
  3. Click the "Office Button" (upper left circle with the funky looking windows button) and click "Excel Options" and then "Customize"

Next, select "All Commands" under "Choose commands from:" and "For all documents (default)" under "Customize Quick Access Toolbar:".  Scroll through all the commands on the left (they're alphabetized") till you find "Paste as Hyperlink" and either double click it or click it and then click the "Add >>" button in the middle.  You'll see it appear in the box to the right. Now single-click it again, then use the up and down arrows on the right to move it to the 4th position (position in the toolbar determines which ALT+'#' combo activates the command, so if it's the 4th command in the list, ALT+4 executes it in Excel).  Last, and MOST IMPORTANTLY, click OK in the bottom right, don't just close the options pop-up or click cancel, otherwise all this effort did nothing.  Verify your positioning and the key combination by clicking back into your excel workbook and then tapping "ALT", look at the QAT and verify what number your new command is linked to.

This should fix your problem, but more importantly, hopefully shows you how you can do this for ANY Excel command you use repeatedly that does not have an existing CTRL+___ shortcut that that you know (doesn't matter if one exists, if you don't know it then this will create and ALT shortcut), simply add it to the QAT, tap ALT and figure out what button combo is linked to it.

Best of luck!
