[Guide] Moonlighter - Tips & Tricks To Start Your - Microsoft Community

half fish half say:

[Guide] Moonlighter - Tips & Tricks To Start Your Adventure On A Good Foot

Moonlighter is a clever twist on the action RPG genre that came out on Xbox One and Windows 10 last year. It was very recently on sale via the Xbox Store Countdown promotion, however, so many players may be coming to it for the first time. I, myself, was one of those players. I've found the dungeon exploration/shop management aspect of it incredibly fun and addictive but I definitely experienced a few bumps along the way. That being said here's some stuff I learned the hard way that you can take into your own playthrough for a smoother starting experience:

1. Don't let the difficulty settings intimidate you.

Moonlighter is one of those games that gives you multiple difficulties and goes so far as to suggest how the game was meant to be played. Normal is the default, Hard is the recommended difficulty, and in addition there is an Easy difficulty and a Very Hard. I don't usually stray from the default when I'm playing but for some reason I decided to jump into Very Hard sight unseen. You know what happened? I found myself having a great time. It makes getting equipment upgrades early on very necessary (unless you never ever get hit) but it's not at all horrendously brutal. That being said, Hard is probably truly the ideal difficulty for those looking for a reasonable challenge. Hard is the new normal.

2. Work with the weapon that you like best... But get the bow.

If you do any reading up on this game you'll likely come across people talking about how the Big Sword is the one true weapon. Honestly? I'm playing on the hardest difficulty and the weapon I fell in love with was the fists. I loved the speed and I loved the crit on the third hit. I just found it the most fun, but I can't recommend the bow and arrow high enough as your secondary.  It won't be the fastest kill (especially early on) but sometimes you just are going to want a long range solution.

Also keep in mind that if you have your eye on a weapon but can't stretch the cost or materials, keep an eye out for skeletons in the dungeons. You can find the base level of every weapon from them if you're lucky enough!

3. Keep your good loot close and your gooder loot even closer.

When you look at your bag you'll notice a single row on the top separated from the other rows. That topmost, isolated row is basically like your pockets. The rest is your backpack. If and when you die you lose everything in your pack but keep everything in your pockets. So if you feel like you're in a high risk situation, consider pre-loading your most valuable stuff into the topmost row.

4. Preload your potions.

I found out the hard way that your inventory isn't accessible during combat. Your potions also don't automatically replenish when you burn through them, even if you have extras in your bag. See where I'm getting at? So if you just got through a really difficult rumble, take the time to equip another grip of potions.

5. Focus on getting to the bottom of the first dungeon as your first goal.

As you progress down the three floors of the Golem Dungeon you'll unlock the three major dungeon tools of the game: The pendant, the portal, and the mirror. All three are essential for safe and sane runs.

The pendant allows you to escape the dungeon into town at the cost of gold, which increases as you progress through the floors and dungeons. Extra pro tip: The pendant works even in combat, so if you find that you've bit off more than you can chew just hold down B and get out of there with your loot intact.

The portal is like a Town Portal in Diablo, allowing you to escape into town and return to the floor you were at later, and as such costs a bit more than the Pendant escape. It requires access to your bag though making emergency escapes with it impossible.

The mirror is how you triage your overflowing bags and generate gold for an emergency escape. It's pretty simple: You take loot you don't want, dump it into the mirror, and get gold in return. It won't be nearly as much as a sale in your shop but if you're stuck in the dungeon and forgot to save enough gold for the pendant, you can dust loot to afford an escape. And no, gold does not drop in the dungeon so keep that in mind. Super extra pro tip: Don't leave loot behind! Even if your bags are full and enemies are dropping trash, put that trash in the mirror if you can. It might take some inventory shuffling to make it happen but if you can do it, do it. Gold is gold.

6. Cash Register and Bed upgrades are top tier.

So you're making money, making sales, working on getting weapon upgrades but what do you put money into in regards to your shop? I highly recommend Register and Bed upgrades. The earlier you can install a better register the better because it increases the income of every sale ever so slightly. Over time this will add up to a lot of free gold. The bed doesn't explain itself super well but it essentially gives you a magic shield that improves with each tier of bed. This extra protection is lost on hit and only replenished when you leave the dungeon, but it's extra survivability and you're going to want that. And no, you don't have to sleep to reap the benefits of your bed. It's that good.

7. The Alchemist is super useful.

In that same vein the Alchemist makes for a great early target because she sells potions and even crafts them at a lower cost if you have the materials for them. You're going to want potions on demand so you're going to want her help. Plus she's just adorable.

8. Customer feedback is important!

When you start selling things in your shop you will likely notice the cute little speech bubbles and such over their head. Those give you an indication of several things. The primary purpose is to home in your pricing, as overly high prices will discourage sales and overly low prices just straight up lose you money. Ideally you want the happy face without the coins in the eyes, as seeing literal gold in their eyes means they are ripping you off big time. Sometimes it takes some trial and error to find the perfect price but don't despair! Whenever you get a new reaction it gets marked down in your in-game notes allowing you to reference them later. It will also auto-save the price you set previously allowing you to just dump your loot on the table and get to selling. Just keep in mind that if popularity for the item is off-center or it is purchased by a super rich NPC (indicated by a top hat and monocle speech bubble) it will not record those prices in the book.

9. Keep the good stuff away from the door.

Before long word of your shop will stretch far and wide including patrons of all sorts, even those who aim to take your hard earned loot without payment. Yes, there are thieves in the world of Moonlighter. Luckily they're easy to spot and have a predictable pattern. When a thief enters your shop it will be, as usual, indicated by a bubble over their head signifying that they're a ne'er do well. They will wander around your shop a bit, look from side to side, wander a little more and then snatch something before rushing toward the door. If you tackle them (LT) between their theft and the door you reclaim your property into your backpack and kick them out. If you don't catch them before they reach the door, however, your loot is gone forever. That in mind it behooves you to put the really valuable stuff as far back away from the door as possible. In the very beginning you're only going to have a single small table to sell off of but this will potentially save you a lot of gold as you expand.

10. Buy once, Play Anywhere.

My last tip isn't so much an in-game tip but Moonlighter is part of the Xbox Play Anywhere program! That means once you buy it you can play it on your Xbox One and your PC without an additional purpose. So if you're at your desk and need to kill a little time, fire up Windows 10 and make a few runs into the depths!

Moonlighter is a neat concept with an addictive gameplay loop and I hope my tips help smooth out your onboarding experience with it. If you have any tips of your own that you'd like to share with the community please comment below!

Play nice and have fun!

    a. user say:

    The behavior you experienced is very possible. A PC is no more capable of withstanding a power outage than any other electrical appliance.

    You should get an uninterruptible power supply - UPS. A UPS for a home PC is inexpensive, and it provides battery backup power for power outages like the one you experienced. A UPS also includes a surge protector, so it's like getting both for the price of one.
