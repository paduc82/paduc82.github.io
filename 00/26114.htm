Your heading style is formatted as All Caps, but the underlying case of the headings varies. When you are typing in an All Caps paragraph, it's easy to lose track of what you're actually typing, which may be all lowercase, Caps and lowercase (which is what you want), or all caps if you have Caps Lock on. That's one reason I like to have an All Caps toggle button on my toolbar so I can toggle the formatting on and off quickly to make sure I've used the correct capitalization. But of course the TOC provides this same information. You could format the TOC 1 style as All Caps to cover up the problem, but the preferred action is to go back to each of the headings in question and retype it with the correct Caps and lowercase formatting.

The special problem of chapters 4 and 9 suggests that you don't actually have a heading for those chapters at all.

"Big data" refers to large spreadsheets in Excel as far as Office goes. Beyond that, <currently> there is no benefit of any significance using 64bit but potential issues with other software which may or may not ever be an issue for you.
