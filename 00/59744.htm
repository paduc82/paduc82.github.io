a. user say:

The SAFE_OS phase in a Windows 10 upgrade is where the 'image' (the actual code) is copied from Microsoft to your hard drive and installation begins. Upgrades typically fail in SAFE_OS because the copy of Windows 10 already on your computer is damaged, so the upgrade cannot proceed.

The usual way forward is to do a clean install. This lays down a fresh, squeaky-clean copy of Windows on your hard drive. You're actually starting from scratch. Make sure you backup all your personal files (documents, music, video, etc.) because your hard drive will effectively be erased by the clean install.

A tip: Before you start doing anything you should find out whether your computer's hardware is compatible with Windows 10. You can find out from the manufacturer's technical support, but for the real low-down ask in the manufacturer's own community forum.

Here's the thing about hardware: As it gets older, its manufacturer drops support for newer operating systems. This will eventually affect everyone, so we'll all have to replace our hardware - if it can be replaced - or buy new computers altogether.

If your hardware is good to go, a clean install is the best way to install Windows. You get a brand new, fresh copy of Windows, unaffected by mistakes and problems of the past. And when it comes to mistakes and problems, there are few who do them better than I, so a clean install is always my choice.
