Then in another column I've got the hourly rate, let's say $30/hour.

In another cell I was multiplying the first data in the summation cell, by the hourly rate in the cell containing the hourly rate. The answer was way off. So I Binged it trying to find how to fix it, and I found some answers on various websites that said what I had to do was include in the multiplication I'd already done another term of 24. So if the summation cell was C20, and the cell that had the hourly rate was D20, and I wanted the answer to be in a third cell, let's call it E20, then the formula I had to put into E20 is:

C20*D20*24

This works perfectly. It gives me the answer I need. But I don't understand why it works. Why do you have to include multiplying by 24?

mike h.. say:

Hi,

If you simply multiply the summed time by the rate you will get the wrong answer and to understand why you have to understand how times are stored in Excel.

Time is stored as a decimal. For example put 12:00 in a cell and format as general and you will see it is .5 or 1/2 a day, 6 PM would be .75. Now if the sum of your cells was 12:00 hours then that's .5 and what we really want is a decimal 12 hours and that where the 24 comes on because 0.5 * 24 = 12. 24 is used because there are 24 hours in a day.

We can take this a stage further, say your rate was paid by the second and the sum of our time was again 12:00. There are 86400 seconds in a day (24hrs*60mins*60seconds) so if we multiply our 12:00 * 86400 we convert that time to seconds.

This site covers this in detail.

http://www.cpearson.com/excel/datetime.htm

Outlook 2013 - Auto text and Building blocks do not save.

Outlook 2013 - Auto text and Building blocks do not save.

Any ideas how to fix this?

jgreg7 say:

Mystery Solved

 

The problem, as it turns out, is that Outlook would not save the NormalEmail.dotm template.  As such, any changes I made were simply lost the next time I started Outlook.  After much investigation, I stumbled upon a discussion related to Word that talked about templates, and compatibility to previous versions. 

 

In office 2013, I had copied the Word Normal.dotm template to make the NormalEmail.dotm.  This was to copy all of the Autotext entries over to Outlook (the recommended way to accomplish this).  As I understand it, this is the only method to share the Autotext between Word and Outlook.  (poor design, but not part of this discussion).

 

It seems the reason the NormalEmail.dotm would not save, is that when it was in Word as the Normal.dotm, I had it set to be compatible with previous versions.   Apparently, Outlook does not know what to do with the compatibility setting, so it simply does nothing.

 

I solved the problem by saving the Word Normal.dotm template as a current version (Office 2013), then recopying it over to the NormalEmail.dotm file.  As a result, Outlook now saves the template properly.

 

I am writing all of this down in the hope that if any else has this problem, this solution may help.

Welcome to Microsoft Community. I'd be glad to assist you with your issue wherein the Search function in Outlook.com is not working. 

Non-functionality of the Search function usually occurs when your account has POP or other account configured with your email address. Please be informed that using POP Aggregation or manage email accounts with large volumes of mail can hit their max quota very quickly.  When the mailbox is very large, the Search index may stop working and require re-indexing.

As a workaround, you can use a mail application such as Windows Live Mail. An email client has different search feature and it will index all of the emails to help him/her search them quickly. Windows Live Mail is a part of the Essentials suite and can be downloaded on this link. 

  • When you run the downloaded Essentials installer, you may choose to install all the Essentials products, or just the Windows Live Mail by checking next to it.
  • The instructions for how to set up Windows Live Mail and to make it work with your account can  be found here:
