    jugglers say:

    I had this problem for several months but finally solved it.
    Using a Jaybird Freedom bluetooth headset that was working on one laptop that had been upgraded from Win7 to Win10. But on my newer laptop that had Win10 installed from scratch, the mic could not be enabled.

    Another sign that something was wrong is shown in the "Devices and Printers" settings window.

    When I opened the Jatbird Freedom device, the Supported audio services for Hands-free Headset could not be selected.

    How i solved it:
    1. Open the "Playback devices" or the "Recording deviced" sound setting (from task bar)

    2. In "Recording" tab, right click your headset device and select properties.

    3. In "General" tab, click Properties.

    4. In the new "General" tab, click "Change settings"

    5. In the new dialog, select the "Driver" tab

    6. Click "Uninstall"

    (Yes this will make you bluetooth headset unusable for now.)

    (Now the Supported audio services for Hands-free Headset  can be selected!?)

    7. Just make sure that your device is removed as a paired bluetooth device.

    8. I restared Windows just to be sure

    9. Pair your bluetooth headset again and make sure that Supported audio services for Hands-free Headset can be checked.

    Now the bluetooth headset can be enabled as "Recording deviced" in sound settings.
