cmdrkeene say:

The best way to improve performance of your computer is limit the number of programs it is running at the same time.  Simultaneous programs running mean the CPU has to spend part of its time on each program, and each program has to share resources such as memory, hard drive access, and processor time.

 

The easiest way to do this is to press Ctrl-Shift-Escape to open the Task Manager, then go to the Startup tab and right-click to disable any program you don't want to run automatically.  Even after you disable it here, you can still use these programs when you want them:  they just won't run on their own and slow down your computer.

See Shawn Keene's replies and let me add the following:

 

You say "CPU Performance," but normally you can't do anything to change the performance of the CPU. Did you mean *computer* performance?  If so please realize that  the CPU is not the whole computer.

"CPU" stands for "Central Processing Unit." In modern personal computers, the CPU is a relatively small electronic chip, an integrated circuit. It sits on the motherboard, inside the computer's case. It is not the case itself, nor is it the total computer, which includes the case and everything within it.

I'm not sure how the incorrect practice began of calling the whole computer the "CPU," but here's my guess: when you buy a computer, you normally get the case and what's in it, along with a monitor, keyboard, mouse, and perhaps a printer. All of these peripherals connect to ports on the case. Because everything connects to the case, the case is "central" to everything else and the term "Central Processing Unit" is sometimes misunderstood and misapplied to it.

I'm not telling you the above to give you a hard time. Rather, I wanted to point out that using the correct terminology is especially important when asking technical question, because if you don't, you are likely to get incorrect answers.
