Hi VolkanKuzucu5421,

1. “How can I decommit my on-premise Exchange server without on-premise server?”

Do you mean removing/decommissioning Exchange Server?

If you will not use DirSync & ADFS in the future, you can safely disable directory synchronization and remove Exchange from the on-premises environment. Later, your mailboxes are hosted in the cloud only environment.

Regarding how to remove Exchange server, you can post at our Exchange server forum: https://social.technet.microsoft.com/Forums/exchange/en-us/home?forum=exchange2010. They are more proficient at on-premise Exchange.

If you want to keep DirSync & ADFS, removing the last Exchange server from the on-premises environment will have adverse effects. In most cases, we recommend that you leave at least one Exchange Server on-premises for mailbox management.

Short answer is no. New accounts that are directly created in cloud will not be affected by on-premise environment.

How and when to decommission your on-premises Exchange servers in a hybrid deployment: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/dn931280(v=exchg.150).aspx

Decommissioning your Exchange 2010 servers in a Hybrid Deployment: http://blogs.technet.com/b/exchange/archive/2012/12/05/decommissioning-your-exchange-2010-servers-in-a-hybrid-deployment.aspx

  • Make sure you have plenty of free space on your drive. Make sure that you have at least three times as much hard disk space available as the current size of your Outlook identity PLUS 10% free for virtual memory.
  • Rebuilding does not fix all issues. It can make some problems worse. Even though the Database Utility will make a backup, I advise making a copy in the Finder first.
  • You can delete the rebuild version after you are satisfied with the results. This copy will be named like this: Main Identity [Backed up 1-21-2012 13.35]  

Tip: If your Identity will open, before you rebuild, check "On My Computer"Inbox and Sent folders to be sure they are under the 2GB limit. Generally this is less than 10,000 messages but with large attachments could be less. Make subfolders e.g. Inbox 2011, Inbox 2010, Sent 2011, Sent 2010 etc to help reduce the number of messages in a folder. (Check for any large archive folders you might have in addition to Inbox and Sent).

Dear SeethaAlagapan,

I am an independent advisor, and I am trying to help other users in Community with my experience in Microsoft products.

In order to close Microsoft Account you had to do the following:
1. Go to Close your account http://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?LinkId=523898
2. When you're prompted to sign in to your account, double-check that it's the account you want to delete. If not, select Sign in with a different Microsoft account.
3. Check that the page shows the correct Microsoft account, and then click Next.
4. Read the list, and select the checkboxes to acknowledge you’ve read each item.
5. In the Select a reason drop-down list, choose the reason you're closing the account.
6. Select Mark account for closure.

When you tap or click the Mark account for closure button, Microsoft will wait 60 days before permanently deleting your Microsoft account in case you change your mind or need to access something on the account before it’s gone forever. After 60 days there is no way to retrieve any information.

For more information and other tips please check this support article https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/help/12412/... Please make sure that you read it carefully, especially part about Before closing the account.

If you have closed the account, there is no way to retrieve it. You can recreate the account after 30 days of closing ( if it will be available) , however it will be like newly created account without any information.
