trevor rabey say:

My recommendation is do not ever link the summaries, ever.

Reason: summaries are not tasks, they are just headings and sub-headings to provide a way to organise the tasks. Only a task can be a predecessor or a successor. If you link summaries you will never be able to find a critical path and it is a certainty that you will ened up with circular references.

Yes, I know that MSP allows you to link the summaries, but that does not mean that it is a good idea.

trevor rabey say:

Ok, here is the way.

First, all tasks must be auto-scheduled.

Do not enter dates or durations or anything else for the summaries, no predecessors, no successors, no resources, no work, no cost.

At 74, "Demolition" is a good main summary

Then 75 should be "Windows Demolition", not just "Windows".

76 should be "Remove Window 1". Task names should start with verbs and should have enough words so that they explain exactly what the task is about without relying on being able to see the associated summaries. Why? Because there will be many times when filtering or grouping that you do not see the summaries.

Estimate the duration of the tasks. Do not type in dates.

Ignore what you think the duration of the summary at 75 will be. It is almost irrelevant.

Whatever the summary duration is, is what it is, because it is derived directly from the overall duration of the tasks. You are setting out to build a network model that will let MSP find out what the duration of the summary is, not tell MSP what it is.

If, and only if, 77 cannot be started until 76 is finished, then link them as FS0 predecessor/successor.

Otherwise, they both are scheduled to start on day 1.

Have a look at the residential construction template that you can find via file, new.

You may have to clean it up a bit.

Get rid of the timeline because it is useless and takes up screen space.

Make all rows one row high.

This is a good example of how it should turn out. Every task has a predecessor and they are all FS0 predecessors. Every task It also has some small faults. See if you can find the two tasks which do not have successors. Every task should have a successor, and it is easy to see what the successors for those two tasks should be.

Hope this helps.

The envelope function can detect the first address in a document, though the other addresses and the formatting of the document may conspire against it; but if you have the three addresses listed at the top of the document as shown in your reply, the linked add-in will print an envelope for all three - if you first select them.
