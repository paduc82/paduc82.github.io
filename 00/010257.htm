trevor rabey say:

It is absolutely essential to separate the idea of duration from the idea of work, and to be very careful about maintaining the distinction when discussing them, otherwise it gets very confusing very fast.

Where you say "I have a task, which is estimated at 20 days of work" I am guessing that you mean the task has a duration of 20 days.

Where you say "I create an employee, set availability at 40%" I am guessing that you have set the max units for the resource at 40%. This is not "availability".

If the resource availability is 2 days/week, you are better off editing the resource calendar and deciding which two days they are, say Monday and Tuesday, and making the other days nonworking days for that resource.

I don't know what "POC" is.

For simplicity, let's say that you assigned the resource at 100%, so the work is 160 hours.

Here is the correct way to track progress.

You are at a point today which is 10 days into a task that was scheduled to start 10 days ago and to have been in progress continuously for the last 10 days.

That means that today 17:00 is your status date. Set the status date in project informationn and format the gridlines to display status date as a vertical solid red line on the chart.

Now, the task may have actually started earlier or later than scheduled, and it may have been interrupted so what with one thing and another there may not be 10 days of actual duration.
But let's say for simplicity that the task did start when scheduled, and there are 10 days of actual duration.

Display the tracking table, type in the actual start date and the actual duration.

Since these are actuals they are facts. Start by logging the facts before moving on to the estimates.

Or, alternatively, since the actuals are as scheduled, just hit the mark on track button.

MSP calculates the % complete as 50%, actual duration = 10 days, remaining duration = 10 days

MSP also therefore calculates the % work complete to be 50%, ie actual work = 80 hours, remaining work = 80 hours.

Now, this is just the default result because MSP has no other information, but the actual work on each day may not have been 8 hours. It may have been something like 8,6,5,2,1,3,4,5,8,8.

Any actual work must have occurred on days of actual duration, and you have already nailed down the actual duration.

Since these work hours are actuals they are facts. Start by logging the facts before moving on to the estimates.

Never ask anyone to estimate how complete they are as a %.

Never type in any number provided by someone as a % into the % complete field or the % work complete field.

Use the task usage and/or resource usage views to type in the actual work for each of the 10 days of actual duration.

Check the remaining duration and the remaining work.

MSP will not and cannot forecast what the remaining duration and remaining work will be based on your "25% finished", other than subtracting the actuals from the total and coming up with remaining, because unless you tell it what they are it cannot know.

What exactly is your guy telling you anyway?

Is he saying that his actual work is 25% of what he needs?

Or is he saying that the actual duration is 25% of what he needs?

Or is it a combination?

MSP will not do the project manager's job. A human is needed here.

If you think that the 20 day task which has 10 days of remaining duration should have 30 days of remaining duration, then type that in to the remaining duration field, then check the remaining work for each day and adjust as required.

Probably best to make the task a fixed duration task while you do this.

Any help?

tom_bpc say:

In addition to the excellent advice that Trevor has provided, you might also consider examining the Earned Value table on both the Gantt Chart (upper pane) and the resource usage (lower pane) views.  The task EAC field promises to incorporate the impact of the cost performance index (CPI) on the total forecast cost of the task.  It does not automatically adjust future resource usage (and task duration) based on productivity to date, however.  You will have to do that yourself, and you might interpret Project's attempts to understand your changes as mindless resistance.

************** Optional Reading ************* Office 365/2013 Code 0-# and Code 300##-##

Part of the design of the “new and improved” <heavy sarcasm> Office 365/2013 is that to be more “user friendly”.  It does make sense since most users are not technically inclined (no surprise) so they don’t want to deal with technical issues.  MS took a few approaches to this “user friendliness”:

1 - the “Click-to-Run” (CTR) “virtual computer” installation

2 - new Code 0-# and Code 300##-## error messages

3 - almost completely eliminating access to the local install version Office 2013

 

#1 CTR takes much of the control of the Office installation out of the user’s hands. You no longer have to worry about Windows Updates, MS does them for you.  But it also means that when something goes wrong there is very little you can do in the way of trouble shooting. You are almost entirely limited to uninstall / re-install. But some of the errors are happening outside of Office, in Windows itself.

 

#2 The new error messages were made “less technical” to be less intimidating to users.  So the first thing they did was become “polite” saying “Something went wrong” and / or “Sorry there was a problem”.  Polite is “nice”, but worse than useless when trying to trouble shoot. Especially when that is all the user reports. They (“you”) usually forget report the “rest” of the text message. The unique, IMPORTANT, part.

As well, when Office 365/2013 was first released the error messages did NOT display the error codes (for the first year or so).  It has only been recently that MS turned on displaying of those codes. This is great for trouble shooters since we can now distinguish between the various “Something went wrong” messages which may have different causes and fixes.

 

Unfortunately, MS did not bother to make available documentation for these new error codes.  So, for now at least, all we can do is collect answers from the forum identifying things that have worked for others. Some of the fixes are just “voodoo”, they are not really a fix, but coincidentally trigger the real fix in the background.

 

I recently talked to a MS Development Manager. He is trying to find “official” publicly accessible documentation of the new error codes, ie KB articles. We can only hope for documentation.

A lot of problems with Office 2013 are caused by third party (non-Microsoft) applications.  This is my (expanding) list of things to try when Office 2013\365 won’t start or crashes:

See the Article “Why can’t I start my Office 2013 application at:

http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/support/why-cant-i-start-my-office-2013-application-HA104011864.aspx

Refer to the following website for information on repairing Office 2013\365:

http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/project-help/repair-or-remove-office-2010-HA010357402.aspx

See Office 2013 install woes - http://office-watch.com/t/n.aspx?a=1834

See the KnowledgeBase article “Office 2013 applications crash on launch at the splash screen” at:

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2813143

Or see the KnowledgeBase article Error: “Something went wrong and we can't sign you in right now” when trying to install Office 2013 at:

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2811180/en-us/

Also see my response in

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_2013_release-word/microsoft-word-2013-has-stopped-working/67b81016-849f-481a-bce5-bf04797ac03b

Do you have Abbyy Finereader installed on the PC? A number of people have reported that removing it overcomes problems with 2013.  See:

Office programs may crash with the FineReader add-in installed at:

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2755838

Do you have “Fun Tools” add-in? A number of people have reported that removing it overcomes problems with 2013

Do you have Toshiba Book Reader installed on the PC?  It has been reported that uninstalling that brings Office back to working status.

Do you have the Acrobat PDFMaker add-in.  It has been reported that uninstalling that overcomes an issue with a message appearing that states "The document contains macros. Macro support for this application is disabled. Features requiring VBA are not available. Would you like to open this document as a read only?"

Also see: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_2013_release-word/microsoft-word-has-stopped-working-when-i-close/8375b5b6-4f7e-46ed-a02b-90152ecad287#LastReply and

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_home-word/word-and-excel-2013-crashing-on-windows-7/5389390c-e989-4c8e-8fe0-62673f1410ce?page=4#LastReply

It appears that there may be some hardware incompatibilities as well.  If you have the NVIDIA driver NVWGF2UM.DLL, try renaming it as: NVWGF2UM.old as was mentioned in:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_2013_release-word/microsoft-word-has-stopped-working-when-i-close/8375b5b6-4f7e-46ed-a02b-90152ecad287#LastReply

From the File tab, select Options and then click on Advanced and then in the Display section of the dialog, check the box for Disable hardware graphics acceleration

For the error message "There was a problem sending the command to the program", use Windows Explorer to access the C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office15 folder and right click on WINWORD.EXE then on Properties, go to the Compatibility tab and uncheck all the boxes.  It has also been reported that the problem can be overcome by editing the registry at HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT\Word.Document.12\shell\Open and deleting the ddeexec key.

The ddeexec can also be deleted from

HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT\Word.DocumentMacroEnabled.12\shell\Open.

That fix is described at ...

http://www.dilloway.co.uk/there-was-a-problem-sending-the-command.html

It has been reported that uninstalling FastPictureView and FastPictureViewer Pro… overcomes the issue of the Minimize, Maximize, Close and Help buttons not being visible in Office 2013.

If you have a Canon 8350 printer, try using the Canon MF8000 UFRI LT XPS driver as detail in the post by Riad Amstha on April 14 in the thread at:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_365hp-word/office-2013-crashes-with-canon-8350-printer/e2bf6530-92e0-47ef-b6ec-e803a91793d0?page=3#LastReply
