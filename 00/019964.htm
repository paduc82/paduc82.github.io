Therefore, the formula in H7 is self-referencing, and it appears that all the formulas in this column are self-referencing. =If(D7>= 8, "Yes", "No").

jllatham say:

As hard as it might seem to get the timing just right, it may be that while writing the information about the location of the autorecover file you took the powerhit, corrupting that information.

About the only way to cover yourself in such a situation is to put an uninterruptible power supply (UPS) into use: plug the UPS into the wall outlet, plug the computer and monitor into outlets on the UPS that are marked as having backup power and not just surge suppressors.  Plug your printer into one of the ones that's surge suppressor only.  I like and trust both American Power Conversion (APC) and Belkin brand UPS units.

What these do is provide instant backup power that usually very effectively deals with power drops that are of short duration, even short enough to just reset the clock on your microwave, or a little longer.  For those longer outages it gives you time to gracefully save and close your work and shut down the system.  Many such UPS units now include 'power smoothing' meaning that as your line voltage changes during the day, it adjusts it to keep it very close to the normal 120V (actually more like 117V, but who's counting).  You might be surprised at just how low the line voltage can go at times like the end of the workday when everyone gets home, turns down the A/C, heats up the stove, turns on the big screen, and fires up their own computer(s).

As for that missing file, I'm afraid it's probably toast at this point.  If you can't find it checking the Deleted Files bin or with the Last Version feature, you'd probably have to do a lot of low-level work to even find if it exists at all in the file directory on the drive.

If I understand, you setup a new computer you used a new email address.

There is no documented easy way of changing the email account associated with a 365 license. MS designed the email account to be the primary "key" to the system and has not provided a way to change it.

What you can do is share the personal license between the 2 computers, as long as you will only have one computer running Office at a time.  Office 365 is setup to handle this easily for you.

When you want to switch office from one computer to the other, log in to http://Office.com/MyAccount .  In the install option, you will see that is currently active on the other machine. Change email address associated with online account
