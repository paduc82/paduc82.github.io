william henning say:

I was able to solve this problem following a post by corrado_boy_g60 as you suggested.  I'll reprint here for posterity:

Here is the solution to those having problems with Microsoft Security Essentials not being removed prior to Windows 10 upgrade and then being left with it still running after the upgrade.

Open File Explorer

Browse to C:\Program Files\Microsoft Security Client\

Locate the file Setup.exe, right click on it and choose properties

Click on the Compatibility tab

At the bottom of the tab click 'Change Settings for all users'

On the next screen check the box for 'Run this program in compatibility mode for:' and choose Windows 7

Click OK

Click OK

From the taskbar search box type cmd.exe and right click and choose 'Run As Administrator'

This will open an elevated command prompt as Administrator

In the command prompt window copy/paste or type the following:

C:\Program Files\Microsoft Security Client\setup.exe  /x  /disableoslimit

Choose to Uninstall

Once this has finished reboot and Microsoft Security Essentials will be gone and Windows Defender should be running correctly.

How can I change from Office 2013 Professional Plus to Office 2013 Home & Business without reinstalling Office?

How can I activate just the H&B products without reinstalling the whole Office suite?

UPDATE: I do not want to reinstall because I want to keep my Office 2013 settings.
rohn007 say:
You can't.

Office bundles and Product keys are matched. You can't use a key from a smaller bundle to activate a subset of applications in Pro Plus. Period.

The only reason it works on factory trials is because they are a special installer known as "Single Image".  When this special installer is run at the factory it does the equivalent of installing the 3 consumer trials: Home and Student, Home and Busines and Professional.  Therefore, when you enter a product key for one of these suites some of the trial applications may not be activated. You will have to clean them up (uninstall) separately.

Uninstalling Pro will be "painless".  The default uninstall from Programs and Features is not very stringent because MS assumes you will be re-installing Office again (ie Office gets messed up so often that re-installs are a normal and expected procedure).  The uninstall leaves configuration files like NORMAL.DOTM and Dictionary files behind. As well, it also leaves some configuration registry entries. 

When you re-install Office, it will pick up on the presence of any relevant configuration files and registry entries and use them in the new installation.

ALL Office uninstall procedures do NOT touch any documents you create. All of your personal files are safe from the uninstall / reinstall process.
