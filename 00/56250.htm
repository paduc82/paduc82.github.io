    mutliple monitors with touchscreen on Surface Pro - Microsoft Community

    garypevey say:

    mutliple monitors with touchscreen on Surface Pro

    I have the original Surface Pro. I have connected a Dell monitor by way of mini-display to Display port cable . From that monitor I have connected a dell touchscreen monitor by way of DP to DP cable. I had to connect a USB to USB-C from Surface to touchscreen monitor to get touchscreen to work.

    Problem: I can not get Surface screen, and each monitor to each show as 3 separate monitors. Assuming connections are appropriate, what are setting to make this happen? At least I need the touchscreen to mirror Surface and have other monitor as #2.

    voun tia say:

    Hi Gary,

    Connecting an external monitor to your Surface lets you choose what to display on your screen, and the problem you have might be due to incorrect settings chosen. To change the type of display you want, select the search box in the taskbar, type project to a second screen, and in the search results, select Project to a second screen. Then choose from one of the following options:

    • PC screen only: You’ll see everything on your Surface screen. (When you're connected to a wireless projector, this option changes to Disconnect.)
    • Duplicate: You’ll see the same things on all screens.
    • Extend: You'll see everything spread over all screens, and you can drag and move items between them.
    • Second screen only: You’ll see everything on the connected screen or screens, and your Surface screen will be blank.

    The number of external screens that your Surface supports depends on your settings for refresh rate and resolution and on whether the display on your Surface is on or off. Click here to check the monitors that are known to be compatible with Surface.

    Let us know if you have any additional concerns.

    Regards.

    garypevey say: Unfortunately, this did not provide a solutions. I am trying to mirror the external touchscreen monitor to the Surface screen and extend the 2nd monitor. Your reply did not address achieving this solution. Can you help?

    garypevey say: Additional info, I am using the original Surface Pro. Will it allow daisy chaining monitors?

    voun tia say:

    By default, when you connect an external monitor to your Surface, the display gets duplicated to your external monitor. To duplicate the display to monitor 1 and extend display to monitor 2, follow these steps:

    1. Right-click on the desktop, go to Display Settings.
    2. Click "Identify" and note the designation of each display.
    3. Select the touchscreen external monitor then set it to "Extend these displays".
    4. Then select the second external monitor and set it to "Duplicate these displays".

    If you're unable to see two monitors on the Display Settings, you might want to try the solution posted by Steven_B on this thread.

    Regards.
