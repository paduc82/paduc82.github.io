If the current short-date form in the Regional and Languages is now dd/mm/yyyy, any pre-existing date that Excel recognized as a date is unaffected.  However, its appearance will be determined by the pre-existing format, not necessary the short-date form.

Note that Excel stores recognized dates as an integer.  The format merely affects the appearance of the date, not the value of the date itself.

However, if you now enter or import text of the form yyyy/dd/mm, that will be treated as text, not a date, because yyyy is presumably larger than 28 to 31, and/or dd might be larger than 12.

If you have a column of "dates", some of which are text of the form yyyy/dd/mm, try using the Text To Column feature to convert the text to bona fide dates.

When you get to the last TtoC menu, select Date and YDM for the column format.  TtoC should then convert the text of the form yyyy/dd/mm to a bona fide Excel date, formatted as Date, IIRC.
