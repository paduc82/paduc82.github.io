robertslattery say: Hi, I'm sure there are more clever ways but this works:

=SUM(IF(MMULT(IF($A$1:$J$33=MMULT(IF(ISNUMBER(ROW($1:$33)),1),IF(ISNUMBER(COLUMN($A$1:$J$1)),1))*M1,1,0),TRANSPOSE(IF(ISNUMBER(COLUMN($A$1:$J$1)),1)))*MMULT(IF($A$1:$J$33=MMULT(IF(ISNUMBER(ROW($1:$33)),1),IF(ISNUMBER(COLUMN($A$1:$J$1)),1))*N1,1,0),TRANSPOSE(IF(ISNUMBER(COLUMN($A$1:$J$1)),1)))>0,1))

you have to enter as an array formula by pressing CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER instead of just ENTER, when you enter the formula.

I'll break it down for you...

This creates an array, the same size as your data, with all elements equal to M1
MMULT(IF(ISNUMBER(ROW($1:$33)),1),IF(ISNUMBER(COLUMN($A$1:$J$1)),1))*M1

This then puts a 1 in every element that is equal to M1
IF($A$1:$J$33=MMULT(IF(ISNUMBER(ROW($1:$33)),1),IF(ISNUMBER(COLUMN($A$1:$J$1)),1))*M1,1,0)

This is a column of 1's with the same number of rows as the columns in your data (10 in your case)
TRANSPOSE(IF(ISNUMBER(COLUMN($A$1:$J$1)),1))

When I matrix multiply the previous two together I get a column, the same height as your data with element i equal to the number of times M1 appears in row i of your data.

I then just duplicate it, but this time with M1 replaced by N1, and create a column of 1s and 0s with a 1 in each row where both numbers are present and finally sum it to get the total number of rows that match your criteria.

I guess its the brute force way but it works.
