mad cow dizease say:

How to Manage your Account Security Proofs - FAQ v1.1

WELCOME!


In this thread you'll learn about your account security proofs, how to manage/change them, and a little about your account security in general:

Table of Contents:

Table of Contents:

  1. What are proofs?
  2. How to manage/change your proofs
  3. How to add a Trusted PC
  4. My proofs are inaccessible!
  5. A little extra on account security


1. What are proofs?


Your account proofs are countermeasures set up to protect your account's vital information from being accessed. When accessing things like billing, payment options and alike, you may be sent a code to an alternate e-mail address, or to your phone if your PC isn't labeled as "trusted". You'll learn how to make your PC trusted and how to manage your proofs if you read on.

By keeping your proofs updated, you'll be able to keep your account safer and easier to access vital information on your account. If you ever have to reset your password, you'll have a way to be confirmed, to help prevent account theft. 
 
2. How to manage/change your proofs

Two proofs are required to keep your account secure: a phone number and alternate email address. Two proofs are required to ensure that your account will remain controllable, even if one of your proofs expires or becomes unaccessible. If you find all Two proofs are required to keep your account secure: a phone number and alternate email address. Two proofs are required to ensure that your account will remain controllable, even if one of your proofs expires or becomes unaccessible. If you find all of your proofs being inaccessible, refer to Part 4!

Adding a proof:
  1. Click here to manage your security proofs.
  2. Depending on which you want to add, underneath Phone Number or Alternate email address, click on Add New.
  3. Once you've added a new phone number or alternate email address, a confirmation code will be sent to your current phone number or alternate email address with instructions to confirm your new security proof.
Deleting a proof:
  1. Click here to manage your security proofs.
  2. Depending on which you want to delete, next to Phone Number or Alternate email address, click on Delete
  3. Select which proof you'd like a confirmation code to be sent to, to confirm the deletion of the proof you'd like to delete.
3. How to add a Trusted PC


NOTE: Internet Explorer and Windows Live Essentials are required
  1. Click here to access your security proofs
  2. Under Trusted PC, click on Make this computer a trusted PC
  3. Type in your computer name
    NOTE: To find your computer's name, open the start menu (or go to desktop), right click on "Computer" and click on "Properties". Your computer name will be listed under "Computer name, domain and workgroup settings" (WINDOWS 7)
  4. Click Save


4. My proofs are inaccessible!

If your Trusted PC isn't set (or is out-of-date), and your phone number and alternate email address are both out-of-date and/or inaccessible, then you'll have to go through the procedure to reset your account proofs. Bear in mind, if just one of your proofs are outdated, you can delete it, confirm it with your valid proof, and add a new one easily, as per the steps in part 2. Here's how to reset your out-of-date proofs:
  1. Go to remove all of your security info.
  2. Click on Remove All.
  3. Enter new accessible proofs, which will be added once the reset process has been completed.
When you chose to remove all of your security information, confirmations are sent to every phone and email on your account in-case you want to undo the process. The process to remove your inaccessible proofs can take from 30 days to 6 months, and the process cannot be expedited by Xbox Support. If this time range is too long, an alternative would be to create a new account altogether, in which new and valid proofs can be entered immediately.
These are methods to work around these issues if you want to:
  • Buy Microsoft Points/Xbox Live Gold
    • Use your Xbox 360 to purchase Microsoft Points/Xbox Live Gold
  • Turn off automatic renewal
    • Contact xbox support

  • Control access to your Xbox Live Account
