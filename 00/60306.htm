sin bin say:

iannugent, 

In case you still have the issue, as I had the same issue myself, the driver would stop working when the registry was refreshing.  However, it couldn't find a dependent driver and would not fully load the driver, which 'turned' your internet off.

The fix is to

- Open your Device Manager,

- Find the SAMSUNG NDIS device under the Network adapters

- Right click on the troubled device and select Update Driver Software

- Select Browse my computer for driver software

- Select Let me pick from a list of device drivers on my computer

- Choose Remote NDIS based Internet Sharing Device and Click Next

...that should be your dependent driver and you should have no issues afterwards as I have no had any since.  Also, there was no reboot needed.  Windows figured it all out once I installed that dependent driver
