Hi Little Beaver,

 

I am glad to assist you on how to stop receiving junk emails in your account. Hotmail has several features to stop receiving spam messages. In order to filter it, I suggest that you delete them from your contact list and then you may follow one of the options below:

 

A. Block a sender or a domain name

 

  1. Sign in to your account. Go to Inbox.

  2. Click the Gear icon located at the upper-right side of the page beside your name.

  3. Select More Mail settings.

  4. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

  5. Click Blocked senders.

  6. Enter the email address or domain on the box labeled Blocked email address or domain: and click

      Add to list.   

 

B. Set the filtering level of the messages

 

  1. Sign in to the Outlook.com website with your Microsoft account.

  2. Click the Gear icon located at the upper-right side of the page beside your name. Select More Mail

      settings.

  3. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

  4. Under Filters and reporting, select the options that you want, and then click Save.

 

Notes:

  > If you choose Exclusive for your junk e-mail filter, only messages from e-mail addresses on your Contacts or Allowed senders lists appear in your Inbox. Messages from senders not on those lists are automatically sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

  > You should occasionally check your junk e-mail folder to make sure that good messages are not sent there by mistake.

 

C. Rules - we can create a rule that can block a specific word from the subject of the spam email.

 

  1. Sign in to your Outlook.com account with your Microsoft account.

  2. Click the Gear icon located at the upper-right side of the page beside your name. Select More Mail

     settings.

  3. Under the Customizing Outlook section, click on Rules for sorting new messages.

  4. Click on the New button.

  5. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to,

          a. First drop-down menu - choose Subject

          b. Second drop-down menu - choose contains

          c. Third field - input the word you would like to block

  6. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply, click on Delete these messages

  7. Click Save button.

 

Hoping that the solutions above will help you protect your account from any spam messages you're receiving. If you have other concerns, please let us know and we will gladly assist you.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

You really need to have a separate row for each bullet point. Then your prices will align easily, and you can use the SUM(ABOVE) function to sum the amounts; for the formatting you have there, you can use { SUM(ABOVE) \# -$,#.00 }.

To add rows above the total row, select a row or rows and right-click. On the shortcut menu, choose Insert | Insert Rows Below.

The SUM field will not update automatically. The only way to get a total that updates automatically is to have a calculation based on text form fields with "Calculate on exit" enabled, and this is not practical in a table with an undetermined number of rows. The total will update when you print or switch to Print Preview.

jay freedman say:

The answer to the second question is easier: Insert a field in the bottom right cell, containing the formula

=SUM(ABOVE)

The easiest way to put this in is to click in the cell, go to the Table Tools > Layout tab, and click the Formula button at the far right of the ribbon. This dialog opens:

Open the "Paste function" dropdown and select SUM, which will appear in the Formula box after the equal sign and followed by a pair of parentheses. Type ABOVE between the parentheses and click OK. The field will be inserted in the cell. If you see {=SUM(ABOVE)} instead of a number, right-click it and click Update Fields (or just click on the field and press F9).

To get the numbers to align with the bullet points, the best solution is to insert a new row in the table for each bullet point; the right-hand cell of the row will automatically align with the left-hand cell. There are several ways to insert rows in an existing table: (1) right-click the row just above or below where you want the new row, click Insert, and click either Insert Row Below or Insert Row Above as needed; (2) click in the table, go to Table Tools > Layout, and click Insert Above or Insert Below; (3) right-click the buttons mentioned in (2) and choose Add to Quick Access Toolbar, and thereafter use the buttons in the toolbar instead.

You can use the Table Tools > Design > Borders button to hide the borders between cells ("Inside Borders" in the menu).

It would be possible to use the table as you showed it, and insert enough paragraph marks (Enter key) or line breaks (Shift+Enter) to make the numbers align. However, that would have to be adjusted if you edit the text in the left column, and possibly even if you just open the document on a computer that has a different printer driver. The multiple-rows solution is stable in those situations.
