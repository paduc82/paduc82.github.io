john mcghie say:

Each section of your document has three independent headers, and three independent footers.

To achieve your aim, you need to switch ON "Different first page".

You then need to ensure that your page numbers are inserted IN the running footer, and not as a floating text box.  To do that, work from the menus, not the Ribbon: makes it a lot easier to get it right.

Go to View>Header and Footer.  That will take you into the Header.

Scroll to the bottom of the page and click in the Footer.  Make sure the cursor is in the footer.

Now use Insert>Page Number.

That will place a page number in the First Page footer.  Hit Next (on the ribbon) and check you also have a page number in the Left page footer, and again and check there is also one in the Right page footer.

In a multi-section document, "Same as Previous" in the header or footer means "Same as the previous section".  So if you have a First Page footer in Section 1, "Same as Previous" in section 2 gives you the first page footer from Section 1, and NOT the previous footer from the previous page.

I recommend that you ALWAYS place the page numbers in the running footer, it makes them so much easier and more obvious to control.

Hope this helps
