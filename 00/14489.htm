Excel 2013 Sumif formula returning incorrect value - Microsoft Community

out2c78 say:

Excel 2013 Sumif formula returning incorrect value

I am using sumif to look for a specific word and then sum cells in a Row but it only calculates the first cell. What am I doing wrong or what am I missing? Below is the formula I am inputting and the columns. I am sure it is simple but can't see the issue.

=SUMIF($B$3, "name", $C$3:$I$3)

invoice Company amount 1 amount 2  amount 3 amount 4 amount 5 amount 6 amount 7 total
150-14 name $1.00 $1.00 $1.00 $1.00 $1.00 $1.00

paulcordts say:

The issue is that your criteria range (B3) and sum range (C3:I3) are not the same size, so your sum range is trimmed to match the size of the criteria range, effectively only summing C3.

Help in Excel sort of explains this (the example used shows how the sum range increases if it is smaller than the criteria range).  It works the same way if the sum range is larger than the criteria range.

You need to change the formula around a little. to get what you want.

=IF(B3="name",SUM(C3:I3),0)

rlesperance say:

Office 365 (2016) and print merge to email for Mac OS ...

Hi,

I am a Office 2011 Mac user ... mostly delighted because I have been using this version for so long that I am a pro now.  But it cannot merge document and send them by email.  So I am trying Office 365 (2016) to see if it can help me do that.  

I want to merge an Excel database with a Word main document like I always did with Office 2011 ... but I want to send the merge document by email to the recipient's email.  

I don't want to use neither Outlook's nor Apple's contact list.  I want to use the «Email adress» field in the Excel database that I used to create the merge document.

How do I do that ?

Regards.

joshua zhang msft say:

Hi rlesperance,

Please follow the steps below to finish mail merge:

1. Refer to Prepare your data source in Excel for a mail merge in Word for Mac to create the spreadsheet.

2. On the Mailings tab, choose Start Mail Merge > Email Messages.

3. On the Mailings tab, choose Select Recipients, and then choose Use an Existing List > Browse to the file you want to use and choose Open > in the Open Workbook dialog box, select the spreadsheet you want to use and select Cell Range.

4. Add and format merge fields. For more information, please refer to Use mail merge to create a form email message in Word 2016 for Mac.

5. After editing the document. On the Mailings tab, choose Preview Results to see how the email messages look.

Note: Use the left and right arrow buttons on the Mailings tab, to scroll through each email message.

6. When the messages look how you want, on the Mailings tab, choose Finish & Merge > Merge to E-Mail to complete the mail merge.

Tip: Merge to E-Mail is unavailable if you have not selected your default email program.

7. In the To box, you can choose the column of recipients' email address that you have created in Excel worksheet.

Regards,

Joshua
