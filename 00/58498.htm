player325173202 say:

Storytime: The Surface Support

Back in October 2018, my Surface Book 2 had a problem with the detachment mechanism (precisely the connection between Base and Clipboard). I went on their website, everything looked alright and I got the exchange device within a week.

I unpacked the device and got a first hint of what to expect from now on. The device I got was scratched all over its bottom. the display looked terrible due to dozens of micro scratches and dirt. Not enough, the hinge seemed to be badly adjusted, the lid didn't close properly.

So, I tried to reach out by phone to the local, german customer support multiple times. No one answered as they claimed due to a technical issue. I also waited about an hour in line with one person before me at the support chat. Must've been a very persistent person.

Later that day, I contacted the US service. Fortunately for me, their devices worked. After explaining to the (very nice and serviceable) agent why I was absolutely disappointed, he decided to send me a new one. The device I received in October was in an almost new condition (only some liquid marks at the bottom and a few very small scratches) and I needed it for study so I decided to keep it.

Not even two months later, at the ninth of January 2019, it started again. I tried to detach the clipboard, the very function I bought the device for - and heard a very unpleasant crack followed by a permanent rattle whenever I moved the device. The muscle wire lock on the right side broke and was now freely moving inside my Surface.

So, again, I turned to the US-service, telling them that I was fed up with the Surface Book. The agent told me that he was very sorry, that I was a good person with a lot of good things to happen in my life (yes, really), that he knew how important it was to have a flawless surface to work with. He ultimately decided to escalate the case, thus pushing it to the next level of service so that we could decide whether I'd receive a Surface Pro 6 (technically a downgrade but I am fine with that) instead of another Surface Book 2. But, he told me, this was a matter to discuss with the Sales Team so I'd have to call them. I decided to try it with the German service once again and their team answered the call and was able to redirect me. Unfortunately, those guys decided not to answer the call, the connection broke after 40 minutes of patiently waiting for an answer. I recalled the previous number and this time they told me not to be able to help me. Unfortunately, the agent was unable to give me any contact info but instead, he tried to navigate me through the absolutely terrible support website in search for the number I tried to call previously. He didn't succeed so he tried to reconnect me and after 20 minutes of hanging in the line again I just hang up.

The next day, I called the surface support directly (whatever that level that was) (figured out the number with help by @MicrosoftHelps earlier). Finally, I got someone with a deeper knowledge I thought. He told me that he could redirect my case to the higher level so that I'd exchange my Book for a Surface Pro 6 but he lowered the expectations. In Germany, the manufacturer appears to be required by law to offer cashback or a completely new device after three failed repair attempts and he clearly told me that my prospects to get an SP 6 after only two exchanges were low. So, he frustratedly recommended me to send in the device for repair once again. I tried just minutes after the call, unfortunately, the portal didn't work due to software issues as the German support told me at this point when I called them to ask. Later that day, I contacted the US-Support to manually get a label. They told me that something was a little out of the ordinary - whatever that means - and that my case needed to be escalated once again so that they could analyze whether everything was fine. The agent didn't explain what it was and how it could be solved but she called me without asking before and told me a lot - unfortunately nothing relevant, just some more support phrases, everything is being taken care of, she's very happy to take my case, Microsofts thanks for my loyalty and that I'd receive further information in up to 72 hours. Later that day I received an email that contained all those useless phrases again but, unfortunately, not a single concrete information. After 72 hours, nothing happened, no email, no call, not the slightest bit of information. I tried to print out my package label once again on my own, it worked out this time, leaving no information as to why it didn't the first time. In the night I received an email from the Microsoft support. It was the exact same I mentioned before though, this time, the agent replaced one line saying that she'd 'personally update on the status of the replacement'. Whatever that means in this context.

I sent in the device for replacement and received the replacement within convenient 5 days. The new device had black, extremely persistent marks on its keyboard and several scratches on its bottom. So, I called the German support once more.

The agent told me that an exchange of the device with a Surface Pro 6 was not possible due to me not contacting the phone support each but using the web portal for the exchange two times. She claimed that I had to call the support each time if I intended to exchange the device and after three times, there might be the possibility of exchanging it with a pro 6. When I asked her where to find that in the terms and conditions (which I read), she asked me to wait and told me a few moments later that she'd escalate the case. Half an hour after our conversation she emailed me and told me that exchanging with a surface book 2 was the only possible solution.

So I turned to @MicrosoftHilft to ask them for help and to probably get in touch with someone from Microsoft to share my complaints. After I explained my whole story starting 4 months ago, they wrote me that they'd escalate my case and that I'd be contacted by someone Monday this week so that we could discuss exchanging with an SP 6. This Tuesday, I received an email, identical to the one above - but with the request to decide, otherwise, my case would be closed - but no call. I wrote the Twitter team that I'd continue to wait for a phone call and answered the email, asking them not to close the case as I still was in contact.

As of today, I did not receive a phone call, so I called the support again. After 10 minutes of conversation (it brought nothing new), the connection broke.


I don't know what to do.
