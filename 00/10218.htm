Interrupt a Macro - Microsoft Community

draretired say:

Interrupt a Macro

How can I interrupt one of my EXCEL macro while it's running? I tried CTRL-Break and ESC. Neither worked.

THanks,

DRA

andreas killer say:

There a some ways to go.

The reason why CTRL-BREAK doesn't work is that your macro gives no time slices to other task, means the keystrokes that you send to the task can not be processed.

Another symptom is that your task sometimes shows a "Not Responding" inside the title bar when your macro needs a long time to run.

A way out of this is to call DoEvents from time to time in your macro, this gives the control back to the OS for a short time, means your task is responding. And the OS (resp. the task) can process keystrokes.

So when you press CTRL-BREAK then, your macro stops... that is okay for you (the developer), but not okay for the end user.

When the end user press CTRL-BREAK, the macro should terminate correctly, maybe undo something and really ends. To accomplish that you can set Application.EnableCancelKey = xlErrorHandler and install an error handler at the end of your code. Please refer to the help for an example.

Andreas.
