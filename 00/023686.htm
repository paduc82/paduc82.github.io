suzanne s. barnhill say:

This is probably your printer being "helpful." Every printer has an irreducible minimum nonprintable area that is greatest on the trailing edge (bottom of portrait pages, one side of landscape). This is increased when you duplex (because the printer has to hang onto enough of the paper to suck it back in and turn it over). If part of your page would be cut off in duplexing, the printer adjusts the print area to make it fit. You may have better luck if you duplex manually, but this requires planning and can be very cumbersome for more than a few pages.

You may (but probably won't) get better results if you abandon Book Fold and set your document up as A5, then print to PDF. In the Print dialog of Acrobat Reader, you can select Booklet and get the same results you get with Book Fold.
