Citizen PN60 Printer Driver for Windows 10

You will a usb to printer adapter for the parallel cable that comes with the Citizen PN60.

A page break is just a character in the document. Normally it's invisible, but you can see it if you click the Â¶ button on the toolbar. It appears as a dotted line across the page, with "Page Break" in the middle, but it's really just one character.

To remove it, put the cursor at the left end of the dotted line and press the Delete key.

Because inserting a page break into a table in Word 2003 also inserts a paragraph mark (the Â¶ symbol), you have to delete that, too -- again, with the cursor to its left, press the Delete key.

When you do that, the two pieces of the table will join up.

With the VLOOKUP call you use, the data in the A-column, including rows 1 and 2, have to be sorted.
If you add the fourth optional "range_lookup" parameter with the value FALSE, the VLOOKUP will work also if the data is not sorted.

=VLOOKUP($F$6,'Calc-A'!A:V,22,FALSE)

If the data in cells A3:A265 are in fact sorted, and there is a risk that the data in A1:A2 will interfere with the LOOKUP, you can try the following modification:

=VLOOKUP($F$6,'Calc-A'!A$3:V$265,22)

Delay Email Delivery

I have set an email to have a delayed delivery on it and it's currently sitting in my Outbox (as I would expect). However, would my Outlook need to be open at the scheduled send time for this to go through when I want it to?
joanna 777 say:

Hi Nick,

An e-mail message may not be delivered at a scheduled time if the following conditions are true:

  • Outlook is running in Cached Exchange Mode.
  • Outlook is not running at the scheduled time of the deferred delivery.
  • When you enable the "Do not deliver before" option in an Outlook message.

If the above conditions doesn't apply to you, then you're good. Outlook will deliver your deferred message automatically at your specified schedule.

See also: Delay or Scheduled Sending Email Messages
