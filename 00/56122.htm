I had this same problem after upgrading to Windows 10 and it drove me nuts. I knew the correct Bluetooth driver was loaded and I could see the Bluetooth support service running in the Services window (services.msc). Bluetooth was just not visible in Device Manager or Settings and just didn't work.

I got a great perspective from my IT manager: The Bluetooth radio is not just a discrete component (at least most of the time). Some functionality could be in the network adapter, some in the chipset. And the BIOS tells all these things how to work together.

Anyway, BIOS update was final step. After the BIOS reflash, the Bluetooth icon returned to the system tray, Device Manager and the ON/Off switch reappeared in Settings.

So the things to verify in case anyone reads this someday are:
-Power Options (Location: Control Panel>Hardware & Sound>Power Options>Choose what Power button does): There "Turn on fast startup" must be unchecked & Save changes.

-Network Card Properties (Location: Right Click on "This PC">Properties>Device Manager>Network Adapters): 
               a-Right click the network card and select properties.
