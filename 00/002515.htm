bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
No offense :-) but I believe you're misinterpreting the behavior. Page Breaks, Next Page Section Breaks, etc. do generate new paragraphs just as a Column Break does. A 'page' or a Column cannot exist in a Word document unless it is occupied by at least 1 paragraph... even if the paragraph has no content.

The actual difference is that a Table cannot be the first content to follow a Column Break. The "why" was explained to me years ago but I honestly can't recall the technical rationale.

One workaround is to drag the Table in order to reposition it. The possible down side of doing so is that it forces Text Wrapping to be applied to the Table.

Another option is to select the empty ¶ at the top of the column & format it as Hidden. When the non-printing characters are not displayed the Table will be at the top of the column.

techfreak_ say:

There are no connection fees deducted on subscriptions.

Be aware that all deductions are rounded up to the next whole minute. After the minutes are consumed your last call will be terminated. Any additional calls will go by Pay as you go rates with connection fees. Be aware the no subscriptions cover special, premium or non-geographical numbers. If you call these numbers, even with an active subscription, pay as you go rates will still apply. Minutes will only deduct on calls that are covered by the subscription.

If you are on a free trial you are only allocated the subscription minutes for the month (ex. 300). You are simply not charged for the first month. If you have no intention of paying for the subscription for the following month, be sure to cancel the subscription no later than 3 days prior to the end of the subscription cycle. A cancellation does not terminate any remaining minutes/time on the subscription. It simply ensures you do not get billed for the next month. If you miss that window, you would have to contact Skype support to request a refund.

techfreak_ say:
Regardless of what verbiage you use, nothing changes. As I stated previously, there are no connection fees deducted on subscriptions (minutes or unlimited). Only dialing a number not covered by a subscription  or exhausting your subscription will revert you to Pay as you go rates with a connection fee, for that call.
