wunders say:

A "Browser App" would be a recognized web browser application like Internet Explorer, Firefox, Chrome, Opera, and the like.  All other applications would be considered "non-browser" apps or applications whose main purpose is something other than to contact sites on the internet at your request.  It is not unusual for some of the "non-browser" apps to periodically connect to a website to check for recent updates, to check email, or other small updates...  but it might be ususual if you had a malware application that connects to a website and downloads all your personal information.

The steps are essentially correct but it's important to "hide" the update after uninstalling, otherwise Windows reinstalls it.  Here are the steps that worked for me:

Go to Control Panel

Click System and Security

Find and click on "View Installed Updates" (small text to the right under the Windows Update section)

Locate KB3035583 (I used the Search box in the upper right of the window) and uninstall it

Afterwards, go back and search for Windows Update, KB3035583 will be there to install again.  Right click on it and and select "Hide update".

As far as uninstalling Windows 10 update.  Microsoft allows you to rollback to your previous operating system version for 30 days after the installation of 10.  To see if you can, follow these steps

Click the "start" button in lower left of screen.

Click Settings

Click "Update & security"

If you're computer is eligible to rollback, there is an option that states "go back to Windows x".

If this option isn't available and you have your original discs or the recovery partition of your computer's hard drive, you can reinstall the operating system that came with your computer.  The downside to this is option is it will likely wipe the entire hard drive and all your data.
