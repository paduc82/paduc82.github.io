grschinon say: First make sure that your router is broadcasting its ESSID. Without that, the phone won't be able to detect it.

If your router is using WEP encryption or WPA/WPA2 you'll also need the WEP key or the WPA pre-shared key.

Next,

Menu > Settings > Connectivity > Wireless LAN

The phone should pick up your home router and display its ESSID. Select your router and then Options > Connect.

The phone will ask for your credentials and, once you've entered them, attempt to connect to the router. If all went well then you'll see the word "Connected" under the router's ESSID. Do Options > Disconnect WLAN. The phone will disconnect and show you that your home router is now "Known".

Press "Back", then go into Destinations > Internet.

Your WLAN should be in there at a higher priority than your operator's packet data access.

Bress "Back" to go back to the destinations. Go Options > Default connection and select "Internet".

Finally, go into each of the network-aware applications that you use and tell them to use the "default connection" as their route to the internet. From this point on, the applications will use your home router whenever possible, or fall back on the packet data connection if your WLAN isn't available.

Set the priorities in your "Internet" destination so that youe WiFi access point is top of the list.

Press 'Windows key' + 'R', type %appdata%, press Enter, then navigate to Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu\Programs\Startup <= THIS is the startup folder to copy the PowerPoint file into, or any shortcuts to any file or programs, for that matter. The full path looks like this:

C:\Users\<user-name>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu\Programs\Startup
