hansv mvp say:

Start Word.

Press Alt+F11 to activate the Visual Basic Editor.

On the left hand side, you'll see an explorer-like treeview with two main items: Normal and something like Project (Document1).

Click on Normal.

Select Insert | Module (or press Alt+I, then M).

Copy the following macro from this post into the module window that appears:

Sub AutoOpen()
    With ActiveWindow.ActivePane.View.Zoom
        .PageColumns = 1
        .Percentage = 100
    End With
End Sub

(this is based on Graham Mayor's code from the thread that you referred to)

Press Alt+F4 to close the Visual Basic Editor.

Now open a multi-page document to test whether the macro works.

When you quit Word, you may be asked whether you want to save changes to the Normal template. If so, answer Yes, so that the macro will be available in the future too.

Accounts become inactive if you don't sign in for more than 270 days VIA WEB BROWSER or within the first 10 days after signing up VIA WEB BROWSER for an account.  IF you have a welcome email this has probably happened.

After an account becomes inactive, all messages, folders, and contacts are deleted. Incoming messages will be sent back to the sender as undeliverable.
Your account name is still reserved.  During this time you wont be able to re-use the same email name.

However, if the account stays inactive for an additional 90 days, the account name may be permanently deleted.
If you don't use your Windows Live ID for 365 days, your Windows Live ID will be permanently deleted.  Once the account is marked as in-active all data is purged so even if you could get the email account back the data would be gone....

jack cook - aka help_line say:
how can i clean my phone storage to get more free space ?


I'm assuming that you are looking at available space on your device and have a concern that you may not have enough.  If you connect your device and view how it is used then the "unknown" is that area called "other"

 

So "other" is basically a storage of data from a variety of sources (photos, music and video as well as reserved space for applications).  Each application that writes (and stores data) has their own specific area to use so WP allocates space according to needs of the device.

 

Windows Phone allocate storage dynamically, so if the phone is relatively empty, it will reserve a lot of space  (as an example, IE and Maps would take space.)

 

As you add more to your device it will release some of the reserved space back to the phone.

 

Pretty much if space is not being used, it will automatically take space and reserve it for use for what is there.  If a new app is installed and it needs more space, the phone will allocate the space needed.

 

arkengel say:

There are different things that take space on your phone.

 

Operating System

- Windows Phone 8 operating System

- RingTones & Alerts

- Undeletable Apps (People, Maps, Messaging, Phone, Camera, etc)

 

Xbox Music or Videos

-Music

-Videos

-PodCasts

 

Picture Hub

- Photos and Videos you recorded with your camera.

- Online folders sync temporarily for you to see the photos on facebook/skydrive but will need to resync again when you go back to them after a few hours or days (I can't confirm this, but it most likely uses temporary storage from reserved storage)

 

Apps & Games

- Some apps are only 1mb while some games can be 200mb or bigger depending on how graphic intensive they are.

 

People Hub (stores contact info)

Maps / Nokia Maps (allows you to download offline maps)

 

Possible Solution:

-MANUAL DELETION-

The best way to clean the storage of your phone is to save all the pictures and videos on you phone to your computer by using the Windows Phone App to sync pictures.

 

Then delete "pictures and videos" from Pictures Hub or uninstall "Games & Apps" you don't need.

This is a somewhat tedius job but grants the highest success of you not deleting something you want.

Apps you can always re-install them, games also.

If your phone allows for external memory like Lumia 810 then you can increase storage on demand or swap memory cards.

 

-HARD RESET- 

Someone above also mentioned a hard reset. You can do that also... but you will delete everything and should only be used if you want to restore the phone to factory settings (aka brand new default set up)

 

Hope this helped and good luck! :)
