vincentchoy say:

There are two Yammer services. One is Yammer Basic which is free. The other is Yammer Enterprise, which comes with a Office365 Business, or Office365 enterprise subscription. Yammer Enterprise does not come with Office365 standalone subscriptions or Office365 Personal Subscriptions.

Since your organisation does not use Office365 for Business/Enterprise, then your team is most likely on the Yammer Basic (Free) subscription. 

When you initially setup a Yammer Basic account, you input a particular email domain name associated with your company, lets say @Contoso.com. Then you invite your co-workers to join. The prerequisite is that they also have an email address with the @contoso.com domain name. That is how Yammer Basic works. The assumption the app takes is that if you work in that particular company you should be given a company email. From then on, any user that wants to access the Yammer Basic network will use a email with the domain name that was initially used to set it up.

Now the problem is you have contoso.onmicrosoft.com thing in your organisation. This is the administrative domain a person picks, when he signs up for Office365 Business or Enterprise workloads. Did someone sign up Office365 Business or Enterprise without some of you knowing? Here's the further complication - If you have Office365 Business Essentials, Business Premium, E1, E3 or E5 or the Microsoft 365 Business, Microsoft 365 Enterprise or some of the more exotic subscriptions, Yammer Enterprise is included. Now if Yammer Enterprise is activated and the former Yammer Basic was upgraded to Enterprise, then the login credentials will now change to the Office365 Business /Enterprise login credential - which can be different from the Yammer Basic (Free) login credential. The strange thing is, your co-workers don't have a problem with their access.

To further complicate matters, the onmicrosoft.com is only an administrative domain. While it works, the usually next step is to replace it with the corporate domain, so when you first register a Office365 Business / Enterprise account with contoso.onmicrosoft.com, the next step in the setup process is that you will add contoso.com into the Office365 account, and hence forth all the users will login with their contoso.com credential instead of the onmicrosoft.com credential.

So far these might be possibilities -

1. You are trying to access a Yammer Basic network. You got your credentials right. There is something wrong with the Yammer Basic network.

2. You are trying to access a Yammer Basic network. You got the credentials wrong. Try to recall what was the credentials that was used to setup the original network. See what credentials your colleagues are using. Are they using bill(a)contoso.com or are they using bill(a)somethingelse.com. There can only be one email domain name that is associated with a Yammer Basic network.

3. You are trying to access the wrong Yammer network, perhaps a test network associated with a Office365 enterprise subscription hence all the credentials don't work

Here are some things you can try -

1. Use a different browser and try to get into your Yammer network again, using the domain that all your other co-workers are using.

2. When you type Yammer.com and get to the login page, click "use another account" and type in your credentials with the same domain name as all your other co-workers.

See if these work.
