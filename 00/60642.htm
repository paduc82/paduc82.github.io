a. user say:

Windows 7 is different in almost every aspect of using Accounts from previous versions, especially if you have come from XP.

In Windows 7, running as a Standard User is not only extremely easy, it's far more secure. There's no question of "liking" it or not - the two types of account look exactly the same and operate exactly the same!

Create your two accounts as administrators, and give both strong passwords. Install all your apps in your daily user account, then log out and log on to the other Admin account. Change your daily use account to Standard User without delay.

You will find that even running as a Standard User, you can perform Admin tasks perfectly well because all that happens is you are asked for the other Admin account password.
