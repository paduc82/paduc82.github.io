Solved

I had exactly the same thing, and searched many sites for a solution. I solved the problem, at least on my Surface.

There is a sensor that normally detects when the cover is folded shut on the front, to automatically turn off the Surface. I think what was happening is that when the cover is folded back,, that sensor is being triggered, not knowing it is picking it up from the back, not the front. So it turns the Surface off.

I solved the problem by attaching two tiny silicone pads to the back of the Surface, on the outer edge where the cover contacts it. I noticed that the cover has 1 cm oval  metal disk embedded at the edge, and I put one of the feet right where it makes contact with the cover from the back. It prevents the cover from getting close enough to trigger the auto-off sensor.

I got the tiny silicone pads at Michael's crafts store. They are back in the framing department, and are used to keep the bottom of picture frames from rubbing on the wall. They cost about two dollars with a coupon. They are clear and about the size of a thumbtack head. I also like that when I put the Surface down, it makes for a gentler padded landing.

Not a single accidental shutdown since.
