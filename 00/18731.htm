b. parker say:

My research has revealed that no one seems to be able to forward an email (with the attachment still attached) from an IMAP account using Windows Phone.

 

Furthermore, it seems that Windows Phone will not let the body of a forwarded email be edited, before it's sent.

 

I frequently have emails sent to me, with attachments that include a dialogue from the sender which I do not wish to share with the person that I need to send the attachment to.

 

So, here's a work-around that I've discovered:

 

For example, let's say your name is John Doe and your primary email address is JohnDoe(at)domainname. 

(I'm not spelling it out properly, with the @ symbol, or it will be blocked by this blog in order to preserve privacy).

 

1.  Create an account with Evernote.com under the name of your primarly email address (the one you would like the recipients to respond back to) - in this case I'd use:

User Name:  John Doe

Email Address:  JohnDoe(at)Domainname.

Evernote will set up an email address which you can email things directly into the default Evernote folder.  Put this email address in your Contacts - you'll need it in the work-around.

 

Install the Evernote App on your Windows Phone and log in.

 

2.  Create an Outlook.com account - this will be a POP3 account.  For example, in this case:  JohnDoeCopy(at)Outlook.com.

It's not necessary, but while you're setting up that account you may wish to make the 'Default From Address' the same as your primary address - JohnDoe(at)Domainname.  This is done under Outlook>Mail>Inbox>Options>Your Email Accounts.  Note:  You need to add the primary account (JohnDoe(at)Domainname) first to the Outlook Account as a 'Send Only Account'.

 

3.  Go into your primary account (JohnDoe(at)Domainname) and set up that Domain to Forward a copy of all your emails which are sent to JohnDoe(at)Domainname to the JohnDoeCopy(at)Outlook.com account.  Usually you can also have the original Domain keep a copy in the original email address inbox.

 

4.  Then set up the JohnDoeCopy(at)Outlook.com email account to also be accessible on your Windows Phone.

 

Done.

 

Now, if an email comes to your account that you don't need to forward, just deal with it in your primary email account as usual and ignore, or delete the copy in the Outlook account.

 

If an email comes into your account which you need to forward, or edit and forward, here's what you can do:

 

1.  Open the JohnDoeCopy(at)Outlook.com inbox and find the appropriate email.

 

2.  Forward the email from this account to Evernote (using that email address which Evernote provided).

 

3.  Open the Evernote app on your Phone and let it sync.  Your forwarded email will appear (with the attachment).

 

4.  Edit the body of the email, in Evernote, as you need to (removing the JohnDoeCopy(at)Outlook.com header and the body text as you see fit).

 

5.  Email the revised email to your intended recipient.  The attachment will go along for the ride.

 

Your revised email, with attachment still in place, will be delivered under you primary email address name - John Doe (JohnDoe(at)Domainname) with the only minor inconvenience of having a small reference to Evernote being included in the body of the email - 'Sent from Evernote', or something like that.

Should the recipient of this email hit reply, it will be addressed to the primary email address (not Evernote, nor the JohnDoeCopy address).

 

There you have it.  It's a little convoluted, but it works!

angel del norte say:

can i upgrade windows 7 32bit to winodows 10 64bit for free ?

No. In general, you can't upgrade any 32-bit installation to 64-bit; it's a different architecture. You'll be able to upgrade after July 29th to Win 10 32-bit, as long as your machine is compatible.

For 64-bit, you'd either have to pay, or install 64-bit Win 7 (if you happen to have a legal copy of that around), and upgrade from that.
