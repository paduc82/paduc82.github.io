This is not quite the procedure that you want, but it‚Äôs how I maintain my Cookies and allow the build-up of others to be removed:

1. Delete all your Cookies. Then in one (if possible) internet session, go to all your favourite sites to create the Cookies. Now, in Internet Explorer, Export the Cookies to a file, which will form the nucleus of your Cookie folder. It‚Äôs best to use external storage as it acts as a backup and you may want to Import the Cookies when reinstalling Windows.

2. When you feel the Cookie folder is getting too large, once again delete all the Cookies and Import the file you Exported above.

3. From time to time you will want to add new Cookies and others will cease to exist. Just delete all the Cookies, Import your backup, delete anything that is unwanted, go to the new websites to add them and then immediately Export them, thereby creating a new nucleus of Cookies.

How can I remove some formatting marks from a Word resume - Microsoft Community

bill w9 say:

How can I remove some formatting marks from a Word resume template? the circles with 4 dashes around circumference

I want to modify a Word resume template slightly to remove two-column content.  Using the "reveal code" symbol (paragraph symbol in proofreading terminology) shows a small o symbol with 4 dashes next to it at +/_ 45 degrees and +/- 135 degrees, which resists all my attempts to delete.  How can I get rid of them?
jay freedman say:

Those symbols are the end-of-cell and end-of-row markers of a table.

With the cursor in the table, click Table > Convert > Table to Text (Word 2003 or earlier) or Table Tools > Layout > Convert to Text (Word 2007 or 2010).

In the resulting dialog, choose either Paragraph marks or Tabs as the separator that will be inserted between chunks of text in the same row, then click OK.

Afterward, you may have to rearrange the chunks if they fall into the wrong order.
