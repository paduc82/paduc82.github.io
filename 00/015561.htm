    If ActiveDocument.ProtectionType <> wdNoProtection Then

Judging only by what I have read you are looking for

1. omicron with psili and perispomeni

and

Is that correct? 

(2) has a specific Unicode code at U+1F37, there is an omicron with psili at U+1F40, but there is no code point or "omicron+psili+perispomeni (which suggests to me that it is never needed in Greek).

However, the appearance of the perispomeni can vary from font to font - e.g. it can be a tilde ~ or the kind of "inverted breve" that your images show. So even in a typeface that has the U+1F37 character, the symbol may not look like the one you have, but may have a tilde (or something else) instead of the inverted breve.

There are also "combining punctuation" marks for psili (U+0313), dasia (U+0314) and perispomeni (U+0342). So in theory, 

 a. if your font does not have (1) but supports the combining marks, you may be able to get it by inserting an omicron (U+03BF) then a psili (U+0313) then a perispomeni (U+0342) 

 b. similarly if your font does not have (2) but supports the combining marks you may be able to get (2) using an iota (U+03B9) then a dasia (U+0314), then a perispomeni (U+0342)

If you have to have the shape of perispomeni you know, you might not be able to get it in the font you are using.
