john korchok say:

Owning the videos simplifies it a lot. Your definitions of embedding vs. linking are accurate. To embed a video:

  1. Open your document in Word.
  2. Click at the location where you want to include the video.
  3. Select the Insert tab in Word, then click on Object (in the Text group), then on Object again.
  4. Choose the Create from File tab, then click on the Browse button.
  5. Select the video to be inserted, then click on the Open button. The video file is inserted as an icon into your Word document.
  6. Double-click on the icon to play the video.

Once the videos are embedded in the Word file, they will travel with the Word file. The Word file will become much larger, since videos are large. You can burn this to a CD or DVD and the video will still be included. This will play on Word 2010 and later versions. For users of Word 2007 and earlier, this would require additional testing to see whether the videos play in older editions.

If the video was linked to the Word file from YouTube, and you burned the Word file to CD, the user of the CD would have to have an Internet connection to access the linked YouTube file. Otherwise it would not play.

Another alternative is to host the video on a web site under your control. You could link to these videos, but since you own the web site, you have more control over access to the files. The person playing the video would still have to have an Internet connection to view them.

johnippolito say:

I think I finally figured out how to fix the problem.

It appears that Outlook will sometimes disable the APLZOD32.dll on its own.

Where the process to re-enable the APLZOD32.dll should correct the problem, it didn't.

It appears that Outlook records more than one disable record, thus, you need to delete all the instances in Outlook to disable this module, then reinstall. This appears to work. I made this fix on 9/18/2017 and Outlook has not disabled the APLZOD32.dll module.

You can not use the standard Outlook method for dealing with the APLZOD32.dll issue. The normal addin process doesn't identify that Outlook was retaining multiple disable instances. What I did was Open Outlook, in the "Tell me what you want to do" box, enter "Disabled  Items." This will list the Disabled Items and showed that the APLZOD32.dll module appears more than once. I deleted all instances of the disable APLZOD32.dll instruction and reinstalled APLZOD32.dll. This appears to have fixed my problem.

btw I only re-engaged on this problem after I installed the Apple  9/18/2017 update, ran the prior fix alternatives and the normal process didn't work (i.e., Outlook would not retain the enabled APLZOD32.dll).

I hope this solution works for you.

Sorting column in Excel 2013

I am having trouble sorting number/letter combinations. For example i have a column with numbers 1-10 and also 1A-10A, how do I sort my column so that the numbers go in succession like 1, 1A, 2, 2A, 3, 3A, 4, 4A, etc.  ??? 
amit tandon say:

Assuming data in A1:A50, create a helper column B by entering this formula (as an array formula - CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER) in cell B1 & copy down:

=--LEFT(A1,COUNT(--MID(A1,ROW(INDIRECT("1:"&LEN(A1))),1)))

This extracts the numerical portion & then select both column A & B, do a custom sort on column B > this will sort the original values (numbers followed by letters/symbols) in column A.
