bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
I had a similar issue. I'm not sure what caused it but what corrected it for me is:
  1. Go to Tools> Envelopes
  2. Choose the option to Use settings from your printer [which is the default.] There should be no need to use Custom unless the size you want is not supported by your printer -- #10 Envelope is pretty much universal.
  3. Click the Page Setup button
  4. Choose your printer from the Format For list
  5. Choose #10 Envelope from the Paper Size list
  6. Make sure Landscape is the selected Orientation
  7. Click OK to close Page Setup
  8. Click OK to close the Envelope dialog
It should be necessary to do this only once. The setting should be retained when you Quit & relaunch Word. If not, reply here -- Your tenacity is admirable but beyond the first 3 none of the steps you've attempted include the one that may be causing the problem :-)

One consideration as far as your step #5...

If you had Word running when you removed the Normal.dotm template you defeated your purpose :-) Word reads the template on launch, so if the template is removed while the program is running it does create a new one when you Quit Word but it builds it from the information already read from the one you removed, essentially recreating a new copy of the old one. Any time it's necessary to replace the Normal.dotm make sure to Quit the program first. The new template then will be built from scratch.

How to Unsync contacts? - Microsoft Community

gustavoreithel say:

How to Unsync contacts?

I have a Nokia Lumia 920. However when I signed up to my Windows account all of my Windows Live Messenger contacts where placed in the phone's contacts. I don't want to see my Windows Live Messenger contacts mixed together with the contacts I store in the phone.
How do I fix this? I only want to see the contacts that I store in the phone. However, whenever I filter the Hotmail contacts all of the contacts are removed, even the ones I stored directly in the phone.
julie fka profjulie say:

Well the contacts that you added to the phone are being synced to your Microsoft account....you know,the same account where your other, so called Windows Live Messenger contacts are stored. To the Microsoft account and to a windows phone, a contact is a contact and there is no way to differentiate a messenger contact from any other kind of contact. When you filter the Microsoft contacts, you filter out all the contacts that are related to and synced to the Microsoft account.

 

You could create a group that includes your most frequently used contacts, something like a speed dial list.  Or, you could delete all your Messenger contacts, but that would permanently delete them from your Microsoft account and I'm not sure you want to do that.

 

If you want to keep your Messenger contacts completely separate from your other contacts, you could create a secondary Microsoft account and sync that account to your phone too. Then when you add a new contact to your phone you'd be prompted to select the account where the contact will be synced (you'd choose the 2nd account). Then if you filter out your original Microsoft account, you'd still see the contacts in the secondary account only.
