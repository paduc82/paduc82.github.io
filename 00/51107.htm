See your other replies; I also recommend that you buy a bigger drive.

Also note that the term "memory" is used to refer to the place where the computer holds the program and data it is working with at the moment. It's normally random access memory (RAM), but that can be extended with the page or swap file on the hard disk. The word "memory" is never used to refer to the space on a drive (whether that's a hard drive, SSD, diskette, CD, DVD, or thumb drive). Space on a disk drive is normally called "storage" or simply "disk space."

Calling disk space "memory" isn't so much wrong as just unconventional. In a sense, disk space is, of course, memory, but the important thing to realize is that the word is never used that way, and if you do so, you do it at the risk of seriously confusing your audience.

arvind m. [microsoft] say:

Some Bluetooth radios have firmware loaded to them by the radio manufacturer's driver.  If this radio is one of those, then it is (at least in theory) possible that the radio manufacturer has a driver that includes newer firmware.  But that driver may never have been tested on your laptop.

I have not run into this issue myself, so please don't take this as definitive guidance.

There's another option - if you have a USB port to spare on the laptop, you could get a USB plug-in Bluetooth radio that supports Bluetooth 4.0 and works with Windows 10.  Windows only supports one Bluetooth radio, so you will have to disable the built-in Bluetooth radio using Device Manager if you go with this option.

arvind m. [microsoft] say:

It is possible that your Sony Vaio laptop has an older version of Bluetooth radio that does not work with the Microsoft Designer mouse.

The Microsoft Designer mouse requires a Bluetooth 4.0 radio on the laptop because the mouse implements (the relatively recent) Bluetooth Smart (LE) technology for increased battery life.

Here's an article that shows how to check whether or not the Bluetooth radio on your laptop supports 4.0 or newer:

https://www.microsoft.com/hardware/en-us/support/bluetooth-4-compatibility

If your laptop does not support Bluetooth 4.0, then if it is feasible, you could try a different model of mouse that will work with the radio version on your laptop.

Hope this helps-

Arvind
