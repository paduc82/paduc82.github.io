Opening recordset with Where clause having variables instead of - Microsoft Community

pantry_helper say:

Opening recordset with Where clause having variables instead of constants

I am in the process of creating a set of applications for a local food pantry so they can keep better track of their operations.  I am currently trying to open a recordset based on matching the current date and matching a foreign key obtained from a form.  The examples I have seen with Where clauses all have constants.   So

"Select * From Visits Where Where Pantry_date = #12/16/2014# AND household_idfk = 8;"

works fine, but it does not support the dynamic behavior I want.  What I need is something like this:

"Select * From Visits Where Pantry_date = Date() AND household_idfk = Forms![client household]![household id] ;"

Is there a way of getting this behavior?

A little more background:  the select is in a VBA module that gets invoked by clicking a button on a form.

ken sheridan say: Concatenate the values of the parameters into the SQL statement, e.g.

Dim rst As DAO.Recordset
Dim strSQL As String

strSQL = ""SELECT * FROM Visits WHERE Pantry_date = #" & _
     Format(DATE(), "yyyy-mm-dd")  & "# AND " & _
     household_idfk = " & Forms![client household]![household id]

Set rst = CurrentDb.OpenRecordset (strSQL)

Note the use of the ISO standard for date notation of YYYY-MM-DD to ensure international unambiguity.
