On October 4th, 2016, we released an Office 2016 Click-to-Run update that included accessibility improvements to Office Form Controls. Unfortunately, these improvements introduced an issue that affects the way VBA form controls look or work under certain conditions.

For example, buttons or other controls may be misaligned or overlapping in a worksheet, making it hard or impossible to use the form. In some cases, your Office app may stop responding when you attempt to open a macro-enabled file containing form controls.

Impact

  • Office 2016 users who have the Click-to-Run version of the September feature update installed. Office 365 subscribers will see Version 1609 (Build 7369.xxxx) in File > Account and Office 2016 perpetual customers will see 16.0.7369.xxxx.

  • Earlier versions of Office, such as Office 2013, if those versions are installed on the same PC as the offending versions of Office 2016.

This issue does will not affect:

  • Those who receive updates through Deferred Channel
  • Those using volume license editions of Office 2016, such as Microsoft Office Professional Plus, who receive updates through Windows Installer technology (MSI)
  • Those who use Office on other platforms, including Office Online

Status: Fixed

  • Version 1609 (Build 7369.2054)
  • Version 1610 (Build 7466.2038)

To move a user from Office 365 to Exchange on-premises or vice versa, you need to have an Exchange-based hybrid deployment. So, are all users in your organization hosted in the Exchange Online?
