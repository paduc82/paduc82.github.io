Calculate hours excluding weekends - Microsoft Community

vivek m r say:

Calculate hours excluding weekends

Hi.

I fount the below formula to calculate the hours excluding week end.
=NETWORKDAYS(A2,NOW())-1+MOD(NOW(),1)-MOD(A2,1)

It works fine, except if the start time in A2 is say 19-feb-2012 20:00 and I am viewing the results on 21-Feb-2012 at 14:00 hours, the value is returning as 18 hours. The correct value should be 38 hours. i.e. 24 hours for Monday and 14 hours for Tuesday.

Please advise where am I going wrong.

Thanks Vivek.
jeeped say:
... It works fine, except if the start time in A2 is say 19-feb-2012 20:00 and I am viewing the results on 21-Feb-2012 at 14:00 hours, the value is returning as 18 hours. The correct value should be 38 hours. i.e. 24 hours for Monday and 14 hours for Tuesday ...

The NETWORKDAYS() function is properly adjusting for A2 starting on a Sunday by not including it, but then you are subtracting MOD(A2,1) or 20:00 or 20 hours at the end of your formula without taking into account that Sunday is to be discounted in its entirety.

NETWORKDAYS() is also inclusive but only work days, so you cannot simply subtract 1 or 2 without checking to see if the start or finish date is a weekend. Try something like this,

=NETWORKDAYS(A2,TODAY())-NETWORKDAYS(INT(A2),INT(A2))-NETWORKDAYS(TODAY(),TODAY())+(NETWORKDAYS(INT(A2),INT(A2))*(1-MOD(A2,1)))+(NETWORKDAYS(TODAY(),TODAY())*MOD(NOW(),1))

I did some preliminary testing on that but you should probably follow up with more to ensure that it is returning the values you want. I want formatting the result as [h]:mm to get the collected hours and minutes.
