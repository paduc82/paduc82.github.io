robert aldwinckle on forums say:

same problem on two different machines on our wireless network.

It's good to have two cases to compare but I think you are looking at the wrong commonality.  Typically "stopped working" is a euphemism for "has crashed" so you should be thinking in terms of what is being loaded.  E.g. does this happen in No Add-ons mode?  Does it happen in safe mode?  In either case this mostly gives you a clue about things it can not be or where to look for things that it might be.  In order to narrow it down onto something that it is you need to do clean boot troubleshooting.  AutoRuns from SysInternals (available from TechNet) is usually suggested for helping with that analysis.  

On a different tack if it is an APPCRASH you can get a list of loaded modules from the associated Report.WER file.  Unfortunately the associated report in the Reliability Monitor does not include that detail, though that diagnostic would certainly help you identify what you were looking for in terms of timestamp and crash location.  Seeing a list of loaded modules often gives you an idea of things which might be avoided and hence change the symptom.

And of course if neither of those approaches seem effective there is always the possibility of creating a dump and using WinDbg on it but even that may not be conclusive because the crash may be due to corruption caused by a module which is no longer loaded.  In fact, a common case is where a module was loaded, then was unloaded but its error handler is left loaded or some other trace of it is left behind that causes a branch to its former location.  Etc.

 

Good luck

Robert Aldwinckle
