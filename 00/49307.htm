Hi all,

There has been a lot of confusion over the much-anticipated Windows Hello feature coming to Windows 10. Don't stall your upgrade though, Windows 10 still has loads of other great features.

For those of you who don't already know, Windows Hello is a sign-in method introduced in Windows 10. It uses Microsoft's 'Passport' technology to not only log you in to your computer using advanced methods, but allows you to log in to websites and buy items online with these methods. These methods include fingerprint scanning, iris detection and facial recognition! However, only a tiny amount of us will be getting any of these features.

Currently, Windows Hello is only available for computers with a special "depth camera" which uses infrared sensors that are far better at figuring out who you are. For security reasons, unless you have one of these cameras, your computer will not have the option to use Hello. For some weird reason, even if you have a compatible fingerprint scanner, Hello is still unavailable for your computer in the form of fingerprint scanning.

Most new computers that are over £300 ($450 roughly) or so will have these special cameras. As will computers made in the last year or so (mid-2014 or later) that are very high spec. But always check that it has one before you buy a new computer.
