That happens with some Touch pads.  They appear too much to the OS as Touch support, so the mouse pointer is unnecessary.   If you have a Ctrl- key you can turn on a Mouse Properties Accessibility option called Mouse Sonar and use a press of a Ctrl- key to let you know where the mouse pointer is.  If you have a real keyboard or at least some emulation of a real Numeric keypad you could use Mousekeys to act as a real mouse to move the pointer and let it show that way.  Otherwise get a real mouse and just use your Touch pad for Touch things such as scrolling and semantic zoom (if your Touchpad supports that).
