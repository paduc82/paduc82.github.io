jetta48 say:

Stalemate is not a move, but a position that may occur. It basically means that when it is a player's turn that player can make no legal move whatsoever with any piece, but is not in check.

Unlike checkmate, stalemate is a draw.

Even if one side has an overwhelming piece advantage if its the opponents turn and they can make no legal move, but are not in check then it is a draw despite the piece advantage. This situation often catches out inexperienced players near the end game who are rubbing their hand in glee at their piece advantage only to be crest-fallen when they carelessly create a stalemate situation thereby turning a winning situation into a draw..

Chess Titans should follow standard chess rules.

jetta48 say:
You say > no leagle move< well if that would be the case woulden't that be a checkmate?


No, in checkmate the king is in check. In stalemate it is not in check - it just does not have any legal move available. You will find some example stalemate postions on Wikipedia here:
