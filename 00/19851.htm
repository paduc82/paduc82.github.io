coleac say: I have figured this out, after trying all the things above.  It came down to one thing, two-factor authentication.  If you have this enabled, to sign into OneNote on your PC you have to use your App Password, not your actual password to your Live account.  So log into your Live account, Click on Security Info, and then click create a new app password.  It will then display the current, or new, app password.  Now use that password that it gives you in OneNote and everything should sign right in.  Now I am able to sync all my files from OneNote to my phone without any issues, without messing with IE settings, and all that other stuff.

An explanation of why the indexing code doesn't ignore letter cases may forestall some complaints.

One of the major uses of automatic indexing is to list people's names. However, many people's names are also common words all in lower case. If you're indexing a document about British Prime Ministers, you wouldn't want every occurrence of "may" to show up.

The various table commands can't be used unless the insertion point is inside a table; that is why the Table tabs on the ribbon are hidden in other contexts.

When Word abandoned the classic menu interface in 2007, contextual tabs were a big part of the new design. Older versions only had contextual right-click menus.

menneisyys say: I had the chance of picking up my 128M iPAQ 210 from home and thoroughly tested Online TVx. In a word: it needs some MAJOR playback optimizations. All streams played with definitely inferior frame speed even in Portrait mode. This has further decreased to about 1-2 fps(!) in Landscape. Of course, there're no such poblems with either Spb TV (tested the latest version of it; it works wonderfully on my iPAQ) and only to a lesser degree with the built-in WMP (both in full screen mode) on the same PDA.

 

All in all, give the trial (which plays back for 5 minutes uninterrupted) a thorough try on your phone before paying anything for it. It may behave far better on your phone than on mine. However, I'd still start with the Spb TV + wiitv combo – at least on my equipment.

 

Let me know if you need more info on how you can easily add the stream addresses as favorites to IEM so that you don't end up having to fire up wwitv.com all the time you want to watch your favorite TV channels. It needs a bit of file editing and hacking.
