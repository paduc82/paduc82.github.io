joel in vic say:

Andrew,

I had the exact same setup as you.  Lumia 950 and CR-200.

I think there are two things at work here.

1) The locations of the charging coils in each device do not quite line up accurately.  That means that even when it indicates "charging" it is right on the edge of being connected and in fact very little charge is transferred to the phone.   I have a ultra thin case on my 950 and the addition of that case moves the two charging coils far enough apart that it won't even register as charging.

2) The newer phones are more power hungry, and even if the coil alignment was perfect it would still be capable of draining the phone faster than Qi is capable of charging.

Have you noticed that someone at Nokia had the foresight to leave the centre of the bottom support on the CR-200 open?  It's perfectly positioned to guide a USB-C cable into the charging port on your Lumia 950.
