scottgem say:

The reason you are getting that error is because you are not identifying where to find TxtClassID. Objects are part of collections, so you need to identify the collection the object belongs in.

I would not use the On click event by the way. Its not clear which is the current form when you are doing this. But the syntax would be something like:

Forms!formname!destinationcontrol = Forms!formname!sourcecontrol

If you are sending it from the Active form to another form then then I would use the After Update event of the control on the Active form. If you are pulling it from another form into the Active form, then I would use the Got focus event of the control on the Active form.

2.    Choose File | Save as | iCalendar format (*.ics)
3.    Click Save.

Email rules allow the user to block specific word from the subject, email address or content of an email. You may follow the steps below on how to setup rules.

 

1. Sign in to your Outlook.com account with your Microsoft account.
2. Click on Gear icon then More mail settings... in the upper right corner of the page.

4. Click on the New button.
5. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to,
a. First drop-down menu - choose Subject
b. Second drop-down menu - choose contains
c. Third field - input the word you would like to block
6. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply, click on Delete these messages
7. Click Save button.
