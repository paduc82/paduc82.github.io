bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
I'm not sure where you got that impression, but IIRC no version of Word has ever worked that way. In fact, you're correct that when you click the Scroll Bar or drag the Scroll Bar button the 'cursor' [insertion point] does not move at all -- it's intended that it stay put. One of the main benefits of the design is to facilitate more efficiently selecting long passages -- You can select in the viewing area, scroll to any other part of the document, then hold Shift while you click to extend the selection to that point. That obviates the need to drag in the document for 2, 3 ,10 pages or more.

I definitely agree about Option+Up/Dn Arrow (preferably Command+Up/Dn Arrow), but that is the technique expressly assigned to moving the insertion point from one using the Document Map, using Go To or the Browse By feature are all more accurate & speedy. Even the use of Outline View or Draft View can be a far more productive means of dealing with long documents. There are numerous other navigation options as well.

It may be useful to take a look at the info in Word Help on the following topics. Pay particular attention to the [NOTES] that pertain to use of a laptop style of keyboard as well as the fact that OS X has confiscated much of the keyboard functionality formerly afforded to applications.
