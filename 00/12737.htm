dsquareduk say: My understanding of your questions is as follows: you have a mobile phone contract which gives you a set number of 'free' minutes/hours each month for accessing the Internet, and also probably a set number of 'free' minutes and texts. Whenever you use more than your 'free' amount you are charge additional money over your normal monthly contract rate.

WLAN, or WiFi, is separate to your monthly mobile contract - i.e. you are not using your mobile provider's mobile/cellular network.

If you connect your phone via a WiFi access point (WLAN or Wireless Local Area Network) then you are NOT using your mobile/cellular access time. This is the same as using your PC on the WLAN.

This assumes that you're on your own WLAN connection or a free WiFi hotspot.

If you're outside and you connect purely via your mobile using 3G (mobile data connection) then you are using your free internet time and anything over your monthly allowance will be chargeable.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

The message is just "There are too many errors in this document." And then "Check spelling as you type" shuts down. You can still run spell check manually. Something similar happens (or used to happen before computers became stuffed with RAM and gigantic HDs) when you had too many graphics; Word would spontaneously start using picture placeholders.

 

In other words, it's a performance issue: when Word detects that continuing to check spelling (or display graphics) would make Word too slow to be usable, it protects itself (and you from terminal frustration) by reducing the number of tasks it is required to perform.
