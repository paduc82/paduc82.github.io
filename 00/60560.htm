tryx3 say:

The phone will set up the new SD card [not just format it] & will set up the phone to use that new SD card so you will need to let it do that. 

 

Unless there is any specific advice on your phone maker's website or your ISP's support pages for that type of phone:

 

1  Make a note of the downloaded apps in your phone & also any apps that have been set to use the SD card as you may need to re-download or at least check the setup of these apps when you have finished.

 

2  Put your old SD card in your PC SD card reader [using a MicroSD card adapter, if applicable].  Or connect the phone to the PC & set the phone's connection options to Disk drive / whatever term your phone uses for its connection types.

 

3  Copy your user files, such as music tracks, from the folders you find on the SD card* to somewhere handy on your PC and make a note of the folders they came from.  The folders used vary between phone OSs & also, possibly, between different makes or even models so you will probably need to look at each folder in turn to find out what's in them.

 

4  Once the copies are on the PC check them again as you could cause a problem if you later copy back system-related or application-related files onto the new card.

 

5  Connect the phone to the PC & set the phone's connection options to Disk drive / whatever term your phone uses for its connection types.  [You could try switching the phone off then putting the new SD card in your PC but you would have to ensure that Windows did not try to "repair" its file system by reformatting it.  You'd then have to put it back in your phone when you have finished copying to make sure that the new SD card remains fully usable.  Only switch the phone back on when the SD card is in it or the phone's OS will try to repair itself for the detected absence of the SD card that it has previously set itself up for]

 

6  Copy across your user files from the PC to the phone's SD card - again, be careful not to copy any system-related or application-related files.

 

7  Check the downloaded apps in your phone & also any apps that have been set to use the SD card as you may need to re-download or at least check the setup of these apps.

 

- - - - - - - -

 

*  Just as a set of examples, my HTC Desire X with Android 4.1 uses these SD card folders:

 

DCIM [photos & pre-4.1 upgrade screenshots]

Download [downloaded documents, with pdf files often being in the subfolder Adobe Reader]

Music

My documents [for files made using the installed Polaris Office, with sound recordings being in  a subfolder My recordings]

Pictures\Screenshots [a new folder that contains screenshots since the 4.1 upgrade]

Podcasts [ones I have downloaded directly from their source]

Ringtones

sms_backup [for deliberately saved backups of my sms app]

[my Meercel RSS reader keeps its cache in subfolders of com.meercel.feedreader.rssdemonad]

9. Click Next. Wait for the driver to be installed, then finally, click Close.

Your Apple device should now be detected successfully within iTunes.
