Just FYI in case it happens at some point.

Certain embedded files that are part of legitimate programs and specialized fix tools (like RKill), may at times be detected by some anti-virus and anti-malware scanners as suspicious, a Risk Tool, Hacking Tool, Potentially Unwanted Program, a possible threat or even Malware (virus/trojan) when that is not the case. This occurs for a variety of reasons to include the tool's compiler, the files it uses, whether files are compressed, packed, or obfuscated to protect code, what behavior (routines, scripts, etc) it performs, any registry strings it may contain and the type of security engine that was used during the scan. In these cases the detection is a "false positive" and can be ignored.

garym123 say:

After a phone reset none of the apps installed on the phone will be gone, apps on the SD card will no longer work. In either, all apps will be installed again when or if you restore from a backup.

From a phone backup, phone settings, start screen layout, and your installed app list is restored.  Backup data content from apps is restored if the app developer designed the app to restore the data.

Your documents, pdf files saved in a pdf app, music and pictures are not backed up if they are on the phone. You will have to ensure all that is saved to your PC or on OneDrive. When you reset you will be asked if you want to format the SD card or not. Apps on the SD card have to be installed again no matter what you do with formatting the SD card. Your data on the SD card will be there following the reset if you choose not to format the SD card.

Contacts, calendar, email and text messages are part of your Microsoft account and you get all that back. Text messages are restored only if you have sync messages between devices turned on in messaging turned on, this is not retroactive if you turn it on now.

Nothing is automatically restored from OneDrive except what is part of phone backup. 

I am not sure about contact groups. I can only guess they will still be there.

Hope that answers all your questions.

I have never reset using buttons so I am not sure if you will be asked about formatting the SD card or not.

I would not take a chance about phone encryption. I would turn it off if you currently have it turned on and power cycle the phone.
