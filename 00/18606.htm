scottgem say:

First, as Daniel said, EVERY multi user app NEEDS to be split. Please read the articles he linked to.

The problem is being caused NOT by data changes, but by design changes. You are probably using functionality that is causing design changes. most likely filtering. If you create a filter on a form or report, the filter is stored with the object. For example. you might be looking for data for a single date. So A filter is created: Datefield = 1/12/16. That is stored as a string in the Filter property, in effect changing the design of the form. But because someone else has the same form open, even to a different record, they can't create a filter because the design of the form has been changed. 

This is why it is imperative that every user has their own copy of the front end. This way the user can create all the filters or similar functionality that affect design without affecting other users.

Hello John,

 

Welcome to the forum! It can be hard to understand why you are seeing such behavior in the email you received-- the sender and receiver of the email is the same. We'll be happy to answer your inquiry.

 

Based on the screenshot you provided, it's very likely that the sender of the email put his own email address in the "To:" field so he can receive a copy of the email or for whatever purpose he has in mind. Since his own address is the only one that's visible in the "To:" field, it's likely that your email address and the other recipients of the message were placed on the BCC (Blind Carbon Copy) field to mask the information of the recipients of the email from each other. The BCC addresses will only be visible to the sender of the email.
