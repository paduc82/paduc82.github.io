Examples of ways Microsoft is dumbing down Word - Microsoft Community

ritergal say:

Examples of ways Microsoft is dumbing down Word

Example 1: Full justification

In versions of Word before 2013 we had the option to "Do full justification the way WordPerfect 6.x for Windows does." This option gives a lovely, smooth, professional look to pages with full justification. Word has always failed that test with their default setting which gives a clunky, clumpy "do-it-yourself-on-the-cheap" appearance. A book self-published with Word sticks out like a sore thumb compared to those laid out in InDesign. Activate that WordPerfect option and you're entirely presentable to the world. 

Workaround:

Save your document as a Word 97-2003 doc. Then open the Advanced tab in Options and scroll down to the bottom to Compatibility option. Select "Do full justification the way WordPerfect 6.x for Windows does." That's the eighth item on that list. Click OK. This setting will stick when you resave in docx format to retrieve the power formatting tools available in 2013. 

Unfortunately if you do this with a heavily formatted document, lots of your setting will be lost. Remember to do it early -- or set it as your default by selecting "all new documents" rather than the current one when you change the setting. 

Example 2: Suppressing space at the top of the page.

Until 2013 Word allowed you a choice about having space above a heading at the top of a page. With the advent of 2013 they removed that option and stripped out space above headings on all pages other than those immediately following a section break. Now, all headings after a chapter title are jammed tight against the top margin. Yuck! 

Workaround:

Edit your heading style. Remove all space above from the paragraph setting, i.e. 19 pt. Open the font menu. On the Advanced tab, select Position and lower the font by the same amount you just removed, i.e. 19 pt. Make sure your style is updated. You have control again! This workaround will add space at the top of a page after a natural page break or New Page break.

Is Microsoft trying to create business for Adobe by forcing people to buy InDesign? Or maybe they think users are too dumb and irresponsible to exercise good taste?

Hi Helge,

Please check if you finished all the preparation of an IMAP migration. Especially the connection to your IMAP server. Please run below commands to verify:

  1. Connect to Exchange Online PowerShell
  2. Run below command:

Test-MigrationServerAvailability -IMAP -RemoteServer <FQDN of IMAP server> -Port <143 or 993> -Security <None, Ssl, or Tls>

For your reference: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/office365/enterprise/powershell/use-powershell-to-perform-an-imap-migration-to-office-365

Besides, may I know if the error occurs to just some specific users or all users? You can try to do an IMAP migration (Data migration) in the Office 365 Admin Center to check you can complete without any error. 

For your reference: https://support.office.com/en-us/article/IMAP-migration-in-the-Office-365-admin-center-4682f2e4-f720-4868-91ab-207f5b0c325d
