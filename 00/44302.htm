  1. Open Settings and click Update & Security.
  2. Click Recovery and then Open System Restore.
  3. Hit Next and click Show more restore points.
  4. Select a restore point where your programs were still installed, then select Scan for affected programs to see what programs will be affected if a system restore is performed.
  5. If you agree with the possible changes on your system, click Close, then hit Next.
  6. Save and close any programs that are opened, then hit Finish.
  7. Click Yes to start the system restore process.

cmdrkeene say:

Hi LindsayC71, I see that this is your first post here.  Welcome to our community!

I can pretty safely say it's the touchpad.  Microsoft didn't design it this way, but most laptop companies have "helpfully" programmed their touchpad to act like a touchscreen.  If you swipe your finger in from the sides of the touchpad, it pretends you really did it on the screen and brings out the charms bar (and the clock), or switches programs.

Since this isn't something Microsoft built, I can't tell you exactly how to turn it off.  Every laptop builder decides to put the options in a different place.  Toshiba laptops don't come with the option at all -- you have to go to Toshiba's website and download the settings program just to turn it off (which is one reason I will not buy a Toshiba laptop).

The best I can tell you is to check all your settings.  Go to Control Panel (press +X to get there easily), and then the Mouse section to see if there are any touchpad gesture controls.  Or if you have a Sony laptop, look for the Sony Control Center program.   If all else fails, check your instruction manual or ask the laptop company where they put the option to turn off touchpad gestures.

Although this isn't anything Microsoft can help with, I wish you the best of luck and apologise you are having to deal with this.  This is just what happens with laptop companies try to be way too "helpful".
