Go to the slide you want to change, then in the Slides group on the Home tab, click the down arrow next to Slide Layout (it may not be labeled as such depending on your screen size, but it's the uppermost of the three buttons next to New Slide.)

Footnotes in Word 2010

 

 

How can I get a series of footnotes to appear in a continuous line, the text wrapping round line breaks as necessary, rather than being stacked one on top of the other, as they are by default?

macropod say:
You can format the paragraph break characters that terminate each footnote as hidden. You may want to ensure there is a space character before them, though, so the following footnote # doesn't get pushed up against the end of the preceding footnote.

williamorwin say:

 

Thanks very much for this, Paul, but how do I "format the paragraph break characters that terminate each footnote as hidden".   If you reply, as I hope you will, please assume that my computer expertise rates about one out of ten.  Thanks.   Bill

stefan blom say:
Press the ¶ button on the Home tab to display nonprinting marks, among them paragraph marks (¶). Select each mark and apply hidden text formatting (for example by pressing Ctrl+Shift+H).

Note, however, that this trick doesn't really conserve any space (if that's part of your objective), because even if the notes appear to be in one paragraph, Word still reserves vertical space for all the footnotes at the bottom of the page.

macropod say:

 

Yes, but presumably the endnotes will still appear at the end of the document, which is not what I want (I want the notes on the same page as the text).   Did you think about the way it is done in that book I mentioned?   Is there no other possibility in another Office package such as Excel or PowerPoint?   Is there no possibility of formatting the footnotes into a set of columns?   Or a table?


Endnotes do not have to be located at the end of the document - they can instead be located at the end of every Section (eg you might put a Section break at the end of a chapter).

 

As Stefan has already observed, compressing the space between footnotes the way I described doesn't reduce the white space at the bottom of the page.

 

Given that the footnotes of concern are so short, perhaps you should consider having their content in-line (bracketed) in the body of the document instead. Alternatively, you could fake it in Word, with the judicious use of (custom-numbered) footnotes every so often (with the numbers formatted as hidden text) and the faking of the desired footnote numbers via superscripted numbers in both in the body and the actual footnotes, plus the corresponding 'footnote' text. A lot of work.

 

Applications like Excel & Powerpoint are quite unsuitable for this. You could probably achieve the result you're after with a page layout program like Publisher.
