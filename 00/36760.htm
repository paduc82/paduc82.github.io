    tonyhanemaayer say: Just so I am clear, after Microsoft does a major update and Creative sound cards quit working it is Creative's fault. Interesting approach.

    a. user say:

    Just so I am clear, after Microsoft does a major update and Creative sound cards quit working it is Creative's fault. Interesting approach.

    That's completely correct. Did you not understand what I wrote previously? Too advanced, perhaps. I'll try again.

    When a hardware manufacturer 'supports' their hardware in Windows - or any other operating system - that means two things:

    1- When the operating system undergoes a major update, like the semi-annual features updates of Windows 10, the hardware manufacturer tests their hardware against advance copies of the revised operating system.

    Microsoft willingly supplies advance copies of proposed revisions to Windows 10 to its hardware partners exactly for that reason, as well as detailed documentation of all changes, as needed.

    If the hardware manufacturer's testing shows that their hardware still works well with the changes to Windows, all is well.

    2- If the hardware manufacturer's testing shows that their hardware won't work properly with the changes to Windows, the manufacturer updates their drivers so that the hardware will continue to work properly with the updated version of Windows.

    As time goes on, and Windows keeps changing (so as not to fall behind its competition, and maybe even to get ahead of its competition,) it becomes increasingly difficult for hardware manufacturers to keep updating their drivers, because old technology can only be modified so much until it just won't work properly, or at all, with the new technology in Windows.

    At some point, the hardware manufacturer will decide that it's just too difficult and expensive, or simply impossible, to keep on modifying their older hardware to keep up with the demands of modern technology. When that time comes, the manufacturer decides to stop trying to modify the drivers . When this happens, we say that the hardware is 'no longer supported.'

    But there's another reason why older hardware loses support, that's got nothing to do with changes to operating systems. Rather, it's all about the money. After a certain time, hardware manufacturers want people to buy their newer products, so they can keep on making money. There's no money to be made by investing in old hardware that people have already bought and paid for.

    In both situations that I explained above, the hardware manufacturer has decided to stop supporting their own products. There's nothing in it for Microsoft.

    We have had people in this forum - conspiracy theorists, you know the type - that believe there's a secret cabal of Microsoft and hardware developers where Microsoft gets paid off to intentionally break old hardware. That, of course, is crazy.

    How do you get a printer back online if it is offline? - Microsoft Community

    westtexaswoman say:

    How do you get a printer back online if it is offline?

    HP 8600 All-In--One was kicked offline and I can't get it back on.  

     

    [Moved from feedback]

    winston m say:

    Hi,

     

    1. Which is the operating system installed on the computer?

    2. What happens when you tried to make the printer online?

    3. Do you get any error messages?

     

     

    This problem occurs if the computer has the following registry value configured when you set the printer to the Offline status.

     

    Method 1:

     

    You may refer this article and perform the steps provided.  

     

    The new printer status is "Offline" after you delete a print queue and then restart a Windows-based computer

    http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2027593

     

    Important This section, method, or task contains steps that tell you how to modify the registry. However, serious problems might occur if you modify the registry incorrectly.     How to back up and restore the registry in Windows

     

    Method 2:

     

    I would suggest you to follow the steps provided below and check if it helps:

     

    a. Open the Start menu, click on control panel and click on the "Printers and Faxes" icon. This will open a window with a list of the printers currently set up on your computer.
    b. Double-click on the icon of the printer you want to change to online. A pop-up window detailing all current print jobs will open.
    c. Go to "Printer" in the menu bar of the pop-up window and uncheck "Use Printer Offline." This action will change the printer from offline to online.
