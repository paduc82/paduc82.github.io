john sr wilson say:
Hi Paul

You are seeing a bug in 2003 i think. The shape is being selected but the screen isn't updating. You can probably force the screen to update by switching views as below.

Sub Locate_All_Shapes()

Dim ppPres As Presentation
Dim ppsld As Slide
Dim ppshp As Shape
 
    Set ppPres = ActivePresentation

    For Each ppsld In ppPres.Slides
   
        ppsld.Select
            ActiveWindow.ViewType = ppViewSlide
            ActiveWindow.ViewType = ppViewNormal
        For Each ppshp In ppsld.Shapes
            ppshp.Select
            MsgBox ("Shape: " & ppshp.Name & Chr(10) & Chr(10) & _
                    "Top: " & ppshp.Top & Chr(10) & Chr(10) & _
                    "Left: " & ppshp.Left & Chr(10) & Chr(10) & _
                    "Bottom: " & (ppshp.Top + ppshp.Height) & Chr(10) & Chr(10) & _
                    "Right: " & (ppshp.Left + ppshp.Width) & Chr(10) & Chr(10) & _
                    "Width: " & ppshp.Width & Chr(10) & Chr(10) & _
                    "Height: " & ppshp.Height)
            ActiveWindow.Selection.Unselect
        Next ppshp
        If MsgBox("Next Slide?", vbYesNo, "Continue") = vbNo Then GoTo NormalExit
    Next ppsld
NormalExit:
    Set ppshp = Nothing
    Set ppsld = Nothing
    Set ppPres = Nothing
End Sub

jacques raubenheimer say:

To get really slick, you would need a macro that allows you to browse your hard drive, find the files/folders you want, and then scan them all, including or excluding subfolders, find all *.docx files, then open them and save them as html.

While that can be done, I am volunteering my time here, so I'm not going to give you as complex a macro as that. Perhaps someone else has something handy that they can copy and paste for you.

What you can do is open all the documents you want converted, and then use this macro:

Sub ConvertOpenDocsToHTML()
Dim doc As Document
For Each doc In Application.Documents
    doc.SaveAs2 _
        FileName:=doc.Name, _
        FileFormat:=wdFormatHTML, _
        AddToRecentFiles:=False
    doc.Close
Next doc
End Sub

karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

I will give that a try, do you know if I can set them up as POP3 without deleting the imap ones until after?

You absolutely can - just another email account which will point to the exact same server email account. Just remember that after you configure your first POP account - either using a previous PST file or letting Outlook create a new one, you will need to make sure that any subsequent POP account you configure uses the same PST file as that configured for the first one.

One more thing that can inadvertently catch you off guard - once you configure your POP account(s) - Outlook is going to download everything once each account is configured SO here are the ramifications of that...

- if you decide to leave messages on the server - an option in the Advanced section of POP configuration, you will have to delete everything manually from the server unless you also set a timeframe where things get deleted automatically

- if Outlook downloads everything and you don't have the <Leave message on server> set - your Imap folders will become empty since it's the same email account/data

Suggestion - before you start configuring your POP accounts --> from within Outlook go to File --> Options --> Advanced --> scroll down to the Send & Receive section --> click the Send/Recieve button --> turn off scheduled send/recieve. Once done, configure your pop accounts so nothing gets downloaded until you want it to be. When you're happy with everything, reset your S/R back to normal.
