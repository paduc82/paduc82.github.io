Hi,

 

 

Depending on the app you wish to purchase for your child, there are two methods for accomplishing this goal.

 

 

Most apps will allow you to purchase the app for your child‚Äôs account directly from within Windows Store by following these steps:

 

 

1.      Have the child sign into Windows 8.1 with their Microsoft account and open Windows Store.

2.      Open the Settings charm and select Your account.

3.      Under ‚ÄúSign in with a different account‚Äù, select Change user.

4.      Sign in with the parent‚Äôs Microsoft account.

5.      Verify that the parent‚Äôs Payment and billing info is correctly associated with Windows Store within Your account.

 

6.      If you‚Äôve already purchased the app on the parent‚Äôs account, swipe down or right-click within the Store, and select Your apps.  Your apps displays all of the apps associated with the parent‚Äôs account.  Select the app and click Install, shown at the bottom of the screen.

 

7.      If you haven‚Äôt already purchased the app, you can search for the app in the Store and purchase it.  The app will automatically install to your child‚Äôs account. 

8.     Important:  Your child is currently signed into the Store with your account and your payment options!  When you are finished, be sure to return to Settings and Your account, and Change User back to your child‚Äôs account (or Sign Out if you have not yet installed Windows 8.1), so that your payment method does not remain available to the child.

 

That technique will not work with every app.  Some apps (such as Halo Spartan Assault) are designated by the developer such that they can only be installed to the Microsoft account that purchased it.  In such cases, if the purchaser‚Äôs Microsoft account is not the same as the person signed into Windows, the app will display an error message.

 

 

 

If the app you want to install onto your child‚Äôs account is designed this way, the child will need to purchase the app.  Unfortunately, there is currently no way to temporarily associate a credit card or PayPal account to the child‚Äôs account, purchase the app, and then remove the payment method fully from the child‚Äôs account.  Therefore, the safest method would be to use a new feature within Windows 8.1 that allows you to purchase apps via gift cards from a retailer.  Xbox gift cards, Bing Rewards tokens, and Windows Phone gift cards can all be redeemed within the Windows Store and attached to a child‚Äôs account. 

 

 

Once you‚Äôve purchased the gift card from a retailer, you can use it to purchase the app for the child by following these steps:

 

1.      Have the child sign into Windows 8.1 with their Microsoft account and open Windows Store.

2.      Open the Settings charm and select Your account.

3.     Type in the Gift Card 5x5 code into the Redeem code window.   Click or touch Redeem.

4.     Go back into the Store catalogue, search for and purchase the app.  Assuming you have enough value left in the card, the app will install automatically.
