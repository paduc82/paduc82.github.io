victorperezcmg say: Hi,

Thanks for contacting us

when you configure your email account in your mobile device using Exchange mode or IMAP, it will sync the read/unread status across all devices. We can only manually mark the emails so it will show as an unread email the next time you open your account on your Outlook client.

As a manual workaround for built in mail app for iOS is to:

- Manually mark emails (tap hold an email), it will display a blue check/mark on the left side, then press the Envelope at the bottom panel (2nd from the left) to mark these selected mails as “Mark as Unread”

- Or In the INBOX, Click the Edit button on the top right corner and manually select the mails you want to Mark, click Mark on the bottom panel, then choose Mark as Unread.

As a manual workaround for OWA for iOS is to:

- Manually mark emails (tap hold an email), it will display a check box on the left side, then press the Envelope at the bottom panel to mark these selected mails as “Mark as Unread”

- Or set a “Quick actions” to swipe the email you want to mark as Unread. To set the Quick action got the “Gear” icon > Quick actions > now set the action to “Mark as read or unread” to either swipe right or left.

As a manual workaround for built in mail app for Android is to:

- Manually mark emails, tap hold an email to show the options, on the top left select the “envelope” icon to mark the email as read/unread.

Let Try something:

Open the default Android email app. Select the option to Add a new account, or if it’s your first time using the app, you’ll be prompted to set up an email right away.

Incoming server settings:

POP3 server: pop3.live.com
Security type: SSL
Port: 995
Delete Email from server: This setting is optional, set whether you want emails deleted only from your phone or from the entire account (server).
Outgoing server settings

SMTP server: smtp.live.com
Security type: TLS
Port: 587
Require sign-in: Yes

Next configure how often the email app will check for new messages. I suggest setting this at either two or four hours, if you need to check mail more often you can manually refresh. when the email frequency is set to a quick interval it drains a lot of battery from the phone.

The last thing to do is to give the account a local nickname. This is is only seen by you on your phone. And then add your name in the second box, and note this name may be seen by the people you email.
