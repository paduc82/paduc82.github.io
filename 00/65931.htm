play slide show on TV - Microsoft Community

jack-c say:

play slide show on TV

I need to play a slide show for a reception in my home. I can put the slide show on a laptop but I am wondering if there is any way I can connect the laptop to a large TV and have the slide show viewed on the TV?

Thanks for any advice.

Jack

jack-c say:

I got no replies, and I suppose I should not have posted this question on this site. Maybe it isn't really something the group deals with. So I apologize. But I eventually figured it out, so I will post the reply to my own question:

First of all, my laptop did not have an HDMI outlet, so I had to get a VGA to HDMI converter.  And, of course, an HDMI cable.

You may have a more modern laptop with an HDMI port, so,if that is the case, all you would need is the HDMI cable.

In my situation, I started by connecting the HDMI+VGA connector to the laptop VGA port, and the other end  to the TV, making note of the particular HDMI input number you are using (it was input 4 on my TV). My VGA to HDMI converter also came with a USB connector, which I connected to a USB port, but I am not sure it was necessary. Didn't hurt to connect it.

You may or may not see the laptop desktop on the TV now.

Now you have to "extend" the display on your laptop. Right click on desktop, choose screen resolution, identify the screens and choose to extend the display.

Now I had to change the resolution on my laptop to a lower than the recommended setting for the laptop. I have forgotten the exact resolution but it was a setting or two below the highest one.  After resetting the resolution on the laptop, that was the first time I actually saw what was on the laptop screen projected onto the TV.

I didn't get any sound, but I didn't need it.

One last point, which probably just reinforces my lack of experience.

I wanted to do my slide show from powerpoint. Powerpoint 2010 did not like the photograph images I created. They were apparently too large. I had to resize them down to about 280 kb. I had 130 images, so that would have been an enormous job, except that I downloaded Irfanview, and there is a batch converter within that program. There are directions on the internet about how to do it. The only slightly tricky part is the "advanced" tab to fill out before starting the batch. I will try to attach an image of that screen.

Also one more mistake I made with my powerpoint slide show was to confuse timing the slides with "rehearse timings". Rehearse timings does not set the timing for the slides. You do that under "transitions"
