gza say:

Looks like that is a WISP ("wireless ISP"), which means you do not need a modem but also means you are likely behind two NAT routers (your Belkin and a router at the WISP).  If so, that creates a problematic double NAT situation that prevents you from getting open NAT unless you take special steps--steps that you probably can't take because they require control over both routers, and you have no control over the WISP's router.

To verify double NAT is the issue, please log in to the web interface of your Belkin router and note the WAN/Internet IP it reports (WAN IP, not the LAN IP!).  If that IP differs from the one reported by http://www.tracemyip.org or similar websites, then you have a double NAT situation.  Again, there is probably not much you can do if so. You can try connecting the Xbox One directly to the ethernet cable from your antenna and hope that your WISP has UPnP enabled on whatever NAT router it is running, which is very unlikely.  Otherwise, your options for getting open NAT are to (1) convince the WISP to forward the necessary ports for you (unlikely for various reasons) or enable UPnP (good luck), or (2) get a cable/DSL ISP that provides you a public IP.
