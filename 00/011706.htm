I RE RECEIVE ALL MAIL IN MY INBOX.


This and the statement you made, "it will resend all the emails in my inbox again", are no where near the same thing.  One talks about sending and the other about receiving.  Those are completely opposite actions.  Please don't confuse the issue by using inaccurate descriptions.

If you are receiving the same messages each time you perform a send/receive, then you have something interfering with the send/receive process and either Outlook or your server are not receiving acknowledgement that the send/receive process has completed properly so that OOutlook and the server don't agree on what's new and what has been received.  The usual cause is an add-in like an AV program scanning mail, but there could be other causes.

Once thing you can do immediately to prevent the old mail from downloading each time is to get it out of the server-side Inbox.  Log into each of your accounts (whichever of them contains the redownloading messages) using a web browser, create another server-side folder, and move to that folder all of the old messages still in the server-side Inbox.  If the messages aren't in the Inbox, Outlook can't see them and won't download them.  Now see if new messages you receive behave properly.

You can also enable diagnostic logging (see http://support.microsoft.com/kb/831053) and review the receive portion of the send/receive cycle to see what transpires between Outlook and the server.  It may lead to what's missing in the transaction.

The problem you were facing was this:

So your PC and device may have different versions of your email queue depending on what their servers or the device has loaded.
