How to consecutively number rows in tables - Microsoft Community

walter seaton say:

How to consecutively number rows in tables

I create many tables the lines of which must be numbered consecutively. The tables are subject to having lines added in the middle, requiring renumbering of all the lines below. In Excel this is easy - I give the first cell in the first line (A1) the value "1", enter [=A1+1] in A2 and then fill down - I can have a thousand consecutively numbered lines in a second. How can I do this in a table in MS Word 2003 (my OS is Windows 7 Professional). Thank you.
doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Select the column where you want the numbers (or the cells in that column where you want the numbers - if you have a header row that should not be numbered) and click on the Numbering button on the Formatting Toolbar

If you do not have the Formatting toolbar display, go to View>Toolbars and click on the Formatting item.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
Instead of using the Numbering button on the Formatting toolbar, use Format | Bullets and Numbering... In the Bullets and Numbering dialog, select the first pane (which shows 1., 2., 3.) and click Customize... In the Customize Numbered List dialog, under "Number format," delete the period, then click OK. You may also want to change the indents and other formatting through this dialog.
