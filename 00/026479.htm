      david in sydney say:


      Perhaps I have misunderstood your situation but this might be an alternative.

      Any user can have virtually any number of notebooks open on their own machine in OneNote.

      You could create a new notebook called something like "Common Pages" in a location accessible by everyone on your team, possibly OneDrive. Or perhaps on a shared network.

      If you are going to use OneDrive, best to start the new notebook there from the outset. Or if you do start in some other location, then right click on the notebook name and use Share This Notebook to relocate it.

      Move all of the pages that you want to share, into that new notebook.

      Then share that notebook with everyone on your team so that they each opened it on their own version of OneNote.

      Everyone will then be working on the same pages in the same notebook and with all the advantages of being able to seewho has done what (have a look at the History tab on the ribbon, to see the built-in "review" functionality).

      Hope that helps.
