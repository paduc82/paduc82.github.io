Conditional formatting to highlight if its over a year. - Microsoft Community

ralphmahon say:

Conditional formatting to highlight if its over a year.

Hi all,

 

I would like some help with conditional formatting so it highlights dates that are over a year old and beyond (some could be over 5 years old) and for them to change colour.

 

I would also like to highlight (change colour) the dates 1 month before they become over due (a year old) is this possible?

 

Thank you.

hansv mvp say:

An alternative:

Select the cells.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > Manage Rules...

 

Click New Rule...

Select Format only cells that contain.

Leave the first dropdown set to Cell Value.

Select less than or equal to from the second dropdown.

Enter the formula  =EDATE(TODAY(),-11)  in the box next to it.

Click Format...

Specify the desired formatting, then click OK twice.

This rule will highlight dates 11 months before today or older.

 

Repeat the above steps, but with  =EDATE(TODAY(),-12)   and different formatting.

This rule will highlight dates 12 months (1 year) before today or older. It will override the first rule if applicable.

 

Repeat the above steps, but with =EDATE(TODAY(),-24) and different formatting.

This rule will highlight dates 24 months (2 years) before today or older. It will override the first two rules if applicable.

 

Click OK.

 

Variations are possible, of course.

hansv mvp say:

The method I described will only highlight the cell with the value, but you can modify it as follows to highlight multiple cells within the same row.

 

Start by removing the existing rules, it's easier to start from scratch.

 

Select the cells in columns A:N that you want to format conditionally. In the following, I will assume that the active cell within the selection is in row 2.

 

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > Manage Rules...

 

Click New Rule...

Select Use a formula to determine which cells to format.

Leave the first dropdown set to Cell Value.

Select less than or equal to from the second dropdown.

Enter the formula  =$J2<=EDATE(TODAY(),-11)  in the box next to it.

Click Format...

Specify the desired formatting, then click OK twice.

This rule will highlight rows with a date in column J that is 11 months or more before today.

 

Repeat the above steps, but with  =$J2<=EDATE(TODAY(),-12)   and different formatting.

This rule will highlight rows with a date in column J that is 12 months (1 year) or more before today. It will override the first rule if applicable.

 

Repeat the above steps, but with =$J2<=EDATE(TODAY(),-24) and different formatting.

This rule will highlight rows with a date in column J that is 24 months (2 years) or more before today. It will override the first two rules if applicable.

 

Click OK.
