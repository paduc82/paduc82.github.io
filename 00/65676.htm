Hi Marsique,

 

You may try resetting the router, delete network connections from Network and Sharing center and restart the router.

Step 1:

Reset the router

Resetting your modem or router can help to resolve issues. To reset your modem or router, follow these steps:

a. Disconnect the cable that connects the computer to the modem.

b. Turn off the modem and the router.

Note If the modem or the router does not have a power switch, disconnect the power to the modem or to the router.

c. Restart the computer.

d. After your computer has restarted, turn on the modem or the router, connect the cable from the computer to the modem or the router, and restart the computer.

e. Make sure that all network cables are securely connected.

Note: If you are not comfortable in doing so please contact the router manufacturer for more information on resetting the router.

 

Step 2:

Delete the network connections.

a. Click Start, type ncpa.cpl, and then hit Enter. 
b. Right-click your wireless network connection, and then click Properties.
c. Click the Wireless Networks tab.
d. Under Preferred networks, click your wireless network, and then click Remove.
e. Click View Wireless Networks.
f. Under Network Tasks, click Refresh Network List.

g. Under Choose a wireless network, click the wireless network to which you want to connect, and then click Connect.
You may also refer the manufacturers website and update the network adapter drivers.

hairyfool say:

Are you logging in with a Local account or your Microsoft Account? Because the latter gives direct access to Microsoft services on line including the ability to make financial transactions Microsoft (and most other on line services) require complex password to reduce the risk of an account being hacked.

"Like before" would indicate a local account, the only option for XP/Vista/7 where you are only giving access to the local PC.

Go to Settings, Accounts. With "Your info" selected on the left do you have an option to "switch to a local account"?

If so then you are logging in on-line with the complex password requirements it enforces. When you set the computer up there was an option to use a local account from the start but the initial dialogues hid it fairly well

Apart from using a PIN the only option is to make that change where you can elect to use the password you want (even none). It will generate a warning about having access to on-line services.

If you make that change and then try to connect to a Microsoft service for the 1st time READ THE DIALOGUE CAREFULLY. Some services will by default take you through turning on the Microsoft Account login again and the option to just log in can be a bit obscure.
