zacabeb say:

When playing back DVD Video or videos from Xbox Video, the Xbox 360 wants to encrypt the picture and sound with HDCP. If it cannot properly detect that the display can handle HDCP, or if the display is an older model which does not support it, then the Xbox 360 will show this message. I'm surprised it doesn't happen with Netflix.

A possibility is that there is damage to the HDMI cable or connectors interrupting the Xbox 360 from communicating properly with the display, leaving them unable to share keys for HDCP encryption (this is done continuously while HDCP remains active).

Make sure that you turn on the display first and let it fully "wake up" before starting up the Xbox 360.

Also, if you're using the original yellow/red/white AV cable for sound rather than an audio dongle, check if leaving the AV cable unplugged and only having HDMI plugged in will resolve the issue. If it does, then you might want to route the sound out of your display to the sound bar rather than directly from the Xbox 360.

What brand/model display is the Xbox 360 connected to? Also, has it worked before on that display?

It could be the way the ID pins are wired in the AV cables to idenfity the type. Third party cables can get this wrong (switching to S-video or SCART mode), and since the Xbox 360 expects only an audio dongle to be connected at the same time as HDMI, it may get confused.

I'm glad it works now.

Check though if your TV retains communication over HDMI with the Xbox 360 when switching inputs - if it doesn't, the Xbox 360 will reboot to output analog video over the AV cable instead. It's better to know in advance if this will happen than to discover it mid-game if temporarily switching over to another input. :)

zacabeb say:

You can solve that problem if you get a used Microsoft AV adapter, or one of the third party brand AV adapters that have been confirmed to work correctly (I don't know off the top of my head which ones do).

The AV adapter is wired to ID as an audio-only connection, and the Xbox 360 will simply wait to re-establish the HDMI link with the TV if it's lost.

Beware though that many third party AV adapters ID as a composite cable, and have the same problem as the regular AV cable, confusing the Xbox 360 to reboot and output analog video.
