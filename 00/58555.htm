how do i overlap pictures to show within my webcam video/music - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

how do i overlap pictures to show within my webcam video/music

I recorded a video with music and would like to superimpose pictures while the music plays, cancelling the video part
considerate_guy say:

Have you checked if your system meets the requirements for Movie Maker 2012?  If so, you could update via the link in the prior post.

 

Another option is to convert your webcam video into an audio-only file and then use it as "music".  The quickest way is to use a 3rd party conversion utility. I've used Freemake Video Converter to take a video file and save it in .mp3 format.  Then I've used that .mp3 as the "music".  You could try that or do research on other conversion options. If you do install Freemake, just be aware that it is freeware and as is pretty typical with freeware, will offer to load extra add-ons.  I always choose not to install these and install just the program.

considerate_guy say:

Is the music part of the video, or is it a separate music file?  Also, which version of Movie Maker are you using?

 

If the music isn't part of the video, you would make a split in your video (using the 'M' key on the keyboard or the 'Split' button on the 'Video Tools | Edit' tab).  Then put your picture(s) in between.  You would then have the music playing along then the photos and then back to the video.

 

On the other hand, if the music is part of the audio inside the original video, it's a little more complicated.  The first thing you need to do is to treat the audio as a separate track.  Assuming you are on the latest version of Movie Maker 2012, you would add a second copy of your video as the music.  The latest version will let you add a video file as the added music, where the prior version will only let you add music/audio files.

 

If you have done this correctly you'll have your video, plus a second copy of its audio as the "music" track.  But now you'll hear the music twice.  So go to the video and mute it (Video Tools | Edit > Video Volume, set it to zero).

 

Okay, now you can remove portions of the video and replace them with the equivalent amount of photos.  For example, let's say you wanted to add 4 photos for 7 seconds each.  That would mean you would need to split out 28 seconds of video, delete it and replace it with the 4 photos.  At the end, the video would still be at the right point to synch back up with the original audio.

 

This can be a little tricky, especially if you plan to add some transitions between the items because it makes it harder to then calculate the amount of video to remove.  In this case, you might want to use the audio waveform view in Movie Maker 2012 so you can match the waveform from the video back to the waveform from the added audio.

 

Anyway, good luck and I hope this helped.
