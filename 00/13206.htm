jllatham say:

Excel can't detect things being typed into cells, so it has no way of really knowing when you've typed anything into one until you press the [Enter] or [Tab] key, use one of the arrow keys or click on another cell with the mouse.

This isn't the first time such a request has been made - you might look at this one discussion and see if any of the offered "work-arounds" might do the job for you.  http://www.mrexcel.com/forum/showthread.php?76114-Can-Excel-be-made-to-auto-advance-to-the-next-cell

You might also be set up a custom number format for the cell which would let them enter the 11 digits without entering the dashes but have it appear with them after they move to the next cell.

Select the cell, choose [Format] then the "Format Cells" option and when you get to the dialog, choose Custom and enter this format

00-000000-000

and [OK] out of the dialog.

If you then type in 12345678987 (and move out of the cell), it will appear as

12-345678-987

(but internally it still remains as 12345678987).

Does that help any?
