Private Sub Worksheet_BeforeDoubleClick(ByVal Target As Range, Cancel As Boolean)
    If Not Intersect(Target, Range("Apples")) Is Nothing Then _

Double clicking any cell in the named range Apples will result in a counter one cell to the right raising by an increment of 1.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

The behavior for Copy is different from the behavior of Cut...

  • When you Paste after Copy the marquee remains in place as a reminder that the copied content is still available to be pasted again. If you find the marquee to be a distraction, press esc to clear the clipboard - the marquee will be removed & the content can't be pasted again.
  • When you Paste after Cut the marquee is removed because the content can be pasted only once & does not remain on the clipboard.

Your second issue sounds like you're selecting the Insert Copied/Cut Cells command rather than the Insert command, itself, but that isn't your fault :-)

After Copying (or Cutting), Insert> Cells in the main menu becomes Insert> Copied Cells (or Insert Cut Cells) & the Insert button on the Ribbon assumes the same default. It remains that as long as the cut/copied content is on the clipboard.

If you want to insert a cell while there is content on the clipboard, right=click & choose the Insert command from the contextual menu or use the keyboard shortcut Shift+Command+➕ [If you have an extended keyboard you can use the ➕ on the Numeric Keypad without including the Shift key.
