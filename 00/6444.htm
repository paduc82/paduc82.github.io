steeladept01 say:

This basically says do the SUMPRODUCT if R3="".  If that is when you DON'T want it to do that, you need to put in another comma before the SUMPRODUCT and say what you want it to do if true.  So it should be something like:

=IF(R3="",<DO SOMETHING>,SUMPRODUCT(($H$3:H3=H3)*($J$3:J3=J3)*($P$3:P3=1)),SUMPRODUCT(($H$3:H3=H3)*($J$3:J3=J3)*($P$3:P3=1)*($Q$3:Q3>DATE(YEAR(Q3),MONTH(Q3)-6,DAY(Q3)))))

 

Where DO SOMETHING is however you want it to react when it reaches a blank.  So if you want it to return a '0', for example, it would be:

 

=IF(R3="", 0,SUMPRODUCT(($H$3:H3=H3)*($J$3:J3=J3)*($P$3:P3=1)),SUMPRODUCT(($H$3:H3=H3)*($J$3:J3=J3)*($P$3:P3=1)*($Q$3:Q3>DATE(YEAR(Q3),MONTH(Q3)-6,DAY(Q3)))))

 

If you just want it to stay blank, just replace the DO SOMETHING with "" again:

 

=IF(R3="","",SUMPRODUCT(($H$3:H3=H3)*($J$3:J3=J3)*($P$3:P3=1)),SUMPRODUCT(($H$3:H3=H3)*($J$3:J3=J3)*($P$3:P3=1)*($Q$3:Q3>DATE(YEAR(Q3),MONTH(Q3)-6,DAY(Q3)))))

Hi,

 

We really appreciate it that you are enjoying your new Outlook.com. You can import your contacts to Outlook.com by first exporting them to a file. Once you have created the CSV file, follow the steps below to import contacts on Outlook.com:

1.     Sign-in to your account at https://outlook.com

2.     Access your People by click on the drop-down arrow beside Outlook at the upper-left side of your screen, then click on People

3.     Under Add people to your contact list, click on Import from file

4.     Follow the steps that you will be given on the next page, then click on Import contacts

 

We hope this will be helpful for you and please let us know if you have any other concern.
