ynnhoj gnahz say:

Hi Paul,

 

Based on your description, I understand you want to separate your company accounts from an Office 365 Business Essentials tenant, but keep using the previous login credentials. Am I right?

1. Export the emails to PST files. You can use the eDiscovery search (account by account) and then export the results to PST files.

As you want to use the old login credentials, you need to remove the custom domain from the Office 365 Business Essentials tenant and verify it in your new Office 365 tenant. Here are the steps to add and verify a domain in Office 365:

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Add-users-and-domain-to-Office-365-6383f56d-3d09-4dcb-9b41-b5f5a5efd611

If you cannot remove the domain from the old tenant, Iâ€™m sorry to tell you that it is not feasible to use the old account credentials in the new tenant as custom domain can only be verified in one Office 365 tenant.

After the domain is verified and users are added in the new tenant, you can use the Import Service to migrate your emails to the accounts in the new tenant. Here are the detailed steps:

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Overview-of-importing-PST-files-and-SharePoint-data-to-Office-365-ba688e0a-0fcb-4bd7-8e57-2b669564ea84

2. Purchase a new 365 subscription from Microsoft, and then perform an IMAP migration to move the mailbox data. The mailboxes on the new subscription can use the default onmicrosoft.com temporarily.

After the migration, remove domain from the Essentials subscription, and verify the domain on the new subscription, and finally change the user mailboxes from onmicrosoft.com to the verified domain.
