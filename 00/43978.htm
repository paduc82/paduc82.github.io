  3. Select Advanced Options.
  4. Select Startup Repair and follow the on screen instructions.

  1. Try connecting to different video outputs on the PC. Sometimes a change in driver can cause the video to be sent:

    • To a different output: from HDMI to DVI, DisplayPort to VGA, or any other combination.

      2. If you see 1 item under Display Adapters, rollback the driver or uninstall the display adapter in Device Manager, and enable the option to delete driver software.

Check the PC or display adapter manufacturer’s site for an updated driver.

Windows 10 : How do I restore files from a backup? - Microsoft Community

barryoates1 say:

Windows 10 : How do I restore files from a backup?

Windows 10 : How do I restore files from a backup?

a. user say:

"Can anyone recommend a backup & restore program that actually works?"

Windows has only one supported folder-and-file backup utility, and that's File History. And while you can use it to backup large amounts of data, that's not what it was designed to do.

There's a ton of great backup software on the internet. For making folder-and-file backups have a look at Backup4All, although many people backup their data folders with a compression utility like 7-zip or PeaZip. For making backup images, Macrium Reflect is popular and so are Acronis TrueImage and EaseUS ToDo, among many other good choices.

Backup and Restore (Windows 7) was included in Windows 10 so that people who used it for backing up in Windows 7 can restore their backups in Windows 10. It's not supported for making new backups in Windows 10, so sometimes it works and sometimes it don't. System Image Builder was deprecated more than a year ago and Microsoft recommends people use a third-party backup image program. System Restore, of course, is not a backup.

If you're interested in the ultimate backup solution, move all your data folders (Documents, Music ... you know) to it's own partition. That way you can backup your software and your data separately. Now we're talking convenience!
