a. user say:

Hi,

Thank you for your interest in Windows 10 Technical Preview.

Run Windows Update Troubleshooter which fixes some problems with Windows Update, and it might resolve this issue.

a. On the Start search, type 'Find and fix problems' and choose that option.

b. Under 'Troubleshooting' window, click on 'System and Security'

c. Click on 'Windows update' and run the Windows update troubleshooter. Follow the on-screen instruction.

                                                                                                                                                                

If the issue persists,, I would have you run the DISM tool to check the system health and will try to restore the files. 

a. Click on 'Start'.

b. Enter 'Command prompt' in the Search box. 

c. In the list of results, swipe down on or right-click Command Prompt, and then tap or click 'Run as administrator'. 

d. In the Administrator: Command Prompt window, type the following commands. Press the Enter key aftereach command:

DISM.exe /Online /Cleanup-image /Scanhealth 

DISM.exe /Online /Cleanup-image /Restorehealth


e. To close the Administrator: Command Prompt window, type Exit, and then press Enter.

Note: The tool might take 15-20 minutes to finish running, so please do not cancel it.

Hope this helps. If you have further queries, we will be happy to help.

john korchok say:

The Master Slide is the larger one at the top when you choose View>Slide Master. It is the parent to the smaller Slide Layouts below it. Those Slide Layouts are the parent to the final slides in your presentation.

Choose a slide that shows an incorrect bullet. Then click on View>Slide Master. The highlighted slide layout in the new view is the one responsible for the slide. Does the text placeholder on that layout reflect the bullet formatting on the master? If not, correct it on the layout.

If the slide layout has the correct bullet, close the master view to return to the slide. Then click on Home>Reset to reapply the slide layout to the slide.

magovoru say: Connecting with Bluetooth

With a Bluetooth connection you can connect without cables; you can also pair your phone and your computer so that they connect automatically whenever they‚Äôre in range.

  1. To pair you computer with your phone, both of them need to have Bluetooth connectivity. To find out if your phone supports Bluetooth connection, go to the Find devices page. Under By key features, select Bluetooth to see all Nokia phones that have Bluetooth connectivity.
    Alternatively, you can check your phone‚Äôs User Guide to find out if the phone supports Bluetooth connections.
  2. Check your computer‚Äôs Bluetooth settings to make sure Bluetooth is turned on and your computer is discoverable.
  3. Make sure your phone has Bluetooth turned on and that it is visible to other Bluetooth devices.
  4. Select Bluetooth as the connection type when running Nokia Suite for the first time, or when adding a new phone via Tools > Add new device.
  5. Select your phone when it shows up in the list, and click Next.
  6. Enter the given passcode on your phone when prompted to pair your phone and your computer.
    On certain newer phones you only need to check that the passcodes match in Nokia Suite and on your phone.
  7. On your phone, accept any connection requests from your computer.
  8. Set your computer as authorised in your phone‚Äôs Bluetooth settings to allow your phone to be automatically connected when in range.
