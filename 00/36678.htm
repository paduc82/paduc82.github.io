At one point, HP sold printers with a "smart install" capability that involved a small program in the printer's firmware that "phoned home" to the HP servers, automatically downloaded the correct software for your printer and your version of Windows, and installed the printer.

As I understand it, when you first connected a printer with this feature, the printer was detected as a mass storage device and when you looked in "Computer," you would see an HP disk.  When you double-clicked on that disk, the installation would start.

Unfortunately, HP discontinued that program and disconnected the servers to which such printers connected.

I suggest the following:

1.  Disconnect the printer.

  • Press the Windows Logo key and E simultaneously and confirm that there is no "HP" disk.
  • Open Devices and printers.  If there is an icon for your Laserjet 1007, right-click and select "Remove device. 

2.  Go here and download the "HP LaserJet P1000-P1500 Hostbased Plug and Play Basic Driver" for Windows 7 x64 -- https://support.hp.com/us-en/drivers/selfservice/swdetails/hp-laserjet-p1007-printer/3435678/swItemId/bi-55362-7

3.  Install the printer using these instructions:

  • Close any software programs running on your computer.
  • Double click on the downloaded file. The self-extracting process will appear.
  • Click to accept the terms in the license agreement. Click Next.
  • Driver installation begins. Driver files are pre-installed during this process.
  • A screen pops up to prompt to connect the USB cable. Insert the USB cable.
  • Device is connected, waiting for PnP to finish installing driver.
  • Click Finish to complete software installation.
