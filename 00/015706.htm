Telecoil works more or less with any phone. There is a Microphone/Telecoil toggle on the hearing aid or its remote control, if the hearing aid is equipped with telecoil.

Notice: Not all hearing aids have a telecoil mode.

- A wired headset or a Bluetooth neckloop can improve the audio quality if needed.

The HAC list is about the audio quality in Microphone and Telecoil mode, and the immunity to interference (buzzing or acoustic feedback) as required in the US.

Notice: Phones without HAC rating may also work fine with a hearing aid.

- To ensure that a particular hearing aid works well with a particular phone, use them together before making a purchase.

Check with your audiologist or hearing-aid manufacturer for more information.

The technical details are explained on

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearing_aid#Compatibility_with_telephones

And some practical info and considerations at

http://www.nchearingloss.org/telecoil.htm

Hearing Aid mode in iPhone modifies the phones acoustic settings to improve compatibility and/or reduces the transmission power in the GSM 1900 MHz (2G) band, when hearing aids are set to telecoil mode.
