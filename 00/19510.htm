jay freedman say:

Documents that use content controls should not also have forms protection -- that protection is meant for use with legacy forms controls, not content controls. If you have both content controls and legacy forms controls, it's recommended to reconstruct the form with only one type of control.

To provide the equivalent of forms protection to a document that has content controls, select the whole document (or whatever portion needs protection), click the Group button in the Developer tab, and click Group on the menu. Editing will be allowed only inside the content controls or in areas that aren't grouped; if any editing or formatting is attempted elsewhere, the status bar will say that "the selection is locked".

It's not on your system Andrew, as Dave already mentioned it's a page redirection or more simply, a malvertising (malicious advertising) item being sent to your PC through the MSN start-up pages by some advertising network that's not fully vetting their advertisements.

The easiest way to stop these is simply to stop using MSN at startup of Edge, but of course that doesn't allow you to use it for news and really doesn't solve the problem, since many other major websites are being attacked by such malvertising as well, so it's just a matter of which are seeing more of these at a given moment.

Another way to try and stop them is installing an ad blocker application, though at times the malvertisers have found ways around these as well, but it is a more general way to try and stop all ads that tends to reduce the number of malvertisements you see as a side effect.

The way I use that's simpler and seems to work in my case, since I never see any of these, is to change the Windows 10 privacy setting "Let apps use my advertising ID..." to Off.  Then in Edge itself, change the setting to "Send Do Not Track requests..." to enabled, which has the side effect of MSN blocking most ads or at least reduces the advertisers ability to track you greatly.  Also, in Edge, find the setting related to Cookies and set it to block 3rd-party (tracking) cookies, since these are used for tracking as well.

What these settings in the previous paragraph actually do is reduce the ability for the advertising networks to track your browsing and other usage patterns, making it difficult for them to collect and collate information about you across multiple users sessions, which is what they use in order to choose their targets to display those popup ads.  It may not stop the popups completely, but should reduce them if you've already been chosen as a target.

However, the side effect of disabling tracking is that any ads that are displayed will tend to be random and not the sometimes relevant ads based on your browsing habits that you may be seeing now.  This is because the malvertisers use the exact same abilities as otherwise reputable advertisers to target you for their ads, but misuse the ads to display the fake popups instead.

The choice then is between not using websites currently displaying these malvertising popups, using an ad blocker to block the popups and other ads when their received or using the available Microsoft settings to try and stop all advertisers ability to track your browsing habits altogether.

Good luck with whatever choice you make, they're all tradeoffs in order to work around malicious advertisers who've chosen you for some reason as their target.  Most likely because you read the same sorts of articles that others the malvertisers have managed to convince to accept their scam in the past often read as well.

jason (office engineer) say:

Sway has two different concepts here that look very similar:  groups and sections.  Groups can be made by selecting multiple pieces of content and grouping them and you have the option to select various group types; you also have a button that allows you to ungroup them.  Sections, however, are tied to the presence of a Heading card.  When you add a Heading, everything underneath that card, up until the next Heading, is considered part of a single section.  The only way to remove a section is to remove the Heading card and, if you do that, the content will become a part of the section for the closest Heading above it.

If you do not wish to use sections, you need to remove the Heading cards.  However, if you merely want to separate content that is in a section into another section, try adding another Heading card and just don't type anything into that Heading.  That will break up the section without new content showing in your Sway.

Hope that helps!
