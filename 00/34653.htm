lemp say:
Because some moderator has split your question from another thread, it's difficult to determine what "reply" you mean.  In the thread linked at the bottom of your post, you wrote that you were using a utility from Doug Knox's site and that your clock was keeping correct time.  Is that not the case?

The problem you have is that in your system, Windows is running in a virtual machine (Parallels).  In this case, the usual response to incorrect clock time (i.e., replace your CMOS battery) doesn't apply because the virtual machine doesn't rely on the CMOS clock in the same way that a non-virtual operating system does.

The following paper is written about VMware -- not Parallels -- but if you read the first two pages of text (pages 3-4), you'll get an idea of what the cause of your problem is likely to be:  http://www.vmware.com/files/pdf/Timekeeping-In-VirtualMachines.pdf

If you want a more definitive answer, I suggest that you ask in the Parallels forum.  Be sure to provide the versions of all software (e.g., your Mac OS, Windows XP, and Parallels)

[Edit]

In a quick look in the Parallels forums, I noticed that some versions of Parallels seem to have a problem keeping the Windows clock properly synchronized.  See http://forum.parallels.com/showthread.php?t=208314&highlight=time+clock
