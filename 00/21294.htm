andrea jones - all about resources say:

You can use an IF() function within your formula to deal with the SQRT when (POWER(C2,2)/POWER(186000,2))<1 as Excel will error as soon as it tries to find the square root of a negative number.

 

Here's an example using the calculation of 1/ γ where v may be greater than c (v is in cell A2 and c is in cell B2):

=IF((A2^2/B2^2)>1,SQRT(ABS(1-(A2^2/B2^2)))&"i",SQRT(1-(A2^2/B2^2)))

this will concatenate an 'i' onto the end of the result obtained from the square root of a negative number.
