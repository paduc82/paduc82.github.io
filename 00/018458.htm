This looks like it might work. What happens if you try?

The other point is that in a proper relational database you should not do this, because Fleet_Summary.Hour_Meter is a calculated field (meaning: it can be calculated by running some other query or code) and thus does not belong in the table. Something just caught my eye: if you want to update the [Fleet_Summary]![Hour Meter] field, the statement should end with:

SET [Fleet_Summary]![Hour Meter] = qryLastWorkOrder.[LastOfMachine Hours];

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

It doesn't matter how close or how far the person sending you mail is.  He or she uses a server just like you do and it's the servers that have to route mail between you and the sender.  Your husband's PC doesn't send mail directly to your PC.  It sends it to your husband's mail provider, which sendt it to your mail provider, and then your PC gets it from there.  Your mail provider's servers could be in Bangalore, India, for all we know (although that's not likely).

There is no option in Outlook to "display immediately upon receipt", so there's no way you can have such an option selected.  Do you have a send/receive interval defined?  While viewing your Inbox, press Alt+Ctrl+S.  You should see a "Send/Receive Groups" dialog.  Make sure the box labeled "Schedule an automatic send/receive every" is checked and specify an interval of about 10 minutes.  Click Close.  Outlook should then check for new mail with the interval you specified.
