john korchok say:

The registry entry is the way to get higher resolution. Here is Steve Rindsberg's page on the subject, along with a couple of alternate methods, including his add-in that will do the job for you: Improve PowerPoint's GIF, BMP, PNG, JPG export resolution

To get higher resolution with the Save As Picture option, increase the slide size and graphic size. Then open the resulting PNG in an image editor to reduce the image dimensions and thus increase the resolution.

john sr wilson say:
Changing the registry does not change the export resolution when you use Save as Picture for a shape / image. It will normally be 150 dpi and I don't think you can modify this.

steve rindsberg say:

To add a bit to what John+John said:

John W is correct, no surprise. Save As Picture for a shape/image doesn't respect the registry settings you can use to increase resolution when you Save As and choose PNG etc. to save the entire slide.

You can instead copy/paste your graphic onto a slide in a new presentation after first setting the slide size for the presentation to something proportional to the graphic itself, then size the graphic to fill the slide.  Then File | Save As and choose PNG.  That will respect the registry settings to give you higher resolution images.

Then use an image editing program to save the PNG again, this time with a transparent background.

If you don't already have such a program, Irfanview (www.irfanview.com) is a good one, will do the job and it's free.
