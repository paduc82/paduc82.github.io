PowerPoint - Premature Slide Transition

If I set the presentation to kiosk mode, which loops continuously, the 4th slide will always transition to the 1st slide after 5 minutes.  Which happens to be before the video ends, and before all the text animations are concluded.

Changing the delay on the slide transition has no effect.

Removing the video has no effect.

Deselecting and selecting kiosk mode turns this issue on and off.

I am using version 14 of MS PowerPoint.

How should I work around this issue?

amprod say:

Re-order the presentation so that the video does not play on the very last slide. This could be as simple as putting a black, fade to black slide after it. Then ensure that PP has instructions telling it how to advance away from the video slide (not back to the beginning, but to slide 5, or whatever lies after it). Do this by telling the Transition Menu to advance to the next slide in 6 minutes and 15.01 seconds.

The reason PP is freaking out: when browsed at kiosk, your entire presentation must be fully atonymous. If it is not, PP will rely on the keyboard and mouse for instructions. If it does not receive instructions from the user, PP will immediately refer back to a preset "factory" command. 

PP hasn't heard from you; the last thing you told it was to browse at kiosk mode. So when does it revert back to slide 1? It isn't getting an instruction from the mouse or keyboard, and the user told it to advance at "mouse click". But it has now been 5 minutes and it hasn't gotten that mouse click. If it is in kiosk, it MUST advance back to slide one. But when?

Luckily the program developer anticipated this and typed "5 minutes" into a line of code.

By automatically reverting to slide one after 5 minutes it is keeping itself from crashing :)

If I read your post wrong and you actually did give it such an instruction, you still have the same general problem, only PP just plain doesn't like going over 5 minutes for the last slide.

Work around this by again, re-ordering the presentation.

Hope this helps,

amprod

    PowerPivot / Data Modelling
    Power Pivot is a data modeling technology that lets you create data models, establish relationships, and create calculations. With Power Pivot you can work with large data sets, build extensive relationships, and create complex (or simple) calculations, all in a high-performance environment, and all within the familiar experience of Excel. In a nutshell, it allows you to use multiple data sources. Then you could import, merge and perform analysis on the resulting data.      Excel can â€œnativelyâ€ only store 1 million rows of data. You can distribute the result to other people without them needing to have access to the original source data.
