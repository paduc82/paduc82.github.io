steeledwand7664 say:

To add to the previous reply.

There are 2 ways for an account to be considered a child account.

1. The DOB on the account when it was originally setup. This is set in stone and would require an adult account attached to it in the family until it reaches its 18th birthday.

2. Putting an adult account into a family as a child account.

Now keep in mind with the second option the child account if they know the email and password for the account can go in and remove themselves from the family due to the fact that their DOB is over 18. 

With the first option in order for it to work with your situation you would need to create a whole new account for your son. With this option though any game progress and/or purchases go with the accounts themselves so if you create a new account for him he would lose any game progress and or purchases that were made on that account.

If games were purchased though you could set the console as that profiles home console and he could then still play those games on the new profile but he would still lose the progress in the game itself.

To add any account to a family as a child account you would need to sign into account.microsoft.com/family with a current adult account and add an account to the family as a child account.
