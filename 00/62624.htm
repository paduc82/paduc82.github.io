Wooh, it's been more than a year.  A test to my memory now, but it was some port forwarding problem with the router.

 

Basically, mine is a wireless printer the physical orientation is the printer connects to the router by WiFi, and my PC connects to the router through POE power plug.  The maker suggests my router to forward TCP 9100 to the wireless printer. 

 

It had been working alright for a pretty long time, and all out of a sudden it printed automatically with this threatening  "GET http://xx.qq.com/ HTTP/1.1" header.  I disabled the port forwarding, the auto-printing stops annoying me but still performs satisfactorily so long the printing was limited to the local network.

 

A few months later, I've got a firmware update.  Then, I resumed the port forwarding and I don't experience that annoying auto-printing any more for more than 9 months.

 

So, my suggestion is to look into the port forwarding matters both in your router and your PC if it is also a server.
