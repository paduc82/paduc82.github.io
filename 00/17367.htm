remove page numbers - Microsoft Community

janethomas(2) say:

remove page numbers

In Word 2016, I went to Insert > Page numbers, but Remove Page Numbers had an X on it, so I could not click on it.  How can I fix this?
charles kenyon say:

Thanks for your reply.  I am using Word 2016, and there is a red x on Remove Page numbers. When I click on it nothing happens--the page numbers are still there.  I am not using any headers or footers.

I have been able to remove page numbers in the past.  I don't know why I can't do it now.  Any other ideas?

The red X is part of the command; always displayed.

The command is working, but as I said, does not necessarily remove all page numbers. Page numbering is inextricably intertwined with Headers and Footers, which are a component of Sections. If you have running page numbers, you are using Headers and/or Footers. You may not have used any commands for headers or footers; you may not have intended to use headers or footers; but, if you use the command to insert page numbers at any location other than "current position" you insert a header or footer.

When the command to remove page numbers leaves one or more page numbers, you can remove page numbers in Print Layout by double-clicking in the header(footer) where the page number appears and deleting it. You may have to do this several times.

A page number may be in a header or in a footer. Each section of a document will have three different headers and footers (although not all of them may be active). These may or may not be linked to previous headers and footers of the same kind. A single page can have multiple sections, in which case you will not see many of the headers and footers that may be coded in the sections of that page.

In a simple document with only one section and without more complex headers and footers where page numbers were inserted using the Insert > Page Number command (the majority of Word documents) the "Remove Page Numbers" command removes all page numbers. If your document does not meet those conditions, it will not.
