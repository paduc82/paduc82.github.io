Finding a specific type of chart for a PPT presentation

The Chart is round and devided into slices like a pie chart, but displays data differently.  Instead of the sum of the slices adding to 100%, each slice displays a different measure and adds to 100%, kind of like a 100% stacked bar chart in each slice.  From what I've seen, it's a good way to get a large amount of data into one chart.

Thanks for your help!

adam vero say:

Anything that uses a circle as the basis for a chart will almost certainly use more space on screen/paper than a rectangular / linear format like a traditional stacked column chart. It will also mean that you are trying to visually compare things at different angles, which distorts segments aligned near vertical compared to those which are laid across the page.

If the layout also requires you to compare angles (like a pie chart) your brain has to do lots of work in an attempt to work out which are larger, whereas the length of a bar is something you can see "pre-attentively" using your highly evolved visual cortex to do most of the hard work rather than the mre analytical parts of the brain.

Typically you will also end up with a horrible arrangement of colours and a legend which requires you to tie up working memory going backwards and forwards to and from the chart t work out which slice means whatt. Your working memory has better things to do like remember what the meaning is, r what the speaker just said.

You may be looking for something like a multiple-ring doughnut chart, but frankly you would do yourself and your audience a disservice by using this rather than a more "boring" but much more accessible stacked column / bar.
