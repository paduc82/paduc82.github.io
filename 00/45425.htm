arya s asok say:

Hi Kjr,

 

You may open device manager and disk management on your computer and check if the external harddrive is detected in this windows.

 

Step 1

 

To open device manager, access the link below and follow the steps:

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Open-Device-Manager

 

To access disk management follow the steps below:

 

a.  Click on start.

b.  In the start search box type diskmgmt.msc and press Enter.

c.  In the disk management verify the drive connected to the computer and the partitions.

 

Step 2

 

If you are able to see the drive in the disk management window, you may make a note of the partition drive letter and try to run chkdsk utility and check if it helps.

 

To run chkdsk utility, follow the steps below:

a.    Click on Start.

b.   In the start search box type CMD.

c.    In the list right click CMD and click on Run as administrator.

d.   In the command prompt window type chkdsk /f /r D: (assuming D: is the drive letter for the external drive)and press enter.

e.    Chkdsk will run after the restart.

 

Once the chkdsk completes verify if the files are visible.

 

 

Note: if the chkdsk utility states that there are irrecoverable errors on the external drive, you may have to replace the drive and also you may loose the data. Make sure you make a backup of the files once its visible.

 

If the above steps does not help and if the harddrive is not detected at all in the disk management and device manager, you will have to send it back to Seagate for data recovery and replacement.
