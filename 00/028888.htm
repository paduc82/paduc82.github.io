bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
Like John, I'm not sure what you mean, but I interpret your question differently...

Perhaps I'm misunderstanding, but it seems to me that you're noticing that other cross-references do not automatically update when you create a new cross-reference. That is how they work by design... The new cross-reference will be correct, but existing cross-reference fields do not update in the manner of Footnotes, Endnotes & Page Numbers.

For any particular cross-reference you can click in it & use the F9 keystroke to update it [if your OS X setup hasn't stolen the keystroke.] You may have to use fn+F9 on a laptop or change the setting in Expose/Spaces to reclaim the F9 key. As an alternative, right-click the cross-reference & select Update Field from the shortcut menu.

If you want to update all fields, use Command+A to select all before doing the above. You can also switch to Draft or Outline view then back to Print Layout view to refresh the fields.

You may also want to enable Update Fields in Word> Preferences> Print so that fields will be sure to update any time you print the document.
