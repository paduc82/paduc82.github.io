Word 2010 Equation Editor - How Do I Create my own Operator? - Microsoft Community

dynamicdoyle say:

Word 2010 Equation Editor - How Do I Create my own Operator?

I'd like an operator that has an equation symbol and an open field above the equation sign, so I can have an = with a d on the top or any symbol I want. How do I go about doing this?
jay freedman say:
This may not be the "official" method, but it works...

  • Insert an equation container (Alt+=).
  • On the Equation Tools ribbon, click the Operator button and choose one of the operator structures that has an entry box above an arrow.
  • Click the down arrow at the right end of the equation container and choose Linear from the menu. The display changes to a square followed by parentheses containing the arrow and an inverted-T character.
  • Replace the arrow character with an equal sign character.
  • Again click the down arrow and choose Professional. You now have an equal sign with an entry box above it.
  • Carefully select the equal sign and the entry box, but not the entire equation container.
  • Click the little arrow in the bottom right corner of the Tools group on the Equation Tools ribbon, to open a dialog. In the dialog, click the Math AutoCorrect button.
  • In the Replace box of the AutoCorrect dialog, type an abbreviation that will be replaced by the new symbol. The convention is to start with a backslash, so something like \eqbox would be appropriate. Click OK in each dialog.


To use the symbol, start entering your equation; at the place you want the symbol, type your abbreviation and then press the spacebar
