gavinbollard say:

OneDrive is really a "technology" rather than a location. 

You'll find that there are two parts to OneDrive;

  • Your Personal OneDrive(s)
  • Your SharePoint OneDrive(s)

Anything that is yours only, goes on your Personal (Corporate) OneDrive

  • You can have a personal OneDrive for your Office365 account, which is like having a HOME drive.
  • You can also have a personal OneDrive for Hotmail/Outlook.com which is like having a DROPBOX or GOOGLE DRIVE. 

Anything that is intended for a Group of People should go on SharePoint.

  • If it's for everyone in your organisation, then a PUBLIC SharePoint group is the way to go
  • If it's for external people as well, you'll need to make a PRIVATE SharePoint group.

You'll find that your users can SYNC the SharePoint document libraries to their computers using OneDrive.

If you have something that needs to be shared with a very Limited number of people, for example, your GOALS might be shared with your boss and your HR manager, then this can go on your Personal (Corporate) OneDrive.

I hope this helps.
