abi99 (2) say:

Smart Dual SIM is a feature introduced with the MediaTek based phones like the Nokia 220 Dual-SIM and Nokia 225 Dual-SIM. It allows you to create call-forwarding rules from SIM1 to SIM2, or from SIM2 to SIM1, or both.

 

That is standard GSM call-forwarding and call waiting services. According to my own tests, this does not happen on a per-call basis but statically once. It got called ‘smart’ because the phone tries to read your numbers from your SIM (might fail) and offers a one button click for that. Furthermore, because this are standard GSM services:

  • it does not work if one of your SIMs does not offer these services
  • you are able to do the same with a Nokia 208 manually.

Creating such a call-forwarding reduces the issues involved with a Dual-Standby handset because with this no number is blocked when one number is active. On the opposite, with a Dual Active phone, both SIM are registered all times and therefore directly available.

 

Consequently, both Nokia 20x and Nokia 22x are Dual-Standby. The Nokia Series 40 and therefore the Nokia 20x offers switching of SIM2 without removing the battery: Nokia calls that ‘Easy Swap’. The Nokia 22x does not allow that.

 

Did that answer your question? As explained before, please, consider the Nokia 208!
