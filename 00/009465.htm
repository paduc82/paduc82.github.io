As a workaround, you can use the following macro. (See http://www.gmayor.com/installing_macro.htm if needed.) The columns aren't formatted properly, but the numbers will be the same.

Sub ShowReadabilityStats()
    Dim rs As ReadabilityStatistic
    Dim msg As String
    With ActiveDocument
        For Each rs In .ReadabilityStatistics
            msg = msg & rs.Name & vbTab & rs.Value & vbCr
        Next rs
        msg = Left(msg, Len(msg) - 1)
    End With
    MsgBox msg, , "Readability Statistics"
End Sub

I finally got it fixed. For those who have the same issue and google sends them over here...

I did a combination of things suggested on
https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/all/cannot-delete-folders-or-files-on-my-pc-in/7c38c729-5d61-4274-93b5-0dacade5c029

Take notice of 'Mike P' & 'QuestionTime1410' comments.

I distilled it to:
1. In the OneDrive settings, on the Settings Tab, unchecked the 'Files on Demand' option.
2. Make a new folder in your OneDrive
3. Copy your files in that folder
4. Wait for a full sync on all your devices
5. Delete the old folder with the move technique
6. Wait for a full sync on all your devices
7. Rename the folder to its original name

Note: I had issues with point 3 with 1 root folder in my OneDrive. When checking the size (explorer, Qdir, WinDirStat) it was about 50GB.
