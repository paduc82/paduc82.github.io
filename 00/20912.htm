    Missing customizations in Office Word for Mac after installing 2016 - Microsoft Community

    Missing customizations in Office Word for Mac after installing 2016 version

    rich michaels - mvp (greatcirclelearning.com) say:

    Assuming you still have your Office 2011 directories on your computer... in theory you should be able to go here:

    /Users/<UserName>/Library/Application Support/Microsoft/Office/User Templates and copy the Normal.dotm template located there and paste it into this location that Office 2016 uses:

    /Users/<UserName>/Library/Group Containers/UBF8T346G9.Office/User Content/Templates

    However, during the beta release of Office 2016 I experienced problems trying to use the Normal template from Word 2011 with Word 2016. So I went about the transferring of Styles and AutoText the long way. Here is the "long way" process:

    1. Open Finder and navigate to your Word 2011 Normal.dotm template, make a copy of it and paste it onto your Desktop
    2. Rename this copied version of your Normal template... call it Transfer.dotm
    3. Open Word 2016 to a new blank document (the name will be Document1)
    4. From the menu bar choose Tools > Templates & Add-ins
    5. Click the "Attach" button and navigate to your Desktop and attach Transfer.dotm to Document1.
    6. After attaching the template click the Organizer button, which is located in the bottom left corner of the open dialog.
    7. Document1 is shown on the left of the Organizer dialog and there are controls along the top for Styles, AutoText, etc
    8. Use the pull down menu located under the label "Styles available in" and set it to reference the Transfer.dotm template that is attached to Document1. Note: you will do this for each category (Styles, AutoText, Macros) you are copying.
    9. Now use the copy controls to transfer your custom Styles, AutoText and

      Hope this helps
