scott mcdaniel say:

That would be expected. Your Main form would be based on the "parent" table, and your subform would be based on the "child" table. Just as in real life, you cannot have a "child" without a "parent" :)

When you save your subform Record, Access will always save the mainform record. You cannot get around that (nor should you).

In your case, it would seem that you'd have a form based on the Transaction table, with Combos based on the Inventory and Employee table. For each Transaction record, the user would select the Inventory and Customer from the combos, and then fill in the other data. You would have a single Transaction record if you did that.

However, we'd need to know more about your business needs before we could determine if this is the best way to handle your data.

Sub Test()
    Dim i As Long
    Dim a As Long
    Dim orgValue As String
    Dim newValue As String
    Application.ScreenUpdating = False
    Application.Calculation = xlCalculationManual
    Application.EnableEvents = False
    a = Cells(Rows.Count, 8).End(xlUp).Row
    For i = 2 To a
        orgValue = Cells(i, 8).Value
        newValue = Left(orgValue, InStr(1, orgValue, " ") - 1)
        Cells(i, 8) = newValue
    Next i
    Range("H1:H" & a).AdvancedFilter Action:=xlFilterCopy, _
        CopyToRange:=Sheets(2).Range("J1"), Unique:=True
    Application.EnableEvents = True
    Application.Calculation = xlCalculationAutomatic
    Application.ScreenUpdating = True
End Sub
