binnaclestar say: Since you are the admin of the receiving system, check the logs for connections from IP addresses with a reverse-DNS of *.outlook.com and *.hotmail.com.  The message indicates that the Microsoft MTAs cannot establish a TCP connection to the target server (i.e. SYN_SENT state till it times-out).  This is either the result of an explicit firewall blocking rule at the destination or a some sort of BGP routing issue preventing connectivity.  Try pinging and tracerouting out to Hotmail/Microsoft MTA IP addresses from the target server.  Perhaps use a BGP looking-glass to troubleshoot the routing (Hurricane Electric has an excellent one at https://lg.he.net).  Easiest way to get the ranges is to issue 'dig +short outlook.com txt' to obtain the SPF record describing where the MTAs are found.  Requires several second-level queries to pull all the addresses.


One possibility is that the receiving MTA has ACLs to drop connections from Azure IP address blocks since Azure is a snake-pit of spammers and abusive exploit attacks.  However the published Azure IP ranges include some Outlook and Hotmail MTAs and these should be subtracted from any such ACL.

rohn007 say:

Yes, it is confusing. MS Marketing and the switch to "subscription" licenses have created a lot of confusion. Lets try to clear it up a little, starting with some definitions.

Product Key - a 25 character code used by MS to verify you have a valid license to use MS products, like Office and Windows. Product keys are specific. The installed version (year) and bundle (365, Home and Student etc) must match exactly.  Thus a 2013 Home and Student key cannot activate any other year of Home and Student. And an Office 365 product key cannot activate a one-time Office 2016 installation.

Activation - the process of submitting a product key to MS to prove that you have a valid right to use Office.  Prior to Office 2016 you did this by submitting the Product Key during install or from inside the application subsequently. With Office 2016/365 activation process starts by requiring an email account to "associate" the license to BEFORE it asks for the Product Key.  After the initial activation of an Office 2016 license or subscription all license "management" is handled through the MyAccount site.

"Traditional" Office License - you pay for your Office license once. You would be given a a Product Key to Activate Office when it was installed on the computer. You can still get traditional Office licenses for Office 2016.

Subscription - starting with release of Office 2013 MS offered a new licensing model, "Subscription".  In this license model you rent your license by the month or year. When you subscription expires you have to pay to renew it. Payment can be set up to "auto renew" by automatically billing your credit card or you can buy a new product key

Account -  Starting with Office 2016 MS has defined a new requirement. ALL Office licenses are now "associated" with an email Account at the start of the activation process. After the initial activation is complete you no longer use the product key for activation. Instead you log in to the MyAccount site: http://office.com/myaccount

when you want to "manage" your license.  This includes installing activated copies of your license on computers.  

Office 365- this is the HIGH level marketing name for all Office subscription licenses. Within that high level, there are many different specific bundles of Office programs and features.  Remember that Product Keys are specific to the bundle.

Upgrade Office - at it's simplest it just means to buy and install a newer version of Office than you currently have.  In the past, up to Office 2007, MS offered a price discount for "Office Upgrades" to anyone who had a "qualifying" product, either specific older version of Office, or some other product. That is no longer offered. Now all "upgrades" require that you buy a new full license to that Office bundle.  One of the features of Office 365 is a "free" automatic upgrade to the next version of Office after it is released is included in the price of the subscription.

About Office - in any Office 2016 application go to the File menu > Account command. This will display the 
Product Information" backstage pane. This pane display activation status, bundle name and version, and the list of applications in the bundle. You can "Log in" to your account on this page. And you can click on the "About" button to get the "Product ID" (sometimes required by MS Support) and access to the MS Office License terms.

End of definitions.

You started by saying you "upgraded" Office. Exactly what did you mean? Exactly what did you do?

It sounds like you have 2 problems.  One is the message that he account is not associated with Office. This is a message that has been inexplicably popping up for a while now.  The fix (hopefully) is to contact MS Billing directly.

Contact MS Accounts and Billing

https://partner.support.services.microsoft.com/en-us/contact/menu/billingissue/

If you get lucky they will be able to help.

The other possible problem is that you installed Office on a computer that already had a factory installed "free trial". The Office activation system gets confused when there is more than one unactivated copy of Office on the machine. The fix for this one is simpler. Go into Control Panel > Programs and Features. Look through the list of programs to see if there is more than one copy of Office installed.  Uninstall the oldest one.  Then do an Office repair of the remaining one. It may fix the activation problem. If it doesn't, since you are dealing with Office 365/2016 log in to the MyAccount site.  Check to see if Office has been installed on this specific computer. If it has, click on the "Deactivate" link. Then click on the Install button to re-install and activate on the computer.

good luck

okinawa---01 say:

Car manufacturers are now building complex car kits into their vehicles.

One of the functions, is that it pulls the phone book data from the phone via bluetooth.

A lot of the Nokia E and N series, Symbian S60 handsets, or Ns40s do not carry the required bluetooth profile in their bluetooth stack.

The phone is missing the Phone Book Access Profile (PBAP) that allows the car to pull data from the phone.

Why the phones do not carry the profile is unknown. What is known is that using the Nokia 810 car kit downloader software, you can partly restore some of this functionality.

The information is here:

http://europe.nokia.com/A4143881

You'll need to delete the pairing in the handset and the car, then install the application.

When it is installed start the application, and then re-pair the phone to the radio, this may then copy your contacts into the radio, be paitent, as it can take a few minutes to copy and setup.

It may copy the phone book over, but the Missed Dialled and Received calls may not work.

I have tested this with an Audi S3, A4, and A6 on a Nokia N82 and N95.

The Nokia E71, appears to have the profile and functions correctly with an Audi car kit.

Installing the 810 downloader only restores phone book functionality, there are some software combinations on car and phones that work without the downloader.

It may also be possible for your car manufacturers franchised dealer to be able to update the software in your car so that additional features are available, they may charge for this service. Also updating software in the phone may also help, as Nokia may see fit to add the profile if they are able.

Some N95 users report that the downloader is not required, I have tried this in my S3 with a Vr30 N95-1 and it does not work at present.

The workaround only resolves the problem with the phone book. Problems such as random disconnecting which also is reported, are beyond the scope of this article.

bernie deitrick say:

Your basic problem is that you should only read the row once, and use it for all the pasting.

So change from this:

Sub Dairycompliation()
'
' Test Macro
'Dim ws As Worksheet

Application.ScreenUpdating = False

For Each ws In ActiveWorkbook.Worksheets
    ws.Select
    Range("A6").Select
    Selection.Copy
    Sheets("Master").Select
    Cells(Rows.Count, "A").End(xlUp).Offset(1, 0).PasteSpecial Paste:=xlPasteValues
    ws.Select
    Range("A9").Select
    Selection.Copy
    Sheets("Master").Select
    Cells(Rows.Count, "B").End(xlUp).Offset(1, 0).PasteSpecial Paste:=xlPasteValues

to this

Sub DairyCompilation()
    Dim ws As Worksheet
    Dim lngR As Long
    Application.ScreenUpdating = False

    For Each ws In ActiveWorkbook.Worksheets
        With Sheets("Master")

            'Find the first empty row (based on column A) for all the values to be copied to

            lngR = .Cells(.Rows.Count, "A").End(xlUp).Row + 1

            'One line per value - hope you see the pattern

            .Cells(lngR, "A").Value = ws.Range("A6").Value
            .Cells(lngR, "B").Value = ws.Range("A9").Value

            'Other lines to get the values of interest

        End With
    Next ws
End Sub

This won't return 0s for blank values, but it will leave the blanks as blanks and not fill in incorrect data for that row.

cjlim say:

The sim trays from the 920 and 025 work on completely different principles. While the 920 tray requires a firm push with the provided pin to pop up the tray, the 925 tray requires no presuure. The pin is inserted and when retracted brings the tray along with it. There is no need for any pressure. Pushing harder does absolutely nothing. Just insert and retract. Much better design than the 920 and way easier to use.
