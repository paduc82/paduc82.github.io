amit tandon say:

Refer below image:

Array formula (CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER) in cell E2:

=INDEX($B$2:$B$100,MATCH(MAX(FREQUENCY(IF($C$2:$C$100>100,IF(YEAR($A$2:$A$100)>2016,MATCH($B$2:$B$100,$B$2:$B$100,0))),ROW($B$2:$B$100)-ROW($B$2)+1)),FREQUENCY(IF($C$2:$C$100>100,IF(YEAR($A$2:$A$100)>2016,MATCH($B$2:$B$100,$B$2:$B$100,0))),ROW($B$2:$B$100)-ROW($B$2)+1),0))

This matches the 2 conditions of column C more than 100 and Year in column A after 2016: (i) $C$2:$C$100>100; and (ii) YEAR($A$2:$A$100)>2016.

Alternate formula - array formula (CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER)  in cell E3:

=INDEX($B$2:$B$100,MATCH(MAX(FREQUENCY(IF($C$2:$C$100>100,IF($A$2:$A$100>DATE(2016,12,31),MATCH($B$2:$B$100,$B$2:$B$100,0))),ROW($B$2:$B$100)-ROW($B$2)+1)),FREQUENCY(IF($C$2:$C$100>100,IF($A$2:$A$100>DATE(2016,12,31),MATCH($B$2:$B$100,$B$2:$B$100,0))),ROW($B$2:$B$100)-ROW($B$2)+1),0))

This formula refers the date instead of the year so that column A is after Dec 31 2016 ie. $A$2:$A$100>DATE(2016,12,31) and column C more than 100.

These formulas will return the correct result even if there is maximum one occurrence or cells are blank. You may update the date DATE(2016,12,31) per your requirement.

For least common text, just replace the MAX in the formula with MIN.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

The issue is that the lines have been created by using the underscore character and when you start typing, you are effectively adding characters to a "word" consisting of those underscores which displaces those at the end of the line to the next line then when you press the space bar, the insertion point is "bound" to the first of the underscore characters so that the characters that you then type become part of the long "word" consisting of that text and the underscore characters which is too long to fit on the first line.  When you then press the space bar after completing that word, it becomes detached from the underscores and as it will then fit on the first line, that is were it is relocated to.

 

For a form that is going to have data entered from a computer, it is not really necessary to have such lines.

tom van stiphout say:

You can have an Access front-end (FE) with a SharePoint (SP) back-end (BE). This works best for small lists, say < 5000 records. If you have bigger tables, you need to use SQL Server, perhaps hosted on Azure. There is no way to store and use an Access BE on OneDrive.

With regards to distributing the FE: you can certainly put that on OneDrive, and instruct users to download the latest version from there and copy to their local machine.

The Access Services  story does not affect you. It is related to Access Web Apps which has been deprecated.
