I can replicate what you're experiencing. I'm not clear the pivot table is converting the years to July-05 dates. =TEXT(R2,0)

I can't tell you *why* Excel is misbehaving here, but the TEXT function puts the years in a format that you can then filter on without issue.

Try to restart your modem and wireless router.

 

This helps create a new connection to your Internet service provider (ISP). When you do this, everyone that is connected to your Wi-Fi network will be temporarily disconnected. Sometimes antivirus or malware-prevention software might prevent you from getting connected. You can see if the connection issue is caused by antivirus and malware-prevention software by turning it off temporarily and then trying to visit a website you trust. If you find it is causing the connection issues, contact the software manufacturer or visit their website to see if updated software is available.

 

Also, reinstalling or upgrading Skype can cause your firewall settings to become out of date. This can affect the quality of your audio calls. Try updating your Windows Firewall to work with Skype.

  1. Click System and Security. Click Windows Firewall and Advanced Settings.
  2. Select and right-click Inbound Rules and select New Rule to display the New Inbound Rule Wizard.
  4. Select Program and click Next. Select This program path and click Browse.
  5. Browse to the following folder, select  the file Skype.exe and click Open. C:/Program Files/Skype/Phone/
  6. Click Next, tick Allow the connection and click Next. Ensure Domain, Private and Public are ticked and click Next.
  7. In the Name field, enter Rules for Skype.
  8. Click Finish. The new inbound rule is displayed in the list of inbound rules.
  9. Right-click Outbound Rules and select New Rule to display the New Outbound Rule Wizard.
  10. Repeat steps 5 to 11 to create a new outbound rule for Skype.
  11. Restart your computer. Sign in to Skype and make a test call.
