Reinstalling the driver for CD\DVD drive should fix the issue. However let's first open the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter to run the automated troubleshooting services.
Step 1:
To run Hardware Troubleshooter:
a. Click Start, Control Panel.
b. In the Control Panel, click System and Security.
c. In the System and Security window, under Action Center, click Troubleshoot common computer problems
d. In the Troubleshoot computer problems window, click Hardware and Sound and under Device select playing and burning CDs, DVDs, and Blu-ray Discs to automatically diagnose and fix a problem.
Open the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter:
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Open-the-Hardware-and-Devices-troubleshooter

Step 2:
If troubleshooter doesnt fix the issue, you may remove and reinstall the existing drivers for CD\DVD drive. To do this, follow these steps:
a. Click on Start, type Device Manager in the search box, press enter.
If you are prompted for an administrator password or for a confirmation, type the password, or click Allow.
c.     In Device Manager, expand DVD/CD-ROM drives, right-click the CD and DVD devices, and then click Uninstall.
e.     When you are prompted to confirm that you want to remove the device, click OK.
f.     Restart the computer.
g. After the computer restarts, the drivers will be automatically installed.
Update a driver for hardware that isn't working properly:
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Update-a-driver-for-hardware-that-isnt-working-properly
