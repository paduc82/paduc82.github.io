I assume these are the shields you're talking about. http://img28.imageshack.us/img28/6352/microsoftsheild.jpg.  They relate to UAC and basically indicate administrator approval will be required to run the program.  It's not a problem - just a helpful reminder of the program's security.

 

See the following for an explanation of what it is and why it is there: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_vista-security/windows-security-icon-on-program-shortcut/b1670a5b-6c8d-47d2-be25-b34d6e294f79.  Though written for Vista, it also applies to W7.

 

This thread explains how to avoid the shield by using a special installation method: http://www.vistax64.com/vista-general/146878-getting-uac-shield-off-desktop-icon.html (but to do this, you need to uninstall it and reinstall it the way that's described).  Again, this will also work with W7.

 

Once installed and on the icons, there's no other way to get rid of them short of turning off UAC and then clearing and restoring the icon cache - and running without UAC is not a good idea so I'd just either live with it or try the option above to see if that helps if you find it intolerable.  Since I always install that way, I've never actually seen one of those shields before researching to respond to this question - so it was also a good learning experience for me as well.

 

I hope this helps.

As far as settings go, I recommend the following to everyone who asks.  It generally resolves all such problems (and I think it will cover your question as well):

 

Make sure that ALL threat levels are sent to Quarantine where you can then decide whether or not to allow them or leave them there, or remove them. It is the safest solution for having the most options for dealing with the situation. Quarantine is as safe as removed, but in some cases you may want to remove them to be more certain - and with this one I would remove it (when the time comes). To do this, open MSE, click on the Settings Tab, and for each of the threat levels, set the action to be Quarantine and save.

 

Go to the History tab and click on the allowed radio button and unless you specifically placed something in allowed, remove everything else from the allowed category. Only put things there that you ABSOLUTELY know are not problems (are "false positives") and triple-check before you do it as doing this could allow a real threat to bypass security and infect you and it won't be detected or rather won't be reported or acted upon by MSE.  To remove something from allowed, select the item and the options to remove it or remove all will enable at the bottom right and you do it there.  NOTE:  You cannot do any of this from the detected items view in History.  That's just a list.  It has no affect on files whatsoever.  You need to deal with Allowed in the allowed section and quarantines in the quarantined section and move from section to section with the radio buttons near the top.  If there is nothing in the section, then there is nothing there to deal with.

 

Go to the Settings Tab and click on Real-time protection and check all the boxes and save.

 

Go to the Settings Tab and click on SpyNet and click on Advanced and save. This is not at all dangerous to you and no information sent will be used by Microsoft to identify you even if some minor identification information is sent in terms of defining the infection, but it can sometimes help with new threats by initiating an immediate update to deal with the problem if it is in the SpyNet database (and it helps Microsoft update definitions to provide you and everyone else better protection).

 

Go to the Settings Tab and click on Advanced and check the box for save Quarantine items and change it from one month to three months so you have more time to decide if you want to restore something.
