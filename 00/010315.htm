rmop say: I assume by "encrypted" you mean that you are being prompted for a microSD card PW, a PW which you never set? If that's the case, your card IS effectively encrypted, even though you had nothing to do with it! You will also sometimes (always, for me) get a "card corrupted" message if you power the phone off/on, but the solution there is to remove/reinsert the card, at which point it is read properly.

I've had E-series phones TWICE set random PW's on SD cards on which I had placed NO PW whatsoever. Both times, this happened in the context of resetting the phone (an E60 in my case) to the factory defaults. In my case, the phone could read the card after the reset, and I then smugly restored all of my data to the phone from by card-based backup. The phone then did a reset (normal after such a restore), but then requested a PW to be able to read the card! I had set NONE!

After some equivocating, Nokia support admitted (by phone -- no written record, of course) that the E60 had applied a random PW to the SD card. Nothing could be done. Nokia as much as said "Nokia's fault, your problem" claiming that because the card was not a Nokia-brand card, they weren't responsible, even though they didn't even suggest that the fault was with the card, but rather the phone. Seriously irritating.

The cards were made by Kingston, and have a lifetime warranty. I explained the situation to Kingston, and they said as much as "Nokia's fault, but our problem" and sent brand new cards! I was impressed by Kingston's support. They indicated they'd had similar reports about Nokia phones pulling this stunt.

There ARE devices (eBay) which will remove the PW and also destroy all of the data. Given the price of microSD cards now, it's usually better to just get a new card. A KINGSTON card, in my opinion. You will NOT find a way to recover data directly from your now-locked SD card. Neither will you find the Nokia-generated PW anywhere in a hidden file on your phone. And you won't find a way to format it either as long as it is PW-protected. It's a really secure little card. I still backup my phone to the SD card, but also backup both phone AND card to my PC. Keep a copy of that SD card somewhere for days like this...

BUT, after those fiascos, I began putting a PW on my SD cards, reasoning that Nokia can't do so if one is already there. Not sure of that logic, but I've had no further such troubles. So, I have concluded after nearly two years of no further episodes that the trick is to always set your OWN PW on these cards so that your phone won't surprise you by doing it for you!

Hope this helps.

For anyone wanting to check it out without downloading the workbook, here's the code.  You will need an Excel workbook with 2 sheets in it.  Name one of the sheets "TeamMixer" and the other sheet must be named "TournamentSchedule".

 

The build-down from 1024 possible combinations to the 496 unique ones takes a bit of time - it takes about 5-10 seconds from the time I click the button on the TournamentSchedule sheet until the schedule is actually placed on the sheet. The code.  To put it into a workbook, open the workbook.  Press [Alt]+[F11] to open the VB Editor.  In the VB Editor, choose Insert --> Module from the menu and copy all of the code below and paste it into the module presented to you.  Close the VB Editor with its 'red'-x or by pressing [Alt]+[Q].  Important save the file as a Macro Enabled workbook, type .xlsm or .xlsb to preserve the code for future use.  Run the macro named BuildSchedule to do the job.

 

'these are constants used by several Subs
'so they are declared outside of any Sub...END Sub process
'they become available to all Subs/Functions in this module
  Const schedSheetName = "TournamentSchedule"
  Const schedKeyCol = "A"
 
  Const mixerSheetName = "TeamMixer"
  Const mixPairCol = "A"
  Const mixRevCol = "B"
  Const mixPN1Col = "C"
  Const mixPN2Col = "D"
  Const mixP1Col = "F"
  Const mixP2Col = "G"
  Const mixPrevPairCol = "H"
 
Sub BuildSchedule()
  Dim schedWS As Worksheet
  Dim mixerWS As Worksheet
  Dim daysCount As Integer
 
  Set schedWS = ThisWorkbook.Worksheets(schedSheetName)
  Set mixerWS = ThisWorkbook.Worksheets(mixerSheetName)
  'begin by clearing out both the old schedule sheet
  'and the Team Mixer sheet
  schedWS.Cells.ClearContents
  mixerWS.Cells.ClearContents
  'rebuild the unique pairings of 32 entrants
  FillWithAll ' this takes 5-10 seconds to complete (3.6GHz system)
  'now we start building daily schedules
  'first day is kind of special
  'start all fresh
  With mixerWS
    .Columns(mixP1Col & ":" & mixPrevPairCol).ClearContents
    .Range(mixP1Col & 1) = "Player 1"
    .Range(mixP2Col & 1) = "Player 2"
    .Range(mixPrevPairCol & 1) = "Previous Pairings"
  End With
  For daysCount = 1 To 12 ' # of days in tournament
    'get a group of 16 teams
    Get16Teams
    'move teaming information to the schedule sheet
    BuildScheduleEntry daysCount
  Next
  Set schedWS = Nothing
  Set mixerWS = Nothing
  MsgBox "Tournament Schedule Build is Finished", _
   vbOKOnly + vbInformation, "Task Completed"
End Sub

 

Private Sub BuildScheduleEntry(roundsCount As Integer)
  Const teamsCount = 16
 
  Dim schedWS As Worksheet
  Dim mixerWS As Worksheet
  Dim srcRowPtr As Long
  Dim destRowPtr As Long
  Dim destColPtr As Integer
  Dim teamCounter As Integer
  'set reference to our team mixer sheet
  Set mixerWS = ThisWorkbook.Worksheets(mixerSheetName)
  'now transfer the information to the schedule sheet
  Set schedWS = ThisWorkbook.Worksheets(schedSheetName)
  'we know that the player # entries on the mixer sheet start at row 2
  srcRowPtr = 2 ' seed the value
  destRowPtr = schedWS.Range(schedKeyCol & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Row + 2 ' seed the value

  schedWS.Range(schedKeyCol & destRowPtr) = "Round #" & roundsCount
  'enter "Team #" entries on a row
  destRowPtr = destRowPtr + 1
  destColPtr = 1
  For teamCounter = 1 To teamsCount
    schedWS.Cells(destRowPtr, destColPtr) = "Team #" & teamCounter
    destColPtr = destColPtr + 2
  Next ' end teamCounter loop
  With schedWS.Range(schedWS.Cells(destRowPtr, 1), _
   schedWS.Cells(destRowPtr, destColPtr))
    .HorizontalAlignment = xlCenterAcrossSelection
  End With
  'enter the team's players on a row
  destRowPtr = destRowPtr + 1
  destColPtr = 1 ' seed the value
 
  For teamCounter = 1 To teamsCount
    schedWS.Cells(destRowPtr, destColPtr) = _
     mixerWS.Range(mixP1Col & srcRowPtr)
    destColPtr = destColPtr + 1
    schedWS.Cells(destRowPtr, destColPtr) = _
     mixerWS.Range(mixP2Col & srcRowPtr)
    destColPtr = destColPtr + 1
    srcRowPtr = srcRowPtr + 1
  Next ' end 2nd teamCounter loop
  With schedWS.Range(schedWS.Cells(destRowPtr, 1), _
   schedWS.Cells(destRowPtr, destColPtr))
    .HorizontalAlignment = xlCenter
  End With

End Sub

Private Sub Get16Teams()
  Const teamsToCreate = 16
 
  Dim mixerWS As Worksheet
  Dim selectionRange As Range
 
  Dim teamCount As Integer
  Dim p1List As Range
  Dim p2List As Range
  Dim pairedList As Range
  Dim lastRow As Long
  Dim rowPtr As Long
 
  Dim testP1 As Integer
  Dim testP2 As Integer
  Dim pairValue As Integer
  Dim foundP1 As Range
  Dim foundP2 As Range
  Dim foundPair As Range
 
  Set mixerWS = ThisWorkbook.Worksheets(mixerSheetName)
  'this clears out any previous tournament day's teamings
  lastRow = mixerWS.Range(mixP1Col & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Row
  If lastRow > 1 Then
    mixerWS.Range(mixP1Col & 2 & ":" & mixP1Col & lastRow).ClearContents
  End If
  lastRow = mixerWS.Range(mixP2Col & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Row
  If lastRow > 1 Then
    mixerWS.Range(mixP2Col & 2 & ":" & mixP2Col & lastRow).ClearContents
  End If
 
  Set p1List = mixerWS.Range(mixP1Col & "1:" & mixP1Col & 1)
  Set p2List = mixerWS.Range(mixP2Col & "1:" & mixP2Col & 1)
  Set pairedList = mixerWS.Range(mixPrevPairCol & "1:" & _
   mixerWS.Range(mixPrevPairCol & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Address)
 
  lastRow = mixerWS.Range(mixPN1Col & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Row
 
  Do Until teamCount >= teamsToCreate
    DoEvents
    For rowPtr = 2 To lastRow
      DoEvents
      testP1 = mixerWS.Range(mixPN1Col & rowPtr)
      testP2 = mixerWS.Range(mixPN2Col & rowPtr)
      'have we used either player yet today
      Set foundP1 = p1List.Find(testP1, lookat:=xlWhole)
      If foundP1 Is Nothing Then
        Set foundP1 = p2List.Find(testP1, lookat:=xlWhole)
      End If
      Set foundP2 = p1List.Find(testP2, lookat:=xlWhole)
      If foundP2 Is Nothing Then
        Set foundP2 = p2List.Find(testP2, lookat:=xlWhole)
      End If
      pairValue = testP1 * 100 + testP2
      Set foundPair = pairedList.Find(pairValue, lookat:=xlWhole)
      If foundP1 Is Nothing _
       And foundP2 Is Nothing _
       And foundPair Is Nothing Then
        DoEvents
        'we have not used either player yet today!
        'nor have we ever used the pairing
        With mixerWS
          .Range(mixP1Col & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Offset(1, 0) = testP1
          .Range(mixP2Col & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Offset(1, 0) = testP2
          .Range(mixPrevPairCol & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Offset(1, 0) = pairValue
        End With
        Set p1List = mixerWS.Range(mixP1Col & "1:" _
         & mixerWS.Range(mixP1Col & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Address)
        Set p2List = mixerWS.Range(mixP2Col & "1:" _
         & mixerWS.Range(mixP2Col & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Address)
        Set pairedList = mixerWS.Range(mixPrevPairCol & "1:" & _
         mixerWS.Range(mixPrevPairCol & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Address)
        teamCount = teamCount + 1
      End If
    Next
  Loop
End Sub

As for re-linking the tables, you don't have to drop them first. The routine I use is this:


Public Function AttachTables(strFileName As String) As Boolean
'Purpose  : Relink tables to Front End.
'DateTime : 8/16/2004 10:19
'Author   : Bill Mosca
    Dim db As DAO.Database
    Dim tdf As DAO.TableDef
    Dim x As Variant
    Dim i As Integer
    Dim RetVal As Integer
    Dim wks As DAO.Workspace
   
    On Error GoTo err_AttachTables

    DoCmd.Hourglass True
   
    RetVal = True
    'Use an member of the Admins group.
    Set wks = DBEngine.CreateWorkspace("", "Admin", "")
    Set db = wks.OpenDatabase(CurrentDb.Name)
    x = SysCmd(acSysCmdInitMeter, "Attaching Tables: ", db.TableDefs.Count - 1)
   
    For i = 0 To db.TableDefs.Count - 1
        Set tdf = db.TableDefs(i)
        If Len(tdf.Connect & vbNullString) <> 0 And Left(tdf.Connect, 10) = ";DATABASE=" Then
            tdf.Connect = ";DATABASE=" & strFileName
            On Error Resume Next
            tdf.RefreshLink
           
            If Err <> 0 Then
              MsgBox "The selected file does not contain the required table(s):" & vbCrLf & tdf.SourceTableName, 16, "Incorrect Database"
              RetVal = False
            End If
           
            If Not RetVal = True Then
              Exit For
            End If
        End If
        x = SysCmd(acSysCmdUpdateMeter, i + 1)
    Next i
   
    x = SysCmd(acSysCmdRemoveMeter)
    AttachTables = RetVal


exit_AttachTables:
    On Error Resume Next
    DoCmd.Hourglass False
    Set tdf = Nothing
    Set db = Nothing
    Set wks = Nothing
    Exit Function

err_AttachTables:
    Dim strErrMsg As String
    Dim lngErrIconBtn As Long
   
    Select Case Err.Number
        Case Else
            strErrMsg = "Error " & Err.Number & " (" & Err.Description & ") " _
                & "occurred in procedure AttachTables of " _
                & "VBA Document basTableLinking"
            lngErrIconBtn = vbOKOnly + vbInformation
    End Select

    If strErrMsg <> "" Then MsgBox strErrMsg, lngErrIconBtn, _
        "Error!"
   
    Resume exit_AttachTables

   
End Function

jay freedman say:

You can modify the style named Bibliography to make the text Times New Roman 12pt double spaced:

  • Press Ctrl+Alt+Shift+S to open the Styles pane.
  • Click in one of the bibliography entries. You should see the highlight on the Bibliography style.
  • Depending on the exact build of Word you have, either right-click the highlighted style or click the down-arrow to the right of it, and choose Modify in the menu.
  • In the Modify Style dialog box, set the font dropdowns to Times New Roman and 12pt, and click the double-spacing button (the third of the three line spacing buttons).
  • Click the option at the bottom for "New documents based on this template", and click OK.

If you're using APA citation style, as it appears from your sample, the first line of each entry will start at the left margin and the other lines will be indented. These things are in the coding of the APA style and can't be changed unless you're willing to dig into the XML language. You can select the whole bibliography and then drag the markers on the horizontal ruler to make the first lines indented the same as the other lines -- but if you later update the bibliography, the alignment will go back to the way APA style is defined.

You can click in the References heading, which is in Heading 1 style (usually left-aligned) and press Ctrl+E, or click the Center button in the Paragraph group of the Home tab. That will remain in place when you update.
