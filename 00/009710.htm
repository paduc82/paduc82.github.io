This is a Yosemite issue, not a Word bug. In Word, you can set the location to look for pictures using the File Locations control panel. In 2011, this can be found at Word>Preferences>File Locations>Clipart pictures.

scott .. b say:

In earlier versions of Internet Explorer, open tabs were placed side-by-side in the order they were opened.  In Win 8/8.1 and IE 10/11, tabs are arranged in "groups", e.g. all tabs you opened that relate to "weather" will be grouped under 1 (1a, 1b, 1c, etc.) and the next tab (#2) may be all Sports tabs, e.g. 2a,2b,2c.   If you open another "weather" related tab, it is placed in group 1, rather than at the end of the string and becomes 1d.  If you open another sports tab, it is placed in the sports group and becomes 2d, etc.

You can turn this off by UNCHECKING "Enable Tab Groups"  Tools>Internet Options>Tabs

restart your computer after making the change

You will have to first deactivate Office license which was installed on old computer otherwise it will be counted as a license. Hence, if you were allowed installation on one computer, you will not be able to activate on new computer.
