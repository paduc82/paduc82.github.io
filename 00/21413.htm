bill manville say:

It is unfortunate that the dates are in descending order as it makes it more difficult.

 

=IFERROR(VLOOKUP(TheDate,TheTable,2,FALSE),INDEX(Prices,MATCH(TheDate,Dates,-1)+1))
where

TheDate is the date you are trying to get the price for
TheTable is the range containing the dates and prices
Prices is the column of prices in TheTable
Dates is the column of dates in TheTable

 

The formula returns the price from the table if there is an exact match on TheDate
Otherwise it returns the price from the row below the row which contains the next date above TheDate

ashish mathur say:

Hi,

 

Sort data on "Sheet where data is stored" in ascending order of dates - suppose data is in range B2:C4.  On the other sheet, enter this formula and copy down

ronrosenfeld say:

This formula must be **array-entered**:

 

A1 contains the first date on the "sheet to return all values"

 

B1:    =INDEX(Sheet2!$A$1:$B$3,MATCH(TRUE,A1>=Sheet2!$A$1:$A$3,0),2)

 

Your second list is on Sheet2 "where the data is stored" in A1:B3
