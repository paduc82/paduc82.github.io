I am an E-Learning teacher who asked students to share their sways from one school board to another. There is an error message that appears when I'm trying to look at the out-of-board student's sway and he isn't able to look at other student's sways either. 

Is there a permissions setting that can be set? This is the error message. 

jason (office engineer) say:

Hi Christina,

By default, when using Office 365 accounts (the types distributed by schools) the permission level on Sways is for only people in the same organization to be able to view them.  The author can open the Sway and change this to "Anyone with a link" in the Share dropdown though.

The only caveat is that the admin for your organization can choose to disable this option for Sway.  If the option is not there, you will need to contact your school's IT support to see about getting it enabled.  Otherwise, you could create and share the Sway from a personal account where Sways are publicly available to anyone with the link by default.

graham mayor say:
The fundamental problem with the grammar checker is that you require a good working knowledge of the language's grammatical structure in order to make intelligent use of it, and if you have that, you don't need it. Grammar checkers make suggestions as to structure, but they cannot be relied upon to produce a well structured document. They should therefore be treated with caution and used only as a guide - and to that end Microsoft's own grammar checker is good enough for the job.
