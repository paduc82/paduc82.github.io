how to format document - Microsoft Community

colette skellenger say:

how to format document

How do I get a degree symbol for latitude and longitude (not temperature0?

How can I eliminate the automatic capitalization of every first word of every phrase?

How can I eliminate the automatic indent and numbering of lines when I want to number a list of things?

How can I eliminate the automatic underlining in red, green, etc of words and names when writing my text? 

How can I set tabs where I want them?

I will soon get Word 2010 and Windows 7; will these problems be easier to resolve in Word 2010?  Will the fixes you give me for Word 2007 be the same fixes in Word 2010?   Thank you.
jay freedman say:
None of these things are "problems". They're just the ordinary setup of Word to your preferences (which appear to be somewhat different from other people's preferences). Yes, the way you set them (not "fixes") will be the same in Word 2010 as in Word 2007, with the only difference being that the Office button will be replaced with a tab that says "File".

Degree symbol: There's a built-in shortcut (press the Ctrl key and the @ key at the same time, release them, then press the spacebar), or you can hold down the Alt key while typing 0176 on the number pad (with NumLock turned on) to get the ¬∞ character. By the way, it's the same symbol for latitude/longitude and for temperature. Read http://sbarnhill.mvps.org/WordFAQs/InsertSpecChars.htm for more info.

Automatic capitalization and automatic indenting/numbering: Go to Office button > Word Options > Proofing and click the AutoCorrect Options button. In the dialog, uncheck the options for "Capitalize first letter of sentences" (and probably also "Capitalize first letter of table cells"). Then click the AutoFormat As You Type tab, and uncheck "Automatic numbered lists" (and probably also "Automatic bulleted lists"). Read http://www.word.mvps.org/FAQs/Formatting/TameAutoFormat.htm .

Underlining: These are indications of spelling "errors" (actually, just things that aren't in Word's online dictionary), supposed grammar mistakes, and possibly inconsistent formatting. If you don't want any of it, go to Office button > Word Options > Proofing and uncheck "Check spelling as you type" and "Mark grammar errors as you type". Then go to the Advanced tab of the Options dialog and uncheck "Mark formatting inconsistencies". If you do want spell checking as you type, but you have names or other words that you don't want Word to mark, right-click the underlined text and click either "Add to dictionary" (for a permanent fix) or "Ignore all" (for just the current session). It's also possible to mark specific text with "Do not check spelling or grammar" by selecting the text and going to the Set Language dialog on the Review tab. Read http://sbarnhill.mvps.org/WordFAQs/MasterSpellCheck.htm .

Setting tabs: If just for a specific paragraph, just click the proper spot on the horizontal ruler. Or press Alt+O, then press T to open the Tabs dialog, and type in the positions (as measured from the left margin, not from the edge of the page). Tab stops on the ruler can be dragged to new positions, or dragged off the ruler to delete them. For a more permanent solution, include the desired tab settings in paragraph styles.

  2. Click File -> Options
  3. In the Options dialog click "Customize Ribbon"
  4. At the upper right of the dialog in the "Customize the Ribbon" drop-down select "Tool Tabs"

  6. Click OK
