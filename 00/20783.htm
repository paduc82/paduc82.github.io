    Some additional thoughts and clarifications....

    Max wrote:

    -----

    From what Ive found, this bug does not affect other integers such as .1803 and .1805, only .1801 is affected.

    -----

    First, 0.1803 et al are decimal fractions, not "integers".

    Second, for integer parts in the range 32768 to 65535, 0.1801 is not the only decimal fraction that has this problem.

    Other examples:  32768.098, 32768.223, 32768.348, 32768.473, 32768.598, 32768.723, 32768.848, 32768.973 (32678.098 + {0,1,..,7}*0.125).  And there are many more examples.

    Max wrote:

    -----

     I can work around the problem by changing the format on the cell to text

    -----

    I wrote:

    -----

    As for a work-around, the only way I can think of is to enter --"33000.1801" or VALUE("33000.1801") or  something like that.  That is, enter a text constant, not a numeric constant.

    -----

    It should be clear from my examples that:  (a) I did not mean what I wrote literally ("text constant"); and (b) I am not talking about changing the cell format.

    I should have said:  enter the text constant into an arithmetic expression.

    And I should note that that is not an easy work-around to apply.  I would enter values as usual (numeric constants) and resort to a "numeric text expression" only when I encounter the problem.  It is relatively rare.
