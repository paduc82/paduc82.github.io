access 2010 - Microsoft Community

yvette bojorquez say:

access 2010

If you no longer want to use an item in a dropdown list, can you delete the item from the list without it being deleted from tables where they were previously used?

ken sheridan say: If you remove an item from the RowSource of a combo box, whether it’s a value list or an SQL statement which references a table, the value will not be deleted from the column in a referencing table for which the combo box is the bound control in a form.  If the combo box's BoundColumn is the visible column, the value will still show in the combo box for any rows in which that value has been selected, even though it does not appear in the control's drop down list.

If, on the other hand, the BoundColumn is a hidden surrogate numeric key value, e.g. if the control is bound to a CityID foreign key column in a form's table, but the text CityName value is visible in the control, then, if a city were removed from the drop down list, for any rows where that city had been selected the combo box would appear empty.  No data would be lost, however, and the value of the combo box would still be the hidden numeric CityID value, but because there is no longer a CityName value in the list to which that number can map, the control would be blank.

A common situation where a row is removed from a combo box's list is where, for instance, only current employees are listed, but the referencing table still contains rows which refer to former employees, now marked as inactive.  In such a scenario the value of the combo box would of course be that of a hidden EmployeeID column or similar, as names can be duplicated, and are consequently unsuitable as keys, so the former employee names would not show in the control.

You'll find an example of how to deal with this in ActiveEmployees.zip in my public databases folder at:

https://onedrive.live.com/?cid=44CC60D7FEA42912&id=44CC60D7FEA42912!169

Note that if you are using an earlier version of Access you might find that the colour of some form objects such as buttons shows incorrectly and you will need to  amend the form design accordingly.  

If you have difficulty opening the link, copy the link (NB, not the link location) and paste it into your browser's address bar.

This little demo file illustrates how to avoid the names disappearing in both single and continuous forms view.  In single form view its simply a matter of setting the RowSource to an SQL statement which returns all employees who are marked Active OR where the employee is the current employee in the form.  In continuous forms view its not quite so simple, and you have to resort to a hybrid control made up of a text box carefully superimposed on a combo box to give the appearance of a single combo box control.

christopher miller say:

Hi rajinder_911,

Have you tried restarting the phone to see that would fix the problem?

To restart your phone, press and hold the Power  button until you see Slide down to power off, then swipe down. (This usually takes about 3 seconds.) Once it's off, press the Power button to turn on your phone again.

If restarting your phone doesn't work, do one of the following:

  • Press and hold the Volume Down  and Power  buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10–15 seconds). Your phone will restart automatically.
Notes
  • Sometimes your phone will vibrate right away when you press the Volume Down and Power  buttons—this is probably due to your ringer changing to Vibrate. Wait to release the buttons until a second vibration after 10–15 seconds.
  • If you don't let go of the Volume Down  and Power  buttons right after the vibration at 10–15 seconds, your phone may show a black startup screen and not restart normally. If this happens, just repeat the button combo to try again.
  • Not all phones support the ability to restart your phone this way, so check with your phone's manufacturer to find out how to force a restart.
  • If your phone has a replaceable battery, remove and reinsert the battery located under your phone's back panel, and then press the Power  button to turn on your phone. (The date and time may be incorrect after restarting your phone, which can cause issues when you try to sign in with your Microsoft account. To set the time correctly, go toSettings  > Date + time.)

Some people have reported this problem happening when the update file was somehow downloaded to the phone twice.  Other people had to turn off the battery saver.  See this message thread for more details.
