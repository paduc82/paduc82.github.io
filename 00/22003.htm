If it is a retail license, then yes, you are allowed to uninstall it from one machine and install it onto another.

A Retail license of Office also allows you to install it on 1 PC and on 1 mobile device (laptop/netbook/tablet) of the main user of that PC. For additional PCs you'll require additional licenses.

Can you elaborate on the type of email messages that you're getting? Specifically, are these generally the same (i.e. bounce back messages) that "appear" to have been sent from your email address? If yes, the most likely issue is that your email address has been "hijacked" (versus "hacked") by a spammer making it appear that the spam messages being sent out came from you. In this instance, there is little you can do to stop it. The spammer will eventually move on to using another "hijacked" email address.

If your email account has been hacked and messages sent from your account, you would see the sent messages in the <Sent Items> folder. You already mentioned changing your password so the other thing that should be done (if not already) is to enable 2 factor authentication.
