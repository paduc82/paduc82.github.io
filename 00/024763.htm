germain navas say:
Hello Alexander !

Let's Suppose you have your domain on an Exchange 2016 mail server on premises. Right now you should have an MX record in your hosting service that points to your server, say domain.net.

Then you decide to use Office 365. In effect, you have to add the domain in Office, as you said.

You can either migrate the organization to Exchange online or you can use a hybrid configuration, in which local mailboxes and cloud mailboxes coexist.

The MX record is the address where your organization wants to receive your mail, and it is placed in the hosting service.


When someone sends an email to domain.net, the records in the hosting provider indicate where "domain" lives and directs them there.

The mail in that case can be left pointing to your old mail server on premises or change the mx record to point to Office, which is the recommended option. It would look something like this: domain-net.mail.protection.outlook.com. This option is recommended so that Exchange online takes over the spam protection.


Assuming that you decide on the hybrid option, the administration of the mailboxes migrated to Office would be performed in the Office.com portal (transport rules, forwarding, audits, message trace, etc.), however, the attributes would be managed from the local servers. Local servers are the source of authority.

Local mailboxes will continue to be managed on the on-premises server, and synchronized mailbox attributes will be managed by the Active Directory. So you would need access to local servers

Basically, if you keep an AD and / or Exchange server, you will need to access those servers

For total cloud management, you need to migrate everything to the cloud.

Here is more info related :

Mail flow best practices for Exchange Online and Office 365 (overview): https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/exchange/mail-...

Answer : Who is hosting your domain? And who is hosting your DNS Server? Your DNS Server contains a directory of information required to interact with the internet services subscribed by your company. Office365 will require you to insert a number of entries into your DNS, firstly to prove that you own the domain (they will try reading these entries from the internet after you insert them). Then they will require you to enter the entries for SPF, Autodiscover, and the rest of the services like Skype for business. For more advanced setting, you may want to also insert entries for email sender authentication (ie DKIM and DMARC). All this would be better explained to you by a deployment partner that you work with.

"I assume that since GoDaddy would be hosting a Domain For me:   Recall my Domain Root Name = MicroSite.net

and since I also will have some way of generating my own Local Premise  MX Record...."

Answer : No you do not generate your own MX Record. A MX record is used to tell the world which mail servers (in your case Exchange Online) accept incoming mail for your domain and where emails sent to your domain should be routed to. So Microsoft will provide you the MX record during Office365 provisioning, and you insert this record into your DNS.

A hybrid deployment is even more complex as this involves routing mail between a on-premise exchange and exchange online. On top of that, there is the issue of authentication with your on-premise AD.

Based on your level of understanding (gathered from the two posts you made so far), the technical skills required to deploy an exchange Online Hybrid for a 500 user company, in my opinion, is far more complex than what you know now.

As a conclusion, work with an experienced partner that has proven track record deploying Office365 in a Exchange Hybrid environment.  You get this wrong, everyone will have mail issues and will have significant impact to your organization.

haiyusky say:

Hi Samer,

If you create groups including term sets in a site collection ->site settings-> term store management (yourdomain.sharepoint.com/.../termstoremanager.aspx), it means groups created here can only be used in this specific site collection.

If you create groups including term sets in SharePoint admin center -> term store (yourdomain-admin.sharepoint.com/.../termstoremanager.aspx), it means all groups created here can be used in all your site collection.

So, where to create them totally depends on your own situation. If you'd like to use the same groups including term sets in different site collections, the best practice is to create them in SharePoint admin center.
