joeu2004 say: William wrote:

Looking for help on a formula..    If my goal is to sell 30 widgets in this month, and on the 20th of the month, I have already sold 20 widgets, then I know that I'm on track to sell 30, or I'm tracking 100% of my goal.. if in this same scenario i have only sold 10 widgets, then i'm tracking to sell 15, or 50% of my goal.  What formula would I use to do this where it looks at the cell displaying the date (TODAY), looks at my actual data, and tells me where I'll end up if I continue on the same sales trend..  not looking to graph or line chart the results... just a number or percentage.


Suppose A1 contains your goal (30), A2 contains the number sold "to date" (20), and A3 contains the current date (that is, the date associated with A2), perhaps =TODAY().

Then the estimated number of sales for the month is (in A4):
=ROUND(DAY(EOMONTH(A3,0))*A2/DAY(A3),0)

And the percentage of your goal is:
=A4/A1
or
=ROUND(DAY(EOMONTH(A3,0))*A2/DAY(A3),0)/A1

formatted as Percentage.

AWESOME... this worked perfectly.. what would I change if I wanted the same results for Year to Date instead of month to date.

I am not sure what you want to compute:  the estimate and %progress for the month-end as before, or the estimate and %progress for the year-end.

Both are computable.  But as a practical matter, I think it dubious to compute end-of-month estimates based on year-to-date sales because it ignores seasonal or other recent variations.

-----

If A1 is the year-end goal, A2 is the year-to-date sales, and A3 is the "current" date associated with A2, the year-end estimate is (in A5):

=ROUND((DATE(YEAR(A3),12,32)-DATE(YEAR(A3),1,0))*A2/(A3-DATE(YEAR(A3),1,0)),0)

And the percentage of your year-end goal is:

=A5/A1

formatted as Percentage.

DATE(YEAR(A3),12,32) is the date following Dec 31, i.e. Jan 1 of the next year.  DATE(YEAR(A3),1,0) is the date preceding Jan 1, i.e. Dec 31 of the previous year.


-----

If A4 is the goal for the month (30 as before, not the year-to-end-of-month goal) and A2:A3 are as above, the estimate for the month is (in A6):

=ROUND(DAY(EOMONTH(A3,0))*A2/(A3-DATE(YEAR(A3),1,0)),0)

And the percentage of your month-end goal is:

=A6/A4

formatted as Percentage.

Note that A2 is the year-to-date sales, not the month-to-date sales as before.
