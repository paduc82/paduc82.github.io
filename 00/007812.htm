rich michaels - mvp (greatcirclelearning.com) say:

Your Normal paragraph style has the font underlined so any new document that you create ends up underlined.

On your Home tab of the Ribbon there should be a group labeled Styles. Locate the style named Normal and Control + Click (a.k.a. right click) on it. Choose the Modify option and in the formating area turn off the underline feature.

Before clicking Ok make sure you check the box in the lower left that is labeled "Add to Template".

Back all the way out of Word and make sure you quit Word completely, this forces a save of the Normal template. When you reopen Word and start a new document, verify that the problem has been corrected.

That means all PT-keyboard users on Mac are unable to type the @ character in Teams, and this is a major problem!

How can I change the default shortcuts in Teams to circumvent this?

joão pedro rodrigues nb say:

Hi Rick,

Thank you for taking the time to research and address this question.

Unfortunately you answer is not correct.  In the Mac PT keyboard, SHIFT+2 will produce " (quotation mark). OPTION+2 will produce @ (at).

Likewise, SHIFT+3 will produce # (hash/pound sign) and OPTION+3 the ‚Ç¨ (euro) character, making it also hard to type the euro character in MS Teams.

I believe this will affect any keyboard layout where keys 1-4 have more than 2 character assignments, which happens in several languages, like Spanish, Slovak or Thai. Check for instance https://keyshorts.com/blogs/blog/37615873-how-to-identify-macbook-keyboard-localization

However unpleasant this may be to Mac users, I believe there is a way to circumvent it. There seems to be a distinction between LEFT OPTION and RIGHT OPTION keys.

The solution that works for me is to use the RIGHT OPTION key to get the desired character.

For instance, RIGHT OPTION + 2 will produce @ in the PT keyboard.

I hope this will help Teams users in other languages as well.
