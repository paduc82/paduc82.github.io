a. user say:

Hello Kurian:

 

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding your question about bluetooth transferred files.  The location of unsupported files should be found in the area specific to that type of file, if they saved to the phone at all.

 

  • Music files would be found in the Music + Videos application.
  • Documents would be found in the Office application.  Once in the application, go to locations > phone.
  • Pictures would be found by going into pictures > albums/date/people.
  • Contacts are not stored on the phone but come from the cloud.  To remove contacts you can do this from the phone or by going to accounts.live.com and deleting that contact.

If you absolutely cannot locate the files and are sure they were saved to the phone, a hard reset would be the last resort option.  The steps to perform a hard reset are outlined here, http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2430020?wa=wsignin1.0.  Before you move forward with a hard reset, please back up your content, http://support.xbox.com/en-US/zune/software/syncing-media.  Be aware that text message history and game progress will be reset.

 

If you prefer not to hard reset, you can always start storing photos, some music and documents on Skydrive and then delete them from the phone.  This will allow you to free up some of that valuable space.

 

Please let us know if you have any additional questions or concerns.
