In regards to the Xbox Live games, keep in mind that most games on the Xbox 360 are peer to peer based networks, which means that even if you have a fast connection, you still get information from other players with their network speed. So if you are playing with someone with a bad and slow internet connection, you will see him lagging and bouncing around, even if you have a fast connection.

As for the Video streaming issues: some providers actively throttle traffic from high bandwith sources like video streaming services. The prices ISPs charge are a mean average of the bandwith the users use, so it is possible for them to keep competitive prices, as a person that only does emails and a bit of websurfing pays the same as someone constantly leeching large files from the internet.

However due to the popularity of video streaming sites, a lot of users are now pulling large amounts of data through the ISP, and it's getting too expensive for your Internet provider.

Therefore, some providers artificially throttle the traffic from known high volume sites like Netflix or Hulu and the likes. See the report here.

To make matters worse: As soon as the ISP detects you are accessing well known speed test sites, they even route you through a faster line so you can be "impressed" by their awesome speed, and after the test is done, you are put back on the regularly throttled line.
