jim gordon mvp say:

Office 2015 for Mac was a preview version. When the final version was released the 2015 version was renamed Office 2016. If you go to the Excel menu and choose About Excel you will find it is version 15.20 (assuming you have applied the required updates).

Command-X is the correct keyboard shortcut for Edit > Cut. This has not changed in the 30 years Excel for Mac has existed. If command-x is not working, you might try a different keyboard and see what happens.

When you paste, you can choose an individual cell and the use Command-V to paste. Assuming the range of cells where the pasted stuff will land is all empty, non-merged cells, it will simply paste. suzanne s. barnhill say:

"Hide spelling errors" is a document setting; it is saved with the document. "Do not check spelling or grammar" is a language setting; it is applied just like font characteristics such as bold and italic and therefore can vary throughout a document. Selecting the entire document and disabling "Do not check" should work, but when the setting is mixed, you will have to check the box twice to completely clear it. "Recheck Document" only rechecks words you've told the spelling checker to ignore.
