Power System and Sleep issues - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Power System and Sleep issues

W10 Power and Sleep issues

Older ACPI motherboard users:  I think I have found a workaround for us.  My 2009 AMD powered HP uses an ECS-MCP61PM-HM motherboard, ACPI v2.1/2.2 compliant, and these changes in available settings under both 'Settings' and 'Control Panel' cured the problems for me.  For me the interface returns blessed Windows 7 functionality as far as Sleep is concerned.  I'm hardwired (Cat6) to an always on cable modem internet connection.  If you're wireless you're going to have to get creative a little bit.  Your Control Panel Network Adapter will be a little different but should offer mostly the same tweaks.  So without further ado, for build 10565 Pro and MAYBE lower builds and other versions... 

CONTROL PANEL:  Go to: 

1.) Device Manager, Network Adapter, Advanced Tab; Disable Wake on Magic Packet.  Disable Wake on Pattern Match.  (Optional, I set Jumbo frame to 9KB MTU). Now select the Network Adapter Power Management Tab; Allow computer to turn off...checked. Allow this device to wake...unchecked.

2.) Device Manager, Display Adapter, double click, and select the Driver Tab. You should have a Windows Hardware Compatibility Lab or WHC Publisher driver installed. Trying to leap ahead to an as yet untested for W10 manufacturer's driver can create issues with the power block and modes according to HP. 

3.) Device Manager, Power Options; Select Plan: HIGH PERFORMANCE, then look back to the left side of the window, and select 'Change when the computer sleeps', select NEVER. || Changing the time the display turns off, select: 30 minutes.  (NOTE: It is your choice of time really, just make sure it's the same everywhere that this change needs to be duplicated here, and in the other Settings Panel (that cute little gear icon available in the Start Menu, All Apps, and the Notification Panel.) || Look down and click on the blue 'Change advanced power settings'.  In the bar just above the box 'High Performance [Active]' should be showing.  Here we go, into the box, and we'll run right down the list, click the plus signs to open each level.  The settings are usually the last to open: || Open all +High Performance +'s to Require a password... select NO.||  +Hard disk, +Turn off, Setting: 20 minutes;  (Optional set to your choice time, but BEFORE the display is set to turn off.  No. I don't know why.)||  +Internet Explorer, +JavaScript Timer, MAXIMUM PERFORMANCE.||  Desktop Background... +Slideshow, +Setting: AVAILABLE.||  +Wireless Adapter... +Power Savings, select: MAXIMUM POWER SAVINGS (UNLESS you are wireless, then leave at default)||  +Sleep, +Sleep after: NEVER.|| +Allow hybrid sleep: ON.||  +Hibernate after: NEVER.||  Allow wake timers: ON;||  +USB, +Selective suspend: ENABLED.||  Power buttons & lid: +Power button: SHUT DOWN.|| +Sleep button: SLEEP.||  +PCI Express, +Link state...  select: OFF.||  +Processor Power...  +Minimum Proc... select: 10%.||  System cooling...  select: ACTIVE.||  Maximum Proc...  select: 100%.|| +Display, +Turn off, Setting: 30 minutes (Optional, see optional comment related to Hard Disk prior)|| +Adaptive brightness, select: ON.  Whew!  Off to settings. 

SETTINGS PANEL: (That cute little gear icon in the Start Menu, Notification Sidebar Panel, or wherever you may have put one.) 

1.) SYSTEM tile, select Power & Sleep in the left sidebar menu:  Screen, 'When plugged in,...'  Set: 30 minutes.|| Sleep, 'When plugged in,...'  Set: NEVER.  Back out to SETTINGS Main Menu.

2.) PRIVACY tile, select General at top:  In top to bottom order OFF || ON || ON || OFF.

3.) PRIVACY Tile, select Location, second down.  The only App I let use my location is Cortana.  Nothing should be using Geofencing unless you have a third party App doing it.  If something is, turn it off.  If that breaks it, uninstall the App and get a better one.

Below here in PRIVACY it's your personal choice what to leave or tweak.  I went through here with more an eye to what might be waking the computer, but you might want to remember your privacy too.  I made sure none of the installed Apps listed in CAMERA had access to my camera, since the ones shown had no conceivable connection to video or still photos.  I was right too, no loss of functionality.   

Hope this helps.  Play and adapt.  It took me months of playing '52 pickup' here, changing all these adjustments one at a time and in all possible different combinations, until I found the one that worked for me.  If you are ACPI compliant, most, if not all, should work for you.  I don’t think whose CPU matters.

 

John
