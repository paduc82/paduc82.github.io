tryx3 say:

Javier,

You are trying to run chdkdsk on the X drive in most of those attempts and I cannot see any changeover to the OS drive.  The last ones seem to be on your OS drive but as I cannot see when you changed to it I have no way of knowing if there is any useful information in any of the results.

You might need to work through dir c:, dir d:, ... until you see contents that match your OS drive.  The result will not be the X drive because that is the drive letter for the virtual drive that is running while you are in the Windows recovery environment [WRE].  If you booted into the WRE with a USB / DVD / CD then the USB / DVD / CD itself will also be found somewhere in your dir results in addition to the X virtual drive - it will look very similar to the results for your OS disk [similar folder structure] so you need to be careful to check the drive labels as well as the folder lists you see.

Then run your desired chkdsk commands on the correct drive.

I once had chkdsk spend most of a day giving a long list of results with thousands of errors but when I simply reinstalled Windows the drive was fine [it had all been Windows corruption not disk failure].  And that drive is still working well ten years later.
