How to add a textured Blender 3D model to your Office365 application - Microsoft Community

rowdogsa say:

How to add a textured Blender 3D model to your Office365 application

There is a challenge importing your own model with textures into Office365 applications. This quick guide will help you get your textured model from Blender into a supported Office365 application.    

  1. Create your model in Blender.
  2. UV unwrap your model. Project from view works best as this is how textures are applied in 3D Builder.
  3. Save your UV map and apply a custom texture in the image editor of your choice and then save the image. Applying existing diffuse image textures with nodes, baking and then saving that image is fine too (if you want lighting effects etc.). I find that if you are using a simple tileable image texture, it is easier to load this directly at step 11.
  4. Export your 3d model as a Wavefront (.obj) file. I haven't found any benefit in Write Materials to a .mtl file, so you can uncheck this option.
  5. Open Windows 10's 3D Builder and open a New Scene.
  6. Insert -> +Add -> Load object
  7. Browse to your model (.obj file) and Open.
  8. Choose your preferred File's units and Import Model.
  9. Click on your object to select it.
  10. Paint -> Texture -> Selected -> Load
  11. Browse to your saved UV map and Open.
  12. If you have a simple model (low poly), applying the texture will be easy. More complex models (high poly) are time consuming and result in larger .3mf files.
  13. Move the mouse over your model and click on the location you want to apply the texture to. Moving the mouse over your model previews the texture and clicking on your model places the texture in that position.
  14. You cannot simply apply the whole UV map to the model. You have to Move, Scale and Rotate the map to fit the faces on your object. Play with the Coverage slider too. It does help. Remember that low poly = more coverage and high poly = less coverage.
  15. Each time you have positioned your texture correctly on a face or faces, just rotate the view, move the mouse over an untextured space and repeat.
  16. Once you are satisfied with the textured model, Apply then Save as (Ctrl + Shift + S) 3MF Format (*.3mf).
  17. Now import this model into any of your applications that supports 3D model import and that's it!
