Is conditional formatting using a formula acceptable? If so:

Let's say the data are in A2:A50.

Select this range; I'll assume that A2 is the active cell within the selection.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > New Rule...

SaveReminder was an old Microsoft macro supplied (I think) with Word 2000 or

The macro creates and maintains a set of registry entries at
 
HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\14.0\Word\Save Reminder (where
14 is the Word version - here Word 2010).
 
If you close Word and delete that registry entry it will recreate the
default settings next time Word is run.
 
The application therefore requires write access to the registry.
 
You cannot convert a DOT template to a DOTM template by renaming it. If you
want it in DOTM format (though it should not be necessary) then open the DOT
file in Word and save it as a DOTM format template.

Hi Ron,

Since safe mode works fine, the issue may be related to add-ins installed in your Outlook, you can refer to the steps below to disable these add-ins one by one and find the root cause.

1. In the upper-left corner of Outlook, choose File > Options > Add-ins.

2. At the bottom of the View and manage Office Add-ins, make sure the Manage box shows COM Add-ins, and then choose Go.

3. As a precaution, when the COM Add-ins listing of your current add-ins opens, do one of the following:

  • Manually record the title of every selected add-in listed under Available Add-ins.
    or
  • Take a screenshot of the property sheet and save the image to a location of your choice.

4. After you've captured the titles of the selected add-ins, clear the selected check boxes one by one, and then choose OK.


CAUTION: Click a check box to clear it. Don't select an add-in and choose Remove. If you have administrator rights, you could actually delete the add-in. For this troubleshooting exercise, you want to disable, not delete, an add-in.

5. Choose File > Exit.

Then restart Outlook, repeat all the steps until you've re-enabled all of the original add-ins and found the specific add-in which caused the issue.

Meanwhile, I've noticed that you are using Outlook 2013 with a lower version. It is suggested that you upgrade Outlook or the Office suites to Office 2016 to check if the issue can be fixed.
