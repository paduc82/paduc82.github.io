Arrrghhhh.....DRM, the pain... the pain....
Here's what is going on.  DRM as you probably know is copy protection.  It works like this.  A DRM'd song is encrypted, and comes with a license file.  The license file has the decryption key, and contains usage rights (the right to burn a CD or not, for instance.)  The license file is tied to your PC and is installed in one of the windows sub-directories.  Without that license key, the application cannot decrypt the file to play it.  So... when you copy the track to a new PC,  or replace a hard drive, etc., that license gets left behind.
But, if there is a license server, you can sometimes recover the license.  That 's what happened to your zune pass songs... they slowly recovered when you logged into your zune account.
Your purchased tracks that are wma have DRM as well.  But the zune market has dropped DRM from purchased tracks, and gone all MP3 now.  (MP3 cannot support DRM.)  Before the transition, you could 'restore' your DRM'd purchased tracks from the marketplace.... you had to have this feature so you could upgrade PCs, replace hard drives...
But, those files are now gone, and the record labels do not allow suppliers to restore MP3s (not zune not amazon, not iToons... and probably not anyone.  you don't need this feature because you can back them up by copying them to a CD, a back up drive, etc.)
You can try calling zune support to see if you can get replacement MP3s, but I doubt they can do it.... this drm problem comes up over and over and I don't recall anyone saying they were able to get them replaced.

Let's say your range is A3:A32, just for illustration purposes.

Select this range. I'll assume that A3 is the active cell within the selection.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > Manage Rules...

 

Click New Rule...

Select "Use a formula to determine which cells to format".

Enter the formula =A3=MAX($A$3:$A$32)

Alternatively, use =A3=LARGE($A$3:$A$32,1)

Click Format... and specify the desired formatting.

Click OK twice to return to the Conditional Formatting Rules Manager.

 

Click New Rule...

Select "Use a formula to determine which cells to format".

Enter the formula =A3=LARGE($A$3:$A$32,2)

Click Format... and specify the desired formatting.

Click OK twice to return to the Conditional Formatting Rules Manager.

 

Click OK to finish.

Calculated fields always use the sum of the fields used in the formula, so totals will only be correct if you add or subtract fields and/or multiply fields with a constant. All other functions (minimum, average) will cause incorrect totals.
