john mcghie say:
Hi Joan:

I publish books direct out of Word.  That's what I do for a living, so I know this game quite well.

I never use Publishing Layout in Word, because it is not suitable for complex professional publishing.  It's designed for doing flyers for the church social and menus for the family restaurant: not 1,000-page documents...

Word can be "slow" for a number of reasons.  Let's run through the likely suspects:

1)  Memory...  You need a Mac Pro to do serious publishing.  16 GB of RAM is a nice round number.  You can do publishing on a laptop, but if you do, you need to be a little forgiving because things will take time.

2)  Other applications running.  Every application is taking a little bit of the machine resources, even if it's minimised.  Quit what you are not using.  And re-start the computer before taking on serious work, to allow it to do its internal house-keeping.

3)  Too many fonts, or problem fonts.  Try this procedure:  http://word.mvps.org/Mac/fontweeding.html

4)  Large graphics. As Bob says, reducing the graphics to only the pixels you intend to print is amazingly helpful.  If you intend to print on an office colour laser printer, the best it will do is about 200 dpi in colour. You can reduce all your images to that resolution in one click using File>Reduce file size.  Note: set your images to the correct size first, because once that command runs, unnecessary pixels have been removed, which means you cannot easily expand the pictures again.

In my experience, the most benefit will come from a combination of numbers 3 and 4.  If you have more than a few hundred fonts loaded, remove the ones you are not using.  Get rid of any fonts that Font Book has an issue with, these will slow down the machine a lot.

Down-size your pictures to the required size, either automatically using "Reduce File Size" or by re-sampling using a graphics program.

You will get a useful speed improvement by doing all of your formatting with styles.  It makes the inside of the document a lot simpler and easier to process.

Personally I have not noticed extra problems coming from blank layers.  However, lots of complex layers will eventually slow things down.  If you had five layers on each page, Word would have to "make up" the entire document five times for each page, just to work out what will be visible and what will not.  That will slow things down a bit.

Since I do this kind of thing quite often, I invested in serious hardware to do it with.  But then, I am a business, and time is money.  I am not suggesting that people doing occasional publishing should run off and purchase a Mac Pro :-)

Bob's suggestions about InDesign and QuarkExpress are right on the money for publishing material with high production values: those programs are designed to do this sort of thing, and their internal structure is adapted to that kind of work, so they do it much more efficiently.

Hope this helps

You may need to grant users the view only permission for the site and uncheck the following two permissions in the view only permission level.
