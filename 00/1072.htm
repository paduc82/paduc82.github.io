On columns - Microsoft Community

the0x539 say:

On columns

As some people may have noticed, ever since their introduction, tiles have quite an odd layout restriction.

They have to fill out columns with a varying height and the width of a wide tile. The rows in the columns are filled left to right, and small tiles form a group the size of a normal tile, also in a "Z".

This actually results in the possible Start configurations being a tiny subset of what they could be, and this restriction isn't present on Windows Phone, or to my knowledge, Xbox One, or else these two configurations would not be possible.

On Windows Phone, there is a layout restriction, but a sane one. If there's any entire blank rows, it simply moves the two ends together, closing the gap. I don't have any personal experience with any Xbox devices, so I can't comment there, but I presume it's similar.

Any posts on the internet about the column restriction are rare, which is somewhat understandable. However, it took me a little over a year to figure out why they exist, the reason being tablets. Tablets can rotate to a portrait orientation, where the height of the display is different. The column layout allows the icons to still populate the entire screen without being enlarged. However, I do not even feel that this is an optimal solution on these devices, as the tiles' position relative to each other changes, leading to a moderate amount of unfamiliarity. The solution makes absolutely no sense on non-rotatable devices, where it simply serves to add more restrictions. I believe the new menu is a fixed height, and I would be extremely happy to see this grid go away in favor of a Windows Phone style grid, though, of course, horizontally scrolling.
