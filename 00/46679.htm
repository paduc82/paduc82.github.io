lemp say:

Bose is not good at providing detailed specifications for its products, but I suspect that your Bose speaker uses a version of Bluetooth first released in 2011 called Bluetooth Low Energy (Bluetooth LE) which is also known as Bluetooth version 4.x.

Bluetooth LE uses less power than original Bluetooth and thus permits longer battery life.

The problem, however, is that the Bluetooth capability built into Windows 7 does not support Bluetooth 4.x.

You can confirm that this is likely your problem by doing the following.

In your computer, open Device Manager by clicking Start, typing Device Manager in the Search box, and pressing Enter.

Double-click the "Bluetooth" category to expand it.

Double-click the entry for your Bluetooth receiver, adapter, transceiver, or similar name to open its "Properties"

Click the "Advanced" tab.

Look for the "LMP" version.

If you have LMP version 6 or higher, you have Bluetooth 4.x or better; if your LMP version is less than 6, you don't.

If you have a relatively new smartphone, see if you can pair it with your Bose speaker.  For example, iPhone 4s and newer than  support Bluetooth 4.x

If your computer does not support Bluetooth 4.x and the speaker works with a smartphone, all is not lost.  Just buy an inexpensive USB Bluetooth 4.x adapter.  For example, https://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.aspx?Submit=ENE&IsNodeId=1&N=100158097%20600315449%20600079718

The adapter should come with a Windows 7 driver (either on a CD or downloadable).  If installing the driver doesn't automatically disable your built-in Bluetooth adapter, you may want to do that manually (see your computer's User Guide or disable it from Device Manager).

Error message when you log on to a Windows Vista-based or Windows 7-based computer by using a temporary profile: "The User Profile Service failed the logon. User profile cannot be loaded"


To resolve this issue, follow these steps:

  1. Delete the profile by using the Computer Properties dialog box. To do this, follow these steps:
    1. Click Start , right-click Computer , and then click Properties .
    2. Click Change settings .
    3. In the System Properties dialog box, click the Advanced tab.
    4. Under User Profiles , click Settings .
    5. In the User Profiles dialog box, select the profile that you want to delete, click Delete , and then click OK .
  2. Click Start
    , type regedit in the Start search box, and then press ENTER.
  3. Locate and then expand the following registry subkey:
    HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion\ProfileList
  4. Right-click the SID that you want to remove, and then click Delete .
  5. Log on to the computer and create a new profile.

Anyway, because of the poor positioning of the LifeCam's mic (on top of the camera, which is on top of my monitor), I chose to disable it in favor of the mic on my headset.  Not surprisingly, the LifeCam continued to work fine while I was still using the computer.  But after I had left the computer and it went into "sleep mode" the LifeCam stopped being recognized by my pc.  And of course I didn't use the camera right away when I came back, so by the time that I finally discovered the detection issue I had no reason to associate it with disabling the LifeCam's microphone.

 

After trying each of the troubleshooting methods at least twice and getting progressively more frustrated that they weren't working, I went to Amazon (where I bought my LifeCam) to return it and write a pretty scathing review.  But instead I serendipitously discovered that the LifeCam will not work without the mic enabled.  Pretty freaking stupid!

 

If this works, I urge Microsoft to include it on the troubleshooting page.
