AutoRecover only works when PPT is able to recognize that it's about to crash.  When it does that, it'll offer to restart itself and try to recover your work. If it doesn't do that, it means that it hasn't detected a crash (which I suspect would be the case when it just freezes).

ken sheridan say:

.....my "student" list contains all contacts, including those who have not taken any courses.



In that case you simply need a table for Visits which references the Contacts table:

Visits
....VisitID  (PK)
....ContactID  (FK)
....VisitDate
....VisitReason

though you might want to think about omitting the VisitReason column and having a separate Reasons table, and a table to model its many-to-many relationship type with Visits:

Reasons
....ReasonID  (PK)
....Reason

and to model the relationship type:

VisitReasons
....VisitID  (FK)
....ReasonID  (FK)

the primary key of this last table would be composite one made up of both columns.

By defining reasons with defined Reason values this aids analysis of the data.  By virtue of the many-to-many relationship type between Visits and Reasons each visit can be categorised by one or more reasons.

2. Office 365 is a subscription which means that you get the most recent versions of software, for as long as your subscription is active. Office 2016 is currently the most recent version, but it won't be forever, of course. :-)

In contrast, if you have a one-time purchase version (perpetual license) such as Office 2016 Home and Student, you won't get any future versions (unless you purchase them again). The advantage, of course, is that you don't have to continue paying for Office every month (or every year).
