brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Your iPad knows a protocol named Exchange ActiveSync, which it uses when you tell it to create an "Outlook.com" account. EAS is not mail-only. It can handle the contacts and calendar as well. Your iPad is not using an IMAP account. The "old" Outlook.com servers can handle POP, IMAP, and EAS.

Outlook for Mac 2016 doesn't know EAS, but it does know IMAP and Exchange. With the Office 365 platform that Outlook.com mailboxes are being moved to, you'll be able to use an Exchange account and have two-way synchronization for mail, contacts, and calendar on the OS X device. However, if your Outlook.com mailbox is on the "old" platform (the one that replaced Hotmail two years ago), then you can't use Exchange yet and since Outlook for Mac 2016 doesn't support EAS, that leaves you with IMAP.

Over the course of the next calendar quarter, all Outlook.com accounts will move to the Office 365 platform and both Windows and OS X systems will be able to use Exchange. Even Outlook for Mac 2011 should be able to connect with an Exchange account.

Some general things you can TRY........
