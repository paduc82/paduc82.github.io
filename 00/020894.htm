suzanne s. barnhill say:

This is a very common numbering scheme. In your document, in the paragraph that you want to be the first main heading, click the Multilevel List button in the Paragraph group on the Home tab. Select the pane that shows I. Heading 1, A. Heading 2, 1. Heading 3. This will apply the Heading 1 style, with roman numbering, to the current paragraph.

Very likely you will not like the formatting of Heading 1 or the placement of the number. You will attack this in two ways. To change the formatting of the paragraph (font, font size, font color, line spacing, Spacing Before/After--everything except any indents on the left side), right-click in the Heading 1 block in the Quick Style Gallery and choose Modify... Make what changes you can in the Modify Style dialog, then click Format and choose Paragraph or Font to make other changes. Do not select Numbering, however, as you can't change multilevel list numbering from this dialog.

To modify the numbering and indents, you must again click Multilevel List and choose Define New Multilevel List. Level 1 will be selected, and you can change the number formatting and style and position, plus tabs and indents, from this dialog (click More>> to expand the dialog and see all the options). While you're there, you can also modify these characteristics for Level 2 (linked to Heading 2), Level 3 (linked to Heading 3) and so on (you'll still have to modify the font and paragraph formatting for each heading through the Modify Style dialog).

By default, the "Style for following paragraph" for all heading styles is Normal. This means that when you press Enter at the end of one of your Heading 1 paragraphs, you're going to get a Normal paragraph. When you modify the heading style, you can change this. You can use Body Text, Body Text Indent, List Continue, or any other style (modified as you choose) as the following style, and you can have a different following style for every heading level. In an outline-type layout, it makes sense to use the List Continue series of styles, which already have progressive indents.

When you want a new heading paragraph, you can use the built-in keyboard shortcuts Alt+Left Arrow to promote the level and Alt+Right Arrow to demote. In addition,  you can use Tab and Shift+Tab if you have checked the box for "Set left- and first-indent with tabs and backspaces" at File | Options | Proofing | AutoCorrect Options | AutoFormat As You Type.

Find a lot more about multilevel lists at http://www.shaunakelly.com/word/numbering/numbering20072010.html.
