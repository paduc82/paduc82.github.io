Hi Essined,

 

Per my test, to grant you permission of creating Microsoft Flows on a SharePoint Online site within another domain, you may contact the Office 365 admin of this domain to change the following settings for you:

 

1. Add you as a guest user in their domain:
 a. Login the Office 365 admin center;
 b. Click "Admin centers" in the left panel > Azur Active Directory > Users > + New guest user > enter your account > Invite;

 

2. Enable external sharing:
 a. Go back to the Office 365 admin center > Admin centers > SharePoint;
 b. In the SharePoint admin center, click Sharing in the left panel;
 c. Check "Allow sharing only with the external users that already exist in your organization's directory" > Save;
 d. Click "Site collections" in the left panel of the SharePoint admin center;
 e. Check the site collection in which you need to create Microsoft Flows > click the "Sharing" button;
 f. Check "Allow sharing only with the external users that already exist in your organization's directory" > Save.
 
3. Add you in the "Members" group of this site collection:
 a. Open the site collection > click the gear icon > Site Settings > People and Groups;
 b. Select the "Members" group > New > enter your Office 365 account.

  

After the admin completed the steps above, please open your Outlook and accept the invitations. After that, you may go to the site collection and create Microsoft Flows with your Office 365 account.

          If Keys(J) = "Sent On :" Then
