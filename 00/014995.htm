jeeped say:

... J5 is a date and time, Name List is just date.


Excel deals with dates and time with a day being 1 and time as a fraction of a day, e.g. one hour = 0.04167 or 1/24. A date is the number of days since Jan. 1, 1900, so today is 40882 and today at noon is 40882.5,

You can pull the date portion of a date/time by grabbing the integer (e.g. whole number) and discarding the fraction. Try,

=IF(ISBLANK(J5),"",IF(COUNTIF('Name List'!$AN$5:$AY$11,INT(J5)),1,IF(COUNTIF('Name List'!$AN$12:$AY$18,INT(J5)),2,IF(COUNTIF('Name List'!$AN$19:$AY$25,INT(J5)),3,IF(COUNTIF('Name List'!$AN$26:$AY$32,INT(J5)),4,IF(COUNTIF('Name List'!$AN$33:$AY$39,INT(J5)),5,"N/A"))))))

There may be a better cyclic approach to your formula since the ranges appear to be in a repeating set. Let me know if this modification to your formula works out.

Hi eesa000,

Because the front text box has text in it, you cannot fade that text box as the text will also fade . . .

Move the front text box to one side

Choose Insert Shape from the Insert Ribbon

Put that shape on top of the first text box and resize to to the same size

Set the fill color to balck

Set the transparency to about 50% using the slider

Right click the second text box and choose 'Bring to Front - Bring to front

Then put that text box back on top of the new shape

If needs be, adjust the transparency of the shape until you are happy that you can comfortably read the text on the top text box . . .
