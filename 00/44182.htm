considerate_guy say:

Are you sure you need to split the video? When you capture video from a mini-DV cassette, it ends up being saved in a format that isn't compressed. You end up with files that take about 13.5 GB per hour of video.

A video DVD holds upwards of 1.5 to 2.5 hours of video on a 5 GB disc.

Given your numbers, it seems like you only have about 35 minutes of video to fit on this disc and it should easily fit. So how is this magic accomplished, that you can get a 7.8 GB video onto a 5 GB video disc? It's because a video DVD transcodes/compresses video using the MPEG-2 codec which is much more efficient. So you should really have no problem creating a video DVD from your footage.

You definitely don't want to just copy the captured video onto the DVD. First, it won't fit, as you noted. And second it won't be readable by a standard consumer DVD player. So you want to use software to create a video DVD in the right format.
