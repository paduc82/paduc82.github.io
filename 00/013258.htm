What's considered a paragraph? - Microsoft Community

jessie4259 say:

What's considered a paragraph?

Hello,

I'm taking my Word 2007 Cert exam next week, and as I was studying, I thought of an odd question I'm hoping you can help. In Microsoft Word 2007, what is considered a paragraph? For example, if a question asks to add a 12pt spacing after every paragraph, is that every line that has a paragraph mark following it (basically the entire document) or is it only those that are 1 or more sentences long and have the look of a paragraph and are not a heading, bullet, or address?

Please help, thanks!

 

jay freedman say:

To expand just a bit on Graham's answer, the "look of a paragraph" has nothing to do with it, nor does the number of sentences, nor do headings, bullets, addresses, or anything else you can throw into the mix.

You could use manual line breaks (Shift+Enter) to separate text into different lines, but they would still be part of the same paragraph. This is often done with multiple-line addresses, specifically to avoid having extra space after each line that would be created by the Space After setting of the paragraph format.

A paragraph could have no sentences, one sentence, or a thousand sentences, but until you come to a  paragraph mark it's all still the same paragraph.

The items in a bullet list are usually separate paragraphs. That is, each item is ended by a paragraph mark, so it is a paragraph. Similarly, a heading is usually ended by a  paragraph mark, so it is a paragraph. What makes them different is thestyle applied to the paragraph.
