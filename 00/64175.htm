There are few settings which you need to change to receive fax automatically or manually.

To receive faxes automatically

1.    Click the Start button , in the search box type Fax, and then click Windows Fax and Scan.

2.    To use Fax view, click Fax at the bottom of the left pane.

3.    Click the Tools menu, and then click Fax Settings.  If you're prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.

4.    Click the General tab, and then, under Send/receive options, select the Allow device to receive fax calls check box.

5.    Click Automatically answer after X rings, select the number of times you want the phone to ring before the modem answers, and then click OK.

To receive faxes manually

If you aren't set up to receive faxes automatically, Windows notifies you during each incoming call. To answer the call as a fax:

1.    Click the Start button , in the search box type Fax, and then click Windows Fax and Scan.

2.    To use Fax view, click Fax at the bottom of the left pane.

3.    On the toolbar, click Receive a Fax Now. The Fax Status Monitor displays the progress of the fax.

stefanfb say:
Hi klaro,
cookies are there for saving information about you. This can be used to track your internet activity over different Websites.
But it also has useful functions. For example if you login to a site, close the Browser and visit the site again the next day you do not need to login again because your session was stored in a cookie.
Totally disabling Coookies isnt a good pactice as you found out already. A good choice would be to accept fist party cookies and block third party cookies. If you do not worry that you have to login to sites more often you can also delete all cookies when you close your browser. You will find this option in Edge by choosing Settings
Privacy -> “Choose what to clear” under Clear browsing data. Select the option to clear cookies and enable the option to “always clear this when I close the browser.”

Hello klaro,
did you choose the "Prompt" setting? That might render your internet experience unusuable because you have to confirm lots of cookie requests manually. Just keep the first Party Cookies on "Accept" and the third Party Cookies on "Block".
