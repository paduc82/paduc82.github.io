jay freedman say:
Something, perhaps an add-in, is preventing changes from being saved to your Normal.dotm template. (That's where "default" settings are supposed to be stored.)

Locate your Normal.dotm template. The most likely place is a hidden folder within your profile; to reach it, paste the following line into the address box of Windows Explorer and press Enter:

%appdata%\Microsoft\Templates

Right-click the Normal.dotm file and choose Open (don't double-click it or click New, as that just creates a document based on the template).

Make the change directly in the Normal.dotm -- there's no need to click Default this time. In the body of the template, type any character and then backspace to delete it -- this tells Word that the file needs to be saved -- and then save it and close it.

teylyn say:

Excel stores dates as numbers. There's nothing to convert. Formatting is how numbers can be shown as dates. But internally, the value will still be a number. 

If you get errors when referencing a cell formatted as a date, the problem is not the format of the cell, but more likely errors in the formula.
