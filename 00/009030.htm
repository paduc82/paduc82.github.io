grschinon say: The frequency bands are the main thing to look out for.

IIRC, Telstra uses the American frequencies.

Basically you need to know the following:

GSM frequencies in the USA are 850 and 1900MHz. GSM frequencies for international versions are 900 and 1800MHz.

3G frequencies in the USA are 950 and 1900MHz. 3G frequencies for international versions are 900 and 2100MHz.

So, you need to know what frequencies your operators in Australia use and choose your phone accordingly.

Note that many phones are now at least tri-band GSM, if not quad-band.

Tri-band phones sold in the USA usually work on the 850, 1800 and 1900MHz bands. Those sold elsewhere usually work on 900, 1800 and 1900MHz bands.

You also want to take warranty issues into consideration. The warranty on a phone bought outside Australia will not be valid in Australia.
