bill manville say:

Assumptions:

- the employees are in Sheet1 starting at A2 with the corresponding manager in B2
- the list ends at the first blank cell in column A

- each employee has a file in a folder C:\Employees with a file name like "John Smith.txt"

- you want to move it to C:\Managers\Amy Jones\John Smith.txt, where Amy Jones is John Smith's manager

 

[as you can see, the more detail you can give us of the requirement the better; otherwise we have to make guesses which will be wrong]

 

Sub MoveEmployeeFiles()
  Dim stFrom As String
  Dim stTo As String
  Dim stFile As String
  Dim stManager As String
  Dim lRow As Long
  stFrom = "C:\Employees"

  stTo = "C:\Managers"
  With Sheets("Sheet1").Range("A1").CurrentRegion
    For lRow = 2 To .Rows.Count
      stFile = .Cells(lRow, "A") & ".txt"
      If Dir(stFrom & "\" & stFile)="" Then
        MsgBox stFile & " not found!"
     Else
       stManager = .Cells(lRow, "B")
       If Dir(stTo & "\" & stManager, vbDirectory)="" Then MkDir stTo & "\" & stManager
       FileCopy stFrom & "\" & stFile, stTo & "\" & stManager & "\" & stFile
     End If

    Next
  End With
End Sub

Repair the disk
The following procedure is the equivalent of booting from your system DVD and doing a disk repair, minus the hassle of finding the plastic disc:

  1. Restart your Mac
  2. As soon as you hear the Boing! sound, press Command S and keep holding until…

  7. The Mac will reboot.
  8. Check if the Documents folder is once again available.
