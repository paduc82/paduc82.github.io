brenda woollard say:

>>>>>On Item #9, do I enter the product key from the Windows 10 Home edition that was initially installed on my computer (the one that was the original free upgrade from Windows 7 Home to Windows 10 Home)?<<<<<

When you upgrade a computer with Windows 7 Home to Windows 10 Home, there is no product key.  If you used a third party program to see a product key, it comes back with a generic key that all upgraders get.  Your computer gets an entitlement to use Windows 10.

Your computer was running Windows 7 Home.  You upgrade to Windows 10 Home.  When you upgrade, the system sees whether or not you are upgrading a genuine copy of Windows 7 Home.  The system then gives your computer an entitlement to use Windows 10 Home. 

The upgrade process embeds information onto your hardware (motherboard?), that your computer had a genuine Windows 7 Home operating system, and then embeds the entitlement to Windows 10 Home onto your hardware.  Microsoft's activation servers reads the embedded information and activates your operating system.  System builders who purchase OEM versions and folks who buy retail versions get product keys.
