cindy meister say:

What do you mean by "off"?


I mean the information Word stores "in" paragraph mark may be slightly scrambled. Another name for it is damaged document or document corruption. The problem is especially known in the old binary file formats (*.doc).

If a document "goes bad" it's often due to the information stored in this area getting scrambled up. It's not something you can see or correct as an end-user. And it could eventually lead to not being able to open the document at all.

If nothing speaks against it, you could also try saving the document to the new file format, or possibly to RTF. Running a file conversion can sometimes get Word to "unscramble" the storage.

Cindy Meister, VSTO/Word MVP
