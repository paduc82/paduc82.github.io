a. user say:

Hi,

Your conversations are saved on your hard drive for a period of time, which you can specify in your history settings. The last 30 days of your conversation history are also stored in the cloud, so you can access it when you're signed in to Skype on any device.

Skype history is stored locally on your device. Once you do restore factory settings, PC system will refresh and will delete all the applications along with their conversation history (unless you do back-up). That being said, I'm afraid to say that there's no other option to recover your Skype history at this time. 

In future, you can back up your history if for any reason you need to make changes on your computer. You just need to copy the main database file in your Skype Name folder. 

  1. Press and hold the Windows key  on your keyboard, then press R to bring up the Run window.
  2. Type %appdata%\Skype into the Run window and press OK. 
  3. Navigate to the folder named after your Skype Name.
  4. Right click on the folder itself and select Copy. This will copy the entire folder including all its contents.
  5. Save the folder to an external media device.
  6. Replace the entire folder (named after your Skype Name) with the folder that you saved in step 4 and 5.

erdeslawe say:

Running the Media Creation Tool after performing the Upgrade to Windows 10 will re-download all of Windows 10 upgrade files, which will in turn overwrite the critical upgrade files that enable you to carry out a roll-back. This is not as minor as Camelslayer seems to believe, as there is little anyone can do to prevent someone from taking an incorrect course of action when they are the System Administrator.

I know this sounds harsh, but you probably destroyed the capacity of the system to carry out the roll-back procedure by overwriting the required management files; and even though your Windows.old file may still be present it can't now be accessed by the system.  If you wanted to download a 'backup' of the Windows 10 system installation media you should really have downloaded an ISO and then used that to create DVD/USB media; but that's a very hard lesson to learn at this stage.

You may be in a situation where the only possible course of action is a complete re-installation of your original OS, Software and data, assuming you have all the necessary media and data backups.  However, I would suggest that you should first try contacting the Microsoft Help Desk to see if there is any possible way to get your Windows 10 installation working correctly.

Good luck, whichever course of action you decide on.

How to back up and restore the registry in Windows

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/322756/
