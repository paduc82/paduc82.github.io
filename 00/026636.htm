TY

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

You created a POP account.  By default, POP accounts (in any POP client) download and delete messages form the server.  If you don't want this to happen, you must tell the client so when you create the account.  In Outlook, that's on the Advanced tab of the account properties "More Settings" dialogue.  Tools>Account Settings.  On the E-mail tab, select your account, click Change, then More Settings.  Select the Advanced tab.  Check the box labeled "Leave a copy of messages on the server".  If you had done this when you created the account, your mail would have remained on the server.

Now that the horse is out of the barn, as long as you are restricted to POP, you're out of luck.  You cannot move the messages back to the server.  You can email them (as attachments is best) to yourself but they'll come from you not the original sender.  If they're attachments, you can open the attachment in order to access the original sender information.

If you can set up an IMAP account, you can move things back.  If you can create an IMAP account, you can then copy all the messages in your default Inbox to the IMAP account's Inbox to put it back on the server exactly as it was before.  Check with Road Runner support to find out if they support IMAP.  My cursory research indicates they don't, however.

Another thing to try is to create a Google Mail account, which can be IMAP, and copy the data to the gmail Inbox.  From there you may be able use Road Runner's web interface to your RR mailbox and see if it has settings that will allow it to POP messages from another mail provider.  You can then POP the messages from your gmail mailbox and they should move back to the RR mailbox, although I don't know how the sender and recipient fields will transfer.  At least you'd have your messages back on RR.  Alternatively, you may be able to have the gmail mailbox forward the messages to your RR mailbox, but again, I don't know how the sender/recipient data will appear afterward.
