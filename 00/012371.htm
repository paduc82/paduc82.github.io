MyPhone versus Mobile Device Center - Microsoft Community

jimvw2 say:

MyPhone versus Mobile Device Center

Can I use MyPhone INSTEAD of Mobile Device Center to keep my Contacts, Appointments and Task List up to date and synchronized?
julie fka profjulie say:

The short answer is "Yes, you can." But it really depends on what your needs are.

If you are accustomed to using Outlook on your computer and you wish to continue using Outlook on your computer, then you'll still want to sync your windows phone to your computer or else you'll wind up having to manually update the computer (with no syncing to the phone) AND manually update the phone or My Phone web page (when you only sync between MyPhone and the windows phone).

If you set up a lot of recurring appointments or tasks, you won't be able to set up these kinds of events on the My Phone web portal but you can sync them from your phone.  So you'd set these kinds of events up on the phone and sync them to the web page.  The recurring events will not display as recurring events on the web page, but they are stored as such even though you won't be able to verify this by viewing how they are appear on the web page.

The My Phone service is designed to provide an online backup option for windows phone users, with some perks (ie, sharing photos between social networking sites, premium "find my phone" type services).  If you lose your windows phone or break the phone, your data is safely stored on the My Phone website until you purchase a new windows phone or repair the phone.

There are a couple of limitations that you need to be aware of:

  • The data on the My Phone web page cannot be converted to be used on another non-windows phone.  So if you plan to switch to another type of phone (Android, iPhone), it won't be easy to sync the data or convert the data to a usable format that your new phone can handle.
  • The data on the My Phone web page cannot be downloaded to your computer.
  • The My Phone web page does not display recurring events or tasks properly and you cannot input recurring events or tasks on the My Phone website.  You'll have to manually key these kinds of items to the phone and take it on faith that they are being stored as recurring events or tasks. (Already mentioned, but stated again). 
For me, My Phone does not replace the need to sync between my computer and my windows phones...
