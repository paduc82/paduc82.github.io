You can change this.

Assuming that Access "does" see the column as yes/no then simply open up the linked table in design view (ignore the read-only message).

henry huang- [o365] say:

Hi Brian,

Yes, it's possible. Once the MX record is pointed to the gateways, the inbound emails will be routed to the gateways first. In this situation, you need a inbound connector between Office 365 and the gateway to receive the emails in Office 365.
For outbound emails, they are stilled handled by Office 365.

Regarding how to configure the inbound connector, it depends on the detailed deployment of your organization. Have you deployed hybrid? If yes, please let us know the
information including the version of servers, and the configuration of your hybrid mail flow.

Is there a way to only have the 2 on-prem domains utilize the connector, and have the other 24 just go out Exchange Online (if a cloud mailbox), (or be routed to Exchange Online if an on-prem) mailbox.

henry huang- [o365] say:

Hi Brian,

In your organization, the incoming emails for the 24 domains are routed to EOP first. If the mailbox is in on-premises, then EOP forwards the email to on-premises. If the mailbox is in the Cloud, the email will also be forwarded to on-premises and then routed back to Office 365. This is what Centralized Mail Transport does.

For the 2 domains, incoming emails are routed to on-premises directly. For this mail flow, you don't need any connector.
