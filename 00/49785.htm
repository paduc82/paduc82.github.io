I'm troubleshooting myself right now & came across your post! 

Nikolaos-

Give you a run down on how I start my Network config & setup / TROUBLESHOOTING sessions. Usually takes 5-10 mins however is a tremendous time-saver in the long run. 

1st - plug-in all devices 

2nd - Through performing a RESET on all computers or devices and POWER-CYCLE (unplug power cord) to routers, gateways, & switches, the devices should update drivers or prompt for specific driver configuration changes. 

3rd - Go to your main terminal(computer/laptop/server) that is plugged into your router/gateway. 

4th - At this main terminal, run your command prompt (Powershell). Then perform in order ipconfig /all, ipconfig /release, ipconfig /release6 (very important-all ethernet connections), ipconfig /flushdns,....

5th - Now, perform a HARD RESET on your particular router/gateway. Usually the red button located on the back your device. PRESS a few times then proceed to HOLD IT DOWN for 15-30 sec count. 

6th - Lastly, type your default router (such as on my Netgear it is routerlogin.com). Or if on a gateway look for your specific device's tag for the URL. 

7th - OPEN your command prompt again, type ipconfig /registerdns. Then run the following commands inorder, ipconfig /renew, ipconfig /renew6

8th - Now visit your URL found on the back of your router/gateway.

All should be good. Not sure if it will help. Let me know how it all comes together.
