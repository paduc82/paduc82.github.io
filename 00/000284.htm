AFAIK this is by design.

 

Excel lets you copy cells which are not contiguous but it allows pasting only on a contiguous selection.

 

Even without filter you can copy, say cells A1, A3, and A5 and paste anywhere... you will get three contiguous cells in the same column. You can not paste it into, say B1, B3, and B5. Same logic is extended to copying and pasting filtered cells.

select the destination column

type = and click the first cell of the source column (or type the address of the corresponding cell in source column)

press CTRL-ENTER to copy the formula to other cells in the filtered range....

boblarson say:

One thing that may help speed it up is changing your declarations to explicit ones:

Dim db As DAO.Database
Dim mytable As DAO.Recordset

 

Also, I notice that you aren't opening an ordered recordset using a query.  You are just opening the table and then iterating through.  I don't know if you knew this or not but your cum is potentially wrong at times because Access does not store records in the table in the order that they were input.  So, you are running a big risk of some of those records being returned out of order in the recordset and then your information applied incorrectly.  You need to open a QUERY with a specific sort order in order to guarantee that the records are returned in the correct order.  You need a field to sort on which would give you the correct order.

Bob Larson, Former Access MVP (2008-2010)

    Dim objApp As Outlook.Application
    Dim objNS As Outlook.NameSpace
    Dim objFolder As Outlook.MAPIFolder
    Dim objDummy As Outlook.MailItem
    Dim objRecip As Outlook.Recipient
    Dim objAppt As Outlook.AppointmentItem
    Dim strMsg As String
    Dim strName As String
    On Error Resume Next
   
    strName = "Calender name"
   
    Set objApp = CreateObject("Outlook.Application")
