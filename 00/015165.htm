jay freedman say:
If the Page Setup dialog has been used properly, the footnotes will stay within the margins without any adjustment when they overflow to the next page.

Excuse me if I include more details than you need in the following steps.

  1. On the Page Layout tab of the ribbon, click the dialog launcher (the small arrow) in the bottom right corner of the Page Setup group.
  2. On the Margins tab in the Page Setup dialog, set the Multiple Pages dropdown to "Mirror margins". Then set the sizes of the four margins and the gutter. It should look like this:


Click OK.

Also, with the cursor in the footnote text, look at the markers on the horizontal ruler to verify that there are no left or right paragraph indents; the markers should be at the points where the white and gray backgrounds of the ruler meet.

To ask a new question (which you really should do when you have a different topic), click the word Participate in the navigation bar near the top of the page (just below the big "Community" label) and click Ask a Question. Use the dropdowns to choose the correct forum and topic. You don't have to address it to an MVP or to any particular person -- the same people come back here almost every day.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
To add to what Jay has said, note that, unless you are arithmetic-impaired, setting a gutter is not necessary. If you're using mirror margins already, you can just set the Inside margin to an amount equal to the outside margin plus the gutter. In Jay's example, the Outside margin would be 1" and the Inside margin 2". It will achieve exactly the same result.
