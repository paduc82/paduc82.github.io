nathan roberts (sunknath) say:

Hey there,

Glad you found where your pictures were. To move them out of the folder, you can right click on them and use the 'send to' option to send them to the appropriate location.

You could also select them all, by holding Ctrl while right clicking onto them. Once you have selected the ones you wanted, you can drag them into the quick access folders. If you hold the photos over the quick access folder, they should display sub folders underneath. You can then select the correct sub folder you wish to save them into.

The third method, which is the method I use a lot, is to open the another file explorer in the location you wish to send the photos to. Then select the photos you wish to move, and drag them into the other window. This will copy them over to the new location. If you wanted them removing from the camera, you could then delete them from the DCIM and empty your recycling bin.

Just wondering, were you talking about the AutoPlay when you plugged in your camera?

If you did, you can turn it on by:

  1. Going to Cortana - Next to the start icon on the task bar
  2. Click into Cortana, and search for 'AutoPlay settings' - using keyboard
  3. Click onto 'AutoPlay Settings' which should open the settings in the settings app
  4. You can then change your settings from there.
