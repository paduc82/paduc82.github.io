how do i take apart the lifecam vx6000 - Microsoft Community

barbarajurgens say:

how do i take apart the lifecam vx6000

I want to adapt the lifecam vx 6000 for use with my telescope. How do i remove the base and how do I attach the webcam to my scope?

everythingastr0n0my say:

I'm sorry it took so long and I know you most likely don't even care anymore, but I happened to have this and also wanted to take it apart for the same reason. Here's what  I did, just by playing around.

1.) The screw on the backside, right under the USB cable, needs to be taken out.

(Optional) Once the screw on the backside is out, take off the little grey rubber tabs on the side where the base connects with a small flat head screwdriver. When those two tabs are off, use a phillips screwdriver to take those two small screws out.

2.) You should now be able to yank off the front end of the whole webcam off, exposing the chip and lens. If you aren't able to get it off, try using a small flat head screwdriver to pry it off. 

3.) You are going to want to take off that lens that moves in and out to focus the LifeCam. So what I encountered was a stiff camera lens sticking out and not budging one bit. If yours happens to be stiff like mine was, you just need give it a little bang on the table or something similar. (Just to get it started) 

4.) You should now be able to twist off the camera lens and see the actual camera sensor in the back of the chipboard. 

5.) Simply line up your "Call" button and snap back together. 

Hope this helps! I was looking online on how to take mine apart, but couldn't find anything. I just did it my self. Also, I figured, let's not leave this question open forever. So I answered it for ya. Clear skies! 

-Ben
