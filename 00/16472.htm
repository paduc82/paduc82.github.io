You can also get to Calendar or Skydrive in the same manner.

Above the search box at the top of the Contact list on the left, is a filter option which says "All ". 
If you click on this you should see the groups you created in the past.  
Click on any of these to filter your list to just the contacts in that group.

Selecting one or many Contacts:
If you click on a contact name in the list, the details for that contact will appear on the right.  
Alternatively, if you click on the check box to the left of the contact picture, you can build up a list of selected contacts on the right by clicking on the check box to the left of multiple contacts.

Adding/Removing from Group membership:
Whether you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Groups.

Your selected contacts will then be updated to be added or removed from the groups you chose as appropriate.

Delete
When you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Delete.  
If you click on Delete and click Delete in the confirmation box, the selected contacts will be deleted.

Edit:

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
As Rich indicated, there is no such feature in Word. Realistically, it's best to properly prepare images before inserting them into a document rather than expecting a word processing program to enable such processes.

IMO, you'd be far better off to use a decent graphics app to save new versions of the images scaled to the required dimensions. Once the images are prepared, replace those in the document with their appropriate counterparts. Depending on how many images are involved it may be a fair amount of work, but it will be far simpler & more effective than trying to force it in a program not intended for the purpose.

If you must do it in Word you can select more than one floating image at a time by shift-clicking each image. Then go to Format> Object - Size to make the change. This won't work if an image is In Line, though -- You cannot select multiple In Line objects or select an In Line object & floating objects at the same time.

Just to make certain that you're aware of it, unless the images all have the same aspect ratio to begin with, changing them to identical dimensions most likely will induce distortion to at least some of them.

sam mathai chacko say: You can define the print area using a dynamic named range. All you need to do is find the last row. One way to do this is to use COUNTA in an offset formula if your data is continuous without any blank cells in between.

So for example, if you know that your column B will always have filled cells, till the last row (whichever that is), you can edit the Print_Area in the names manager, and instead of the static print area already assigned, you can use a dynamic formula in its place like so

=OFFSET(Sheet2!$B$1,0,0,MAX(1,COUNTA(Sheet2!$B$1:$B$4000)),1)

Here you are assuming that your data doesn't go beyond 4000 rows. At the same time, you are counting the values in range B1:B4000, so depending on how many cells are filled, the counta will feed the correct number in to the row height parameter of the offset function, thus making your print area dynamic. Make sure you keep formula calculation to automatic.

For non contiguous ranges also you can find the last filled row. Post back if that's what you need.

How do I hide/Disable Microsoft Word's built in templates from showing up

We do not want to see all the Microsoft Templates from appearing when we click on My Templates.  We are a law firm and have a lot of our own templates.  In 2003 it was easy to hide this but we can't figure it out for 2007 and 2010 which we are slowing upgrading users.   We have already given everyone a direct link button the My Templates on their QAT, but it still shows all the tabs and templates to all the Microsoft templates that we do not need to see. 

Can you help guide me please?  Thank you

kim stomboli say:

I just deleted my last reply that said I fixed it by removing the subfolder 1033 because all those darn templates came back.  But I have found the fix and IT has roled it out in a group policy update and we all no longer have those templates showing up for all 2007 and 2010 users.  My way didn't work because it was still cached somewhere.  This was the permanent fix. 

Charles, I found the fix in a google search that pointed me to your website, LOL.  So thank you very much.  It said to do this the registry tweak below. This has completed fixed out issue:  

Word 2007-2010

This is a registry tweak. If you don't already know how to do edit the registry with regedit don't attempt this. Backup your registry before making any modifications.

With all Office components closed, use regedit to expand to

HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT\Installer\Components\8F622368F04 F7B849A7B2021EE668F21

Select individual keys and rename them to remove individual templates, or rename the top level key (8F622... etc) to remove them all, including the tabs. Add the word "NOT" to the start of the key to make this easily reversible. (This will put it at the end of the alphabetical order.)

Restart your computer. (I expect, but do not know, that Word 2013 works the same way.)
