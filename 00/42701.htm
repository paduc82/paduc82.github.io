arminatorx say:

Some routers don't play well when two Xbox consoles try to do multiplayer together inside the same network. 

So to test, that your partner is able to use your shared Xbox Live Gold membership, have your partner simply start a Party. A gold membership is required to start a party, and if you successfully shared your Gold membership to her console, she should be able to start the party. 

If that doesn't work, sign in with your own Gold account, and again check in the settings under Personalization that your partner's Xbox One S is indeed your Home Console. 

If she is able to create a party, go to each of your console's network settings, and under the advanced network settings, try to set a different Xbox Live Port for your consoles. If you want to play with two consoles in the same household, each console should use a different Port to listen to Xbox Live data. 

If your router supports UPnP, you should be done (but just restart all your consoles and the router for good measure). 

If the router doesn't support UPnP, you might have to set up port forwarding rules for each custom port of your consoles, and forward the respective ports to the proper console's IP.
