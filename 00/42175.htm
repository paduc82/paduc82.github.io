Alot of the times when someone gets disconnected from xbox live it has to do with the internet speed dropping all of a sudden.  Xbox live requires that the MTU value be above a certain point or else it will disconnect itself.  I advise that you check the following:  

  • Is your internet speed rated high enough to be able to use xbox live?  
  • Is your internet provider having any maintenance or area trouble?

wunders say: To delete a computer from the network, you simply disconnect it from the network.

When you look under "My Network Places", the computers you see there are the computers that are known to the "Master Browser", which is one elected computer on your network that monitors comings and goings of computers on the network and sends out a list whenever a computer asks.   If a computer disappears off the network suddenly, it may take up to 15-20 minutes before the Master Browser realizes it is gone and automatically deletes it from the list.  Anytime a computer comes on the network, it announces itself and gets back on the list.

Failure to connect to a computer you see there is another matter entirely.  It is one thing for a computer to announce its presence, it's another to accept an incoming connection from another computer.  Most failures in this category are due to firewalls or sharing / permissions setting on the target (server) computer.

If you don't want to see a computer on the network, then change its network name to end in a dollar sign ($) and it will not show up on the network.
