Hi Jenny. I'm Greg, an installation specialist and 8 year Windows MVP, here to help you.

Unfortunately, Windows Live Essentials are discontinued and actively being shut down now. So I would transition to the Windows 10 Photos and Mail apps which are better and work great, anyway.

I would certainly disagree with you that the Windows 10 Photos app is better than Windows Photo Gallery. For a start:

  • It doesn't support the use of industry-standard XMP metadata tags, which in turn means:
  • The Truth is no longer in the File - the "Truth is in the File" was the mantra that Microsoft used for years for the value of photo metadata.
  • You can't search on user-defined Tags
  • People Tags in the Photos app are local to the app database on each PC - they are not part of the metadata. This means you can't share tags across devices and with OneDrive
  • The Video Creation feature in the app is slow, limited and buggy (it crashes frequently) - Windows Live Movie Maker still runs rings around it.

I'll give you that the Mail app is not bad - but it still does not support the use of contact groups, which is something that Windows Live Mail had from day one.

I find it truly depressing that, after five years (these apps were first introduced with Windows 8), these apps are still not on a par with the applications in the Windows Live Essential suite.
