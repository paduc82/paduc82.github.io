davidf2 say:

DavidF

Sorry, I should of been more clear. The blue dots are from a publisher template, I just changed the color from white to blue so you can see them. It's in greeting cards>holidays>Christmas 20

That helps.

The object is a line so it will have only two grab points unlike the rectangle of a text box. You can grab either end and rotate it in any direction you want or extend or shorten it. You can right click it > Format autoshape > Colors and lines and change the size of the dots by changing the weight. And as you discovered you can change the color, etc. in that dialog.

To be more precise if you grab one of the grap points and drag that end to something other than absolutely horizontal or vertical then you will get the 4 grab points and rotation point like a text box. I find that more difficult to work with than just the two grab points at the end of the line. Tip: Hold down your shift key as you drag the end of the line to extend or shorten it and the line will remain horizontal or vertical.

As per having it 'snap to' first go to Page Design > Layout group and check Align to Guides and Objects. Now as you drag the line across the page you will see when it 'snaps to' guide lines that appear as you drag the line or to an object as you approach it. This 'snap to' function changed as of Publisher 2010 if you are remembering the way the older versions of Publisher worked. You can of course create visible guide lines to help you layout the page.

It also will help if you go to View > Show group and check Boundaries. Now you will be able to see the boundaries on the text box and other objects without a mouseover. It also makes it apparent when you drag your line or other objects to 'snap to' another object. I don't know why the default was to leave this unchecked as I don't see how you can layout a page without seeing the boundaries of all the design elements on the page.

And finally you can center the line or any other object on the page. Select the line > Drawing Tools and format > Arrange group > click the down arrow beside Align and choose Relative to Margin Guides and then go back to Align down arrow once again and all the options will be activated.

HTH and Merry Christmas!

DavidF
