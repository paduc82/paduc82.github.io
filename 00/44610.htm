yleclerc say:

Computer internal devices all generate "heat."  Most computers manufacturer add temperature controller fans on important internal devise or locations.  These will spin faster when "hot" temperatures are detected.  With the fans, there are usually "ventilation holes" needed to push this hot "air" out.  Unfortunately these holes let in dust and "other debris' into the fans, thus making them "spin" harder and faster.  This is normal.  You might want to take your computer to a local repair centre that you trust and ask for a internal "cleaning out" of this "dust."

amaranthe say:

Don't put off having the computer cleaned and checked out for fan failure as Yleclerc suggested.  Often you will have multiple fans in computer and if you are having the loud noise that cuts out it could be the bearings are failing.  Eventually, the fan could stop spinning and cause your processor or power supply to overheat which could cause physical damage to your components.
