Hi Deano,

On Windows 10, you can share an internet connection with other devices by turning your computer into a wireless hotspot. However, your computer must have at least one wireless adapter to make it all work. Here are the steps on how to create a wireless hotspot on Windows 10:

  1. Select the Start button, then select Settings > Network & Internet > Mobile hotspot.
  2. For Share my Internet connection from, choose the Internet connection you want to share.
  3. Select Edit > enter a new network name and password > Save.
  4. Turn on Share my Internet connection with other devices.
  5. To connect on the other device, go to the Wi-Fi settings on that device, find your network name, select it, enter the password, and then connect.

Once you have completed the steps, you can connect up to 8 devices on your computer. users can simply connect as they would normally do to access to a Wi-Fi network.

Hi there,

DISCLAIMER: If you break your computer, I am not responsible, etc.

If you are comfortable with reformatting your computer, I strongly recommend the below. I have done this twice now for these reasons:

Windows 10 killed the hotfix, and I could not get my keyboard to work at all - even when telling Windows 10 it was a Japanese keyboard it would use the US layout and be completely unusable.
  2. On one of my laptops - the Samsung software never worked again after the upgrade - even reinstalling the Samsung stuff didn't work. Backlight stuck at 100%, no bluetooth either (same issue as you)

After doing a fresh install, both systems work fine (other than the USB audio recording issue in the giant thread you have probably seen in the same forum).

After upgrading to Windows 10, you received a digital entitlement on your device. I strongly recommend backing up your data, then performing a clean install (wipe the disk).

You will need 2 USB sticks, one must be at least 4GB in size.

To prepare yourself for this:

  • Required: Download all manufacturer drivers for your computer onto a USB memory stick. Do this first because there is no guarentee that Windows 10 will have a built in driver for your wireless / ethernet adaptor when you reinstall it.
  • Optional: Download any drivers you will need for other devices now (for convenience).
  • Required: Make a note of what software you have installed, as you will be reinstalling it. Pay particular care to software that has activation (e.g. Rosetta Stone), you may need to deactivate them first.
  • Required: Create a USB bootable Windows 10 installer using the Windows 10 Media Creation Tool ( Link here ). DO NOT run this tool on the USB stick you put your drivers on. It wipes it.
  • Required: Back up all of your data.

If your motherboard supports UEFI, now is a great time to enable it too perhaps - you will get faster booting times. But that is complicated and I wont cover it here unless you ask. (You would need to change the boot drive type to GPT before doing that).

Then once you are ready, boot from that USB media, and perform a clean installation of Windows 10. You will need to say "I want a clean installation" and then use Drive Options -> Advanced and delete all of the partitions on your boot disk (yes, all of them!), then choose the free unallocated space and tell Windows to install there. It will automatically partition the disk correctly for you.

Windows will ask for a product key. Click "Skip". Later on, it will detect the digital entitlement and activate. Do not worry about that.

Once Windows finishes installing and you login for the first time, perform these steps in this order:

  • First, allow Windows 10 to try to connect to the Internet and install drivers by itself. If you have a high end GPU like an nvidia 970, you may notice it telling you that it is doing this for you. Leave it to sit on the desktop for 30 minutes.
  • Reboot.
  • Insert your USB media with your drivers and install the chipset drivers first from your motherboard / computer manufacturer.
  • Reboot.
  • Install the remainder of the motherboard / computer manufacturer drivers.
  • Reboot again.
  • Now your PC should be useable - you can proceed with installing drivers for anything else you use, putting your data and programs back.
