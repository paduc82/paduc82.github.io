Usually the picture dimension measures in pixel per inch.

Pixels per inch (PPI) or pixel density is a measurement of the resolution of devices in various contexts; typically computer displays, image scanners, and digital camera image sensors.

PPI can also describe the resolution, in pixels, of an image to be printed within a specified space. Style based headings and keep-with-next: Handling documents with - Microsoft Community

johnqflorida say:

Style based headings and keep-with-next: Handling documents with many many headings in an uninterrupted row?

I have a template I made years ago that follows the Style-Based heading guidelines.  It works very well for me most of the time.

One annoyance, and perhaps there's a better way:  Suppose the nature of the document ends up with a long stream of Heading styles (all with keep-with-next), one after the other, no interruptions?  That creates a block of connected text that can trigger a page break that leaves up to a full-page of white space on the previous page.

When that happens, my manual method of avoiding that is to insert an empty "Normal" styled line (no keep-with-next) that breaks the chain, but like I said, it's manual and won't automatically maintain itself.  Is there a purer/proper way?

jay freedman say:

You can use a Normal-styled paragraph as you're doing now, or you can manually override the keep-with-next setting on a specific heading paragraph by going to the Paragraph dialog and unchecking the setting.

Both methods suffer from the drawback that they aren't self-maintaining. If you need to edit the text before the break and that changes where the break should be, you have to fix it manually.

This may be unhelpful for you, but I'll mention it anyway: Many publications, including much technical documentation, don't allow a heading to be immediately followed by another heading. There must be at least one paragraph of Normal text after each heading, which solves the KWN problem -- but at the cost of bloating the document with often unnecessary paragraphs.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

If the document is in the nature of an outline, your choices are:

  1. Use styles other than the heading styles.
  2. Remove the KWN property from the lower-level heading styles. For example, leave Heading 1 and Heading 2 as KWN, but allow a break after Heading 3 and lower.
