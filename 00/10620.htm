a. user say:

Word's Table of Contents (ToC) feature lists paragraphs that have specific Paragraph Styles applied to them. By that, I do not mean that you apply formatting such as "bold" to them - you actually have to apply a named style. By default, Word's ToC looks for paragraphs with style names Heading 1, Heading 2 and Heading 3. You can set the style of your heading paragraphs using the Styles group in the Home tab of Word's ribbon (see below).

However, because it sounds as if you have applied some direct formatting (e.g. bolded the text), it will probably be better to clear that formatting first (see the "Clear all formatting button" in the image above). Direct formatting is formatting that you applied directly to a piece of text, rather than by using a character or paragraph style to do it.

So what I suggest is that you

 a. select the text of the first heading you want in your ToC

 b. click the Clear All Formatting button

 c. click the Heading 1 button. (If you do not see the Heading 1 button, try using the scroll bars at the right of the row of Style buttons to see more style buttons).

At this point the heading may not be formatted how you want. To fix that, right click on the Heading 1 button and Select Modify... (see below)

Word will open the Modify Style dialog box. You can use the controls in the middle of the dialog to modify some of the formatting (e.g. font name and size). Further options are available by clicking on the Format... button at the bottom left. Judging by your descriptin, you will at least need to use that to apply some automatic numbering to the Headings, and you will probably need to delete the manual numbering too (not in this dialog, but by editing the heading paragraphs). When you have finished all the changes, click the OK button.

Now, for every other paragraph that you want to be in the ToC,

 a. select the paragraph

 b. click the Clear all formatting button

 c. click the Heading 1 style button. You should see that the formatting choices you made in the Modify Style dialog are applied to each of your heading paragraphs.

Finally, click in the existing ToC and click the Update Table option. You may see a further dialog box - if so, I usually select the "Update entire table" option and click OK.

The ToC should now include your headings. If it does not, please click Alt-F9, which should reveal the "field code" that WOrd is using to generate the ToC. By default, it will look something like

{ TOC \o "1-3" \h \z \u }

If you see something else, I suggest you delete the text between the "{" and the "}" and copy/paste 

TOC \o "1-3" \h \z \u

in there instead, then click Alt-F9 to show "field results," then update the ToC again.

NB., Paragraph and Character styles are an important feature in Word - it is difficult to do some things without using them (as above), and learning how to use them may save you a great deal of time in future. The limit of 9 that you have seen is not a limitation on the number of headings you can have - it is if you like the number of Heading styles (Heading 1, Heading 2,..., Heading 9) that Word recognises.

Reveal Formatting & Display Nonprinting Characters are 2 separate features which are not related. Although commonly referred to as "formatting marks" the nonprinting characters actually have more to do with layout & flow control. Your description suggests that what you've done is to toggle on the display of nonprinting characters, possibly by inadvertent use of the keyboard shortcut Command+8... Use the same keystroke to toggle them off.
