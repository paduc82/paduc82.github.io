This behavior can occur if you configure the service to log on to a user account, and any of the following conditions are true:

  1. The right to log on as a service is revoked for the specified user account.

  2. The password is changed on the user account that the service uses to log on.

  3. The password data in the registry is damaged.

I suggest you to follow the steps given below and check if it helps you.

  1. Press Windows Keys + R Key, type services.msc and hit enter.

  2. You will see the list of services on your computer, locate the services you have referred.

  3. Right click on it and click on Properties.

  4. Go to Log On tab and click the radio button against "This Account". 

  5. Browse your computer and find the "Local Service" account.  Wipe out the password so that it is blank and apply. 

  6. You will be presented with a dialog stating the account has been granted the login as a service right. Then start the service. That should fix your problem.
