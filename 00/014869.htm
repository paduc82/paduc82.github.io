charles kenyon say:

A paragraph style when applied to a paragraph is going to override direct formatting that involves more than half of the paragraph. That is built into how they work.

I am not going to tell you there is any way to do what you want using a macro.

A possibility would be to simply have the style in place under your direct formatting and rather than reapplying it, modify it in place. Your direct formatting will then not be disturbed.

It may be a bit of work to set it up to start with if you do not already have the style applied, but after that, you modify the style and the modification will underly your direct formatting.

Understanding Styles in Microsoft Word

Keep in mind that in Word both Bold and Italics are toggles. If your style is Bold and your text is formatted as Bold using direct formatting you will end up with un-bolded text.

stefan blom say:

This basic macro, which I'm sure can be improved, will store in an array the font formatting of each character in a selected paragraph, apply a paragraph style, and then re-apply the font formatting:

Sub TestMacro()
'Macro created by Stefan Blom, MVP, October 2018
'In the first paragraph in the selection, store font settings in
'an array, apply the style of your choice, and then reapply the
'font formatting
Dim x() As Font
Dim counter As Long
Dim j As Long
Dim m As Long
counter = Selection.Paragraphs(1).Range.Characters.Count - 1
For j = 1 To counter
ReDim Preserve x(1 To counter)
Set x(j) = Selection.Paragraphs(1).Range.Characters(j).Font
Next j

Selection.Paragraphs(1).Style = "Heading 1"   'Use "Menu" here

For m = 1 To counter

Selection.Paragraphs(1).Range.Characters(m).Font = x(m)
Next m
End Sub

john w. vinson say:

Be aware that Text fields sort alphabetically - so either '71' or '777777777777' will in fact be BETWEEN '7000' and '7999' (just as 'GB' is BETWEEN GAAA and GZZZ).

The WHERE condition just needs to be a text string, either a single text literal enclosed in doublequotes, or something concateneated from two or more pieces using the & concatenation operator. The final result just needs to be a valid SQL WHERE clause without the word WHERE. So you should be able to use

DoCmd.OpenReport "rptCensusDelinquent", acViewPreview, , "[DEPARTMENT] = [Forms]![frmPowerUserMenu].[cboCensusDept] AND [SECT] NOT BETWEEN '7000' AND '7999'"

or

DoCmd.OpenReport "rptCensusDelinquent", acViewPreview, , "[SECT] BETWEEN '7000' AND '7999'"
