Clear Photos App Live Tile Images in Windows 8.1 - Microsoft Community

barb bowman say:

Clear Photos App Live Tile Images in Windows 8.1

Technical Level : Expert

Summary

Technical Level: Basic

Applies to: Windows 8.1 and Windows RT 8.1

How to get rid of embarrassing or unwanted pictures that scroll through the Windows 8.1 Photo App Live Tile even after being deleted from your Photo Library. Or remove images that appear after you have permanently deleted from your hard drive.


Details

If you delete an image from your library, the Live Tile does not immediately sync and select a new picture to replace it. This was an issue in the original Photos App in 8.0 and Microsoft did not change this in Windows 8.1. You have to wait 24 hours for new images to appear and after 24 hours, you may still need to open the Photos App and let it run in the foreground for new images to appear on the Live Tile. You can use the info below to change the images displayed on the Photos App Live Tile any time you wish to make a change. You still cannot pick and choose what is displayed, unfortunately. Microsoft states that the Tile slide show consists of 5 images selected from your MOST RECENT 50 IMAGES, which can be stored locally,  or on SkyDrive.

I have tested this on both a Desktop and Surface RT running Windows 8.1. Here are the steps to follow:

1. Open the Photos App, select Charms, Options

2. If the Shuffle option is on, turn it off

3. Close the Photos App

4. On the Start Screen, if the Live Tile is On, turn it OFF.

5. Close the Photos App and make sure it is not running. To do this, either open it and drag it down to the bottom of your screen about half way and hold it there until the icon flips around to show the Photos Tile Logo OR on the Start screen, type TASK MANAGER and open it and end task on Photos and then check Task Manager a second time to insure the Photos App is closed.

6. Go to the desktop and navigate to

C:\Users\your_user_name\AppData\Local\Packages\FileManager_cw5n1h2txyewy\LocalState

7. Delete all the Tile*.jpg files and any *.dat files – you may have two dat files and EMPTY THE RECYCLE BIN.

8. Open the Photos App and turn the Shuffle option back on

9. Turn the Photos App Live Tile back on

10. Leave the Photos App running in the foreground for 10-15 minutes

11. You should have a new set of images appear on the Live Tile within a few minutes.

It probably means your disk drive is corrupted.  Could even be failing.

canadian tech say:

I am first worried that you are not starting your computer on that repair disk.  You must start pressing the F12 key about once a second as soon as the computer starts.  That should bring up a menu that allows you to choose to boot to the DVD drive.  That should lead you to options that may work.

Your hard drive could be failing, but lets assume not and that it is simply so corrupted it can not begin.

First choice is a Factory Restore 

Press Ctrl+F11 during the Dell screen

You will lose everything on the hard drive -- EVERYTHING

It will re-install exactly what was there when you started the computer for the very first time.

If you have data that is not backed up, you need to find someone that can externally mount the hard drive from your computer to another computer and read and copy as much data as possible on to some other media which you will be able to copy back on to your computer once it is re-built.

It is also possible the hard drive hardware itself is failing or has failed.  When you take that drive to someone else with the know how and equipment to copy the data, that person can download the hard drive manufacturer's drive tester software to see if it is failing.  If it is failing and you need to replace....

You will then be forced to do a clean install from your Windows 7 install disk that I hope you got from Dell.  If you did not, you can probably get them to send you one for a small fee.
