nsilmike say: If you've had it for several years, there are two likely scenarios.
1)  it needs calibration.  If you saw the charge indicator just before it died and it showed charge remaining, this is probably the issue.  The charge circuits operate by detecting empty and full batteries correctly, and they slowly lose this calibration over time depending on your charging habits.  You can recalibrate by setting it to play, and letting it go until it dies.  Restart it and repeat until it stays on less than about 5 minutes.  Then connect it to charge, and let it charge UNINTERRUPTED until full.  Done.  Note that this is strictly for lithium batteries, including most likely your laptop, your phone, etc...  it is just the nature of the battery chemistry.
2) Battery is dead.  After many charge cycles the lithium battery slowly loses capacity, and eventually just dies.  If you can't get it going at all, this is probably the issue.  You can get it repaired at rapidrepair.com and/or pdasmart.com.  But, try reinitializing it first:  How to Reboot, Reformat, and Restore your Zune device (Now - Microsoft Answers

Oh.. and to do a boot on the zune 120:  either shut it down fully by holding back and down (it'll reboot the next time you start it up,) or hold back and up for an immediate shutdown and restart with a boot.  
FYI, you should do this somewhat regularly.  I let mine sleep during the day, but the last time I shut down for the day, I do the full shut down to get a reboot the next time I use it.
