techfreak_ say:

The purpose of launch Skype in the background is to initially launch Skype in the background. Instead of launching Skype in the foreground/maximized, it is launched minimized to the system tray/notification area. It serves no other purpose as it is not connected to notifications.

If you are not receiving notifications for calls or chats this setting should be unrelated. Lack of notifications generally means:

1. Your Skype status is set to Do Not Disturb.

2. Notifications have been disabled in your general notification settings.

3. Notifications have been disabled in your notification settings for that particular contact.

4. If the incoming call originates from Skype, the person calling is using a damaged version of Skype.

5. Your installation of Skype is damaged or running an earlier problematic version. Make sure you are running the latest version of Skype (currently Skype 8.38).
