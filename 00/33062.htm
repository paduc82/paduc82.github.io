Personally I would advise you to update your graphics/display drivers.


You can usually download drivers from your computer manufacturer but to get the very latest drivers find out who manufactured your graphics card, you should see it in the Device Manager under Display Adapters. Go to their web site and then download the latest driver.

The difference between downloading them from your computer manufacturer (OEM) and downloading them from the graphics card manufacturer themselves is usually a couple of versions difference - since the OEM usually tests the graphics drivers before making them available on their web site.

 

Most manufacturers provide a self-installing executable that you can just double-click on to install.

 

As a workaround, you might want to disable some of the power management features such as Hibernation (if you have it enabled) or other power management features. As you say, it occurs when you leave the computer for some time, it's probably going into power saving and that is causing the issue. You can workaround the problem by disabling some of those power saving features until you have rectified it.

 

You might also like to try disabling and re-enabling hibernation as I have also known that to resolve a similar issue.

 

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/920730

 

Power Management options you might consider disabling as a workaround are the "Turn off the display" option and the "Sleep" option. You can turn these back on again after you have updated the drivers.
