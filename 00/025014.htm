survey my site say:

Creating Templates in Word

Word 2019 Help says previous versions of my personal templates will transfer from my previous versions. Where can I find these? They are not in the Custom Office Templates Folder. And really wish that the templates I create would be available from the "New" command instead of having to click again to "Personal".

jay freedman say:

In previous versions, templates that you create were stored in the folder %appdata%\Microsoft\Templates. (The environment variable %appdata% will expand to C:\Users\<your login name>\AppData\Roaming when you enter that path in the address box and press Enter.) They should still be there.

To get everything in one place, you can either cut/paste the template files from the older Templates folder to the newer Custom Office Templates folder or vice versa.

You should also go to File > Options > Save and enter the folder path to your templates in the "Default 

personal templates location" box, and then go to File > Options > Advanced > File Locations and modify the "User templates" location to the same folder path.

For much more about the mess Microsoft has made of template paths and how to fix it, read http://www.addbalance.com/usersguide/fileNew.htm#2013Changes. (Everything there applies to Word 2016 and 2019 as well.)

rohn007 say:

I also created a WIKI article similar to Jay's. I noticed that there were some minor changes between the labels used in 2013 and 2016

Word 2016 Templates: Featured vs Personal vs Shared vs Custom location names! - wiki

2016 version : https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/msoffice/wiki/msoffice_word-msoffice_custom/word-2016-templates-featured-vs-shared-vs-custom/d23e9c8f-5420-4bcd-84fd-e502e21ab0a5

Word 2013/2016 changed (again, <sigh> ) the way templates are handled. Unfortunately the names used are not obvious. I‚Äôve been puzzled about it for a while now and finally tripped over some answers. This wiki tries to summarize what I‚Äôve found.

In the Word ‚ÄúFile menu > New command‚Äù backstage ‚Äúpain‚Äù <sic, grin> I have read many questions and replies describing a ‚ÄúFeatured‚Äù location and multiple other second locations such as ‚ÄúPersonal‚Äù or ‚ÄúShared‚Äù or ‚ÄúCustom‚Äù.  But no one was ever clear on the significance of those 3 names.  I finally tripped over the answer, and will share it with you.

 

MS has done it's best to separate templates from various sources.  What you want to do is pick one of the file names on the default New screen. Find that file on your computer.  For example, on my computer I found:

E:\Office\Word\Templates2016\Family photo calendar (any year, 1 page).dotm

So on my computer I could use the instructions in the links we have provided to point my "Personal" folder at the location "E:\Office\Word\Templates2016".  In theory that should put the new templates there for you to see in the New dialog.  

You could also delete some (all) of the default templates that you do not expect to use to make it easier to find your personal templates in the display.
