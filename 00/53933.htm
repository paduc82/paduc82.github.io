bossdweeb say:

The same as the difference between two i7 4790k cpu's made in Vietnam.

That is to say that no two cpu's are identical.  Some of the cpu's made in batch 'A' will be slightly better

at overclocking than others from that batch.

The cpu's that are verified to be able to exceed a particular clock speed will get the 'K' designation.

When the demand for 'K' models is not as high more cpu's that are not designated with a 'K' that can

also sustain a high overclock will slip by.

The 'K' is a guarantee of sorts that your cpu will be stable when overclocked to whatever speed Intel have

determined a suitable OC to justify the extra money for the 'K' model.

Overall quality and reliability would be the same. The high tech process and Intel management would assure

consistency on this level, but there are always some duds in the mix, as with all manufactured objects.
Vietnam would be the newer factory. Intel cpu's have been manufactured in Malaysia for a long time now.
