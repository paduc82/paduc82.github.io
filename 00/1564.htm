I recently wrote a blog post on the the ethics of data use for monitoring devices like the Microsoft Band 2:

Physiologic Monitoring Devices:  Who Owns the Data and What Purpose Should it Serve?

My Hardware Devices:

Microsoft Band 2

My Nature:

I am a high level tech person but a conservative one.

Have Samsung Note 4 paired with Google Gmail and calendar and 7GB of LTE data
No home Interent except via LTE tether by smartphone
No television or radio (These media contain no useful information)

Why Microsoft Band 2:

From the above I seem like the most unlikely buyer of this product.  Indeed I believe both Microsoft and the Federal Government should be paying me to use this device.

But my decision to actually buy this product comes from 3 directions:

1) I think it can give me useful information about myself
2) I think it can give society useful information about the human condition.
3) I feel our medical industry and leaders are mired in willful disinformation and I have zero faith in just about anything they say*

* I do not say this lightly.  I am a medical professional myself in bacterial science and I attend many seminars and know how to read the scientific literature.  Medial science related to human health is literally a badly made house of cards.  Those few well crafted and important works of science are ignored if inconvenient.

So I see devices such as the Band 2 as the only way to move forward and cut through the sloppy and willful deceptions and actually generate valid health data that can be used individually and aggregated to make social policy decisions related to health.  More sophisticated versions of these devices may for the first time find out what is actually important for good health. Doctors and health scientists are salesmen and cannot be trusted to do this.

Ideally these devices would provide constant in-depth physiologic, hormonal and mental state data AND they would link to a smart device where people can enter how they are feeling and what they are experiencing in real time. Data and context,  the foundations of real science. People, especially pregnant mothers should be paid to use these devices.  This might be the most valuable information humanity has ever generated.

The Microsoft Band 2 Hardware:

It is primitive and probably deliberately crippled but it is a great start.

I can't even wear a watch but I have no complaints about the Band 2 worn on my left hand inside facing. The size and shape is just right for me.  It isn't a work of art but it is well designed and has a good utilitarian style.

It seems over designed but I love the clip and the button feel and the screen is just right and clearly visible.

When I took it out of the box I assumed it would have a induction charger and spent 15 minutes trying to get it to charge and then searched the Internet to find out how.  Thinking about it I am glad it has a contact charger because induction is way to slow.  My Samsung Note 4 seems to charge full in 15 minutes with it's dedicated charger.


The Microsoft Band 2 Software:

I would say the local software and firmware are incredibly well designed yielding an eminently usable package with information quickly accessible and nothing you don't need (if you configure it that way).

Media controls work well, texts show up and allow canned responses and Google notifications fire properly.

The downsides are that you don't have direct access to raw data and it obligately goes through the cloud and Microsoft's servers. No doubt Microsoft will sell the aggregated de-identified data to the highest bidder and it will probably be misused to make false claims and sell products.

In Android, the Band 2 doesn't integrate with Google Voice Services only Cortana on Windows Phones.  As a avid user of Google Voice Services I can say voice dictation of texts probably won't work on the Band 2 anyway.  There is too much room for disaster and mis-translations that won't be easy to detect and correct.  I am happy with canned text responses but there should be more than 4 of them.

In the Android and PC App the user should have the option to hide the tiles they don't want to see, like Golf.

Overall:

It is a great product that doesn't try to do what it cannot do well or would be much easier on your phone.

What About the Data?:

Far to early to give a decent assessment.

One thing is for sure ..... I want direct access to the rawish data.

stefan blom say:

You can restart page numbering following a section break. See http://wordfaqs.mvps.org/FrontMatterRibbon.htm.

For information on printing a page range in a multi-section document, see http://word.mvps.org/faqs/formatting/printmultiplesections.htm.

Note that printing ranges of pages in a multi-section document will be easier if you display the "Section" number and the "Formatted page number" on the status bar; to do so, right-click the status bar and click to select both of these options.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

In each Section, set the Different First Page option and do not put a { page } in the First Page Header or Footer as the case may be.   in the Primary Header or Footer, insert a { page } field where you want the page number to appear and then via the Page Number>Format Page Numbers... facility in the Header & Footer section of the Header & Footer Tools>Design tab of the ribbon, set the page numbering to start at 0.  The page numbering that then begins to appear on the second page of the Section will start from 1.

When printing specific pages from a document such as that you describe, you must specify the Section number as well as the Page number in the format of p7s2-p9s2
