interpreting omega ratio. thanks - Microsoft Community

luvtiramisu say:

interpreting omega ratio. thanks

how to interpret the omega ratio. What does a high and low omega ratio indicates. thanks

samirkhan say: Bear in mind that the Omega Ratio encapsulates all the information in the returns distribution. Other measures like the Sharpe Ratio approximate the returns distribution with the mean and variance (hence discarding any information that does not fit neatly inside a normal distribution - like extreme losses, for example).

The Omega Ratio is the probability weighted upside divided by the probability weighed downside .  In other words, a high Omega indicated an investment with a low probability of making an extreme loss.  However, an investment with a high Omega Ratio may have a higher volatility (i.e. standard deviation) than an investment with a high Sharpe Ratio.

If you look at hedge fund data, Omega Ratios of over 1 are considered good, and anything approaching two is excellent. There's an guide to the Omega Ratio here.  If you have any more questions about the Omega Ratio, post a comment at that site, they're good at responding.
