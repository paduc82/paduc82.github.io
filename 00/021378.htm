Band 2 and Bluetooth connectivity problems with Cortana on the Lumia - Microsoft Community

nick arnott-smith say:

Band 2 and Bluetooth connectivity problems with Cortana on the Lumia 950XL.

Problem:  Syncing between the band and MS Health app appears to work. Despite that, and Bluetooth is confirmed connected and on, Cortana commands initiated from the band fail to action the phone and the response: 'Make sure Bluetooth is on and your phone is nearby' appears on the band display. 

There have been bits and pieces written about the Band 2 and Bluetooth connectivity problems with Cortana on the Lumia 950XL.  To make things easier for you here's a consolidation of what I have come across that seems to have worked to get Cortana going again correctly with the Band 2 and Lumia 950 XL ...in my case ... so far.  Incidentally, In my case I transitioned the band 2 from a Lumia 920.

1) follow the Microsoft recommended steps - restart phone, band and make sure everything (phone, app and band) are correctly updated. But hey, don't do this zillions of times thinking something different will happen, try it once or maybe twice... if it doesn't solve the problem for you, move along the steps below.

2) Sync your band to the health App (for peace of mind confirm your data is safe by logging into Health Web dashboard and confirming your most recent data is there)

3) reset your band using the 3rd Solution - Manual Reset 
https://www.microsoft.com/microsoft-band/en-us/support/phone-app/reset  - but don't reconfigure it yet.

4) at this stage I tested my band to confirm it was working well by setting it up with my Lumia 920 and associated MS health app. - everything worked as expected.  After testing I then removed the band from this phone using the same 3 steps above. I think the whole of step 4 could be omitted if you choose.

5) Go back to your 950 xl and remove the MS health app from your phone.

6) ensure there is no reference to your band in the Bluetooth settings for the phone. > settings > Devices > Bluetooth

7) RESTART YOUR PHONE.

8) Go to the store and download and reinstall the MS health app - once installed, open MS Health. The app should repopulate itself with your latest synced data from MS health Web Dashboard.

9) It might be unnecessary, but I like to see how each change works AFTER a system reboot, so reboot your phone again now.

10) After the restart, with WI-FI off, and Bluetooth enabled, open the health app. Your data should be there, but no band will be present.

11) It is important that Bluetooth is enabled on your phone but don't pair your band using your phone's Bluetooth setting. USE the MS Health app to pair your band. 

12) Prepare to Add your band. If you have correctly returned the band to factory default as done in step 3, your first screen on the band will be language choice. Following both the steps on your Band and the health app (use the Add Band menu) pair the band. Make sure you select 'windows phone'.  At this stage your band should pair as a generic Microsoft Band ***.  If you see your personalised named band in the pairing screen of the app, you've probably not removed the old references in your phone's Bluetooth settings.

13) Once paired successfully, the app will update and set up the band and then sync it with your account.

14) You'll need to reconfigure your preferred settings on the band, but other than that, you should be good to go.....

So, less than 24 hours later the problem returned.  hmmmm

All my devices were set to Canada English but after reading a reply to this post I've made an assumption that the Band is United States English. I have now changed the region in MS Health app to United States, without changing any other regional settings elsewhere on the phone. Here are some additional steps to try.

15) Open MS health app, go to  > preferences > Choose your region and select United States.

16) Restart phone and band.

So far this worked for sending 1 text message and then the problem has returned.
