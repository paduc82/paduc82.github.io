In summary: I changed the imap server from "imap.gmail.com" to "imap.googlemail.com" and the smtp server from "smtp.gmail.com" to "smtp.googlemail.com". And that worked.

Step-by-step procedure (for me in Windows 10):

1) Goto 'Control Panel' > Mail (Microsoft Outlook 2016).

2) In dialog "Mail setup": click "Email Accounts" button.

3) In dialog "Email Accounts": in "Email" tab:  in the list of your email accounts, select the google account where you have problems with and click the "change" button above the list of accounts.

4) In dialog "Change Account": change the value of the textbox "Incoming mail server" from "imap.gmail.com" to "imap.googlemail.com". Also change the value of the textbox "Outgoing mail server (SMTP)" from "smtp.gmail.com" to "smtp.googlemail.com".

This did it for me. Hopefully this works for you too. Good luck.

How to correct horizontal spaces between lines Word Mac 16? - Microsoft Community

ralphhm say:

How to correct horizontal spaces between lines Word Mac 16?

Perhaps a Word Mac expert is out there?

I have a long document that I imported into Word Mac 2016 from Scrivener (writer's tool).  All is generally good, except for the spacing between the end of one sentence in the same paragraph and the beginning of the next (one too many spaces).  Is there any way to correct this for the entire document at once?

Thanks

john mcghie say:

I don't know about "out there", but about 20 of the most expert Word pilots in the world are "in here" :-)

You can solve this problem with Word's Advanced Find and Replace command.

But first you need to find out what is in there: is it "two spaces" or a "single em space"?

To find out, turn on your non-printing characters, so you can see what you are doing: click the ¶ until the blue dots appear.

A "space" will show up as a single blue dot at the mid-point of the text line.  An em-space shows as a small blue circle.

In the picture, the top sentence break contains two spaces, the bottom contains an em space.

  1. Now, go to the Advanced Find and Replace command on the Edit menu.
  2. Make sure the Find box contains nothing (back-space over it, you need to be SURE)
  3. If you have two spaces between sentences, hit the space bar twice. If you have em-spaces, hit the caret and a plus sign ^+
  5. In the Replace box, type a single space.
  6. Now, Replace All.

That will fix it.
