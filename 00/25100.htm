teylyn say:

May Tab: R5 - the formula is calculating correctly.

=Q5+May!Q5

You are basically duplicating the value in Q5. Did you mean to add May!Q5 to another sheet's Q5?? 

May Tab: R4 - it's the same formula as in Q5, just copied, so it shows as

=Q4+May!Q4 

Again, which cells do you REALLY want to add up, seeing that the cell IS ALREADY in sheet May. April maybe?

May Tab: Q10 - The formula in this column seems to be different in each row, not a consistent formula that can be copied down. That is very dangerous and will raise alarm flags with anyone who works with spreadsheets a lot.

Q4 has =P4-O4

then in Q8 you have a constant instead of a formula.

Q9 is blank

Q10 has =SUM(J10*K25)+(K10*K26)+(L10*M25)+(M10*M26)

When that is copied down, ALL row references will change. You probably want the references to the cells in rows 25 and 26 to remain fixed and not change with every row. Because in Q13 the formula now refers to =SUM(J13*K28)+(K13*K29)+(L13*M28)+(M13*M29) and there is text in these cells.

A well-formed Sum function will ignore text, but the + operator will throw an error if you use it with text. 

Why do you have a Sum around the multiplication???? Why do people wrap things in a Sum function that can be calculated on their own??

Anyway, change the formula in cell Q10 to

=SUM(J10*$K$25)+(K10*$K$26)+(L10*$M$25)+(M10*$M$26)

Then copy down. The dollar signs in the cell reference mean that the reference will not change when the formula is copied.
