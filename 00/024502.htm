rich007a say:

On each sheet insert a new column to the left of Column A.  In that new column A, in A1, you can enter:

 

=SUBSTITUTE(IF(LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))>1,LEFT(B1,SEARCH("^^",SUBSTITUTE(B1," ","^^",LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))))+1),B1),",","")

 

Do that in both sheets, then (at least for the examples you have shown) you should get matching names.

 

This removes any commas:

=SUBSTITUTE(IF(LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))>1,LEFT(B1,SEARCH("^^",SUBSTITUTE(B1," ","^^",LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))))+1),B1),",","")

 

If there is more than one space...

=SUBSTITUTE(IF(LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))>1,LEFT(B1,SEARCH("^^",SUBSTITUTE(B1," ","^^",LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))))+1),B1),",","")

 

...then take everything to the left of and including the first letter of the last word:

=SUBSTITUTE(IF(LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))>1,LEFT(B1,SEARCH("^^",SUBSTITUTE(B1," ","^^",LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))))+1),B1),",","")

 

...otherwise just remove commas from the text in B1:

=SUBSTITUTE(IF(LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))>1,LEFT(B1,SEARCH("^^",SUBSTITUTE(B1," ","^^",LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))))+1),B1),",","")

 

 

Hope that helps.

Cheers
Rich

 

PS. In your sample data, there is a double-space between "DOE" and "SALLY" in Sheet1 col A.  If that is really in your data, you could either do a Ctrl+H find and replace for "  " with " " (find two spaces, replace with one space), or you could wrap the above formula in another SUBSTITUTE function:

 

=SUBSTITUTE(SUBSTITUTE(IF(LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))>1,LEFT(B1,SEARCH("^^",SUBSTITUTE(B1," ","^^",LEN(B1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(B1," ",""))))+1),B1),",",""),"  "," ")

 

PPS.  So if your data starts in A1 on both sheets, you can use:

=D1=VLOOKUP(A1,Sheet2!$A$1:$C$4,3,FALSE)

to return TRUE if the employment status is the same (otherwise it will return FALSE).
