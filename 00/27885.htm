nsilmike say: Folks... there is a lot that can cause this, and much of it is out of your (and sometimes MS also ) control.
- your PC can be slow (yes, it's true, but I will assume most of you can check this yourself.)
- your ISP can be throttling you (rare outside of Canada so let's assume it's not this in most cases.)
- you are far away from the source (not necessarily physically although this would affect this) but in terms of of internet routes.  If it takes many hops, your internet latency is slow.  effectively your packets are jumping from node to node on a big network, and it takes a lot of time to ack/nack, do error checking, etc.
- a router somewhere between you and the source is messed up, making a normally fast route slow
- there's a bad node or link causing dropped packets and too many errors and retransmits...
- the source server is overloaded- I'd say not terribly likely for someone like MS, unless it's a brand new top hit movie that suddenly a gazillion folks want.
- and many more...
the point is that often, this is temporary and/or out of your control unfortunately....
