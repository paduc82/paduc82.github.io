Let us follow these methods one by one to check if that helps.

Method 1: Reschedule the work of Windows Defender.

  1. Open the run window, type ‘taskschd.msc’ and click OK.

  2. Now, look at the left panel in the window that opens and expand (by double-clicking them) the following sequence of folders: Task Scheduler Library > Microsoft > Windows.

  3. In the Windows folder, find one called Windows Defender.

  4. Now, look at the panel in the center and double-click Windows Defender Scheduled Scan.

  5. In General Tab, click to put a tick in a checkbox next to Run with highest privileges.

  6. Finally, do the rescheduling part. Go to Triggers tab, and then click New. And customize the scan schedule according to your preference. Click OK to finish. Before you do, make sure the “Enabled” box is checked.

Method 2: Add the Antimalware Service Executable to Windows Defender exclusion list.

1. Open the Settings screen and click on Update and Security.

2. Go to Windows Security > Virus & Threat Protection > Virus & Threat Protection Settings.

3. Under Exclusions, click Add or Remove Exclusions.

4. Choose to Add an Exclusion > Process.

5. Type MsMpEng.exe and click Add.

Now, the process should be consuming less CPU power and resource.
