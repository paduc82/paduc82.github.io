steve rindsberg say:
Two issues here, really:  What you are *able* to do and what you are *permitted* to do.

The fonts themselves are TrueType and may or may not be embeddable.  The Microsoft Typography extension for Explorer would tell you more about that.

As to what you're permitted to do, the license with the viewer says this:
    Font Components 
    You may use the fonts that accompany the PowerPoint Viewer only to display and print content from a device running a Microsoft Windows operating system. Additionally, you may do the following:
    • Embed fonts in content as permitted by the embedding restrictions in the fonts
    • When printing content, temporarily download the fonts to a printer or other output device
    You may not copy, install or use the fonts on other devices. 

=========

So it would seem that as long as the fonts themselves permit embedding, you may embed them, and so long as you do it on a Windows system, you'd be ok doing the other things you ask about.
