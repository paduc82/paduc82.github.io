How do I revert back to Internet Explorer 9.

Original title : Fed up with IE 10! 

 

How do I go backwards to Internet Explorer 9?  Internet Explorer 10 hinders my access to my financial accounts and other important sites.  How do I prevent automatic update to IE10 on older systems when they update during shutdown?

 

I'm running IE10 on a Windows 8 system, not impressed with either.  At my brokerage account site, all but the start page render incorrectly.  The entire page renders in less than 5% of the available display real estate.  Selecting compatibility mode has no effect.  What now?  Also, I maintain my father's and in-law's computers, at their age, change this drastic is too much to ask.  They currently use IE8 or 9 on Windows 7 systems.  But I just allowed my older system to run through its updates during shutdown only to discover to my horror that an automatic update to IE 10 was included in that update.  I really get disgusted by the lack of compatibility, thinking of switching to Chrome or Firefox permanently so I don't have to put up with this any more.  Please help.

scott .. b say:

Rodney.........

 

You have a number of questions all rolled up into one, so I will try to address them individually.

 

1)  Can you go backward from Internet Explorer 10 to IE 9.?

If you have a computer with Windows 8 and Internet Explorer 10 pre-installed, you cannot go back to Internet Explorer 9.  That is because Internet Explorer 10 is part of the Windows 8 operating system and cannot be uninstalled.  It can, however, be disabled , which  will allow you to use another browser, if you choose.

 

2)   Can you go backward on an older computer that was running Windows 7 with Internet Explorer 9 but was upgraded to Internet Explorer 10 by an automatic Windows update?

Yes. It is possible to revert back to Internet Explorer 9 on a Windows 7 computer by simply uninstalling the Windows update.  You will find it in System......Windows Update.

 

3)  Can you prevent future Windows updates from automatically changing your older computers to IE 10?

Yes.  You can do that by deselecting" automatic updates" in the Windows Update.  And, in fact you will have to do that if your revert your WIN 7 computer back, because, if you don't, Windows will just change it back in the next update.

 

 

Lot of information here, and quite a bit of "how to" detail still needed to clarify, but thought Ide give you some general answers to allay your concerns.

 

 

 

How to uninstall Windows IE10 update on a Windows 7 computer:

 

Start button > in the search box, type programs and features > Enter > left side, click View Installed Updates > scroll down to Microsoft Windows heading to find Windows Internet Explorer 10 > right click > click Uninstall.

Restart your computer.

You should be back with your previous IE version which, in this case, is IE9.

 

 

 After uninstalling the Windows IE10 update....................

 

Change Windows Update to Manual.  If you leave it set on automatic, IE10 will come up in Windows Update again, and will be re-installed.  (If you change your mind later, set Windows update to "automatic" and IE 10 will be right back...hopefully improved by then).

 

 Start > in the search box, type windows update > press Enter > left side, click Change Settings > under Important Updates, click the pointer and change it to " Download updates but let me choose whether to install Them " > click OK when done. 

 

 

(We can next address the issue you are having with page rendering and compatibility on your brokerage website, if you like.)
