Macro to insert date for specific day of the week automatically - Microsoft Community

cheecake say:

Macro to insert date for specific day of the week automatically

We utilize a publication which is issued for a specific day of the week, but prepared in advance of that day. Is there a way to have the document insert the date for the specific upcoming day of the week. For example, prepare the document on Wednesday but have it insert the date for Friday?
hansv mvp say:

The following macro will insert the next Friday (if it's Friday, it will insert that Friday's date, not the one a week later):

Sub InsertFriday()
    Selection.TypeText Date + 7 - Weekday(Date, vbSaturday)
End Sub

It will use the system's Short Date format. If you want another format, you can specify it in the macro:

Sub InsertFriday()
  Selection.TypeText Format(Date + 7 - Weekday(Date, vbSaturday), "dddd mmmm d, yyyy")
End Sub

Hi,

Word will replace all instances within the scope of the replace. If you use Ctrl+A to select all text and then run the replace, areas not reached will include headers and footers and text boxes. Names in images will not be included. This is one reason that, as a lawyer, I use templates rather than documents as the basis for new documents. Many (most?) users are not aware of this scope limitation and in the case of headers/footers may not even see them since some headers/footers may not even display when a document is edited (i.e. even-page headers).

Take a look at the document that you sent out, before and after the replace. Look in headers and footers.

For headers and footers, I seldom include client or sensitive information directly but rather use a StyeRef field to incorporate the information from the body of the document.

steve rindsberg say:

PowerPoint doesn't have margins as such, and at least in part the margins you get when you print are dependant on your printer; very few printers can print clear to the edge of the page.

Vinay has explained how to set a custom page size.

I would do something like this:

Set the page size to A4, assuming that's what you'll be printing on.  Create a slide that's nothing but a colored background.  Print it, making sure to REMOVE the check next to "Scale to fit paper".  That will cause PPT to send the slide out to the printer at full size, not scaled down to accommodate the printer's margins.

The resulting page will likely have an unprinted margin.  That'll tell you how close to the edge your printer is physically capable of printing.  In all probability, the margin will be a bit less than 1cm, in which case you can do what you're after. 

The next step would be to define a custom page size that's A4 dimensions less 20mm in both directions (10 mm border on each side). 

Shapes will land where you put them, but PPT will snap them to the edges of the slide so it'll be fairly simple to ensure that they land where you want them to.  There may be a few other tricks to get what you want but let's start with that and not try to solve problems that you don't have. ;-)

When you print, you'll need to make sure that "Scale to fit paper" remains unchecked.
