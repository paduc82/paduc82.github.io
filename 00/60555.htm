The following info for Movie Maker 2.1 will work for 2.6.

In timeline view...drag a second copy of the video to the Audio/Music
track and drag it left or right to align it to the Audio track. (This will
provide uninterrupted audio)

Then right click/mute the Audio track.

Now you can split the video anywhere you wish...Ctrl+L...and remove
a section...then insert a Photo and give it the same duration as the
segment you removed.

Here's another method:

Split your video clip on each side of the area where the stills will be.

Now...drag the cut portion to the Audio/Music track. BTW...If you wish
to add a 5 second still...remove 5 seconds of the video clip. (info below
on splitting if you need it)

Drag your still shots to the time line and drop them in the location where
you removed the video.

The Audio clip on the Audio/Music track can be dragged right or left to
adjust it's position.

More info on splitting....

"Splitting A Clip In MM2.1"

Drag the video/audio clip to the Timeline.

Play the video/audio by clicking the "Play" button under the view screen.

You will see the Playback Indicator (vertical line) advancing across the
timeline.

Click the "Pause" button at the point where you wish to trim and type...
Ctrl+L or click the "Split" button at the bottom of the view screen.
(you can also make adjustments by dragging the Playback Indicator)

Make as many splits as you want and remove the unwanted clips by right
click / delete.

No. He'd have to break through Gmail's defences again.

(2) How do I stop this from happening  

It's very unlikely that hacking your Gmail account was anything to do with Windows Live Mail. See here: Make your account more secure

I cannot now log-on to [my Gmail account] through live mail, 

What happens when you try? When the program is unable to send or receive, it will almost always display an error message explaining what went wrong. Remove any unsent messages from the outbox (below the account folders in the folder pane), then compose a new test message with a unique subject and send it to yourself (i.e. from the account that's misbehaving to the same account). 3) How can safely start using live mail again (to look at my gmail account)? 

That depends on what is wrong at the moment, which the error message will tell us. Meanwhile, I would guess that you are unable to authenticate yourself to the Gmail servers because

(a) you enabled 2-step verification, in which case you have to generate an app password and use that instead of the account password you changed to recently,

and/or

(b) you haven't enabled access to your account for 'less-secure apps'.

Both of these are explained on the Google help pages I referred to earlier. Despite appearances, (b) is not leaving you at risk.
