I'd like to answer your question orderly.

Sub Show_AddIns()
Dim ppamAddIn As AddIn
Dim pptCom As COMAddIn
Dim strReport As String
Dim strLoaded As String
strReport = "PowerPoint AddIns" & vbCrLf & vbCrLf
For Each ppamAddIn In Application.AddIns
If ppamAddIn.Loaded Then strLoaded = True Else strLoaded = False
strReport = strReport & ppamAddIn.Name & " Loaded= " & strLoaded & vbCrLf
Next
strReport = strReport & vbCrLf & "COM AddIns" & vbCrLf & vbCrLf
For Each pptCom In Application.COMAddIns
strReport = strReport & pptCom.ProgID & "Loaded: " & pptCom.Connect & vbCrLf
Next
MsgBox strReport
End Sub

Hello pears150,

The product key on the Certificate of Authenticity (CoA) on the bottom of your laptop is for the Windows installed on the computer, it is not for Office.  The bonehead from HP should have known that :-(

The product key for a full packaged retail (traditional disc) product of Office H&S 2007 would be inside the retail package that was purchased at Best Buy.  The PK is printed on a yellow-orange Proof Of License label that is located on the pivoting disc holder tray in the retail package.  Entering this PK into Office should "unlock" it and convert it to a full licensed and activated product.

There is a slight chance that the above may not work.

If you enter the PK from the package into Setup or the Activation Wizard and it is rejected as "invalid," that tells us that the type of Office installation that was put back onto the computer after running the factory recovery is the "2007 Office System" which will not accept the retail product key.  In that case, you would uninstall the 2007 Office System that got put back on the computer by the recovery process, restart, and then either install Office H&S 2007 using the full retail discs bought at Best Buy, OR, download the retail installation files for Office H&S 2007 from this link: http://www.microsoft.com/office/downloads/
