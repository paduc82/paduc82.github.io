Slow Ring is good for checking the usability of new features over time.  New builds are about once a month.

There are two branches of Fast Ring.

Fast Ring, Active Development of Windows is good if you like puzzles; i.e., finding and reporting bugs.  Big bugs.  Dangerous bugs.  Crash-your-computer-and-blow-away-your-files bugs.  New builds are about once a week.

Fast Ring, Skip Ahead is not a choice right now.  It is closed to new Insiders.  It is the same as Fast Ring, Active Development of Windows but for the version of Windows to follow the current beta.

Do not use Insider builds of any ilk for daily computing or on an office or production machine.  This is the mantra Microsoft and most veterans here repeat.  Heed the caution.

Do not use an Insider build for any gaming beyond casual gaming.  No competitive gaming.  

Always use a "secondary" device or virtual machines for testing Insider previews.

If you are not equipped for handling frequent crashes, lock-ups, trashed files, poor performance, settings glitches, etc., in an environment that does not risk valuable data then wait for the next version of Windows 10 to release to the public and adopt it then.
