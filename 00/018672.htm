jay freedman say:

This is a setting in the Trust Center > Privacy Options.

If the Document Inspector has once been used to remove personal information from the document, the setting is turned on and remains turned on in that document until the user goes to the Options dialog and turns it off. adam vero say:

I can reproduce what you are seeing, but I wonder if the problem is that you don't really want assistants, but subordinates.

Usually you would have the big boss, then senior management then middle management then those that do the real work at the bottom. In some places you may also have an assistant, for example the personal assistant to the big pointy-haired boss. They can't  be shown at the same level as the senior managers as that is not really their 'rank', but they do need to be shown reporting to the PHB. They are usually shown to one side of the line down from the boss to the next level, almost like a callout box.

Your org chart sounds like you have several assistants to one person, but I wonder if you really just mean they are direct reports.

Normally you can simply add people to the text pane next to the Smart Art - type a name and hit enter for the next person, or tab to 'indent' to the next level of hierarchy, shift-tab to outdent back up.

You will see that if you have three direct reports they are shown equally spaced in a single line (by default) rather than assistants where it tries to show them all associated with the down line and won't move them across the page.
