bill '-) say:

Yep, for sure. The typical range of WiFi (in the home) will be between a few metres and (say) about 20 metres. You'll be very unlikely (typically/on average) to ever be as close to a mobile antenna as 20 metres even in a built up area with very dense cells (and more antenna) so probably likely to be in excess of 200 Metres (or very much more!).

Thanks to the way that wireless waves propagate through the air when  you double the range, you'd need to more than double the power to maintain a useable signal, so the mobile data transmitter in your phone is highly likely to be using much more power than it is for it's WiFi  transmitter. Another reason to use WiFi whenever possible.

How to reset Word 2011 for Mac to the default settings - Microsoft Community

zauberdrache say:

How to reset Word 2011 for Mac to the default settings

Hi,

how can I reset Word 2011 for Mac to the default settings?

Kind regards,
Tom
daniel g. grau say:

There are several degrees to resetting Word, starting with the usual suspects and culminating in a total removal and reinstall of Office. Let's start with the bare minimum:


Rebuild your Normal template:
  1. Quit Word
  2. Go to the Finder
  3. Select Go > Go to folder
  4. Paste this in the blank text field: ~/Library/Application Support/Microsoft/Office/User Templates
  5. Move Normal.dotm to the Desktop (Word will rebuild a stock version)

Delete your preference settings:
  1. Repeat Steps 1-4 above with: ~/Library/Preferences
  2. Locate files com.microsoft.Word.plist and com.microsoft.Office.plist and move them to the Desktop (Word will rebuild them)
Try the above and check if they provide a fix. You can do both steps at once, or in sequence, or do one and if the problem is not fixed, restore the files to their original place and try the other step. Why do it stepwise? Because you will be losing your customizations, which are stored in such files, so the less you change, the better.

Test Word after each file movement. Once you get it running to your satisfaction, dispose of the files you dragged to the Desktop.


For a more radical resetting, you could follow the various steps except Step 2 in these instructions. However, it is possible that you'll later need to reinstall some Office components (e.g., fonts).

PS (April 14, 2015): User philmcmahon states the above deletions did not suffice to make Word start in a stable manner. He advocates a further deletion that solved his problem:

  • ~/Library/Saved Application State/com.microsoft.Word.savedState
