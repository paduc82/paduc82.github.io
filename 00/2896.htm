rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:

You can get your counts without filtering your data first. The following formula will work with or without filtering your data... since you didn't give us your layout, I will assume your Customers are listed in Column A, the Products they bought are in Column B and the date they bought the product is in Column C. I will use hard coded customer name (Customer A), product name (Product A) and month of interest (7 for July), but you can put these values in cells and then use the cell address in place of the hard coded values in this formula.

Count of "Customer A" purchased in July...
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
=SUMPRODUCT((A2:A1000="Customer A")*(MONTH(C2:C1000)=7))

Count of "Customer A" purchases of "Product A" in July
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
=SUMPRODUCT((A2:A1000="Customer A")*(B2:B1000="Product A")*(MONTH(C2:C1000)=7))

Count of "Customer A" purchases of "Product A" for all months
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
=SUMPRODUCT((A2:A1000="Customer A")*(B2:B1000="Product A"))

You can change the ranges as necessary, but there must be the same number of cells in each range used.

We tried to simulate your issue regarding categorizing the emails and we did it successfully in Outlook. But when we synched with Live Mail, the category is lost since categories are not supported in Live Mail.

 

As I read through your post, I determined that the better solution for your issue is for you to create folders and set rules to properly segregate your incoming emails. You can create folder for Business, Friends, Important, etc., and set rules to automatically move your incoming mail to their designated folders. With this approach, you will be able to see your emails in the separate folders in your Outlook, Live Mail, as well as in your phone every time it synchs automatically with Outlook.

 

To create folders and set up rules to move you incoming emails to the folders, please follow the steps below:

  1. Login to https://mail.live.com.
  2. Click on the gear icon located at the upper right part of the mailbox page and choose More mail settings.
  3. Under Customizing Outlook, click on Rules of sorting new messages.
  4. Click on New to create a new rule.
  5. On Step 1, it can be:
    Sender's address > contains > "your Company's name"
  6. On Step 2, choose:
    Move to a new folder > Business
  7. Click on Save.
  8. Repeat steps 4 to 7 if you want to create additional rules for other folders.

Please note that with the above rules in place, you will not see any more in your main inbox the emails that were moved to the new folders.
