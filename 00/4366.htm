j.e. mcgimpsey say: The reason is that, like all well-behaved Mac applications, Cut/Paste is treated as a MOVE, while Copy/Paste is treated as duplication. In that, it exactly conforms to Apple's OS X Human Interface Guidelines.

You can see that this is the case if you try this:

  With this formula in L11:
      =IF(C11=1, D11, 0)

cut (using the contextual menu or CMD-x) C11:D11 and paste to C16:D16.

L11 will now read

   =IF(C16=1, D16, 0)

So in your scenario, when you MOVE C16:D16 into C11:D11, then the original C11:D11 no longer exist, hence the #REF! errors.

Now, why WinXL would treat Cut and Copy the same? Dunno...
