You do this in one of two places. The place most people do it in is the body of the document, numbering the relevant headings. That is the easiest method.

a. user say:

Hi,

I understand that you are unable to receive emails coming from your contacts.

For us to isolate the real issue that you have, I need you to provide the following information that been asked by my co-moderator. Again, please check your private message area so you can view the questions that needed to be answered.

In the meantime, here are the settings that possibly cause the issue.

One of the possible reason why issues occur is that the emails sent to your account were received on the other folders that you have. I suggest you to check all the folders that you have on your Inbox page.

 

In addition, please refer to the steps below to know what you can do when you aren’t getting emails from friends/family.

 

A. Email Forwarding

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings in the drop down list.

2. Under "Managing your account", click Email forwarding.

Note: If it's set to forward your mail to another email account, tick on Don't forward then click Save.

 

B. Blocked Sender's List

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select “Option”.

2. Under "Preventing Junk email", click Safe and blocked senders.

3. Click Blocked senders.

4. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as Outlook.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click<< Remove from list.

 

C. Add the email address to your Safe senders list (if account was already provided)

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings in the drop down list.

2. Under "Preventing junk email", click Safe and blocked senders.

3. Click Safe Senders.

4.In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then click Add to list >>.

 

D. Check your Email Rules

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings in the drop down list.

2. Under "Customizing Outlook", click Rules for sorting new messages.

3. Perform one of the actions as per your requirement:

Click Edit next to the rule that you want to edit.

Or

Choose the rule that you want to delete and click Delete.

Note: Not all international characters are supported as filters.

 

E. Check your Junk Mail Settings

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings in the drop down list.

2. Under "Preventing junk email", click Filters and reporting.

3. Under "Choose a junk e-mail filter", choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will have been sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

 

F. Check your Reply-to address

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings in the drop down list.

2. Under "Writing email", click Reply-to address.

3. Select Current e-mail address radio button.

4. Click Save.

 

If you have further clarifications, feel free to ask.

Forms![Frm_EmailViewerUpdate].[frm_EmailViewerUpdate subform].Form.RecordSource = strLinkedTableName

 

strLinkedTableName is a variable that has the name of a Query.  I am trying to set it in a subform.

tom van stiphout say:

The correct syntax:

Forms![Frm_EmailViewerUpdate]![frm_EmailViewerUpdate subform].Form.RecordSource = strLinkedTableName

Note that the third element must be the name of the subform control, which is not necessarily the same as the SourceObject of the subform.
