I know that this thread is fairly old, but with the new Windows 10 update once again more & more people (including myself) are losing the brightness bar sliders (or the square where the battery button is that allowed you to adjust brightness in 25% increments). I have found a work around that is convenient without downloading third party apps or having to go the "long way" (which is into your Settings icon>System>Display where the brightness adjuster bar is.)

Steps to get the convenient option to adjust brightness on your taskbar:

1. Click the windows icon (start bar), go to your Settings icon>System>Notifications & Actions

2. You will now see a grid which will contain various icons and the title above should say "Quick Actions"

3. Find the "Brightness" icon (it will have a sun image) and drag it to the top row (put it anywhere you like, as long as it is in the TOP row)

4. Feel free to drag any other icons you may find useful for quick access into the TOP row

5. Close your settings

6. On your task bar on the far right there should be a square bubble (looks like a squared speech bubble, usually next to your time/date). This is known as the Action Center.

7. Click that icon, you will now a pane pop up on the right side of your screen. At the bottom of the pane your brightness icon will now be there which you can click to adjust your brightness in 25% increments. If you would like the actual slider, you will have to adjust it with the "long way" I mentioned at the beginning of this post.

Note: If you like to turn on the "night light" option to adjust to warmer tones instead of blue light, this is also possible using this method, simply drag the night light icon into the TOP row (you can put it next to the brightness icon for convenience).

Hope this helps! A simple work around to make the brightness option back to a ONE-TIME CLICK process versus going through all of your settings each time!
