Another conditional formatting bug! - Microsoft Community

sly411 say:

Another conditional formatting bug!

As per Help in Excel, Conditional format precedence are evaluated from highest to lowest. If no conflicts, then both formats are applied. If conflicts, then the higher rule is applied.

This works, except, if a higher level rule sets a fill format and the lower rule changes the fill to agradient fill effect, then the lower level gradient will overide the fill format from the higher rule when both rules are true. All the other higher level rule formats e.g. font will remain the same.  This only happens when the lower level rule sets a fill gradient; solid fill works fine i.e. the higher level rule maintains precedence.

I haven't tested it, but I suspect the problem exists in 2010 as well.
