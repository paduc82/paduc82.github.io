brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

POP simply cannot sync calendars. If your Outlook 2010 calendar was synching with the calendar on other devices, then you were not using POP. Perhaps you were using the Outlook Connector to connect to your Outlook.com mailbox. The Outlook Connector uses a protocol that syncs everything between Outlook 2010 and the Outlook.com mailbox. Other devices, like a smart phone, can use a protocol called Exchange ActiveSync, with Outlook.com, which also syncs everything. Outlook 2013 also can use EAS with the Outlook.com mailbox. So, if Outlook 2010 was connected with your Outlook.com mailbox using the Outlook Connector, Outlook 2013 was connected with EAS, and the phone was also connected with EAS, they'd all sync with the same mailbox and all contain the same thing. When you switched to POP on the one PC, you broke that sync for that PC.

Now, of course, I'm not psychic and can't see what you did, but this explanation fits everything you've said. Only you can know the true story, however, since only you were there. But I'd bet my next paycheck I'm correct.
