brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] on directly say:
Its most likely that your account has not been hacked. Rather, your address is being "spoofed" - that is, a spammer is sending out his junk using your address (not your account) as the sending address. It is easy peasy to use any address at all as the sending address without accessing the account of the address's owner. Spammers do this because they want to hide where the message truly originates.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] on directly say:
Just as the sending address can be anything the spammer wants and it has nothing to do with the real sender, so to the recipient address has nothing to do with the actual recipient. Just like when you get mail at your home, there is an envelope and contents The envelope has the real recipient address on it, while the contents can say whatever the real sender wishes and can completely disagree with what the envelope says. For email, you don't get to see the envelope, typically. The From and the To are actually part of the message contents and don't determine who sent it or who receives it.

So, in your case, the spammer is using the same string before the @. What comes after the @ doesn't really mean anything in terms of delivery.

bill manville say:

That the GPF error occurs indicates a bug in Excel or in a library that it uses, not a bug in your program.
However, there are some unusual features in your code that might provoke a problem.

The use of OXL1 is unnecessary and potentially confusing.
You can remove all references to OXL1 and just use Application.EnableCancelKey etc.

You are clearing column A of Sheet3 and then attempting to add a new querytable to that sheet, with data in more than just column A.  I would use Sheet3.UsedRange.Clear to clear everything from Sheet3 before adding the new querytable.

The error is a failure in a component of Excel itself. 
I don't believe that Excel is able to resurrect that component so there is no way for you to handle the error and continue.  You could catch the error and give the user a different message.

Private Sub Worksheet_Change(ByVal Target As Range)
  On Error Goto Whoops
  <your code>
  Exit Sub
Whoops:
  If Err.Number = -2147417848 Then  
    MsgBox "Sorry, Excel has failed.  Please exit the program and start again"
    If Application.UserName = "Jenifer2705" Then  ' or whoever your copy of Excel has as its username
      Stop  ' give yourself a chance to find which statement is causing the problem.

  End If
