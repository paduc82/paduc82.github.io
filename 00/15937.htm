To do it right, you will need a temporary String variable to manipulate while you straighten the text out. Here is the code snippet to do that...

Dim TempString As String
....
....
TempString = Me.TextBox1.Value & ", " & Me.TextBox2.Value & ", " & Me.TextBox3.Value & vbLf & _
             Me.TextBox4.Value & ", " & Me.TextBox5.Value & ", " & Me.TextBox6.Value
Do While InStr(TempString, ", , ")

Vacation accrual - Microsoft Community

ttocsdor say:

Vacation accrual

I am trying to develop a spreadsheet that tracks vacation accrued and taken.  The vacation accrues by 40 hrs every 120 days an employee works from date of hire.  Can't figure out the formula. Thanks!!!

jllatham say:

Subtracting the current date from their start date gives you the number of days between the dates, but that's going to include weekends and holidays.

The function NETWORKDAYS() will calculate the number of workdays between two dates, so if the start date is in cell A2 and the current date is in B2

=NETWORKDAYS(A2,B2) will give you the number of days worked.

Take that number and divide it by the 120 days required for accrual and that gives you the number of 40 hour periods worked.  Multiply that by 40 hours to get the time accrued.

A2 = 1/1/2014

B2 = 3/14/2014

53 workdays (and NETWORKDAYS() can be set up to account for holidays also)

53/120 =0.4167 of a full accrual period, and .4167 * 40 = 16.668 hours accrued.

Written into a single formula:

=NETWORKDAYS(A2,B2)/120*40

jllatham say:

There is a difference when using the DATEDIF() function and the NETWORKDAYS() function.

DATEDIF() gives you the total number of days difference, including weekends and holidays - and I doubt if your people are working 7 days a week?

NETWORKDAYS() gives you the number of workdays between the two dates:  it excludes weekends and optionally you can give it a reference to a list of dates representing holidays or other dates that are not to be counted as workdays.
