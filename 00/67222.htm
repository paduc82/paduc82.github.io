¡firedog say:

This has nothing to do with the general deprecation of gadgets on security grounds.

The recent problems with gadgets seem to be linked to the upgrade of Internet Explorer to v.11. This version of the browser seems to be particularly sensitive to display options, and if the screen DPI is set to anything other than 100%, some things - including gadgets - may be prone to display faults.

Use display options in Control Panel to reset the screen DPI to 100% to test this hypothesis. If your gadgets are resurrected, you'll have to decide which is more important - higher DPI or functioning gadgets. Mine all work properly at 105% DPI. You could also uninstall the IE11 update to restore your previous version pending a potential improvement in a later update.


There's plenty of discussion in the IE forum, so this thread might just as well stay here to spread the message.
