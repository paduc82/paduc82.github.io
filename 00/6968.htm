  1. Log in to http://mail.live.com with your custom domain email address

  1. Log in to http://www.outlook.com with your custom domain email address
  2. From your inbox, click the options "cog" icon in the top right corner, and select "More mail settings" from the drop-down list
  3. Under the "Managing your account" heading on the "Options" page, click on "Create a Outlook alias"
  4. Follow the prompts to setup your new @outlook.com alias

robert aldwinckle on forums say:
Oops! Google Chrome could not find download.microsoft.com


It's not that simple.  E.g. in a cmd window use nslookup to see what that host name entails just to do a lookup:

<nslookup>

 

Non-authoritative answer:
Name:    a767.ms.akamai.net
Addresses:  184.84.243.27, 184.84.243.66
Aliases:  download.microsoft.com, download.microsoft.com.nsatc.net
          main.dl.ms.akadns.net, dom.dl.ms.akadns.net, dl.ms.d4p.net

</nslookup>

If the complete lookup for it is not cached IE is at the mercy of the responsiveness of your DNS.  This is why it sometimes works a second time.  The first try partially caches some of the lookup and then the second try can complete it.

 

Unfortunately in order to get a usable cached lookup for IE to use you might need to do lookups for all of those aliases plus the canonical name.  The latter is the most important because it is where the IP addresses come from but even if you had that cached if you didn't have the intervening chaining to it there might be enough of a delay due to slow lookup for IE to abandon its request.

To see how this works you can use  ping -n 1 -w 1  and  ipconfig /displaydns.  First flush the dnscache: 

ipconfig  /flushdns

Then ping the host name that you want to use:

ping -n 1 -w 1  download.microsoft.com

Then inspect what that accomplished in terms of getting a lookup done for IE to use:

ipconfig  /displaydns

All that has been accomplished is to find that the host name that you want has an alias and that now a (recursive) lookup needs to be done for that alias:

download.microsoft.com
----------------------------------------
Record Name . . . . . : download.microsoft.com
Record Type . . . . . : 5
Time To Live . . . . : 7
Data Length . . . . . : 4
Section . . . . . . . : Answer
CNAME Record . . . . : download.microsoft.com.nsatc.net

Also, note that the Time To Live (in seconds) is really quite short so if IE is to get any benefit of the cached lookup you had better be quick getting it started after achieving the caching.  In fact, it might be necessary to put all of your commands into a cmd file including one to get iexplore.exe (or chrome.exe) using the specified host name as soon as possible.  On the other hand it could still be helpful to do all the steps to get the lookup cached manually in the OS because it could have the effect of making the DNS servers, both yours and any downstream ones which were involved in the lookup, to be ready to improve the performance of the lookup the second time it is tried (within their TTL <w>).  Then your browser would not be able to state that it "could not find download.microsoft.com"


That's just the getting the lookup done.  Let's say that the lookup is working.  You still have to connect to an HTTP server (port address 80) and get a response from it.   You can try to do that with telnet but those servers seem to be particular about using HTTP 1.1 and don't give us too much time to do an HTTP 1.1 simulation either.
