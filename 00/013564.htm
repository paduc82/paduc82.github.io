Week 1 = $100

joeu2004 say: K wrote:

How do I create a formula that takes an original value, and decreases the original value by half of a percent progressively over 140 occasions?
Example. Original value of $100 reduced by 1/2 % each week
Week 1 = $100
Week 2 = $99.50
Week 3 = $99.00
and so on.


Unfortunately, your example is ambiguous.

Are you reducing by $0.50 each week -- that is, 0.5% of the initial value?  (Straight-line depreciation?)

Or are you reducing the remaining balance by 0.5% each week?  (Declining-balance depreciation?)

Note that $99.50*(1-0.5%) is $99.0025, which might be displayed as $99.00 typically.

Also, do you want a weekly result -- a column of formulas?

Or do you want a single formula ("a formula") that computes the final balance?



The final balance is:

=MAX(0, 100-140*100*0.5%)

More generally, put 100 into A1 (initial value), 0.5% into A2 (reduction rate), and 140 into A3 (number of weeks), and substitute the cell references in the formula, to wit:

=MAX(0, ROUND(A1-A3*A1*A2,2))

The use of MAX(0,...) is a precaution for the general case.  It ensures that the original amount is not diminished to less than zero.  Replace 0 with any other "residual value".

The use of ROUND eliminates rounding errors for the general case.

For the weekly balance, if the initial value ($100) is in C1, put the following formula into C2 and copy down 140 times:

=MAX(0, ROUND(C1-$A$1*$A$2,2))

Note:  You might notice a difference in the final value due to weekly rounding.



The final balance is:

=MAX(0, ROUND(FV(-A2, A3, 0, -A1),2))

The use of -A1 is a personal choice.  It ensures that the result of FV is a positive number.  It can be different ways.

For the weekly balance, if the initial value ($100) is in C1, put the following formula into C2 and copy down 140 times:

=MAX(0, ROUND(C1*(1-$A$2),2))

Again, you might notice a difference in the final value due to weekly rounding.
