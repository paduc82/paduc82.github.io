Sub Picture_Click()
  Dim S As Shape
  Dim SaveHeight As Single, SaveWidth As Single
  With ActiveSheet.Shapes(Application.Caller)
    'Save width and height
    SaveHeight = .Height
    SaveWidth = .Width
    'Enlarge it
    .Height = .Height * 2
    If Not .LockAspectRatio Then _
      .Width = .Width * 2
    DoEvents
    'wait 4 seconds
    Application.Wait (Now + TimeValue("00:00:04"))
    'Restore the picture
    .Height = SaveHeight
    .Width = SaveWidth
  End With
End Sub

 Sub EnablePictureClick()
  Dim S As Shape
  For Each S In ActiveSheet.Shapes
    If S.Type = msoPicture Then
      S.OnAction = "Picture_Click"
    End If
  Next
End Sub

CUMIPMT formula in Excel 2007 - Microsoft Community

tjtp say:

CUMIPMT formula in Excel 2007

Will the CUMIPMT formula only give the correct answer if you enter the calculated payment?  For example, I want to use a monthly payment amount of $1895 rather than the calculated payment of $1652.42 (Loan amount $263,998.00, rate of 4.375%, term of 240 months) and I am trying to get the interest paid for the first 60 payments.  If I use the calculated payment amount in the CUMIPMT formula I get the correct amount of interest, but when I enter the higher payment amount the result is not correct based on an amortization schedule for the same loan.  Thanks for any help with this!

joeu2004 say: TJTP wrote:
Will the CUMIPMT formula only give the correct answer if you enter the calculated payment?

In Excel 2003 (I do not have Excel 2007), CUMIPMT has no way to pass the actual payment.  So yes, of course it uses the minimum payment, namely what PMT would return.

TJTP wrote:

For example, I want to use a monthly payment amount of $1895 rather than the calculated payment of $1652.42 (Loan amount $263,998.00, rate of 4.375%, term of 240 months) and I am trying to get the interest paid for the first 60 payments.

If A1 is 263,998, A2 is 4.375%, B2 is =A2/12 (periodic interest rate), A3 is 240, A4 is 1895, and A5 and A6 are the starting and ending period numbers, you can compute the cumulative interest using:

=A4*(A6-A5+1)-FV(B2,A5-1,A4,-A1)+MAX(0,FV(B2,A6,A4,-A1))

Note:  If the last payment is the same as the regular payment, the straight-forward formula A4*(...)-FV(...)+FV(...) would not count the excess payment (more than the interest and last outstanding balance) as interest [1].  I think we should.  The MAX(0,...) expression accomplishes that.

-----
Endnotes:

[1] Technically, the excess is a finance charge, not interest.  The point is:  it is not principal.
