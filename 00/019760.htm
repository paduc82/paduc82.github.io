Good question.  Sway attempts to display your content at an ideal size to fit on whatever screen it is on.  In the first screenshot, Sway has the entire window size to work with and shows each image at its native height/width ratio.  In the slideshow control from your second screenshot, however, it is sizing that control to fit all of your images which could potentially have different height/width ratios.  Additionally, Sway content is sized to fit whatever window you show it in.  While the navigation layout and design type you pick for your Sway will influence this, I think you will find that if you resize your window, you will end up with different sized content and the black bars may or may not be there.  All of that is to say, you may not be able to remove them completely.

zorvek say:
Here is a UDF that will give you the correct answer. Add this code to a general code module:

Public Function NetWorkHours(ByVal StartTime As Date, ByVal EndTime As Date, ByVal StartOfDayTimes As Variant, ByVal EndOfDayTimes As Variant)

    Dim Day As Long
   
    For Day = Fix(StartTime) + 1 To Fix(EndTime) - 1
        NetWorkHours = NetWorkHours + EndOfDayTimes(Weekday(Day)) - StartOfDayTimes(Weekday(Day))
    Next Day
    NetWorkHours = NetWorkHours + Application.Max(0, EndOfDayTimes(Weekday(Fix(StartTime))) - Application.Max((StartTime - Int(StartTime)) * 24, StartOfDayTimes(Weekday(Fix(StartTime)))))
    NetWorkHours = NetWorkHours + Application.Max(0, Application.Min((EndTime - Int(EndTime)) * 24, EndOfDayTimes(Weekday(Fix(EndTime)))) - StartOfDayTimes(Weekday(Fix(EndTime))))

End Function

The formula to calculate the net work hours is:

    =NetWorkHours(A1,B1,{0,8,8,8,8,8,0},{0,16,16,16,16,13,0})

where A1 is the start Date/Time and B1 is the end DateTime.
