rebeccaprescott say:

I have a Nokia Lumia 900 and it recently stopped playing any type of video/clips, is there something I can do to fix this?

I don't get error messages, I just can't play video anymore; including video clips I record using my phone.

a. user say:

Hello RebeccaPrescott:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the issue you are having with video playback.  This sort of issue tends to indicate there is an issue with your phone software, hardware or with the video files themselves.

The first thing I would like you to try doing is connecting your phone to a computer with the Zune software.  Via the software you should be able to copy these videos taken on the phone to your computer, http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/how-to/wp7/music/sync-files-with-my-phone .  The purpose of this is for you to then play this video via the Zune software, Windows Media Player or some other media management software.  If the video plays here without issue we know the files are fine.  If the videos do not play in this fashion either, this could be part of the issue.

If the videos do playback on your computer then we need to turn our focus to your phone.  The first thing I would suggest you do is a soft reset.  I would start this by going to settings > applications > Internet Explorer > delete history.  Once the history is deleted, press and hold the volume keys, power button and camera together for 3-5 seconds.  After the phone reboots, try playing the videos on your phone and even try creating a new video and play that back.

If the issue continues from here the next thing to consider is a factory reset of the phone.  Keep in mind that this will set the phone back to it's factory settings.  Media can be backed up to your PC, http://support.xbox.com/en-US/zune/software/syncing-media.  Contacts and calendar events should be backed up to the cloud.  You can verify this by going to http://contacts.live.com and http://calendars.live.com.  The information on your phone should be listed here.  Text message history and game progress will be reset.  To reset the phone, tap settings > about > reset your phone.

If doing the above does not lead to progress, I am afraid the last thing to consider is this is hardware related and you will want to discuss repair or replacement options with your mobile operator or Nokia.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns.

Mark W.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

None of the suggestions are working for you because what you have displayed in the screen shot is not Gridlines - it's Text Boundaries. Go to Word> Preferences - View to clear the check for Text Boundaries.

The words you're referring to (on the bottom edge of the Ribbon) are Group Titles. If you don't want them to display, clear the corresponding check at the bottom of that same View Preferences pane.

You cannot find the View tab [not menu) because your document window is too narrow to display the entire Ribbon. Note the >> to the right of the Layout tab... Either widen the window to display the remaining tabs or click that expander to display a listing of the unseen tabs.

[Note that one of the >> symbols indicates the same situation with your Quick Action Toolbar (QAT) at the top of the document window. That's why you cannot see all tools on the QAT or the document Title. Widening the window will address that as well.
