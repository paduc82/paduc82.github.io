john mcghie say:
The most important thing you can do to prevent this happening again is to NEVER save or edit documents to a removable drive.  Just don't do it!

The reason is that removable drives sometimes suffer read-write errors, or may run out of space.  If they do that while you have a document open, this is what happens.  Chances are, when you want to create the PDF, Word attempted to latch a space twice the size of the document on the flash drive, couldn't get it, and this is the result.

If you try "File>Open" from inside Word, using the Word File Menu, that Word File MAY open up.  A work file is like the scribbled notes on my desk at work: some of them are complete documents, some of them are empty, or anything in between.

Now you have your document back, you can delete that work file.  These things are temporary files: if Word had not crashed, it would have deleted it for you.

In future, NEVER work on a flash drive.  Work on the local drive, save and close the document, then use the Finder to copy the result to the flash drive.  You don't want this to happen again :-)

Cheers
