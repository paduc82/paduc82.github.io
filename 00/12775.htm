CJCMEG -

 

Thank you for keeping in touch in regards to your query about managing the contacts on your phone and accounts!

 

All of the contacts on your phone should be linked with the primary Microsoft Account that you used to set up the phone with (listed as Windows Live under Settings -> Email+Accounts).  When you add a second email address, if you set it up to sync contacts it will import those contacts and add them to the primary account's contacts.  You can view all of the contacts currently linked to your Microsoft Account here:

 

http://contacts.live.com

 

There is an option as soon as you sign in there to import contacts from another account, or from a CSV file.  Most other email providers have a way to export contacts as a CSV file which gets saved on your local harddrive.  If you want to export the contacts from your Microsoft Account, you'd go to Manage on the website, and there will be an Export option in that dropdown.  There should be a way to import from CSV in Outlook.  In the end, all contacts on your phone will pretty much always be linked to your Microsoft Account, which is how the phone is set up to back things up.

 

If you're wanting to move contacts around between other accounts, separate from the Microsoft Account, you'd want to contact support for those email providers to find out how to do that through their systems, but as Entegy mentioned this wouldn't be done through the phone, but through their website or software on your PC.

If your touchscreen doesnt respond correctly to touch, if some gestures dont work follow the methods below:

a)      Press Windows key + X and click on Control Panel.

e)      Note: If the Reset button is grayed out, your Surface touch calibration is still at factory settings and you can move to method 2.

f)       Use the Tab key to select Yes, press Enter, and then follow the instructions on the screen.

g)      After computer removes recalibration data, test touch input again.

Method 2:

Install the latest driver for the touch screen.

a) Press Windows Key +R, type devmgmt.msc to open the Device Manager.
