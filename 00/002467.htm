I do daily reports using Excel 2010, so how can I combine them all for - Microsoft Community

sarahnelson-bihm say:

I do daily reports using Excel 2010, so how can I combine them all for my monthly report as a workbook?

I work for a oil and gas refinery and keep track of all trucks that bring in product and pick up product.  I also keep track of our barges that bring in crude or sometimes product and ship out the same.  My job is to everyday enter into my Excel spreadsheet each truck ticket of what was picked up or brought in, and also enter in what was brought in or taken out by our barges.  This gives me a total of how much crude we have to run the plant, and how much we have of all the different products as well.  At the end of the month, I am to do a monthly report for my boss and would like to be able to have every daily report link together to make my monthly report.  I have tried this a few times, but it just has not come out right.  I can do workbooks when they are only 2 or 3 worksheets, but for some reason have not been able to do one this big.  Does anyone have any advice?

 

I am desperate to save time!! S.N.

charley kyd say: Sarah,

Try this: 

Set up an Excel Table with columns that describe each ticket. (That is, use Insert, Tables, Table.) Include date, additions to inventory (as plus values), reductions from inventory (as negative values), and so on. (Alternatively, maintain one column for additions and another column for reductions.) Make sure all relevant data is in the Table.

Then, in separate workbooks, create all your reports (daily, weekly and monthly) to summarize the data from that Table in your database workbook. That is, don't add up daily or weekly reports to create monthly reports; instead, reference your original

To summarize the values, you'll probably use SUMPRODUCT and/or SUMIFS. SUMPRODUCT usually is more powerful, but SUMIFS typically calculates more quickly. You might also use array formulas, but I'd start with SUMPRODUCT instead.

(See: http://exceluser.com/blog/1272/the-two-functions-you-must-know-to-return-values-from-excel-tables-and-databases.html)

To return labels or other unique values, you'll probably use INDEX-MATCH. (See: http://exceluser.com/blog/465/excels-best-lookup-method-index-match.html)

And you never would use VLOOKUP here, in spite of its popularity. Instead, INDEX-MATCH is much more flexible and powerful. (See:http://exceluser.com/blog/1107/why-index-match-is-far-better-than-vlookup-or-hlookup-in-excel.html)

In your reports, set up cells to specify beginning and ending dates. That way, you can reuse your reports period after period. You just add new data to your Table, change the dates, and then recalculate.

Also, if you want to report activity for specific barges, trucks, or other entities, you can do it easily with this setup. You just add cells to contain that filter information and then you expand your SUMPRODUCT or SUMIFS formulas to include that criteria. 

After you meet your immediate reporting needs, you might find other useful ways to report this data. For example, it might be useful to summarize the total number of shipments by hour in the day, which might identify scheduling issues. Or if you include prices for the deliveries in your Table, you might be able to value your inventory under either LIFO or FIFO. 

Hope that helps.

Charley
