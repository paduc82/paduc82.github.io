slance310 say:

Hi,

 

I have recently done this myself... A lot of SSD's come with a program to "Clone" your Hard Drive to the SSD... A lot of times it's a Acronis product...

 

Since your SSD is most likely going to be smaller than your HDD, the partition would need to be resized... which the cloning software should do, but you will need to check, depending on what you end up with...

 

In my case, I already had a copy of Acronis TrueImage which comes on a bootable CD...

 

1) I hooked an external HDD up to my computer and booted from the Acronis CD...

    You can also use a 2nd internal drive if you have one

2) I "Backed Up" my OS partition to an "IMAGE" on the external drive..., instead of Cloning...

   This way, I have a permanent copy I can reload at any time...

3) I removed the HDD with the OS on it, and put the SSD in it's place...

4) I again booted with the CD and this time "RECOVERED" the image to the SSD...

 

I then rebooted my machine to windows, and was finished... No reactivating or whatever necessary.

 

Cloning should work about the same except you don't have a spare image if something goes wrong.... But as long as Win 8.1 is still on the HDD, you can try again...

 

You can then save the HDD for backup in case the SSD fails, or reformat it and use it for storage.

johann eva say:

Hi HotDogs19,

Windows Update resets graphics card driver due to major changes in the software. What is the version of your graphics card driver? Otherwise, you need to perform the workarounds below to troubleshoot it.

Important: Check the website of your graphics card manufacturer for the latest drivers and download it. If it is not available, contact your manufacturer to check if they have the diagnostic tools to scan your video cards’ performance and compatibility with your current system.

Option #1: Perform a driver update in the Device Manager. This method lets your computer detects available resources to download new driver versions if you are connected to the Internet.

1. Press Windows + X.

2. Click Device Manager.
3. Expand Display adapters.
4. Double-click the driver to open the Properties window.
5. Click Driver tab, then click “Update Driver…”
6. Wait for the installation to finish.

7. Restart your PC to apply the changes.

Option #2: Download and run our easy fix solutions from this link: How to use Microsoft easy fix solutions. Once there, click the Find and fix problems with devices and hardware hyperlink. Download and run it to start the troubleshooting.

Let us know if updating your graphics card resolves this issue.
