suzanne s. barnhill say:
Okay, let's back up. By design, Word preserves Spacing Before at the top of the first page of a document or section or after a manual ("hard") page break. After an automatic ("soft") page break, the Spacing Before is suppressed (Spacing After is also suppressed at the bottom of a page). The Compatibility Options recommended to you are irrelevant because they do the opposite of what you want (they suppress the spacing after a hard page or column break; there is no option to suppress after a section break).
 
If your heading style will always appear at the top of a page, then you can include "Page break before" in the style formatting, and the spacing will be retained; if not, you can add the PBB formatting to specific headings as required (this is preferable to inserting a manual page break for a number of reasons).
 
As others have pointed out, Spacing Before and After are not cumulative unless you enable the Compatibility Option "Don't use HTML paragraph auto spacing."

hansv mvp say:

A .csv file is a plain text file. If you save a workbook as a .csv file, the only thing that is saved are the values. All formulas, formatting, data validation, macros etc. will be lost.

If you want to keep data validation, save the workbook as an Excel workbook, not as a .csv file.
