rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:
Any characters included between the quote marks delineating a text constant have no meaning other than the individual characters you see. So, as you constructed it, VB sees cell1 as a c followed by an e followed by two l's followed by 1 as a text character. In order to make VB see cell1 as the variable name,  you must concatenate it in to the text, this way it won't be included between quote marks. Try it like this...

Sheets(sh).Cells(r, 4).Formula = "=BDP(D$3,""px_last""," & cell1 & "," & cell1 & ")"
