jllatham say:

Merging cells: bad idea for pretty much anything except labels.  Could be the cause of the problems.  To test, try unmerging them and then setting up your links.

One way to get the APPEARANCE of merged cells on a row is to select the group of cells you would have merged and then go to Format --> Format Cells then on the [Alignment] tab in the Horizontal Alignment area choose the "Center Across Selection" option.  Now when you make an entry in the leftmost of the group, it will appear centered across the entire group - and your linking formulas and anything in macros should work much better for you.

paulcordts say: Check the formula for the one giving you the #Value Error.  If the formula is =Sheet2!E3:F3, =Sheet2!E3:E7 or some other link to a range of cells that would be the issue.  When using the mouse to select a cell for a formula, if the target cell is a merged cell you will get the entire range.  Edit the formula to remove the range and just keep the first cell.  =Sheet2!E3

One caution on the "Center Across Selection" method (which I have also used).

Sometimes this can make data appear to be in a different column than it actually is.  Not a big deal if it your workbook since you know what you did.  It can confuse other users who may not be familiar with the method.  They will "see" data in one column but when they position the cursor on the cell where they see the data the formula bar will be empty.

It is of course just a column or two over, but some people seem to have a hard time with this concept.

4. Under the Safe and blocked senders, click on Blocked senders.
5. In the Blocked senders list, add the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you don't want to receive mail from, and then click Add to list >>.

You can create rules to delete incoming junk mails. Here's how to:

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on More mail settings.
2. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
3. Click New and then under Step 1, click the drop down arrow on the first box and choose Sender's address.
4. Under Step 1, type in on the third box the email address.
5. Under Step 2, choose Delete these messagesâ€”don't even put them in my Deleted folder.
6. Click Save at the bottom of the page.

You may also set your Junk email filter to Exclusive. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will be sent to the Junk folder. By default, the Junk folder will automatically delete messages after 10 days. This is more convenient as we don't have any control if senders are randomly changing their address.

 

Here are the steps on how to set to Exclusive:

 

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on More mail settings.

2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

3. Under Choose a junk email filter, select Exclusive
