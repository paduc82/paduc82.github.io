Sub Demo()
Application.ScreenUpdating = False
With ActiveDocument.Range
  With .Find
    .ClearFormatting
    .Replacement.ClearFormatting
    .Text = "\[[!\{]@\{"
    .Replacement.Text = ""
    .Forward = True

    .Find.Execute

Yes, you can rename your account back to your previous @Hotmail, @Live or @Outlook address.  To do so, just complete the following steps:

 

Note: The following steps will currently only work if your previous account was @Hotmail, @Live or @Outlook ending in .com, .jp, .kr, es, .au, .ca, etc.  This rename process will not work if you previously renamed an @MSN.com account.  There is no way to rename an @Outlook, @Hotmail or @Live account back to an @MSN.com account at this time.

 

  1. In your browser, navigate to https://account.live.com/ManageAssocIds
  2. Sign in with your new @outlook, @hotmail or @live account.  (if you are already signed in with your outlook.com account this step will not apply)
  3. Under ‚ÄúOther email addresses‚Äù click the Remove link below your previous address.
  4. Click the Remove button to confirm the message.
  5. You should now see a message on the page stating, ‚ÄúYou've removed an email address from your Microsoft account.‚Äù
  6. Navigate to https://account.live.com/ChangeId.aspx in your browser.
  7. Verify that ‚ÄúCreate a new email address‚Äù is selected.
  8. In the New Microsoft account box, enter your previous address and select your previous domain (hotmail.com or live.com).  Note:  You must enter your previous address or the process will fail.
  9. Select the Save button.

 

Now you can use your original @Hotmail, @Live.com or @Outlook account to sign into your Microsoft services as you did before renaming your account.  The @Outlook, @Hotmail or @Live address will now be an alias on your account from which you can still send and receive messages within Outlook or Hotmail.

I cancelled my Business Essentials subscription today. How can I retrieve all the emails I had in my Exchange accounts?
ruel mayoya msft say:

Hi Opal45,

If you are an admin and can access Exchange Admin Center (EAC), you can use e-discovery search to recover the emails as an Outlook Data File (PST).

To search the emails in e-Discovery:

  1. Check if your account has Discovery Management Role permissions. EAC > permissions > admin roles > add ‚ÄúDiscovery Management‚Äù
  2. Go to EAC > Compliance management > In-Place eDiscovery & Hold, specify the needed information to filter the mailbox you need to search for emails.
  3. Copy the search results to a discovery mailbox (optional)
  4. Export the search results to a PST file.

Feel free to post back if you have further questions.
