Another tip is that wireless signals are usually stronger when going down, for example, when the router is above you, the signal would be better than if you were a floor above it, that's why most large buildings with Wi-Fi will have their wireless receivers on the roof to amplify the signal downwards.

There are instances that if you quickly and repeatedly insert and remove a USB device, the USB port may stop responding. When the port is in this state, it no longer recognizes any USB device, and the USB device will not work.

andre da costa say:
If the license came with the previous motherboard (this means it is an OEM License). OEM Licenses once installed and activated are not transferable to another motherboard.

If its a full version retail license, you can transfer it to another computer, but you will need to reactivate by telephone.

If its a upgrade retail license, you can transfer it to another computer as long as the computer you are transfering to has a qualifying license installed such as Windows XP, Vista or Windows 7.
