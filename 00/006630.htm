tony gore say:

Suggested improvement to Outlook

I have a suggested improvement. Just as we can click and tag an email as "Junk", why not add the facility to tag as "scam".

What would this do?

Firstly, it would move it to a folder similar to "junk" where it would disable all the links to protect you. Why a separate folder - well as a lot of stuff ends in junk without being tagged, most people have to at least take a quick look through junk for the odd thing that might have slipped through. Ideally something you have clearly identified as a scam doesn't want to be in there. Why not just delete scams, I hear you ask?

Well I keep them - partly so I can show people what they look like, so they don't respond to them. Sometimes, you may want to keep them as information/evidence.

But my main reason for not just deleting them is that they could be part of developing another layer of protection.

For example, the action of marking it as a scam could also trigger actions to help protect. With the growing compute power, this saved scam could be used as a template and a background process compare new emails against it. (This whole train of thought is down to getting very similar scan emails in quick succession - ones imploring me to unblock my email, which of course isn't blocked or I would not have been able to receive the email...).

But furthermore, the same trigger could send information back to the Exchange server that a particular email is a scam. Once a few people have marked it as a scam, a system can start to analyse them for what is common and what is different in order to build a template to match scams against. The all future emails flowing through the system can be tagged as "potential scam" and go straight into the user's "scam" folder, where they would be sandboxed until the user actively flagged them as safe.

Thus we would be using the collective intelligence of those of us who can spot the scams more easily to protect the community of email users.

I am sure there are details that would need to be worked out, such as mechanisms to prevent abuse e.g. by a group of users acting in concert to tag legitimate emails as "scams", and whether it should be only server side, or it could be client side or a combination of the two. There is a lot more to think about - for example, an option for just one user on a domain to flag an email as a scam to then ensure that all very similar copies are automatically flagged as potential scams and diverted into everyone's "scam" folder; this would allow someone such as an admin to immediately get it out of all their users' inboxes, as it is not uncommon for scams to be sent to every valid email address at a domain.

If you read this and think it is a potentially interesting idea, then comment, and hopefully Microsoft may then think about it.

Sometimes if you persist long enough, it is possible to help reduce the scammers. After several attempts over a period of time, I eventually got the some of the UK Government sites to stop putting links in their emails on the basis that everyone who had an account knew how to access it, and once people got to know that genuine emails didn't have links, they had one means of separating out the genuine notices from the scammers. So now when I get an email that says there is a tax notice waiting for me and there is no link in it, I am fairly sure it is genuine, and since I am going to log on without clicking a link in the email, I am far less exposed anyway.
