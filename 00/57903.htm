i did not press anything that would delete what's inside my file

This is probably caused by a trap that many people fall into (except for those who can touch-type). It goes like this:

  1. You type away, keeping your eyes on the keyboard.
  2. You start a new sentence like so: About five minutes later . . .
  3. Instead of pressing Shift+A, you mistakenly pressed Ctrl+A. This marks the whole text in your document.
  4. When you now press the letter b (in "About"), all of the marked text is replaced by the letter b. You could undo the mistake by pressing Ctrl+Z immediately but because your eyes are on the keyboard instead of the screen you do not notice what is going on.

There are two things you should now do:

  • Back up your file to an external device regularly, e.g. every two hours. Lots of people are under the impression that backing up files is for wimps. They usually change their mind after suffering a major disaster.
  • Right-click the problem file, then left-click "Restore previous version". If "System Restore" works as it should then you can select a number of dates from which you wish to restore the lost text. If the current (truncated) version contains any text that you wish to keep then you must save the file under a different name before using the System Restore method.

I would also recommend learning how to touch-type. Very handy, much faster, far fewer mistakes. There are numerous courses you can download from the Internet.
