To prevent duplicates from alias accounts, you must configure the alias account not to receive email messages since they are already being received by the original account:

  1. Click File.
  2. Go to Options, then select the Advanced tab.

  3. Click Edit.
  4. Select the alias account.
  5. Uncheck the Receive mail items box then click OK.

Hi JacoZR,

Since you are talking about OneDrive credentials, I suppose you have your password changed. You can follow the steps below to update your credentials:

1. Sign out and exit all Office applications.

2. Removing all the stored credentials in the credentials manager (Control Panel > User Accounts > Credential Manager > Windows Credentials). 

3. Run regedit as administrator.

4. From Registry Editor, browse to:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\16.0\Common\Identity    

5. Delete the Identity folder.

6. Open an Office application like Word and sign in with the Office 365 account.

7. Note: Before you modify the registry, back up the registry for restoration in case of problems occur. 



WARNING: Using Registry Editor incorrectly can cause serious problems that may require you to reinstall your operating system. Microsoft cannot guarantee that problems resulting from the incorrect use of Registry Editor can be solved. Use Registry Edit at your own risk.
