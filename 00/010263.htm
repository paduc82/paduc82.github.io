ronrosenfeld say:

I am guessing (again, examples would be better) that you only want to have in your second table information about those that were absent.  You could, of course, filter your original table.

But for a formula that shows the route number only for the Absent drivers,

This formula must be **array-entered**:

M21:  =IFERROR(INDEX($A$1:$A$32,SMALL(($D$2:$D$32="A")*ROW($A$2:$A$32),COUNTIF($D$2:$D$32,"<>A")+ROWS($1:1))),"")

----------------------------------------

(The $A$1 after the INDEX function is NOT a typo.  To return the Driver Name, replace $A$1:$A$32 with $C$1:$C$32

and fill down.

To **array-enter** a formula, after entering
the formula into the cell or formula bar, hold down
<ctrl><shift> while hitting <enter>.  If you did this
correctly, Excel will place braces {...} around the formula.
