lreska say:

How do i global replace a carriage return with a semi colon in a cell?

How do I do a global replace a carriage return with a semi colon in a cell?

 

jeeped say:

I believe you probably want a line feed (e.g. ASCII 0×0A or dec 10) rather than a carriage return (e.g. ASCII 0×0D or dec 13).

If the problem is the line feed character (e.g. CHAR(10)), tap Ctrl+H.

Put the cursor in Find what: and hold the right ALT key down while typing 0010 on the numpad. Release the ALT key.

Tap the Tab key and put a semi-colon in the Replace with: text box and click Replace All.

 

Addendum: a hard return is typically CHAR(13)+CHAR(10) while a soft return is usually just CHAR(10). These are invisibile characters but can be exposed with formulas like:

=CODE(RIGHT(A1,1))

rohn007 say:

Stefan has touched on the answer to your problem.

Word 2013 does NOT perform OCR to extract text from PDFs.  Instead it depends on the PDF to contain text and images in separate layers.  It only "extracts" the selectable text in the PDF.  In your case there is none.

You will have to find software that does perform OCR, extracting text from images.  Fortunately one such program is OneNote.  You can either insert the PDF into oneNote. If that does not work, you can open the PDF in your PDF reader program, then Print it, selecting the "Send to OneNote" printer.

Once it is in OneNote, you can right click on the image and select on of the 2 "Copy text from ... " options.  Then you can paste the copied text into Word.
