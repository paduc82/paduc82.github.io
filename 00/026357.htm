You are misunderstanding what is said in the article: How do I open Publisher 95 in Vista: http://www.ehow.com/how_6919352_do-ms-publisher-95-vista_.html#ixzz1mzTxGj00 This article is saying that if after you install Publisher 95 you have problems running it, then you can run it in 'compatibility mode' under Windows 95. It is not saying that you can change the properties of an individual publisher file. If after installing Pub 95 it does not run correctly then you change the properties of the Publisher program, not the individual files.

What operating system is on the computer where you ran Pub 95? Is it Win95, Win98se or what? The article suggests that you may need to run under Win95 but I am not sure that is the case. I think that the late, great Mary Sauer had Pub 95 installed on her Windows 7 machine and it ran just fine. In fact I don't remember her ever saying that she had to run any version of Publisher in compatibility mode.

Bottom line is I see no reason to not just try to install Publisher 95 on your new Win7 machine. It is not going to hurt it. If Publisher 95 is part of an Office disk(s), then do a custom install and don't install the rest of Office. At the minimum just install it in its own folder rather than a default Office folder. Do you even have another version of Office installed? That is the only risk I can think of...that you might overwrite a newer version, and a custom install will avoid that. Also set a restore point just in case.

Then try running Pub 95 and you might be pleasantly surprised. And if you have problems then try running it under Windows XP mode before you go all the way back to Win95 mode. I am betting you won't need to run a virtual pc.

As to sticking to old legacy programs, I am not the best to ask. I am one of those people who follows the if it ain't broke don't fix it approach. I use a number of legacy programs that won't run on Windows 7. I thought Vista was a total failure and saw it a as a 'downgrade' and am still running XP on most of my computers, Win98se on one, and even have my original DOS machine, though I don't run it anymore. Win7 is much better, but I just have not seen a need to move on to Win7 and give up a couple of my legacy programs.  Unless a new version of a program offers me something I need that the old version doesn't, I don't upgrade. And even then you have to decide if the tradeoffs are worth it. Most upgrades also mean giving up features and functionality and sometimes the trade off is not worth it.

With that said, you will need to move on eventually. You may find that the printer drivers on your current printers will not work with Publisher 95. In that case you can print to a pdf tool such as www.primopdf.com and workaround the problem for the short term. However eventually we all have to give up on our oldy but moldy programs. And remember you don't have to burn the bridge. I currently have Pub 2000, 2003, 2007 and 2010 installed on this computer and 2002 on another. Just remember that once you save a file in Pub 2010 you will not be able to open it in Pub 95 again.

Good luck.
