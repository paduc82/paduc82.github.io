john korchok say:

You have a section break somewhere on page 7. Then the page number field immediately after that break is set to restart from a set number instead of incrementing the number from the section before it.

Start by making the section breaks visible:

  1. In Word, choose Word>Preferences>View.
  2. In the Show Non-Printing Characters section, check All. OK out.

The section break should now be visible as a pair of horizontal blue lines with text in the middle that reads one of Section Break (Continuous), Section Break (Next Page), Section Break (Odd Page) or Section Break (Even Page). Deleting that break should restore the page number.

But deleting the break could also alter the formatting of the page. If that is the case, try these steps:

  1. Click on the page immediately after the break.
  2. Choose Insert>Page Numbers.
  3. Click on the Format... button.
  4. Select the Continue from previous section radio button. OK out.

The page number should now be 8 instead of 2.

steve rindsberg say:
You're probably using way bigger images than are really necessary.

First thing to check on:  how large is the screen at the trade show (in pixels/dots, not inches/cm)?  

That, or a little bit more, will be the maximum resolution you need for your full-screen images.  Images that don't fill the screen can be proportionally smaller.

See, PowerPoint will resize images on the fly to match the resolution of the current display device, whether that's your monitor, a projector or an external display of some kind.   If the images are any bigger than necessary, the extra resolution is thrown away.  The more data it has to throw away, the longer PPT takes to display or refresh the image, which is why you're seeing the lag in editing that's driving you nuts.
