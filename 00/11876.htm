In you subtotal rows under total hours, format the cells using the standard time format (under format cells, number format, time)  of  37:30:55  

or use a custom number format  [hh]:mm   

the brackets around the hh  tells excel to comput total hours even if they exceed 24.   Since time and dates are usually stored together,  as the number of whole days (dates) and fractions of a day (hours/minutes/seconds), when you have hours exceed 24, they are counted as whole days and only the remaining hours are shown.  

for Subtotal Wave 3 where it shows total hours of 1:15, that actually will show 25:15 when you change the formatting.  

(if you formatted it as   dd hh:mm just to experiment  then you would see that it would display as   1  1:15 where the first one is one whole day (24 hrs).
