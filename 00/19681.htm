banditob say: Okay, I think that I've gotten this figured out now.  I thought I'd post what I found in case it helps someone else.

Originally, when I set up my styles for the numbered headings, I started by making an entry on a new line of the name for the style, such as My Heading 1.  I then started new lines with My Heading 2, My Heading 3, etc.  I then formatted each line to look the way I wanted it to and then went back to the first line, My Heading 1, and turned on numbering in the type that I wanted; 1, 1.1, 1.1.1, etc.  Then I went to line two, My Heading 2, and did the same.  Once I had completed this for each style, I returned to the beginning, selected the line for My Heading 1 and then clicked on New Style.  I named the style, added it to the template and then clicked OK.

Even though everything looked the way I wanted it to, I had, by using this method, set up individual numbering schemes that were not linked to each other and I could find no way to get them to link up.

After some more experimentation, I hit upon the right way to get them to link up.  It is as follows:

1. Enter the list of your heading names or any text for that matter, but the heading names keep things clearer for creating the styles later.  Do not do any formatting at this time!

2.  Select the entire list and then click on the numbering button in the ribbon and select the type of numbering that you want.

3.  Your list will now all be numbered at the single top-most level; 1., 2., 3., 4., etc. Now, go through the list from the second line on down and indent each one using either the Indent button in the ribbon or the tab key.  Now you will see your multi-level list or outline format.

4.  You can now go through and format to your heart's desire to get things to look just the way that you want them to.

5.  When you've finished formatting, select one line at a time and then click on the New Style button in the Styles window and name the style.  Then, if you want, click on the checkbox to add them to your template.  Otherwise, they will only be available in this particular document.  Click on OK and then repeat for each of the remaining styles that you want to create.

By creating the numbered heading list in this fashion, Word apparently links each of the headings to the same list so that they are all dependent upon the previous level's number.  As I mentioned earlier, there doesn't appear to be any other method to link up the numbering lists after the fact.  It must be done up front or not at all.

I hope that this helps!

Here goes...


I probably didn't really explain too well what I was really after in my original message.  What I wanted, and was eventually able to create, was a procedural document where every heading and paragraph had a corresponding number to it so that they could all be referenced by this number.  It would look something like this:


1.  My Heading 1

     1.2  My next My Paragraph 1 underneath My Heading 1...

     1.3  My Heading 2

            1.3.1  My Paragraph 2 underneath My Heading 2 that further develops the information in My Paragraph in section 1.2 with a proper hanging indent.

            1.3.2  More My Paragraph 2 under My Heading 2 in section 1.3

and so on and so forth.


It took quite a bit of trial and error, but I was finally able to accomplish what I needed using the following method:


I created the initial headings list as I described in my second post on August 1.  Then I set up the formatting for the My Paragraph text by linking their styles to the My Heading styles.  The tricky part was that I had to link the My Paragraph styles to the next lower level My Heading style to keep the numbering the way I wanted it.  So, in other words, My Paragraph 1 style is based upon My Heading 2 style.  My Paragraph 2 Style is based upon the My Heading 3 style and so on.  Each My Heading style is followed by its corresponding My Paragraph style, so once you've entered in a My Heading, it automatically gives you a My Paragraph style immediately following it like so:


1.0  My Heading 1 <Enter>

       1.1  My Paragraph 1 <Enter>

       1.2  My Paragraph 1, etc.


Each My Paragraph style is then followed by another My Paragraph style at the same level so that the procedure writing flows as naturally as possible.


This accomplishes the formatting that we needed; however, it does not allow for you to change levels easily using the tab key or Indent buttons, because My Headings only link to other My Headings, so you can only jump between heading styles and not amongst the paragraph styles.  I guess it's a small price to pay to get the formatting that we needed.


Again, I hope this will help someone else out who is trying to get a similar format to work.

    Dim oSl As Slide
    Dim oSh As Shape
    Dim oNewSh As Shape
    Dim x As Long
    
    For Each oSl In ActivePresentation.Slides
        For x = oSl.Shapes.Count To 1 Step -1
            Set oSh = oSl.Shapes(x)
            If oSh.Type = msoEmbeddedOLEObject _

                oSh.Copy
                Set oNewSh = _

                With oNewSh
                    .LockAspectRatio = False
                    .Top = oSh.Top
                    .Left = oSh.Left
                    .Height = oSh.Height
                    .Width = oSh.Width
                    .LockAspectRatio = oSh.LockAspectRatio
                End With
            End If
        Next
    Next

    For Each oSl In ActivePresentation.Slides
        For x = oSl.Shapes.Count To 1 Step -1
            Set oSh = oSl.Shapes(x)
            If oSh.Type = msoEmbeddedOLEObject _

                oSh.Delete
            End If
        Next
    Next

End Sub
