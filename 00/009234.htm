jay freedman say:
The default shortcut assignments (in all versions of Word, as far as I'm aware) include the three combinations Ctrl+S, Shift+F12, and Alt+Shift+F12 for the Save command and both Ctrl+Z and Alt+Backspace for the Undo command.

If the Ctrl+S and Ctrl+Z shortcuts don't work in your installation, it's possible that they were "stolen" by an add-in -- have you installed any new add-ins lately?

You can restore them by clicking the Office button in the top left corner of Word, clicking Word Options at the bottom of the menu, clicking Customize on the left side of the Options dialog, and clicking the Keyboard Shortcuts: Customize button near the bottom left corner. In the Customize Keyboard dialog, make sure Normal.dotm is selected in the "Save Changes In" box. Then:
  • With the "Office Menu" item selected in the Categories list, find and click on the FileSave item in the Commands list. If Ctrl+S isn't listed in the Current Keys box, click in the "Press new shortcut key" box and type Ctrl+S. Then click the Assign button at the bottom left.
  • Scroll the Categories list to the bottom and click on "All Commands". Then find and click on the EditUndo item in the Commands list. If Ctrl+Z isn't listed in the Current Keys box, click in the "Press new shortcut key" box and type Ctrl+Z. Then click the Assign button at the bottom left.
  • Click the Close button at the bottom right.

When you exit from Word, if it prompts you to save changes to the Normal.dotm template, click Yes.

Sub ReplaceName()

  Dim rng As Range
  Dim strAdd As String
