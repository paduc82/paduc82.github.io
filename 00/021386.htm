Try this.

Right-click on your sheet's tab and View Code.

Paste in the following:

 

Private Sub Worksheet_SelectionChange(ByVal Target As Range)
    Cells.Borders(xlInsideVertical).LineStyle = xlNone
    Cells.Borders(xlInsideHorizontal).LineStyle = xlNone
    ActiveCell.BorderAround ColorIndex:=3, Weight:=xlThick
End Sub

 

The active cell should now be highlighted with a bright blue border.

Experiment with different values for ColorIndex.  2 gives black, 4 gives pink, 5 gives yellow, 6 gives blue, 7 gives green and 8 gives red.

bad printout of fonts from Internet Explorer 8 when using dot matrix - Microsoft Community

dascom engineering say:

bad printout of fonts from Internet Explorer 8 when using dot matrix or line printers

When you print to a dot matrix printer (e.g. Oki ML320) or a line printer (e.g. Printronix P7210) from Internet Explorer 8 and select an asymmetrical graphics resolution (e.g. 120x72 dpi), the printout gets too large and is not properly spaced. The problem does not happen with lasers or thermal printers, which typically have a symmetrical graphics resolution (e.g. 300x300 dpi). It only happens when IE8 tries to render a browser font to graphics. The larger the difference betwenn horizontal and vertcal resolution is, the worse the printout gets. IE7 does not have this problem, neither have other browsers like Firefox or other applications like MS Word, Wordpad or Notepad. This problem is reported on several forums on the web and needs to be adressed urgently, as we have customer complaints from the field. The problem does not depend on the OS version, it was tested with WinXP, but was also reported from Vista- and Win2K3-server-users.
