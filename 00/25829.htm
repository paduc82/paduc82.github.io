jay freedman say:

Charles, that doesn't help much if the AutoCorrect entries themselves contain curly quotes -- you'd have to correct every document where you use those entries.

There is a way to change the entries themselves:

  • Download AutoCorrect2007.zip from http://jay-freedman.info/. (Despite the name, the template it contains is compatible with all versions from 2007 to 2016.) Extract the file AutoCorrect.dotm from the zip and store it in your Templates folder (usually %appdata%\Microsoft\Templates). Right-click the file, click Properties, and click the Unblock button.
  • Make a new document based on the template. In the message that appears, click the Backup button. This will create a backup document that contains a table, with all your current AutoCorrect entries. You'll be asked to provide a name to save the document; once it's saved, it will automatically close.
  • Reopen the backup document you just saved. Use Charles's method to convert all the curly quotes in this document to straight quotes. Save the document again.
  • Create another document based on AutoCorrect.dotm. In this one, click the Restore button. Click Yes in the next prompt and then select the backup document that now contains straight quotes. These entries will overwrite the ones stored in Word.

To set up wireless sync

If you don't want to physically connect your phone to your computer every time you need to sync, you can set up your phone to automatically sync with your computer over your home Wi-Fi network.

  • 1) Connect your phone to your PC using the USB cable that came with your phone.
  • 2) In the Zune software, click Settings > Phone >Wireless Sync.

After you've completed the steps above, files will automatically sync to your phone wirelessly when the following conditions are met:

  • Your phone and PC are both connected to your home Wi-Fi network. Note that wireless sync is not supported on workplace Wi-Fi networks.
  • Your phone battery has at least a medium-level charge and your phone is connected to an AC power adapter (that is, your phone must be connected to the wall charger and not connected to your computer).
