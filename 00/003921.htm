I am using document properties to create letterheads and other fields. Some of these fields (such as address fields) have require the text value to be distributed over several lines. However, when I try and change the text in File -> Properties -> Custom I cannot introduce carriage returns by pressing RETURN. How do I do that?

Bruno

a. user say:

You may be able to do what you need in VBA, e.g.

Sub custpropwithCRLF()
Const strPropName As String = "bmsAddress"
Dim bFound As Boolean
Dim cp As Office.DocumentProperty
Dim cps As Office.DocumentProperties
Dim strValue As String
strValue = "abc" & vbCrLf & "def" & vbCrLf & "ghi"
Set cps = ActiveDocument.CustomDocumentProperties
bFound = False
For Each cp In cps
  If cp.Name = strPropName Then
    ' we should really check it is the correct kind of property
    ' and is not linked, but let's keep it simple...
    cp.Value = strValue
    bvalue = True
    Exit For
  End If
Next
If Not bFound Then
  cps.Add Name:=strPropName, _
    LinkToContent:=False, _
    Type:=Office.DocProperties.offPropertyTypeString, _
    Value:=strValue
End If
Set cps = Nothing
End Sub

You may also need to check that the property value isn't too long because trying to set it.

Page Break kills text 'justification' - Microsoft Community

govt mike say:

Page Break kills text 'justification'

Here is my issue:

I have a paragraph that is 2.25 lines long. The last line is a single word.

Word is currently taking the single word onto the next page - 'orphaning' it.

Using orphan control, causes Word to take the ENTIRE PARAGRAPH to the next page, leaving a large, empty white space at the bottom of the page it was on.

When I attempt to put in a 'page break' after the first line of the paragraph (to move lines 2 & 3 to the next page), I lose the justification on the remaining line.

How the heck do I move two lines to the next page, leave the first line on the original page, and keep all of it justified to the right margin?

HELP?

jay freedman say:

There is no really good way to handle this. Most of what follows is covered or implied by

http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/BottomLine.htm

Probably the best course is to select the entire paragraph, go to the Font dialog, go to the Character Spacing tab, and set the spacing to Condensed by a few tenths of a point (try 0.3 pt or 0.4 pt). This may shorten the lines enough to make the paragraph only two lines, which will probably fit at the bottom of the page.

The next best course would be to play with the Space Before and/or Space After settings of several paragraphs on the page, either to make room for all three lines at the bottom of one page or to force all three to the next page while occupying the space at the bottom.

The two difficulties you're running into are:

  1. Widow/orphan control, when turned on, is specifically programmed to place at least two lines at the bottom of one page and at least two lines at the top of the next page. When the paragraph has exactly three lines, the only solution it knows is to put all three on the next page. If that leaves too much space at the bottom, you have to adjust the spacing of the paragraphs that come before the space.
  2. Inserting a manual page break also inserts a paragraph mark. Therefore your single line at the bottom of the page is actually a one-line paragraph, and the last (or only) line of a justified paragraph is not justified. There is a way to force it: With the cursor on that line, press Ctrl+Shift+J to apply "Distributed" instead of "Justified" formatting. Be aware, though, that this so-called solution will cause a problem (such as an empty page) if a change in any of the text preceding the page break causes one or more additional lines to occur; and this can happen even without any editing if the document is opened with a different printer driver selected (http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/TextReflow.htm).
