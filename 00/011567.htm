tina chen msft say:

Hi Guðný,

Thanks for sharing your experience with us.

As Vijay_Verma mentioned, there is a limitation in Excel that the significant digits for a number is 15 digits.

Excel follows the IEEE 754 specification on how to store and calculate floating-point numbers. Excel therefore stores only 15 significant digits in a number, and changes digits after the fifteenth place to zeroes.

The workaround is to format the cell as text, then type the numbers.  You can refer to the following article to get detailed information: Last digits are changed to zeroes when you type long numbers in cells of Excel

We understand your concerns, and understand when the number has more than 15 digits, it would be convenient if Excel can treat the number as text or give any warning or notification before changing data.

The reason that you can not get it through the updater is probably because your handset is network branded or network locked.

Not all networks approve new firmware so you may be stuck with what you've got.
