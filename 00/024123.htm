On the Home tab of the Ribbon, I am guessing that there is a blue rectangle around the Normal style. This is the style that controls the line formatting.

  1. Right-click on it and choose Modify.
  2. In the Modify Style dialog, click and hold on the Format button until the menu pops up.

  5. OK out.

All paragraphs in your document should have one line directly under another.

Automatically add a subject to a new email

How do I create a rule according to which my company name will be added as a subject in every new email I write?

ariel acu say:

Hi,

There are several ways for you to automatically set a subject to a new email using an Outlook app. One way to do this is by using Quick Steps in Outlook. To get started, kindly perform the following steps:

  1. From Home tab, click Create New in the Quick Steps box.
  2. In the pop-up window, create a name for your Quick Step.
  3. Click Choose an Action, then select New Message in the Respond section from the drop down list.
  4. Click Show Options to expand the preset message option.
  5. Enter your desired Subject in the box provided.

      6. Once done, click Finish.

After performing the following steps and want to create a new message, just click the Quick Step Name that you created inside the Quick Step Box.

Let us know if that works for you.
