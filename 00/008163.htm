Start-Process powershell -Verb runAs

Get-AppXPackage -AllUsers | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register "$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml"}

Solutions for Remote Lock and &quot;Memory Card - Microsoft Community

spy6x say:

Solutions for Remote Lock and &quot;Memory Card corrupted&quot; on E71

Hi guys,
After a terrible night of searching for ways to get back my memory card after my E71 was locked by remote lock function, I finally find out some ticks for myself and want to share for those who are feeling annoyed with this security setting on E71.
How to use Remote Lock on E71:
On your E71, go to Menu - Tools - Settings - General - Security - Phone and SIM Card:
1) Set your NEW lock code (default code is 12345, assume that you have not changed the code so far):
         - Enter 12345
         - Enter your new lock code (this code is used to unlock your phone after being locked remotely): example, If you want to set the code "iloveyou", then enter iloveyou
         - Verify your new code: as exampled above, you have to enter iloveyou
2) Allow remote lock:
         - Change from No to YES
         - New remote lock message: (enter the message you use to send from another phone to you E71. As soon as your E71 receive the message, your E71 will be locked). Assume you enter the message "lockmyphone" (without quotes)
         - Verify new remote lock message: enter "lockmyphone"
         - Lock code: (you will be asked for lock code to make sure that you are the owner of E71 on your hand, if you enter the lock code incorrectly, all you have done in step 2 will be unsaved), as exampled, enter: iloveyou
        - After "code accepted" appeared shortly, you can return you phone to homescreen and your E71 is ready to receive remote lock message.
Must remember:
You have to remember 2 things before you can check how secured your phone is after activating Remote Lock function.
First, Remember LOCK CODE to unlock your phone. As in the example, you have to remember the phrase iloveyou (If you dont, be ready for a nightmare at noon... hahaha)
Second, remember REMOTE LOCK MESSAGE to send a message from another phone asking for your E71 locked and (very important) REMOTE LOCK MESSAGE is the password to make your microSD accessible to your card reader, you have to remember the phrase: lockmyphone
If you don't, to format your memory card from your phone is the only way to make a PC read you memory card.
REMOTE LOCK YOUR E71:
Use another phone, and send an SMS message to your E71. The message content must be exactly the same as you set in REMOTE LOCK MESSAGE. Example, on another phone, write a message like this: lockmyphone and then send it to your phone number.
As soon as the message is sent and your E71 receive the message, your phone, I mean, E71, is automatically locked. To unlock your phone, enter LOCK CODE you already set, as in this example, enter iloveyou
Issues after remote lock
Issue 1: Some newbie experienced "memory card corrupted" and was asked for formatting.
Solution 1a: On your E71, go to Menu - Tools - Memory - Options - Remove memory card - YES (for the warning message on the screen), and remove your memory card from the slot then press OK as required on your screen. Finally, re-insert the card into the slot. Now, your phone can read the memory card.
Solution 1b: In case solution 1a does not help, try to set LOCK CODE to 12345 and ALLOW REMOTE LOCK to NO (refer to the paragraph above for details). [Some say addtionally, go to Menu - Tools - Encryption, and set Phone Memory, Memory Card to OFF. You, guys can check it]. After that, follow the solution 1a.
Issue 2: your E71 can read you memory card, but PC cannot see your card through a card reader.
Solution 2: Insert your memory card into your E71, go to Menu - Tools - Memory - Options - Remove password - YES (for the warning message). The password you have to enter is REMOTE LOCK MESSAGE, as the example above, the password is: lockmyphone
If "password removed" appears on the screen, then go to Options - Remove memory card - Select - Yes - Remove it from the slot. Now your card can be accessible by your PC thru a card reader.
HOPE ALL THESE HELP!

alisonrandall1 say:

Office Word 2016 Won’t print page borders.

miguelchiappori say: hello
You‚Äôve created, what you consider to be, your perfect document. So when you print it, why are your page borders either being lopped off on one side, the bottom, or not printed altogether? The usual reason has to do with something called your ‚Äúprinter‚Äôs minimum margin setting.‚Äù

There are a couple of ways to figure that out so that you can adjust your margins accordingly before you print (thereby skipping the angst).

The short answer is: You are using too big of a page border. If the border extends too close to the edge of the paper, your printer may not be able to print it. This is because all printers have what is called "dead space" around the edges of the paper. This space is typically used for mechanical handling of the paper, and therefore cannot be printed upon. Different printers have different amounts of dead space; you should check with your printer manufacturer for complete information.

To correct your page borders and make sure they are not in the dead space area, follow these steps if you are using Word 2013 or Word 2016:

-Make sure the Home tab of the ribbon is displayed.
-Locate the Borders tool within the Paragraph group. Click the down-arrow at the right of the Borders tool. Word displays a palate of options.
-Select the Borders and Shading option at the bottom of the palette. Word displays the Borders and Shading dialog box.
-Make sure the Page Border tab is displayed and click on the Options button. Word displays the Border and Shading Options dialog box.

-Use the controls in the dialog box to specify how you want your border to appear. These controls are very similar to those used by Word to set up paragraph borders.
-Adjust the Top, Bottom, Left, and Right margin settings so they are larger than the amount of dead space imposed by your printer.
-Click on OK two times.

If you are using earlier versions of Word, use the following steps:

-Make sure the Page Layout tab of the ribbon is displayed.
- Click the Page Borders tool in the Page Background group. Word displays the Page Border tab of the Borders and Shading dialog box.
-Click on the Options button. Word displays the Border and Shading Options dialog box.
-Use the controls in the dialog box to specify how you want your border to appear. These controls are very similar to those used by Word to set up paragraph borders.
-Adjust the Top, Bottom, Left, and Right margin settings so they are larger than the amount of dead space imposed by your printer.
-Click on OK two times.
When you reprint, if the border still won't print, then it could be an interaction between your page margin settings and your page border settings. You should make sure that the page border settings are larger than your dead space, but less than the applicable page margin. Thus, if your page border is set to print on the bottom at 36 points (one-half inch), then your page margin for the bottom of the page should be greater than one-half inch.
