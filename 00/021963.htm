cbarnhorst say:

14393 was never an insider preview build.  It is the Anniversary Update everybody got last year.  If your build is 16199 you have the latest preview build.  A new one is expected sometime next week.  

You can "Stop receiving insider builds" in Settings/Update and security/Windows Insider Program.  You will not receive any more previews.  You will receive the next version of Windows, Fall Creators Update, in the Fall when everyone else gets it.  But you will not receive security updates until then since those are issued to the general public in cumulative updates to 15063, or in new insider builds to insiders.

Files cannot be lost if you are backing them up.  

If you decide to return to the release build, 15063 (Creators Update), then you will not be able to keep your files, apps, and settings and will have to reinstall your files from your backup and apps from your original sources.

tug'tekin say: But what about dropping out from Insider Preview having this os build? Will it hurt my files and any other things?

cbarnhorst say:

No, opting-out does not itself change anything but some registry settings.  The real problem is that being on an insider preview in the first place puts files at risk.  This is why Microsoft advises us not to use a preview build on a primary or production device.  What you should do is start the opt-out process and read the page that appears listing your options.  Then decide.

In any case put the mouse and keyboard down (so to speak) and back up your files now.
