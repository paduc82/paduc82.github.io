In case anyone else comes here in search of a solution ...

It appears that the current generation of HP printer drivers are, once again, trying to be clever.  In this case, it looks as though information identifying the physical device is stashed away in the driver somewhere (presumably in the port information).

That may not correlate with your experience, but that's what's caused this issue for me - a printer was replaced (an HP '276DW') with an identical printer, and on the same IP address.  Replacing a printer isn't exactly an uncommon thing to have to do, but it appears that if you do so, it completely breaks whatever ports you might already have configured.

What worked for us was:

Go into "Devices and Printers" (this is on a "Windows 7" box), find your errant printer, and remove it.  If it's a multi-function device, you'll have multiple entries - remove them all.

Use the normal "Add Printer" wizard.  Fill in the blanks, let it auto-detect everything, et voila!
