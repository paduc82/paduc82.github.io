 sockatume---01  say:
            
            
            
            

radical24: It's got nothing to do with charging the battery for 6-12 hours as in the NiMH days, it's an issue of calibrating the battery's controller.


 


By default, the battery control circuitry reads off certain values of internal resistance as meaning "empty battery" and "full battery". Without fully charging the battery, it doesn't actually know what the full battery level is, so it may tell you that
 the battery is full before it actually is full. Likewise, without fully discharging the battery*, it doesn't know what an actual empty battery looks like, so it will whinge at you about being nearly out of charge for hours before it actually dies.


 


It won't affect the battery's service life either way.They start to oxidise pretty much from the moment they leave the factory, and operating temperature has as much stronger effect than any careless charge cycling. If you were to really batter a battery
 day and night, you might be able to shorten its life significantly, but the sad fact is that it's going to die of old age long before you can kill it.


 


*Well, as close to fully discharging as the battery's controller will let you. Completely emptying the battery could cause irreversible damage, so it has an absolute minimum level it won't let the battery cross. Self-discharge might go through that limit
 and on a long enough period of time, damage the battery.
