My entire document is one section. None of the pages of the document are linked to the previous pages. In other words, none of the footers in the document show that they are linked to the previous footer. All the even pages are automatically linked and all the odd pages are automatically linked.

In fact, all the footers are linked - simply because they're all in the same Section. What they're not linked to (and cannot be) is the footer in a previous Section.

The simplest way to suppress pages numbering in the way you describe is to:

• insert 'Next Page' Section breaks either side of those two pages;

• deactivate the 'same as previous' setting for the footers in both the Section to be unnumbered and the one following it

• delete the page numbering from the footer(s) of the Section to be unnumbered.

Hello,

I'm glad you found a solution. Here are some thoughts.

Link to previous will never be available in a single-section document because it is talking about linking to the header/footer in the previous section. There is no previous section.

You have two footers, an even-page footer and an odd-page footer. Unless you do something unusual, a change to one of these will not change the other. They are independent footers.

You do have fields. They are the PAGE fields that give you page numbers.
