donald howard - pgh say:

One of the first issues I noticed with Windows 10 is the long load/boot time.

It appears that windows 8 does this by default (Or atleast always has for me)

You need to follow the steps below and change the way to shut down your computer  :(

Control Panel

Choose what the power buttons do

Scroll down and verify "Turn on fast startup" is enabled

Right click on desktop and create a new shortcut

In the location type in "%windir%\System32\shutdown.exe -s -hybrid -f -t 00" and name it Hybrid Shutdown or whatever you want.

Now use this to shut down your computer and you will find that your startup time is back to what you may be accustomed to.

I found that this also corrected an issue if you are using alternate locations for your user files located on a different storage device.

Example: I have a Samsung ATIV 500t tablet with 64GB internal memory and a 128GB secondary Micro SD storage. I have changed all of my user locations to the D drive due to the additional storage abilities. If I start the tablet without using the Hybrid option, my D drive does not initialize before the desktop loads and I get an error of invalid location. If I use the Hybrid option, all of my storage locations work properly.

I hope this helps.  :)

david marcovitz say: As Gina says, the limitations aren't really within PowerPoint. They are limits for the server where you are putting it. Many servers have both size and bandwidth limitations. That is, how much you can store on the server (size of files) and how much can be downloaded in a given period of time (bandwidth). For example, you might be able to store 1GB on your server, and you might be limited to 50GB of bandwidth per month. That would mean that if you had a 1GB file stored there, you couldn't put anything else up. Additionally, after the file is download 50 times, no one would be able to download it again until the next month. These numbers are just examples. You will have to check on the real limits of your server. It may be that you have exceeded the limits of your server for all files you have posted or for the size of a single file.

Additionally, you want to consider the people downloading your file. 58MB is probably on the high side for what you would generally expect people to download. That could take a long time on a slow Internet connection.
