rabmag say:

Thanks Niranjan!

I solved the problem after my rather rude (though understandably frustrated) post yesterday.

It turns out that simply turning off the Anti-Virus program before the install may not be enough. I discovered this after I installed the program on a second computer in my home. The computer I was having problems with had ZoneAlarm Extreme Security and the other computer had BitDefender Antivirus. Although I had turned off the real time virus scanning of both AVP's the ZoneAlarm program still had other protection services running in the background which were preventing access to certain keys (HKLM\Classes) in the registry.

I uninstalled ZoneAlarm and then was able to install Office 2010 without any problems and it was running correctly without any more annoying "Wait while Windows..." messages. I then re-installed ZoneAlarm and everything still works fine.

It may not be necessary to uninstall an AVP if one knows what services it may be running in the background and turns them off in the Services Contol Panel but removing and reinstalling an Anti Virus Program is certainly far easier and quicker than formatting a hard drive and reinstalling everything.

If people are receiving this message it is most likely due to their Anti-Virus/Anti-Malware program interfering with the install.

a. user say:

Hello there SalmaUmme Sweeti! Thanks for posting your query on Microsoft Community.

About your concern, you can backup your photos on OneDrive. With your People photos concern, please restart your phone to get back on track.

To restart, press and hold the Power button until a Slide down to power off message appears on-screen, then swipe down. (It usually takes about three seconds for the message to appear.) To turn your phone back on, press the Power button.

If that doesn't work, press and hold the Volume down and Power buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10 to 15 seconds), then release the buttons. Your phone will restart automatically.


Please be reminded that:

Your phone may vibrate as soon as you press the Volume down and Power buttons because it's changing your ringtone to vibrate. Wait to release the buttons until you feel the second vibration 10 to 15 seconds later.

If you don't let go of the buttons immediately after the vibration at 10 to 15 seconds, your phone may show a black startup screen instead of restarting normally. If that happens, try pressing the button combo again.

Not all phones restart this way, so check with your phone's manufacturer to find out how to force a restart.


If your phone has a replaceable battery (it's often under your phone's back panel), you can also restart your phone by removing and reinserting the battery, and then pressing the Power button to turn on your phone. The date and time may be off after you restart this way, but you can fix that by swiping over to the All apps list from Start, then selecting Settings > Time & language > Date & time.
