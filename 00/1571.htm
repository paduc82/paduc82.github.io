Sorry, I didn't realize you were an admin for your tenant.  Usually setting transport rules for the types of things you are looking at can get messy.  Imagine a 10,000 user tenant where every user wants a similar transport rule for some reason.  That would be a nightmare to manage.

As far as taking different actions for different members of a distribution group that the rule is set to trigger on... That you can't do with a transport rule.  If it were an on-premise deployment, you would have to set up something before it reached your transport service to do distribution group expansion and then apply the rule at your transport servers.  But in your case, the email is going to get to the transport server, the rule will evaluate it based on the group you put in and it will either delete it (for everyone) or do whatever other action you tell it to do (for everyone).  

For the level of granularity you are talking about, the rule has to be on the client side.
