TIP: If you plan on purchasing Norton Internet Security (NIS) or any other Norton application (e.g., NAV; N360), you must do so BEFORE the free-trial expires! The same would apply if you will be replacing the NIS free-trial with any other application.

Norton applications are notorious for not upgrading (or uninstalling) "cleanly" so proceed as follows:

1. Download the Norton Removal Tool, saving it to your desktop: ftp://ftp.symantec.com/public/english_us_canada/removal_tools/Norton_Removal_Tool.exe

2. Close all open applications (i.e., anything with an icon on the taskbar) and uninstall the free-trial: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Uninstall-or-change-a-program 

3. Close all open applications, right-click on the file you saved in #1 above and select Run as Administrator to run the utility. Don't TOUCH your keyboard until the run completes, then reboot.

4. Install the purchased version of NIS.

NB: If you decide NOT to purchase NIS or any other Norton application, do Steps #1 through #3 above before installing the replacement application.

Sorry, this is an ugly situation that MS activation just isn't set up to handle. They allow us to use Office when we travel, but when it comes to purchase and activation they have massive tunnel vision.

Exactly which Office bundle did you buy, one of the Office 365 subscriptions, or Office 2019 one-time payment.

MS has split the world into several sales regions. Keys bought in a region have to be activated in the same regions.

Short form, they have 2 main reasons, US Export laws limit where they can sell certain features (specifically encryption) and second currency exchange rates.  They don't want someone in a "1st world" country buying from online store in a "3rd world" country where the combination of the local price and the currency exchange rate would allow you to buy it at a "discount".

So when you buy and activate Office, the activation site makes assumptions about where you are. It uses your IP address to guess your geographic location. It uses that to assume the base language you want office installed in and the associated languages. If it is 365, it also REQUIRES a "local" billing address and local credit card.

Especially if you have 365, even if you manage to beat Office into submission and activate it there, you WILL have problems when you get home and try to buy a new subscription in a year's time. But even with 2019 I expect you will keep running into unwanted licesnse issues when you get back to the states.

What is the fix? There is no good, or simple fix ...

#1 Gift / sell the product key to someone who lives in France, wait till you get back to the US to buy Office

#2 take it back to the store, ask for an exchange for a US based product key.  Most stores have a no return no exchange policy about product keys ...

#3  Contact MS Support and ask for a replacement key.  Maybe, but good luck with that. The first person you talk to is a "script monkey". They MUST ask you a bunch of questions that are irrelevant to this problem. But they have to ask.  First thing, ask for a "trouble ticket number". Why, because don't be surprised if they "accidentally" hang up on you ... yah... Second, keep asking to be transferred to a supervisor.  They'll do it, eventually. There are maybe one or two "supervisors" who can do this, now you have to be lucky enough to accidentally be connected to one.

Frankly, for your peace of mind, and stress level, just bite the bullet and go with #1.

In the mean time you can use Office Online or one of the free Office alternatives.
