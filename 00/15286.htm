#2 choice


MsgBox Title:="box title", prompt:="hello"

MsgBox prompt:="hello",Title:="box title"

 

Note how the ORDER or POSITION of the parameters DOES NOT matter. However, ALL THREE of the above are functional THE SAME command.

As far as I can tell, neither Word 2016 on Windows nor Windows 2016 on Mac lets you move the right tab stop beyond the margin using the ruler. Word 2010 (Windows) used to let you do that.

However, you can achieve the same result in Mac Word 2016 by

 a. clicking Home->Styles Pane to open the Styles pane

 b. locate each TOC style that you actually use (by default it would be TOC 1 to TOC 3) and...

 c. click the dropdown to the right of the style

 d. Click Modify Style...

 e. Click the dropdown to the right of "Format" at the bottom left of the dialog box

 f.  Click Tabs... 

 g. Click the tab you need to change, if any (e.g. here I see a right tab at 6.3" ) and click the "-" button below the list of tab stops to delete it

 h. Click the "right" radio button and select the tab leader you want.

 i. Enter the tab value you want (say 6.8") and click the + button. 

(NB, unless something has changed, you have never actually been able to alter the settings for existing tabs - you always have to delete the one you want to change, and create a new one with the settings you want).

I believe it was Word 2013 that first removed the possibility to set a tab stop outside the page margin. At least in Word for Windows, you will have to use the Tab dialog box to set the tab stop; you can't use the ruler for tab stops outside of a right indent.
