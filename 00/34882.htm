cbarnhorst say: A vm is always safer regarding the impacts of bugs on the primary OS, but a vm requires a new license, unlike re-installation of Windows on a partition.  A vm has to be registered as a Windows 10 device of its own (largely due to their portability between hosts).  If you have an unused Windows 10 license or are willing to buy one then a vm is a good way to go.  A new partition on the system drive is relatively safe but it is still possible for a severe bug or installation malfunction to render the device inoperable and could require re-installation of the primary partition as well.  Not common, but be prepared with a known-good-state image backup at all times.   The best solution, of course, is to use a "test-box" device and keep previews completely away from your primary or production devices.
