I wrote:  "Alternatives:  [...] 2. DATA/column14 is zero (or close to it), not 28.93 as you believe".

That is exactly the explanation.

The named range DATA corresponds to Data!A4:W131.

We find 4000000 in A4.

You labeled column P as 14; and indeed, P4 contains 28.63, the value you want to extract.

But in fact, column N is the 14th column in the named range.  And indeed, N4 is blank, which Excel treats as zero in this context.

The disparity is caused by the fact that you have hidden columns K and N.  But they still count as part of the range defined by DATA.

The correction in 'Sys 1 Shift 1'!C63 is:  change VLOOKUP(C$61,DATA,14,FALSE) to VLOOKUP(C$61,DATA,16,FALSE).

CCBeeBiz wrote:

lots of other Vlookups on the same worksheet are working as expected


They produce non-zero results.  But I believe that at least some do not produce the correct results "as expected".  GIGO!

For example, 'Sys 1 Shift 1'!C64 contains the following formula labeled "Ingredient Cost":

=IF(C$61=0,0,(VLOOKUP(C$61,DATA,17,FALSE)))*C$16

The displayed result is $776.00.

But since Data!S4 is labeled "17" and "Ingredients" with a value of 47.34 and C$16 is 388, I suspect the "expected" result in C64 is $18,367.92 (47.34 * 388).

776 is 2*388.  The 2 comes from Data!Q4, 2 columns to the left of S4 and labeled "15" and "Bag / Lot".

The formula in 'Sys 1 Shift 1'!C64 should probably be:

=IF(C$61=0,0,(VLOOKUP(C$61,DATA,19,FALSE)))*C$16
