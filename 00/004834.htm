I figured it out - the 19th appt is really the one for the 20th (3 AM UTC) and outlook creates the other two in the pattern on UTC Mon / tues, which is Sun/Mon here.

Your code is missing a tzid  so outlook uses utc as the tzid.

DTEND;TZID="Eastern Standard Time":20110919T030000

1. Can I have more than one anti-virus application or security suite installed?

No.  Having more than one real-time anti-virus application or security suite installed will compete with other anti-malware product(s), and can cause severe performance problems and system stability issues, and may limit the effectiveness of the products installed.  Even if you attempt to have more than one product installed, with one active and another disabled, the disabled product will likely still have active components and/or drivers installed that will conflict with WD.  The important issue here is that any other product with real-time scanning will conflict with WD (or any other real-time product).

However, you can have an on-demand scanner, such as Malwarebytes, installed.  Malwarebytes offers two different scanners – one on-demand (free), and one real-time (paid).  The on-demand scanner does not conflict with WD’s real-time scanning.  Some users consider having one real-time product (e.g. WD) and an on-demand product (e.g. Malwarebytes (free)) a good combination on their system.

I have very little Excel experience but would like to learn some tricks to help with budgeting.

joeu2004 say:

Does the following example do what you want?

You can download the Excel file from https://app.box.com/s/j64rnzsx4akuhorr6goawzu0ah9emy7c .

Enter the following into the yellow-highlighted cells:

B1: 0% expiration date

E4: Remaining balance

A5:A6:  first two payment dates of a month

A7: =IF(N(E6)<=0, "", EDATE(A5,1))

B5: =2*(DATEDIF(A5,$B$1,"m")+1)

B6: =IF(A6="", "", B5-1)

C5: =IF(A5="", "", IF(E4<=C4, E4, ROUNDUP(E4/B5,2)))

D5: =IF(A5="", "", ROUNDUP(C5,2))

E5: =IF(A5="", "", ROUND(E4-D5,2))

Copy B6 into B7

Copy C5:E5 into C6:E7

Copy A7:E7 down the column

For this example, the "equal" payments are $228.62 and $228.61.  The penny-off difference is due to rounding.

Now, suppose you make a larger payment ($450) on 2/1/2019.  Enter $450 into D13 to override the formula.

Note that the subsequent "equal" payments change to $223.70 and $223.69 automatically.

I tried to keep the initial design simple in order to make it easier for you to understand and confirm (or deny) that this is what you want, for the most part.

Limitations:

1. As noted, there will penny-off differences in the "equal" payments due to rounding.  Acceptable?

2. The payments dates in A5:A6 cannot be the 29th, 30th or 31st of a month.  Acceptable?

3. There must be two initial payments in the first month.  Acceptable?  Or do you want to allow the first payment in the table (A5) to be the second payment of a month?

4. The formula in C5 calculates a payment that pays off the remaining balance if it is less than previous "equal" payment.  That prevents such nonsense like equal payments of $0.02 until the expiration date.  To demonstrate, enter $10,300 into D13 instead of $450.  Acceptable?

macropod say:

To do something like that, you'd need to use either a Directory merge, so that you get a down-then-across format, plus a record that contains only the first letter of the alphabet for each group.

For the directory mailmerge main document setup:
1. On the Page Layout tab, in the Page Setup group, click Columns and choose More Columns...
2. In the Columns dialog, select however many columns you want to have.
3. In the Spacing box, set the width to whatever space you want to have between the columns.
4. Create a single-cell table with a ficed height & width in the first column. Make it high enough to accomodate as many data lines as the longest record will contain.
