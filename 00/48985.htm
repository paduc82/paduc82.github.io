    bossdweeb say: Assuming you are not speaking of sound coming from the speakers -
    Are you able to isolate where the noise comes from ?
    Power supplies and graphics cards sometimes get what is generically termed 'capacitor squeal'.
    This noise is caused by high frequency vibrations and can emanate from capacitors or other
    components.
    It is not generally something that can be easily fixed. There are technical/mechanical fixes, but
    depending on the source of the squeal, it may not be possible to implement a fix.
    If you can isolate the source it may be best to return the component for warranty replacement,
    if the machine is a "home build", or take the PC to where you purchased it from for warranty repair.

    EDIT: I just learned something new about cap squeal.
    If this is happening when playing games, and the games are running with a very high frame rate,
    if you enable V-sync (sometimes the setting says 'sync every frame') it should stop the noise.
    Sounds reasonable, as the graphics card would be working  sort of like a car engine over-revving.

    Sounds like it could be the CPU fan bearings. As the CPU kicks up a step, the fan controller will increase voltage to the fan and speed it up briefly. CPU fans and heat sinks are real dust magnets. As the dust accumulates over the fins of the heat sink, dust gets blown back into the fan bearings and craps them out.

     

    OEM CPU fans are rarely a stock size as found in computer stores, so you either have to buy a complete CPU fan and heat sink package or go for a heat sink which takes a stock 120mm fan.
