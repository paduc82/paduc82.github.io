john in cheltenham say: OK guys - here is the solution to this problem. I finally found someone at Microsoft who put this solution together after a whole lot of guesses.

At the Windows start button type in msconfig - when this opens click on the "Services " tab. A long list of services comes up all with ticked boxes. Click on the hide Microsoft Services box then uncheck all the remaining services (I have about 30 most of which you can easily identify). Click on "Apply" then exit and restart your PC.

I found after I did this that the problem went away but of course this is not a final solution as any non Microsoft service is not running. Make sure your Power Settings are set so that the PC does not try to sleep or hibernate and it is a good idea to activate Windows Defender as your Anti Virus & Firewall will not be working if it is third party such as Norton 360 as in my case. Head off and leave your PC for a couple of hours and guess what - on return it will be working as it was when you left.

Now having proved that your PC is working you can now repeat the process above running msconfig - only this time you should enable a few services at a time leaving the Microsft ones enabled and take a note of which ones you have done.  Restart your PC and leave it running for a couple of hours and on return if it is working correctly then select a few more and repeat the process. If not working (frozen) then the culprit will be one of the last few you checked so uncheck this one and restart. Remember that there might be more than one problem service. Once you have identified the problem service or services you can leave this disabled and get on to the 3rd party for a solution. Meanwhile all should be working perfectly on your PC and you can reset your power settings to sleep etc.

The real issue here for Microsoft is that installing or upgrading to Windows 7 involves running a Windows 7 compatability program before the install kicks off and clearly Microsoft have not designed this to work correctly.

Good luck all
