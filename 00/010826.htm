Get a link

jay freedman say:

Graham's reply is correct. The important point is not whether you were typing English words, but what the proofing language is at the point where you typed the quote marks. Select the

If it's one of the French variations (or certain other languages that also use guillemets), Word will automatically format quotes as guillemets. Although that appears in the Undo list as an Autoformat action, it isn't one that you can disable in the Options dialog.

Proofing language is treated by Word as a character-by-character format, much like bold or italic. It can be set through the dialog, but it can also ride in on text pasted from a source such as a web page or another Office document. Further, if the "Detect language automatically" box in the dialog is checked, Word will try to guess which language applies to each word as you type. While that may be convenient for people who type in a mixture of languages, Word's guesses are often wildly wrong. For your purposes, be sure to clear that check box.

One more comment: Because you have chosen the option to hide the spelling errors, you won't see that the English words would be marked with red wavy underlines, because Word is comparing them to the French dictionary. (Hiding the errors is not the same as not checking them -- the latter would be the result of the "Do not check spelling or grammar" check box in the Proofing Language dialog.)
