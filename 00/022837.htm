The answer is Yes and No. (don't you love that!)

I have clients running off of a NAS and it works great.  I also have had a client that the minute the db was on the NAS, concurrency was not possible.  So it depends on the NAS.  It would seem not all NASs are equal.  What the difference is exactly, I do not know, but obviously there is a difference at the file system/OS that makes some NASs more conducive than others to allowing simultaneous access to files on them.

daniel pineault say:

Also, depending on your NAS, you might want to experiment with Oplock settings

scottgem say:

A NAS is simply a file server is not considered a computer on the LAN. So, while its mappable, It can't be logged into, like a regular server.

So yes, normally, you should be able to. However, you should not put the application on a NAS, only the back end data. The front end of the app should go on each user's PC linked to the back end's tables.
