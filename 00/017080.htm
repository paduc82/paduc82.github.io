zackary vanosdol say:
Try the following steps to delete a user account from the Control Panel.

Right click on the Stat button and select the Control Panel.
Click on the User Accounts and Click on the Manage another account link.
If prompted by UAC, click on Yes.
Click on a user account which you want to delete.
Click on the Delete the account link.
Click on Delete Files or Keep Files for what you want to do with the user account's personal files.
Click on Delete Account to confirm.
When finished, you can close the Control Panel if you like.

Hi Dan,

Please follow the steps provided on the link below, that procedure will uninstall the internal key and cache on credentials.

Reset Office activation
Open Command Prompt and navigate to the following folder
C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office\Office16
C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office16
Run: cscript ospp.vbs /dstatus
Observe the results and uninstall each product key
cscript ospp.vbs /unpkey:<last 5 chars of product key shown in dstatus>

Remov Credentials in HKCU registry:
Navigate to HKC_User\Software\Microsoft\Office\16.0\Common\Identity\Identities and remove all of the identities under the key.


Remove the stored Credentials in the Credential Manager:

Open Control Panel > Credential Manager. Remove all Windows credentials listed for Office15 or Office16
