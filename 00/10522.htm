  1. This was originally discovered while working on a production document, but its not document specific as we can reproduce on a fresh document.

  3. I can also reproduce on a [Hyper-V] VM running Windows 10 and Office 2016 at various resolutions (e.g.: 1024x768, 1366x768, 1280x1024 etc.) whether or not ClearType is enabled suggesting it's not the hardware (graphics adapter or monitor) but potentially a software issue.

  6. I can reproduce on a vanilla installation of Windows 10 (not domain joined) and vanilla installation of Office 2016 (not customized).

  7. I cannot reproduce on a laptop or VM running Windows 7 & Office 2010 suggesting this may simply be a difference between Office versions.


Steps to Reproduce:

  1. Create a new blank document
  2. Under the View tab
    1. Ensure your zoom level is set to 100%
    2. Ensure you're in the Print Layout
  3. Insert a table with 1 column & 6 rows
  4. Select the table & ensure the cell margins are set to 0
    1. Select Table
    2. Secondary mouse click on the table & select Table Properties
    3. Go to the Cell tab
    4. Click the Options button
    5. Ensure top and bottom are zero even if grayed out
    6. Optionally uncheck 'Same as the whole table' and set top & bottom to 0
    7. Click OK twice
  5. Add text in each cell (e.g.: name, address line 1, address line 2, city, state, zip.)
  6. Remove the borders from the table
    1. Select Table
    2. Go to the Design tab
    3. Click the Borders drop down
    4. Select 'No Border' from the list
  7. Apply Borders to the Paragraph
    1. Select Table
    2. Go to the Design tab
    3. Click the Borders drop down
    4. Select 'Borders & Shading' at the bottom
    5. Select 'Paragraph' from the Apply to drop down
    6. Click the bottom area to apply the underline
    7. Click OK
  8. Adjust your view via zoom slider in the bottom right area of Word (feel free to use the plus & minus buttons)
  9. At various zoom levels, one or more underlines will disappear.
