scottgem say:

Are you all on a LAN? I can understand the use of Dropbox, but I'm really not sure how that will work with attachments. 

If you are on a LAN then this should be relatively easy. I would create a folder on your LAN for the image files and then synchronize that folder with Dropbox. I would then store the location of the images folder in a parameters table in the back end or as a Constant in VBA.

Public CONST conImageFolder As String = "d:\foldername\"

To display the image on a form I would use an unbound Image control and use the On Current event to set the Picture property of the image control to:

Me.imgProduct.Picture = conImageFolder & Me.ProductCode & ".jpg"

You can use that same expression to display the image wherever you need to.

This PC is always on, always logged in, and simply "rotated" from individual to individual as the day progresses and shifts change.

Additionally, we have OneDrive installed, pointing to the Operations account O365 OneDrive. There are approximately 100 files stored here.  Our six Operations employees can either access the files locally, via Windows Explorer using the installed OneDrive application and Office 2013 suite (word, excel, etc.); or access them remotely via the online O365 login on a web browser.  Sometimes, these employees continue projects from other computers in the office, or from home. But always by logging in to O365 as the "Operations User".

As we contemplate transitioning this process to having each user log into their own Windows PC profile, and open up Outlook with their own O365 business account profile, and then access the Operations email via a "shared account" process, we have many questions that we'd like to hash out first.  

Note: We have read this article on the technical process: https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Convert-a-user-mailbox-to-a-shared-mailbox-Admin-Help-2e122487-e1f5-4f26-ba41-5689249d93ba

1. Do all of the sub-folders and the email contents within them stay intact?

rudy xu msft say:

Hi Jim,

 

Thanks for posting here.

 

Here are the answers below:

 

  1. Based on my test, they can be stayed intact in the shared mailbox.
  2. The limits on shared mailboxes in the Small Business and Enterprise plans was raised to 50 GB. However, shared mailboxes in Exchange Online Plan 1 and Plan 2 have a quota of 10 GB.
  3. You can enable the In-Place Archive feature in the EAC. Or add the retention policy for Shared mailbox, hope the reply from Barry can help you in the link below: https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/msoffice/forum/msoffice_o365admin-mso_security/retention-policy-for-shared-mailboxes/d55d70d8-a590-435b-bb26-6499c2b8d6eb
  4. It's not feasible to access these files, since shared mailbox has no license and OneDrive is not provisioned for this user. However, the admin can access these files in the next 30 days after delete the license, since the files can be stored 30 days. Beyond to 30 days, these files will be deleted and not recoverable. So, we suggest you back up these files.

 

After converting to the shared mailbox, you need grant the member Full Access permission to let the shared mailbox auto-map to the member's Outlook client.

 

If anything is unclear, please feel free to let us know at your convenience.
